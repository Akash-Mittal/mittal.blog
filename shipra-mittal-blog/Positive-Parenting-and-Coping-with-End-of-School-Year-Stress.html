The Story of Sarah and Her Daughter

Sarah is a busy working mom who tries her best to balance her job, household chores, and her daughter's needs. Her daughter, Emily, is a bright and energetic 9-year-old who loves learning but also enjoys playing with her friends and doing various after-school activities.

As the end of the school year approaches, Sarah notices that Emily becomes more irritable, moody, and anxious. She also complains about headaches, stomachaches, and difficulties falling asleep

After some research, Sarah realizes that Emily may be experiencing end of school year stress, which is common among children and teenagers. She decides to apply some positive parenting strategies to help Emily cope with this stress and enjoy the last days of the school year.

Real Life Examples of Positive Parenting Strategies

Positive parenting is an approach that emphasizes the building of strong and respectful relationships between parents and children, based on communication, empathy, trust, and mutual support. Here are some examples of positive parenting strategies that can help children cope with end of school year stress:

  • Listen actively: Children need to feel heard and validated, especially when they are feeling stressed or overwhelmed. Sarah takes time to listen to Emily's concerns and emotions without judgment or interruption, and she offers empathy and reassurance.
  • Encourage self-care: Children need to learn how to take care of their physical, emotional, and mental health. Sarah helps Emily establish healthy routines such as regular exercise, nutritious meals, enough sleep, and relaxation techniques.
  • Promote positive thinking: Children need to develop a positive mindset and self-talk in order to manage stress and anxiety. Sarah encourages Emily to focus on her strengths, achievements, and goals, and to challenge negative thoughts and self-doubts.
  • Plan fun activities: Children need to have time and opportunities for leisure, creativity, and enjoyment. Sarah plans some fun activities with Emily, such as a picnic, a movie night, or a baking session, which can boost their mood and bond.

Conclusion: 3 Critical Comments

  1. End of school year stress is a common and normal experience for children and teenagers, but it can also affect their academic performance, behavior, and mental health.
  2. Positive parenting is an effective and evidence-based approach to help children cope with end of school year stress, by promoting communication, empathy, trust, and mutual support.
  3. By applying positive parenting strategies, parents can not only help their children manage stress and anxiety but also strengthen their relationship, enhance their well-being, and develop their life skills.
