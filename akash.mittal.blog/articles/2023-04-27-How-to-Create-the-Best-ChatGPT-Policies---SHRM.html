Creating Effective ChatGPT Policies: Tips and Examples from SHRM

Imagine you're the manager of an e-commerce store and you've just implemented ChatGPT as your customer service channel. The feature has been a hit among your customers, but you've noticed that your team is struggling to keep up with the volume of inquiries. You've also noticed that some customers are getting frustrated with long wait times and inaccurate responses. You need to create ChatGPT policies that will optimize the channel for both your team and your customers.

So, how do you create effective ChatGPT policies? You can start by following these tips from the Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM):

  1. Define the purpose and scope of the ChatGPT channel. What types of inquiries should the ChatGPT handle? What should be escalated to a human representative? What's the expected response time? Defining these parameters will help your team prioritize inquiries and manage expectations.
  2. Create a knowledge base. To enable the ChatGPT to provide accurate responses, you need to create a comprehensive knowledge base. This database should include product information, service policies, and frequently asked questions. You can also pre-program responses to common inquiries to speed up response times.
  3. Train your team. Even though the ChatGPT is automated, your team still needs to be trained on how to manage the channel. This includes monitoring the channel for accuracy, escalating inquiries when necessary, and analyzing the data to improve the ChatGPT's performance.

These are just a few examples of how to create effective ChatGPT policies. By following these tips, you can optimize the channel for both your team and your customers.
