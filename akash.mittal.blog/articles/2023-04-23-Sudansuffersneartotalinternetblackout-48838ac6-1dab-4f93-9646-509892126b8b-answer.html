Sudan Suffers From 'Near-Total' Internet Blackout

  • About
  • Examples
  • Conclusion

What Happened?

Sudan recently experienced a near-total internet blackout, leaving many in the country without access to the web. The blackout occurred after the government shut down access to the internet, reportedly to quell unrest in the country.

Examples of the Impact

The impact of the blackout has been felt across the country. Online banking, e-commerce and social media platforms have been affected. For example, many Sudanese who rely on electronic banking are unable to pay bills or access their accounts. NGOs and media outlets have also been affected, as they rely heavily on the internet to communicate with their audiences.

Conclusion

  • The Sudanese internet blackout has caused distress to many in the country and highlights the importance of internet freedom and access around the world.
  • The impact of the blackout has affected industries ranging from banking to media, showing the widespread nature of the effects of shutting down access to the web.
  • It is important for governments to respect internet freedom and ensure that their citizens have access to the web. The Sudanese government's actions serve as a reminder of the importance of defending internet freedom and access worldwide.
