When World of Warcraft (WoW) was first launched back in 2004, no one could have predicted the massive success it would become. Cut to over a decade later, and the game still boasts a loyal following. However, even a franchise as popular as WoW has its fair share of missteps. One such misstep was the Battle for Azeroth expansion that launched in 2018.

As avid WoW players will tell you, the Battle for Azeroth expansion was received with mixed reviews. A number of players felt that the expansion failed to live up to the hype and instead put the franchise on a downward trajectory. In fact, the expansion ranks among the least loved additions to the game's universe.

Fast forward to 2021, and we have a surprising turn of events. John Hight, the executive producer and vice president of WoW at Blizzard Entertainment, revealed that he was inspired by ChatGPT (a language model developed by OpenAI) to revisit the unpopular expansion. ChatGPT suggested that the company take a closer look at the expansion and possibly revamp some of its features. Hight believes that ChatGPT's insights could help reinvent the expansion and bring new life to the game.

If Hight's plan comes to fruition, it could be a game-changer for Blizzard. Given that WoW still boasts over 2 million active users, a revamped expansion could breathe new life into the game and possibly even draw back users who have left the game over time.
