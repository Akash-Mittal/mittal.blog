Family Friendly Dining in Boston: Your A to Z Guide

When I first moved to Boston with my young family, we were eager to explore all the great restaurants the city had to offer. But as we quickly realized, not all restaurants are created equal when it comes to accommodating kids. We had our fair share of tantrums and awkward moments before discovering which places were truly family-friendly. And that's why I've compiled this guide - to help other families navigate the Boston dining scene with ease.

A is for Anna's Taqueria. This local chain is a favorite among families for its delicious, affordable Mexican fare. Kids love being able to customize their own meals, and parents love the fast service and convenient locations.

B is for Boston Pizza Tours. This unique experience takes families on a tour of the city's best pizzerias, offering a slice of history along the way. It's a great way to try multiple spots without having to commit to a full meal at each one.

C is for Cafe Fleuri. This elegant restaurant inside the Langham Hotel offers an indulgent chocolate buffet on Saturdays, complete with a chocolate waterfall and endless desserts. It's the ultimate treat for kids and adults alike.

D is for Dylan's Candy Bar. While technically not a restaurant, this Back Bay candy store is a must-visit for families. With three floors of candy heaven, it's hard not to leave with a sugar high.

E is for Earl of Sandwich. This fast-casual chain is a great option for families on the go. Their menu of sandwiches, salads, and soups is sure to please everyone, and their convenient location in the Prudential Center makes it the perfect spot to grab a quick bite while shopping.

F is for Flatbread Company. This fun and festive pizza spot in Davis Square offers organic, wood-fired pizza and a lively atmosphere. Kids will love watching their pizzas cook in the open kitchen, and parents will appreciate the commitment to local and sustainable ingredients.

G is for Grendel's Den. This Harvard Square staple is a go-to for families looking for affordable, hearty meals in a cozy setting. Their kids' menu is extensive and offers everything from quesadillas to spaghetti and meatballs.

H is for Henrietta's Table. This Cambridge restaurant offers a farm-to-table experience in a relaxed, kid-friendly atmosphere. Their brunch buffet is a standout, with options for all dietary needs and a play area for kids.

I is for Island Creek Oyster Bar. While oysters may not be the first thing that comes to mind for a family-friendly meal, this Kenmore Square seafood spot truly shines when it comes to accommodating kids. Their expansive menu offers plenty of options beyond seafood, and their welcoming staff make families feel right at home.

J is for JP Licks. This beloved Boston ice cream chain has been a staple for families for years. With locations all over the city, it's the perfect spot for a sweet treat after dinner or a mid-day snack.

K is for Kupel's Bakery. This Brookline bakery is a must-visit for families with a sweet tooth. Their menu of Jewish pastries and sweets is unrivaled in the area, and their cozy atmosphere makes it the perfect spot to spend a lazy afternoon.

L is for Legal Sea Foods. This longtime local chain is a reliable option for families looking for fresh seafood in a casual setting. Their kids' menu offers classic favorites like fish and chips and mac and cheese, and their commitment to sustainability and responsible sourcing is admirable.

M is for MET Back Bay. This upscale Back Bay eatery offers a kids' cooking class on select weekends, giving young ones the chance to learn from the restaurant's chefs. It's a unique and educational experience that's sure to inspire a love of cooking.

N is for Neptune Oyster. While this North End seafood spot can get crowded and noisy, it's worth braving the crowds for their legendary lobster rolls. Kids will love the decadent sandwiches and the bustling atmosphere, and parents will appreciate the upscale ambiance.

O is for Otto Pizza. This popular pizza chain has multiple locations around the city, making it an easy choice for families on the go. Their creative toppings and thin, crispy crusts are a hit with kids and adults alike.

P is for Paddle Boston. While not a restaurant, this spot on the Charles River is a great way to make a day of dining out with the family. Rent a kayak or canoe and paddle down the river, then enjoy a picnic lunch on the banks or at one of the nearby restaurants.

Q is for Qdoba. Another fast-casual chain that's great for families, Qdoba's menu of customizable Mexican food is a hit with kids. Their convenient locations and fast service make it the perfect spot for a quick lunch or dinner.

R is for Raising Cane's. This beloved chain recently opened its first Boston location, and it's already a hit with families. Their menu of chicken fingers and sides is simple but delicious, and their welcoming staff and playful decor make it a fun place to eat.

S is for Scampo. This upscale Italian eatery in the Liberty Hotel offers a kids' cooking class on select Saturdays, giving young ones the chance to learn from Executive Chef Lydia Shire. It's a unique and educational experience that's sure to inspire a love of cooking.

T is for Tasty Burger. This popular burger chain has locations all over the city, making it an easy and affordable option for families. Their classic burgers and fries are a hit with kids, and their local beer selection is a plus for parents.

U is for Union Oyster House. This historic restaurant in the heart of Faneuil Hall is a must-visit for families. With a menu that ranges from classic New England seafood to burgers and pizza, there's something for everyone. Kids will love the old-timey atmosphere and the chance to see the oldest continuously operating restaurant in the country.

V is for Volo Coffeehouse. While not technically a restaurant, this South End cafe is a great spot for families looking for a cozy place to hang out. With board games, toys, and plenty of seating, it's the perfect place to grab a coffee and spend a lazy morning or afternoon.

W is for Wagamama. This Asian-inspired chain has a location in the Seaport district, and it's a hit with families. Their kids' menu offers options like chicken katsu and noodles, and their communal seating makes it a fun and interactive experience for kids.

X is for Xo Cafe. This South End spot offers a delicious brunch buffet on weekends, complete with a make-your-own Bloody Mary bar. It's a great option for families looking to indulge in a leisurely meal on the weekend.

Y is for Yume Wo Katare. This ramen spot in Cambridge is not your typical family-friendly restaurant, but it's an experience not to be missed. The restaurant only serves one type of ramen, and diners are encouraged to share their life goals with the staff after finishing their meal. It's a unique and memorable experience that's sure to be a hit with adventurous families.

Z is for Zaftigs Delicatessen. This Brookline staple is a go-to for families looking for classic Jewish deli fare. Their menu of matzo ball soup, pastrami sandwiches, and knishes is unbeatable, and their spacious seating makes it easy to accommodate larger groups.

Conclusion:

In summary, Boston is a great city for families looking for dining options that cater to both kids and adults. Whether you're in the mood for pizza, seafood, or international cuisine, there's a spot in Boston that can accommodate your family's needs. From brunch buffets to cooking classes, there are plenty of unique and memorable experiences to be had. So don't be afraid to explore the city's diverse dining scene with your little ones in tow.

Some additional tips for dining out with kids:

- Look for restaurants with kids' menus or customizable options so that everyone can find something they like.

- Be mindful of noise level when choosing a restaurant - some places may be too quiet or too loud for young kids.

- If your child has a special dietary need, call ahead to ensure that the restaurant can accommodate them.

- Bring some small toys or activities to keep kids entertained while waiting for food.

- Don't be afraid to ask for modifications or substitutions - most restaurants are happy to accommodate reasonable requests.
