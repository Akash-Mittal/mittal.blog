India to Host SCO Summit in Virtual Format in July

India is all set to host the Shanghai Cooperation Organization (SCO) summit on July 13 this year, for the first time since it joined the organization as a full-fledged member in 2017. The summit will be held in a virtual format keeping in view the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic.

SCO is a powerful regional security organization that brings together Russia, China, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, India, and Pakistan. It has been actively working towards promoting regional cooperation, mutual trust, and coordination among its members.

The summit holds great significance for India as it provides an opportunity for the country to engage with the neighboring countries in the region, especially with China, with whom it has been facing border tensions in recent times.

India's hosting of the SCO summit comes at a time when the world is fighting the coronavirus pandemic, which has disrupted the normal functioning of countries across the globe. Against this backdrop, India's decision to hold the summit in a virtual format is a remarkable feat, as it offers a safe and secure platform for leaders from the SCO member countries to deliberate upon issues of mutual interest.

Quantifiable Examples

The SCO is one of the largest regional security organizations in the world, covering an area of over 30 million square kilometers and accounting for 60% of the Eurasian landmass. Its members represent almost half of the world's population and 22% of the world's GDP.

The organization has been instrumental in promoting regional connectivity, trade, and investment among its member countries. In 2020, despite the pandemic, trade among the SCO member countries grew by 8.8% to over $200 billion.

The SCO has also played a key role in maintaining regional peace and security. It has helped to resolve border disputes, promote peaceful co-existence among its members, and combat terrorism, separatism, and extremism in the region.
