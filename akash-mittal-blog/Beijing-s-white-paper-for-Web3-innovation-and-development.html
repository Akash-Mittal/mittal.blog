How Beijing is paving the way for web3 innovation and development

Once upon a time, in the bustling city of Beijing, a group of tech enthusiasts decided to embark on a mission to change the world. They believed that the current internet infrastructure was insufficient and outdated, and that it was time for a new era of decentralized, user-centric web solutions. That's how they began to explore the uncharted territory of web3.

Web3 is a term used to describe a new paradigm of internet applications that are decentralized, blockchain-based, and user-centric. This means that instead of relying on centralized servers, web3 applications use peer-to-peer networks, smart contracts and distributed ledgers to operate in a more open, transparent and secure manner.

Thanks to the efforts of these Beijing pioneers, and the support of the local government, web3 technology is now rapidly advancing in China. Recently, the Beijing Municipal Bureau of Economy and Information Technology has released a white paper outlining its vision and strategy for the development of web3 technology in the city.

Examples of web3 innovation in Beijing

So, what are some quantifiable examples of web3 innovation in Beijing? Here are a few:

  • Neo: A blockchain-based platform that aims to create a smart economy by digitizing assets and automating the management of digital identity, digital assets and smart contracts.
  • Ontology: A decentralized identity verification platform that allows users to securely manage their identity and personal data.
  • Wanchain: A cross-chain platform that enables interoperability between different blockchain networks, allowing users to exchange assets between chains in a decentralized and secure manner.
  • Qtum: A blockchain platform that combines the best of both Bitcoin and Ethereum, with a focus on enterprise adoption and smart contract execution.

These are just a few examples of the vibrant web3 ecosystem that's emerging in Beijing. In fact, according to the white paper, there are over 1,000 web3 companies currently operating in the city, with a total market value of over 300 billion yuan (about 45 billion US dollars).

Beijing's strategy for web3 development

So, how is the Beijing government planning to support and foster this growing web3 ecosystem? Here are the key points from the white paper:

1. Creating a favorable policy environment

The Beijing government plans to create a favorable policy environment for web3 development, with a focus on fostering innovation, attracting talent, and providing support for entrepreneurs. This includes supporting the development of blockchain-based applications in key areas such as finance, logistics, healthcare, and education.

2. Building a collaborative, open ecosystem

The government will also work to build a collaborative, open ecosystem for web3 development, with a focus on promoting cooperation between different stakeholders in the industry, such as businesses, research institutions, and investors. The goal is to create a more interconnected, interoperable web3 ecosystem that can drive innovation and growth.

3. Promoting international cooperation and exchange

Finally, the government plans to promote international cooperation and exchange in web3 development, with a focus on fostering partnerships with other regions and countries that are also exploring web3 technology. This includes hosting international events and conferences to showcase Beijing's web3 ecosystem and attract global talent and investment.

Conclusion

Beijing is quickly establishing itself as a major hub for web3 innovation and development, with a vibrant ecosystem of startups, investors, and research institutions all focused on creating a more decentralized, user-centric internet. By fostering a favorable policy environment, building a collaborative ecosystem, and promoting international exchange, the Beijing government is aiming to create a leading global center for web3 technology.
