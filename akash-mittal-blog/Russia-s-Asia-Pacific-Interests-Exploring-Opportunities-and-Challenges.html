The Roadmap to Russia's Asia Pacific Interests

In the past few years, Russia has been actively expanding its presence and influence in the Asia Pacific region. One interesting story that captures Russia's move eastward was the opening of a new energy bridge between Russia and China in December 2020, which marked a significant milestone in their energy partnership. The project, which involved the construction of a cross-border transmission line and a substation, will enable Russia to export electricity to China, which is one of the world's largest energy consumers.

The energy bridge is just one of the many initiatives that Russia has undertaken to strengthen its ties with Asia Pacific countries, including China, Japan, India, and South Korea. As the world's largest country in terms of territory, Russia is uniquely positioned to play a significant role in the region, which is home to more than half of the world's population and some of the world's fastest-growing economies.

  • Russia is the largest supplier of energy to China, accounting for about 15% of China's crude oil imports and 10% of its natural gas imports.
  • Russia is a member of the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) forum, which brings together 21 member economies to promote free trade and investment in the region.
  • The two-way trade between Russia and the Asia Pacific region has been growing steadily, reaching $198 billion in 2019, up from $115 billion in 2010.
  • Russia's share of the Asia Pacific region's total exports has been increasing, rising from 5.5% in 2010 to 7.5% in 2019.

These examples highlight Russia's growing economic and geopolitical interests in the Asia Pacific region, which offer both opportunities and challenges for Russia.

Three Key Points

  1. Russia's Asia Pacific interests are driven by its desire to diversify its economy, reduce its dependence on the West, and increase its strategic influence in the region.
  2. Russia faces several challenges in pursuing its Asia Pacific agenda, including competition from other powers such as China and the United States, geopolitical tensions, and regulatory barriers.
  3. To succeed in the Asia Pacific region, Russia needs to adopt a pragmatic and flexible approach that combines economic, political, and cultural engagement with a clear-eyed assessment of the region's realities and challenges.

In conclusion, Russia's move eastward represents a significant shift in its foreign policy and strategic priorities. While there are many opportunities for Russia in the Asia Pacific region, there are also significant challenges and risks that need to be carefully navigated. By adopting a strategic and adaptive approach, Russia can enhance its economic, political, and security interests in the region and contribute to a more stable and prosperous Asia Pacific community.
