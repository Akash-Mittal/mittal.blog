It was a typical day on Wall Street when two traders walked into a bar. As they sat sipping their drinks, one trader remarked, "I'm thinking of investing in auto stocks this month." The other trader scoffed, "Why bother? The auto industry is in shambles."

But the first trader was undeterred, and he had good reason to be. As it turns out, there are three auto stocks that could continue to rise in June, and investors would be wise to take note.

The three auto stocks in question are Ford Motor Company (F), General Motors Company (GM), and Tesla, Inc. (TSLA). According to recent data, these stocks are undervalued considering their low P/E valuations, and positive earnings estimate revisions have started to reconfirm this fact.

Take Ford, for example. The company has a P/E ratio of just 6.13, compared to the industry average of 14.46, and its earnings estimate for the current year has risen by 15.4% over the past 30 days.

General Motors also boasts a low P/E of 5.97, and its earnings estimate for the current year has risen by 7.9% over the past 30 days.

And then there's Tesla, which has a higher P/E of 73.14, but its earnings estimate for the current year has risen by a whopping 61.8% over the past 30 days.
