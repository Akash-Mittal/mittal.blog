The Future of AI: Two AI-Related Bills Introduced in US Congress

The future is now, and artificial intelligence (AI) has become one of the most significant technological advances in the 21st century. Every day, new AI technologies are developed, and with their development comes many complex issues that need to be addressed. One of these issues is the regulation and compliance standards of AI-based tools.

The Introduction of Two AI-Related Bills in US Congress

In response to the need for regulation and compliance standards in AI-based tools, two US lawmakers have introduced AI-related bills in Congress. These bills aim to establish regulatory standards and compliance guidelines for the use of AI-based tools in the US.

The first bill, the Algorithmic Accountability Act, aims to address the potential bias and discrimination that may arise from the use of automated decision-making algorithms. The bill requires companies to conduct impact assessments of their algorithms' potential bias and implement measures to minimize that bias.

  • Quantifiable Example 1: In 2018, Amazon scrapped its AI recruiting tool after it was found to be biased against women, reflecting a wider concern that machine learning tools may perpetuate discrimination and entrench stereotypes in the workforce.
  • Quantifiable Example 2: Studies have revealed racial biases in facial recognition technology, leading to false arrests and wrongful convictions of people of color. In a recent case in Michigan, a Black man was arrested based on a false facial recognition match and wrongfully accused of theft.

The second bill, the National AI Research Resource Task Force Act, aims to establish a task force to develop a national research cloud for AI. The bill proposes the creation of a task force with representatives from industry, academia, and government to develop a roadmap for the research cloud, including its technical requirements, governance structures, and funding options.

  • Quantifiable Example 1: The research cloud for AI would provide researchers with faster access to data and computing resources, accelerating the development of new and innovative AI-based technologies.
  • Quantifiable Example 2: The task force's creation could lead to the development of new and innovative AI-based technologies, such as more effective cancer treatment plans and improved climate change models.

The Significance of These Bills

The introduction of these two AI-related bills marks an important step towards regulating the use of AI-based tools in the US. The bills address the potential biases and ethical considerations of AI, while also promoting the development of new and innovative technologies.

  • Conclusion Point 1: The Algorithmic Accountability Act aims to prevent algorithmic bias and promote fairness in AI-based decision-making.
  • Conclusion Point 2: The National AI Research Resource Task Force Act aims to promote the development of new and innovative AI-based technologies through the creation of a national research cloud.
  • Conclusion Point 3: These bills are part of a wider effort by US lawmakers to establish regulatory and compliance standards for the use of AI-based tools.

As we move closer to a world in which AI plays an increasingly significant role in our lives, it is essential that we address the potential biases and ethical considerations of these technologies. The introduction of these two AI-related bills is an important step towards achieving these goals and promoting the development of responsible and innovative AI-based technologies.
