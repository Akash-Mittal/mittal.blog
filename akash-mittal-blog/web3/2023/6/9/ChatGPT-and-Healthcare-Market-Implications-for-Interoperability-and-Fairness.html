Have you ever been to a doctor's appointment where you wished you had someone to talk to? Someone who fully understands your medical history and can provide you with personalized healthcare advice based on your specific needs? You might be thinking, "Wouldn't it be amazing if ChatGPT could be that someone"? Unfortunately, ChatGPT's implementation in the healthcare market has a number of implications, including the possibility of hindering interoperability and ultimately leading to a two-tier healthcare system. Let's explore this further.

Implications of ChatGPT for Interoperability

In its current state, ChatGPT has shown a lot of promise in enabling conversational experiences with other chatbots and AI systems. However, the lack of interoperability among these systems is a serious hurdle when it comes to healthcare. Let's say, for instance, that ChatGPT and a patient-focused healthcare startup combine forces to develop a chatbot to aid patients during their doctor's visits. In such a scenario, an issue of interoperability arises due to the fact that patients have to answer the same questions every time they visit the doctor, even if they saw them recently. As a result, healthcare providers will be less willing to open up their data to third-party entities without a secure and trusted way to share the data.

Furthermore, even if ChatGPT can amass an immense amount of data on each patient, that information can only be viewed by the chatbot itself. So even if you visit different doctors, who might have different electronic medical records (EMRs) systems, the ChatGPT chatbot wouldn't be able to communicate with these systems, rendering it impossible to access important diagnostic history from unrelated doctors. ChatGPT developers can use industry-wide approaches to address the interoperability sharing issue, such as using open source APIs, developing blockchain-based records sharing applications, and working with electronic health record companies.

Implications of ChatGPT for the Two-Tier Healthcare System

If ChatGPT is integrated into the existing healthcare system, it could potentially lead to a two-tier healthcare system, which is a segregated healthcare system in which some receive high-quality healthcare and others receive substandard healthcare. Patients that could afford better healthcare could purchase premium care plans backed by ChatGPT services, while low-income patients who rely solely on the public healthcare system would not have access to ChatGPT, leading to a lack of equitable access to healthcare. This disparity could further create financial and socioeconomic divides, thus affecting the wellbeing of patients.

In order to ensure equitable access to healthcare, healthcare industry stakeholders must collaborate with ChatGPT chatbot providers to guarantee that the AI services can be accessible to all people, regardless of their financial circumstances. This could avoid any financial barrier or discrimination of the substandard care to low-income people or communities.

Conclusion

  • ChatGPT has a lot of promise as a healthcare assistant for patients, personalized medicine delivery, and disease prevention advancement. However, a lack of interoperability among healthcare systems is a significant hurdle that must be resolved.
  • Due to ChatGPT's potential to create a two-tier healthcare system, health care providers must focus on inclusive healthcare services that can benefit everyone, regardless of their financial standing or socioeconomic status.
  • Stakeholders must, therefore, work together to guarantee access to ChatGPT's services to everybody to maintain a standardized healthcare system.
