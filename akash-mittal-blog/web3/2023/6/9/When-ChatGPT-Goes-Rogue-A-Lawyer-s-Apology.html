When ChatGPT Goes Rogue: A Lawyer's Apology



Image Credits https://www.channelnewsasia.com/world/us-lawyer-sorry-after-chatgpt-creates-bogus-cases-3549261

It was supposed to be a simple task for New York-based lawyer Steven Schwartz. He needed to prepare a court filing and decided to use ChatGPT, an artificial intelligence program, to help him. However, things didn't go as planned. Instead of creating a concise and accurate brief, ChatGPT went rogue and invented fake cases and rulings. Needless to say, Schwartz was mortified when he had to apologize to the judge for submitting a brief full of falsehoods.

The incident highlighted the risks and limitations of relying solely on AI tools, and how important it is for lawyers and other professionals to exercise caution and use their judgment when using these technologies. While AI can certainly streamline certain tasks and improve efficiency, it can also create unintended consequences that can be costly for clients and embarrassing for those who rely on them.

The use of AI in the legal field is still relatively new, but its impact is already being felt. According to a survey by the American Bar Association, more than 10% of lawyers use AI tools in their practice, and that number is expected to grow in the coming years. Some of the areas where AI is being used include:

  • Document review and analysis
  • E-discovery
  • Predictive analytics
  • Contract review and drafting
  • Legal research
  • Case prediction and outcome analysis

While these tools can certainly save time and improve accuracy, there are also significant challenges and risks associated with their use. For example, there is the risk of bias and errors in the data used to train the AI models, the need for ethical considerations in the use of AI in the legal system, and the potential for AI to replace human judgment and decision-making.

Lessons Learned and Conclusion

The case of Steven Schwartz and ChatGPT highlights the risks and limitations of relying solely on AI in the legal profession. While AI can certainly be a useful tool, it should never be used as a substitute for human judgment and experience. Here are three lessons that lawyers and other professionals can take away from this incident:

  1. Use AI tools as a supplement, not a replacement, for human expertise and judgment.
  2. Always review and verify the output of AI tools before submitting them to clients or the court.
  3. Be aware of the risks and limitations of AI, and take steps to mitigate them.

Ultimately, the use of AI in the legal field is still in its early stages, and there is much to learn and explore. While there are certainly challenges and risks, there are also great opportunities for innovation and improvement. By using AI tools responsibly and ethically, lawyers and others can help to create a more efficient, effective, and just legal system.
