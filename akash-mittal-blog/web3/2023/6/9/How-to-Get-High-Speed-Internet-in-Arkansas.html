How to Get High-Speed Internet in Arkansas



Image Credits https://www.mysaline.com/internet-services/

Many Arkansans struggle with accessing high-speed Internet in their homes and communities. Without reliable and affordable broadband, it can be difficult to work remotely, take online classes, or simply stay connected with loved ones. However, there are several ways you can obtain broadband Internet in Arkansas, and even save some money while doing so.

An FBI Warning for Spanish-Speaking Arkansans

Before diving into the ways of getting broadbands, we must first acknowledge the potential scams that can happen. Recently, the FBI has issued a warning to the Spanish-speaking community in Arkansas to be wary of virtual kidnapping schemes. The scammers may pretend they have kidnapped a family member or loved one and demand a ransom. They may also ask for personal information or demand payment for supposed debts. To avoid falling prey to these scams, it's important to verify the whereabouts of loved ones and never give out personal information or payment over the phone.

Verify Broadband Availability in Your Area

The first step in getting broadband Internet is to determine if it's available in your area. The Federal Broadband Map is a resource that allows you to enter your address and see which broadband providers are available. This map also shows areas that lack access to broadband and may be eligible for federal funding to help bring broadband to those communities.

Explore Federal Programs for High-Speed Internet

The federal government has several programs to assist individuals and communities with obtaining high-speed Internet. For example, the FCC's Lifeline program offers monthly discounts on high-speed Internet for eligible households. To qualify for this program, you must have an income at or below 135% of the federal poverty guidelines, or participate in certain federal assistance programs such as SNAP or Medicaid.

Another federal program aimed at expanding broadband in rural areas is the USDA Rural Development Broadband ReConnect Program. This program provides loans and grants to service providers to build broadband infrastructure in rural areas. Arkansas was recently awarded $5.8 million through this program to upgrade its rural broadband access. This funding will help provide high-speed Internet to over 1,000 households, businesses, and farms in the state.

Access the Federal Stimulus Money for Broadband

The most recent federal stimulus package also includes $47.5 million for Arkansas to increase access to affordable broadband. This funding will be used to expand broadband infrastructure, upgrade networks, and support digital inclusion and adoption efforts in low-income and rural areas. This funding will enable even more Arkansans to access high-speed Internet at affordable prices.

Conclusion

  1. Verify your address on the Federal Broadband Map to determine which broadband services are available in your area.
  2. Explore federal programs such as Lifeline and the USDA Rural Development Broadband ReConnect Program for discounts and funding opportunities.
  3. With the recent federal stimulus money for broadband, even more Arkansas residents will soon be able to access high-speed Internet at affordable rates.

By taking advantage of these available resources, you can get high-speed Internet in your home, save on monthly bills, and even help bring broadband to your entire community. Don't be left behind in the digital age, and make sure to do your research on how you can take advantage of these resources.
