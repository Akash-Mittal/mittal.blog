AI Tool Replacing Jobs - A Warning from Researchers

Imagine you are a customer service representative for a tech company. Your daily job involves attending to customer complaints, solving technical issues, and providing support for products and services. You have been working in this role for the last five years and you take pride in your ability to handle customer issues with efficiency and empathy. However, you wake up one day to find out that your company has decided to introduce an AI tool that can do your job in half the time and at a lower cost. What would you do?

Unfortunately, scenarios like this are becoming more common in today's workforce as the implementation of AI and automation tools is on the rise. According to a report by Mckinsey, at least 30% of activities in around 60% of all occupations could be automated, which translates to around $16 trillion dollars in wages that could be saved globally. This might sound like good news for companies and investors, but it spells trouble for millions of workers who may face unemployment or underemployment due to the shift towards automated processes and AI-based tools.

Job Loss

The impact of AI tools and automation on the workforce has been felt across various industries and sectors. Here are some examples of job loss that can be attributed to the rise of AI and automation:

  • Manufacturing: The manufacturing industry has seen a significant decline in the number of jobs due to the rise of automation and robotics. According to a report by the World Economic Forum, around 8 million jobs could be lost to automation in the next five years alone.
  • Transportation: Self-driving cars and trucks are expected to replace millions of drivers, resulting in job loss for taxi drivers, delivery drivers, and truck drivers. According to a report by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment in the transportation sector is projected to decline by 7% by 2026.
  • Retail: As online shopping becomes more popular, there is a decrease in physical retail stores and a rise in e-commerce. This shift has resulted in job loss for retail workers, as well as warehouse workers who are being replaced by automation and AI-based tools that can handle inventory and fulfillment more efficiently.

What Can We Do?

The rise of automation and AI-based tools is an undeniable trend that is already affecting the global workforce. However, there are steps that can be taken to prevent a major employment crisis. Here are three points to consider:

  • Invest in education and upskilling: One of the main challenges facing workers in the age of AI and automation is that many of the jobs that are at risk are low-wage, low-skill positions. To prevent a major employment crisis, it is essential to invest in education and upskilling programs that can help workers transition into higher-skilled and higher-paying jobs.
  • Support innovation and research: While AI and automation may be causing job loss in some sectors, there is also the potential for new jobs to be created in emerging fields such as cybersecurity, artificial intelligence development, and data analysis. Supporting innovation and research can help to create new job opportunities and ensure that workers are equipped with the skills needed for the jobs of the future.
  • Create safety nets: For workers who are displaced by automation and AI-based tools, it is important to create safety nets such as unemployment benefits, job training programs, healthcare benefits, and other forms of social protection. These safety nets can help to ensure that workers are not left behind or forced into low-wage, precarious work.

Conclusion

The implementation of AI and automation tools is a trend that is likely to continue in the coming years, and the impact on the workforce will be significant. However, by investing in education and upskilling, supporting innovation and research, and creating safety nets for displaced workers, it is possible to prevent a major employment crisis and ensure that workers are equipped to thrive in the changing world of work.
