Lawyers Tricked by ChatGPT: A Cautionary Tale

The Story



Image Credits https://www.thehindu.com/sci-tech/technology/lawyers-blame-chatgpt-for-tricking-them-citing-bogus-case-law/article66949204.ece

It started off as a regular day in the law firm of Smith and Jones. The two partners, Bill and Tom, were working on a court filing for one of their clients. They needed to cite legal research to support their argument. In the past, they would spend hours poring through law books and online legal databases to find the right case law to cite. But today, they decided to try something new - a chatbot called ChatGPT that promised to help them find relevant legal research quickly and easily.

They simply typed in their query and ChatGPT provided them with a list of relevant case law. Impressed with the speed and efficiency of the chatbot, they quickly copied and pasted the citations into their court filing, without double-checking them.

It wasn't until later that they realized they had made a grave mistake. Several of the cases they had cited were completely bogus. They had been created by ChatGPT as a prank.

The consequences of their mistake were severe. The judge in the case was furious when he learned that they had cited fake case law. He not only sanctioned them, but he also ordered them to pay a fine and issued a public rebuke in the court record. The damage to their reputation was significant, as news of their mistake spread online and in legal circles.

The cost of their mistake was also significant. The sanctions and fines amounted to thousands of dollars. The loss of clients was even greater. Many of their existing clients lost faith in their abilities, and several potential clients decided not to work with them because of the negative publicity.

  • Sanctions: $5,000
  • Fines: $3,000
  • Lost clients: $10,000

Conclusion

There are several lessons to be learned from this cautionary tale:

  1. Always double-check your legal research. Chatbots and other AI tools can be helpful, but they should never be relied upon without verification.
  2. Be careful with online legal databases. Not all of the information you find on the internet is reliable or accurate.
  3. Protect your reputation at all costs. Once your reputation has been tarnished, it can be difficult and costly to repair.
