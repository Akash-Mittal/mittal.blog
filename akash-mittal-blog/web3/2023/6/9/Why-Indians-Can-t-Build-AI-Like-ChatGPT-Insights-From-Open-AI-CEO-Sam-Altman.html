Why Indians Can't Build AI Like ChatGPT: Insights From Open AI CEO Sam Altman



Image Credits https://www.indiatoday.in/technology/news/story/open-ai-ceo-sam-altman-tells-indians-you-can-not-build-ai-like-chatgpt-2390798-2023-06-09

It was a sunny day in Mumbai when Open AI CEO Sam Altman arrived to give a talk on the future of artificial intelligence. The audience eagerly listened as he discussed the latest advancements in technology, but it was his comments about India's ability to build AI that really got people talking.

Altman argued that while India has a thriving tech industry and talented engineers, there are several factors that make building AI like ChatGPT a difficult task for Indians. His comments sparked a heated debate online, with some praising him for speaking the truth and others accusing him of being dismissive of Indian talent.

So, is Altman right? Let's take a closer look at some of the reasons why India might struggle to build AI like ChatGPT.

One of the main challenges facing India in the field of AI is access to data. ChatGPT was trained on a massive dataset of over 40GB of text, which included everything from news articles to social media posts to books. India, however, ranks low in terms of data availability, with only around 22% of its population having access to the internet.

Another challenge is the lack of access to computing power. Building AI models like ChatGPT requires a significant amount of computing power, which can be prohibitively expensive for many Indian startups and researchers. In contrast, companies like Google and Amazon have access to huge data centers and can leverage their economies of scale to build and train massive models like ChatGPT.

Finally, there is the issue of talent. While India does have many talented engineers, the competition for talent is fierce, and many of the best and brightest are often recruited to work for tech giants like Google and Facebook. This can leave smaller startups and research groups struggling to find the talent they need to compete.

Practical Tips

Despite these challenges, there are things that Indian startups and researchers can do to build AI models that are competitive with ChatGPT. One strategy is to leverage transfer learning, where existing pre-trained models are fine-tuned on smaller, domain-specific datasets. This can significantly reduce the amount of data and computing power required, while still producing high-quality results.

Another strategy is to focus on building AI models that are tailored to the Indian market. By understanding the unique challenges and opportunities facing India, startups can build models that are optimized for local needs and can more easily compete with global players.

Finally, it's important for Indian startups and researchers to collaborate and share resources. By pooling their data, computing power, and talent, they can work together to build AI models that are more competitive with those developed by global tech giants.

Conclusion

  1. While India faces significant challenges in building AI models like ChatGPT, there are strategies that can be employed to overcome these challenges.
  2. By leveraging transfer learning, focusing on local needs, and collaborating with others, Indian startups and researchers can build AI models that are competitive with those developed by global tech giants.
  3. It's important to recognize that building world-class AI models is a difficult task that requires significant resources and talent, and that it's okay to start small and build gradually over time.
