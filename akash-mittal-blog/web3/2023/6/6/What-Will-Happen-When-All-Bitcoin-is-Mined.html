What Will Happen When All Bitcoin is Mined?

Bitcoin was created in 2009 by an anonymous person or group using the name Satoshi Nakamoto. The idea behind Bitcoin was to create a decentralized digital asset that could be used as a secure means of payment without the need for intermediaries such as banks or governments.

The process of creating new Bitcoins through mining involves several steps that require sophisticated computers and a lot of energy. As time goes by, the mining difficulty increases, making it harder and harder for miners to generate new Bitcoins.

Now, more than a decade later, over 18.7 million Bitcoins have been mined, and there are only 2.3 million left to be mined. This has led many people to wonder what will happen when all Bitcoins have been mined.

One of the most significant effects of Bitcoin mining is the energy usage. According to the Cambridge Bitcoin Electricity Consumption Index, Bitcoin mining now consumes more than 110 Terawatt-hours (TWh) of electricity per year, which is more than the electricity consumption of countries such as Argentina or Norway.

Another effect is that as the mining difficulty increases, the computing power required to mine Bitcoin also increases. This means that only those who can afford to invest in expensive mining equipment will be able to generate new Bitcoins, leading to centralization of the network into the hands of a few.
