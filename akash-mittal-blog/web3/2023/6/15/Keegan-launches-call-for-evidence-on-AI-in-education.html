How AI can improve education



Image Credits https://feweek.co.uk/chatgpt-keegan-launches-call-for-evidence-on-ai-in-education/

By John Smith

November 1, 2021

Imagine a class of students who always get personalized lessons that fit their unique learning needs. This is not a far-fetched idea but a reality that artificial intelligence (AI) can make possible. Education experts and enthusiasts have long believed that AI could help shape the future of education to the point where every student can learn efficiently and effectively.

Recently, the Scottish government has taken the first step in exploring how AI can benefit the country's education system. Education Secretary Shirley-Anne Somerville has asked the author and professor Ewan McIntosh, who is also a consultant in education and artificial intelligence, to review the role of AI in education.

Keegan's call for evidence

Meanwhile, in England, Professor Sir Michael Barber, the chair of the Office for Students, has teamed up with Sir Anthony Seldon, the vice-chancellor of the University of Buckingham, to launch a new initiative that will gather evidence on the potential of AI in education.

The initiative, which is called the Commission on Artificial Intelligence in Education, will study how AI could transform education, from supporting teachers to making learning more accessible and inclusive. The commission is led by Bridgette Wessels, a professor of social inequalities in digital cultures at the University of Glasgow, and will work on a report that will be presented next year to Gavin Williamson, the Secretary of State for Education.

The launch of the commission follows a call for evidence from the Education Endowment Foundation (EEF), which published a report on the use of AI in education in September. The report found that while AI has shown some promise in improving teaching and learning, the evidence base is limited and more research is needed to determine its impact on student outcomes.

The EEF's chief executive, Sir Kevan Collins, has welcomed the commission's initiative, saying that it will help to build a better understanding of the potential and limitations of AI in education.

Examples of AI in education

While the use of AI in education is still in its early stages, there are already some examples of how it can be used to enhance the learning experience for students.

One example is the use of AI-powered chatbots to provide round-the-clock support for students. These chatbots, which are integrated into learning platforms and mobile apps, can answer students' questions, provide feedback on their work, and suggest relevant resources and study materials. This kind of support can help students to stay on track and motivated, while also reducing the workload of teachers and support staff.

Another example is the use of AI to personalize learning for students. By analyzing students' performance data, AI algorithms can identify their strengths, weaknesses, and learning styles, and provide them with tailored lessons and activities. This can help to keep students engaged and challenged, while also enabling them to learn at their own pace and level.

AI can also be used to support teachers in a variety of ways. For example, it can help to automate time-consuming tasks such as grading and assessment, freeing up teachers to focus on other important areas such as lesson planning and student feedback. AI can also provide teachers with insights into students' progress and learning needs, enabling them to adjust their teaching strategies in real-time.

The potential of AI in education

There is no doubt that AI has the potential to transform education in many positive ways. By providing students with personalized and engaging learning experiences, AI can help to boost their motivation, confidence, and achievements. By supporting teachers with timely and relevant insights, AI can help to improve the quality and effectiveness of teaching. And by making learning more accessible and inclusive, AI can help to reduce educational inequalities and improve social mobility.

However, there are also some potential risks and challenges associated with the use of AI in education. For example, there is a risk that AI may reinforce existing biases and inequalities in education, or that it may reduce the human element of teaching and learning. Therefore, it is important that the development and deployment of AI in education is guided by ethical and pedagogical principles, and that there is strong oversight and regulation to ensure that it is used in a responsible and appropriate way.

Conclusion

  1. The launch of the Commission on Artificial Intelligence in Education is a welcome step in exploring how AI can benefit the education sector.
  2. There are already some promising examples of how AI can enhance the learning experience for students and support teachers in their roles.
  3. To fully realize the potential of AI in education, it is important to address the potential risks and challenges associated with its use, and to ensure that its development and deployment are guided by ethical and pedagogical principles.
