Bringing AI into Religious Practice

A young woman walked into the church on a Sunday morning, looking for a place to pray. She sat down in one of the empty pews and closed her eyes, intending to connect with God. After a few minutes of silence, she reached into her purse, took out her phone, opened a chat app, and typed her prayer into the text box. Within seconds, she received an automated response from an AI-powered virtual assistant, offering her some comforting words and a verse from the Bible that was relevant to her situation.

This scenario might seem strange to those who grew up in a traditional religious environment, but it's becoming increasingly common in the digital age. As technology advances, so do the ways in which people seek spiritual guidance and religious connection. The question is, what role should AI play in the church, and how can it be used to enhance, rather than replace, traditional religious practices.

Examples of AI-assisted Religion

Some religious groups have already started using chatbots and other AI technologies to connect with members and offer support. Here are a few examples:

  • Abide: A mobile app that provides users with daily prayers, meditations, and spiritual teachings through an AI-powered chatbot. Users can also request one-on-one prayer with a trained volunteer from around the world.
  • Orthodox Chatbot: Developed by the Orthodox Christian Network, this chatbot allows users to ask questions about faith and receive answers from theological experts in real-time.
  • Laura: A chatbot developed by the United Methodist Church, designed to help newcomers learn about the church and answer common questions about worship and theology.
  • The Church of England: Has launched a new digital initiative, through which users can ask Amazon's Alexa voice-activated assistant to read out a daily prayer. The Church has said it hopes the project will target busy families and professionals who may not be able to attend traditional church services.

These examples show that AI can be used in various ways to enhance religious practice, from providing daily meditations and prayers to answering important theological questions. However, there are also concerns about the implications of relying too heavily on machines for spiritual guidance.

The Implications of AI-Assisted Religion

One concern is that AI-assisted religion could become a substitute for traditional religious practices, leading to a loss of community and personal connection. There's also the risk that the AI algorithms could reinforce existing biases and discriminatory beliefs, rather than promoting diversity and inclusion.

Another issue is that using AI to connect with the Divine may make people overly reliant on technology, instead of putting in the work to cultivate a genuine spiritual practice and relationship with God.

However, advocates of AI-assisted religion argue that technology can enhance religious practices in many ways. AI-powered chatbots can help people access spiritual teachings and connect with communities that they may not have been able to reach otherwise. AI algorithms can also help to provide a more personalised and relevant spiritual experience, tailoring teachings and meditations to individual needs and preferences.

Overall, the tension between tradition and innovation when it comes to religion and technology is not new, but it is particularly acute when it comes to AI-assisted religion.

Conclusion - Balancing Tradition and Innovation

So, what is the way forward for individuals and religious communities who are interested in using AI to enhance their spiritual practices?

Firstly, it's essential to approach technology mindfully, and to acknowledge both its potential benefits and risks. Leaders and believers should undertake a collective exploration of AI to develop and agree on guidelines, models and best practices for technology utilisation in spiritual practices.

Secondly, we need to recognize the value of both technology and traditional religious practices. Embracing new modes of practice doesn't mean we should forget old ones. They can complement each other in such a way that the conveniences of technology can bring people into houses of worship that they may otherwise not have encountered.

Thirdly, AI-powered chatbots and virtual assistants can be a useful adjunct to traditional religious practices, but they should not replace fundamental practices, including meditation, prayer, worship to foster personal growth.

Three Takeaways:

  1. AI can be a powerful tool for providing personalized spiritual guidance, but it should never replace traditional religious practices.
  2. We need to approach technology mindfully in religious practice and acknowledge the benefits and risks that come with it, to ensure that it doesn't disrupt the established traditions and practices.
  3. Technology has the power to help people feel connected with others across the globe and create access to religious guidance, but to achieve this, people have to be mindful in how it's integrated into one's spiritual growth.
