How ChatGPT can help – and harm – healthcare



Image Credits https://www.mobihealthnews.com/video/how-chatgpt-can-help-and-harm-healthcare

It was a typical workday evening for Dr. John, a public health consultant at the local hospital. He had just finished his rounds and was sitting at his desk, catching up on his paperwork when his phone rang. Looking at the caller ID, he saw it was from an unknown number. Out of curiosity, he answered the call.

The voice on the other end was that of a young lady, in severe distress. She complained of chest pain, difficulty in breathing and showed all other symptoms of a heart attack. Dr. John quickly took charge of the situation, asking her pertinent medical questions. However, it soon became apparent that she was nowhere in the hospital or close vicinity. She had simply stumbled upon their website while surfing the internet and had accessed their chatbot, assuming it to be an emergency helpline.

This incident highlights the potential benefits and drawbacks of using ChatGPT in healthcare. Chatbots and AI-powered medical assistance can possible augment and supplement traditional healthcare practices by providing 24/7 accessibility, prompt diagnosis and treatment. However, they can also create irrevocable harm by providing misleading or false information, fueled by misinterpretation of inputs by machine algorithms.

How ChatGPT can help healthcare

The increasing popularity and convenience of online portals, chatbots, and other virtual doctor options present several benefits to healthcare. Here are some ways in which ChatGPT can help healthcare:

24/7 accessibility

Hospitals and clinics have limited working hours, and emergency rooms are packed with patients. With Chatbots, individuals can access medical assistance at any time of the day without having to wait in long lines or travel timorously to the hospital. It's especially useful for people living in remote areas or those who face difficulties, risking their lives.

Cost-effective

Chatbots can examine multiple patients simultaneously, providing an efficient and cost-effective solution for healthcare. It can also reduce the time spent on excessive administrative work, freeing up personnel to focuses on the more critical aspect of their job.

Quick and accurate diagnosis

Chatbots offer personalized treatment solutions that can examine symptoms faster than conventional healthcare methods. The chat bot AI has been programmed to identify and classify symptoms according to their urgency, recommend prevention tips, or provide actionable advice to improve patient health. This can ensure timely treatment and save lives.

How ChatGPT can harm healthcare

Irrelevant Information

Although chatbots provide a significant advantage by offering medical advice once the symptom has been articulated, they often start by asking the patient questions about their symptoms that need irrelevant or non-contextual information. Such irrelevant information contradicts both fluency and personal satisfaction of patients, causing them to feel unsatisfied and lose their trust in the use of Chatbots or AI-powered medical assistance systems.

Machine learning biases

Chatbot diagnosis and treatment options highly depend on its programming. Hence, background ideologies of the team that develops its programming can lead to unintentional biases that might harm patients' health. The machine learning algorithms might unknowingly suggest the wrong medication or treatment option for specific demographics or racial groups, which might be dangerous, leading to further health problems and even death. Such biases can not only harm an individual but also their communities.

Lack of human empathy

Certain patients may feel more comfortable speaking to a human healthcare professional rather than a machine. Unlike human empathy and experiences, chatbots are not empathetic, making patients feel unwanted or disregarded. When artificial intelligence and chatbots are forced to handle high volumes of patients simultaneously, they may not still recognize the tone or severity of the patient's situation leading to inappropriate suggestions.

Conclusion

While chatbots have significant potential in healthcare, it's crucial to note that they should not completely replace human-to-human interaction. It's essential to focus on the right combination of technology and human interaction to achieve a perfect balance that benefits patients and providers alike. It's essential to design Chatbots that prioritize accessibility, accuracy, empathy and prevent misleading or false information entirely for developing populations. Thus, Chatbots can improve healthcare in terms of cost, accessibility, and quality while keeping in mind that the field of healthcare is about empathy and benevolence.
