Google and Microsoft AI Experiments raise concerns for Advertisers



Image Credits https://searchengineland.com/google-microsoft-generative-ai-advertisers-428083

The Story

Advertisers' concerns were raised when it was revealed that both Google and Microsoft Bing are conducting experiments with ad placements in AI search results. While the tech giants say that the aim of the experiments is to improve user experience and increase engagement, some advertisers are worried that their ad spending will be affected.

Examples

One of the concerns raised by advertisers is the possibility of their ads being placed further down the search results page. With AI making the decisions, there is a chance that an advertiser's ad may not show up at all, if it is not deemed to be relevant to the user's search. This could result in a reduction in ad impressions and clicks, which would be detrimental to advertisers who rely on paid search to drive traffic and sales to their websites.

Another worry is that the ads could be placed in a less visible location on the search results page. Traditionally, ads have been displayed at the top of the page, where they are more noticeable to users. However, AI may prioritize organic search results over paid search results, causing ads to be placed further down the page. This could result in a decrease in click-through rates and a loss of revenue for advertisers.

Some advertisers have already started to withdraw their ad spending from Google and Microsoft. They argue that the experiments are being conducted without their consent, and that AI should not be making decisions about their advertising placements. They also fear that these experiments could lead to a shift in the way that paid search advertising works, which would have a negative impact on their businesses.

While Google and Microsoft have reassured advertisers that the experiments are still in the early stages and that they are working closely with them to address any concerns, the lack of transparency and control over where their ads are being placed is still a cause for alarm for many.

Conclusion

The use of AI in search results is becoming more prevalent, and it is clear that both Google and Microsoft are keen to explore this area further. However, it is important for both advertisers and the tech giants to work together to ensure that advertising placements are relevant and visible to users, while also respecting the concerns and needs of advertisers. Transparency and control over advertising placements should be a top priority for both companies.

Ultimately, the success of AI search results will depend on how well it can balance the needs of users, advertisers and search engine providers. By working together, we can ensure that the future of paid search advertising is both effective and fair.
