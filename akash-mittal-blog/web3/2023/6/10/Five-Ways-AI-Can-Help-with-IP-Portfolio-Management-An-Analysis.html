Five Ways AI Can Help with IP Portfolio Management - An Analysis



Image Credits https://ipwatchdog.com/2023/06/09/five-ways-ai-can-help-ip-portfolio-management/id=162181/

Intellectual property (IP) is a crucial asset for any business, and managing an IP portfolio can be a daunting task. However, with the rise of artificial intelligence (AI), managing your IP portfolio can be less burdensome and more efficient. AI can help businesses reduce their workload and increase efficiency in numerous areas of IP portfolio management. In this article, we will explore five ways AI can help with IP portfolio management.

Story: How AI Transformed IP Portfolio Management for a Pharmaceutical Company

A leading pharmaceutical company was struggling to manage its IP portfolio, which consisted of over 10,000 patents and trademarks spread across different countries. The in-house legal team was overwhelmed with the volume of data they needed to process and analyze. The process was time-consuming, expensive, and manual.

However, the company decided to try something new. They invested in AI-powered IP management software that could automatically scan and analyze the patent and trademark data. The software helped identify renewal deadlines, monitor competitor movements, and draw up reports on the company's IP portfolio performance in near-real-time. The time saved allowed the legal team to focus on more strategic work, such as identifying new licensing opportunities, identifying potential infringements, and advising business units on IP protection strategies.

The company was pleased with the results and found that the software paid for itself in less than six months. The investment contributed to a more streamlined and efficient process that optimized portfolio management, saved money, improved risk management, and allowed legal teams to focus on more strategic work.

Five Ways AI Can Help with IP Portfolio Management

1. Automated Search and Prioritization of IP Assets

AI-powered software can scan through your patent, trademark, and copyright portfolios, grouping them into distinct categories based on different criteria such as market segments, technology areas, and competitors. The software can then help prioritize each category, which makes it easy to track and manage your IP assets. AI can also detect any potential conflicts, overlaps, or gaps in your portfolio, providing more granular data that will support strategic portfolio decisions in the future.

2. Timely Identification of Potential Infringements

One of the most important aspects of IP portfolio management is proactively identifying potential infringements. AI-powered software can scan through vast amounts of data much quicker than any human team, analyzing the risk of potential IP infringement with machine learning algorithms. This can help you detect, track, and manage potential IP violations much sooner, helping your legal team act faster and more decisively.

3. Streamlined Administration of Trademark Registrations and Patent Filing

Patent filing and trademark registration are some of the most time-consuming and labor-intensive parts of IP portfolio management. AI-powered software can automate these processes, streamlining administration while reducing the workload on legal teams. Furthermore, software tools with powerful natural language processing (NLP) capabilities can help legal teams develop a comprehensive and nuanced classification in a much more simplified, efficient way.

4. Predictive Analytics and Portfolio Optimization

AI-powered software supports predictive analytics to forecast the performance of your IP portfolio, including trends in the field and how your portfolio might map onto them. This provides insight into how effective the current strategy is, which sets a clear direction for future investment. For instance, AI analytics can reveal links between internal and external patent asset management aspirations and help you negotiate around the most challenging portfolio red flags. Banding, aka assessing IP based on specific value or technical criteria, can also be done comprehensively and objectively, allowing for optimized management of assets.

5. Increased Efficiency and Reduced Workload

Finally, AI-powered IP portfolio management automation tools can dramatically reduce the workload on legal teams while increasing efficiency. This is accomplished by freeing up legal teams from grunt work such as data collection, IP portfolio analysis, and business risk compliance. This allows the legal team to focus on more intellectually stimulating and high-value tasks such as assessing licensing deals with external partners or focusing strictly on specialty patent prosecution.

Conclusion

Investing in AI-powered IP management software can greatly benefit any company looking to manage their IP portfolio both efficiently and effectively. With the assistance of AI, legal teams can avoid time-consuming and labor-intensive processes while dramatically reducing the likelihood of an infringement business risk. There are several areas where AI can help streamline the IP portfolio management process, from prioritizing IP assets to identifying potential infringements to automating processes. By utilizing this technology, companies can spend less time on portfolio management and more time on high-value, strategic work.
