Has Qualcomm Already Beaten Apple to the Punch in Smartphone AI?



Image Credits https://www.fool.com/investing/2023/06/09/has-qualcomm-already-beaten-apple-to-the-punch-in/

While the world is clamoring for Apple's mixed reality headset, Qualcomm may have already solved a pressing AI challenge.

A Story of Smartphone AI

As we continue to use our smartphones for practically everything, artificial intelligence (AI) has become an essential ingredient in delivering a smoother and more personalized user experience. This is the challenge that Qualcomm, one of the largest smartphone chipmakers, has been working on for years.

In fact, the company's latest flagship chip, the Snapdragon 888, has made significant strides in smartphone AI, helping to overcome many of the barriers that were previously thought to be insurmountable.

Examples of Qualcomm's AI Technology

One area where Qualcomm has excelled in AI development is the way it manages and processes information, particularly image recognition. This has been made possible through its multi-core AI engine, which provides a more efficient way of processing complex AI algorithms, such as natural language processing and machine learning.

Qualcomm's AI technology is also proving to be useful in other areas, such as gaming, where the chipmaker has developed specialized tools to deliver a more immersive experience. For example, the Snapdragon's Elite Gaming technology helps with reducing latency and improving graphics performance, while its Hexagon 780 AI processor provides dedicated hardware acceleration for AI tasks.

Conclusion: Qualcomm's Advantage

Qualcomm's expertise in smartphone AI has enabled it to achieve several key milestones in this field, giving it a critical edge over its competitors. Some of these achievements include:

  1. Multi-core AI engine to process complex algorithms
  2. Elite Gaming technology for reducing latency and improving graphics performance
  3. Hexagon 780 AI processor for dedicated hardware acceleration

Qualcomm might have beaten Apple to the punch in smartphone AI, but that doesn't mean Apple won't catch up. As we have seen time and again, the two companies are experts in competing with each other, and their rivalry has often translated into better products for consumers.
