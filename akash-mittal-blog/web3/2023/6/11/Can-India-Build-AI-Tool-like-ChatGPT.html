A Story of Growing AI in India

India has long been a hub for technological innovation. From the creation of the first microprocessor to the launch of the Mars Orbiter Mission, India has a rich history of scientific and technological accomplishments. With the rise of artificial intelligence (AI) in recent years, many in India are eager to see this trend continue.

In May 2021, Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI, made headlines when he called building an AI tool like ChatGPT (an AI tool that can be utilized for various purposes like answering questions, generating text, completing coding, and much more) in India "hopeless." However, the CEO of Tech Mahindra, C.P. Gurnani, responded that the challenge is accepted. His statement has given hope to India's AI community and signified the country's ambitions to become a leader in AI.

Examples of AI Advancement in India

Despite Altman's comment, India has already made significant contributions to the field of AI. Here are some examples:

1. Healthcare:

Indian companies like Wysa are using AI chatbots to help people manage their mental health. Niramai uses AI to detect breast cancer in its early stages. Cardiotrack utilizes AI to predict cardiovascular disease and prevent fatal heart attacks. These are just a few examples of how AI is being used in healthcare in India.

2. Agriculture:

Agriculture is a significant sector in India's economy, and AI is being used to help farmers improve their yield. Companies like CropIn and SatSure apply computer vision and remote sensing to help farmers make better decisions about planting, fertilizing, and harvesting crops.

3. Education:

Indian edtech companies like Byju's and Embibe are using AI to personalize learning for students. Byju's AI-based platform analyses student's learning patterns and offers personalized courses according to their requirements and performance. Embibe's AI-powered platform provides feedback on students' performance and progress and offers personalized coaching and guidance. In addition, Indian schools are also incorporating AI into their classrooms to enhance learning and improve student outcomes.

Conclusion in Three Points

India has already made significant strides in AI, and it is not impossible for the country to develop a tool like ChatGPT. Here are three key takeaways:

1. India has a Rich History of Technological Innovation:

From the creation of the first microprocessor to the launch of the Mars Orbiter Mission, India has a long history of scientific and technological accomplishments. This legacy has positioned the country to become a leader in AI, and it should be celebrated.

2. AI is Being Applied Across Multiple Sectors:

India is utilizing AI in several sectors like healthcare, agriculture, education, and more. These applications are improving efficiency, productivity, and enhancing human capabilities in these fields.

3. The Challenge is Accepted:

C.P. Gurnani's statement that Tech Mahindra accepts the challenge of building an AI tool like ChatGPT is a testament to the country's ambition and determination to become a leader in AI. India recognizes that AI technology offers massive possibilities, and it aims to realize them.
