Why AI Chatbots like ChatGPT can't Create Convincing Scientific Papers Yet?



Image Credits https://www.livescience.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/ai-chatbot-chatgpt-cant-create-convincing-scientific-papers-yet

Chatbots have been around for a while. They are AI-powered virtual assistants that can simulate human conversations and perform tasks accordingly. They can be used in various fields such as customer service, personal assistance, and even education. However, can they be trusted to write scientific papers?

The Story

Recently, a research study was conducted to evaluate the efficiency of AI-powered language models such as GPT-2 and GPT-3 in generating scientific papers. The results showed that while these models were able to invent text that sounded genuine and scientific, they often contained factual errors, logical fallacies, and other forms of problematic content. Moreover, these language models were unable to match the writing quality of human experts in the field, which made their generated papers easier to detect as fake.

Examples

Some examples of fake scientific papers generated by AI Chatbots include:

  • A paper that claimed to have found a correlation between eating chocolate and growing taller.
  • A paper that suggested a new method for converting carbon dioxide into oxygen using unicorns.
  • A paper that used made-up data to prove that the Earth is flat.

The Conclusion

The results of the study clearly show that AI Chatbots like ChatGPT are not yet ready to replace human experts in scientific research. Although these models can be used for several applications, including writing news articles, product descriptions, and chatbot scripts, scientific research papers require a higher level of expertise and quality assurance that only humans can provide.

3 Points to consider

  1. AI Chatbots lack the expertise and experience required for scientific research. So, they are prone to creating flawed, incomplete, or simply false papers.
  2. AI Chatbots can't match the writing quality of expert humans in the field, specifically when it comes to proper language usage, formatting, and technical terminology.
  3. AI Chatbots can't ensure the ethical and legal compliance of the research, which is crucial in scientific research. Specifically, when it comes to data protection, intellectual property rights, and research integrity.

Personal Tips

Although AI Chatbots are not suitable for scientific research, they can still be used in several ways to enhance research. For instance:

  • AI Chatbots can be used to extract relevant information from scientific papers, analyze research data and present it in a user-friendly format, and even communicate with other researchers.
  • AI Chatbots can be used to create interactive tools for scientific research, such as virtual laboratories, simulations, and predictive models.
  • AI Chatbots can be used to assist human experts in the research process, by providing fast feedback, suggesting new ideas, and assisting in data analysis and visualization.
