Artificial Intelligence in Music Production: Should AI-Generated Songs be Eligible for Grammy Awards?



Image Credits https://arstechnica.com/information-technology/2023/06/purely-ai-generated-songs-declared-ineligible-for-grammy-awards/

It was a beautiful day outside, and Sarah was walking down the street, humming to herself. Suddenly, a song started playing on the nearby radio. Sarah recognized the melody and started singing along. It was a catchy tune, with a nice beat and interesting lyrics. But there was something off about it. As she listened closer, Sarah realized that the song made no sense at all! The lyrics were completely random, and the melody seemed to be made up on the spot. She laughed at the absurdity and continued walking, wondering who could have come up with such a bizarre composition.

As it turns out, the song was generated by an artificial intelligence (AI) program. The software had analyzed thousands of existing songs in order to create a completely new piece of music. And while the result may have been entertaining, it was not eligible for Grammy Awards - one of the most prestigious music awards in the world.

Why are AI-generated songs ineligible for Grammy Awards?

According to the Grammy Awards rules, in order for a song to be eligible, it must meet certain criteria. One of these criteria is that the song must be "written or co-written by the performer(s)." However, in the case of AI-generated songs, there are no performers or human composers involved in the creation process. Rather, the software generates the entire composition on its own.

Another issue is that the Grammy Awards place a high value on human creativity and originality. While AI-generated songs may be technically impressive, they lack the emotional depth and personal touch that comes from human experience and expression.

Examples of AI-generated songs

Despite their ineligibility for Grammy Awards, AI-generated songs have garnered a fair amount of media attention in recent years. Here are a few examples:

1. "Daddy's Car" - Sony CSL Research Lab

In 2016, researchers at Sony's Computer Science Labs created an AI program called Flow Machines. The program was designed to analyze existing music styles and create entirely new compositions in those styles. One of the program's most notable creations was "Daddy's Car," a song in the style of The Beatles. While the song lacks the lyricism of classic Beatles hits, it's an impressive feat of technology and has a catchy melody.

2. "Break Free" - Amper Music

Amper Music is a company that specializes in AI-generated music for use in video games, advertisements, and other media. Their software allows users to input parameters such as genre, mood, and tempo, and generates a unique composition based on those inputs. "Break Free" is one of their most popular offerings, with a high-energy beat and electronic sound.

3. "I Am AI" - Taryn Southern

While "I Am AI" is technically an AI-generated song, it was created with heavy human involvement. Singer and songwriter Taryn Southern used an AI program called Amper to generate the basic chord progressions and melody, but she wrote the lyrics and performed the vocals herself. The result is a catchy pop song with a futuristic feel.

Should AI-generated songs be eligible for Grammy Awards?

While there's no denying that AI-generated music is impressive from a technological standpoint, there are valid arguments for why it should not be eligible for Grammy Awards. Here are three reasons why:

1. Lack of human emotion and experience

Music is often described as an art form because it involves the expression of human emotion and experience. AI-generated songs lack this crucial element, as they are created entirely by machines with no personal connection to the subject matter. While the resulting compositions may be pleasing to the ear, they lack the soul that makes music truly great.

2. Ethical concerns around copyright

Another issue with AI-generated music is the question of who owns the copyright to the compositions. In traditional music creation, the rights to a song belong to the songwriter or composers. However, with AI-generated music, it's unclear whether the rights should belong to the software developers, the users of the software, or the AI program itself.

3. Recognition of human creativity and artistry

The Grammy Awards are meant to recognize the artistry and creativity of human performers and composers. Allowing AI-generated songs to be eligible for awards would diminish the value placed on human contribution and originality in the music industry.

Conclusion

While AI-generated music is undoubtedly impressive from a technological standpoint, it lacks the emotional depth and personal touch that humans bring to music creation. The Grammy Awards, as an institution that prizes human creativity and originality, should continue to exclude AI-generated songs from eligibility. As technology continues to advance, it's important to remember the value of human expression in the arts.
