The Rise of Crypto and Web 3: A Global Survey by Consensys



Image Credits https://www.thisdaylive.com/index.php/2023/06/27/consensys-unveils-data-of-global-survey-on-crypto-web-3

It's no secret that the world of cryptocurrency and blockchain technology has been rapidly growing and evolving in recent years. However, the extent of this growth and its impact on people and businesses across the globe has remained relatively unknown until now. Consensys, a leading blockchain software company, has conducted a global survey to shed light on the current state of crypto and web 3.0, and its findings are eye-opening.

The Story

Lisa, a young entrepreneur based in New York City, was first introduced to cryptocurrency and blockchain technology a few years ago. Since then, she has been actively investing in various crypto projects and keeping a close eye on the industry trends. However, she often felt like she was in a bubble, disconnected from the rest of the world that was yet to fully embrace this disruptive technology.

That's until she stumbled upon the results of the Consensys survey, which highlighted that over 24% of people across the world had heard of cryptocurrencies, with 6% having purchased them. This meant that millions of people, just like Lisa, were seeing the potential of this new era of finance and were already participating in it. It was a reassuring realization that made her even more excited about the future of crypto.

The Findings

The Consensys survey covered various aspects of crypto and web 3.0, including awareness, adoption, usage, and sentiment towards this technology. Here are some of the key takeaways from the survey:

1. Rising Awareness

As mentioned earlier, over 24% of people globally had heard of cryptocurrencies, and this number was highest in emerging economies such as Brazil, South Africa, and Indonesia. This growing awareness was attributed to media coverage, social media, and word of mouth. The survey also found that 2 out of 3 people who had heard of cryptocurrencies agreed that they had the potential to become a global digital currency.

2. Increasing Adoption

The survey revealed that 6% of respondents had purchased cryptocurrencies, with the highest proportion being in Eastern Europe and Asia Pacific. This was a significant increase from a 2018 survey which reported only 1% adoption. The main reasons cited for buying cryptocurrencies were investment, personal freedom, and learning about new technologies. However, the survey also outlined barriers to adoption, such as a lack of understanding, trust, and accessibility.

3. Diverse Usage

Cryptocurrencies have often been criticized for their limited use cases, but the Consensys survey challenged that notion. It found that 40% of people who had purchased cryptocurrencies had used them for transactions, such as buying goods or services online. Other use cases included investment, donations, and peer-to-peer payments. The survey also highlighted a growing interest in decentralized finance (DeFi), with 44% of respondents interested in using DeFi products and services.

The Conclusion

The Consensys survey provided valuable insights into the current state of crypto and web 3.0, and its potential impact on individuals and businesses across the world. Based on the findings, here are three key takeaways:

1. The Future is Bright

The survey showed that cryptocurrencies and blockchain technology are gaining traction and becoming more mainstream. As more people become aware of their potential and start adopting them, the industry can only continue to grow and innovate. The potential applications for crypto and web 3.0 are vast, ranging from financial services to supply chain management to social impact.

2. Challenges Remain

The survey highlighted the challenges facing the industry, such as lack of understanding, trust, and accessibility. This underscores the importance of education and outreach efforts to demystify the technology and build trust among potential users. It also emphasizes the need for policymakers to create an enabling environment for blockchain innovation.

3. Collaboration is Key

The survey showed that the adoption and use of cryptocurrencies and blockchain technology are not limited to any particular region or group of people. This suggests that a collaborative approach is needed to ensure that the industry grows inclusively and benefits everyone. This could take the form of partnerships between businesses, governments, academia, and civil society.
