Nigeria Leading in Crypto Knowledge Globally



Image Credits https://technext24.com/2023/06/27/consensys-reveals-nigeria-leads-the-way/

Consensys' "Global Survey on Crypto and Web3" reveals an interesting fact - Nigeria leads the way in knowledge of crypto globally.

A Story to Begin With

John, a young Nigerian guy, had invested in Bitcoin back in 2016 when the cryptocurrency was not very popular in Nigeria. He had read about it online and was fascinated by its decentralized nature and potential to become a new global currency. Today, John is one of the early adopters of cryptocurrency in Nigeria and has made significant profits from his investments.

John's story is not unique. It is representative of a growing trend in Nigeria where more and more people are getting interested in crypto and blockchain technology. The country has emerged as a leader in crypto and blockchain adoption, not just in Africa but globally.

Examples of Crypto Adoption in Nigeria

Nigeria has a large and young population who are tech-savvy and believe in digital currencies. The country also has a weak traditional financial system, which makes it more vulnerable to fraud and scams. This has led many Nigerians to look for alternative ways to store and transfer money.

Crypto and blockchain technology offer a safe and secure way to do so. Here are some examples of how crypto is being adopted in Nigeria:

  • P2P Trading: Platforms like Paxful and LocalBitcoins have gained popularity in Nigeria as they allow people to buy and sell Bitcoin directly with each other without the need for a traditional intermediary.
  • Remittances: Nigeria is one of the largest receivers of remittances in Africa, and crypto offers a cheaper and faster way to transfer money across borders. Platforms like BitPesa and BitSpark are providing this service in Nigeria.
  • Startups: The Nigerian startup scene has exploded in recent years, and many of them are using blockchain technology to solve real-world problems. For example, OneWattSolar enables people to buy solar power using cryptocurrencies, while SureRemit allows for the purchase of vouchers for goods and services using crypto.

Why Nigeria Leads in Crypto Knowledge?

Consensys' survey, which interviewed over 10,000 people from 10 different countries, found that Nigeria had the highest percentage of respondents who said they understood cryptocurrency (93%). This is higher than the global average of 72%. So why is Nigeria leading in crypto knowledge?

One reason is the aforementioned weak traditional financial system. Nigerians are familiar with financial scams and embezzlement, which has made them more cautious and discerning when it comes to money matters. Crypto, with its transparent and decentralized nature, appeals to these sensibilities.

Another reason is the relatively low barriers to entry in crypto. All one needs is an internet connection and a smartphone to start buying and selling cryptocurrencies. This accessibility has made it easier for Nigerians to learn about and participate in the crypto market.

In conclusion, here are the key takeaways from Nigeria's rise as a global crypto leader:

  1. Nigeria has a young and tech-savvy population who are open to new technology.
  2. The country has a weak traditional financial system that has made people look for alternative ways to store and transfer money.
  3. Crypto and blockchain offer a transparent and decentralized way to do so, which has led to more adoption and knowledge of this technology in Nigeria.
