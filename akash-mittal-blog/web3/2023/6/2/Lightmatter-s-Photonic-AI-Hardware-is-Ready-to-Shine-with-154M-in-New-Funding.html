But what exactly is photonic AI hardware, and why is it so revolutionary? Simply put, photonic AI hardware uses light instead of electricity to transmit and process data. Traditional electronic computing relies on electrons moving through metal wires to convey information, which results in latency, heat, and electromagnetic interference. Photonic computing, on the other hand, uses photons (particles of light) to transmit data at the speed of light, without any resistance or interference.

This approach is not new, but until now it has been prohibitively expensive and difficult to mass-produce. This is where Lightmatter comes in. The company has developed a unique photonic chip architecture that can be manufactured using standard microchip fabrication techniques, at a fraction of the cost of existing photonic chips. This breakthrough technology makes it possible to integrate photonic computing into a wide range of applications, from data centers to autonomous vehicles to medical equipment.
