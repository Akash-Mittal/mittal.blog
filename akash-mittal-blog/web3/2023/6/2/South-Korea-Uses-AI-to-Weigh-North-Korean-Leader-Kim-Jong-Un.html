South Korea Uses AI to Weigh North Korean Leader Kim Jong Un

It was a scene straight out of a spy novel. A South Korean intelligence agency had received an unusual request from one of its field agents – to find out the exact weight of North Korean leader Kim Jong Un before an upcoming summit between the two Koreas. Knowing that they couldn't just ask their northern neighbors, the agency turned to artificial intelligence.

How AI Weighed Kim Jong Un

The South Korean intelligence agency used satellite images to analyze the movements of Kim Jong Un and his entourage, as well as the logistics involved in their travels. The data was then fed into an AI algorithm that calculated the weight of the North Korean leader based on his body proportions and the amount of food and other supplies that were being transported with him.

The resulting estimate put Kim Jong Un's weight at around 140 kilograms (308 pounds). While this number may seem trivial, it is actually important for various reasons. For one, it provides a glimpse into the health of the North Korean leader, whose weight has been a topic of speculation for years. Additionally, knowing his weight would help South Korean officials plan for the summit, such as by providing the right-sized chairs or adjusting the size of the podium.
