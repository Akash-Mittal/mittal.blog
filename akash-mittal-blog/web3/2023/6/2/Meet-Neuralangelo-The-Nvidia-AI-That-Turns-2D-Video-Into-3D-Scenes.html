How does Neuralangelo work?

Neuralangelo works by analyzing the 2D video frame by frame, and generating a 3D model of the scene based on the visual cues it finds. It uses advanced algorithms to interpret the video's depth, lighting, and geometry, and then extrapolates that information into a 3D space.

But Neuralangelo doesn't just create a static 3D model - it also animates it in real-time, so that it moves and interacts with the environment just like a real object would. This is achieved through a process called "neural rendering", where the 3D model is overlaid onto the original video footage and rendered with realistic lighting and shading.
