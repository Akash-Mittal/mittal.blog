Deutsche Bank Hires Southeast Asia Vice Chair

Deutsche Bank has hired a new Vice Chair for Southeast Asia region in a bid to continue its growth plan and strengthen its foothold in the region.

Meet the New Hire

The new hire is none other than Ms. Jane Tan, a seasoned banker with over 25 years of experience in the industry. Ms. Tan has an impressive track record of successfully leading and growing businesses in Asia-Pacific region and has been recognized for her exceptional performance by various industry publications.

Prior to joining Deutsche Bank, Ms. Tan held the position of Managing Director and Head of Corporate Banking for Asia at a leading international bank. In her new role as Vice Chair for Southeast Asia, Ms. Tan will be responsible for driving business growth in the region and building deeper relationships with local clients.

The Growth Plan

Deutsche Bank's growth plan for Southeast Asia region is built on three pillars â€“ expanding its footprint, strengthening its platform, and deepening its client relationships.

Under the expansion pillar, the bank aims to enter new markets and increase its presence in the existing ones. The bank has identified Vietnam, Indonesia, and the Philippines as the key growth markets in the region and plans to expand its operations in these countries over the next few years.

Under the strengthening pillar, the bank aims to enhance its product offerings and upgrade its technology platform to better serve its clients. The bank has recently launched a new digital wealth management platform and plans to roll out more such platforms in the future.

Under the deepening relationships pillar, the bank aims to build deeper relationships with its existing clients by providing them with personalized solutions and superior service. The bank has dedicated relationship managers for its key clients and provides them with access to a wide range of experts and resources.

Deutsche Bank's growth plan for Southeast Asia region has already yielded impressive results. In the last financial year, the bank's revenue from Asia-Pacific region grew by 7% YoY and its profit before tax grew by 23% YoY.

The bank also won several prestigious awards for its performance in the region. It was named "Best Investment Bank in Asia-Pacific" by Global Finance and "Best Transaction Bank in Asia-Pacific" by The Banker.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Deutsche Bank's hire of Ms. Jane Tan as the Vice Chair for Southeast Asia region is a strong indication of the bank's commitment to continue its growth plan and strengthen its foothold in the region. The bank's expansion, strengthening, and deepening relationships pillars have already shown impressive results and are likely to yield more in the future.
