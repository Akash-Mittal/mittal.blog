Revolutionizing Conversations with ChatBots

Writing a Better Bot

As a business owner trying to engage visitors to your website, I was really struggling. I had great traffic but no conversions. I had hired knowledgeable sales reps, but you know what, people don't like talking to sales reps. They are pushy, they ask invasive questions, and very rarely do they answer your queries without trying to sell you a product. Something was definitely missing in my marketing strategy and that's when I stumbled upon Chatbots.

Yes, I know what you're thinking. Chatbots are as annoying as sales reps, but there was something different about these Chatbots that had caught my attention. They are based on Artificial Intelligence and help people get the right information quickly. It's a text-based conversational interface that's designed to help customers find solutions to their problems. AI technology is intelligent enough to understand what the customer is saying so it can easily answer their queries in a way that's helpful rather than pushy.

Offering Chatbots as part of my business was a turning point for me. It helped with customer engagement and improved lead generation. Within a week of introducing the Chatbot, my sales had increased 40% percent. By the end of the month, I had exceeded my monthly target by 20% percent. It was both a great and profitable experience for me.

It's not just me who has experienced the benefits of Chatbots. They have been adopted by a variety of companies, big and small, across the globe; and some of them have reported an astronomical increase in sales and customer engagement.

1. H&M Uses Chatbots for Customer Engagement

H&M, the international fashion brand, introduced a Chatbot on Kik Messenger, a popular mobile messaging app. The Chatbot had a simple purpose: to help customers find clothes. The bot presented a series of conversational responses and helped them pick items according to their preferences. The Chatbot saw a 70% percent increase in CTR (click-through-rate) and an 11% percent increase in customer satisfaction. H&M was able to achieve this by abandoning the traditional sales pitch.

2. Grupo Boticario Uses Chatbots to Improve User Experience

Grupo Boticario, the largest cosmetic franchise in Brazil, uses Chatbots to improve user experience by simplifying buying processes. They introduced a Chatbot that helped customers find products according to their skin type, helped them select the right product, and provided them with a checklist of ingredients for the products they were interested in. As a result, the company saw a 30% percent increase in daily orders.

3. Pizza Hut's Chatbot for Easy Ordering

Pizza Hut's Chatbot helped customers to order pizza without leaving Facebook Messenger. Customers are able to share location and delivery instructions as well. The Chatbot has seen both an increase in pizza orders and is also appreciated by customers who have found it convenient.

Creating Your Own Chatbot

If you run a business, it's worth considering creating a Chatbot that fits your business's requirements. Here are four tips to create an optimal chatbot.

1. Understand Your Customers

Before creating a chatbot, spend some time understanding your customer base. Who are they, what are their needs, and what do they expect from your business? Make sure that your chatbot is designed to be helpful rather than just an extension of your business. It should be conversational and engaging, rather than pushy and annoying.

2. Keep it Simple

Conversations should be kept simple, and the responses should be framed in a simple and concise way. The conversations and responses should be structured in a way that's easy for your customers to understand. A complex chatbot can be frustrating and will likely lead to a negative experience from your customer.

3. Train your Chatbot

Just like any other employee, your Chatbot needs to be trained to understand your customer's needs; it should know all the ins and outs of your business. Training should be ongoing to keep improving responses and customer engagement.

4. Test Your Chatbot

Before going live, test your Chatbot thoroughly. Make sure that customer feedback is actively monitored; the Chatbot should take action based on customer feedback. This helps to improve quality and customer engagement.
