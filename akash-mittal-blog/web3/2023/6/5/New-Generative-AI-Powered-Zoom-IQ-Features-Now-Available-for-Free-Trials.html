New Generative AI-Powered Zoom IQ Features Now Available for Free Trials

COVID-19 has accelerated the demand for video conferencing solutions as remote work has become the norm in many industries. Zoom, one of the most popular video conferencing platforms, has been experiencing unprecedented growth. By the end of April 2020, Zoom had more than 300 million daily meeting participants, compared to just 10 million in December 2019. To meet the increasing demand for seamless video experiences, Zoom has introduced new generative AI-powered Zoom IQ features that promise to revolutionize the way we connect remotely. These features are now available for free trials. Let's take a closer look at what they offer.

1. Immersive Scenes

If you're tired of having a boring white background during video calls, immersive scenes are for you. Using generative AI, Zoom creates virtual backgrounds that look like real offices, conference rooms, or even outer space. These backgrounds are more realistic than traditional virtual backgrounds, and they move in response to your movements, making the experience more immersive and engaging. If you're a teacher, you could use immersive scenes to create a more interactive virtual classroom. If you're a salesperson, you could project an image of your product in a fancy showroom. Immersive scenes give you the opportunity to showcase your creativity and make your meetings more fun and memorable.

2. Transcription and Translation

If you've ever struggled to keep up with the conversation during a video call, you will appreciate Zoom's new Transcription and Translation feature. This feature uses AI to transcribe spoken words into text, which appears in real-time on the screen. This is especially helpful for people who are deaf or hard of hearing, or for people who don't speak the same language as the speaker. The feature also translates the text into multiple languages, making it possible for people who speak different languages to participate in the same meeting. This feature is a game-changer for businesses that have international teams, as it makes communication more seamless and inclusive.

3. Smart Gallery

Zoom's new Smart Gallery feature uses AI to arrange video feeds in an optimal way, depending on who is speaking and who is active in the meeting. The feature detects who is speaking and makes sure that their video feed is the largest on the screen. It also arranges the other video feeds in a grid-like pattern, so that everyone can be seen at the same time. This feature reduces visual clutter and makes it easier to focus on the speaker. The Smart Gallery feature is especially useful for large meetings or webinars, where there are multiple speakers and participants.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Zoom's new generative AI-powered Zoom IQ features are a game-changer for the video conferencing industry. These features promise to make remote work more immersive, inclusive, and engaging. Whether you're a teacher, a salesperson, or a team leader, these features can help you showcase your creativity, enhance your communication, and maximize your productivity. So why not give them a try? Currently, Zoom is offering free trials for these features, so you can experience them firsthand before making a commitment. Don't miss this opportunity to take your video meetings to the next level!
