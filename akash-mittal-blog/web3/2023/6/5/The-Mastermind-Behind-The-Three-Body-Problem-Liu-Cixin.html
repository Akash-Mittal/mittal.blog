The Mastermind Behind "The Three Body Problem": Liu Cixin

How one man's imagination made him one of China's most beloved sci-fi writers

The Formation of a Creative Mind

Liu Cixin was born in 1963 in Yangquan, Shanxi Province, China. Growing up during the Cultural Revolution, he was exposed to both the hardships and the redemptive power of literature, stories, and the human imagination.

As a student of geological engineering at the Henan University of Science and Technology in Luoyang, Liu read widely and voraciously, consuming every science fiction book he could get his hands on.

It was during this time that he began to experiment with writing his own science fiction stories, and after winning an award for one of his stories, he decided to pursue writing seriously.

The Breakthrough

Liu Cixin struggled for many years to break through as a writer. His rejection letters numbered in the hundreds, and he spent many long nights wondering if he would ever be able to make a living from his writing.

But he persisted, and in 1999 he won the prestigious "China Galaxy Science Fiction Award" for his story "The Village Teacher". This award was a turning point for Liu, and he began to gain recognition and critical acclaim for his work.

However, it was his trilogy of books known as "The Three Body Problem" that truly catapulted him to fame. The first book in the trilogy was published in 2008, and it quickly became a sensation in China, winning the Hugo Award

The Impact of "The Three Body Problem"

The impact of "The Three Body Problem" cannot be overstated. Not only did it introduce a new generation of young Chinese readers to the wonders of science fiction, but it also helped to bring global attention to the thriving sci-fi scene in China.

  • Within two years of its release, the trilogy had sold over 1.5 million copies in China alone.
  • The books have been translated into 21 languages and have sold over 8 million copies worldwide.
  • Netflix is currently developing a TV series based on the books.

Perhaps most significantly, "The Three Body Problem" has been praised for its incorporation of Chinese history, culture, and philosophy into the sci-fi genre, making it a uniquely Chinese contribution to the field.

In Conclusion

  1. Liu Cixin is one of China's most beloved and celebrated science fiction writers, and his trilogy "The Three Body Problem" has made an indelible impact on readers around the world.
  2. His success is a testament to the power of persistence, imagination, and the human spirit.
  3. Through his writing, he has helped to showcase the rich cultural heritage and creative spirit of China to the world.
