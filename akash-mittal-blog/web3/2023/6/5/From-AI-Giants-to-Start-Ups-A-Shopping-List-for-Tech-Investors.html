From AI Giants to Start-Ups: A Shopping List for Tech Investors

Artificial intelligence has been around for decades, but it has only recently started to make a significant impact on society. Today, AI is everywhere, from Siri and Alexa on our phones, to the Google algorithms that control the search results we see. The potential for AI in the future is limitless, and investors are starting to take notice. In this article, we will explore some of the top AI companies to buy stock in and some of the smaller AI businesses that show promise.

The Story of X.AI

It's been a long day, and you are trying to wind down for the evening. The last thing on your to-do list is scheduling a meeting with a potential client. You don't have the energy to send yet another email back and forth to find a time that works for both parties. This is where x.ai comes in to save the day.

X.ai is a New York based start-up that has created an AI assistant that schedules meetings for you. The assistant's name is Amy, and she is entirely powered by deep learning algorithms. All you have to do is cc Amy on an email to the person you want to meet with, and she takes care of the rest. Amy suggests times that work for both of you, adds the meeting to your calendar, and even sends out follow-up emails to confirm the meeting.

The idea for x.ai came from the company's CEO, Dennis Mortensen, who was tired of scheduling meetings himself. Mortensen estimates that he spent approximately 1,000 hours a year on scheduling. X.ai has raised over $44 million in funding so far, and some of its investors include Softbank and prominent angels like Ed Sim.

Why AI is a Smart Investment

The recent advancements in AI have opened up a world of possibilities for investors looking to profit. Many companies have AI integrated into their core operations, and those that do not are rushing to catch up.

By 2025, the global AI market is forecast to be worth over $60 billion. Many of the world's largest tech companies, including Google, Microsoft, and Amazon, are investing heavily in AI research and development. AI technology is being deployed in a wide range of industries, from healthcare to finance, and everything in between.

Investment in AI is also an ethical choice. AI is being used to aid in the development of new medical treatments, improve disaster response efforts, and even combat climate change.

Top AI Stocks to Buy

NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA)

NVIDIA is one of the largest and most well-known AI companies in the world. The company has been at the forefront of AI development for years and has been integral in developing the hardware necessary to support AI. NVIDIA's GPUs are used in some of the world's most advanced AI-powered systems, including robots, self-driving cars, and supercomputers.

NVIDIA has seen significant growth in recent years. The company's stock price has more than tripled since 2016, and its revenue has grown from $6.91 billion in 2016 to $10.92 billion in 2020. The company is expected to continue experiencing growth as the demand for AI-powered systems increases.

Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (AMD)

AMD is another major player in the AI industry. The company's CPUs and GPUs are used in a wide range of AI-powered systems, from gaming PCs to data centers. AMD has been investing heavily in AI research and development to keep up with its competitors. The company has also been expanding its AI partnerships, working with companies like Baidu and Microsoft to integrate AMD's technology into their AI systems.

In 2020, AMD's revenue grew by 45% year-over-year, reaching a record $9.76 billion. The company's stock price has more than doubled since 2019, and it's expected to continue experiencing growth as the demand for AI-powered systems increases.

Alphabet Inc. (GOOGL)

Alphabet, the parent company of Google, has been investing in AI research for years. The company's DeepMind subsidiary is one of the most well-known AI research labs in the world. Alphabet has also been using AI to improve its core products, like Google search and YouTube. The company has been working on developing new AI-powered products, like its healthcare subsidiary, Verily.

In 2020, Google reported $182.53 billion in revenue, with a significant portion coming from its AI-powered products. Alphabet's stock price has been steadily increasing, and the company is expected to continue experiencing growth as it expands into new AI-powered markets.

Smaller AI Companies to Watch

UiPath Inc. (PATH)

UiPath is a software company that uses AI to automate business processes. The company's automation technology can be used in a wide range of industries, including finance, healthcare, and manufacturing. UiPath's technology can help companies reduce costs, improve efficiency, and reduce errors.

In 2021, UiPath went public, with its stock price increasing by 23% on its first day of trading. The company's revenue has been steadily increasing, and it's expected to continue experiencing growth as the demand for automation technology increases.

C3.ai, Inc (AI)

C3.ai is an enterprise AI software company that provides a platform for developing and deploying AI applications. The company's technology can be used in a wide range of industries, including energy, healthcare, and manufacturing. C3.ai's technology focuses on delivering predictive insights and operational efficiencies.

C3.ai went public in 2020, with its stock price more than doubling on its first day of trading. The company's revenue has been steadily increasing, and it's expected to continue experiencing growth as the demand for enterprise AI software increases.

Investing in AI for the Future

AI is a rapidly growing industry that shows no signs of slowing down. The potential for AI is endless, and investors who are looking to get on board early have the potential for significant returns. While there are many promising AI companies out there, investors should approach investing in AI cautiously, as the sector is still relatively new and unpredictable. Still, for investors who are willing to take the risk, the potential rewards are vast.
