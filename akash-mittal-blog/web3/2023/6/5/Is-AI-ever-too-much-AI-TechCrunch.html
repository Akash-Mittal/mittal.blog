Is AI ever too much AI?

An Unintended Consequence of AI

Once upon a time, there was a company that decided to implement AI in its customer service department. The company believed that AI would be able to handle a large number of inquiries quickly and efficiently, without the need for human intervention. And it did.

The AI was able to process and understand most of the inquiries it received and provide appropriate responses. Customers were happy with the quick and accurate service they received, and the company was able to save money by reducing the number of human employees.

However, one day, an elderly woman called the customer service department to inquire about an issue with her account. She was upset and frustrated because she couldn't understand the responses she was receiving from the AI. She felt like she was talking to a machine and not a human being. She hung up feeling even more frustrated and confused than before.

This is just one example of how AI can sometimes be too much AI. While AI is incredibly powerful and useful in many situations, it can also have unintended consequences, especially when it comes to customer service.

  • According to a survey conducted by PwC, 59% of consumers feel that companies have lost touch with the human element of customer experience due to the increased use of technology.
  • Another survey by Accenture found that 83% of consumers prefer dealing with human beings over digital channels when it comes to customer service.
  • Research by MIT and Boston Consulting group found that while companies that use AI and automation grow faster and have higher profits than those that don't, they also experience higher employee turnover rates and lower customer satisfaction scores.

When is AI too much AI?

Here are three points to consider:

  1. When it replaces human interaction entirely - as illustrated in the story above, sometimes customers just want to talk to a human being. AI can be great for answering simple questions, but for more complex or emotional issues, there needs to be a human touch.
  2. When it reduces empathy - AI does not have emotions, which means it cannot empathize with customers in the way that a human can. Empathy is an essential aspect of customer service, and it is something that AI cannot replicate.
  3. When it becomes too complex - sometimes, AI is so complex that it becomes difficult for customers to use or understand. This can lead to frustration and confusion, which is the opposite of what a company wants to achieve with its customer service.

Conclusion

AI can be a powerful tool in customer service, but it is essential to use it in the right way. Here are a few practical tips:

  • Use AI to handle simple inquiries or repetitive tasks, but make sure that customers always have the option to speak to a human being if they want to.
  • Train customer service representatives to use AI effectively, so they can provide the right level of support to customers.
  • Regularly survey customers to determine the overall level of satisfaction with your customer service and make changes accordingly.

By using AI in a way that complements and enhances human interaction, companies can provide excellent customer service that meets the needs and expectations of their customers.
