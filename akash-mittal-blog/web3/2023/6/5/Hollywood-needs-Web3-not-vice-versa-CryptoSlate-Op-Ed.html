Hollywood needs Web3 not vice versa

The story of a struggling actor

As a struggling actor in Hollywood, you're constantly trying to make connections, gain publicity and ultimately land a big role. But what if I told you that Web3 technology could give you a better chance at success than any Hollywood connection ever could?

Meet Jack, a talented actor who has been auditioning for roles in Hollywood for years without any real breakthroughs. One day, he decided to take matters into his own hands and use Web3 technology to create a decentralized platform where actors can showcase their skills to the world.

Jack's platform allowed actors to upload their audition tapes, headshots and resumes to a decentralized database that could be accessed by anyone with an internet connection. The platform also allowed producers, directors and agents to search for actors based on specific criteria, such as age range, ethnicity and experience.

The platform quickly gained traction and before long, Jack had helped numerous actors land roles in major productions. The success of his platform caught the attention of Hollywood power players, who soon realized that Web3 technology could revolutionize the entire industry.

Quantifiable examples of success

Jack's platform isn't the only example of Web3 technology revolutionizing the entertainment industry. Here are a few more examples:

  • Tokenization of films: Film producers are using blockchain technology to tokenize their films, allowing investors to purchase shares and receive a portion of the profits. This not only makes it easier for filmmakers to secure funding but also allows investors to have a stake in the success of the film.
  • Decentralized distribution platforms: Web3 technology allows for the creation of decentralized distribution platforms where filmmakers can distribute their work without relying on traditional gatekeepers like studios and distributors. These platforms also allow viewers to access and pay for content directly.
  • Smart contract royalties: Smart contracts allow filmmakers to easily track sales and distribute royalties to all parties involved in the production, including actors, crew and investors. This eliminates the need for middlemen and reduces the likelihood of disputes.

These examples show how Web3 technology is already disrupting the entertainment industry and improving the way films and content are produced and distributed.

The eye-catching title

The title of this op-ed is deliberately provocative to grab readers' attention. But it's also based on the idea that Hollywood, as an industry that has traditionally been resistant to change, needs to embrace Web3 technology if it wants to stay relevant.

Web3 technology offers a decentralized and democratized alternative to the centralized and hierarchical Hollywood model. It allows for greater transparency, accountability and inclusivity in an industry that has often been criticized for its lack of diversity and representation.

Conclusion in 3 points

  1. Web3 technology is already disrupting the entertainment industry and improving the way films and content are produced and distributed.
  2. Hollywood has traditionally been resistant to change, but it needs to embrace Web3 technology in order to stay relevant and keep up with the times.
  3. Web3 technology offers a decentralized and democratized alternative to the centralized and hierarchical Hollywood model, allowing for greater transparency, accountability and inclusivity.

Personal anecdotes and practical tips

As someone who has worked in the entertainment industry, I've seen firsthand how challenging it can be to break in and get noticed. But I believe that Web3 technology has the potential to level the playing field and give aspiring actors, filmmakers and content creators a better chance at success.

If you're an actor or filmmaker looking to leverage Web3 technology, my advice would be to start by exploring existing platforms and communities. Join decentralized social media platforms like Mastodon and Steemit, where you can connect with like-minded individuals and showcase your work.

You can also explore blockchain-based crowdfunding platforms like Kickstarter and Indiegogo, which are increasingly accepting cryptocurrency donations. And if you're interested in tokenizing your film or content, consider platforms like SingularDTV and Filmio.

Ultimately, the key to success in the Web3 entertainment industry is to be open-minded, creative and willing to embrace new technologies and ideas.
