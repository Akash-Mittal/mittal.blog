How AI Protects and Attacks Your Inbox

Stories and examples of how Artificial Intelligence is used in email security

Jane had just finished sending an important email to her boss. She had attached a confidential document and was about to hit the send button when she noticed an unusual warning. The email system had flagged her attachment as sensitive and asked if she really wanted to send it. Jane was confused as she had checked the document multiple times, and there seemed to be nothing wrong with it. After some investigation, she found out that her email provider had implemented AI-based security to prevent confidential documents from leaving the company's network. Without the AI system, Jane would have unintentionally leaked sensitive information and put the company at risk.

Email security is essential in today's digital age where phishing attacks, malware, and ransomware are prevalent. AI-based email security systems can detect and prevent such attacks. Here are some quantifiable examples of how AI is used for email security:

  • In 2020, Google blocked over 100 million phishing emails daily using AI
  • In a survey, 82% of organizations reported a reduction in email-based threats after implementing AI-based email security
  • Microsoft Office 365's AI system analyzes over 6.5 trillion threat signals daily to prevent malicious emails from reaching users' inboxes

How AI is Saving Your Inbox from Malicious Attacks

and Case Studies

AI-based email security systems are not foolproof and can sometimes produce false positives that can lead to emails being flagged as sensitive or spam. It can be frustrating for users who have to navigate through their inbox to find legitimate emails. However, these false positives are a small price to pay compared to the security risks of not having AI-based email security. Moreover, with regular usage, AI systems become more accurate in identifying and preventing threats.

One case study is the financial services firm Investec. They implemented an AI-based email security system that analyzes emails for any suspicious links or attachments. The AI system was able to detect and block several phishing emails, preventing a potential data breach. Investec reported that the AI system had saved the company over $400,000 in potential losses.

Practical Tips

Here are some practical tips to prevent email-based attacks:

  • Enable two-factor authentication on your email accounts
  • Be cautious when clicking on links or downloading attachments from suspicious emails
  • Regularly update your email security settings and keep your software up-to-date

  1. AI-based email security systems are essential in preventing malicious attacks
  2. AI systems can produce false positives, but the benefits outweigh the risks
  3. By following practical tips, users can prevent email-based attacks and ensure their inbox's security
