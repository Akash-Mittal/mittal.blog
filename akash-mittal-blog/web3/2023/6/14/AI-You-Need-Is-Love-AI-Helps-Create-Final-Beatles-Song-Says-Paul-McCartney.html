AI You Need Is Love: AI Helps Create ‘Final' Beatles Song, Says Paul McCartney



Image Credits https://www.forbes.com/sites/roberthart/2023/06/13/ai-you-need-is-love-artificial-intelligence-helps-create-final-beatles-song-paul-mccartney-says/

Story

Legendary music icon Paul McCartney recently revealed that Artificial Intelligence (AI) was used to isolate John Lennon's vocals from a "ropey" old demo tape to create a new, never-before-heard song by The Beatles. The AI technology, according to McCartney, helped create the band's "final" song in collaboration with Lennon.

Speaking in an interview, McCartney said that the old demo tape featured both him and Lennon but needed to be digitally processed to remove the noise and isolate Lennon's vocals. This is where the AI technology proved to be useful, enabling the Beatles to bring John Lennon's voice back from the past and create a new song that they could have worked on together if things had gone differently.

The release of the song showcases the potential of AI technology in the music industry, and the possibilities are limitless for what else it could achieve in the future.

Examples

This is not the first time AI technology has been used to create something unique in the music industry. Here are a few examples of how Artificial Intelligence has already made its mark -

  • AIVA (Artificial Intelligence Virtual Artist) - A startup that uses machine learning algorithms to create unique and original music. AIVA has already composed albums in numerous genres, including Classical, Pop, and Jazz.
  • Magenta - A collection of machine learning tools, developed by Google, which can assist musicians in writing new songs, producing music, and even creating new instruments.
  • Audiate - A tool that can separate the different components of a song, enabling users to isolate and manipulate individual tracks. It also helps to remove noise and other unwanted elements from recordings.
  • Sony CSL Research Laboratories - A research group that has been using AI to assist in the creation of new music since 1992.

These are just a few examples of the many ways in which AI is already disrupting the music industry, and it is only a matter of time before we see more. From aiding in songwriting to producing entire albums, the potential is endless.

Conclusion

The use of AI in the music industry is still in its early stages, but it has already shown that it has the potential to disrupt the scene entirely. From creating entirely new songs to aiding in the production process, AI technology has already made its mark on the industry, and it is only a matter of time before it becomes a staple tool in music studios worldwide.

The Beatles' new song is just a glimpse of what is to come, and as technology advances, we can expect more groundbreaking achievements in the industry. However, it is worth noting that as much as AI technology is useful and exciting, it can never fully replace human creativity and emotion. Ultimately, music is an art form, and it takes a creative mind and a human touch to produce something truly unique.

With that said, as AI technology continues to evolve and improve, there is no doubt that it will become an indispensable tool in the music industry, enabling musicians to create sounds and express emotion that may have been impossible otherwise. The possibilities are limitless, and we can't wait to see what the future holds.
