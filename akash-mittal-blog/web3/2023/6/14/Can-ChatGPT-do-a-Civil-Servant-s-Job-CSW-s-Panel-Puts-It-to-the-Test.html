Can ChatGPT do a Civil Servant's Job? CSW's Panel Puts It to the Test



Image Credits https://www.civilserviceworld.com/in-depth/article/chatgpt-civil-service-job-chatbot-ai-ministerial-pq-job-application-policy

As technology continues to advance, many wonder whether artificial intelligence will replace human workers. The Civil Service World's panel put a popular AI language model, ChatGPT, to the test. They asked a cross-section of officials to play around with ChatGPT and establish how good it is at some of the everyday tasks they perform in their job.

The Panel's Findings

The panel found that ChatGPT could perform some civil servants' jobs with ease, while struggling with others. Here are some examples:

  • Writing Emails and Reports: ChatGPT is a natural language AI model, which means it can write with relative ease. The officials found that ChatGPT was particularly good at writing simple reports and emails without any grammatical errors.
  • Answering Simple Queries: Many of the civil servants' jobs involve answering queries. The panel found that ChatGPT was excellent at providing quick answers to simple queries, such as holiday entitlements and tax information.
  • Identifying Complex Problems: Unfortunately, ChatGPT struggled when it came to identifying complex problems. For example, when asked to identify the root cause of a policy issue, the AI model failed to provide a coherent answer.

Despite the limitations of ChatGPT, the panel believes that the AI model could be very useful as a support tool for civil servants. For example, ChatGPT could be used to generate draft reports or answer simple queries, freeing up civil servants' time to focus on more complex issues.
