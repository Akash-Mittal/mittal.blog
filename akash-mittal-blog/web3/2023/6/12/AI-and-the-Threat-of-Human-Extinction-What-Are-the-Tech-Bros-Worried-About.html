AI and the Threat of "Human Extinction": What Are the Tech-Bros Worried About?



Image Credits https://www.salon.com/2023/06/11/ai-and-the-of-human-extinction-what-are-the-tech-bros-worried-about-its-not-you-and-me/

Imagine a world where robots have taken over. You wake up in the morning, only to find that your job has been replaced by an algorithm. You try to go to the grocery store, but all the cashiers and baggers have been replaced by machines. You watch the news, only to hear that some AI system has become sentient and is now ruling the world. Is this the future we are headed towards? Are we really on the brink of "human extinction" because of AI?

Some tech-bros seem to think so. They warn us of the dangers of AI and urge us to take action before it's too late. They claim that AI has the potential to become so advanced that it will surpass human intelligence, and that it will inevitably turn against us, either intentionally or unintentionally, leading to our demise.

But what are these tech-bros really worried about? Surely they don't care about the fate of the average citizen. After all, they are the ones who are building and profiting from these advanced AI systems. So why the sudden concern for our well-being?

Perhaps the answer lies in the fact that what they are really worried about is the threat to their own existence. They fear that if AI becomes too powerful, it will render their expertise and skills useless, and they will be replaced by machines. In other words, they are not worried about "human extinction" per se, but rather about their own extinction as a privileged class of experts in the tech industry.

Take for example the case of Elon Musk, who is perhaps the most vocal of all the tech-bros on the issue of AI. He has warned us time and time again of the dangers of AI, claiming that it is "more dangerous than nuclear weapons" and that it represents "an existential threat" to humanity. But what is he really worried about? Is it the fate of humanity as a whole, or is it the threat to his own position as the top dog in the tech industry?

It's easy to see why Musk is so concerned about AI. As the CEO of Tesla and SpaceX, he has built his entire career on the premise that he is creating the technology of the future. But what happens if AI surpasses his own creations? What happens if the machines become smarter than him? His entire identity and sense of importance would be threatened.

Of course, it's not just Musk who is worried about his own extinction. Many other tech-bros have similar fears. They worry that they will become obsolete, that their skills and expertise will no longer be needed in a world where machines do everything better. They worry that they will be left behind in a world where the machines are in charge.

But the truth is, their fears are unfounded. AI is not going to lead to "human extinction". It is true that AI has the potential to become extremely powerful, and that we need to be careful about how we develop and use it. But there is no evidence to suggest that AI will inevitably turn against us and wipe us out. In fact, there is plenty of evidence to suggest that AI can be a force for good, if we use it wisely.

For example, AI can be used to solve some of the most pressing problems of our time, such as climate change, poverty, and disease. It can help us make better decisions, optimize systems, and improve our quality of life. It can also help us better understand ourselves and the world around us.

So instead of worrying about "human extinction", let's focus on using AI to create a better future for all of us. Let's use it to solve the problems that we face, and to build a world that is more just, equitable, and sustainable. Let's not let the fears of a privileged few hold us back from realizing the full potential of this revolutionary technology.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the claims that AI threatens the extinction of our species are overblown. While it is true that AI has the potential to become extremely powerful, there is no evidence to suggest that it will inevitably turn against us and wipe us out. The real concern of tech-bros is not "human extinction" per se, but rather the threat to their own privilege and expertise in the tech industry. Instead of giving in to these fears, let's focus on using AI to create a better future for all of us.
