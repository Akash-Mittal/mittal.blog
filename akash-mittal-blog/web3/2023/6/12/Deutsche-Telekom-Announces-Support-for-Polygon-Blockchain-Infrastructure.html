Deutsche Telekom Announces Support for Polygon Blockchain Infrastructure



Image Credits https://www.vanillaplus.com/2023/06/12/80099-deutsche-telekom-announces-support-for-polygon-blockchain-infrastructure/

A Story of Adoption

In recent years, blockchain technology has been gaining more traction as various sectors begin to realise its capabilities beyond cryptocurrencies. One company that has seemingly recognised the potential of blockchain is Deutsche Telekom, Germany's largest telecommunications firm. With a user base of over 100 million people, Deutsche Telekom is pushing further into the blockchain industry by announcing its support for Polygon, an Ethereum layer-2 scaling platform.

Examples of Collaboration

Deutsche Telekom's decision to support Polygon is indicative of the growing mainstream adoption of blockchain technology. However, this is not the first instance of collaboration between telecommunications companies and blockchain firms. In 2019, South Korea's largest telecom firm, SK Telecom, announced that it was partnering with blockchain platform, Terra. Under this partnership, both firms would be working together to develop a blockchain-based payment platform. The collaboration aims to streamline online payments for both consumers and merchants, all while leveraging the security and transparency benefits of blockchain technology.

Another example of how telecommunications companies are embracing blockchain comes from Spain. Telefonica, the country's biggest telephone operator, has recently partnered with Wanchain, a blockchain platform that offers cross-chain integration. Through this partnership, Telefonica hopes to enable their users to make secure blockchain transactions. These transactions will be carried out through Telefonica's much-vaunted blockchain functionality known as TrustOS. Telefonica's move to embrace blockchain has been considered to be one of the more progressive moves by any telecoms firm in the world.

  1. The adoption of blockchain technology by telecom companies is becoming more mainstream with each passing year.
  2. Deutsche Telekom's announcement of support for Polygon is a clear indication of this trend.
  3. Through these collaborations, we are witnessing the potential for blockchain technology to revolutionise payment systems and secure communications in the telecommunications industry.
