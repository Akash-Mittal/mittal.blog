ChatGPT Tricks Lawyers into Citing Bogus Cases - Minnesota Lawyer

The Story

Two lawyers had to face the wrath of a judge after being caught including fictitious legal research in their court filing. The lawyers expressed their apologies and shifted the blame to ChatGPT. They claimed that the AI-based research tool had tricked them into citing bogus cases, and that they were not aware of it until they were confronted by the judge.

This incident has raised concerns about the reliability and accuracy of AI-based research tools like ChatGPT, and the responsibility of lawyers in ensuring the authenticity of their legal research.

Examples

There have been many instances where lawyers have relied on ChatGPT and other AI-based research tools to conduct their legal research. While these tools can be useful in saving time and effort, they may not always provide accurate information. Here are some examples:

  • In one case, a lawyer cited a law that did not exist, based on the advice of an AI tool.
  • In another case, a lawyer cited a case that had been overturned, based on outdated information provided by an AI tool.
  • In yet another case, a lawyer cited a case that was not relevant to the issue at hand, based on a flawed analysis by an AI tool.

These examples show that relying solely on AI-based research tools can be risky, as it can lead to inaccurate information being presented in court. Therefore, it is important for lawyers to double-check the information provided by AI tools and ensure its accuracy before citing it in their court filings.

Conclusion

The incident involving ChatGPT and the two lawyers has highlighted the need for caution when using AI-based research tools. While these tools can be efficient and save time, they are not foolproof and can make mistakes. Lawyers must take responsibility for the accuracy of their legal research and double-check any information provided by AI tools before using it in court.

Here are three key takeaways:

  1. AI-based research tools like ChatGPT can be useful, but they are not always accurate.
  2. Lawyers must take responsibility for the accuracy of their legal research and double-check any information provided by AI tools before using it in court.
  3. While AI-based research tools can save time, relying solely on them can be risky, as it can lead to inaccurate information being presented in court.
