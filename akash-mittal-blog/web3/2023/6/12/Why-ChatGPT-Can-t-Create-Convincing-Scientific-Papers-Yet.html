Why ChatGPT Can't Create Convincing Scientific Papers Yet



Image Credits https://www.livescience.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/ai-chatbot-chatgpt-cant-create-convincing-scientific-papers-yet

Chatbots have become increasingly popular in recent times. They're used for a variety of purposes, from customer service to personal therapy. However, there are limitations to what chatbots can do. A recent study shows that ChatGPT, an AI chatbot, can't create convincing scientific papers.

The Story

The creators of ChatGPT, OpenAI, trained their artificial intelligence model on a vast amount of text from the internet. The aim was to develop a language model that could respond to text-based prompts in a human-like manner. However, the researchers didn't just stop at that. They also tested if ChatGPT could create convincing scientific papers. Unfortunately, the results were not as good as they expected.

In a study published in PLOS ONE, researchers created eight versions of scientific papers, four of which were fake, generated by ChatGPT, and the other four were authentic papers written by humans. They then asked 198 scientists to identify which papers were fake. The results showed that the scientists could detect the ChatGPT-generated fake studies more than 99% of the time.

Examples

Here are some of the reasons why ChatGPT failed to convince scientists with fake papers.

  • Unrealistic results - In one study, ChatGPT generated results that seemed too good to be true, which is often a red flag in scientific research.
  • Inconsistent research methods - ChatGPT's fake papers often had inconsistent research methods, which made it difficult for scientists to trust the results.
  • Unnatural language - Despite ChatGPT's ability to respond in a human-like manner, the chatbot still struggled to use scientific jargon correctly, making the language in the papers seem unnatural.

Conclusion

The limitations of ChatGPT in creating authentic scientific papers stem from the fact that it was not specifically designed for that purpose. The model was trained mainly on internet text, which is not necessarily scientific in nature. Additionally, scientific research involves complex methodologies, which ChatGPT may not be able to comprehend fully. Therefore, while chatbots are becoming increasingly popular, they still have limitations and should not replace human scientists in creating authentic scientific research. Instead, chatbots can be used to assist in certain aspects of research, such as data analysis and organization.

Three Points to Consider

  • ChatGPT, an AI chatbot, can't create convincing scientific papers as shown in a study published in PLOS ONE.
  • The limitations of ChatGPT in creating authentic scientific papers stem from the fact that it was not specifically designed for that purpose.
  • Chatbots have limitations and should not replace human scientists in creating authentic scientific research.
