When AI Doom Mongers Try to Scare Us All Over Again



Image Credits https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2023/jun/12/lost-job-learn-code-ai-humans-skills

It was a bright Monday morning when I received the news that I had been laid off. My heart sank as I packed up my things and walked out of the office building for the last time. I had no idea what I was going to do next.

But then, I decided to take matters into my own hands. I had always been interested in technology, so I decided to learn to code. It wasn't easy, but I worked tirelessly, watching online tutorials, practicing with coding exercises, and building my own projects. Eventually, I landed a job as a junior developer, and it was one of the best decisions I've ever made.

However, lately, I've been hearing a lot of talk about how AI is going to take over our jobs and render us all useless. The doom mongers in Silicon Valley want us to believe that humans are predictable and our skills replaceable. But as someone who has experienced the power of learning new skills, I know this is nonsense.

Examples of the Power of Learning New Skills

There are countless examples of people who have reinvented themselves and overcome adversity by learning new skills. One such example is Amanda Pasciucco, a licensed therapist who lost her job during the pandemic. Instead of giving up, she decided to take her skills online and started a virtual therapy business. Now, she's helping more people than ever before and has become an advocate for mental health.

Another example is Dara Khosrowshahi, the CEO of Uber. He was born in Iran and moved to the US when he was a teenager. After graduating from Brown University, he worked in finance before transitioning to tech and eventually becoming the CEO of one of the world's largest ride-hailing companies.

These stories show that with determination and hard work, we can learn new skills and become successful in any field. We are not replaceable, and our potential is limitless.

Conclusion

As AI and automation continue to advance, it's easy to feel intimidated and scared. But we must remember that we have the power to overcome and adapt. We can learn new skills, reinvent ourselves, and continue to grow. The future may be uncertain, but our passion and perseverance will always guide us towards success.
