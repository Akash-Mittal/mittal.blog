Have you ever searched for a product online and then suddenly see ads for that product everywhere you go online? Or do you sometimes find that websites you visit remember your login information even after you close your browser? If yes, then you have probably encountered HTTP cookies. An HTTP cookie (also known as a web cookie, browser cookie, or simply a cookie) is a small text file that a website or web application saves on the user's computer or mobile device when they visit the site. Cookies are designed to be a reliable mechanism for websites to remember stateful information (such as items added to a shopping cart in an online store) or to record the user's browsing activity (such as which pages they visited and when). When a user visits a website that uses cookies, the website sends a cookie to the user's browser. The browser stores the cookie on the user's computer or mobile device. The next time the user visits the same website, the site reads the cookie and recognizes the user. This allows the website to remember the user's preferences or login information, and personalize the user's experience. Cookies are not harmful to the user's computer or mobile device. They cannot be used to identify you personally, and they cannot carry viruses or install malware on your computer. However, the use of cookies has raised concerns about online privacy and online tracking.
