ChatGPT's Limited Humor: A Study



Image Credits https://www.pcmag.com/news/chatgpts-humor-barely-extends-beyond-the-same-25-jokes-researchers-find

Have you ever chatted with a chatbot and tried to make it tell you a joke? If you have, you probably have noticed that chatbots' sense of humor is not exactly sophisticated. According to a recent study, ChatGPT, one of the most famous chatbots, seems to rely on the same 25 jokes over and over again.

ChatGPT is a language model developed by OpenAI that can answer questions, translate languages, and generate text. It has been trained with a huge amount of data from the internet, but when it comes to humor, its output is surprisingly limited. Researchers at the University of Edinburgh conducted a study to test ChatGPT's ability to tell jokes and found that the chatbot's humor relies on a small number of templates that are filled out with different nouns and verbs.

How the Study Was Conducted

The researchers used a dataset of 221 jokes that had been rated by humans for their funniness. They then asked ChatGPT to generate jokes based on the same templates as the dataset jokes. For example, one template was "Why did the chicken cross the road? To get to the other side." ChatGPT would generate jokes using the same structure, but with different animals or objects.

Overall, ChatGPT was able to generate jokes that were funny about 20% of the time, according to the human raters. However, the chatbot's jokes tended to score lower than human-generated jokes with the same structure.

  • Example of a human joke using the chicken template: "Why did the chicken join the band? Because it already had drumsticks."
  • Example of a ChatGPT joke using the same template: "Why did the politician cross the road? To get to the debate."

Why Chatbots Struggle with Humor

One explanation for why chatbots like ChatGPT struggle with humor is that humor is a complex form of language that often relies on cultural references, wordplay, and unexpected twists. Chatbots may have access to a lot of data, but they lack the ability to understand the nuances of human language and society.

Another factor is that humor is subjective. What one person finds funny may not be funny to someone else. Chatbots, being programmed by humans, can only generate jokes based on the data they have been fed and the rules they have been given.

Implications for Chatbot Technology

While the limitations of ChatGPT's humor may seem like a small issue, it actually has important implications for the field of chatbot technology. Chatbots are becoming more common in customer service, healthcare, and other industries, and they need to be able to communicate effectively with humans in order to be useful.

If chatbots are unable to generate unique and nuanced responses to human queries, they may end up frustrating users and reinforcing negative stereotypes about AI.

One potential solution is to incorporate more advanced natural language processing and machine learning techniques into chatbot development. By allowing chatbots to learn from human feedback and improve over time, they may eventually be able to develop their own sense of humor and understand the complexities of human language.

In conclusion, ChatGPT's limited humor is a reminder that AI still has a long way to go before it can fully understand human language and culture. However, with continued research and development, chatbots may one day be able to tell us jokes that are truly funny.
