Humor Largely Restricted to the Same 25 Jokes



Image Credits https://themessenger.com/news/chatgpts-humor-largely-restricted-to-the-same-25-jokes-study-finds

A Study by The Messenger

Have you ever heard a joke and thought to yourself, "I've heard that one before"? According to a recent study by The Messenger, ChatGPT's humor is largely restricted to the same 25 jokes.

The Experiment

As part of the experiment, ChatGPT was asked to generate a joke 1,008 times. The researchers were looking to see if there was a wide range of humor being produced or if there was repetition in the jokes.

The Results

The results were surprising. ChatGPT produced only 25 unique jokes. The rest of the jokes were some variation of those 25 jokes. The researchers found that the majority of the jokes were about animals, food, or puns.

The Explanation

So, why is ChatGPT restricted to the same 25 jokes? The researchers believe that it has to do with the way the AI was trained. The machine learning algorithms that ChatGPT uses were likely trained on a limited dataset of jokes that were predominantly about animals, food, or puns.

Examples

Here are some examples of the 25 jokes that ChatGPT produced:

  1. Why did the chicken cross the road? To get to the other side.
  2. Why did the tomato turn red? Because it saw the salad dressing.
  3. Why did the scarecrow win an award? Because he was outstanding in his field.
  4. What do you get when you cross a snowman and a shark? Frostbite.
  5. Why don't scientists trust atoms? Because they make up everything.

Conclusion

While it's amusing to see what ChatGPT comes up with, it's clear that its humor is limited to a small set of jokes. It's possible that as the AI continues to learn and be trained on a wider range of data, it will be able to produce a wider range of humor.

Three Points to Takeaway

  1. The humor produced by ChatGPT is largely restricted to a set of 25 jokes.
  2. The AI was likely trained on a limited dataset of jokes, which is why its humor is restricted.
  3. As the AI continues to learn and be trained on a wider range of data, it will be able to produce a wider range of humor.

As someone who has interacted with ChatGPT, I have definitely noticed the repetition in its humor. It can be amusing at first, but it quickly becomes predictable. However, I do think that as the technology improves, we'll see AI-generated humor that is both creative and unexpected.
