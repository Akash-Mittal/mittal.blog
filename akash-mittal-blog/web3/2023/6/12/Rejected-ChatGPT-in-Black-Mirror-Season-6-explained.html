Rejected ChatGPT in Black Mirror Season 6 Explained

Why the ChatGPT concept failed to make the cut by Black Mirror creator Charlie Brooker.

The ChatGPT Proposal

Black Mirror is known for its ability to touch on a wide range of technological advancements which can either harm or benefit the world. It comes as no surprise when an idea for AI was introduced for Season 6 of the show in the form of ChatGPT.

ChatGPT is an AI designed to mimic human conversation with the assistance of algorithms and machine learning. Its purpose is to assist people in talking through their everyday lives and even aid with relationships. However, Charlie Brooker, the show creator had other ideas.

Brooker revealed that the ChatGPT concept was rejected as it was just "looking up all the synopses of Black Mirror episodes and sort of mushing them together." He explained that creating an AI that simulates a human conversation would not work as it did not align with the show's interests. The AI concept is not inspired enough for the show's standards.

This is not the first time Black Mirror has scrapped proposed concepts for upcoming seasons. In the past, concepts that didn't meet the show's high standards were rejected. It's easy to see why Black Mirror is one of the most innovative shows on television when their standards are so high.

Why a Poor Concept is Detrimental to the Show

A poor concept could be detrimental to a TV show that prides itself on its forward-thinking. Black Mirror has proven to be a master of futuristic, technological experimentation and can't afford to compromise on this. The show's brand is associated with its ability to explore and predict how our increasingly complex relationship with technology will affect us.

Viewers expect each installment to be a surprise and engage them intellectually. The show challenges our perception of what is possible while forcing us to question the potential impact of new technologies. A poorly thought out concept could lack imagination, ultimately leading to a weak episode. Creating great television is a tough job, and half-baked ideas won't do.

Brooker adds to that the ChatGPT idea was created without much inspiration and did not offer the same futuristic appeal as seen with the show's previous episodes. Black Mirror aims to push the limits of viewers' imagination, keeping the audience engaged and thinking long after the episode has ended.

The importance of sticking to a TV show's standards

All shows have concepts that are not up to standard, but the difference between this and great television is knowing what to keep and what to cut. In the case of Black Mirror, Brooker believes the ChatGPT idea was not good enough for the show to accept. It would not be frowned upon by critics but garner negative reception from fans.

This is why it is crucial to stick to the show's standards, even if it means cutting something that writers and producers have put countless days and months into creating. Great TV shows understand that just because something sounds good, it doesn't mean it will translate into great television. As Brooker has stated, the ChatGPT concept was lazy and lacked distinctive qualities that could be associated with Black Mirror.

The Benefits of Rejecting a Poor Concept

Instead of creating the ChatGPT idea, the team managed to conceive the "Bandersnatch", where viewers could choose what happens or which way the story goes. This proved to be one of the groundbreaking episodes of the entire series, showcasing interactivity and immersion to the viewers. It was a resounding success and a great illustration of what Black Mirror stands for.

The rejection of poor ideas often leads to much breakthrough as well. For instance, every idea that doesn't make it into the show potentially inspires a better and unique concept. Turned down ideas and projects can ignite an avalanche of innovation, instilling the show with a fresh perspective that might not have been there at first.

As Brooker stated earlier, "we don't want to do something that someone else has already done." Therefore, it is essential to hear several ideas and settle for the best. This way, each episode of Black Mirror continues to attract new viewers and retain its existing fans.

The importance of standing apart

Black Mirror has mastered the art of being unique. The show stands apart from others in its ability to delve deeply into relevant topics and provide a new perspective to viewers. The perfect combination of great writing, excellent visuals, and exceptional editing makes it an outstanding show.

It's not just a show focused on technology alone, but one that strives to showcase the world through a futuristic lens. Given that viewers are intrigued by the show's unique approach, it's essential for Brooker and his team to keep trying harder and keep abreast of technology's evolving terrain.

By keeping a creative mindset and exploring science fiction, Black Mirror can offer viewers thrilling and exciting features that aren't seen on any other show. It is, therefore, a testament to the show's success and resilience, even with the ChatGPT's rejection.
