The Case for Bottom-Up AI



Image Credits https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2023/6/12/the-case-for-bottom-up-ai

When I was a kid, I used to read science fiction novels that imagined a future where machines were smarter than humans, and I was terrified. But as I grew older and learned more about artificial intelligence, I came to see things differently.

Today, I believe that AI can help us build a more inclusive, innovative, and democratic society, but only if we take a bottom-up approach.

A Bottom-Up Approach

What do I mean by a bottom-up approach? I mean that we should focus on building AI systems that are open source, decentralized, and based on collaboration rather than competition.

Open source AI means that the code is freely available for anyone to use, modify, and redistribute. This approach provides transparency and accountability, and it ensures that AI is not controlled by a small group of powerful companies or individuals.

Decentralized AI means that the data and processing power are distributed across many different devices, rather than being concentrated in a few data centers. This approach provides resilience and security, and it ensures that AI is not vulnerable to cyberattacks or system failures.

Collaborative AI means that different AI systems can work together to solve problems, rather than competing with each other for resources or data. This approach provides diversity and adaptability, and it ensures that AI is not limited by narrow or biased perspectives.

An Inclusive Society

One of the main benefits of bottom-up AI is that it can help us build a more inclusive society.

For example, open source AI can enable people from all over the world to participate in the development of AI systems, regardless of their background, education, or resources. This approach can help to reduce the digital divide and ensure that AI is not biased towards certain groups or interests.

Moreover, decentralized AI can enable people in remote or underserved areas to access AI services, even if they don't have reliable internet or electricity. This approach can help to improve healthcare, education, and other essential services in these areas, and it can empower local communities to solve their own problems.

Finally, collaborative AI can enable people with different perspectives or worldviews to work together on common goals, even if they don't share the same language, culture, or values. This approach can help to foster creativity, innovation, and social cohesion, and it can lead to new insights and discoveries that benefit all of us.

An Innovative Society

Another benefit of bottom-up AI is that it can help us build a more innovative society.

For example, open source AI can enable researchers, developers, and entrepreneurs to build on existing AI systems, rather than reinventing the wheel every time. This approach can help to speed up innovation and reduce duplication, and it can facilitate the sharing of best practices and knowledge.

Moreover, decentralized AI can enable experimentation and prototyping at a small scale, without the need for large investments or infrastructure. This approach can help to test new ideas and applications before scaling them up, and it can allow for more agile and responsive development.

Finally, collaborative AI can enable interdisciplinary and cross-sectoral collaboration, bringing together people from different fields and sectors to work on common challenges. This approach can help to bridge the gap between academia and industry, and it can facilitate the transfer of knowledge and skills across different domains.

A Democratic Society

A third benefit of bottom-up AI is that it can help us build a more democratic society.

For example, open source AI can enable public scrutiny and participation in the development and deployment of AI systems, rather than leaving these decisions to a small group of experts or policymakers. This approach can help to ensure that AI is aligned with societal goals and values, and it can increase public trust and confidence in AI.

Moreover, decentralized AI can enable local communities and stakeholders to control and shape AI systems that affect their lives, rather than relying on distant and impersonal authorities. This approach can help to strengthen community resilience and participation, and it can create a sense of ownership and responsibility for AI systems.

Finally, collaborative AI can enable diverse and inclusive decision-making processes, rather than being limited by the biases or interests of a few individuals or groups. This approach can help to ensure that AI systems are fair, transparent, and accountable, and it can prevent the concentration of power and wealth in the hands of a few.
