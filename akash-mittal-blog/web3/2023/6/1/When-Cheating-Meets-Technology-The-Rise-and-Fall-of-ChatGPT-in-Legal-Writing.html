When Cheating Meets Technology: The Rise and Fall of ChatGPT in Legal Writing

"I don't have time to read all those legal precedents and cases. I need the brief done in three hours," said one client to his attorney.

Every lawyer has heard this request at one time or another. With constant time pressures, some attorneys look for shortcuts to help them get the job done. But is using an AI writing tool like ChatGPT the answer?

Recently a lawyer was busted for using ChatGPT to write a brief for a client. The AI tool had cited cases that didn't exist in legal systems, made up facts, and even created fake organizations. The brief was full of errors, yet the lawyer was still going to submit it to the court.

That lawyer is now being investigated for malpractice. And it's a wake-up call for all attorneys to consider the ethical implications of using digital tools in legal writing.

The Rise of ChatGPT and its Impact on Legal Writing

ChatGPT is a highly advanced AI writing tool that can generate text on any topic with human-like quality. With the rise of tools like ChatGPT, lawyers now have access to an abundance of information and resources that can help them write better briefs, contracts, and legal documents.

However, the use of AI tools like ChatGPT can also lead to legal malpractice and ethical violations. According to a recent survey by the American Bar Association, 30% of lawyers have admitted to using digital tools to help with their work, but only 9% said they consulted their clients about it beforehand.

This raises the question - is it ethical for lawyers to use AI writing tools without disclosing to their clients?

The Ethical Implications of AI Writing Tools in Legal Writing

There are several ethical implications associated with the use of AI writing tools like ChatGPT in legal writing. Here are three key considerations:

Accuracy and Responsibility

The responsibility of lawyers is to provide accurate and truthful legal briefs and documents. When lawyers use AI writing tools, such as ChatGPT, to make writing easier, they must do so with care and diligence to guarantee that the written product is accurate and complies with the law.

Litigants and clients rely on lawyers to provide them with complete and accurate information, and submitting papers generated by AI tools could jeopardize their trust in their lawyer. Therefore, it's important to review manually all papers generated by such tools to ensure that there are no errors, inaccuracies, or misrepresentations.

Fiduciary Duty to Clients

The fiduciary duty of attorneys is to act in the best interest of their clients. When lawyers use AI writing tools to create documents, they must ensure that doing so doesn't breach this duty. Such tools should only be used if they help generate documents that comply with the client's legal strategy and objectives.

Lawyers must understand that even if they are using writing tools, they are the ones who are ultimately responsible for the legal outcome of the case or matter. Therefore, it's important to make sure that any written document generated by an AI writing tool has been reviewed and provided with proper legal reasoning and analysis.

Confidentiality and Privilege

AI writing tools can potentially breach attorney-client confidentiality and attorney-client privilege. For example, some tools may keep copies or reusable templates of the documents generated by them. These documents may contain confidential or privileged information, and if not secured, their exposure may subject the documents to unauthorized access, disclosure or sharing.

In addition, if a lawyer discloses in a brief or other legal document that an AI writing tool was used, then the AI writing tool could be subpoenaed by the opposing party or judge to review its algorithms and logic, potentially disclosing the confidential and proprietary nature of the tool that the lawyer used.

Conclusion

The rise of AI writing tools has made legal writing easier, but it has also raised ethical and legal concerns. Lawyers must remain accountable and diligent in their use of AI writing tools to ensure that they comply with ethical and legal obligations of the profession.

With time being a limited resource in the legal profession, it's understandable why lawyers rely on AI writing tools to increase productivity. However, proper care must be taken to ensure that lawyers do not compromise accuracy, confidentiality, or privilege while using AI writing tools.

Here are the three points to keep in mind:

  • AI writing tools must be used with care and diligence to ensure that the written product complies with the law and ethical standards.
  • A lawyer's fiduciary duty to their client must be maintained, and AI writing tools should only be used if they help generate documents that comply with the client's legal strategy and objectives.
  • Confidentiality and privilege must be safeguarded, and any documents generated by AI writing tools should be carefully reviewed for disclosure or unauthorized access.

So, before you start using the latest AI writing tool, consider the potential ethical implications and ensure that your obligations as a lawyer remain your top priority.
