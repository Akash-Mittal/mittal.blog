C3 ai Stock Falls After Earnings Outlook Can't Match Hype

Once again, hype is not enough to keep a share price up, as C3 ai experienced its worst week since its IPO in December 2020.

Image Source: Unsplash

The artificial intelligence software company, C3 ai, was expected to continue its upward trajectory after reporting strong earnings in the previous quarters. However, that was not the case after the company's earnings outlook did not match market expectations, leading to a sharp decline in its stock price.

During the past week, the C3 ai share price suffered a significant drop of more than 20% after losing $6.5 billion in market value. The company reported disappointing guidance for the current quarter, citing longer sales cycles and tougher competition with well-established rivals as reasons for the lack of projected growth.

As a result, analysts have downgraded their recommendations on C3 ai's stock from a "buy" to a "hold" rating, while the company's management expressed disappointment in the market's reaction to the guidance, explaining that the softness in the current quarter was due to time delays, not lost deals.

Numbers don't Lie

The numbers speak for themselves. C3 ai's third-quarter revenue was $52.8 million, a 28% growth rate year over year and higher than expected, while its net loss reached $28.3 million, compared to a $16.7 million loss in the same quarter last year. Despite this, the company's guidance for the current quarter predicted slower revenue growth, lower margins, and higher operating expenses, indicating that profitability may be further away than expected.

Furthermore, C3 ai's customer concentration risk is still high, with its top customer accounting for 35% of its annual revenue, and the company's negative free cash flow remains an issue as it has been for some time. The company has made numerous acquisitions in the past year, which could have contributed to its lack of profitability, leading some investors to believe that the management's focus may be on growth rather than profitability for the time being.

What's Next?

While some investors have expressed their doubts about C3 ai's ability to deliver on its promises, others believe that the company's technology is incredibly scalable, and it may lead to even more opportunities as time passes. However, the current situation shows that the market may have expected too much from the company too soon, and without a significant change in its fundamentals, the C3 ai share price may have a hard time making a comeback in the short term.

  • Investors should keep an eye on C3 ai's customer concentration and diversification strategies, which could minimize its dependency on a single customer and reduce the risks associated with having such a large proportion of its revenue coming from a single customer.
  • The company's ability to improve its profitability and cash flow is also critical, especially after its recent acquisition spree. Investors will be looking for signs of progress in these areas over the coming quarters.
  • Lastly, it will be interesting to see how C3 ai competes with larger, more established players in the AI software industry over the long term, and how it adapts to new market conditions and changing customer needs.

The Bottom Line

It's no surprise that hype alone cannot sustain a share price forever. C3 ai's recent performance showed that the market still values profitability and sustainability over growth potential alone. The company has a lot of potential, but it must deliver on its promises, and the recent guidance has not prompted investors to believe that it will happen anytime soon.

Investors should remain cautious regarding C3 ai's stock price in the short term until it can demonstrate good fundamentals and the ability to deliver sustained growth and profitability over time.
