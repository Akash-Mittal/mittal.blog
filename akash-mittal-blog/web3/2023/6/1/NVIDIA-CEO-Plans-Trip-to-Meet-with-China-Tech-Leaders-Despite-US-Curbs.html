NVIDIA CEO Plans Trip to Meet with China Tech Leaders Despite US Curbs

China's technology industry is on the rise, and it is no secret that many companies from around the world are looking to expand their reach in this market. One such company is NVIDIA, the graphics processing unit manufacturer which has made headlines with their cutting-edge technology. Despite the ongoing trade war between the US and China, NVIDIA's CEO, Jensen Huang, is planning a trip to China to meet with tech leaders. This move has raised eyebrows, but it could be a game-changer for the company.

An Interesting Story

In 2019, NVIDIA announced that it has been selected to develop and deliver the AI supercomputer for the Chinese Academy of Sciences. The new supercomputer, named "Tsinghua AI," is capable of processing over 1.5 exaflops, making it one of the fastest supercomputers in the world. However, this announcement was met with criticism from some US politicians who alleged that NVIDIA's technology could be used for military purposes. This led to the company being added to the US government's Entity List, which restricted their ability to export technology to China.

Despite this setback, NVIDIA has continued to invest in China. In December 2020, the company announced that it would be building a new research center in the country, which will focus on developing new technologies in areas such as AI and autonomous vehicles. It is clear that NVIDIA sees a lot of potential in China's tech market, and now the company's CEO is planning to visit the country to meet with industry leaders.

Quantifiable Examples

China is currently the world's largest market for gaming, and according to a report by Newzoo, the country's gaming industry is expected to reach $53.6 billion in revenue by the end of 2021. This presents a huge opportunity for NVIDIA, whose GPUs are used in many gaming devices. If the company can successfully expand its reach in China, it could see a significant boost in revenue.

In addition to gaming, China is also a major player in the AI industry. According to a report by IDC, China's AI market is expected to reach $12.3 billion by the end of 2024. As a leading player in the AI industry, NVIDIA is well-positioned to capitalize on this growth. By building partnerships with Chinese companies and investing in research in the country, the company can expand its reach in the AI market and solidify its position as a top AI provider.
