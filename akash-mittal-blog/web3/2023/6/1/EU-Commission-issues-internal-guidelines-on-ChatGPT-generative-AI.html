EU Commission issues internal guidelines on ChatGPT generative AI

It's 2025, and you're using a new personal assistant to help you complete your daily tasks. As you speak to it, you notice that it always has the most up-to-date information and can even predict your needs before you ask for them. This assistant uses a generative AI model, known as ChatGPT, which has been trained to understand human language and produce responses that are both accurate and natural.

The use of generative AI models such as ChatGPT is becoming more prevalent, which is why the European Commission has issued internal guidelines on their development and use. These guidelines aim to ensure that such models are developed and used in a responsible, ethical manner while also promoting innovation and growth in the AI industry.
