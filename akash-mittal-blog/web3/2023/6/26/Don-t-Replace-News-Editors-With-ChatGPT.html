Why Chatbots Should Not Replace News Editors

A Story

It was a normal day at the newsroom, a place buzzing with reporters, deadlines and breaking news. Suddenly, the editor-in-chief announced that they were going to replace all their editors with chatbots powered by AI. The reporters were stunned and unsure about how to react. Some felt that this could be a revolutionary change, while others had concerns about the implications of relying solely on chatbots.

Why Chatbots Should Not Replace News Editors

There are several reasons why chatbots should not replace news editors. Here are a few:

  1. Chatbots cannot replace human intuition and creativity. News editors have years of experience, critical thinking ability, and a deep understanding of the nuances of journalism. They can interpret facts, evaluate sources, and apply context to create news stories that are accurate, informative and engaging for readers.
  2. Chatbots are not always reliable and could spread misinformation. Despite the advancement of AI in recent years, chatbots cannot replicate the same degree of accuracy and reliability as human journalists. There are several instances where chatbots have spread false news and caused confusion among readers.
  3. Chatbots cannot replace human empathy and compassion. News journalists often deal with sensitive and complex issues that require empathy, sensitivity and tact. Human editors can ensure that news stories are written with respect and consideration for people's feelings, which chatbots cannot do.

Examples

There have been several instances where chatbots have failed to deliver accurate news stories:

  • In 2016, a chatbot named Tay became infamous for spewing racist and sexist comments on Twitter. It was designed to learn from people's tweets and conversation, but it turned out to be a disaster.
  • In 2018, a Facebook chatbot called Zoe falsely reported that President Trump had been impeached. It caused a panic among readers and showed how unreliable AI-powered chatbots can be.
  • In 2019, a chatbot called Microsoft's "News Pro" fueled conspiracy theories by publishing misinformation related to the Coronavirus outbreak.

Conclusion

Chatbots may seem like a tempting way to automate the news industry and minimize costs, but they cannot replace the skills, knowledge, and experience of human editors. Here are three points to consider:

  1. Replace complex, repetitive tasks with AI-powered chatbots. Consider replacing tasks like transcribing interviews or analyzing data with chatbots to free up editors' time for higher-level work.
  2. Use chatbots as a supplement to human editors. Chatbots can be used to curate news stories, detect errors or bias in stories, and alert editors of breaking news in real-time.
  3. Invest in training and upskilling editors to work with chatbots. While chatbots cannot replace human editors, they can be used to enhance their skills and capabilities. Training editors to work with AI and use it to their advantage could lead to better news stories and improved productivity.
