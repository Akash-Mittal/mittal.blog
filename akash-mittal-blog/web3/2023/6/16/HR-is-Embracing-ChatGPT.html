HR is Embracing ChatGPT, But Still Wary of Risks



Image Credits https://www.shrm.org/resourcesandtools/hr-topics/technology/pages/hr-is-embracing-chatgpt-but-still-wary-of-risks.aspx

During a recent Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM) session on ChatGPT, HR professionals and company leaders shared their experiences and concerns with adopting the technology.

One story shared by a participant described how their company had implemented ChatGPT to help with employee inquiries and support. The initial results were promising, with a significant reduction in HR help desk calls and quick resolution of employee issues. However, after a few months, the company started receiving complaints from employees that the ChatGPT wasn't always providing the correct answers or was difficult to understand.

Despite this hiccup, many others shared success stories of how ChatGPT had transformed their HR operations. For example, one company's HR department was able to use ChatGPT to streamline their recruitment process, enabling them to quickly fill open positions and hire top talent. Another organization reported using ChatGPT to provide virtual onboarding and training for new hires, which helped decrease the time and resources required for in-person onboarding.

One of the key takeaways from the session was the importance of considering both the benefits and risks of adopting ChatGPT. On the positive side, ChatGPT can save HR professionals time and resources by automating tasks such as answering employee questions, processing paperwork, and scheduling appointments. It can also improve employee engagement and satisfaction by providing 24/7 support and quick resolution of issues.

However, there are also potential risks to consider. For example, if ChatGPT is not properly programmed or maintained, it could provide incorrect answers or fail to understand employee inquiries, which could lead to frustration and mistrust. Additionally, there are privacy and security concerns to consider when adopting any new technology, such as ensuring that employee data is protected and confidential.

Despite these risks, many HR professionals are choosing to embrace ChatGPT as a valuable tool for improving their operations and enhancing employee experience. Here are three key points to consider when implementing ChatGPT:

  1. Start Small: Rather than implementing ChatGPT across all HR functions, start with a small pilot program to test how it works and identify any potential issues or areas for improvement.
  2. Train Employees: Just as with any new technology or process, it's important to train and educate employees on how to use ChatGPT effectively and ensure they understand how it fits into their daily work.
  3. Maintain and Update: Regularly updating and maintaining ChatGPT to ensure it is providing accurate information and keeping up with changing HR policies and procedures.

Despite the potential risks, ChatGPT offers numerous benefits for HR professionals and companies. As technology continues to transform the HR landscape, it's important to embrace new tools and processes that can improve efficiency and enhance the employee experience.
