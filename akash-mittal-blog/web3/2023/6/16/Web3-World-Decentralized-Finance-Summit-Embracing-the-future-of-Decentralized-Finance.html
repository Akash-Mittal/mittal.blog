The Birth of Decentralized Finance

It started when Aaron, an entrepreneur, received a letter from his bank that his account will be closed in a week. He had no other bank account, and he had clients he needed to pay. He tried opening an account in different banks, but all applications were rejected. Banks justified their decision by citing regulatory compliance and perceived risks associated with Aaron's business operations. Aaron found it unfair because he was running a legitimate business.

The rejection of his bank applications threw him into a financial crisis because without a bank account, his business operations came to a halt. He saw a need for decentralized finance that will liberate people from the centralized control of banks and other financial intermediaries. Aaron's experiences led him to the conceptualization of decentralized finance (DeFi).

What is Decentralized Finance (DeFi)?

Decentralized finance refers to a new financial system that operates on decentralized networks. It is an alternative to the traditional financial system dominated by centralized intermediaries, such as banks, stock exchanges, and clearinghouses. The decentralized finance system allows individuals and businesses to access financial services and undertake financial activities without the need for intermediaries.

Examples of DeFi applications include cryptocurrency wallets, decentralized exchanges, stablecoins, decentralized lending platforms, insurance, and prediction markets.

Web3 World Decentralized Finance Summit

The Web3 World Decentralized Finance Summit is an annual conference that brings together experts, investors, developers, and enthusiasts in decentralized finance to discuss the emerging opportunities, challenges and future of DeFi. The 2021 summit held on July 12-13, brought together over 3000 attendees both online and offline.

The summit featured keynote speeches, panel discussions, masterclasses, workshops, and networking opportunities. The speakers discussed a wide range of topics, including decentralized exchanges, stablecoins, yield farming, decentralized insurance, NFTs, and governance.

Decentralized Exchanges

Decentralized exchanges (DEXs) are one of the key innovations in decentralized finance. Unlike centralized exchanges that rely on intermediary custody services, DEXs allow users to trade cryptocurrencies without intermediaries. DEXs operate on decentralized networks and are more secure and censorship-resistant compared to centralized exchanges.

One of the popular DEXs is Uniswap, which is built on the Ethereum network. Uniswap enables users to trade Ethereum and other ERC-20 tokens without intermediaries. Liquidity providers earn fees from the trading activities on the platform. Uniswap has recorded a significant growth in its trading volume, with over $1 billion in daily trading volume as of July 2021.

Stablecoins

Stablecoins are cryptocurrencies that are pegged to a stable asset, such as the US dollar or gold. Stablecoins aim to address the volatility issues of other cryptocurrencies, such as Bitcoin and Ethereum. Stablecoins have gained popularity in DeFi because they provide a stable unit of account and a reliable store of value.

One of the popular stablecoins is Tether (USDT), which is pegged to the US dollar. Tether has a market capitalization of over $60 billion and is widely used in DeFi lending platforms and decentralized exchanges.

Yield Farming

Yield farming is a DeFi application that allows users to earn rewards for contributing to liquidity pools in decentralized exchanges. Liquidity providers contribute funds to the pools, which are used to facilitate trading activities on DEXs. Liquidity providers earn a share of the trading fees generated on the platform, as well as rewards in the form of tokens or cryptocurrencies.

Yield farming has become popular in DeFi because of its high rewards, but it is also associated with high risks due to the volatility of the cryptocurrencies used in the pools. It is important for users to understand the risks involved before participating in yield farming.

Decentralized Insurance

Decentralized insurance is a new application in DeFi that aims to provide insurance services without intermediaries. Decentralized insurance platforms use smart contracts to automate the insurance process and eliminate the need for intermediaries. The insurance policies are governed by the community, and claims are paid out automatically based on predefined conditions.

One of the popular decentralized insurance platforms is Nexus Mutual, which is built on the Ethereum network. Nexus Mutual enables users to purchase insurance cover for smart contracts and exchanges. The claims are paid out automatically based on the outcome of smart contracts and other predefined conditions.

NFTs

Non-fungible tokens (NFTs) are another innovation in DeFi, which enable the creation and ownership of unique digital assets. NFTs enable creators and artists to monetize their digital creations, such as music, art, and videos, by selling them on NFT marketplaces.

One of the popular NFT marketplaces is OpenSea, which is built on the Ethereum network. OpenSea enables users to trade NFTs with other users and earn rewards for contributing to liquidity pools.

Governance

Decentralized finance platforms are governed by the community, and decisions are made through a consensus mechanism. The governance mechanism ensures that the platform is operated in a transparent and fair manner, and the interests of all stakeholders are taken into account.

One of the popular governance platforms is Compound, which is built on the Ethereum network. Compound enables users to vote on proposals and earn rewards for participating in governance activities.

Conclusion

Decentralized finance is a new financial system that is emerging as an alternative to the traditional financial system dominated by centralized intermediaries. DeFi enables individuals and businesses to access financial services and undertake financial activities without the need for intermediaries. The Web3 World Decentralized Finance Summit is a platform that brings together experts, investors, and enthusiasts in DeFi to discuss the emerging opportunities and challenges in the space. The key innovations in DeFi include decentralized exchanges, stablecoins, yield farming, decentralized insurance, NFTs, and governance.
