Artificial Intelligence: A Potential Threat to Human Jobs



Image Credits https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-65855333

Once upon a time, a man named John lost his job as a driver due to the increasing popularity of self-driving cars. He had worked as a taxi driver for over 20 years and had never thought that his job would be replaced by a machine. He was not alone in his struggle; thousands of drivers all over the world lost their jobs, and many other professions are now at risk because of the rapid advancement of Artificial Intelligence (AI).

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to learn from their experiences and make decisions based on that data. It is the ability of machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, perception, and decision-making. AI is prevalent in many areas, including finance, healthcare, transportation, manufacturing, and more.

Is AI dangerous?

AI can be both beneficial and harmful, depending on how it is used. On the positive side, AI has the potential to improve people's lives by creating new jobs, enhancing healthcare services, and boosting economic growth. However, there are concerns about the potential risks of AI development, such as privacy invasion, cyber attacks, and misuse of AI for unethical purposes.

Moreover, the rapid implementation of AI technology may lead to job displacement, especially in industries that are highly susceptible to automation. Jobs that involve repetitive and predictable tasks are more likely to be replaced by AI, including drivers, factory workers, and data entry clerks.

For example, in 2017, Amazon introduced Amazon Go, a new type of convenience store that uses AI technology to enable customers to shop without cashiers. The system tracks the items that customers pick up and automatically charges them to their Amazon account. This means that cashiers' jobs were no longer necessary, which could lead to significant job losses if this technology becomes widespread.

What jobs are at risk?

According to a report by the McKinsey Global Institute, up to 375 million workers worldwide may need to switch occupational categories and learn new skills because of AI and automation by 2030. The following are some examples of jobs that are at risk of being replaced by AI and automation:

  • Customer Service Representatives
  • Delivery Drivers
  • Data Entry Clerks
  • Financial Analysts
  • Insurance Underwriters
  • Loan Officers
  • Manufacturing Workers
  • Telemarketers
  • Travel Agents

Conclusion

AI has the potential to revolutionize many industries and create new opportunities for economic growth. However, it is essential to consider the potential risks of AI development, especially in regards to job displacement. While some jobs may become obsolete due to AI and automation, new ones will emerge that require skills such as creativity, critical thinking, and problem-solving. As such, it is critical to prepare for the future by investing in education and training to ensure that people have the skills to adapt to the changing job market.
