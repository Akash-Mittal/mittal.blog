Protecting Your Brand in the Wild West of Web3



Image Credits https://circleid.com/posts/20230612-how-to-protect-your-brand-in-web3

In 2014, computer scientist Gavin Wood coined the term "Web 3.0". The phrase, which has now been shortened to "Web3", refers to the third generation of the internet that's designed to be truly decentralised and free from a central authority. The potential advantages of Web3 for businesses are clear: by creating additional avenues to reach consumers, companies can tap into new customer bases and increase their profits. However, the less regulated nature of Web3 also means that brands have the potential to be taken advantage of, both by users and by the providers in the space.

A Tale of Two Brands

Consider the story of two brands. The first is a well-established beauty company with a global reputation. The second is a new up-and-coming brand that's made a splash in the market due to its unique product offerings. Both companies have decided to embrace Web3, and have allowed their products to be sold on decentralised marketplaces on the blockchain.

The beauty company has a lot to lose, and therefore invests heavily in building a strong brand identity. They maintain their own website and social media pages, and they invest in search engine optimisation to ensure their products appear at the top of search results when consumers are looking for beauty products online. They're careful about who they choose to sell their products through, and only work with reputable retailers.

The up-and-coming brand, on the other hand, is hungry for success and is willing to sell their products through anyone who will buy them. They don't invest in building a strong brand identity, and they don't even have their own website or social media pages. They rely on the decentralised marketplaces to sell their products, certain that their unique offerings will be enough to attract buyers.

One day, both brands are hit by a wave of negative reviews from customers who've purchased their products on a decentralised marketplace. The beauty company is devastated, as they've invested so much in their brand, and they're certain that the negative reviews will hurt their reputation. The up-and-coming brand, on the other hand, isn't too bothered, as they don't place much value on their reputation anyway.

Protecting Your Brand in Web3

So, how can you protect your brand in the Wild West of Web3? Here are three tips:

1. Build a Strong Brand Identity

Building a strong brand identity is even more important in Web3 than it is in traditional e-commerce. With less regulation and more decentralisation, consumers have more options when it comes to buying products, and a strong brand identity can help you stand out in the crowd. Invest in building your own website and social media pages, and ensure your products appear at the top of search results through search engine optimisation.

2. Work with Reputable Retailers

Only work with reputable retailers when selling your products on decentralised marketplaces. Look for retailers with a proven track record of success, and take the time to research their customer service and dispute resolution procedures. By working with reputable retailers, you can avoid negative reviews and protect your brand reputation.

3. Monitor Your Reputation

Stay on top of what consumers are saying about your brand online by regularly monitoring your reputation. Set up Google Alerts to receive notifications when your brand is mentioned online, and consider investing in reputation management software to help you track and respond to negative reviews. By monitoring your reputation, you can address negative feedback quickly and protect your brand image.

Conclusion

Web3 has enormous potential for businesses, but it also presents new challenges when it comes to protecting your brand. By building a strong brand identity, working with reputable retailers, and monitoring your reputation, you can avoid the pitfalls of Web3 and reap the benefits of this exciting new technology.
