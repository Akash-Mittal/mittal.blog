An HTTP cookie is a small text file that a website stores on your device when you visit it. It contains information about your interaction with the website, such as the pages you visited, items you added to your cart, and your login credentials. Cookies can be classified into two types: session and persistent. A session cookie gets deleted as soon as you close the website, while a persistent cookie stays on your device for a longer period. Persistent cookies can be used for various purposes, such as keeping you logged in across different sessions or showing you personalized content. When you visit a website, your web browser sends a request to the server for the webpage. Along with this request, it also sends any cookies that belong to that website. The server can read the cookie and use the information contained within it to customize the webpage for you. For example, if you visit an online shopping website and add some items to your cart, a cookie is created that contains the details of the items you selected.
