Web3 Foundation Leads the Music Industry's Headway into Web3 Ecosystem



Image Credits https://www.crypto-reporter.com/press-releases/web3-foundation-leads-the-music-industrys-headway-into-web3-ecosystem-47232/

Once upon a time, music industry stakeholders had endless meetings, protests, and court battles against streaming platforms they believed were financially exploiting them. However, emerging technologies such as blockchain, smart contracts, and NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens) are precipitating an industry shift towards Web3, a decentralized ecosystem that thrives upon transparency, inclusivity, and fair monetary compensation.

The Web3 Ecosystem Explained

The Web3 ecosystem is essentially a decentralized digital economy that fosters a peer-to-peer connection between users without relying on intermediaries or profit-seeking middlemen who monetize user data for their financial benefit. The ecosystem uses blockchain technology, smart contracts, and other innovative Web3 technologies to build trust and transparency in transactions and communications.

The basic principle of Web3 is decentralization, which means a distributed network of devices that collectively create and store systems and applications. Decentralization eliminates server-based monopolies, enabling users to enjoy secure, transparent, and user-controlled platforms, which allows the democratization of information sharing and collaboration processes.

Web3 Use Cases in The Music Industry

The music industry can particularly benefit from the Web3 ecosystem given its historical challenges, from copyright infringement to revenue sharing disparities. Web3 can address some of these grievances by providing a transparent and decentralized infrastructure that ensures all stakeholders enjoy a fair share of the revenues.

NFTs

On the one hand, NFTs are digital collectibles whose ownership is authenticated on a blockchain. The music industry can use NFTs to monetize unreleased music, concert tickets, and various other fan experiences that can be tokenized and sold as unique digital assets.

Notably, Kings of Leon made history earlier this year by being the first band to release an entire album as an NFT. The album received 35,000 unique NFT buyers, underlining the increasing demand for music NFTs as a new way of distributing music content that benefits artists, fans, and investors.

Smart Contracts

On the other hand, smart contracts are self-executing codes embedded on a blockchain that automatically execute contract terms between two parties. In the music industry, smart contracts enable artists to automatically receive royalties every time their music is streamed. This removes intermediaries like music publishers and aggregators who often demand commissions up to 30%, leaving artists with little to no income.

.Music, a domain name extension exclusively reserved for music, is one of the music industry's most significant Web3 projects aimed at mitigating copyright issues. The platform allows artists to pre-register their intellectual property rights to prevent copyright infringement by other parties. Through smart contracts, .Music ensures the fair distribution of revenues between stakeholders using a transparent and incentivizing system.

Artist Tokens

Furthermore, artists can issue their tokens to facilitate transactions in their ecosystem, enabling them to crowdsource funds, sell merchandise, and distribute revenues to their teams and fans. Artist tokens are beneficial for fans as they can trade tokens with other fans, access exclusive content, and receive revenue sharing that reflects the token's value.

TDE, a renowned hip-hop label, issued its fan token, TDE, to reward fans for their loyalty and engagement. TDE's token holders have an inclusive voice in decision-making processes, such as artist collaborations and merchandise designs. Additionally, fans holding TDE tokens receive access to exclusive content, merchandise, and live events.

Conclusion

The Web3 ecosystem offers an unparalleled opportunity for the music industry to address its various sticky problems and give voice to all stakeholders. The use cases of NFTs, smart contracts, and artist tokens have shown that Web3 has revolutionized the way the traditional music business functions. Diving deeper into the Web3 ecosystem and adopting emerging technologies, the music industry can finally achieve transparency, fairness, and inclusivity, ultimately benefiting artists, fans, and investors.
