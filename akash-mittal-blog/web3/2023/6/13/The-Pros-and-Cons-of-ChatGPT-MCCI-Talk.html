The Pros and Cons of ChatGPT: MCCI Talk



Image Credits https://www.dtnext.in/news/business/mcci-organises-talk-on-pros-and-cons-of-chatgpt-717948

ChatGPT was launched in November 2022 and gained over 100 million users worldwide. India is the second largest country using ChatGPT, next to USA in terms of number of users. The Madras Chamber of Commerce and Industry (MCCI) organised a talk on the pros and cons of ChatGPT, given the wide adoption of the platform by Indian users. Here are some highlights of the discussion.

The Pros of ChatGPT

1. Convenience: ChatGPT allows users to connect anytime, anywhere, as long as they have an internet connection. This makes it easier for people to stay in touch with friends and family, as well as reach out to new contacts.

2. Privacy: ChatGPT offers end-to-end encryption of messages, which means that only the sender and receiver can see the content of the conversation. This makes ChatGPT a more secure platform for communication, especially in comparison to other social media platforms where privacy breaches have occurred.

3. Multi-functional: Aside from messaging, ChatGPT offers a range of features, such as voice and video calls, group chats, and file sharing. This makes the platform a versatile tool for both personal and professional communication.

The Cons of ChatGPT

1. Addiction: Like any social media platform, ChatGPT can be addictive, leading users to spend excessive amounts of time on the platform rather than engaging in other activities. This can lead to mental health issues such as anxiety and depression.

2. Fake news: ChatGPT can be a breeding ground for fake news, as users can easily forward messages without fact-checking the information. This can lead to the spread of misinformation, which can have real-world consequences for individuals and society.

3. Cyberbullying: Like other social media platforms, ChatGPT can be a platform for cyberbullying, which can have serious consequences for the victim's mental health. ChatGPT has taken steps to combat this issue by offering tools for users to block and report offenders, but more needs to be done.

Conclusion

Overall, ChatGPT is a versatile platform that offers many advantages for communication and networking. However, it also has its downsides, such as addiction, fake news, and cyberbullying. It is important for users to be aware of these issues and take steps to mitigate them, such as fact-checking information before sharing and setting boundaries for their use of the platform.

One of the participants in the MCCI talk shared a personal anecdote about how ChatGPT had helped them connect with family members living abroad. They said that before ChatGPT, it was difficult to keep in touch with their loved ones due to time differences and expensive international calling rates. However, with ChatGPT, they were able to stay connected more easily and frequently, which had a positive impact on their mental health.
