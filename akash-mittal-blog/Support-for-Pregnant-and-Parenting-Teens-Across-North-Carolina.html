Being a teenager is hard enough, but when you add pregnancy or parenting into the mix, it can be overwhelming. Fortunately, there are programs across North Carolina that provide support to pregnant and parenting teens.

In this article, we'll take a closer look at some of these programs, hear from real teens who have benefited from them, and provide some practical tips for finding help and support.

Real Stories: How Support Programs Changed Lives

When I found out I was pregnant, I didn't know what to do. I was scared and felt alone. But then I found the Teen Parenting Program at my school, and it changed everything for me.

That's what Sarah, a 17-year-old high school student, had to say about her experience with the Teen Parenting Program. This program, run by the school district, provides educational and emotional support to pregnant and parenting teens.

According to Sarah, the program helped her with everything from finding a doctor to providing childcare while she attended classes. "I don't know where I'd be without it," she says.

When my girlfriend told me she was pregnant, I was scared. I didn't know how I was going to provide for her and the baby. But then I discovered the Young Fathers Program.

That's what James, a 19-year-old, had to say about his experience with the Young Fathers Program. This program, run by a local nonprofit, provides support and resources to young fathers.

James says the program helped him with everything from finding a job to learning how to be a good parent. "I never knew there were so many resources out there for young fathers," he says. "It's made a huge difference for me and my family."

Support

  • The Teen Parent Program in Raleigh has a 98% graduation rate among participants.
