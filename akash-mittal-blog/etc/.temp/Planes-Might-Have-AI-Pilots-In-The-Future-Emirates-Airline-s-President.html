Planes Might Have AI Pilots In The Future Says Emirates Airline's President

Flying has always been a fascinating experience for people around the world. From the Wright Brothers to the modern-day commercial aviation industry, the way we fly has changed drastically. However, what if the industry changes even further? What if planes can fly themselves without human intervention? This might sound far-fetched, but Emirates Airline's President, Sir Tim Clark, believes that planes might have AI pilots in the future.

Why AI Piloted Planes Might Become a Reality?

With the advancement in technology, artificial intelligence is becoming more capable of handling complicated tasks. According to Emirates Airline's President, the cost of training human pilots is continually increasing, and a shortage of pilots might follow. Thus, there is a need to explore modern technologies, including AI, to fill this gap.

Moreover, AI pilots can bring a lot of benefits. For example, AI pilots do not experience fatigue, can make calculated decisions without being influenced by emotions, and can learn from experience. Additionally, using AI pilots can save a considerable amount of fuel, reduce emissions, and even make air travel safer.

Quantifiable Examples of AI Piloted Planes

Although the idea of AI-piloted planes might seem like science fiction, several companies are already testing this technology. In 2020, Boeing conducted a series of test flights that involved AI pilots. In one of the tests, the AI pilot was given full control of a Boeing 737 simulator, and it successfully executed 90 takeoffs and landings without any human assistance.

Similarly, Airbus revealed in 2019 that they are working on developing autonomous planes. They are collaborating with aviation authorities to overcome regulatory hurdles that come with such technology. It is worth noting that Airbus's autonomous technology has already passed some initial safety tests.

Three Implications of AI Piloted Planes

The shift towards AI-piloted planes will transform the aviation industry significantly. Below are some of the implications:

  1. Reduced Pilot Shortages: As mentioned earlier, the aviation industry is facing a shortage of pilots. Implementing AI-piloted planes can fill this gap and even create job opportunities for operators and technicians.
  2. Reduced Operating Cost: AI pilots do not need hotels, food, or other expenses that come with human pilots. Additionally, AI pilots can show fuel-efficient behavior, which can result in significant savings in operating expenses.
  3. Improved Safety: AI pilots can make precise decisions in critical situations without being hindered by human emotions. Additionally, AI pilots do not experience fatigue during long flights, which can increase overall flight safety.

Personal Anecdotes and Case Studies

As an experienced airline pilot, I understand the significance of this change in revolutionizing the aviation industry. However, I also realize that it will take some time for the public to trust AI-piloted planes. Safety is always a significant concern in aviation, so the transition will have to be gradual.
