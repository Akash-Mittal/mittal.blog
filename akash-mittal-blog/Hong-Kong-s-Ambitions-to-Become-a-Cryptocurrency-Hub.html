Hong Kong's Ambitions to Become a Cryptocurrency Hub

Once upon a time, there was a small region in Asia called Hong Kong. It had long been known as a financial hub, with a thriving stock market and a bustling financial district. However, in recent years, Hong Kong has set its sights on becoming something even more innovative and exciting: a hub for the Web3 industry and cryptocurrency.

As cryptocurrency becomes increasingly popular and mainstream, many countries and regions are vying to become the leader in this new industry. But Hong Kong is uniquely positioned to succeed in this competition. It has a strong financial infrastructure, a thriving startup scene, and a government that is both supportive and forward-thinking.

Recently, a group of industry associations in Hong Kong have banded together to push for the city's ambitions to become a cryptocurrency hub. These associations include the Hong Kong Blockchain Association, the Hong Kong Cryptocurrency Investment Association, and the Hong Kong Digital Asset Managers Association.

Hong Kong has already made some big strides in becoming a cryptocurrency hub. For example:

  • In 2019, Hong Kong's Securities and Futures Commission introduced a regulatory framework for cryptocurrency exchanges. This gave legitimacy to the industry, and made it easier for businesses to operate in Hong Kong.
  • In 2020, Hong Kong-based cryptocurrency exchange BitMEX reached a record $1 trillion in yearly trading volume.
  • In 2021, the Hong Kong Monetary Authority and the Bank of Thailand announced that they would be conducting a joint study on the use of central bank digital currencies (CBDC) for cross-border payments.
