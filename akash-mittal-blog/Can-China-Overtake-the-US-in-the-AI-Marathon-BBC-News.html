Can China Overtake the US in the AI Marathon?

By BBC News

The competition between China and the US in the field of Artificial Intelligence (AI) has been heating up in recent years. China has been ramping up its investments in AI technology and research, and has been making significant strides in the field. On the other hand, the US has been a traditional leader in the field and has been investing heavily in AI for decades. However, a recent incident has captured the attention of the world and has raised questions about China's ability to overtake the US in the AI marathon.

In 2017, a team of Chinese researchers developed an AI program that defeated the world champion at the game of Go, a complex board game that has been regarded as a benchmark of human intelligence. The AI program, called AlphaGo, used machine learning algorithms and neural networks to learn from past games and develop its own strategies. It defeated the human champion in a best-of-five series, winning four games to one. The victory was seen as a major milestone in the field of AI and a sign of China's growing dominance in the field.

The victory of AlphaGo is just one example of China's growing dominance in the field of AI. Here are some more quantifiable examples:

  • China has filed more AI patents than any other country in the world, accounting for more than 40% of all AI patents filed globally in 2019. The US ranks second with 30% of all AI patents filed.
  • China has been ramping up its investments in AI research and development. In 2018, the country invested more than $7.5 billion in AI research, surpassing the US's investment of $4 billion.
  • China has been using AI to transform its industries, particularly in the areas of finance, healthcare, and transportation. For instance, the country has been using facial recognition technology to make payments and has been testing self-driving cars in various cities.

The Future of AI

Despite China's growing dominance in the field of AI, the US is not far behind. The US still leads in the development and adoption of AI technologies, and has a long history of innovation in the field. However, the competition between the two countries is intensifying, and the future of AI will likely be shaped by the race between China and the US.

In the coming years, we can expect to see more breakthroughs in AI technology, particularly in the areas of deep learning, machine learning, and computer vision. We can also expect AI to transform various industries, from healthcare to finance to transportation.
