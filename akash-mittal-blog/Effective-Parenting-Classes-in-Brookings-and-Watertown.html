Effective Parenting: Classes in Brookings and Watertown

Every parent wants to raise their children to be happy, healthy, and successful adults. However, it's not always easy, and sometimes parents need help. That's where parenting classes come in- they offer practical tips, advice, and support to help parents navigate the challenges of raising a child in today's world. If you're a parent in Brookings or Watertown, then you're in luck- here are some upcoming parenting classes that you won't want to miss!

Brookings

Positive Discipline: The Key to Successful Parenting

When: Monday evenings for six weeks, starting September 13th

Where: Brookings Public Library

Cost: $50 per person or $75 per couple

Positive discipline is a parenting approach that emphasizes mutual respect, communication, and problem-solving. It's an effective way to teach your child self-discipline and responsibility, while also promoting a closer relationship between parent and child. This six-week class will cover the principles of positive discipline, as well as specific strategies for implementing it in your home. You'll also have the opportunity to share your experiences and learn from other parents in the class.

The Power of Play: Helping Your Child Learn and Grow

When: Saturday, October 2nd, from 9am to 12pm

Where: Brookings High School

Cost: Free

Play is more than just fun and games- it's also an essential part of your child's development. In this workshop, you'll learn about the different types of play and how they help your child learn and grow. You'll also learn how to create a play-friendly environment in your home, and how to use play to strengthen your relationship with your child. This workshop is open to parents of children of all ages.

Watertown

Parenting in the Digital Age: Navigating Screen Time, Social Media, and Online Safety

When: Tuesday evenings for four weeks, starting September 14th

Where: Watertown High School

Cost: $40 per person or $60 per couple

In today's world, children are growing up surrounded by technology. While technology can be a great tool for learning and communication, it can also have negative effects on your child's development and well-being. This four-week class will teach you how to navigate the challenges of parenting in the digital age. You'll learn about screen time guidelines, social media safety, and strategies for promoting healthy technology use in your home. You'll also have the chance to share your own experiences and learn from other parents in the class.

Raising Resilient Kids: Strategies for Building Confidence, Coping Skills, and a Positive Mindset

When: Saturday, October 9th, from 10am to 1pm

Where: Watertown Middle School

Cost: Free

Life is full of challenges, and it's important for children to develop the resilience they need to overcome them. In this workshop, you'll learn strategies for building your child's confidence, coping skills, and positive mindset. You'll also learn how to respond to setbacks and failures in a way that promotes growth and learning. This workshop is open to parents of children of all ages.
