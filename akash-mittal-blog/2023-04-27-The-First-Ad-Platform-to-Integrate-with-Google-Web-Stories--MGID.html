The First Ad Platform to Integrate with Google Web Stories: MGID

Once upon a time, there was a company called MGID. This company was founded in 2008 and quickly became one of the leading native advertising platforms in the world. Its goal was simple - connect advertisers with publishers in order to drive traffic, increase engagement, and boost revenue.

Over the years, MGID has continued to innovate and expand its offerings. In 2021, it became the first ad platform to integrate with Google Web Stories, a new format for mobile that allows publishers to create immersive, full-screen experiences using a combination of text, images, and videos.

What does this mean for advertisers and publishers? Here are some concrete examples:

  • Advertisers can now reach audiences in a more engaging and interactive way, creating ads that resemble the look and feel of Web Stories content.
  • Publishers can monetize their Web Stories by featuring MGID ads, generating revenue while providing users with seamless, non-intrusive experiences.
  • Both parties benefit from MGID's advanced targeting and optimization capabilities, ensuring that the right content is delivered to the right people at the right time.

Overall, this integration is a major win for the industry, as it brings together two powerful platforms that are focused on user engagement and ad effectiveness. It's also a sign of the ongoing evolution of native advertising, which continues to evolve as new formats and technologies emerge.

Conclusion:

  1. MGID is the first ad platform to integrate with Google Web Stories, creating new opportunities for advertisers and publishers alike.
  2. The integration allows for more engaging and interactive ad experiences, while also generating revenue for publishers.
  3. By leveraging advanced targeting and optimization, MGID is helping to shape the future of native advertising.
