How India Has Transformed In Less Than A Decade

India, a country that has always been known for its vibrant culture, rich heritage, and chaotic streets, has undergone a dramatic transformation in a span of less than a decade. The changes that India has witnessed have been phenomenal, and there are many quantifiable examples that prove the same.

The Story of Transformation

Let me take you back to the year 2011, when India was a different place altogether. The country was grappling with corruption, poverty, illiteracy, and a plethora of other problems. India was not considered a preferred destination for investors, and many people were skeptical about doing business in the country. But things started to change with the new government that took over in 2014.

The new government brought in sweeping reforms and policies that aimed at transforming India into a global superpower. The focus was on economic growth, job creation, and infrastructure development. The government also launched several flagship programs such as Make in India, Digital India, Skill India, and Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, to name a few.

Fast forward to 2021, and the India we see today is vastly different from what it was a decade ago. India has emerged as the fastest-growing major economy in the world, surpassing even China. The country has attracted billions of dollars in foreign investments, and many multinational companies have set up shop in India. The government has also launched several initiatives to promote entrepreneurship, innovation, and start-ups.

There are many quantifiable examples that showcase India's transformation in the last decade:

  1. India's GDP has grown at an average rate of 6.9% from 2014-2019.
  2. The stock market has surged by over 500% since 2014.
  3. India is now the world's second-largest smartphone market, with over 760 million users.
  4. The number of internet users in India has grown from 120 million in 2011 to over 700 million in 2021, making India the second-largest internet user base in the world.
  5. India has become a leading exporter of software services, with companies such as TCS, Infosys, and Wipro being globally recognized brands.
  6. The government's Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, an initiative aimed at cleaning up the streets and promoting hygiene, has successfully built over 100 million toilets across the country.
  7. The Digital India initiative has connected over 250,000 villages with broadband internet, bringing digital literacy and e-services to the rural areas.

India's transformation has touched millions of lives, and here are a few personal anecdotes and case studies that showcase the same:

Case Study 1: Roshni Sen

Roshni Sen, a 25-year-old from Kolkata, had always dreamt of starting her own business. However, lack of resources and support made it seem like an impossible task. That's when she heard about the government's Startup India initiative, which aimed at promoting entrepreneurship and supporting start-ups. Roshni applied for the program and received funding and mentorship support. Today, her company, which makes eco-friendly packaging materials, has clients across the country and is growing at a rapid pace.

Case Study 2: Rajesh Singh

Rajesh Singh, a farmer from a small village in Uttar Pradesh, was struggling to make ends meet. He had a small piece of land and was barely able to make a living from it. However, the government's Soil Health Card initiative, which aimed at improving soil fertility and productivity, changed his fortunes. Rajesh received a soil health card and was able to improve the quality of his soil by following the recommendations provided. Today, he is able to grow more crops and earn a better income.
