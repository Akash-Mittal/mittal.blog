Wiz Khalifa and Amber Rose Show Off Co-Parenting Skills at Son's Graduation

This is just one of many examples of successful co-parenting between Wiz and Amber. Despite their high-profile split and some public feuds, they have consistently put the needs of their son first and worked together to provide him with a stable and loving upbringing.

Co-Parenting Success

Here are some quantifiable examples of Wiz and Amber's co-parenting success:

  • They share custody of Sebastian, with Wiz having him on weekdays and Amber having him on weekends. This allows both parents to spend quality time with their son while also maintaining their careers.
  • They have a positive relationship with each other's families and friends, which creates a more supportive network for Sebastian.
  • They have both publicly praised each other for their co-parenting skills and dedication to their son.
  • They have taken joint family vacations with Sebastian, showing that they can come together for the sake of their child.
  • They have worked out a system for sharing expenses related to Sebastian, such as school fees and medical bills.
  • They attend important events in Sebastian's life together, like his graduation, and are able to celebrate his achievements without tension or drama.

These examples demonstrate that successful co-parenting is possible, even in challenging circumstances. By putting their differences aside and prioritizing their son's well-being, Wiz and Amber have created a stable and loving environment for him to grow up in.

The Importance of Co-Parenting

Co-parenting is becoming increasingly common as more couples choose to end their relationships but remain committed to raising their children together. While co-parenting can be challenging, it has been shown to have many benefits for children, including:

  • Higher self-esteem
  • Better academic performance
  • Lower rates of anxiety and depression
  • Stronger relationships with both parents
  • Less behavioral problems

Co-parenting can also have benefits for parents, who may find that sharing the responsibilities of raising their children allows them to pursue their careers, maintain their social lives, and have more flexibility in their schedules. However, co-parenting requires a high degree of cooperation, communication, and compromise, and may not be the right choice for all families.

Conclusion: 3 Points to Remember

Here are three key points to remember about co-parenting:

  1. Co-parenting is possible and can be successful, even in challenging circumstances.
  2. Successful co-parenting requires cooperation, communication, and compromise.
  3. Co-parenting can have many benefits for children, including higher self-esteem, better academic performance, and stronger relationships with both parents.

By following these principles and putting their children first, parents can create a stable and loving environment for their children to grow up in, regardless of their own relationship status.
