Graviton Announces First Cohort of Web 3 Accelerator - Seven Indian Startups Selected

Graviton, a leading blockchain-based startup accelerator, has announced the selection of seven innovative Indian startups for its first cohort of Web 3 accelerator program. The selected startups represent a diverse range of industries and technologies, but all of them share a common vision - to leverage the power of decentralized Web 3 technologies to solve the most pressing challenges of our time.

An Interesting Story

The story of how Graviton came into existence is an interesting one. The founders of Graviton, Christoph and Philipp Jentzsch, were already successful entrepreneurs in their own right, having founded Slock.it, a blockchain-based IoT platform. However, they were frustrated by the lack of support and resources available to early-stage blockchain startups.

That's when they decided to launch Graviton - a fully decentralized accelerator program that offers startups all the necessary resources and support to bring their ideas to life. Unlike traditional accelerators, Graviton does not take any equity in the startups it supports, and the program is completely free for the selected startups.

Personal Anecdotes and Case Studies

One of the most compelling aspects of Graviton's accelerator program is its focus on community and collaboration. Each cohort is made up of a carefully selected group of startups, all of whom are given the opportunity to work alongside and learn from one another. This not only helps to accelerate the growth and development of each individual startup, but also fosters a sense of camaraderie and support within the blockchain community.

One example of this community spirit is the story of how two of Graviton's alumni - Spherity and ChainGuardian - came together to tackle the problem of secure and decentralized supply chains. After meeting at a Graviton event, the two startups decided to join forces and combine their respective technologies to create a powerful new solution for the logistics industry.

Conclusion

  • The success of its alumni is a testament to the effectiveness of the program and the potential of Web 3 technologies to solve real-world problems.
