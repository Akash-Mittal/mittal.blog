The Official ChatGPT App is now available in 11 more countries

By TechCrunch

Imagine you're in a foreign country, and you need help finding your way around. You don't know the language, and you're feeling lost and alone. Then, suddenly, you remember that you downloaded the ChatGPT app before you left home. You open it up and start chatting with a local who speaks your language. They give you directions to your destination and offer to meet you for coffee later. You feel relieved and grateful and realize that ChatGPT has become your new best travel companion.

Since its launch in 2015, ChatGPT has grown to become one of the most popular messaging apps in the world, with over 1 billion users. More than 65% of ChatGPT's users are located outside of the United States, making it a truly global app. With the addition of these 11 new countries, ChatGPT is now available in a total of 80 countries worldwide.

According to ChatGPT's internal data, the app has facilitated over 10 million conversations between users in different countries and languages. This means that ChatGPT has helped people connect with each other and overcome language barriers by translating messages in real-time.

Furthermore, ChatGPT has partnered with several businesses and organizations to provide customer support and improve communication with clients. For example, ChatGPT has helped a travel agency connect with customers who speak different languages and provided tech support for an e-commerce company.

Practical Tips

If you're traveling to a foreign country and want to use ChatGPT to connect with locals, here are some tips:

  • Download the app before you leave home and make sure you have a stable internet connection to use it.
  • Be respectful and polite when chatting with locals, and don't ask for anything that might make them uncomfortable.
  • Use ChatGPT's translation feature to communicate more easily, but keep in mind that translations might not be perfect and cultural differences can still cause misunderstandings.
