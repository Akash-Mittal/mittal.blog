The Unrealised India Underwater Train: Sir Harley Dalrymple-Hays's Plan



Image Credits https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-india-65773871

Sir Harley Dalrymple-Hays was a British engineer who worked in India during the British Raj. He is known for his unrealised plan to build an underwater train in Kolkata, the capital city of West Bengal.

The plan was proposed in the early 1900s, at a time when Kolkata was facing overcrowding, traffic congestion, and transport problems. Dalrymple-Hays's idea was to build an underground rail that could run beneath the Hooghly river, which flows through the city.

The concept of an underwater train was not new, but it had never been realised before. Dalrymple-Hays believed that his plan could revolutionise urban transport in Kolkata and make the city a model for innovation and modernisation.

The Plan

Dalrymple-Hays's plan involved the construction of a tunnel that would run under the Hooghly river, from Howrah to Kolkata. The tunnel would be around 1.5 miles long and would house an electric train that could carry up to 160 passengers at a time.

The train would run on a track that was suspended from the tunnel roof, and would be powered by electricity that was supplied through a third rail. The train would be designed like a boat, with a streamlined shape that would enable it to travel smoothly through the water.

The entrance to the tunnel would be through a station building, which would be constructed at either end of the tunnel. The station building would be a multi-storey structure that would house offices, waiting rooms, and other facilities.

The Challenges

Dalrymple-Hays's plan faced several challenges, the most significant of which was funding. The construction of an underwater train was a complex and expensive project, and the British government was not willing to invest in it.

Another challenge was the technical feasibility of the project. The Hooghly river is a tidal river, with a strong current and a depth of up to 80 feet. The construction of a tunnel and a train that could withstand the water pressure and the tidal movements was a daunting task.

Finally, there were concerns about the environmental impact of the project. The construction of the tunnel and the operation of the train could have disrupted the river's ecosystem, and there were fears about the potential for pollution and other environmental hazards.

The Legacy

Sir Harley Dalrymple-Hays's plan to build an underwater train in Kolkata may have remained an unrealised dream, but it continues to inspire engineers and innovators around the world.

The concept of underwater transport has been explored in recent years, with projects such as the Channel Tunnel and the Marmaray Tunnel in Istanbul. These tunnels connect different countries and continents, highlighting the potential of underwater transport to connect people and places.

The legacy of Sir Harley Dalrymple-Hays's plan is also seen in the development of modern urban transport systems, such as the Kolkata Metro. The metro offers a fast, efficient, and eco-friendly mode of transport for millions of commuters every day.

Conclusion

Though Sir Harley Dalrymple-Hays's plan to build an underwater train in Kolkata did not become a reality, it remains a fascinating concept that has captured the imagination of many. The challenges of funding, technical feasibility, and environmental impact may have been too great, but the legacy of the idea lives on in the development of modern urban transport systems around the world.
