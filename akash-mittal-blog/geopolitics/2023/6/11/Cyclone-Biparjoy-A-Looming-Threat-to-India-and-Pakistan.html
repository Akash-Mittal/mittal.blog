Once upon a time, there was a small fishing village situated on the coast of the Arabian Sea. The villagers lived a simple life and had a deep connection with the sea, which provided them with their livelihood. However, every year during the monsoon season, they faced a grave danger â€“ cyclones.

These cyclones often wreaked havoc on their village, destroying their homes, boats and fishing nets. The villagers were forced to rebuild their lives from scratch every year. They prayed for a solution to their problem.

The Impact of Cyclone Biparjoy

The story of the fishing village is not unique. Many coastal regions in India and Pakistan face the same threat every year during the monsoon season. And now, Cyclone Biparjoy has become the latest addition to this list.

The category 2 tropical cyclone has been brewing in the Arabian Sea and is likely to hit the coasts of India and Pakistan in the next four days. As per the latest reports, it is expected to bring heavy rains and strong winds, along with storm surges that may cause flooding in low-lying coastal areas.

The impact of such cyclones is not limited to the loss of property. They also disrupt the economic activities of the affected regions, which mainly depend on agriculture and fishing. This, in turn, affects the livelihoods of the people living in these regions.

Case Studies

Let us take a look at some of the past cyclones that have hit the Indian subcontinent and their impact:

Cyclone Nisarga (2020)

Cyclone Nisarga hit the western coast of India in June 2020, during the COVID-19 pandemic. The cyclone caused damage to buildings, uprooted trees and disrupted power supply in many areas. The pandemic made it difficult for authorities to conduct relief operations and evacuate people.

Cyclone Fani (2019)

Cyclone Fani hit the eastern coast of India in May 2019, causing extensive damage to infrastructure, power lines and crops. The cyclone also caused a storm surge that flooded many low-lying areas. The disaster caused a loss of over USD 2.5 billion.

Cyclone Hudhud (2014)

Cyclone Hudhud hit the eastern coast of India in October 2014, causing extensive damage to infrastructure, crops and property. The cyclone disrupted the livelihoods of the people living in the affected regions, particularly those in the fishing and agriculture sectors.

Conclusion

Cyclones are a recurring threat in the coastal regions of India and Pakistan. They cause widespread damage to property and disrupt the economic activities of the affected regions. It is important for the authorities to be well-prepared to tackle such disasters by conducting timely evacuations and providing relief to the affected people.

At the same time, it is also important for individuals and communities to take preventive measures and be prepared to face such disasters. This includes building cyclone-resistant structures, creating emergency kits and staying informed about weather updates.
