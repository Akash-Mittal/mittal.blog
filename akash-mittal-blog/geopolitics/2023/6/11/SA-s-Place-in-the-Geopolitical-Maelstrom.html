Interest vs Alignment: SA's place in the Geopolitical Maelstrom

South Africa has always been a country of great interest to many countries due to its abundant natural resources and strategic location. However, the geopolitical shifts that have occurred in recent years have led to a need for South Africa to rebalance its policies to align with its needs. This article will explore the various factors that contribute to this need and how it can be achieved.

The Importance of Alignment

Alignment with other countries is essential for South Africa's economic and political stability. In order to achieve this alignment, South Africa needs to reassess its current policies and identify areas where changes can be made to better align with its needs.

Case Study: China

China is one of South Africa's biggest trading partners and has invested heavily in the country's infrastructure. However, this relationship has not always been without its challenges. In recent years, concerns have been raised that China's investment in South Africa's energy and transport sectors may be detrimental to the country's long-term sustainability. Many experts believe that South Africa needs to take a more strategic approach to managing its relationship with China to ensure that it is mutually beneficial and not just focused on short-term gains.

Case Study: The United States

The United States has long been an important ally of South Africa. However, the Trump administration's America First policy has created some challenges in this relationship. South Africa's inclusion on the list of countries whose steel and aluminum exports are subject to 25% and 10% tariffs respectively has caused significant tension between the two countries. South Africa has since filed a complaint with the World Trade Organization regarding these tariffs, highlighting the need for South Africa to take a strong stance when it comes to protecting its interests in the international arena.

Rebalancing Policies

In order for South Africa to achieve alignment with other countries, it is essential that it rebalances its policies to better align with its needs. This means identifying areas where changes can be made to promote economic growth, social development, and political stability.

  1. Economic growth: South Africa needs to focus on promoting sustainable growth that benefits all of its citizens. This means investing in education, healthcare, and infrastructure, as well as attracting foreign investment that will drive the economy forward.
  2. Social development: South Africa needs to address the high levels of inequality that exist in the country. This means investing in programs that will promote social mobility, such as job training and education initiatives, as well as improving access to basic services such as healthcare and housing.
  3. Political stability: South Africa needs to create a stable political environment that encourages investment and growth. This means addressing issues such as corruption and ensuring that the rule of law is upheld.

Conclusion

The geopolitical shifts that have occurred in recent years have made it clear that South Africa needs to rebalance its policies in order to achieve alignment with its needs. This means identifying areas where changes can be made to promote economic growth, social development, and political stability. By doing so, South Africa can position itself to take advantage of the opportunities that arise in a changing global landscape.

In 2013, South Africa faced criticism from the international community for allowing Sudanese President Omar al-Bashir to attend the African Union summit in Johannesburg. This was despite an International Criminal Court warrant for his arrest on charges of genocide and war crimes.

While many African countries supported South Africa's decision, it underscored the difficult balancing act the country must perform in terms of aligning itself with African interests versus global expectations and obligations.

Examples and Case Studies

South Africa's position within the wider geopolitical landscape has been shaped by historical events, as well as its membership of various organizations and agreements.

One example is the country's membership of the BRICS (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa) group of nations. While this grouping provides South Africa with greater geopolitical leverage, it also necessitates a certain level of alignment with the interests of other member states, particularly China.

Another example is South Africa's position within the African Union (AU). The country has been a key driver of the AU's Agenda 2063, which seeks "an integrated, prosperous and peaceful Africa". However, it is also grappling with issues such as the ongoing conflict in Libya, which may require South Africa to balance its alignment with Africa against its own strategic interests.

Case studies of South Africa's foreign policy choices include its increasing ties with Russia, with whom the country is exploring cooperation in areas such as energy and defense. This has put South Africa in a challenging position vis-a-vis its traditional allies, such as the United States.

Overall, South Africa's foreign policy choices have been influenced by a variety of factors, including historical legacies, democratic values, and economic and security interests.
