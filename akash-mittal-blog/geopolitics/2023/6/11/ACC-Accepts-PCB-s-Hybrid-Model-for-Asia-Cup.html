ACC Accepts PCB's Hybrid Model for Asia Cup



Image Credits https://www.devdiscourse.com/article/sports-games/2484111-acc-could-accept-pcbs-hybrid-model-for-asia-cup-pakistan-team-set-to-travel-to-india-for-wc

Cricket, as a sport, has evolved and grown exponentially over the years. With its popularity soaring high, various cricket boards have come up with creative ways and means to promote and sustain the sport. One such recent development in the world of cricket is the Hybrid Model proposed by the Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) for the Asia Cup games.

The Hybrid Model proposed by the PCB for the Asia Cup games entails conducting four non-India games in Pakistan while the rest of the matches will be played in Sri Lanka's Galle and Pallekele. This proposal was accepted by the Asian Cricket Council (ACC) headed by the BCCI president Jay Shah.

Case Studies

The concept of Hybrid Model is not new in the world of cricket. In fact, there have been instances where this model has been used successfully. Let's take a look at some examples below:

Caribbean Premier League

The Caribbean Premier League (CPL) is an example of the Hybrid Model being successfully integrated into cricket. The tournament comprises six teams and is played over a period of six weeks. Each team plays ten games in the group stage, five games at home and five away.

However, due to the small size of the Caribbean islands, it is not possible to have home and away games for all the teams. Therefore, the idea of Hybrid Model was introduced, where some teams would play their home matches in one country and the away matches in another.

This model has been working well for the league, and fans have loved the concept, which has increased the popularity of the sport in the island nations.

IPL

The Indian Premier League (IPL) is one of the most popular tournaments in the world of cricket. The concept of Hybrid Model was introduced during the 2020 season when the tournament was being held in the UAE, owing to the COVID-19 pandemic.

The tournament was split into two parts, with the first half played in India, and the second half played in the UAE. However, due to the pandemic, the second half of the tournament was held without any crowds in the stadiums.

The concept of Hybrid Model was successful in the IPL, as it allowed the tournament to continue despite the challenging situation. The tournament was a massive success, with viewership numbers higher than ever before.

Conclusion

The acceptance of the Hybrid Model proposed by the PCB for the Asia Cup games is a significant step for the sport of cricket. It not only promotes the sport but also allows fans across different countries to enjoy the game.

The success of the model in the CPL and IPL shows that it is a feasible solution, and it could pave the way for similar models to be introduced in other cricket tournaments.

All in all, the acceptance of the proposal by the ACC is a significant event for the world of cricket, and it will be exciting to see how the venture turns out.
