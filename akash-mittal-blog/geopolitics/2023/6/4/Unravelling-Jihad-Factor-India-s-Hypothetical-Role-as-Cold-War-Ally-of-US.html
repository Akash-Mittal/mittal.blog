Unravelling Jihad Factor: India's Hypothetical Role as Cold War Ally of US

India has always been a land of diverse cultures, religions, and belief systems, but it has also been a victim of terrorism for a long time. The country has faced several terrorist attacks in the recent past, mainly perpetrated by the jihadist groups operating in the region. This article looks at the hypothetical scenario of India becoming a Cold War ally of the US and its impact on the jihadist factor in the region.

The Cold War Scenario

If we look back at the Cold War era, the US was always looking for allies to counter the Soviet Union's influence in various parts of the world. India, being one of the largest democracies in the world, with a stable government and economy, could have been a potential ally of the US in the region. Suppose the US had invited India to be a part of its alliance against the Soviet Union. In that case, it would have acted as a counterbalance to the Chinese-Pakistani alliance in the region, which was primarily responsible for the spread of jihadism in Asia.

Case Studies

The Afghanistan Factor

Afghanistan has been a hotbed of jihadist activity for decades. During the Cold War era, the US and its allies provided arms, ammunition, and training to the mujahideen to fight against the Soviet Union. This war created the Taliban, which was nurtured by the Pakistani government to serve its interests in Afghanistan. The Taliban, in turn, provided a safe haven to Al Qaeda and other jihadist groups, which were responsible for several terrorist attacks around the world, including the 9/11 attacks in the US.

If India had been a Cold War ally of the US, it could have played a crucial role in preventing the Taliban's rise in Afghanistan. It could have also provided support to the Northern Alliance, a coalition of anti-Taliban forces, which was fighting the Taliban regime in Afghanistan.

The Pakistan Factor

Pakistan has been a significant sponsor of jihadism in the region for decades. It has provided support to various jihadist groups, including the Taliban, Al Qaeda, Lashkar-e-Taiba, and Jaish-e-Mohammed, which have carried out several terrorist attacks in India. If India had been a Cold War ally of the US, it could have put pressure on Pakistan to stop its support to these groups or face the consequences.

India could have also provided support to Balochi separatists, who have been fighting for their independence from Pakistan. This support could have weakened Pakistan's position in the region and prevented it from using jihadist groups as a proxy to serve its interests.

Conclusion

  1. India's hypothetical role as a Cold War ally of the US would have been crucial in preventing the spread of jihadism in the region.
  2. It could have provided support to anti-Taliban forces in Afghanistan and put pressure on Pakistan to stop its support to jihadist groups.
  3. This support could have weakened Pakistan's position in the region and prevented it from using jihadist groups as a proxy to serve its interests.
