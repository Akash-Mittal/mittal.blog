China Expels Indian Journalists: The Implications for Press Freedom

Introduction

On August 20, 2020, reports emerged that China was expelling almost all Indian journalists working in the country. The move came in response to India's decision to restrict Chinese state-run media outlets in the country. This development has serious implications for press freedom and bilateral relations between India and China.

In this article, we will analyze the reasons behind China's decision and examine its impact on press freedom in China and India. We will also look at some case studies of how the Chinese government exercises control over the media. Finally, we will conclude by discussing the broader geopolitical implications of this decision.

The Reasons Behind China's Decision

China's decision to expel Indian journalists is primarily a retaliatory measure against India's decision to curb Chinese state-run media outlets in the country. In April 2020, the Indian government issued a circular making it mandatory for foreign journalists to seek prior permission before conducting interviews with Indian citizens and organizations. Subsequently, in May 2020, the Indian government asked two Chinese journalists to leave the country for allegedly violating journalistic norms.

China's move is also part of a larger effort to control the narrative around the COVID-19 pandemic. Chinese authorities have been accused of suppressing information about the outbreak of the virus and silencing whistleblowers. By expelling Indian journalists, China is attempting to exert more control over what information is being reported about the pandemic.

Impact on Press Freedom

China's decision to expel most Indian journalists has serious implications for press freedom in the country. China already ranks as one of the worst countries in the world for press freedom, where journalists face censorship, harassment, and imprisonment for their work. With the expulsion of Indian journalists, there will be even less scrutiny of the Chinese government's actions and policies.

The expulsion of journalists has also come at a time when China has been cracking down on dissent in Hong Kong. In recent months, China has imposed a national security law on Hong Kong that has curtailed freedom of expression and the media. China's decision to expel Indian journalists is an extension of this broader crackdown on press freedom in the region.

Furthermore, the expulsion of Indian journalists raises serious concerns about the safety and security of journalists in China. In recent years, several foreign journalists have been detained and forcibly expelled from the country. The expulsion of Indian journalists sends a clear message that foreign journalists working in China are at risk of being targeted by the government.

Case Studies of Media Control in China

China's decision to expel Indian journalists is not an isolated incident. The Chinese government exercises strict control over the media and uses various tactics to censor and manipulate the news. Some notable case studies include:

  • The Great Firewall of China: Also known as the Golden Shield Project, the Great Firewall of China is a sophisticated system of online censorship and surveillance. The Chinese government uses this system to block access to websites and social media platforms that are critical of the government.
  • The 50 Cent Party: The 50 Cent Party is a group of internet commentators who are paid by the Chinese government to post comments on social media sites in support of government policies. The term "50 Cent Party" refers to the amount of money they are paid for each post.
  • The Case of Cheng Lei: Cheng Lei was an Australian journalist who worked for China's state-run broadcaster CGTN. In August 2020, she was detained by Chinese authorities on suspicion of "endangering national security." Her detention has been widely criticized as an attempt by the Chinese government to silence critical reporting by foreign journalists.

Conclusion

China's decision to expel most Indian journalists is a worrying development for press freedom and bilateral relations between the two countries. The move is part of a broader effort by China to control the narrative around the COVID-19 pandemic and crack down on dissent within its borders. The expulsion of Indian journalists will make it even more difficult for international media to report on the situation in China and hold the government accountable for its actions.

From a geopolitical perspective, the expulsion of Indian journalists could further strain relations between India and China, which are already tense due to recent border clashes. It could also signal to other countries that China is willing to use its media muscle to retaliate against perceived slights. This could have a chilling effect on press freedom across the region.

It is imperative that we continue to support journalists working in China and other countries where press freedom is under threat. The only way to ensure a free and open society is to hold those in power accountable and give a voice to the voiceless.
