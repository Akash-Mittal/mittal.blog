iPhone in India: Foxconn set to manufacture smartphones in Karnataka by April 2024

India is a country with over 1.3 billion people, and with that comes massive demand for smartphones, particularly iPhones. Apple, the manufacturer of iPhone, has already started manufacturing some of its products in India to cater to the huge Indian market. Recently, it was announced that Foxconn, one of Apple's key manufacturing partners, will start manufacturing iPhones in Karnataka by April 2024.

Gaining market share in India

India is one of the fastest-growing smartphone markets in the world, and Apple has been looking to gain a significant market share in the country for years. However, the high price of iPhones has been a key barrier to entry in a country where the average household income is a fraction of that in developed nations.

Apple first started manufacturing iPhones in India in 2017 to cater to the demand for cheaper iPhones. The company continued to ramp up its production in India and recently announced that it will start manufacturing the iPhone 12 in the country. With the support of Foxconn, Apple is poised to expand its manufacturing capabilities in the country and further accelerate its growth in one of the world's largest smartphone markets.

Benefits of manufacturing iPhones in India

There are several benefits to having manufacturing facilities in India. Firstly, it allows Apple to avoid import duties while also easing congestion at ports. This, in turn, leads to faster delivery of iPhones to Indian customers who no longer have to wait for weeks to get their hands on the latest models. Secondly, it allows Apple to reduce the cost of iPhones by manufacturing them locally, which helps the company make its products more affordable in a price-sensitive market like India.

Case studies

Huawei's strategy of applying for licenses to operate in India failed when the Indian government refused to grant them. As a result, Huawei had to rely on the import of its phones into India, which was met with high competition from other brands leading to a rise in prices and lower sales. Conversely, Samsung has invested heavily in manufacturing in India and has seen the company become the market leader for several years, surpassing the sales of Apple and Xiaomi in the country.

Conclusion

With the news of Foxconn setting up its manufacturing facility in Karnataka, Apple is set to further expand its manufacturing capabilities in India. This will allow the company to cater to the massive Indian market and gain a significant market share. Manufacturing locally will help Apple reduce import duties while also making its products more affordable to the Indian consumer.

In summary, the three main points for Foxconn's manufacturing facility in Karnataka are:

  • To cater to the growing Indian market
  • To avoid import duties, ease congestion at ports, and provide faster service to customers
  • To make iPhones more affordable to Indian consumers
