US Defense Secretary Says Washington Won't Stand for Coercion and Bullying from China

As China continues to flex its muscles in the South China Sea, US Defense Secretary Mark Esper has warned that the United States won't stand for coercion and bullying from China.

Esper made these remarks during his speech at a virtual event of the International Institute for Strategic Studies on 20th August 2020. He said that the US is increasingly concerned about China's use of military, diplomatic, and economic pressure to intimidate its neighbours.

Examples of China's Coercion and Bullying

China's aggressive territorial claims in the South China Sea have been a major concern for the US and its allies. China claims almost all of the South China Sea, disregarding the territorial claims of its neighbours. China has built military structures on several disputed islands in the region and has repeatedly harassed other countries' vessels operating in the area.

Another example of China's bullying is its treatment of Hong Kong. In June 2020, China passed a national security law that imposes new restrictions on Hong Kong. The law gives Beijing broad powers to clamp down on dissent and targets activists and pro-democracy lawmakers in Hong Kong. The law has drawn widespread criticism from the international community.

Case Studies

The Philippines

The Philippines is one of the countries that have been most affected by China's coercion in the South China Sea. In 2012, China seized Scarborough Shoal, a disputed territory in the region, after a standoff with the Philippine Navy. Since then, China has restricted access to Scarborough Shoal, preventing Filipino fishermen from accessing its rich fishing grounds. In 2016, an arbitration tribunal in The Hague ruled in favour of the Philippines, declaring China's territorial claims in the South China Sea as invalid. However, China rejected the ruling and continued its aggressive behaviour in the region.

Taiwan

Taiwan is another country that has faced China's bullying. China considers Taiwan as its own territory and has repeatedly threatened to use military force to reunify with the island. China has also been trying to isolate Taiwan diplomatically and has pressured other countries to cut off ties with the island.

Conclusion

China's aggression and bullying tactics have raised concerns not only in the US but also among its allies in the region. The US has been stepping up its military presence in the Indo-Pacific region to counter China's growing influence. However, a military response alone will not solve the problem. The US needs to work with its allies in the region to develop a unified response to China's aggression. The US should also look for ways to engage China in dialogue and find a peaceful solution to the disputes in the region.
