Pakistan's Collapse and Its Implications for India

Imagine a country that is visibly struggling with its internal problems. A country that's facing issues like political instability, an extremist insurgency, a weak economy, and an unsustainable debt crisis. And if this country happens to be your neighboring state, then it's not a good sign for your national security concerns. Pakistan's collapse can have significant implications for India, its neighbor in the south.

Examples of the Impact of Pakistan's Instability

The Hindu Kush Himalayan region in South Asia, which is an important source of freshwater for India, is also at risk if Pakistan collapses. The destruction of the hydropower infrastructure in Pakistan, which is already suffering from acute water scarcity, could lead to a major water crisis in the entire region. Without adequate water resources, the food production would be severely impacted, which would lead to social unrest and political turmoil.

Furthermore, the stability of Afghanistan's government, which is already on shaky grounds due to its reliance on Pakistan for its security, could be severely affected if Pakistan falls. And if Afghanistan turns into a failed state, it could lead to a spiraling of extremist violence and terrorism in the region, creating a security nightmare for India.

Case Studies That Demonstrate the Consequences of Pakistan's Collapse

In 1971, Pakistan was forced to cede East Pakistan, which became an independent nation called Bangladesh. The secession was primarily due to the unfair treatment of the Bengali people by the Punjabi-dominated Pakistani establishment. The aftermath of the secession led to a refugee crisis, with millions of refugees fleeing into India. The armed conflict between India and Pakistan intensified, resulting in a full-fledged war, which India won conclusively.

If Pakistan collapses today, then it's likely that a similar scenario could play out in the form of a civil war, and the resulting refugee crisis could have severe implications for India's social fabric, economic stability, and national security.

  1. Pakistan's collapse could lead to a severe water crisis in the region, affecting the food production and leading to social unrest.
  2. Instability in Afghanistan could spiral into violent extremism and terrorism, creating a security nightmare for India.
  3. The fallout of Pakistan's collapse could lead to a refugee crisis, damaging India's social fabric, economy, and national security.
