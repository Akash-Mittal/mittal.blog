Can China-India Unease Lead to a Nuclear War?

The world has witnessed many wars in the past, but the consequences of a nuclear war would be catastrophic. There have been many incidents in the recent past that have increased tensions between the two nuclear-armed nations, China and India. This unease could potentially lead to a nuclear war, which would not only affect these two countries but the entire world. It is, therefore, important to examine the grounds upon which a nuclear war between these two nations could start.

Case Studies of China and India's Tensions

  • Galwan Valley Incident: In June 2020, a deadly clash between Indian and Chinese soldiers in the Galwan Valley of Ladakh resulted in the deaths of at least 20 Indian soldiers and an unknown number of Chinese soldiers. This was the worst border clash between the two countries in over four decades, and tensions have remained high ever since.
  • Trade Relations: India has also been wary of China's growing economic influence in South Asia. In 2017, India boycotted China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) as it includes projects in Pakistan-administered Kashmir, which India claims as its own territory. Recently, India banned several Chinese apps, including popular social media platform TikTok, citing national security concerns.
  • Border Disputes: China and India share a disputed border, with both claiming ownership over certain areas. This has resulted in multiple military stand-offs, the most recent of which occurred in May 2021 with both sides accusing the other of violating the Line of Actual Control (LAC).

Three Factors That Could Trigger a Nuclear War

While a nuclear war between China and India may seem unlikely, it is not impossible. Here are three factors that could trigger such a catastrophic event:

  1. Border Disputes: If the border disputes between China and India escalate and lead to a military conflict, it could potentially trigger a nuclear war. Both countries possess nuclear weapons, and any military conflict could turn into a nuclear one if either side perceives that its sovereignty and territorial integrity are threatened.
  2. Regional Influence: China is wary of India's growing influence in the region and has been taking steps to counter it. If India continues to assert its role as a regional power, it could lead to further tensions with China and potentially result in a nuclear war.
  3. Nuclear Deterrence: Both China and India possess nuclear weapons as a deterrent against each other. However, if either side perceives that the other is weakening its nuclear deterrence, it could lead to a pre-emptive nuclear strike resulting in a nuclear war.

Conclusion

The world cannot afford a nuclear war, especially between two countries with such large nuclear arsenals. China and India need to find a peaceful solution to their border disputes and economic rivalry, and avoid any actions that could lead to a military conflict. Diplomatic efforts need to be made to ensure that the lines of communication remain open between the two nations, and a peaceful resolution to any disputes is achieved. The stakes are too high to let tensions between these two nations escalate any further.
