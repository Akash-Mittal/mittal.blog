10 Worst Rail Accidents in the Last Decade

Rail travel is considered one of the safest modes of transportation. However, accidents still happen. And when they do, the consequences can be disastrous. In the last decade, there have been several rail accidents that have claimed numerous lives and caused severe damage.

1. India's Patna-Indore Express Derailment

The Patna-Indore Express was headed to Indore from Patna when it derailed near Kanpur in the early morning hours of November 20, 2016. The accident claimed over 150 lives and injured more than 200 passengers. The cause of the accident was found to be a track fracture.

2. Pakistan's Tezgam Train Fire

The Tezgam Train was traveling from Karachi to Rawalpindi when it caught fire near Liaquatpur, Pakistan, on October 31, 2019. The accident claimed over 70 lives and injured more than 40 passengers. The fire was caused by a gas cylinder explosion that was being used by passengers to cook breakfast.

3. Greece's Megara Train Derailment

The Megara Train was traveling from Athens to the Peloponnese region when it derailed near Megara, Greece, on July 13, 2017. The accident claimed four lives and injured more than five passengers. The cause of the accident was found to be a misaligned switch.

4. India's Jagdalpur-Bhubaneswar Express Derailment

The Jagdalpur-Bhubaneswar Express was headed to Bhubaneswar from Jagdalpur when it derailed near Rayagada, India, on January 21, 2017. The accident claimed over 40 lives and injured more than 70 passengers. The cause of the accident was found to be a track fracture.

5. Pakistan's Quetta Train Accident

The Quetta Train was traveling from Quetta to Rawalpindi when it derailed near Aab-e-Gum, Pakistan, on October 6, 2019. The accident claimed over 20 lives and injured more than 30 passengers. The cause of the accident was found to be a track malfunction.

6. India's Kalinga Utkal Express Derailment

The Kalinga Utkal Express was headed to Haridwar from Puri when it derailed near Khatauli, India, on August 19, 2017. The accident claimed over 20 lives and injured more than 150 passengers. The cause of the accident was found to be a track fracture.

7. Pakistan's Karachi Train Collision

Two trains collided near Karachi, Pakistan, on November 3, 2016. The accident claimed over 20 lives and injured more than 100 passengers. The cause of the accident was found to be a signal malfunction.

8. India's Hirakhand Express Derailment

The Hirakhand Express was traveling from Jagdalpur to Bhubaneswar when it derailed near Kuneru, India, on January 22, 2017. The accident claimed over 30 lives and injured more than 100 passengers. The cause of the accident was found to be a track sabotage by Maoist rebels.

9. Pakistan's Multan Train Derailment

The Multan Train was traveling from Multan to Rawalpindi when it derailed near Khanewal, Pakistan, on October 12, 2019. The accident claimed over 10 lives and injured more than 70 passengers. The cause of the accident was found to be a track malfunction.

10. India's Dibrugarh Rajdhani Express Derailment

The Dibrugarh Rajdhani Express was headed to Dibrugarh from New Delhi when it derailed near Chapra, India, on December 28, 2013. The accident claimed over four lives and injured more than 20 passengers. The cause of the accident was found to be a rail fracture.

Conclusion

In conclusion, rail accidents can happen due to several reasons such as track malfunction, misaligned switches, signal malfunction, track sabotage, and even gas cylinder explosions in some cases. It is crucial for railway authorities to ensure regular checks and maintenance of tracks, signals, and switches to avoid such devastating accidents. Furthermore, passengers should also be aware of safety measures and adhere to them while traveling on trains to ensure their safety.
