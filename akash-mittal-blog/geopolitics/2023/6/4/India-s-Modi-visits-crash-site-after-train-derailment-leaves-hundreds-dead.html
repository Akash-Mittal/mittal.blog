India's Modi visits crash site after train derailment leaves hundreds dead

On Sunday, November 21, 2021, India's Prime Minister Narendra Modi visited the site of a train derailment in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh. The incident, which occurred on November 20, left hundreds of passengers dead and injured. This tragic event has once again brought to light the importance of railway safety in India and the need for urgent reforms.

The Importance of Railway Safety in India

India has one of the largest railway networks in the world, which carries millions of passengers and freight every day. However, the country has a poor record of railway safety, with frequent accidents, derailments, and collisions occurring due to various reasons, including human error, technical glitches, and poor infrastructure.

According to official data, there were 10,360 train accidents in India between 2011 and 2020, which resulted in 3,639 deaths and 9,822 injuries. The most common causes of these accidents were derailments, collisions, and level crossing accidents. The railway authorities have been criticized for their lax approach to safety measures, including maintenance of tracks and equipment, training of staff, and implementation of safety protocols.

Case Studies

The Khatauli Train Derailment, 2017

One of the deadliest train accidents in recent times occurred in Khatauli, Uttar Pradesh, on August 19, 2017, when 14 coaches of the Puri-Haridwar Kalinga Utkal Express derailed, killing 23 passengers and injuring 156 others. The accident was caused by a fracture in the railway track, which was allegedly not detected during the routine maintenance.

The accident highlighted the poor state of railway infrastructure and maintenance in India, and the lack of safety measures in place to prevent such disasters. The government ordered an inquiry into the accident and promised to take corrective measures to improve railway safety.

The Amritsar Train Accident, 2018

Another tragic incident occurred on October 19, 2018, in Amritsar, Punjab, when a speeding train hit a crowd of people watching Dussehra celebrations near a railway track, killing 61 people and injuring 143 others. The accident was caused by the failure of the railway authorities to inform the local authorities and the public about the train schedule and the closure of the railway crossing during the event.

The accident raised questions about the negligence of the railway authorities and the lack of coordination between the different agencies involved in organizing public events. The government ordered an inquiry into the accident and promised to improve safety measures and procedures for organizing public events near railway tracks.

The Need for Urgent Reforms

The recent train derailment in Uttar Pradesh once again highlights the urgent need for reforms in the Indian railway sector. The government and the railway authorities need to take immediate steps to improve safety measures, upgrade infrastructure, and implement effective safety protocols.

Some of the reforms that are needed include:

  1. Investment in railway infrastructure and maintenance to ensure the safety and efficiency of the railway network.
  2. Training and skilling of railway staff to improve their competency and awareness of safety measures.
  3. Implementation of advanced safety technologies, such as automatic train protection (ATP), to prevent accidents caused by human error or technical glitches.

Conclusion

The train derailment in Uttar Pradesh is a tragic reminder of the need for urgent reforms in the Indian railway sector. The government and the railway authorities need to prioritize safety and invest in the necessary infrastructure and technology to improve the safety and efficiency of the railway network. Unless concrete steps are taken to address the systemic issues that plague the Indian railway sector, such accidents will continue to occur, putting millions of lives at risk.
