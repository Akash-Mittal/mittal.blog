Bipartisan Leaders Invite Indian Prime Minister Modi to Address Congress

There have been few instances in the history of US-India relations when a US President and an Indian Prime Minister shared a personal equation that transcended the contours of diplomacy. However, the current US President, Barack Obama, shares a warm personal relationship with Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi, which has brought a new dimension to US-India relations over the last few years. In continuation of this trend, a group of bipartisan leaders from the United States Congress recently extended an invitation to Indian Prime Minister Modi to address a joint session of Congress.

The Invitation

The invitation to Prime Minister Modi was extended jointly by Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell, Senate Democratic Leader Harry Reid, House Speaker Paul Ryan, and House Democratic Leader Nancy Pelosi. In their letter to Modi, the leaders stated that they would be honored to host the Indian Prime Minister to address a joint session of Congress on June 8, 2016.

The invitation is significant because it is being extended to a leader of a major power, which is also a rising economic power, at a time when there are many challenges to global peace and security. The leaders emphasized the strategic importance of US-India relations and the need for greater cooperation in areas such as international trade, military cooperation, and counter-terrorism.

The Background

The invitation to Prime Minister Modi is not an isolated event but the culmination of a process that began almost two years ago when President Obama, during his visit to India, said that India was a pivotal power in the Asia-Pacific region. Since then, there has been a steady growth in US-India relations, characterized by not just cooperation on trade and economic issues but also on strategic issues such as counter-terrorism, maritime security, and military-to-military relations.

In the area of trade, President Obama and Prime Minister Modi have set a target of increasing bilateral trade five-fold to $500 billion. In 2015, the two countries signed a strategic and commercial accord which aims to reduce barriers to investment and trade and increase cooperation on innovation and entrepreneurship. While the target of $500 billion may be ambitious, the two countries are well on track to meet their bilateral trade target of $100 billion by 2020.

In the area of defense and security cooperation, the two countries have signed a number of agreements, including the Joint Strategic Vision for the Asia-Pacific and Indian Ocean Region, which seeks to ensure a peaceful, open, and prosperous Indo-Pacific region. The US has also agreed to transfer technology to India to build a fleet of fighter jets under the "Make in India" initiative. The two countries have also deepened their strategic partnership in the Indian Ocean Region, which is of vital importance for the security and stability of the Indo-Pacific region.

The two countries have also stepped up their cooperation on counter-terrorism. During his visit to India in January 2015, President Obama announced that the US and India would hold a bilateral dialogue on counter-terrorism. This dialogue has since been held twice, in June and December 2015, and has focused on areas such as sharing of information, capacity-building, and countering violent extremism.

The Significance

The invitation to Prime Minister Modi is significant for three reasons:

Firstly

It is a testament to the bipartisan support for US-India relations. The invitation was extended jointly by leaders of both parties, reflecting the fact that there is a broad consensus in the US on the importance of bilateral relations with India. This is a positive development for India, which can count on the support of both parties in the US.

Secondly

It is a recognition of the strategic importance of US-India relations. The invitation comes at a time when there are many challenges to global peace and security, and the two countries need to work together to address these challenges. The joint statement issued during President Obama's visit to India in January 2015 stated that "The India-US partnership can be an anchor of global security, prosperity and stability." The invitation to Prime Minister Modi is a reaffirmation of this sentiment.

Thirdly

It is an opportunity for Prime Minister Modi to address the US Congress and the American people, and articulate India's vision for the future. In recent years, India has emerged as a major economic power, with a growing role in global affairs. Prime Minister Modi's visit to the US will be an opportunity to showcase India's achievements, its aspirations for the future, and its willingness to play a proactive role in addressing global challenges.

Conclusion

The invitation to Prime Minister Modi to address Congress is a clear indication of the positive trajectory of India-US relations. The two countries have made significant progress in areas such as trade, defense, and counter-terrorism, and are poised to make even greater strides in the coming years. Prime Minister Modi's visit to the US will be an opportunity to deepen bilateral cooperation and build on the strong foundation of friendship and partnership that exists between the two countries.
