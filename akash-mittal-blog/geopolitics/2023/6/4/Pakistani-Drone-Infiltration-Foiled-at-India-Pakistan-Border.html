Pakistani Drone Infiltration Foiled at India Pakistan Border

On June 20, 2021, Indian Border Security Force (BSF) troops foiled a drone infiltration attempt by Pakistan at the India Pakistan border in Gurdaspur district of Punjab. The drone was discovered near the Tendiwala border outpost in the district. The security forces seized the drone along with a large amount of arms and ammunition, including grenades and automatic weapons.

Unfortunately, this is not an isolated incident. Pakistani drones have been increasingly used to smuggle narcotics and weapons into India. In March 2021, the Anti-Terrorism Squad (ATS) of Maharashtra Police arrested two men in Nagpur for allegedly smuggling drugs from Mumbai to Nagpur using a Pakistani drone. The ATS seized heroin worth around Rs. 40 crores from the accused.

This is a serious problem that needs to be addressed by both India and Pakistan. Let's look at some case studies to understand the magnitude of the issue.

Case Study 1: Jammu and Kashmir

Jammu and Kashmir has been a center of terrorist activities for many years. In recent times, cross-border infiltration has increased, and drone technology has made it easier for terrorists to smuggle weapons and explosives into the state. According to a report by the Jammu and Kashmir Police, around 11 kg of heroin was seized in 2019, which was smuggled into the state using drones.

In July 2020, the Indian army destroyed a drone which was attempting to cross the border from Pakistan. The drone was carrying a payload of weapons and ammunition, including AK-47 rifles and grenades. The army has since been on high alert and has been using counter-drone technology to foil any attempt of infiltration through this route.

Case Study 2: Punjab

Punjab has been a major transit point for drug trafficking in India. According to a report by the Narcotics Control Bureau (NCB), around 300 kg of heroin was seized in Punjab in 2020. The report also stated that drones are being used extensively to smuggle drugs into the state from Pakistan.

In October 2020, the Punjab Police seized a drone which was being used to deliver weapons from Pakistan. The drone was carrying a payload of two AK-47 rifles, four magazines, and 200 cartridges. The police were able to trace the drone to a house in Amritsar and arrested two people in connection with the case.

Case Study 3: Maharashtra

Maharashtra has also seen an increase in the use of drones for smuggling drugs. In March 2021, the Maharashtra ATS arrested two men in Nagpur for allegedly smuggling drugs from Mumbai using a Pakistani drone. The ATS seized around 50 kilograms of heroin from the accused. In another incident in May 2021, the Mumbai Police arrested two men for allegedly using drones to smuggle drugs into the city from Navi Mumbai.

These case studies show that the problem of Pakistani drones infiltrating India is not limited to one state or region. It is a national problem that needs a coordinated effort to address.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the use of drones by Pakistani terrorists to smuggle narcotics and weapons into India is a serious concern for the country's security forces. The Indian government needs to take urgent steps to counter this threat, including investing in counter-drone technology, strengthening border security, and coordinating with Pakistan to address the issue. As citizens of India, we also have a responsibility to report any suspicious activities near the border and support the efforts of our security forces in securing our nation.
