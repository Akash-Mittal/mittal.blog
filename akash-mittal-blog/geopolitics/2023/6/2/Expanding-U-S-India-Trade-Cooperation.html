Expanding U.S.-India Trade Cooperation
Why It is More Important Than Narrow Dialogues

Once upon a time, there was a small business owner named Raj who wanted to sell his handcrafted products to customers around the world. He had heard that American consumers loved unique and high-quality items, so he decided to explore the possibility of exporting his products to the United States.

Raj quickly realized that there were many hurdles he had to overcome. He needed to find reliable buyers who were interested in his products, comply with complex regulations and documentation requirements, and manage shipping and logistics. On the other side, U.S. importers faced their own challenges in sourcing, purchasing, and importing goods from India.

Despite these obstacles, Raj and many other business owners in India and the United States are finding ways to expand their trade cooperation and fuel economic growth in both countries. They understand that trade is not a zero-sum game, but rather a mutually beneficial exchange of goods and services that creates jobs, increases innovation, and improves living standards.

Let us take a look at some of the quantifiable examples of U.S.-India trade cooperation:

  • The bilateral trade in goods and services between the United States and India reached $149 billion in 2019, up from $126 billion in 2017. This is a significant increase, but there is still ample room for expansion considering the size and potential of both economies.
  • The United States is one of the largest investors in India, with over $40 billion in cumulative foreign direct investment (FDI) by American companies since 2000. This investment has supported the growth of various sectors, such as information technology, pharmaceuticals, and energy.
  • Indian companies have also been investing in the United States, with over $18 billion in FDI in the same period. These investments have created jobs in many states and contributed to the development of local communities.
  • The United States and India have been working together to address trade barriers and promote greater market access for their respective businesses. For example, they have been negotiating a bilateral trade agreement, which if successful, would significantly boost two-way trade and investment. They have also been cooperating in forums such as the World Trade Organization (WTO) to advance shared interests.
  • Individual companies and entrepreneurs are also forging partnerships that leverage the strengths of both countries. For instance, Indian IT firms are collaborating with U.S. companies to develop cutting-edge technologies in areas such as artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and cybersecurity. Similarly, American manufacturers are teaming up with Indian suppliers to access cost-competitive and high-quality components for their products.

The above examples demonstrate that U.S.-India trade cooperation is not only desirable but also feasible. It requires concerted efforts by both governments, businesses, and civil society to identify and address the challenges and opportunities of trade, and to build trust and mutual understanding.

Importantly, expanding U.S.-India trade cooperation is more important than narrow dialogues that focus only on specific issues or disputes. While it is essential to resolve trade frictions and ensure a level playing field for all stakeholders, it is equally critical to look at the big picture and the long-term benefits of trade for both countries.

There are several reasons why U.S.-India trade cooperation deserves priority attention:

  1. Both countries have complementary strengths and needs that can be leveraged through trade. For instance, India has a large and young population that is increasingly tech-savvy and mobile. The United States has advanced technologies, capital, and expertise that can help India develop its infrastructure, education, and healthcare systems.
  2. Both countries face common challenges and opportunities in the global economy, such as the rise of China, the digital revolution, and climate change. By working together, they can benefit from each other's experiences, insights, and solutions, and enhance their competitiveness and resilience.
  3. Both countries share democratic values, cultural affinities, and strategic interests that make them natural partners. By deepening their economic ties, they can strengthen their political, diplomatic, and security cooperation, and promote peace and stability in the region and beyond.

In conclusion, expanding U.S.-India trade cooperation is a win-win solution that can bring tangible benefits to both countries and strengthen their ties. It requires vision, determination, and creativity from all stakeholders, and a commitment to building trust and understanding. By doing so, they can create a brighter future for Raj and many other entrepreneurs who want to bring the world closer together.

Key Takeaways
U.S.-India trade reached $149 billion in 2019, but can be further expanded.
Cooperation between governments, businesses, and civil society is crucial for realizing the potential of trade.
Expanding U.S.-India trade is not only desirable but also feasible, and can bring long-term benefits to both countries.
