Bank of China Chairman Visits PNG Amid Sino-U.S. Rivalry

How economics and finance are being used as weapons in a geopolitical struggle

The Story

The Bank of China Chairman has recently been to Papua New Guinea as part of his tour of Asia-Pacific countries. His visit comes amid a trade and finance rivalry between China and the United States. Both nations are vying for economic influence with the Pacific nation, which has significant natural resources and a strategic location at the crossroads of Asia and the Pacific Ocean.

In recent years, China has made considerable investments in Papua New Guinea, developing the country's infrastructure and extracting its resources. The United States has responded with its own aid and investment, aimed at curtailing China's growing influence in the region.

The Bank of China has played a significant role in financing China's infrastructure projects in Papua New Guinea. According to the Papua New Guinea Investment Promotion Authority, Chinese investment in the country has grown from US$300 million in 2013 to over US$4.5 billion in 2020. The Bank of China has also provided financing for Papua New Guinea's government bonds, which were issued to cover the country's budget deficit.

However, these economic ties are increasingly being viewed through the prism of geopolitical rivalry, with both China and the United States using trade and finance as weapons in their struggle for influence. Papua New Guinea finds itself caught in the middle of this struggle, with its economic development becoming entangled with a wider geopolitical rivalry.

Quantifiable Examples

China's investment in Papua New Guinea has had a significant impact on the country's economy. According to the World Bank, Papua New Guinea's GDP grew by 5.8% in 2019, partly as a result of Chinese investment. In addition, Chinese infrastructure projects such as highways, bridges, and ports have helped to improve the country's transport infrastructure and boost trade.

However, China's investment has also been criticized for its lack of transparency and the social and environmental impact of its projects. For example, the PNG government's deal with a Chinese state-owned company to build a fishery processing plant has been criticized for its potential impact on local fishing industries and the marine environment.

The United States has also made its mark in Papua New Guinea, primarily through its aid programs and investment in health and education. According to the US State Department, the United States provided US$569 million in aid to Papua New Guinea in 2020, with a focus on health, education, and governance. The United States has also invested in Papua New Guinea's energy sector, with ExxonMobil leading the development of the country's liquefied natural gas industry.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the rivalry between China and the United States is playing out in Papua New Guinea in the form of trade and finance. While both nations are investing in the country's economic development, their motives are often seen as being driven by geopolitical considerations rather than a genuine desire to help Papua New Guinea.

However, it is important for Papua New Guinea to balance its economic ties with both China and the United States, rather than being forced to take sides in the rivalry. By diversifying its economic partnerships, Papua New Guinea can ensure its economic development is not compromised by external factors.

At the same time, it is essential for China and the United States to recognize that their rivalry cannot be allowed to undermine the economic development of other countries. Trade and finance should be used to foster economic cooperation and growth, rather than as tools in a geopolitical struggle.
