What Does EU's Growing Engagement With India, Bangladesh, Nepal and Bhutan Mean For?

As a member of the European Parliament, I've had the opportunity to witness firsthand the increasing engagement between the European Union (EU) and the countries of India, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Bhutan. It's been exciting to see the partnerships and collaborations that are developing across a broad range of areas, including trade, investment, education, and cultural exchange.

One of the most significant areas of collaboration between the EU and these countries is trade. For example, according to the European Parliament's website, two-way trade between the EU and India amounted to €115 billion in 2018. Similarly, the EU is Bangladesh's largest trading partner, and trade between the two rose from €17.2 billion in 2014 to €24.4 billion in 2019.

In addition to trade, the EU is also partnering with these countries on initiatives to combat climate change. For example, the EU is working with Nepal to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and strengthen climate resilience in the agriculture sector, and has committed €8 million to the initiative.

Anecdotes and Case Studies

One area where the EU's engagement with India has been particularly fruitful is higher education. Many Indian students are pursuing degrees at European universities, and there are several exchange programs in place to facilitate these opportunities. For example, the Erasmus+ program has supported over 5,000 students and staff members from India to study or train in Europe.

Similarly, the EU's partnership with Bangladesh has had a positive impact on the country's garment industry. According to a report by the International Labour Organization (ILO), the EU's engagement has "contributed to significant improvements in the working conditions of millions of workers in the Bangladesh garment sector."

Conclusion in Three Points

  1. The EU's growing engagement with India, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Bhutan is leading to increased economic opportunities and partnerships.
  2. The EU is playing a significant role in supporting initiatives aimed at combatting climate change and promoting sustainable development in these countries.
  3. Through programs such as Erasmus+ and partnerships with the garment industry in Bangladesh, the EU is also promoting cultural exchange and helping to improve the lives of people in these countries.
