India Upgrading BrahMos to Close Missile Gap with China

Once upon a time, there was a young boy named Raj who lived in a small village in India. Raj dreamt of being an aerospace engineer and developing missile technology for his country. The BrahMos missile is a joint project of India and Russia. It is named after two rivers, the Brahmaputra of India and the Moskva of Russia. The missile is capable of traveling at speeds of up to Mach 3.0 and has a range of about 290 km. It is the fastest cruise missile in the world and can be launched from land, sea, and air.

India's military planners are currently upgrading the BrahMos missile to close the missile gap with China. China has developed a range of missiles that can reach India, and the BrahMos is seen as a key tool to deter China. In recent years, China has also upgraded its missile technology, and India needs to stay ahead to maintain its strategic advantage in the region.

  • The upgraded BrahMos missile will have a range of about 500 km, which is more than double its current range. This will allow it to reach deeper into Chinese territory.
  • The upgraded BrahMos missile will have a speed of about Mach 5.0, which is almost twice as fast as the current version. This will make it harder for China to intercept the missile.
  • About 50% of the components of the BrahMos missile are made in India. The upgrade will increase this to about 65%, which will boost the Indian defense industry.
