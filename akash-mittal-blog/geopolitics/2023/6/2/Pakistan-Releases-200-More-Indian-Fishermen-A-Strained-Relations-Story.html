Pakistan Releases 200 More Indian Fishermen: A Strained Relations Story

India and Pakistan have a long-standing history of being arch-rivals for years and any incident that further strains their relations is always met with a sense of dread and unease. The release of 200 Indian fishermen by Pakistan has come as a sigh of relief and an opportunity to repair their relationship.

The Crux of the Matter

The Pakistani authorities on Friday released 200 more Indian fishermen as a gesture of goodwill amid strained relations. The Indian fishermen crew had been detained in Pakistani jails for illegal fishing in its waters. According to the reports, these fishermen were taken into custody for allegedly entering Pakistani territorial waters illegally and fishing there.

Indian fishermen have always had a longstanding issue with Pakistan when it comes to fishing in the Arabian sea and have often been caught by Pakistani authorities. India has always maintained that the fishermen's issue is a humanitarian one and that they should be released immediately, but Pakistan has been hard on them, calling them violators of international maritime laws.

The release of the Indian fishermen is a much-needed respite for the Indian government and the fishing community as a whole. This move could act as a catalyst in the larger scheme of things, allowing for a much-needed thaw in the otherwise frosty relations between the two nations.

India and Pakistan have differences when it comes to the maritime border, owing to the nature of the sea border present between the two countries. These disputes have often led to the detention of hundreds of fishermen from both countries. As per the information sourced by the Indian authorities, about 430 Indian fishermen are in the custody of Pakistan, while around 270 Pakistani fishermen are in Indian jails. In March 2020, India had released 120 Pakistani fishermen as a goodwill gesture.

  • In 2019, India and Pakistan exchanged the lists of prisoners lodged in each other's jails, according to the provisions of the Agreement on Consular Access signed between the two nations in 2008.
  • Bilal Ahmad, a spokesperson for Pakistan Fisherfolk Forum, has stated that the release of these Indian fishermen could serve to help in strengthening the friendship and peace between India and Pakistan.

Strengthening the Relationship

The release of the Indian fishermen marks a step in the right direction; the relations between India and Pakistan have often been fraught with tension and acrimony. By releasing the fishermen, Pakistan has shown an inclination towards promoting peace and stability with India.
