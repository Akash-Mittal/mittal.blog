What About PM Modi? Imran Khan Attacks India to Defend Afghan Taliban

Recently, the Prime Minister of Pakistan, Imran Khan, has been making headlines with his controversial statements about the Afghan Taliban and its relation with India. In a recent interview, Khan defended the Taliban and criticized India for its alleged interference in Afghan affairs. His comments have raised eyebrows around the world, with many wondering what his true intentions are.

The Story Behind the Statements

The Afghan Taliban has been in control of large parts of Afghanistan since US troops began their withdrawal from the country earlier this year. The group has been widely criticized for its human rights abuses and its oppressive policies towards women. However, Imran Khan has defended the Taliban, calling them "normal citizens" who are "sharing power" in Afghanistan.

Khan's comments have been seen by many as an attempt to curry favor with the Taliban, which is widely believed to have ties to Pakistan's military and intelligence agencies. However, Khan has denied any such links and has repeatedly denied accusations that Pakistan is supporting the Taliban's insurgency in Afghanistan.

Despite Khan's denials, there is evidence to suggest that Pakistan has been supporting the Taliban in some way. According to a report by the US Defense Intelligence Agency, Pakistan's Inter-Services Intelligence agency has been providing "logistical support and sanctuary" to the Taliban for years.

In addition, there have been numerous reports of Taliban fighters receiving medical treatment in Pakistan and of Pakistan's intelligence agencies providing the group with weapons and training. Although Khan has denied these allegations, many are skeptical of his claims.

Conclusion
