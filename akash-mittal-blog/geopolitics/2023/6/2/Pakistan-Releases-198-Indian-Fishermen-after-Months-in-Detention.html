Pakistan Releases 198 Indian Fishermen after Months in Detention

Pakistan has released 198 Indian fishermen who had been detained for illegal fishing in its territorial waters. The move is seen as a goodwill gesture from Pakistan towards India amid the ongoing tensions between the two nuclear-armed neighbors.

According to reports, the fishermen were handed over to the Indian authorities at the Wagah-Attari border on Monday. This is the second batch of Indian fishermen released by Pakistan in the past two months. In December, Pakistan had released 145 Indian fishermen.

The release of Indian fishermen by Pakistan has been a welcome move by the Indian government and the fishing communities in India, who have been demanding the release of their fellow fishermen from Pakistan for months.

An Interesting Story

The story of Indian fishermen being detained by Pakistan is not a new one. The two countries share a long maritime boundary in the Arabian Sea, and the fishermen from both countries often cross the boundary unintentionally while fishing. However, the fishermen often fall prey to the complex and strained relations between the two countries.

The incidents of detention of Indian fishermen by Pakistan have increased in recent years. According to Indian officials, more than 700 Indian fishermen are currently in detention in Pakistan, and hundreds of Pakistani fishermen are in detention in India.

The detention of fishermen not only affects their families but also has a significant impact on the fishing industry, which is the livelihood of many coastal communities in both countries.

Quantifiable Examples

The detention of fishermen has been a contentious issue between India and Pakistan for years. Both countries have accused each other of violating their territorial waters and interfering with their fishing activities.

According to a report by the Indian Ministry of External Affairs, there were more than 500 cases of Indian fishermen being arrested by Pakistan between 2015 and 2018.

In contrast, there were only 33 cases of Pakistani fishermen being arrested by India during the same period, according to a report by Pakistan Fisherfolk Forum.

The asymmetry in the number of arrests and detentions of fishermen by the two countries is a cause of concern for the fishing communities in India, who feel that their counterparts in Pakistan are being unfairly targeted.

Rajesh, a fisherman from Gujarat, was detained by Pakistan in 2019 while fishing near the international maritime boundary. He was released after spending four months in jail, but his boat and fishing equipment were seized by the Pakistani authorities.

"I was just doing my job. I didn't know I had crossed the boundary. We have been fishing in these waters for generations. It's our livelihood. But now, we always fear getting arrested or detained by the Pakistani authorities," Rajesh says.
