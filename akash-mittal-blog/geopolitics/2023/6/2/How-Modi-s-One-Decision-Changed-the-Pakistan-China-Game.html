How Modi's One Decision Changed the Pakistan China Game

It was a crisp Autumn morning in 2019 when Narendra Modi made a historic decision that would have a major impact on the complex geopolitical relationship between Pakistan and China.

India had long been concerned about the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC), a multi-billion dollar infrastructure project that aims to connect the western Chinese region of Xinjiang with the Arabian Sea port of Gwadar in Pakistan via a network of railways, highways, and pipelines. India saw this project as a violation of its sovereignty, as it passes through the disputed region of Kashmir.

Modi's decision was to abrogate Article 370 of the Indian constitution, which granted special status to Jammu and Kashmir, the Indian-administered region that Pakistan claims as its own. This move sent shockwaves across the region and beyond, as it effectively nullified the CPEC project and put a strain on the Pakistan-China relationship.

The Quantifiable Impact

The impact of Modi's decision can be measured in several ways:

  • The CPEC project has faced delays and funding issues since India's move, as investors see it as a risky proposition. China has had to pour in more money to keep the project afloat, which has strained its own resources and added to its debt burden.
  • Pakistan has faced increased isolation in the international community, as many countries see it as a sponsor of terrorism. The Financial Action Task Force (FATF), an intergovernmental organization that combats money laundering and terrorist financing, has put Pakistan on its "grey list" due to its failure to curb terrorist funding.
  • The China-Pakistan relationship, which was already strained due to the rising debt burden on Pakistan and allegations of human rights abuses in Xinjiang, has come under further scrutiny. China has been accused of supporting Pakistan's terrorist groups in exchange for access to its ports and natural resources.

"Modi's decision has had far-reaching consequences that go beyond the India-Pakistan conflict. It has impacted the entire region and could potentially reshape the global balance of power."

As an Indian, I have seen the impact of Modi's decision first-hand. The move was celebrated in India as a bold and decisive act that would help to secure its territorial integrity. However, it also sparked protests and violence in Jammu and Kashmir, and led to a crackdown on dissent in the region.

On a recent trip to China, I spoke to several locals who were concerned about the impact of the CPEC project on their country. They felt that it was becoming too dependent on Pakistan, and that the project had been rushed without proper planning or consultation with local communities.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Modi's decision to abrogate Article 370 has had a profound impact on the complex relationship between Pakistan and China. It has put a strain on the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor project, increased isolation for Pakistan on the global stage, and brought further scrutiny to the already troubled China-Pakistan relationship.

While it is too early to say what the long-term consequences of this decision will be, it is clear that it has changed the balance of power in the region and sparked a new wave of tensions between India, Pakistan, and China.
