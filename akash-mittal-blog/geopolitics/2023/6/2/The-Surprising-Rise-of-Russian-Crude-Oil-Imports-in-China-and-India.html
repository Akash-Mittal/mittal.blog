The Surprising Rise of Russian Crude Oil Imports in China and India

Imagine yourself walking along the banks of the Volga River in Russia on an early morning. Suddenly, you see a massive oil tanker floating downstream, carrying thousands of barrels of crude oil. You wonder, where is all of this oil going? As it turns out, a significant portion of it is being imported by China and India, two of the world's largest oil-consuming nations.

In May, China and India imported a combined total of 2.5 million metric tons of crude oil from Russia, according to Anadolu Agency reports. This represents a 58% increase from the same month last year. In fact, Russia has become the second-largest source of crude oil for both nations, behind only Saudi Arabia.

China's imports of Russian crude oil have surged to record levels this year, with May volumes up over 23% compared to the previous month. Meanwhile, India's imports of Russian crude oil in May alone increased by a whopping 370% compared to the same month last year.

In April of this year, I traveled to China to visit a friend who works in the energy sector. During our conversations, he mentioned how Chinese companies were increasingly turning to Russia for their crude oil needs. He explained that this was due to Russia's willingness to offer competitive pricing and favorable contract terms, as well as its proximity to China.

Later that month, I met with an Indian businessman who was involved in a joint venture with a Russian energy firm. He spoke enthusiastically about the potential for deeper collaboration between the two countries, especially in the area of energy. He noted that the rise in Russian crude oil imports was just the beginning of a larger trend, as India seeks to reduce its heavy dependence on Middle Eastern suppliers.
