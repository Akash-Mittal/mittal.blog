Why China and India Trust Pharma More Than Europe and US

The Story of a Sick Child in Rural India

When Sharmila's son fell ill with a high fever and a persistent cough, she panicked. The nearest hospital was a two-hour drive from her village, and she didn't have the money to pay for her son's treatment. She resorted to buying medicine from a local pharmacy, hoping it would relieve her son's symptoms.

To her surprise, the medicine worked, and her son recovered within a few days. When she asked the pharmacist about the brand of the medicine, he replied: "It's made by a company in India, and it's affordable and effective."

This story illustrates a phenomenon that is happening in many parts of the world: people are turning to local and regional pharma companies instead of multinational ones, because they trust them more and find their products more accessible and affordable.

The Numbers Speak for Themselves

A recent survey conducted by Edelman, a leading global communications firm, found that 71% of respondents in China and 62% of respondents in India trust their country's pharma companies, compared to only 38% in the US and 29% in Europe.

This trust is not just a matter of perception. It is based on tangible factors such as price, quality, and accessibility. In India, for example, the government has implemented policies that promote the use of generic drugs, which are often cheaper and equally effective as their branded counterparts. This has helped local pharma companies gain a foothold in the market.

Another factor that contributes to the trust in local pharma companies is their focus on addressing the specific health needs of their populations. For example, Indian companies are known for producing affordable drugs to treat diseases such as tuberculosis, malaria, and HIV, which are prevalent in their country.

Implications for the Pharma Industry

The fact that local and regional pharma companies are gaining trust and market share poses both challenges and opportunities for multinational pharma companies. On one hand, they need to prove that their products are worth the premium price they charge and that they are committed to addressing the health needs of the populations they serve.

On the other hand, they can learn from the success of local pharma companies and adapt their strategies accordingly. For example, they can invest in research and development of drugs that target specific diseases prevalent in a region, or partner with local companies to manufacture and distribute their products more efficiently.

Ultimately, the key to success in the pharma industry lies in building trust with consumers and demonstrating value beyond the bottom line. Companies that can do that will thrive, regardless of their size or location.
