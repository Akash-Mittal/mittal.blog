Why India Must Step Back from the Himalayan Border Confrontation with China



Image Credits  https://www.marketscreener.com/quote/index/TOPIX-INDEX-61714390/news/China-India-must-step-back-from-Himalayan-border-confrontation-Indian-minister-44068789/

Recently, the tensions have escalated along the border between India and China in the Himalayan range. Both countries have reinforced their military presence on the disputed border and engaged in heated rhetoric, making it the largest military build-up in the region in decades. However, escalating the situation is not in the interest of either country, and India must take the initiative to step back from the confrontation.

Why India Should Not Escalate the Conflict

Firstly, the escalation of the conflict could lead to war. The border dispute between India and China in the Himalayas has been ongoing for decades, but a full-scale conflict between the two countries could destabilize the entire region. The two countries have nuclear weapons, and the use of these weapons in a conflict would have serious consequences for the global community.

Secondly, trade between India and China could be affected by the escalation of the conflict. The two countries are each other's largest trading partners, and a war would significantly disrupt this trade. Many Indian businesses rely on Chinese imports, and if the conflict continues, trade between the two countries could be negatively impacted.

Thirdly, the escalation of the conflict could lead to international isolation. India is trying to build a coalition of countries against China, but an escalated conflict could put it in a difficult spot with some of its key partners. The international community has already raised concerns over the situation, and an escalation of the conflict could force India to face international criticism and condemnation.

Case Studies

The current situation in the Himalayan border bears many similarities with the Doklam standoff between India and China in 2017. During the standoff, India and China deployed their troops to a disputed area in the Himalayas, and the situation escalated to a point where both countries seemed to be on the brink of conflict. However, India and China both took steps to deescalate the situation, and the standoff ended peacefully.

Similarly, in 1999, India and Pakistan engaged in the Kargil War. The war was fought over border territory in the Himalayas, and it had serious consequences for the entire region. The conflict led to significant loss of human life, and both India and Pakistan faced international condemnation. After the war ended, both countries were left to pick up the pieces and rebuild their relationship.

Conclusion

The tension along the Himalayan border between India and China must be addressed, but not through escalation of the conflict. The current situation is not in the interest of either country, and both India and China should take the initiative to step back from it. Any escalation of the conflict could have serious economic, political, and security consequences for the entire region, and could have a negative impact on both countries.
