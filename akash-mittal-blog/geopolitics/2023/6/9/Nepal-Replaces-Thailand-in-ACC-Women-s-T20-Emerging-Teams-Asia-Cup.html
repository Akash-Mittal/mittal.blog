Nepal Replaces Thailand in ACC Women's T20 Emerging Teams Asia Cup



Image Credits  https://myrepublica.nagariknetwork.com/news/acc-women-s-t20-emerging-teams-asia-cup-nepal-replaces-thailand-to-take-on-india-a-and-pakistan-a/

Cricket is one of the most popular sports in Nepal, with the national team making waves in international tournaments in recent years. However, it's not just the men's team that is making an impact - the women's team has also been making strides in the sport.

Therefore, it's great news that the Nepali women's team will be taking part in the 2023 ACC Women's T20 Emerging Teams Asia Cup. They replace Thailand in the competition, and will be facing off against India A and Pakistan A in the Hong Kong-based tournament.

The Rise of Women's Cricket in Nepal

Nepal has a long and proud history of cricket, with the men's team first playing in 1951. However, it wasn't until the 2000s that the sport started gaining popularity among women, and the national women's team was formed in 2013.

Since then, the team has gone from strength to strength, winning the gold medal in the South Asian Games in 2019 and taking part in the 2020 Women's T20 World Cup Qualifier in Scotland.

But it hasn't been easy for the women's team. When they first started playing, they faced many challenges - from a lack of facilities and equipment to societal attitudes towards women playing sports.

Overcoming Challenges and Breaking Barriers

One of the biggest challenges faced by the Nepali women's cricket team was the lack of facilities and equipment. When the team was first formed, they didn't have a proper ground to practice on and had to rely on using public parks.

However, they didn't let this obstacle hold them back. Instead, they used their creativity and resourcefulness to find ways to practice and improve their skills. For example, they would practice catching and fielding by using a tennis ball and a wall.

The team also faced societal attitudes towards women playing sports. In Nepal, traditional gender roles can often lead to a lack of support for women's sports, as they are seen as less important than men's sports.

However, the Nepali women's cricket team has been breaking down these barriers and stereotypes by becoming successful on the international stage. With each win and each milestone achieved, they are proving that women's cricket in Nepal is just as important and worthy of recognition as men's cricket.

Conclusion

The inclusion of Nepal in the ACC Women's T20 Emerging Teams Asia Cup is a testament to the hard work and determination of the women's cricket team in the country. Despite facing many challenges and obstacles, they have continued to push forward and achieve success on the international stage.

By taking part in this tournament, they will have the opportunity to showcase their skills and talents to the world, and inspire more women in Nepal to take up the sport.

We wish the Nepali women's cricket team the best of luck in the tournament and look forward to seeing them continue to rise and make an impact on the sport.
