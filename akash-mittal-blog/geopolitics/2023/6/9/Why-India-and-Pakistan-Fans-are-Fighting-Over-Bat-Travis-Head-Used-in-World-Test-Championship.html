Why India and Pakistan Fans are Fighting Over Bat Travis Head Used in World Test Championship

Cricket is not just a sport, it's a religion for many countries. The World Test Championship final between India and New Zealand was one of the most anticipated cricket matches of the year. Although New Zealand emerged as the winner, the Australian batsman Travis Head stole everyone's attention with his outstanding performance. Head scored 163 runs and played a crucial role in Australia reaching the semi-finals of the tournament. However, it's not just Head's performance that is making headlines; it's the bat he used that has become a matter of debate between the Indian and Pakistani fans.

The Origins of the Bat

After the match, a picture of the bat went viral on social media. The bat was designed and produced by Kookaburra, an Australian manufacturing company that is known for manufacturing some of the best cricket bats in the world. Both India and Pakistan have claimed that the bat is from their country and that it belongs to their respective cricket teams.

India's Claim

India's claim is that the bat was manufactured in Jalandhar, Punjab, which is known for producing high-quality cricket bats. India is one of the biggest cricketing countries in the world, and it's no surprise that the country produces some of the world's best cricketing equipment. The Indian cricket team has been using bats manufactured in this region for many years, and they believe that the bat Head used in the final is from Jalandhar. Many Indian fans have taken to social media to express their disappointment that the bat ended up in the hands of an Australian batsman.

Pakistan's Claim

Pakistan, on the other hand, has claimed that the bat was made in Sialkot, which is known for producing the best cricket balls in the world. Sialkot is also known for manufacturing cricket bats, and Pakistan claims that Kookaburra sourced the wood for the bat from this region. Pakistani cricket fans have taken to social media to express their pride that a bat made in their country was used in such a significant match.
