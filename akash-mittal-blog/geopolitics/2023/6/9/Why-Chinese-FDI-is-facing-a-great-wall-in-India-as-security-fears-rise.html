Why Chinese FDI is facing a great wall in India as security fears rise?



Image Credits https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/chinese-fdi-faces-great-wall-in-india-as-security-fears-rise/

Imagine a scenario where you walk into a car dealership to buy a new car. After finalizing it, you are informed that the car you chose was manufactured by a Chinese company. Would you still buy it or will you go for a car from a company with an Indian origin?

This is the dilemma that India is facing today when it comes to accepting Chinese Foreign Direct Investment (FDI). Despite India's need for capital investment and economic growth, Chinese Investment is facing a great wall in India due to rising security fears and geopolitical tensions between the countries.

Examples of Chinese FDI in India

Before we dive into the reasons behind the opposition to Chinese Investment in India, let's take a look at some of the high profile deals that have faced scrutiny in recent times:

  • Paytm: In 2019, Paytm, India's largest digital payment firm, received $1 billion in funding from Ant Financial, an affiliate of China's Alibaba Group. The deal was under scanner for its potential security implications.
  • Zomato: In January 2021, Zomato, India's leading online food delivery platform, announced a fundraise of $660 million from various investors, including a Chinese Investment firm. However, the company faced backlash on social media for accepting Chinese funds.
  • MG Motors: MG Motors, a subsidiary of China's SAIC Motor Corporation Limited, entered the Indian market in 2019. While the carmaker has been successful in India with popular models like Hector and ZS EV, it has also faced criticism for its Chinese ownership.

Reasons for opposition

The opposition to Chinese FDI in India arises out of various reasons:

Geopolitical tensions

India and China have a long-standing border dispute and have been engaged in multiple military standoffs in recent years. The 2020 Galwan Valley clash, which led to the death of Indian soldiers, further escalated tensions between the two countries. As a result, many Indians view Chinese Investment as a threat to India's sovereignty and security.

Cybersecurity concerns

Chinese Investment has raised concerns over cybersecurity threats in India. Many policymakers and security experts believe that Chinese companies could use their investments in India to gain access to sensitive data and critical infrastructure, thereby posing a security risk.

Unequal market access

Many Indian policymakers believe that China does not provide a level playing field for Indian companies operating in its market, while expecting unrestricted access to India's market. This has led to a perception that Chinese Investment in India is a one-sided affair that benefits China more than India.

Case studies: Impact of Chinese FDI in India

Despite the opposition to Chinese Investment, there have been several instances where it has helped Indian companies grow and expand:

  • Paytm: The $1 billion funding received by Paytm from Ant Financial helped the company consolidate its position as India's leading digital payment firm, with a user base of over 400 million. The investment also enabled Paytm to expand its offerings into the financial services sector.
  • Zomato: Zomato's partnership with Chinese Investment firm Ant Financial helped the company become profitable in 2020, after years of losses. The funds were used to expand the company's footprint in both India and international markets.
  • MG Motors: MG Motors' entry into the Indian market has created thousands of jobs and contributed to the country's GDP. The company has also invested heavily in R&D and local manufacturing, boosting India's manufacturing sector.

Conclusion

The rise of nationalism and geopolitical tensions have impacted Chinese investment in India. While some Indian companies have benefited from Chinese Investment, there is growing concern over cybersecurity threats and unequal market access. Moreover, the border dispute with China further fuels suspicion and mistrust, making it difficult for the two countries to engage in mutually beneficial economic relations. Only time can tell whether Chinese Investment in India will continue to decline, or whether both countries will find a common ground to work towards.
