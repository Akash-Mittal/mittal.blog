History Repeats Itself: The New Cold War between Cuba, China and the US



Image Credits https://www.nationalreview.com/2023/06/history-repeats-itself-cuba-china-and-the-new-cold-war/

In the summer of 2019, Americans were stunned when news reports emerged that the Chinese government was eavesdropping on sensitive conversations between the US and its allies via a listening post in Cuba. The symbolism of China replacing the USSR in spying on America from Cuba could not have been more revealing about the conflict we are in. In this article, we will discuss the history of the Cold War, how China and Cuba are playing significant roles in the new Cold War, and what this could mean for the future of global politics.

The Cold War: A Brief History

The Cold War, which lasted from 1947-1991, was a political and ideological struggle between the Western powers led by the US and the Eastern bloc led by the Soviet Union. The conflict saw the world divided between two economic and political systems, each vying for global dominance.

The Cold War was marked by espionage, nuclear arms race, proxy wars, and widespread propaganda. The Soviet Union and the US both engaged in spying on each other and their allies, often using countries like Cuba as listening posts. The Cuban Missile Crisis of 1962 was one of the defining moments of the Cold War, bringing the world to the brink of nuclear war.

The New Cold War

In recent years, tensions between the US and China have been steadily rising. The US accuses China of practices such as intellectual property theft, cyber espionage, human rights abuses, and unfair trade practices. Against this backdrop, China's growing presence in Cuba again symbolizes the struggle for global dominance.

China is investing heavily in Cuba, providing economic and political support to the country. It has built a deep-water port in Mariel Bay, just a short distance from the US coast. Cuba, in turn, has allowed China to build a listening post in the vicinity of the US base in Guantanamo Bay.

This intelligence-gathering facility is part of China's larger global espionage network, which includes an estimated 50,000 spies operating in more than 170 countries. The US is also accusing China of sending its citizens to study and work at American universities and research labs to gather sensitive information.

The Role of Cuba

Cuba, a longtime ally of the Soviet Union, has always been a thorn in the US's side. The US has imposed economic sanctions on the country in an attempt to bring about political change. Cuba, in turn, has adopted an anti-American stance and sought allies like China to counter US influence.

With the collapse of the Soviet Union in 1991, Cuba lost its chief patron. China has stepped in to fill the void. China has provided extensive economic aid to Cuba, including infrastructure projects, trade agreements, and debt relief. Cuba, in turn, has provided China with a strategic foothold in the Caribbean, from where it can keep an eye on the US.

Conclusion

China's growing presence in Cuba is a cause for concern for the US and its allies. China's espionage activities in Cuba and its worldwide network of spies give it an edge in the new Cold War. The US will need to remain vigilant and devise strategies to counter China's global ambitions. Cuba, too, must realize that it risks becoming a pawn in the struggle for global dominance.

The new Cold War is not just about China and the US. Rather, it is a struggle for global hegemony between two different economic and political systems. How this conflict plays out will determine the future of global politics.
