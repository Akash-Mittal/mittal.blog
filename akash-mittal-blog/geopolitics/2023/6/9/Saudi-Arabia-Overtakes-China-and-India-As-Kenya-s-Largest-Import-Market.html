Saudi Arabia Overtakes China and India As Kenya's Largest Import Market



Image Credits https://africa.businessinsider.com/local/markets/saudi-arabia-overtakes-china-and-india-as-kenyas-largest-import-market/gmm9srr

Kenya's import market has been on the spotlight lately, and for good reason. The East African country has experienced a shift in their trading partners, with Saudi Arabia now becoming their largest single import market. This shift highlights the importance of petroleum products in fueling the nation's trade imbalance.

The Impact of Saudi Arabia's Dominance

According to the latest data from the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics, Saudi Arabia has surpassed China, India, and the United Arab Emirates (UAE) to become Kenya's largest single import market. Kenya imported goods worth Ksh 119.5 billion ($1.1 billion) from Saudi Arabia between January and August 2021, representing a 21.6% increase compared to the same period in 2020.

The rise of Saudi Arabia's dominance in Kenya's import market can be attributed to the increasing demand for crude oil and petroleum products. Kenya currently imports 98% of its crude oil requirements, which is a staggering fact that highlights the country's over-reliance on foreign oil. The presence of Saudi Arabia in Kenya's import market provides stability to the country's energy sector, ensuring a continuous supply of crude oil to meet the country's energy demands.

The shift in Kenya's trading partners is not unique, and there are several examples of countries experiencing a similar shift over the years. One of the most notable examples is China's emergence as Africa's top trading partner in the past decade. The shift in the trading landscape has been driven by a combination of factors, including China's growing economy, its demand for natural resources, and its strategic investment in Africa.

Case Study: China's Investment in Angola's Oil Industry

China's investment in Angola's oil industry in the early 2000s is a perfect example of how strategic investment can change a country's trading landscape. In 2004, China's state-owned oil company, Sinopec, signed a $2 billion agreement with Angola's state-owned oil company, Sonangol, to develop Angola's offshore oil fields. The investment provided China with a stable supply of crude oil, which made Angola one of China's top trading partners in Africa.

The Importance of Diversifying Kenya's Economy

Kenya's economy is over-reliant on the agriculture sector, which contributes to more than 30% of the country's GDP. The shift in Kenya's import market highlights the need for the country to diversify its economy and reduce its dependence on foreign oil. The country needs to invest in alternative energy sources and encourage local production to reduce its import bill.

The presence of Saudi Arabia in Kenya's import market is not all doom and gloom. The country's dominance provides an opportunity for Kenya to strengthen its ties with Saudi Arabia and explore new areas of cooperation. Saudi Arabia has a vast wealth of knowledge and expertise in several sectors, including renewable energy, manufacturing, and infrastructure. Kenya can leverage this expertise to develop new industries and reduce its dependence on imports.

Case Study: Kenya-Saudi Arabia Bilateral Trade Agreement

The signing of the Kenya-Saudi Arabia bilateral trade agreement in 2020 is an example of the two countries' efforts to strengthen their economic ties. The agreement aims to enhance trade relations between the two countries, promote investment in various sectors, and facilitate the exchange of technical expertise. The agreement provides Kenya with an opportunity to tap into Saudi Arabia's expertise in several key sectors and create new business opportunities.
