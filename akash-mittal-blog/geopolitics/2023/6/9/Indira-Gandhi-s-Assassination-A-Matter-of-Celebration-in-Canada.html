Indira Gandhi's Assassination: A Matter of Celebration in Canada?



Image Credits https://tfipost.com/2023/06/ex-indian-pm-indiras-assassination-is-a-matter-of-celebration-in-canada/

It is shocking to learn that some individuals in Canada celebrated the assassination of former Indian Prime Minister Indira Gandhi. On October 31, 1984, Gandhi was shot dead by two of her bodyguards, and the news of her demise sent shock waves across the globe. While the majority of people mourned her death, some in Canada responded with obnoxious celebrations. What could be the reason behind such behavior?

One possible cause is the racial tensions that have existed between the Indian and Sikh communities in Canada. The assassination of Gandhi was an act of vengeance for the Indian government's military operation against Sikh militants who had occupied the Golden Temple in Amritsar earlier that year. The operation, known as Operation Blue Star, was regarded by many Sikhs as a brutal attack on their religious shrine, and it led to widespread unrest and violence in the Sikh community. The assassination of Gandhi was seen by some Sikhs in Canada as a justifiable response to the Indian government's actions.

Another possible reason is the political ideology of the individuals who celebrated Gandhi's assassination. Some may have seen Gandhi as a tyrant who suppressed dissent and encouraged a culture of corruption during her time as Prime Minister. Additionally, Gandhi's imposition of Emergency Rule in India from 1975 to 1977 is still remembered by some as a dark period in Indian democracy. Those who held such views may have celebrated her assassination as a victory for freedom and democracy.

Whatever the reason, it is clear that the celebration of Indira Gandhi's death is inappropriate and disrespectful. As we remember her legacy today, let us also reflect on the importance of respecting diverse perspectives and upholding the values of compassion and tolerance.

In conclusion, the celebration of Indira Gandhi's assassination in Canada can be attributed to racial tensions and political ideologies. However, such behavior is unacceptable and goes against the core values of humanity. It is important to remember and honor the legacies of leaders like Gandhi, who fought for justice, equality, and peace.
