India Can See China-like High-growth Phase for a Decade



Image Credits https://www.moneycontrol.com/news/business/economy/india-can-see-china-like-high-growth-phase-for-a-decade-says-cea-nageswaran-10771291.html

Imagine a country with a young and rapidly growing population, a strong investment and political climate that encourages entrepreneurship, and a government that is taking measures to boost economic growth. If that sounds like China during its high-growth phase, you wouldn't be entirely wrong. But it could also be India in the coming decade.

Examples and Case Studies

CEA Nageswaran, former chairman of the Madras School of Economics and former member of the Prime Minister's Economic Advisory Council, believes that India is on the cusp of a China-like high-growth phase. He cites a number of government measures that have set the stage for sustained rapid economic growth, including:

  • The Goods and Services Tax (GST) which has consolidated multiple taxes into a single tax system, simplifying the tax code and boosting efficiency
  • The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC) which has streamlined the process of resolving insolvency cases, reducing non-performing assets (NPAs) in the banking system, and improving the overall health of the financial sector
  • The Digital India initiative which has promoted the use of technology to improve government services, increase financial inclusion, and create new business opportunities
  • The "Make in India" campaign which aims to promote domestic manufacturing and attract foreign investment

These measures have already begun to bear fruit. India's GDP growth rate has averaged over 7% in recent years, making it one of the fastest-growing major economies in the world. But Nageswaran believes that this is just the beginning.

He argues that India's large and young population, which is projected to surpass China's by 2027, will provide a strong base for future economic growth. This demographic dividend, combined with the government's efforts to improve infrastructure, create a business-friendly environment, and encourage entrepreneurship, could propel India into a sustained period of rapid economic growth.

Nageswaran's views are supported by a number of recent studies. McKinsey & Company predicts that India's consumer market will grow from $1.3 trillion in 2020 to $6 trillion in 2030, driven by rising incomes and a growing middle class. The Boston Consulting Group predicts that India's e-commerce market will grow from $30 billion in 2020 to $200 billion in 2026, powered by increased internet penetration and the rise of digital payments.

India is poised to experience a China-like high-growth phase over the next decade.

  • The government's measures to support economic growth, including the GST, IBC, and Digital India, have laid the foundation for sustained rapid growth.
  • India's large and young population, combined with rising incomes and a growing middle class, will provide a strong base for future economic growth.
  • The growth potential of India's consumer and e-commerce markets highlights the country's potential for sustained rapid growth in the coming years.
