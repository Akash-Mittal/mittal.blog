The Rise of Debt Financing: SBI to Raise 500 Billion Rupees



Image Credits https://www.reuters.com/world/india/indias-top-lender-sbi-raise-up-6-bln-via-debt-fy24-2023-06-09/

Once upon a time, businesses used to rely on equity financing to raise capital. However, with a greater demand for credit and changing market dynamics, debt financing has become the preferred choice for many. And, India's largest lender, the State Bank of India (SBI), is all set to raise up to 500 billion rupees ($6.06 billion) this financial year via debt issuance. Let's explore the reasons behind this shift to debt financing and the implications it has on the Indian economy.

The Emergence of Debt Financing: A Case Study

For decades, equity financing has been the go-to option for businesses looking to raise funds. But, in recent years, the growth of debt financing has outpaced that of equity. Why? Both lenders and borrowers stand to benefit from debt financing.

Let's consider the case of a hypothetical business, ABC, which is looking to expand its operations. ABC's management has two options: raising funds through equity or debt. Let's take a look at both options:

Equity Financing

With equity financing, ABC would raise capital by issuing shares to investors. In return, investors would become shareholders with a stake in the company. While equity financing has some advantages, such as not being obligated to pay dividends, it also has some drawbacks:

  • ABC would have to dilute ownership of the company by issuing shares.
  • ABC would have to share profits with shareholders.
  • ABC would not be able to retain complete control over the company.

Debt Financing

With debt financing, ABC would borrow money from a lender. In return, ABC would be obligated to repay the loan with interest. While debt financing has some disadvantages, such as mandatory repayments with interest, it also has some benefits:

  • ABC would retain ownership of the company.
  • ABC would not have to share profits with lenders.
  • ABC would not have to dilute ownership of the company.

Thus, given the pros and cons of both options, ABC decides to go for debt financing as it allows them to retain ownership of the company while raising funds.

Implications for the Indian Economy

The shift towards debt financing has important implications for the Indian economy. Firstly, it allows businesses to expand their operations and create more jobs. Secondly, it enables lenders to earn interest income and generate profits, thereby creating a virtuous cycle of credit and growth. Thirdly, it promotes financial stability by reducing the risks associated with equity financing. Finally, it encourages innovation by providing businesses with the capital needed to fund research and development.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the shift towards debt financing is a positive development for both businesses and lenders. By raising 500 billion rupees via debt issuance, the State Bank of India is further contributing to this trend. Debt financing enables businesses to expand their operations while retaining ownership and control, and allows lenders to earn interest income and generate profits. Moreover, it promotes financial stability and encourages innovation. As such, it is expected that debt financing will continue to be an increasingly popular method of financing for businesses in India and around the world.
