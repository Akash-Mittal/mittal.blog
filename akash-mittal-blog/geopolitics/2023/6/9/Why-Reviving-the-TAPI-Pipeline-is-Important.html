Why Reviving the TAPI Pipeline is Important



Image Credits https://oilprice.com/Geopolitics/Asia/Why-Russia-Wants-To-Revive-The-TAPI-Pipeline.html

Back in the 1990s, the leaders of Turkmenistan, Afghanistan, Pakistan, and India envisioned a pipeline that would transport natural gas from Turkmenistan's gas fields to energy-hungry South Asia. The project, known as the Turkmenistan–Afghanistan–Pakistan–India (TAPI) pipeline, was seen as a win-win for all involved nations. Turkmenistan could diversify its gas exports, Afghanistan and Pakistan could benefit from transit fees, and India could meet its energy demands. However, due to geopolitical and security challenges, the project stalled for years. Now, Russia is pushing for the revival of the TAPI pipeline. Why? Let's find out.

Geopolitical and Security Problems with the TAPI Pipeline

The TAPI pipeline is a massive project that requires the cooperation of four countries, each with its own set of interests and challenges. Here are some of the main problems that have plagued the project:

Security Concerns in Afghanistan and Pakistan

Afghanistan and Pakistan, two of the countries that the TAPI pipeline would pass through, are plagued by security challenges. Afghanistan has been in a state of war for decades, with the Taliban and other insurgent groups controlling large swaths of territory. Pakistan has also faced serious security concerns, with terrorism and insurgencies affecting many parts of the country. These security concerns have made the construction and operation of the TAPI pipeline extremely challenging. In fact, the pipeline has been targeted by militants in the past, which is one of the reasons why the project has stalled for years.

Case Study: In 2018, an attack on a TAPI pipeline survey team in Afghanistan killed three people. This attack highlights the security risks associated with the project.

Sanctions on Iran and Russia

India, one of the countries that would benefit from the TAPI pipeline, has been hit by sanctions on Iran and Russia, two of its major energy suppliers. This has put pressure on India to diversify its energy sources. The TAPI pipeline, which would transport gas from Turkmenistan, could provide India with an alternative source of energy. However, given that Russia is involved in the project, it is unclear whether India would risk further sanctions by participating in the project.

Case Study: In 2020, the United States imposed sanctions on a company involved in the construction of the Nord Stream 2 gas pipeline, a project that is seen as critical for Russia's energy exports. This move highlights the potential risks associated with being involved in energy projects with Russia.

Pricing and Political Issues with Turkmenistan

Turkmenistan, the supplier of gas for the TAPI pipeline, has experienced its own set of challenges in recent years. The country has been criticized for its lack of transparency and human rights abuses. Additionally, Turkmenistan has been involved in pricing disputes with some of its gas customers, which has made it difficult for the country to export its gas. These issues have made it challenging for Turkmenistan to secure investments for the TAPI pipeline.

Case Study: In 2015, Russia stopped buying gas from Turkmenistan, citing a pricing dispute. This move hurt Turkmenistan's economy, which heavily relies on gas exports.

Why Russia Wants to Revive the TAPI Pipeline

Given the challenges associated with the TAPI pipeline, why would Russia want to revive the project? Here are some possible reasons:

Gaining Influence in South Asia

Russia has been seeking to expand its influence in South Asia, which is seen as a critical region due to its energy resources and strategic location. By participating in the TAPI pipeline, Russia could gain a foothold in the region and potentially challenge the influence of other powers such as the United States and China. Additionally, the TAPI pipeline would allow Russia to strengthen its ties with Pakistan, a country that it has traditionally had a cold relationship with.

Diversifying Energy Exports

Russia is heavily dependent on oil and gas exports for its economy, which has made it vulnerable to fluctuations in the global energy market. By participating in the TAPI pipeline, Russia could diversify its energy exports and potentially reduce its reliance on the European market, which has been a major source of revenue for the country.

Countering the Chinese Belt and Road Initiative

China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) has been seen as a major threat to Russia's economic and strategic interests. The BRI aims to connect Asia, Europe, and Africa through infrastructure projects, including ports, railways, and pipelines. By participating in the TAPI pipeline, Russia could counter the BRI and demonstrate its own ability to undertake large-scale infrastructure projects.

Conclusion

The revival of the TAPI pipeline is a complex project that has been plagued by geopolitical and security challenges. However, with Russia pushing for the project's revival, there is a possibility that it could move forward. Ultimately, the success of the TAPI pipeline will depend on the ability of the involved parties to overcome the challenges and work towards a common goal. If successful, the TAPI pipeline could provide a much-needed alternative source of energy for South Asia, while also benefiting the energy suppliers and the investors in the project.
