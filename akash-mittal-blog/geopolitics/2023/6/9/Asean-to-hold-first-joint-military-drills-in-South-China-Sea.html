Asean to hold first joint military drills in South China Sea



Image Credits https://www.todayonline.com/world/asean-hold-first-joint-military-drills-south-china-sea-2189236

Southeast Asian nations have agreed to hold their first-ever joint military drills in the South China Sea, Indonesian officials said on Thursday (June 8), as tensions grow over China's increasing assertiveness in the region.

The Story

China, in recent years, has become increasingly assertive in the South China Sea, claiming sovereignty over nearly all of the area's waters and its land features, including disputed territories that are also claimed by other countries in the region, such as the Philippines, Vietnam, Malaysia and Brunei. The United States has called the Chinese claims illegal and conducted freedom of navigation operations near Chinese-held islands, sparking tensions with Beijing.

In response to China's actions, Southeast Asian nations have been trying to build unity and consensus to counterbalance China's growing influence in the region. In a significant move towards this end, Asean (Association of Southeast Asian Nations) has agreed to hold its first joint military drills in the South China Sea.

Examples with case studies

The joint military drills are a significant step towards building a collective defence against China's assertiveness in the region. It is hoped that this will enhance communication, coordination, and interoperability among the participating nations, improve their military capabilities, and deter any potential aggression from China.

One case study that shows how joint military drills can be effective in enhancing defence cooperation between nations is the Malabar exercise. The Malabar exercise is an annual naval exercise between India, the United States, and Japan. The exercise started as a bilateral naval exercise between India and the United States in 1992 and was expanded to include Japan in 2015. The aim of the exercise is to increase interoperability among the three navies, share knowledge and resources, and improve the ability to work together in crisis situations. The joint exercises have proven to be successful in increasing the capabilities of the participating navies and enhancing regional security.

Another case study is the Cobra Gold exercise, which is an annual multinational military exercise hosted by Thailand and the United States. The exercise started as a bilateral exercise between the United States and Thailand in 1982 and has since expanded to include other countries such as Japan, South Korea, and Singapore. The aim of the exercise is to improve military interoperability, regional security, and the ability to respond to humanitarian emergencies. The exercise has been successful in enhancing collaboration between nations and improving their military capabilities.

The Conclusion

In conclusion, the decision by Asean to hold its first joint military drills in the South China Sea is a significant step towards building collective defence and enhancing regional security in Southeast Asia. The joint exercises will not only improve communication, coordination, and interoperability among the participating nations but also enhance their military capabilities and deter any potential aggression from China. By working together, Southeast Asian nations can counterbalance China's growing assertiveness and ensure that the region remains peaceful and stable.

As seen in the case studies of the Malabar and the Cobra Gold exercises, joint military drills can be highly effective in enhancing defence cooperation between nations and improving their military capabilities. It is hoped that the joint military drills in the South China Sea will be just as successful in achieving these goals and ensuring peace and stability in the region.
