It was just another day at work for French cybersecurity expert, Julien (name changed on request), until he was assigned to investigate a peculiar case. A French renewable energy firm, specialised in offshore wind farms, had filed a complaint about unusual cyberattacks. The attacks had originated from China, and the motive was not to steal data, but to disrupt the firm's operations.

The firm was hesitant to reveal its name in public, but it was certain that its business was affected significantly. Julien's investigation revealed that the attackers had exploited a vulnerability in the firm's logistics software, causing it to malfunction. They had also sent multiple phishing emails to the firm's employees, trying to trick them into revealing their login credentials.

Julien concluded that the attack was a classic case of cyber espionage, but the motive of disruption was not common. He suspected that the attackers were sponsored by the Chinese government, which was trying to sabotage the firm's operations and hold its monopoly over the renewable energy market.

China's Interference in France: A Growing Concern

The above story is just one example of how China's interference in France is becoming increasingly aggressive. The French parliamentary report on foreign interference, published on Thursday (June 8), highlights the same concern and warns that China's methods of interference are becoming more sophisticated and challenging to detect.

China's interference in French affairs can take many forms, ranging from cyberattacks to propaganda and political influence. Some examples of its interference include:

  • Cyber Espionage: As seen in the above example, China's hackers target French companies to steal sensitive information or disrupt their operations. In 2019, French auto parts maker Valeo reported a cyberattack that originated from China. The attack was designed to steal intellectual property related to Valeo's autonomous vehicle technology.
  • Propaganda: China's state-run media outlets such as Xinhua News Agency and China Daily have a significant presence in France. They often publish biased news articles that favour China's interests. For example, during the Yellow Vest protests in France in 2018, Xinhua News Agency published numerous articles that portrayed the French government as oppressive and the protesters as victims.
  • Political Influence: China often tries to influence French politicians to make decisions that favour its interests. For example, in 2019, Chinese ambassador to France, Lu Shaye, warned the French government against following the US in banning Chinese company Huawei from its 5G network. He stated that such a move would have consequences for French companies operating in China.

The Consequences of China's Interference

China's interference in French affairs not only undermines the sovereignty of the nation but also poses a threat to its economy and security.

France is home to numerous multinational corporations and companies that are vulnerable to cyberattacks. Any disruption in their operations could have severe consequences on the French economy. Furthermore, China's propaganda and political influence can undermine the French government's ability to make independent decisions based on its national interests.

However, the most significant threat posed by China's interference is the risk of espionage and theft of intellectual property. French companies are known for their innovative technology and products. Any theft of such intellectual property can significantly harm their competitiveness and innovation capabilities.

Conclusion

China's aggressive interference in France is a growing concern that needs to be addressed. The French government must take steps to protect its sovereignty, economy, and security. Such steps could include investing in cybersecurity, countering Chinese propaganda, reducing reliance on Chinese technology, and strengthening its diplomatic ties with other countries to counter China's political influence.

The world needs to understand that China's aggressive interference in foreign affairs is not limited to France alone. It has been reported in other countries, including the US, Australia, New Zealand, and others. It's time for the world to wake up to this threat and take appropriate measures before it's too late.
