An Analysis of the Disputes between Two Asian Giants

It's no secret that India and China have been engaged in territorial disputes for decades. However, recent events have increased tensions between the two countries, leading to a "boxing match" that has yet to be resolved.

The Doklam Stand-Off

The 2017 Doklam Stand-Off in Bhutan was a significant event in the ongoing border disputes between India and China. The stand-off lasted for 73 days and began when Chinese troops crossed into Bhutanese territory and attempted to build a road. India, which has a defense agreement with Bhutan, intervened and stationed troops in the area, leading to a face-off between the two countries.

The Maldives Crisis

In 2018, the Maldives crisis erupted when the Indian government expressed its concerns over the country's ties with China. The Maldives has been a traditional ally of India, but the election of President Abdulla Yameen in 2013 led to a shift in the country's foreign policy towards China. India feared that China's growing influence in the Maldives would threaten its security interests in the region, leading to a tense stand-off between the two countries.

The Nepal Kalapani Border Dispute

In 2020, a border dispute erupted between India and Nepal over the Kalapani region. Nepal claimed that the region belonged to them, while India maintained that it was part of the Indian state of Uttarakhand. The dispute escalated when Nepal released a new map that showed the region as part of Nepal, leading to a tense stand-off between the two countries.

The Role of China in these Disputes

China has been accused of instigating these disputes, as it seeks to increase its influence in the region. By targeting countries that have close ties with India, China hopes to weaken India's position in the region and establish itself as the dominant power. This strategy is known as the "String of Pearls," which refers to China's efforts to establish a network of military and economic bases in the Indian Ocean region.

Conclusion

The disputes between India and China are unlikely to be resolved anytime soon, as both countries continue to pursue their strategic interests in the region. However, it is essential that both countries engage in dialogue and find peaceful solutions to these disputes, as any escalation could have severe consequences for the entire region.
