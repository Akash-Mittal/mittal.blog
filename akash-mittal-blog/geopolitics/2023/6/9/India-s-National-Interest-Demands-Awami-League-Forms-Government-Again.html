India's National Interest Demands Awami League Forms Government Again

In 2008, India's relations with its eastern neighbor, Bangladesh, were at an all-time low. The Bangladeshi government, led by the Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP), had been unresponsive to India's pleas for greater cooperation on issues such as cross-border terrorism, smuggling, and illegal immigration. The situation was further complicated by the presence of several militant groups in Bangladesh that were hostile to India.

Then, in December of that year, Bangladesh held parliamentary elections, and the Awami League emerged as the victor. The party, which had close ties to India, immediately began to take measures that addressed India's concerns. Within weeks of taking office, the Awami League launched a crackdown on militant groups, resulting in the arrest of many top leaders.

Since then, India-Bangladesh relations have improved significantly. The two countries have signed multiple important agreements, including the Land Boundary Agreement (LBA), which resolved a long-standing border dispute, and a transit agreement that gives India access to Bangladeshi ports. India is now Bangladesh's largest trading partner, and there is extensive cooperation on issues such as counter-terrorism, energy, and connectivity.

Examples with Case Studies

Case Study 1: Border Security

One of the key challenges India faces is securing its borders with Bangladesh, which is over 4,000 km long. Before the Awami League came to power, there was little cooperation between the two countries on this issue. However, since then, there have been significant improvements. For example, a Joint Border Working Group was established to enhance coordination between the border forces of both countries. In 2015, a new border management agreement was signed, which has helped reduce border incidents and smuggling.

Case Study 2: Connectivity

Another area of cooperation between India and Bangladesh is in connectivity. In 2016, the two countries signed a landmark agreement to establish a coastal shipping service, which has significantly reduced the time and cost of transporting goods between them. India is also working with Bangladesh on several major infrastructure projects, including a rail link that will connect the two countries. These initiatives will help boost trade and investment, create jobs, and improve the quality of life for millions of people.

Conclusion

  1. India's national interest is best served by having a stable and friendly neighbor in Bangladesh.
  2. The Awami League has a track record of taking measures that address India's concerns, such as cracking down on militants and improving border security.
  3. The Awami League's victory in the upcoming elections will ensure that the positive momentum in India-Bangladesh relations continues.
