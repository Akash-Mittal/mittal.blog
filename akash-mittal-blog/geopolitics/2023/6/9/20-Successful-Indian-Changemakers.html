20 Successful Indian Changemakers



Image Credits  https://m.timesofindia.com/business/india-business/20-successful-indian-changemakers/articleshow/100851431.cms

In a nation teeming with talent, resilience, and boundless potential, India has produced a remarkable array of individuals who have risen to remarkable heights, leaving an indelible mark. Here are 20 such successful Indian changemakers:

1. Ratan Tata

Ratan Tata, the Chairman Emeritus of Tata Sons, one of India's largest conglomerates, is one of the most respected industrialists in the world. He is known for his philanthropic works and has received numerous honors for his contributions to Indian society. Under his leadership, Tata Group became a global brand and diversified into multiple industries such as Tata Motors, Tata Steel, and Tata Communications.

Case Study:

After the Mumbai attacks in 2008, Tata Motors, under Ratan Tata's leadership, developed the Tata Armored Personnel Carrier (APC) - the first combat vehicle developed entirely in India.

2. Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw

Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw, the founder of Biocon, an innovative biopharmaceutical company, is India's wealthiest self-made woman entrepreneur. She started Biocon in 1978 in a garage with a seed capital of Rs. 10,000 ($140). Today, the company has a market capitalization of over $11 billion and is a global leader in biosimilars and contract research.

Case Study:

In 2020, Kiran Mazumdar-Shaw's Biocon launched the world's first biosimilar of a drug used to treat breast and stomach cancer in the United States.

3. Sanjeev Bikhchandani

Sanjeev Bikhchandani, the founder and executive vice-chairman of Info Edge India, is considered the pioneer of online recruitment in India. He started Naukri.com, India's leading job site, in 1997, when the Internet was still in its infancy in the country. Today, Info Edge has diversified into multiple sectors such as real estate, education, and matchmaking.

Case Study:

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Info Edge launched an online platform, nTalents.ai, to help companies hire remote workers and upskill their existing workforce.

4. Vandana Shiva

Vandana Shiva, a Delhi-based environmental activist and author, is known for her advocacy of sustainable agriculture, biodiversity, and farmers' rights. She has authored over 20 books and has won numerous awards for her contributions to the environment and social justice. She founded Navdanya, a network of seed banks and organic farms, to promote seed sovereignty and ecological agriculture.

Case Study:

Navdanya's Biodiversity Conservation Farm in Dehradun, Uttarakhand, has conserved over 500 varieties of rice, wheat, millet, and other crops.

5. Nitin Nohria

Nitin Nohria, the former Dean of Harvard Business School, is a thought leader in the field of leadership and management. He has authored or co-authored 16 books and over 50 articles, and has been a consultant to numerous multinational companies. He was the first Indian-born dean of a top-ranked business school in the United States.

Case Study:

Nitin Nohria was instrumental in shaping the curriculum and culture of Harvard Business School, emphasizing the importance of ethical leadership, social responsibility, and global perspective.

6. Arundhati Bhattacharya

Arundhati Bhattacharya, the first woman Chairperson of State Bank of India, India's largest bank, is one of the most powerful women in the world in business. She had a 40-year career in banking, starting as a probationary officer in SBI in 1977 and rising to the top post in 2013. She has received numerous awards for her leadership and contribution to banking and finance.

Case Study:

Arundhati Bhattacharya introduced several innovative initiatives during her tenure at SBI, such as the 'Digital Village' project to promote digital literacy and financial inclusion, and the creation of a women's entrepreneurship cell.

7. Nandan Nilekani

Nandan Nilekani, the co-founder of Infosys, one of India's largest IT companies, is a technocrat and social entrepreneur. He served as the Chairman of the Unique Identification Authority of India, which launched the Aadhaar program, the world's largest biometric identification system. He is a philanthropist and has pledged to give away most of his wealth to social causes.

Case Study:

Nandan Nilekani co-founded EkStep Foundation, a non-profit focused on improving education outcomes by leveraging technology, data, and analytics.

8. Naveen Tewari

Naveen Tewari, the founder and CEO of InMobi, a global mobile advertising company, is a serial entrepreneur and investor. He started InMobi in 2007, when mobile advertising was a nascent market, and grew it into a unicorn valued at over $2 billion. He is an angel investor and mentor to several startups in India and overseas.

Case Study:

Naveen Tewari founded Glance, a lock-screen content platform that provides news, entertainment, and commerce content to mobile users. Glance has over 100 million daily active users in India and Southeast Asia.

9. Kalpana Saroj

Kalpana Saroj, the Chairperson of Kamani Tubes and the Director of Mukand Ltd., is a self-made industrialist and social activist. She was born in a Dalit family and faced discrimination and abuse during her childhood. She started working in a garment factory when she was 12 and went on to acquire several companies in the steel, engineering, and real estate sectors. She is a philanthropist and has established the Kalpana Saroj Foundation to support women's empowerment and education.

Case Study:

Kalpana Saroj turned around the fortunes of Kamani Tubes, a bankrupt company, by restructuring its debt and improving its operations. Today, the company is profitable and has expanded into new markets.

10. Ajay Piramal

Ajay Piramal, the Chairman of Piramal Group, a diversified conglomerate with interests in healthcare, financial services, and real estate, is one of India's most successful entrepreneurs and investors. He started his career in the textile industry and went on to acquire several companies in different sectors. He is a philanthropist and has donated millions of dollars to education and healthcare causes.

Case Study:

Ajay Piramal acquired Bayer India's diagnostic business and grew it into a leading player in the Indian healthcare market. He also invested in new ventures such as Piramal Swasthya, a rural healthcare initiative.

11. Kailash Satyarthi

Kailash Satyarthi, a Nobel Peace Prize laureate and child rights activist, has dedicated his life to fighting child labor and trafficking. He has rescued thousands of children from bonded labor and exploitation and has campaigned for the right to education and freedom from slavery. He founded the Bachpan Bachao Andolan, a grassroots movement that has mobilized millions of people against child labor.

Case Study:

Kailash Satyarthi led the Global March Against Child Labor, a massive mobilization of civil society organizations, trade unions, and governments in 1998 that resulted in a UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

12. Rochelle Potkar

Rochelle Potkar, a poet and writer based in Mumbai, is one of India's most talented literary voices. She has authored two books of poetry, 'The Arithmetic of Breasts and Other Stories' and 'Paper Asylum', and has won several awards for her writing. Her work explores themes of gender, identity, and social change.

Case Study:

Rochelle Potkar's poem 'Abandoned' explores the plight of abandoned women in India and the social and cultural factors that lead to their marginalization.

13. Rajiv Bajaj

Rajiv Bajaj, the Managing Director of Bajaj Auto, one of India's largest two-wheeler manufacturers, is known for his entrepreneurial and managerial acumen. He has led Bajaj Auto to become a global player in the motorcycle industry, with a presence in over 70 countries. He has won several awards for his business leadership and innovation.

Case Study:

Rajiv Bajaj pioneered the concept of 'value engineering', a cost-cutting technique that created the highly successful Bajaj Pulsar motorcycle brand.

14. Anand Mahindra

Anand Mahindra, the Chairman of Mahindra Group, a diversified conglomerate with interests in automobiles, aerospace, and financial services, is a seasoned business leader and an influencer on social media. He has led the transformation of Mahindra from a tractor company to a global player in multiple sectors. He is a philanthropist and supports several social causes.

Case Study:

Anand Mahindra launched the Mahindra Rise initiative, which promotes social entrepreneurship, innovation, and sustainability, and has supported over 100 startups.

15. Kiran Bedi

Kiran Bedi, a retired Indian Police Service officer and social activist, is known for her pioneering work in prison reform and women's empowerment. She has served as the Director General of India's Bureau of Police Research and Development and has won several awards for her contributions to law enforcement, justice, and human rights. She is a motivational speaker and a role model for women in India.

Case Study:

Kiran Bedi initiated the Navjyoti Delhi Police Foundation, which works towards eradicating drug addiction and its associated crimes among juveniles and underprivileged people.

16. Shiv Nadar

Shiv Nadar, the founder of HCL Technologies, a leading IT services company, is one of India's wealthiest entrepreneurs and philanthropists. He started HCL in 1976 with a capital of Rs. 187,000 and grew it into a global brand with over $10 billion in revenue. He is a philanthropist and has established the Shiv Nadar Foundation to support education and research.

Case Study:

Shiv Nadar founded the SSN Institutions, a group of engineering and management colleges in Chennai, which has become a premier educational institution in India.

17. Deepika Padukone

Deepika Padukone, one of Bollywood's leading actresses, is also a social activist and mental health advocate. She founded the 'Live Love Laugh' Foundation, which focuses on creating awareness about mental health and providing support to people with anxiety and depression. She is a role model for millions of young people in India and has won several awards for her acting and social work.

Case Study:

Deepika Padukone has spoken publicly about her own struggles with depression and has launched several campaigns to promote mental health awareness among young people.

18. Mallika Srinivasan

Mallika Srinivasan, the Chairman and CEO of Tractors and Farm Equipment Limited (TAFE), India's second-largest tractor manufacturer, is a dynamic business leader and an advocate of rural development and gender equality. She has transformed TAFE into a global brand and diversified into multiple sectors such as agriculture, engineering, and healthcare. She is a philanthropist and supports several social causes.

Case Study:

Mallika Srinivasan founded the Ammachatram Foundation, which provides education and healthcare services to over 100,000 people in rural Tamil Nadu.

19. Harsh Mariwala

Harsh Mariwala, the founder and Chairman of Marico Limited, a leading consumer goods company, is one of India's most successful entrepreneurs and investors. He started Marico in 1990 with a capital of Rs. 50,000 and grew it into a global brand with over $1 billion in revenue. He is a philanthropist and supports several social causes.

Case Study:

Harsh Mariwala launched the Marico Innovation Foundation, which promotes entrepreneurship and innovation by recognizing and rewarding entrepreneurs and their companies.

20. Saina Nehwal

Saina Nehwal, one of India's most successful badminton players and Olympic medalists, is also a role model for young women in India. She has won numerous international titles and has broken barriers for women athletes in a male-dominated sport. She is a philanthropist and has supported several causes related to education and youth empowerment.

Case Study:

Saina Nehwal's Saina Nehwal Total Badminton Academy, a world-class badminton training facility in Hyderabad, has produced several international champions.

Conclusion:

These 20 successful Indian changemakers are just a tiny fraction of the countless individuals in India who are making a difference in their fields and communities. Their stories inspire us to dream big, work hard, and create a better future for ourselves and for others. Whether through business, social activism, art, or sports, they have shown us that talent, resilience, and boundless potential can overcome any obstacle. We can all learn from their achievements and strive to make our own mark on the world.
