How BRICS is Beating the Odds to Achieve Economic Dominance



Image Credits https://tfiglobalnews.com/2023/06/08/how-was-brics-formed-and-why-is-it-yet-to-achieve-its-true-potential/

In 2001, an economist from Goldman Sachs coined the term "BRIC" to represent the four nations with the greatest potential for growth in the 21st century – Brazil, Russia, India, and China. The term quickly gained traction and the four countries began to realize the immense benefits of cooperating with each other to achieve their goals. In 2010, South Africa joined to form BRICS, a formidable intergovernmental organization with huge economic potential.

Achievements of BRICS

  • BRICS has a combined GDP of over $16 trillion, accounting for almost 25% of the world economy.
  • The group has established its own development bank – the New Development Bank – with a capital base of $100 billion, aimed at financing infrastructure and sustainable development projects in developing countries.
  • BRICS has been successful in creating a sustainable mechanism for trade and investment cooperation, with intra-BRICS trade reaching $84.5 billion in 2020.
  • The countries have collaborated in areas such as energy, agriculture, science and technology, and social welfare, forging closer ties and enhancing mutual benefit.

Case Studies

India and China – Increasing Cooperation

India and China, the two largest members of BRICS, have had a history of strained relations but have recently increased cooperation through economic agreements. In 2020, India and China signed a new trade agreement aimed at balancing trade between the two countries, which had previously been in favor of China. This cooperation has opened up avenues for increased trade and investment between the two countries, leading to economic growth for both.

Russia and Brazil – Energy Cooperation

Russia and Brazil, two of the largest energy producers in the world, have established a strategic partnership for energy cooperation. The two countries have been working together to invest in oil exploration, refine crude oil, and develop natural gas resources. This partnership has not only increased trade between the two countries but has also led to diversification of energy sources and reduction of reliance on a single supplier for both countries.

Potential of BRICS

  • BRICS has the potential to become a key driver of global economic growth if the member countries continue to cooperate and deepen their integration.
  • The group can use its collective strength to advance the cause of globalization, reduce trade barriers and promote a fairer and more equitable world order.
  • BRICS can play a leading role in sustainable development, reducing carbon emissions, and finding solutions to global issues such as climate change and health pandemics.

Conclusion

Despite facing challenges such as political differences, differing economic interests, and diverse cultural backgrounds, BRICS has still managed to achieve significant economic growth and development. The group has established itself as a key player in the global economy and has the potential to become an even more formidable force in the years to come.

With continued cooperation, integration, and collaboration, BRICS can lead the way in establishing a fairer and more equitable global order, promoting sustainable development and reducing poverty and inequality.
