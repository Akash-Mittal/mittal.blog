Europe Seeks Neutral Role in China-US Rift



Image Credits https://asianews.network/europe-seeks-neutral-role-in-china-us-rift/

International relations have always been complex, and the ongoing rift between China and the US is no exception. As these two major powers continue to clash, many other nations are finding themselves in a difficult position. One such group is Europe, which is trying to play a neutral role in this conflict.

Why Europe Wants to Stay Neutral

There are several reasons why Europe is pushing for neutrality in the China-US rift:

  1. Economic Interests: Europe has a lot to gain from both China and the US, so it makes sense for them to stay impartial. According to a report by the European Union Chamber of Commerce in China, European businesses have invested over €110 billion in China, making the country one of its biggest trading partners. At the same time, Europe is also heavily invested in the US, with over €2.2 trillion invested in the country as of 2018. Therefore, it is in Europe's best interest to maintain positive relationships with both countries.
  2. International Stability: Europe has always been a proponent of international cooperation and diplomacy. They want to see a world where nations can work together towards common goals. However, the current China-US rift is threatening this stability. If Europe takes sides, it could potentially escalate the conflict and damage international relations even further.
  3. Political Outcomes: Taking sides in the China-US rift could have political implications for Europe. If they side with China, it could hurt their relationship with the US and potentially damage their reputation as a democratic nation. On the other hand, if they side with the US, it could damage their trade relationship with China and put them at a disadvantage.

Case Studies

Let's take a look at some recent examples where Europe has tried to play a neutral role in the China-US rift.

World Trade Organization

In June 2020, the US blocked the appointment of a new judge to the World Trade Organization (WTO) Appeals Court. This move was seen as a way for the US to gain an advantage in the ongoing trade war with China. However, Europe stepped in and proposed a compromise that would allow the WTO to continue functioning without the US. This proposal was accepted by China and other members of the WTO, thus preventing the organization from collapsing.

5G Technology

The US has been pressuring its allies to ban the use of Chinese tech company Huawei's 5G technology, citing national security concerns. However, Europe has been hesitant to take sides, instead calling for an international review of the technology. In January 2020, the European Commission published recommendations for member states that included increased security measures for 5G networks, but stopped short of banning Huawei outright. This allowed Europe to maintain positive relationships with both the US and China, while also safeguarding its own security interests.

Conclusion

Europe's decision to take a neutral role in the China-US rift is understandable, given the economic, political, and social factors at play. By staying impartial, Europe can maintain positive relationships with both countries while also promoting international stability. The recent case studies show that this approach can be effective in finding compromises and solutions that benefit all parties involved.
