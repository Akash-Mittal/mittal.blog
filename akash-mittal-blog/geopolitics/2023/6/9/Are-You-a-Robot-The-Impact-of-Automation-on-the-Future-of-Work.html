The Impact of Automation and Artificial Intelligence on the Future of Work

Introduction

The rise of automation and artificial intelligence is changing the way we work and live. Machines are taking over tasks that were once the sole domain of humans, from manufacturing to customer service to white-collar jobs like accounting and finance. Companies are investing heavily in automation technologies, and millions of jobs are at risk of being automated in the coming decades.

While automation has the potential to increase efficiency and productivity, it also poses significant challenges for society. What will happen to the millions of people who will be displaced by automation? Will we have enough new jobs for them to transition into? How will we ensure that everyone benefits from the benefits of automation, rather than just a select few?

Examples

Let's take a look at some real-world examples of automation and its impact on the workforce.

Factory Automation

One of the most well-known examples of automation is in manufacturing, where robots have been used for decades to assemble products and perform other tasks. As technology has advanced, robots have become more sophisticated and cost-effective, making them even more attractive to manufacturers. According to a report by the World Economic Forum, by 2025, 52% of the tasks currently performed by humans will be done by robots, and 75 million jobs could be displaced by automation.

Take the case of Foxconn, the Taiwanese electronics manufacturer that produces Apple products. In 2016, Foxconn announced plans to invest $10 billion in a new manufacturing facility in Wisconsin, with promises of creating 13,000 jobs. However, in 2019, Foxconn scaled back its plans, saying that it would only create 1,500 jobs, and that the majority of the work would be done by robots. The company cited rising labor costs and a shortage of skilled workers as the reasons for the change.

Retail Automation

Retail is another industry where automation is having a significant impact. Self-checkout machines are becoming increasingly common in grocery stores and other retail outlets, reducing the need for human cashiers. In addition, companies like Amazon are experimenting with cashier-less stores, where customers simply walk in, pick up what they want, and leave, with the cost automatically deducted from their account.

However, there are concerns that these technologies will lead to job losses and exacerbate income inequality. According to a report by the Economic Policy Institute, the introduction of self-checkout machines has already resulted in the loss of more than 140,000 cashier jobs in the US. In addition, these machines often require customers to have access to a smartphone or credit card, excluding low-income people who may not have these resources.

White-Collar Automation

While automation has traditionally been associated with manufacturing and other manual labor jobs, it is increasingly being used to automate white-collar tasks as well. For example, software programs can now perform tasks like data analysis and financial reporting, reducing the need for human accountants and analysts.

Take the case of JP Morgan Chase, which in 2016 created a software program called COiN that can analyze legal documents and extract important information for human review. The program can perform in seconds what would take a team of lawyers hundreds of hours to do. While the program has led to a reduction in the number of lawyers needed at the bank, it has also made the legal review process more efficient and cost-effective.

Conclusion

Automation and artificial intelligence are transforming the way we work and live. While there are many benefits to these technologies, there are also significant challenges that need to be addressed. It is critical that we ensure that everyone benefits from the advances in automation, rather than just a select few. To do this, we need to invest in education and training programs that prepare workers for the new jobs that will be created by automation. We also need to ensure that there are adequate social safety nets in place to support workers who are displaced by automation.

The future of work is uncertain, but one thing is clear: we need to be proactive in adapting to the changes that are coming. By embracing automation and AI, we can create a future in which everyone has the opportunity to thrive.
