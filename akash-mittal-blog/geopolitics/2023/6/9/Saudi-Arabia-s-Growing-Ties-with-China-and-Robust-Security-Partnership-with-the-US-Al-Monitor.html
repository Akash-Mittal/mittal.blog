Saudi Arabia's Growing Ties with China and Robust Security Partnership with the US



Image Credits https://www.al-monitor.com/originals/2023/06/saudi-fm-china-cooperation-likely-grow-us-security-ties-robust

Imagine a country that is heavily dependent on its oil exports to fuel its economy, but its largest purchaser of oil is starting to look elsewhere. That country is Saudi Arabia, and that purchaser is China. Over the years, Saudi Arabia has increasingly relied on China for its economic growth, with China surpassing the United States as its largest trading partner. However, Saudi Arabia does not want to jeopardize its longstanding strategic partnership with the United States, particularly in terms of security cooperation. So how is Saudi Arabia navigating these two important relationships?

Examples with Case Studies

One way Saudi Arabia is deepening its ties with China is through its Belt and Road Initiative (BRI), which aims to promote trade and infrastructure across Eurasia and Africa. Saudi Arabia has become a key participant in the BRI, with China investing heavily in the country's infrastructure, including a high-speed rail line connecting the holy cities of Mecca and Medina. China is also the largest foreign investor in Saudi Arabia's oil industry, with state-owned companies such as Sinopec and China National Petroleum Corporation securing deals to explore and extract oil in the country.

On the other hand, Saudi Arabia sees the United States as its most important security partner, particularly in light of regional threats such as Iran and extremist groups like ISIS. In 2017, Saudi Arabia signed a $110 billion arms deal with the United States, with the aim of enhancing its military capabilities and strengthening its relationship with the US. The two countries also have a longstanding intelligence partnership, which has been crucial in counterterrorism efforts.

Another way Saudi Arabia is maintaining its security partnership with the US is through its role in the Arab coalition fighting in Yemen. While the US has decreased its military involvement in the conflict, it has continued to provide logistical and intelligence support to the coalition, with Saudi Arabia as its de facto leader.

Overall, Saudi Arabia is managing to balance its growing economic ties with China and its robust security partnership with the United States. By deepening its cooperation with China through the BRI and oil deals, Saudi Arabia is diversifying its economy and securing its long-term economic growth. At the same time, by continuing its security partnership with the US, Saudi Arabia is ensuring its regional security and stability.

  1. Saudi Arabia is rapidly building ties with China, its largest trading partner and a key player in the country's economic growth.
  2. However, Saudi Arabia recognizes the importance of its longstanding security partnership with the United States, particularly in light of regional threats.
  3. By balancing its economic relationship with China and its security partnership with the US, Saudi Arabia is securing its long-term economic growth and regional security and stability.
