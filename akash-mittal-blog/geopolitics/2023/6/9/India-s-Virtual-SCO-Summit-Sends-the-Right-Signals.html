India's Virtual SCO Summit Plan Sends the Right Signals



Image Credits https://asianews.network/indias-virtual-sco-summit-plan-sends-the-right-signals/

Several countries are beginning to show signs of cooperation in the face of global pandemic COVID-19. One such example of cooperation occurred on November 10, 2020, when India hosted the virtual Summit of the Shanghai Cooperation Organization (SCO). At the Summit, leaders of the member states of the SCO pledged to further enhance cooperation in various fields such as economy, security, and cultural exchanges.

SCO and India

SCO is a regional organization founded in 2001 to promote cooperation and stability in the Asian region. The organization was established with the aim of strengthening mutual trust, promoting cooperation, and ensuring regional peace, security, and stability. It comprises eight member states, namely China, India, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Pakistan, Russia, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan. Its observer states include Afghanistan, Belarus, Iran, and Mongolia.

India became a full member of the organization in 2017, and since then, its ties with the other members have grown stronger. In 2020, India was scheduled to host the annual SCO Summit. However, due to the ongoing pandemic situation, the meeting was postponed. Instead, a virtual summit was conducted on November 10, where Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi held talks with other leaders of the organization.

Key Highlights of the Summit

The virtual Summit was attended by the leaders of all member states and observers. The Summit focused on exploring ways to combat COVID-19 and reinvigorate the economies of SCO member states. It also discussed regional security and stability. Some of the key highlights of the Summit include:

  • The leaders from SCO countries pledged to deepen cooperation in combating COVID-19 by exchanging information and enhancing support to each other.
  • The Summit emphasized the promotion of trade and economic linkages between the member states by facilitating businesses and investing in infrastructure.
  • The leaders discussed the joint efforts to maintain regional security and stability by enhancing cooperation in counter-terrorism, drug control, and transnational crimes.
  • SCO members showed their commitment to promote cultural exchanges and tourism in the region to enhance people-to-people connectivity between the nations.

Case Studies

The Summit's outcomes are expected to provide a significant boost to regional cooperation among SCO members. To understand the potential impact of this cooperation, we can look at some of the case studies which demonstrate the various benefits of collaboration:

India and China's Economic Cooperation

The economic cooperation between India and China has been one of the most crucial drivers of the SCO's development. In recent years, India and China have significantly increased their trade and investment partnership. The two nations have been working together on various infrastructure projects like railways, highways, and ports. The positive cooperation between the two nations on economic fronts has played a significant role in promoting regional stability and development.

Tajikistan and India's Cultural Exchanges

Tajikistan and India share a lot in common historically and culturally, but the two nations have limited people-to-people connectivity until recently. However, that has changed - the two countries have been focusing on enhancing cultural exchanges in various fields like sports, literature, and arts. These exchanges help bring people closer and strengthen mutual trust. This cultural cooperation has helped promote tourism in Tajikistan and create more job opportunities in the country.

Russia and India's Defense Cooperation

India and Russia have a positive relationship on various fronts. India has been a significant defense partner of Russia, with both nations developing a close defense partnership for decades. India accounts for the highest volume of Russian defense exports, making it one of the most important defense partners for Russia. Besides the defense partnership, the two nations also cooperate in space exploration, nuclear energy, and other key sectors. This partnership has played a crucial role in enhancing regional security and stability.

Conclusion

The virtual SCO Summit was an essential step in promoting regional cooperation among its member states. The leaders of the SCO countries pledged to further strengthen their cooperation in various domains. The outcomes of the summit will have significant positive impacts on the entire region. It is essential to maintain and enhance the regional cooperation to face the new challenges that the world has brought.
