The Road to Make-in-India Goal: A Chinese Journey

Fun Zoo Toys is a classic Indian manufacturing excellence story that started as a humble family business in 1979, specializing in the production of heart-shaped cushions and "Little Ganesha" dolls.

However, the company was not contented to merely establish its place in the Indian toy industry. They had a larger vision to expand their business to other countries. In their search for growth and expansion, they could not ignore the elephant in the room: China.

China: The Manufacturing Giant

When it comes to manufacturing, China has always been the colossus in the room. From apparel to electronics, machinery to automobiles, China is the world's largest manufacturing hub.

Most of the global brands rely on China and its huge manufacturing capacity to fulfill their production requirements. According to a report by Deloitte, China manufactures over 28% of the world's goods.

However, the Chinese manufacturing monopoly has also raised concerns in India. The Indian government has been working tirelessly to establish a brand for Indian-made products and thereby lessen the country's dependency on foreign manufacturers.

Make-in-India Initiative

Enter the Make-in-India initiative, launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi in 2014. The goal was to make India a global manufacturing hub and encourage foreign investors to set up businesses in India. The initiative aimed at boosting manufacturing in India and increasing India's share in the global economy.

The Make-in-India initiative has since then garnered support from several corporates. Companies such as Samsung, ABB, Siemens, and Foxconn have pledged to invest in India and set up manufacturing facilities. Additionally, India has made notable progress in various sectors such as automobiles, electronics, and textiles.

The China-India Connection

While the aim of Make-in-India is to reduce the country's dependency on foreign manufacturers, it cannot ignore the immense opportunity that China presents. In fact, China could be the key to realizing India's ambitious manufacturing goals.

Indian businesses looking to expand globally have found a promising opportunity in China's vast manufacturing capabilities. A few Indian companies such as Fun Zoo Toys have successfully leveraged China's manufacturing prowess to expand their businesses.

Case Studies

Fanuc India

Fanuc India, a joint venture between Japan's Fanuc Corporation and India's ABB group, deals in robotics and factory automation products. The company had been facing challenges in meeting the rapidly increasing demand for its products from the Indian market.

The solution was to expand their production capabilities, and China offered precisely what they were looking for. They invested in a state-of-the-art facility in China that increased their production capacity and helped them reduce lead times. Today, Fanuc India has tripled its production capacity and increased revenue by over 75%.

Adani Group

Adani Group, a large Indian conglomerate, has a large footprint in the energy sector. The company had been facing challenges in producing high-quality solar panels at competitive prices. Their search for a solution led them to China, where they found several manufacturers producing high-quality solar panels at affordable prices.

This led to Adani Group acquiring a 50% stake in a Chinese company called Risen Energy Co. Ltd., which manufactures solar panels. This move helped the company reduce its production costs and meet the burgeoning Indian market's demand for solar energy.

Vedanta

Vedanta, a mining company, faced challenges in producing copper cathodes in large quantities in India due to the lack of essential infrastructure and skilled labor. The solution was to establish a world-class facility in China that helped them increase production and meet market demand.

The Chinese facility, in conjunction with a facility in India, helped Vedanta become a major contender in the copper cathode market. Today, Vedanta manages to produce over 400,000 tonnes of copper cathodes annually, solidifying its position in the market.

Conclusion

The journey towards the Make-in-India initiative's ambitious goals involves several challenges, such as increasing production capabilities, developing infrastructure, and eliminating regulatory hurdles. However, India cannot ignore the immense potential that China presents.

Indian companies willing to venture into China's vast manufacturing capabilities have found success. Leveraging China's manufacturing skills, Indian companies have managed to expand their production capabilities, reduce production costs and meet market demand.

The China-India connection presents an opportunity for both countries to enhance their bilateral ties, especially in the field of manufacturing. As India continues its journey towards a global manufacturing hub, China could well be the key to its success.
