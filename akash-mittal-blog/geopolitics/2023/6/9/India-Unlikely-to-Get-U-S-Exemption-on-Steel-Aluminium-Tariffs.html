India Unlikely to Get U.S. Exemption on Steel, Aluminium Tariffs



Image Credits https://www.reuters.com/markets/commodities/india-talks-with-us-steel-aluminum-tariff-exemption-sources-2023-06-08/

When it comes to international trade, tariffs can be a major point of contention. That's currently the case between India and the United States, as India has requested an exemption from US tariffs on steel and aluminium. India has proposed removing some tariffs on US agricultural goods in exchange, but the US has shown little interest in the offer.

This situation is a prime example of the complexities and challenges involved in international trade negotiations. Let's take a closer look at the issue and its potential impacts.

The Current Situation

In March 2018, the United States announced tariffs of 25% and 10% on imported steel and aluminium, respectively. These tariffs apply to imports from all countries, including India. Since then, India has been seeking an exemption from the tariffs, citing significant impacts on its export ability and domestic steel and aluminium industries.

India has proposed a solution in which it would remove some tariffs on imported US agricultural goods, giving US farmers greater export access to the Indian market. While the proposal has been raised in discussions between the two countries, the US has yet to seriously consider the idea.

Potential Impacts

If India is unable to secure an exemption from US steel and aluminium tariffs, it could experience a major blow to its export industry. The country is a significant exporter of steel and aluminium, with India accounting for about 2% of the US steel market, according to the International Trade Administration. India has already seen a decline in steel exports to the US since the tariffs were put in place.

On the other hand, if India does grant greater access to the Indian market for US agricultural goods, American farmers could see significant benefits. India is a massive market for agricultural products, with a population of 1.3 billion people and growing. Giving US farmers greater access to this market could help offset some of the losses they've experienced as a result of trade disputes with China.

Conclusion

The ongoing negotiations between India and the United States regarding steel and aluminium tariffs are complex and far-reaching. While India's proposal for removing tariffs on US agricultural goods may seem reasonable on the surface, it remains to be seen whether the US will take it seriously.

Regardless of the outcome, this situation highlights the challenges and intricacies of international trade negotiations. Countries must consider not only their own interests, but also the potential impacts on other industries and countries. Only through careful negotiation and compromise can effective trade agreements be reached.
