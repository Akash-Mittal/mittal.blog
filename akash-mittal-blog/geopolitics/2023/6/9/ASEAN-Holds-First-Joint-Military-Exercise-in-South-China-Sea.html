ASEAN Holds First Joint Military Exercise in South China Sea



Image Credits https://edition.cnn.com/2023/06/08/asia/asean-military-exercises-south-china-sea-indonesia-intl-hnk/index.html

Several countries in the South China Sea region have been entangled in a territorial dispute for several decades. While some countries have conflicting claims over the islands and waters, China has tried to establish its dominance over the entire region.

The ASEAN countries, which include Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, and Vietnam, have been particularly concerned about China's growing influence in the region. Tensions have been further fueled by China's militarization of the South China Sea and its building of artificial islands.

As a response to these developments, ASEAN has organized its first joint military exercise in the South China Sea. The exercise is aimed at enhancing regional security and improving the interoperability of the participating nations' armed forces.

The exercise, which is called "ASEAN-China Maritime Exercise", involves naval forces from China and ten ASEAN countries. The exercise is being held from October 22 to 28, 2018, in the city of Zhanjiang in southern China.

Case Studies

Case Study 1: The Philippines

The Philippines, which has had a long-standing territorial dispute with China, has been particularly vocal about its concerns over China's militarization of the South China Sea. In 2016, an international tribunal ruled that China's claims over the region are invalid. However, China has refused to recognize the ruling and has been continuing to build military facilities in the region.

As a response to China's aggressive actions, the Philippines has been increasing its defense capabilities and has been seeking alliances with other countries. The country is now participating in the ASEAN-China Maritime Exercise.

Case Study 2: Vietnam

Vietnam is another country that has been involved in a territorial dispute with China. In 2014, China moved an oil rig into waters claimed by Vietnam, triggering a standoff between the two countries. The tensions escalated to the point where Chinese and Vietnamese ships collided, and anti-China riots occurred in Vietnam.

As a result of the incident, Vietnam has been increasing its military and maritime capabilities. The country has also been fostering closer ties with the United States and other countries in the region.

Conclusion

The ASEAN-China Maritime Exercise is a significant step towards improving regional security in the South China Sea region. The exercise is a demonstration of the ASEAN countries' determination to uphold their rights and interests in the face of China's aggression. The exercise is also an important platform for improving communication and cooperation among the participating nations' armed forces.

In conclusion, it is crucial that all countries in the region work together to maintain peace and stability in the South China Sea. The ASEAN-China Maritime Exercise is a positive step towards achieving this goal.
