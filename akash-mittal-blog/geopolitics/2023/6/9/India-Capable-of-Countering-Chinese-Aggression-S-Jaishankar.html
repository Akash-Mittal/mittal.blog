India Capable of Countering Chinese Aggression: S Jaishankar



Image Credits https://www.livemint.com/news/india-capable-of-countering-chinese-aggression-refuses-to-join-nato-says-s-jaishankar-11686288765836.html

India's External Affairs Minister, S Jaishankar, has recently made a statement that India is capable of countering Chinese aggression and has no intentions of joining NATO. His statement comes in response to recent recommendations made by a US Congressional Committee.

India and China share a long history of border disputes. The most recent one took place in May 2020 when Chinese troops entered Indian territory in the Galwan Valley and clashes between the two armies resulted in the death of 20 Indian soldiers. Since then, there have been several rounds of talks between the two nations to resolve the issue. However, tensions between the two countries continue to escalate with China expanding its military presence at the border.

Examples with Case Studies

The Indo-China border issue is not new and has been a cause of conflict between the two countries for decades. The recent clash in the Galwan Valley has highlighted the need for India to be self-reliant when it comes to its defense capabilities.

One of the key factors that help India in countering Chinese aggression is its geographical location. India is surrounded by the Himalayas on one side and the Indian Ocean on the other. This makes it difficult for China to launch a full-scale invasion of India. The hostile terrain and difficult weather make it almost impossible for China to sustain a large-scale military operation in India.

Another factor that makes India capable of countering Chinese aggression is its military strength. Despite several limitations, India has the fourth-largest military in the world and has a well-equipped and well-trained army. India's nuclear deterrence capability is also one of the factors that discourage any aggressive action by China.

In addition to this, India has been strengthening its ties with other countries in the region to create a coalition against China. India has signed defense agreements with countries like the US, Japan, Indonesia, and Australia to counter China's increasingly assertive behavior in the region.

Given all the factors, it is safe to say that India is capable of countering Chinese aggression. Here are the three key takeaways from this discussion -

  • India's geographical location and difficult terrain make it challenging for China to launch a full-scale military invasion.
  • India's well-equipped and well-trained military with nuclear deterrence capability is a deterrent to any aggressive action by China.
  • India is building a coalition with other countries in the region to counter China's hostile behavior in the region.
