The Pakistan India World Cup Deadlock: A Case of Sports Diplomacy

It was the final match of the 2017 ICC Champions Trophy. The world had its eyes glued to their television screens as two of the fiercest rivals in cricket history, Pakistan and India, faced off at The Oval in London. Pakistan, the underdogs of the match, had already stunned the world with their performance in the tournament. However, India had always been their Achilles heel, as they had not won any of their past ten matches against them in ICC events.

Pakistan won the toss and chose to bowl first. Indian skipper, Virat Kohli, and dynamic opener, Rohit Sharma, walked out to bat. It was a crucial moment. If India got off to a good start, they would be able to set a big target for Pakistan to chase. On the other hand, if Pakistan managed to take some early wickets, it would give them the confidence to dominate the rest of the match. What followed next was a stunning display of sportsmanship, cricketing skills, and sports diplomacy by Pakistani fast bowler, Wahab Riaz.

Riaz started with an explosive spell of fast bowling, hitting the Indian batsmen with some hostile deliveries. His aggression was visible, but what followed next was something that the cricketing world had never witnessed before. When Riaz and Kohli exchanged some heated words, Riaz decided to take a step back. He walked up to Kohli, hugged him, and asked him to keep his cool. The crowd was left stunned, and so were Kohli and the Indian team. This act of sportsmanship by Riaz not only diffused the situation, but it also created a lot of goodwill between the two teams.

This incident highlights the role of sports diplomacy in promoting peace, harmony, and goodwill between countries. The relationship between India and Pakistan has always been strained due to a long history of political, economic, and social issues. However, when it comes to cricket, the two nations share a deep-rooted bond. It is this shared passion and love for the sport that creates opportunities for dialogue, understanding, and cooperation.

Examples of Sports Diplomacy between India and Pakistan

The role of sports diplomacy has been instrumental in bringing India and Pakistan closer to each other. The following examples illustrate how sports, particularly cricket, can be a powerful tool in promoting peace and diplomacy in the region:

The 2004 India Pakistan Cricket Series

In 2004, India and Pakistan played a historic cricket series that was called the "Friendship Series." The series was played after a gap of almost 15 years, and it was the first time that the two teams played a bilateral series since the 1999 Kargil war between the two countries. The series was coined as the "Friendship Series" as it was meant to promote peace and friendship between the two neighboring countries.

The series was a huge success, not just in terms of cricketing performance, but also in terms of the cultural exchange that took place. Both teams were given warm welcomes wherever they went, and fans from both sides cheered for the opposing team. The series was an excellent example of how sports can bring people together, regardless of their differences.

The 2011 World Cup Semi-Final

The 2011 World Cup semi-final between India and Pakistan was the most-watched cricket match in the history of the sport. The match was played in India, and it attracted an estimated audience of over one billion people worldwide.

While the match was a high-pressure game, what was most remarkable was the decision of the two teams to support each other. Walkie-talkies were used to build communication between the two teams, and both captains exchanged pleasantries during the toss. When India won, the Pakistani players were the first to congratulate them. The sportsmanship displayed by both teams was exemplary, and it showcased the power of sports in building bridges between nations.

The 2020 T20 World Cup

The recent deadlock between India and Pakistan over the T20 World Cup has once again sparked a debate on the role of sports diplomacy. While India has refused to play any bilateral series with Pakistan due to ongoing tensions between the two nations, the ICC's decision to host the T20 World Cup in India has put the two teams on a potential collision course.

The Pakistani government has called on the ICC to intervene and ensure that the team gets visas in time for the tournament. The Indian government, however, has maintained that it will not compromise on its security concerns, and has left it to the ICC to decide on the matter.

Conclusion

The Pakistan India World Cup Deadlock highlights the need for more sports diplomacy between the two countries. Sports has the power to unite people and promote peace, and it is essential that both governments recognize this and work towards building constructive relationships through sports. The role of sports in promoting cultural exchange and understanding cannot be ignored, and it is up to both governments to ensure that any tensions between the two countries do not spill over onto the cricket field.

As Wahab Riaz demonstrated in the 2017 Champions Trophy Final, sportsmanship, goodwill, and diplomacy can go a long way in building relationships between two nations, even when they have a history of conflict. While resolving issues between the two countries may not be easy, sports can be a first step towards peace and cooperation.
