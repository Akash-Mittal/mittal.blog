Upgrading India-U.S. Defense Ties to Counter China

Recent discussions between Indian and U.S. defense chiefs have centered around upgrading defense ties to counter China's growing military presence in the Asia-Pacific region. India and the U.S. share common concerns around China's aggressive territorial claims, human rights abuses, and cyber espionage activities.

India-U.S. Relations

India and the U.S. have been working to strengthen their relations for several years now, with defense cooperation playing a key role. In 2016, the U.S. designated India as a "major defense partner," paving the way for increased technology transfer and collaboration on defense projects.

One example of this collaboration is the joint production of the Apache helicopter, of which India is set to receive 22 units. The two countries are also jointly developing a next-generation aircraft carrier, reflecting India's ambition to increase its naval capacity and play a larger role in the Asia-Pacific region.

Case Studies

1. India's Acquisition of S-400 Missile System from Russia

One issue that has caused strain in India-U.S. defense ties is India's recent acquisition of the S-400 missile system from Russia. The U.S. has threatened to impose sanctions on India under its Countering America's Adversaries Through Sanctions Act (CAATSA), which imposes sanctions on any country that purchases defense equipment from Russia. However, India has argued that the S-400 is a critical part of its defense capabilities, particularly given China's growing military power.

While this issue has caused a temporary hiccup in defense ties, Indian officials are optimistic that it can be resolved through continued dialogue with the U.S.

2. U.S. Moves to Strengthen Cybersecurity Cooperation with India

The U.S. has also been working with India to strengthen cybersecurity cooperation in light of China's aggressive cyber espionage activities. In July 2020, the two countries held their first-ever "2+2" ministerial dialogue on cybersecurity, where they discussed ways to enhance their collaboration on cyber threats.

This dialogue came on the heels of India's decision to ban 59 Chinese mobile apps, citing national security concerns. The move was seen as a reflection of growing tensions between India and China, and the U.S. has expressed support for India's efforts to protect its sovereignty.

Conclusion

Overall, upgrading defense ties between India and the U.S. is seen as a critical step in countering China's growing military power in the Asia-Pacific region. While there have been some hiccups in the relationship, such as India's acquisition of the S-400 missile system and the U.S.'s imposition of sanctions on India under CAATSA, the two countries remain committed to deepening their collaboration on defense projects.
