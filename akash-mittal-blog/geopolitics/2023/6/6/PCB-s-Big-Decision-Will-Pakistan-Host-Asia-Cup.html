PCB's Big Decision: Will Pakistan Host Asia Cup?

Cricket is not just a sport in Asia but an emotion that brings people together. Asia Cup is one such cricket tournament that is eagerly awaited by fans across the continent. The tournament has been held regularly since 1984 and features teams from Asia and the Middle East. Pakistan was set to host the 2020 edition of the tournament but it got postponed due to the Covid-19 pandemic. Now, with the tournament scheduled for September 2021, PCB (Pakistan Cricket Board) has a big decision to make - whether to host the tournament or not.

The Importance of Asia Cup for Pakistan

For Pakistan, hosting Asia Cup is not just about organizing a cricket tournament but about showcasing the country's ability to host international events and presenting a positive image to the world. Hosting the tournament also provides an opportunity for local businesses to generate revenue and promotes tourism.

The Security Concerns

Security concerns have always been a major factor for international teams when deciding whether or not to tour Pakistan. The country has faced security threats from extremist groups in the past, leading to many teams refusing to travel to the country. However, in recent years, Pakistan has made significant efforts to improve its security situation, and this has resulted in the return of international cricket to the country.

In 2019, Sri Lanka became the first team to play a Test series in Pakistan since 2009, and since then, Pakistan has hosted Bangladesh, Zimbabwe, and South Africa. However, the security situation in the country is far from perfect, and the recent bombings in Quetta and Lahore have raised concerns among the cricketing community.

The Financial Implications

The financial implications of not hosting the Asia Cup are significant for Pakistan. The country is already facing an economic crisis, and canceling the tournament could result in a loss of revenue for the local businesses and the Pakistan Cricket Board. In addition, the cancellation of the tournament could also have a negative impact on the morale of the cricket fans in the country.

Conclusion

In conclusion, hosting the Asia Cup is a big decision for the Pakistan Cricket Board. The board needs to take into account the security situation in the country, the financial implications of canceling the tournament, and the importance of showcasing Pakistan's ability to host international events.
