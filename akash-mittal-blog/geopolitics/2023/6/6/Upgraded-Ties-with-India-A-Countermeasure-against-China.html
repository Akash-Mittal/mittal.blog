Upgraded Ties with India: A Countermeasure against China

The US Defense Secretary, Lloyd Austin, has discussed plans to upgrade ties with India as a strategy to counter China. During his visit to India in March 2021, Austin emphasized the importance of the US-India relationship in promoting regional stability and economic prosperity. He also discussed ways to enhance defense collaboration between the two nations, including joint military exercises and technology transfer.

Why Upgrade Ties with India?

India is a key player in the Indo-Pacific region, which has become a strategic priority for the US in recent years. The region is home to some of the world's busiest sea lanes, through which more than $5 trillion worth of trade flows annually. It is also a hotspot for territorial disputes and geopolitical tensions, with China asserting its influence and expanding its military presence in the region.

By strengthening ties with India, the US aims to counterbalance China's influence and promote a free and open Indo-Pacific. India, on its part, sees the US as a valuable partner in its efforts to modernize its military and secure its borders. The two nations have already signed agreements that deepen defense ties, such as the Logistics Exchange Memorandum of Agreement (LEMOA) and the Communications Compatibility and Security Agreement (COMCASA).

However, there is still room for further cooperation and collaboration, particularly in the defense technology sector. For example, India has been seeking to purchase armed drones from the US to boost its counterterrorism capabilities, but faced bureaucratic hurdles. The US, in turn, has been seeking to establish a stronger defense industrial base in India, as part of its strategy to reduce dependence on China.

Case Studies

One example of the US and India's defense collaboration is the joint development of the MH-60R Seahawk helicopter. The US approved the sale of the helicopters to India in 2020, and the two nations have since been working together to manufacture them in India. The MH-60R is a multi-mission helicopter that can be used for anti-submarine warfare, search and rescue, and naval gunfire support. This joint project is aimed at boosting India's maritime security capabilities and enhancing interoperability between the two nations.

Another example is the US-India Defense Technology and Trade Initiative (DTTI), which was launched in 2012 to promote collaboration on defense technology and co-production. One of the initiatives under DTTI is the joint development of a lightweight small arms technology. The project aims to develop a carbine rifle that is more effective and reliable than the existing Indian small arms, and to manufacture it in India. The US hopes that this project will help establish a long-term partnership in the defense technology sector.

Conclusion

The US and India share a common interest in promoting regional stability and countering China's influence in the Indo-Pacific. By upgrading ties with India, the US aims to enhance defense cooperation, boost economic ties, and promote a free and open Indo-Pacific. Through joint projects and initiatives, the two nations have been working together to strengthen their defense capabilities and deepen defense technology collaboration. However, there is still potential for further cooperation and collaboration, especially in areas such as armed drones, cybersecurity, and artificial intelligence.
