US-India Agree Roadmap for Defence Industry Cooperation

On October 27, 2021, India and the United States signed a new defence agreement that outlines a roadmap to deepen ties between the two countries' defence industries. The agreement highlights cooperation on emerging and critical technologies, joint research and development, and strengthening supply chains. This agreement follows a series of high-level interactions between the two nations, including the Quad summit, which brought together the leaders of India, the US, Australia, and Japan. Additionally, the US Secretary of Defence Lloyd Austin recently visited India as part of his two-week tour to Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

The Importance of the US-India Defence Cooperation

The US-India defence partnership has been growing steadily over the last few years, and the latest agreement expands on that progress. Both countries see the partnership as critical in balancing the power dynamics in Asia-Pacific and countering China's growing military influence in the region.

The joint statement issued by India and the United States outlines the objectives of the defence cooperation agreement:

  • Enhance the capabilities of Indian armed forces through training and advanced technologies.
  • Increase collaboration on research and development of defence technologies in areas such as artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and space.
  • Strengthen defence industry supply chains and promote investments in the sector.

Examples of US-India Defence Cooperation

The US-India defence cooperation has been growing steadily over the years, and there have been several successful collaborations between the two countries. Here are a few examples:

Apache Helicopters

In 2019, India received the first batch of four AH-64E Apache helicopters from the United States. The Indian Air Force considers them a game-changer in their combat capabilities. The Apache helicopters are equipped with state-of-the-art sensors and weapons systems that provide India with superior tactical and operational advantages. This was the first major defence deal between India and the United States since the election of Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi in 2014.

Integrated Air Defence Weapon System

Another successful collaboration between the US and India has been the development of an Integrated Air Defence Weapon System. The project was undertaken by India's Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in collaboration with the US-based Raytheon Corporation. The system was tested in 2019 and is expected to be deployed soon. This sophisticated system will be capable of detecting and tracking incoming enemy aircraft and missiles and provide a comprehensive air defence network to India.

Defence Technology and Trade Initiative

The US-India Defence Technology and Trade Initiative (DTTI) was established in 2012 to promote collaboration in the defence sector. As part of this initiative, several joint projects have been undertaken. One of the successful projects under the DTTI is the co-development of a lightweight small arms technology. The technology will provide Indian soldiers with advanced and lighter weapons that enable them to operate more effectively on the battlefield.

Conclusion

The US-India defence cooperation is critical in balancing the power dynamics in Asia-Pacific and countering China's growing military influence in the region. The recent defence agreement between India and the United States is a significant step forward in strengthening the partnership between the two countries. The agreement highlights the importance of emerging and critical technologies, joint research and development, and strengthening supply chains. This is a win-win situation for both countries as it will enhance India's defence capabilities and open up new opportunities for US companies in India's rapidly growing defence market.
