Why Upgrading Ties with India is Key to Countering China

US Defense Secretary, Lloyd Austin, recently discussed the importance of upgrading ties with India to counter China's growing military influence. The United States views India as a key strategic partner in the Indo-Pacific region, with a shared interest in preserving the rules-based international order and ensuring regional stability.

China's aggressive military actions in the South China Sea and along the disputed border with India have raised concerns among regional powers about its intentions. The United States has been working to strengthen its partnerships in the region to counter China's expanding influence.

India, with its large military force, strategic location, and democratic values, is seen as a critical partner in this effort. The two countries have been deepening their ties and increasing military cooperation in recent years.

One example of this partnership is the Quad, a regional security alliance that includes the United States, India, Japan, and Australia. The four countries share concerns about China's aggressive actions and have been working together to preserve regional stability.

Case Studies

The United States and India have been increasing their military cooperation in recent years through joint exercises and training programs. In 2020, the two countries conducted their largest-ever joint military exercise, known as the Tiger Triumph, off the coast of India.

India has also been purchasing advanced military hardware from the United States, including Apache attack helicopters, Chinook transport helicopters, and P-8 Poseidon maritime surveillance aircraft. These acquisitions have bolstered India's military capabilities and helped it to better defend its borders.

The United States has also been working to strengthen India's maritime capabilities, particularly in the Indian Ocean. The US has been sharing intelligence with India and conducting joint patrols to ensure the safety of shipping lanes and counter piracy and other threats.

One area where the two countries have been increasing cooperation is in space, with the United States supporting India's efforts to develop its space program. The two countries have also been working together on research and development of advanced technologies, such as artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and quantum computing.

Conclusion

Upgrading ties with India is crucial for the United States to counter China's aggressive actions and preserve regional stability in the Indo-Pacific region. The US sees India as a critical partner in this effort, with its large military force, strategic location, and shared democratic values.

The US and India have been deepening their military cooperation in recent years through joint exercises, training programs, and advanced military hardware acquisitions. The US has also been working to bolster India's maritime capabilities and support its space program.

Together, the US and India are working to preserve the rules-based international order and ensure that the Indo-Pacific region remains free, open, and peaceful.
