How China and India Expelled Each Other's Journalists in Tit-for-Tat Visa Spat

August 2020 - When Ajay, a journalist from India, arrived in Beijing to cover the annual summit of the Chinese Communist Party, he was greeted with a surprise. His visa had been cancelled, and he was ordered to leave the country immediately. As it turned out, China had just expelled 19 Indian journalists in retaliation for India's decision to expel 13 Chinese journalists earlier that month.

the Visa Spat

  • On June 18, 2020, India expelled 13 Chinese journalists from three newspapers - China Daily, People's Daily, and Global Times - and cancelled their visas, citing "concerns arising from the activities of these journalists."
  • On June 23, 2020, China retaliated by expelling 19 Indian journalists from two newspapers - The Hindu and The Economic Times - and cancelling their visas, citing "fallacious and unsubstantiated reporting."
  • By July 2020, the number of journalists from China and India in each other's countries had been reduced to single digits.
