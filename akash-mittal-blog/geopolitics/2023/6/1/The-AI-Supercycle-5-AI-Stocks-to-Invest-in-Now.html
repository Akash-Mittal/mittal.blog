The AI Supercycle is Here

In 2020, the global AI market was valued at $62 billion, and is projected to reach $309 billion by 2026. That's a compound annual growth rate of 33.2%. In the next five years, it's estimated that the AI market will create over $7 trillion in value.

One of the biggest industries to be impacted by AI is healthcare. AI-enabled medical devices are projected to save $150 billion annually for the healthcare industry by 2026, while also improving patient outcomes. Another industry that's seeing major AI adoption is finance, with AI-powered fraud detection systems projected to save banks $40 billion annually by 2023.

5 AI Stocks to Buy Now

  1. Alphabet Inc. (GOOGL) - Google's parent company has a dominant position in the search engine market, but they're also a leader in AI research. Google's DeepMind division developed AlphaGo, the computer program that beat a professional Go player in 2016. It's estimated that Google's AI projects generate over $3 billion in revenue annually.
  2. Nvidia Corporation (NVDA) - Nvidia produces graphics processing units (GPUs) that are used in a variety of industries, including gaming, automotive, and data centers. Their GPUs are also well-suited for AI training and inference, which has led to rapid adoption of Nvidia products in the AI market. In 2020, Nvidia's revenue from data center sales increased by 124% year-over-year.
  3. Microsoft Corporation (MSFT) - Microsoft has been investing heavily in AI research and development, with a particular focus on natural language processing (NLP) and computer vision. Their Azure cloud platform offers a variety of AI services, including language understanding, speech recognition, and image recognition. In 2020, Microsoft's revenue from Azure increased by 48% year-over-year.
  4. Amazon.com, Inc. (AMZN) - Amazon has been developing AI technologies for several years, including the popular Alexa virtual assistant. Their AWS cloud platform offers a variety of AI services, including machine learning, speech recognition, and predictive analytics. In 2020, Amazon's revenue from AWS increased by 30% year-over-year.
  5. Twilio Inc. (TWLO) - Twilio is a cloud communications platform that offers a variety of messaging, voice, and video APIs. Their AI-powered product, Twilio Autopilot, allows developers to build conversational chatbots that can understand natural language inputs. In 2020, Twilio's revenue increased by 55% year-over-year.
