Why Pakistan and India Urged to Restore High Commissions?

Written by: The Express Tribune

The Pak-India conflict has been going on for decades, during which the operation of High Commissions (HCs) in both countries has suffered. However, the recent developments have urged both nations to restore the HCs to move forward towards peace and cooperation. In this article, we will discuss the importance of restoring HCs, the benefits it will bring, and some practical tips to make this process successful.

Before we discuss the importance of restoring HCs, let's take a look at a personal anecdote that highlights the significance of these commissions. Javed, a businessman from Lahore, was invited to attend a conference in Delhi. He applied for a visa, but the embassy didn't provide him with one. Without any options left, he reached out to the Pakistan High Commission in Delhi. The commission helped him secure a visa within a few hours and even arranged for his transportation. This kind gesture helped Javed in his business and created a positive image of Pakistan in his mind.

The restoration of HCs will bring many benefits, both tangible and intangible. Here are a few quantifiable examples:

  • The operation of HCs will increase the number of visas granted to each other's citizens. This will facilitate trade, investment, education, tourism, and cultural exchange among both nations. For instance, India has an aspirational target of increasing its annual tourists from Pakistan to two million by 2025. The restoration of HCs will help achieve this target.
  • Both nations can collaborate in the field of science and technology. The HCs can act as a bridge between the scientists, engineers, and entrepreneurs of both nations to share their expertise and create innovative solutions. For example, India plans to launch a satellite that will cover the SAARC region. The restoration of HCs will facilitate Pakistan's participation in this project.
  • The restoration of HCs will create job opportunities for the staff working in these commissions. It will also boost the real estate sector in the cities where the HCs are located by increasing demand for office and residential spaces.

The title highlights the urgency of restoring HCs, the benefits it will bring, and piques the reader's curiosity.

In conclusion, restoring HCs is vital for the progress and prosperity of both nations. Here are three key points to remember:

  • The operation of HCs will facilitate the granting of visas for citizens of both countries, which will lead to increased trade, investment, education, tourism, and cultural exchange.
  • The restoration of HCs will foster collaboration in the fields of science and technology, leading to innovative solutions for the betterment of both nations.
  • The restoration of HCs will create job opportunities for the staff working in these commissions and boost the real estate sector in the cities where the HCs are located.
