How China's Private Airlines Are Making Profits Through Their Strategic Moves

China's aviation industry is experiencing a significant growth phase and this can be attributed to the efforts of the country's private airlines. A few years ago, it was a struggle for private airlines to make a name for themselves in the aviation industry, as the sector was dominated by state-owned companies. However, with their focus on lower fares and regional expansion, these airlines have since carved out their own niche in the industry and are now seeing increased profits.

The Beginning

Private airlines in China started in the early 2000s, competing with the already established state-owned airlines. It was a challenging road ahead, as they had limited routes, aircraft, and expertise to start with. However, they found their footing and took advantage of the changing travel patterns and growing middle-class population in China.

One of the airlines that have emerged from this competitive landscape is Spring Airlines. The CEO, Wang Zhenghua, launched the airline by targeting budget travelers and expanding its coverage to secondary cities. The demand was there, and Spring Airlines capitalized on it through its low-budget offerings.

Lower Fares

In order to stay competitive, private airlines in China have focused on providing lower fares compared to state-owned airlines. This has been a critical factor in gaining a significant market share and winning over budget-conscious consumers. Spring Airlines has been able to achieve this through its no-frills offerings and reducing costs. They don't offer free meals, but passengers can purchase snacks and drinks on board, which helps cut down on weight and fuel costs. The airline also streamlined its operations to keep costs low, reducing the time it takes for each flight to get in the air. All of this culminates in providing lower fares and attracting more passengers.

Regional Focus

Another successful strategy of China's private airlines has been its regional focus. While state-owned airlines tend to focus on international routes, private airlines have focused on underserved domestic routes. This allows them to capture a market share that is not covered by the larger airlines. Operating at a smaller scale allows private airlines to provide more personal service while keeping fares lower.

Results

Since their inception, private airlines in China have been gaining market share and seeing profits. Spring Airlines, for example, has seen a significant growth in profits since 2014. In fact, their profits have consistently been higher than the state-owned airlines in China.

Another example of a private airline that has profited from this growth trend is Juneyao Airlines. In 2019, they saw profits rise by 17.5% to $251 million. They have also expanded their international destinations and increased their coverage in China's second-tier cities, bringing more tourism and business travel to smaller regions.

Conclusion

In conclusion, China's private airlines have succeeded in carving out a significant market share in the aviation industry by focusing on lower fares and regional routes. Their ability to streamline their operations and provide personalized services has allowed them to remain competitive while capturing underserved markets. With China's middle class expected to continue growing in the coming years, the demand for affordable air travel will continue to rise, making private airlines a very attractive investment opportunity.

Practical Tips:

  • Consider traveling during off-peak seasons to take advantage of lower fares
  • Check out private airlines when looking for domestic flights in China
  • Join frequent flyer programs for private airlines, as they offer incentives for repeat travelers
