The High Inflation Rate in Pakistan: A Burden on the Poor

An Eye-Opening Story about Inflation and its Consequences

By: [Your Name]

Abdul was a middle-class man who lived in Karachi, Pakistan. He had a decent job in a private company and was able to support his family without any financial difficulties. However, things started to change when the inflation rate skyrocketed in the country.

Abdul's monthly expenses started to exceed his income, making it nearly impossible for him to make ends meet. He was forced to cut down on his child's education, skip meals, and delay medical treatments. Abdul's story is not unique; it is one of many stories of people who are struggling to survive in a country that is plagued by high inflation rates.

The inflation rate in Pakistan has hit a record high of 37.97 percent in 2021, causing many citizens to feel the pinch of rising prices. The skyrocketing prices of essential goods and services have made it challenging for people to make ends meet. The rising inflation rate has affected everyone from the working-class to the middle-class, and even the wealthy are experiencing the effects of inflation.

Inflation in Pakistan

  • In 2019, the inflation rate was 7.34 percent, while in 2020 it was 10.74 percent.
  • The prices of all food items increased by 14.43 percent year-on-year in June 2021.
  • The prices of petroleum products increased by 47 percent in the last two years.
  • The rate of sugar increased by 120 percent, and the rate of wheat increased by 78 percent in one year.

Consequences of High Inflation Rates

  • The high inflation rate has made it difficult for people to afford basic necessities such as food, shelter, and healthcare.
  • Many businesses have been forced to shut down due to rising costs of production, resulting in job losses.
  • The rising prices have also had an adverse effect on the country's economic growth.

Conclusion

  1. The high inflation rate is a significant challenge for the people of Pakistan, especially those who are living on a limited income.
  2. The government needs to take immediate action to control inflation and reduce the burden on citizens.
  3. Citizens can help to control inflation by adopting prudent strategies such as budgeting and saving money wherever possible.
