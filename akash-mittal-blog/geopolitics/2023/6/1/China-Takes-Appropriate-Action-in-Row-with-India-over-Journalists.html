China Takes Appropriate Action in Row with India over Journalists

How China's response to India's expulsion of Chinese journalists has sparked a new round of tensions between the two countries

In September 2020, India expelled two Chinese journalists working for state-run media companies after they were found to have violated their visa regulations. China criticized the move as unjustified and retaliated by expelling two Indian journalists based in Beijing. This was just the latest escalation in a longstanding conflict between the two countries over territorial disputes and trade imbalances.

  • In 2019, India banned Chinese mobile apps such as TikTok and WeChat, citing national security concerns and unfair competition for Indian startups.
  • In 2020, India began using more stringent criteria for allowing Chinese companies to invest or take part in government contracts, further limiting their participation in India's economy.
  • In the first nine months of 2020, China's exports to India fell by 16.3% year-on-year, reflecting the impact of the pandemic and the deteriorating relationship between the two countries.
