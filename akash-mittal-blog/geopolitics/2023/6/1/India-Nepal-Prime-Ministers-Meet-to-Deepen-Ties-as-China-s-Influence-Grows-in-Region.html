India Nepal Prime Ministers Meet to Deepen Ties as China's Influence Grows in Region

An interesting story on the current political situation in South Asia

On May 15th, 2018, Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Nepalese Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli met in Nepal's capital, Kathmandu. The two leaders discussed ways to deepen cooperation between the two countries in areas such as trade, energy, and connectivity. This meeting is significant in the context of China's growing influence in the region and India's efforts to counter it.

China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) aims to connect China with Europe, Asia, and Africa through a network of infrastructure projects. One of its key components is the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC), a $62 billion project that seeks to connect China's western province of Xinjiang with Pakistan's Gwadar port. CPEC passes through the disputed territory of Gilgit-Baltistan, which India claims as part of its territory, and has raised concerns in India about China's strategic intentions in the region.

In response, India has been seeking to deepen ties with its neighbors, including Nepal, Bhutan, and Bangladesh, through initiatives such as the South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC), the Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Nepal (BBIN) initiative, and the Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation (BIMSTEC).

India Nepal Ties

  • India is Nepal's largest trading partner, with bilateral trade worth $7.2 billion in 2016-17.
  • India is Nepal's largest source of foreign direct investment (FDI), with investments worth $2.29 billion in 2017.
  • India is Nepal's largest source of tourists, with 160,832 Indian tourists visiting Nepal in 2017.
  • An integrated check-post (ICP) at Birgunj-Raxaul, which is the main trade route between India and Nepal, has been constructed with Indian assistance.
  • India is helping Nepal in the construction of the 900 MW Arun III hydropower project, which is expected to generate about 3.9 billion units of electricity annually.
  • India is helping Nepal in the construction of the Raxaul-Amlekhgunj oil pipeline, which will supply petroleum products to Nepal at a lower cost than the present mode of transportation.
