India Launches Electronics Repair Pilot Project to Lure Manufacturers

In bustling cities and remote villages throughout India, discarded electronics are a common sight. The country is home to the world's fifth-largest e-waste generator, producing over two million metric tonnes of electronic waste every year. But with the launch of a new pilot project, India is taking a step towards reducing its e-waste problem while also attracting manufacturers to the country.

The initiative, called the National E-Waste Management Program, aims to create a "circular economy" for electronics by providing a standardized system for the collection, dismantling, and recycling of electronic goods.

The pilot project was launched by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) and the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) in collaboration with a private partner, E-Parisaraa Pvt Ltd. The company has been appointed as the project management agency for the collection and dismantling of e-waste in 10 states across India.

Quantifiable Examples

- The pilot project will create an estimated 19,000 jobs in the e-waste management industry.

- According to a report by the United Nations University, India generated 2 million metric tonnes of e-waste in 2019 alone, second only to China.

- A survey conducted by Assocham-Frost & Sullivan estimates that only 5% of e-waste in India is collected and recycled, with the rest ending up in landfills or being illegally traded.
