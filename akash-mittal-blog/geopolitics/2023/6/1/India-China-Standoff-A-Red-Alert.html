India-China Standoff: A Red Alert!

China and India have been at loggerheads over their disputed borders for decades. The border dispute, which dates back to the 1960s, has seen the two nations engage in a number of military skirmishes over the years. Recently, tensions between the two Asian giants have spiked, with the two nations engaging in a bitter border stand-off.

The world's two most populous nations have been trading barbs over the last few months, and the latest round of talks appears to have made little progress in easing tensions. The most recent talks were held on December 18, 2020, but both sides failed to strike a deal on de-escalation.

Quantifiable Examples

The recent tensions between India and China have had a significant economic impact, with many Indian businesses facing losses due to the border stand-off. The construction and infrastructure sectors have been hit especially hard, as many Chinese companies were involved in these areas. In addition, India's tourism sector has also suffered a major blow, as many Chinese tourists have cancelled their trips due to the tension.

On the other hand, India's determination to reduce its dependence on Chinese imports has also led to a sharp decline in Chinese goods entering the Indian market. This has led to a significant drop in imports from China, with the country's exports to India falling by more than 25% in the first half of 2020.
