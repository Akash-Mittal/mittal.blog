India-China Diplomatic Talks in New Delhi

An effort to resolve the ongoing border dispute in Eastern Ladakh

India and China held in-person diplomatic talks in New Delhi after months of tension and military build-up at the Line of Actual Control (LAC) in Eastern Ladakh. The talks were held at the foreign secretary level with both countries hoping to find a way out of the prolonged border impasse.

Image source: NDTV

The border dispute between India and China in Eastern Ladakh began in May 2020 when both countries accused each other of violating the LAC. This led to a military stand-off between the two countries with troops from both sides facing each other at several points along the LAC. The situation had escalated to the point where both countries had deployed thousands of troops, tanks, and artillery guns along the border.

After several rounds of talks at the military and diplomatic level, both countries had agreed to disengage their troops from Pangong Tso, one of the major flashpoints in the border dispute. This was seen as a positive step towards resolving the border impasse, but tensions remained high as both countries continue to hold a large number of troops and military equipment along the LAC.

The in-person diplomatic talks in New Delhi were seen as a crucial step towards finding a permanent solution to the border dispute. The talks were held amidst strict COVID-19 protocols with the foreign secretary of India and China discussing several issues related to the border dispute. Both countries expressed their commitment to resolving the issue peacefully through dialogue and consultation.

  • India and China have held several rounds of diplomatic and military talks since the border dispute began in May 2020.
  • Both countries have deployed a large number of troops and military equipment along the LAC in Eastern Ladakh.
  • The disengagement of troops from Pangong Tso was seen as a positive step towards resolving the border impasse.
  • The in-person diplomatic talks in New Delhi were the first since November 2020 and were seen as a crucial step towards finding a permanent solution to the border dispute.
