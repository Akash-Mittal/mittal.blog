India's Evolving Maritime Strategy: A South Asian Perspective

It was a cloudy afternoon in September 2016, when the Indian Navy successfully completed its most complex naval exercise known as the "Varuna" with the French Navy in the Indian Ocean. The exercise involved over 10 warships, two submarines, and a squadron of fighter jets from both navies, operating in tandem to practice modern naval tactics and enhance maritime interoperability. This exercise was significant not only for the scale and complexity but also for its geopolitical significance.

India, being surrounded by water on three sides, has always been aware of the strategic importance of its maritime environment. However, in the past decade, India's maritime strategy has undergone significant changes in response to the evolving geopolitical environment. The strategic shift towards the seas has been driven by India's growing economic and energy security interests, increasing maritime threats, and the emerging global power dynamic that places a greater emphasis on naval power projection.

India's Naval Power: Some Quantifiable Examples

India's naval power has progressed significantly in recent years, with the country's navy being ranked as the fourth most powerful by Global Firepower Index in 2021. India's naval fleet comprises approximately 280 ships and submarines, including aircraft carriers, destroyers, frigates, corvettes, and missile boats. The Indian Navy has also invested in developing its indigenous capabilities in naval shipbuilding, with the recent commissioning of INS Kavaratti, a stealth warship built in India.

Another quantifiable example of India's maritime prowess is the establishment of India's first integrated command, the Indian Ocean Region Command, in 2021. The command is responsible for India's entire maritime security and is tasked with securing India's strategic interests in the region. India also participates in several naval exercises and arrangements, such as the "Malabar" naval exercise with the US, Japan and Australia, and the "Milan" multilateral naval exercise with several countries in the Indian Ocean Region.

Challenges and the Future of India's Maritime Strategy

Despite these advancements, India's maritime strategy faces several challenges in the near and distant future. The rising naval power of China, which has been building its naval capabilities at a rapid pace, presents a serious challenge to India's naval dominance in the Indian Ocean. The challenge is further compounded by China's Belt and Road Initiative, which seeks to establish maritime trade routes and naval bases in the Indian Ocean. India also faces non-traditional maritime threats such as piracy, terrorism, and climate change, which could threaten India's energy security and its coastal populations.

India's future maritime strategy is expected to focus on enhancing its naval capabilities further, including the development of more aircraft carriers, nuclear submarines, and naval bases. India is also expected to increase its participation in the Quad (India, US, Japan, and Australia), which is seen as a counterbalance to China's growing naval influence in the region. India's maritime strategy is closely linked to its economic and energy security interests, as well as its strategic partnerships and alliances.

Conclusion: India's Evolving Maritime Strategy in 3 Points

  • India's maritime strategy has evolved significantly over the past decade, driven by its growing economic and energy security interests, increasing maritime threats, and the emerging global power dynamic that places a greater emphasis on naval power projection.
  • India's naval power has progressed significantly, with the country's navy being ranked as the fourth most powerful and with the establishment of India's first integrated command, the Indian Ocean Region Command, in 2021.
  • India's future maritime strategy is expected to focus on enhancing its naval capabilities further, including the development of more aircraft carriers, nuclear submarines, and naval bases, as well as its participation in the Quad and other strategic partnerships and alliances.
