The Bright Future of China-UK Business Relations: Insights from China Britain Business Council Chair

*This article is a transcription of an interview with Mr. Jim James, China Britain Business Council Chair, conducted by our correspondent, Lily.

A Story of China-UK Business Friendship

Mr. Jim James shares an interesting anecdote from his personal experience to illustrate the strong bond and potential of China-UK business relations. In his early days of working in China, he was invited to a meal by a Chinese business partner he had never met before. Despite the language barrier, they broke the ice through friendly gestures and a shared love of football. They exchanged gifts and, by the end of the meal, they had become friends.

Fast-forward a few years, and Mr. James had become a board member of a British company that was considering overseas expansion. They chose China as their destination, and Mr. James was put in charge of the project. Much to his surprise, one of the Chinese investors who was willing to back the British company was the very same business partner he had shared a meal with years prior. This example, according to Mr. James, showcases the potential for long-term business relationships built on mutual respect and trust.

To quantify the strength of China-UK business relations, Mr. James offers some impressive statistics. Additionally, there are more than 10,000 joint ventures between UK and Chinese companies, with over 400 British companies having a presence on the ground in China. In reverse, over 800 Chinese companies have invested in the UK, creating over 75,000 jobs.

An : Why China-UK Business Relations are Set to Flourish

As the global economy continues to face ongoing challenges such as the pandemic, trade tensions, and political uncertainty, why should businesses consider China-UK relations in the first place? According to Mr. James, there are a number of reasons. Firstly, the UK's relatively open economy has weathered the storm better than most, making it an appealing location for Chinese companies to expand into. Furthermore, with the UK leaving the EU, it now has an opportunity to shape its own trade policies and agreements with countries such as China. Finally, China's economy has been on the rise for decades, and it is now the world's largest by purchasing power parity. As such, it presents a wealth of opportunities for UK businesses looking to break into the Asian market.

Conclusions: The Three Pillars to Establish Sustainable China-UK Business Relations

So, what can businesses do to ensure long-term, sustainable China-UK relationships? Mr. James identifies three key pillars:

  • Establish Personal Relationships: As the anecdote at the beginning of this article demonstrates, building personal relationships is key to establishing trust and loyalty in business partnerships. It is important to take the time to build these relationships, even if it may seem like an initial hurdle.
  • Understand Cultural Differences: The UK and China have vastly different business practices and cultural norms. It is important to educate oneself on these differences in order to avoid misunderstandings or offense. Additionally, showing respect for Chinese traditions and expectations can go a long way in building trust with business partners.
  • Invest in Technology and Innovation: Both the UK and China have thriving technology and innovation sectors. By investing in these areas, businesses can establish competitive advantages and create mutually beneficial partnerships with companies in the other country.

By prioritising these three pillars, businesses can work to establish long-lasting, mutually beneficial relationships between the UK and China.

and Case Studies

Mr. James offers several personal anecdotes to illustrate his points. For example, when he first began working with China, he was struck by how long it would take to get a deal done compared to the UK. Chinese business culture prioritises relationship-building and trust-building over quick decision-making, so it is important to be patient and work to establish connections before expecting deals to happen.

In terms of case studies, he cites the example of Jaguar Land Rover (JLR), a UK-based car manufacturer that has found great success in China. JLR was able to quickly identify the increasing demand for luxury vehicles in China and adapt its business model accordingly. Today, JLR has more than 200 dealerships in over 100 cities in China, making it one of the most successful British brands to break into the Chinese market.

Practical Tips for Businesses

Mr. James offers several tips for businesses looking to expand into China:

  • Take the Time to Build Relationships: As stated earlier, building relationships is key to success in China. This means investing in frequent meetings, face-to-face conversations, and other relationship-building exercises.
  • Be Patient: Many businesses struggle to make quick deals happen in China, where face-to-face meetings and long-term relationship building take precedence over immediate results.
  • Consider Partnerships: Finding the right Chinese partner can go a long way in helping a UK business establish itself in the Chinese market. A local partner can offer invaluable insight into cultural practices, business customs, and established relationships with other companies in the market.
