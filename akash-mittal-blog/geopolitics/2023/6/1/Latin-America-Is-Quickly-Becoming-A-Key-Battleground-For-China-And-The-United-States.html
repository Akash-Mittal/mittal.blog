Latin America Is Quickly Becoming A Key Battleground For China And The United States

Latin America has traditionally been an area of influence for the United States, due to its geographic proximity and historical ties. However, in recent years, China has been increasing its presence in the region, becoming one of its main trading partners and investing heavily in its infrastructure, natural resources, and technology.

According to the Inter-American Development Bank, Chinese trade with Latin America grew from $270 million in 1990 to $306 billion in 2019, representing an increase of over 1,000 times. In contrast, United States trade with Latin America grew from $130 billion to $680 billion during the same period.

"Latin America is still considered America's backyard, but China is building its own swimming pool."

The rise of China in Latin America presents a dilemma for the United States. On one hand, it fears that China's growing influence in the region could threaten its own security and economic interests. On the other hand, it recognizes that Latin America is an important partner in its global strategy and that it cannot afford to neglect it.

The Battle for Latin America

The competition between China and the United States for influence in Latin America is taking place on multiple fronts, including trade, investment, infrastructure, education, and technology. Here are some examples:

Trade

China has become the main trading partner of Brazil, Chile, Peru, and Uruguay, and is the second-largest trading partner of Argentina and Mexico. It has also signed free trade agreements with Chile, Peru, and Costa Rica, and is negotiating agreements with Brazil and Mexico.

Investment

China has invested heavily in Latin America, particularly in infrastructure projects such as ports, railways, highways, and airports. For example, it has financed the construction of a $50 billion interoceanic canal in Nicaragua, a $6 billion railway in Mexico, and a $3.7 billion hydroelectric plant in Ecuador. It has also invested in natural resources, such as oil, gas, copper, and soybeans, and in technology, such as 5G networks and artificial intelligence.

Infrastructure

China's infrastructure projects in Latin America have helped improve connectivity, reduce transportation costs, and increase trade. However, some experts warn that they could also lead to debt traps, environmental damage, and political influence. For example, China has been accused of using its investment in the Brazilian telecoms company Oi to spy on Brazil's government and businesses.

Education

China has been increasing its support for education and cultural exchanges with Latin America, in order to promote its language, culture, and soft power. For example, it has established Confucius Institutes and classrooms in many Latin American countries, offering Chinese language courses, cultural events, and scholarships. However, some observers have criticized these initiatives as a tool for propaganda and espionage, and have called for greater transparency and scrutiny.

Technology

China has been promoting its technology products and services in Latin America, particularly in the areas of 5G networks, smartphones, drones, and surveillance systems. It has also been offering training and support for Latin American technicians and entrepreneurs, in order to create a local ecosystem of innovation and entrepreneurship. However, some experts warn that Chinese technology could pose a risk to privacy, security, and intellectual property, and have called for stronger regulation and standards.

The Implications for Latin America

The growing competition between China and the United States for influence in Latin America has both positive and negative implications for the region. On one hand, it offers more opportunities for trade, investment, and development, and reduces its dependence on one single partner. On the other hand, it increases its vulnerability to external pressures, political interference, and environmental degradation.

Some experts argue that Latin America should adopt a more independent and proactive approach to its relations with China and the United States, based on its own interests and values. For example, it could seek to diversify its trade partners, attract more investment in sustainable infrastructure and technologies, strengthen its institutional and environmental safeguards, and promote dialogue and cooperation among all actors.

The Way Forward

In conclusion, the battle for Latin America is a complex and dynamic process that involves multiple actors and dimensions. However, we can highlight three main points:

  1. Latin America is a key battleground for China and the United States. Their competition for influence in the region has intensified in recent years, and is likely to continue in the future.
  2. The implications for Latin America are both positive and negative. While it offers more opportunities for development and diversity, it also poses risks of dependency and vulnerability.
  3. The way forward for Latin America is to adopt a more independent and proactive strategy. It should seek to balance its relations with China and the United States, based on its own interests and values, and to promote sustainable and inclusive development for all.
