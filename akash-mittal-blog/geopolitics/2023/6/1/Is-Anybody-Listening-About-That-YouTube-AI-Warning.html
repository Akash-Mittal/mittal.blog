Is Anybody Listening About That YouTube AI Warning?

It was a typical Saturday morning when Michael, a father of two, sat down with his phone and opened up the YouTube app. He intended to enjoy a few videos before attending to household chores. As he scrolled through the suggested content, he noticed something strange: all of the recommended videos centered on parenting. It was like the app knew everything about him and just how to keep him hooked.

Michael was startled by the realization, but experts in AI have been warning about social media algorithms for years. YouTube, just like other platforms, uses AI to learn about users and recommend content based on what they have watched before. The problem is that these algorithms are designed to keep users engaged and on the platform for longer periods of time - at any cost.

Did you know that, on average, people spend over an hour on YouTube every day? An increase in viewing time means an increase in ad revenue, so the platform benefits from keeping users engaged. In fact, studies have shown that YouTube's algorithms have been responsible for the spread of hate speech, conspiracy theories, and extremist content. An investigation by The Guardian found that YouTube's recommendations directed users towards extremist content over 15,000 times. These algorithms have also been linked to the rise of anti-vaxxers, flat earthers, and climate change denialists.

  1. We must recognize the role of algorithms in shaping our online experiences and how they can negatively impact our beliefs and behaviors.
  2. We need to limit our exposure to content that has the potential to affect our wellbeing, such as extremist material and conspiracy theories.
  3. We must be proactive in educating ourselves and our children about how social media operates, and how to make informed decisions when using these platforms.

One particular case study that comes to mind is that of Paul Horner, a fake news writer who claimed to have helped influence the outcome of the 2016 US presidential election. Practical Tips

  • Use ad blockers to minimize the number of targeted advertisements you see.
  • Be conscious of how much time you spend on social media platforms, and try to limit your usage accordingly.
  • Take breaks from social media altogether, especially if you find yourself becoming easily addicted to it.
