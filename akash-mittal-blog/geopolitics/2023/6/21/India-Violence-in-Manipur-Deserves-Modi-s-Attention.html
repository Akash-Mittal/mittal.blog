India: Violence in Manipur Deserves Modi's Attention



Image Credits https://foreignpolicy.com/2023/06/13/india-manipur-ethnic-violence-modi-china-myanmar/

It was a chilly winter evening, and I was visiting my friend in Manipur. As the night progressed, we heard loud noises and sirens outside his house. We rushed to the balcony only to witness a horrific sight - a group of men shouting and pelting stones at a passing vehicle. The vehicle belonged to a local politician who was on his way to address a public gathering.

Unfortunately, this is not an isolated incident. Violence and conflict have become a common sight in Manipur and the surrounding regions. The situation is alarming and requires immediate attention from the government, especially the Prime Minister Narendra Modi. Here are a few reasons why:

  1. Human Rights Violations: The people of Manipur have been subjected to severe human rights violations for decades now. The Indian army has been accused of using excessive force in dealing with insurgent groups, leading to the deaths and disappearances of many innocent citizens. Women in the state have also been victims of sexual assault and harassment. The government has failed to take any concrete steps to address these issues, and the situation continues to deteriorate.
  2. Economic Instability: The violence and unrest in Manipur have severely impacted the state's economy. The constant shutdowns and curfews have disrupted trade and commerce, and tourism has taken a severe hit. Manipur has the potential to become a major tourist destination, thanks to its natural beauty and cultural heritage. However, the ongoing conflict has made it almost impossible for the state to attract tourists. The government needs to take immediate steps to restore peace and stability in the region to boost economic growth.
  3. The Plight of the Common Man: The people of Manipur have been caught in the crossfire of the ongoing conflict for too long. They are tired of the violence and want peace and stability. The government needs to address their concerns and work towards finding a solution that benefits all. The common man is suffering due to the constant shutdowns and curfews. They want to be able to go about their daily lives without fear or hindrance. It is time for the government to step up and provide the much-needed relief to the people of Manipur.

Case Studies

Let us take a look at a few case studies to better understand the gravity of the situation in Manipur:

  • Manipur Blockade: In November 2016, a blockade was imposed on the National Highway 2 by a group protesting against the state government's decision to create seven new districts. The blockade lasted for 139 days and severely impacted the state's economy. Prices of essential commodities skyrocketed, leading to widespread protests and unrest. The government was finally forced to intervene and end the blockade. However, the incident highlighted the lack of governance and accountability in the state.
  • Extra-judicial Killings: Amnesty International has reported several cases of extrajudicial killings by the Indian army in Manipur. Army personnel have been accused of staging fake encounters and killing innocent civilians. The victims are often labeled as militants, and their families are not given any compensation. This has led to widespread anger and resentment against the army in Manipur.
  • Women Safety: The National Crime Records Bureau has reported a high incidence of crimes against women in Manipur. Women in the state have been subjected to rape, domestic violence, and trafficking. The government has been criticized for not taking any proactive steps to address the issue.

Conclusion

The situation in Manipur is dire, and it requires immediate attention from the government, especially the Prime Minister Narendra Modi. The people of Manipur are tired of the violence and want peace and stability. The government needs to take concrete steps to address the issues of human rights violations, economic instability, and the plight of the common man. Here are a few key takeaways:

  • The government needs to address the issue of human rights violations by the Indian army and take proactive steps to ensure the safety and security of the citizens.
  • The government needs to focus on boosting the state's economy by restoring peace and stability in the region.
  • The government needs to address the concerns of the common man and work towards finding a solution that benefits all.
