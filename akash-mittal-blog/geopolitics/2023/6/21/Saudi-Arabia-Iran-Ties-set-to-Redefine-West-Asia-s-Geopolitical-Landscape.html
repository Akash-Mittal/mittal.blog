Saudi Arabia-Iran Ties set to Redefine West Asia's Geopolitical Landscape



Image Credits https://www.deccanherald.com/international/world-news-politics/saudi-arabia-iran-ties-set-to-redefine-west-asia-s-geopolitical-landscape-1226718.html

Once there was a border shared by two powerful nations, Saudi Arabia and Iran. However, over the years, their relations have been tumultuous, often marked by tension and animosity. Nevertheless, in recent times, there has been a turn of events that suggest a shift in their dynamic. It seems that they have resolved to redefine the geopolitical landscape of West Asia.

Examples of recent developments:

  • In 2018, Saudi Crown Prince Mohammad Bin Salman said that he believed that Iran's supreme leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, was "the new Hitler of the Middle East."
  • In 2019, the US accused Iran of orchestrating a drone attack on Saudi oil facilities, causing a major disruption in global oil supply. Saudi Arabia, in response, launched a military campaign against Iran-backed Houthi militants in Yemen.
  • In 2021, Iran's foreign minister, Javad Zarif, extended a hand of friendship towards Saudi Arabia, offering to negotiate a "long-term framework for cooperation" to resolve regional conflicts.
  • In May 2021, the two countries held secret talks in Baghdad, brokered by Iraq's prime minister, Mustafa al-Kadhimi, to find a way to reduce tensions.

Case studies of the impact on the region:

The shift in Saudi Arabia-Iran relations is likely to have far-reaching implications for the geopolitics of West Asia. Here are some of the most significant:

  • Impact on Regional Conflicts: The two nations have backed opposing sides in the conflicts in Yemen, Iraq, Syria, Lebanon, and Bahrain. If they were to find common ground, it could lead to a resolution of these conflicts.
  • Impact on Oil Prices: The tensions between Saudi Arabia and Iran have often led to volatility in global oil markets. If they were to cooperate, it could stabilise oil prices and lead to greater economic growth in the region.
  • Impact on Geostrategic Alliances: Saudi Arabia has long been aligned with the US, while Iran has been aligned with Russia. If the two nations were to come together, it could shift the balance of power in the region.

Conclusion:

The recent developments in Saudi Arabia-Iran relations are significant and could have a profound impact on the geopolitics of West Asia. If they manage to resolve their differences and work together, it could lead to greater stability, prosperity, and peace in the region.

  • Regional conflicts could be resolved.
  • Oil prices could stabilise.
  • Geostrategic alliances could shift.
