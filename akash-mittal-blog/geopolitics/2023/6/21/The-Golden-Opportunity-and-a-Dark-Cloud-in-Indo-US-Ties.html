The Golden Opportunity and a Dark Cloud in Indo-US Ties



Image Credits https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/the-golden-opportunity-and-a-dark-cloud-in-indo-us-ties/articleshow/101114053.cms

Once upon a time, there were two countries - India and the United States - with a shared vision for a peaceful, prosperous, and democratic world. They had a long history of cooperation in various fields, such as trade, defense, education, and culture, and a strong desire to deepen their strategic partnership to tackle global challenges together.

However, despite the golden opportunity that lies ahead, there is also a dark cloud hovering over the Indo-US ties, threatening to disrupt the harmony and trust between the two nations. Let's explore some examples and case studies to understand both sides of the story.

The Golden Opportunity

India and the United States have many reasons to strengthen their friendship and mutual interests. Here are some compelling arguments:

  • Economic growth: Both countries have large and growing economies, with vast potential for trade and investment. According to the U.S.-India Strategic Partnership Forum, bilateral trade in goods and services between India and the U.S. reached $149 billion in 2019, making the U.S. India's second-largest bilateral trade partner after China. Furthermore, the U.S. is the largest source of foreign direct investment (FDI) in India and the Indian diaspora in the U.S. contributes to both countries' economies.
  • Regional stability: India and the U.S. share a common interest in maintaining peace and stability in Asia, which is home to several hotspots of conflict and geopolitical competition. Risks from China's increasing assertiveness in the South China Sea and on the border with India, North Korea's nuclear and missile threats, and terrorism emanating from Afghanistan and Pakistan require closer coordination and cooperation between India and the U.S. At the same time, both countries also support freedom of navigation, peaceful resolution of disputes, and respect for international law and norms.
  • Mutual values: India and the U.S. share many core values, such as democracy, human rights, rule of law, and pluralism. As the world becomes more polarized and illiberal, the Indo-US partnership can become a beacon of hope and inspiration for those who believe in these values. By working together, they can advance their common interests and address global challenges, such as climate change, health pandemics, cyber threats, and space cooperation.

A Dark Cloud of Challenges

Despite these opportunities and advantages, the Indo-US relationship faces several challenges that need to be addressed to move forward. Here are some examples:

  • Trade barriers: Despite the high potential for bilateral trade, both countries face significant trade barriers that hinder their economic growth and competitiveness. Some of these include tariffs, non-tariff measures, intellectual property rights, data localization, and market access. India's protectionist policies in some sectors, such as e-commerce, agriculture, and medical devices, have also been a bone of contention.
  • Defense ties: Although India and the U.S. have made significant progress in their defense cooperation in recent years, some issues remain unresolved or contested. For example, India's purchase of Russian S-400 air defense system and its reluctance to sign the Communications Compatibility and Security Agreement (COMCASA) have raised concerns about interoperability and technology transfer. Moreover, the U.S.'s focus on Pakistan's role in Afghanistan and its ties with China have complicated the U.S.-India defense partnership.
  • Geopolitical alignment: India and the U.S. share common concerns about China's rise, but they also have divergent interests and priorities in the Indo-Pacific region and beyond. For example, India is wary of the U.S.'s unilateralism, protectionism, and military interventions, and seeks to maintain its strategic autonomy and multipolarity. The U.S., on the other hand, wants India to be a key part of its Indo-Pacific strategy, which aims to counter China's assertiveness and expand its influence in the region.

Case Studies

Let's examine some recent case studies that illustrate both the opportunities and challenges in the Indo-US ties:

Opportunities: COVID-19 and Climate Change

The COVID-19 pandemic has brought India and the U.S. closer in their fight against the deadly virus. The two countries have collaborated on various fronts, such as vaccine research, development, and distribution, medical supplies, technology, and knowledge sharing. India has also supplied hydroxychloroquine and other drugs to the U.S. and other countries in need, despite the domestic demand.

Climate change is another area where India and the U.S. can work together to reduce global emissions and mitigate the impact of the warming planet. The U.S. has rejoined the Paris Agreement on climate change, which India has ratified, and both countries have committed to net-zero emissions by mid-century. India has also launched several initiatives, such as the International Solar Alliance and the Coalition for Disaster Resilient Infrastructure, which can benefit from U.S. expertise and support.

Challenges: Social Media and Human Rights

The recent controversy over social media platforms' regulation and freedom of speech has highlighted the divergent views of India and the U.S. While the U.S. has criticized India's new IT rules, which seek to regulate social media platforms, India has argued that it is necessary to protect its sovereignty, security, and democratic values. The issue has also raised concerns about privacy, censorship, and accountability.

Human rights is another sensitive issue that has strained the Indo-US ties in recent times. The U.S. has expressed concerns about India's treatment of religious minorities, particularly Muslims, and its crackdown on civil society and free press. The recent farmer protests in India have also drawn international attention and criticism, with some U.S. lawmakers and celebrities supporting the cause.

Conclusion: A Way Forward

The Indo-US relationship is a complex and dynamic one, with many opportunities and challenges. To maximize the former and mitigate the latter, both countries need to engage in constructive dialogue, trust-building, and compromise. Here are some actionable steps:

  • Strengthen bilateral trade and investment by removing barriers, promoting innovation and diversification, and setting ambitious targets for growth.
  • Enhance defense cooperation by deepening interoperability, sharing technology and intelligence, and addressing mutual concerns and interests.
  • Pursue strategic alignment in the Indo-Pacific and beyond by respecting each other's priorities, interests, and sensitivities, and avoiding zero-sum thinking and confrontational rhetoric.
  • Promote mutual values and people-to-people ties by supporting democracy, human rights, pluralism, education, culture, and innovation.
  • Address challenges and differences through constructive dialogue and engagement, rather than coercion or imposition.
