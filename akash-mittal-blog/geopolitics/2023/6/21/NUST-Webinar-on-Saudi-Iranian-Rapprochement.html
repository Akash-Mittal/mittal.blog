NUST Think Tank Holds Webinar on 'Saudi-Iranian Rapprochement'



Image Credits https://dailytimes.com.pk/1102697/nust-think-tank-holds-webinar-on-saudi-iranian-rapprochement/

The recent webinar organized by the National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST) think tank on 'Saudi-Iranian Rapprochement' highlighted how the countries of Saudi Arabia and Iran have recently taken steps towards improving their relations. The discussion aimed to bring together experts and scholars to explore the possibility of a breakthrough in their bilateral ties and the impact it could have on regional dynamics in the Middle East.

Why Is This Rapprochement Significant?

The relationship between Saudi Arabia and Iran has been strained for several decades, with both countries competing for influence in the region in various ways. However, the geopolitical landscape of the Middle East has been changing rapidly, and both countries have come to recognize the limitations of their confrontational policies. The pressures of the Covid-19 pandemic, unprecedented economic challenges, and the shifting political balances in the region have led to a change in the approach of both countries towards each other.

  1. Cultural Ties - Despite their political disagreements, the population of Saudi Arabia and Iran share a rich cultural heritage and history. The people-to-people ties between the two countries have been sustained even through periods of political hostility. The resumption of cultural exchanges, particularly in the form of Hajj and Umrah pilgrimage, as well as literary and artistic events, can be seen as an important indication of the potential for improved bilateral relations.
  2. Economic Cooperation - The two countries possess significant natural resources, and their economies are complementary. The lifting of sanctions on Iran via 2015 nuclear deal, which was building on international consensus, has opened up new opportunities for economic cooperation.The recent Saudi outreach towards non-oil exports and tourism and the Tehran's push for westwards trade all further their shared economic interests. Economic cooperation can serve as a stabilizing factor in their relations and provide impetus for regional trade and development.
  3. Regional Stability - As two of the most significant players in the Middle East, Saudi Arabia and Iran's disputes have fueled conflicts in Iraq, Syria, and Yemen, and the various militant groups operating in the region. Steps towards reducing tensions between the two countries can help in achieving much-needed regional stability, especially in Syria and Yemen.

Case Studies: Attempts at Reconciliation and Cooperation

While Saudi-Iranian relations have been marked by unresolved conflicts, there have been some significant attempts at reconciliation and cooperation in the past. These case studies underscore the potential for future breakthroughs in their relations.

1. The 2004 Riyadh Agreement:

The agreement was signed between the leaders of Saudi Arabia and Iran in an attempt to de-escalate regional tensions and form a common approach to regional issues. This agreement led to stronger trade ties and economic cooperation between the two countries. However, the benefits of the agreement were short-lived, and both countries eventually returned to their adversarial posturing.

2. The Iran Nuclear Deal:

The Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) was signed by Iran, Saudi Arabia, and other global powers in 2015 to lift economic sanctions on Iran in exchange for limiting its nuclear program. The lifting of sanctions opened up the Iranian market to investment, strengthening opportunities for economic cooperation between Iran and the rest of the world. Despite political disagreements on other issues, the implementation of the deal represented a significant willingness by both countries to engage in diplomacy and find common ground.

3. The Doha peace talks:

In September 2020, representatives of the Taliban and the Afghan government met for peace negotiations in Doha, Qatar, which were facilitated by a number of countries, including Saudi Arabia and Iran. The two regional powers have committed to supporting the peace process in Afghanistan, with the hope that it will lead to a more stable region as a whole.

The Prospects for Sustained Rapprochement

The prospects for a sustained rapprochement between Saudi Arabia and Iran are contingent on several factors.

  1. Political Will: The Saudi-Iranian relationship is hampered by deep-seated mistrust and fundamental ideological differences, however the political will of their respective leaderships is critical for any meaningful progress.
  2. Regional Dynamics: The shifting regional dynamics will influence the trajectory of Saudi-Iranian rapprochement, with the roles of external powers such as the US, China, and Russia having a significant impact on their interactions.
  3. Peaceful Resolution to Regional Conflicts: The resolution of long-standing regional conflicts such as the Yemeni civil war, Syria's future, and the Israel-Palestine conflict would create an environment meant for cooperation for both countries

The resumption of talks, cultural exchanges, and potential economic cooperation merely serves as a crucial first step, and it remains to be seen whether these initial positive signs ultimately lead to sustained reconciliation between Saudi Arabia and Iran. Nonetheless, it is hoped that these gestures and talks will go some way towards stabilizing the Middle East and enabling a more peaceful and prosperous future for the region.
