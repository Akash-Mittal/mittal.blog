Germany Calls China a Growing Threat as it Steps up Focus on Security

Interesting Story:

Once upon a time, there was a small country called Germany which had great concerns about the rise of another powerful nation, China. Germany noticed China's growing influence in the global market, especially in the tech industry, and saw it as a threat to their national security.

Examples of Germany's Concerns about China's Growing Threat

Germany has been increasingly vocal about its concerns regarding China's role in global affairs. Here are some examples:

  • Germany's foreign ministry issued a policy paper in 2020, identifying China as a "systemic rival". The paper highlights China's human rights abuses, lack of transparency, and aggressive foreign policy.
  • German intelligence officials have also warned about the risks of Chinese espionage and the theft of intellectual property. Hence, the German government has recently barred Huawei, a Chinese tech company, from participating in the country's 5G wireless network due to security concerns.
  • Germany is also raising concerns about China's Belt and Road Initiative, which is a massive infrastructure project aimed at boosting Chinese economic influence across Asia and beyond. Germany believes that China is trying to create dependencies in other countries, and this may threaten Europe's stability.

Case Studies: How China's Actions are Influencing Germany's Security

China's growing threat has had a significant impact on Germany's security. Here are some case studies:

  • Cybersecurity: In 2015, the German parliament was hacked, and some documents were leaked to the public. German authorities suspected that Chinese hackers might be behind the attack. Since then, Germany has been investing heavily in cybersecurity to prevent future attacks.
  • Industrial Espionage: There have been several instances of Chinese espionage in German companies. In 2019, Chinese intelligence officers were accused of stealing information from a German engineering company and a university in Aachen. The incident led to the expulsion of two Chinese diplomats from Germany.
  • Economic Dependence: China is Germany's biggest trading partner, and Germany worries that being too dependent on China's economy will make them vulnerable to pressure from Beijing. Germany fears that China may use its economic power to influence German policies and decisions.

Conclusions: What Germany is Doing to Counter China's Growing Threat

Germany has taken several measures to counter China's growing threat:

  1. Increasing Cooperation with Allies: Germany is working closely with its allies like the US, Japan, and Australia to counter China's aggressive foreign policy. Recently, Germany has been enhancing its military cooperation with Japan, which shares similar concerns about China's rise.
  2. Investment in Critical Infrastructure: To counter China's Belt and Road Initiative, Germany has announced a new program called "Global Infrastructure Investment Alliance", which aims to support countries to develop their infrastructure sustainably. Germany believes that this initiative will offer an alternative to China's predatory practices.
  3. Investing in R&D: To reduce their dependence on Chinese tech, Germany is investing heavily in research and development of new technologies. Germany wants to become a global leader in innovation, especially in areas related to AI, quantum computing, and cybersecurity.

At the end of the day, Germany knows they can't stop China's rise, but they want to ensure that it happens within the framework of international law and respect for human rights.
