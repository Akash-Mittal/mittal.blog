Bangladesh Leads on HDI Parameters Compared to Most Countries

An Inspiring Journey

Bangladesh, a country known for its poverty and disasters, has made a remarkable transformation in the last decade. Despite facing challenges such as low literacy rates, limited resources, and political instability, the country has forged its way ahead in various administrative, economic, and social spheres. One of the most significant advancements is the country's progress in the Human Development Index (HDI) parameters, which indicate the living standards, education, and income of its citizens.

Back in the 1970s, Bangladesh was among the world's poorest countries, with a low HDI score of 0.328. However, the country has come a long way since then, with a current HDI rating of 0.632, according to the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) report in 2020.

Examples of Bangladesh's Progress in HDI Parameters

Here are some of the key parameters where Bangladesh has shown remarkable progress compared to other developing countries like India:

  • Life Expectancy: Bangladesh's life expectancy has increased from 44 years in 1970 to 72.6 years in 2020, even surpassing India's life expectancy rate of 69.7.
  • Education: Bangladesh has made strides in improving literacy rates, with a literacy rate of 74.4% in 2020, compared to India's 77.7% literacy rate.
  • Gross National Income (GNI): Bangladesh has seen a 170% increase in GNI per capita, from $1,062 in 2010 to $1,824 in 2020.

Case studies of Bangladesh's Success

Some of the factors that have contributed to Bangladesh's success are:

  • Investment in Healthcare: Bangladesh's government has focused on improving healthcare infrastructure to reduce infant mortality rates. The government has implemented various social programs to provide better nutrition, health, and sanitation facilities to the people. This effort has resulted in a decrease in infant mortality rates from 76 per 1,000 live births in 1990 to 22 per 1,000 live births in 2020, a rate lower than India's 29 per 1,000 live births.
  • Women's Empowerment: The country has made significant advances in women's empowerment, with a female participation rate in the workforce of 36.6% in 2020 compared to India's rate of 21.2%. This has helped to reduce poverty levels in families, improve their standard of living, and increase their contribution to the country's Gross Domestic Product (GDP).
  • Rural Development: The government has established policies to promote rural development by investing in infrastructure like education, transportation, and communication. This has reduced the gap between rural and urban areas, which has positively impacted the HDI score.

Conclusion

Bangladesh's progress in the HDI parameters is indeed remarkable and inspiring. The country's focus on issues like healthcare, education, and women's empowerment has resulted in positive changes. Positive changes in HDI rates not only signify the better living conditions of citizens but also the progress of the country's economy and its international identity. However, there is still a long road ahead for Bangladesh, as it continues to battle poverty, inequality, and political instability. But this journey shows that with the right strategies, a developing country can pave its way towards development and progress.

Main Takeaways:

  1. Bangladesh has made significant progress on HDI parameters over the years, surpassing most countries, including India.
  2. The country's focus on healthcare, women's empowerment, and rural development has helped to improve life expectancy, increase literacy rates, and reduce poverty levels.
  3. The government needs to continue its efforts towards improving living conditions and reduce poverty, as the country still has a long path to establish its position as a developed nation.
