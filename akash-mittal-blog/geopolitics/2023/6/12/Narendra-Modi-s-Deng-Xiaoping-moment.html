Narendra Modi's Deng Xiaoping moment



Image Credits https://indianexpress.com/article/opinion/columns/c-raja-mohan-jake-sullivan-india-visit-pm-modi-us-8658900/

In 1978, China's then-leader, Deng Xiaoping, embarked on a series of economic reforms that would transform his country from an agrarian economy to the world's second-largest economy today. It was a bold move that opened up the country to foreign investment and technology, and made China the manufacturing hub of the world.

Today, the Indian Prime Minister, Narendra Modi, has the opportunity to follow in Deng Xiaoping's footsteps and seize the geoeconomic possibilities opened up by Joe Biden's policies and the global churn.

Biden's Policies

Joe Biden's policies have created a unique opportunity for India to increase its global footprint and become a leading player in geopolitics. By announcing an ambitious infrastructure plan and a shift towards clean energy, Biden has created a framework that India can use to increase its presence in the global economy.

For example, India has the potential to become a major player in the production of electric vehicles and renewable energy. This could help India reduce its reliance on fossil fuels, reduce its carbon footprint, and create new industries that can fuel economic growth.

Case Studies

To understand the potential of India's geoeconomic possibilities, let's look at a few case studies:

  • Indo-US relations: As per C Raja Mohan, Jake Sullivan's recent visit to India is a sign that Indo-US relations are entering a new era of cooperation. India and the US can cooperate to develop infrastructure in the Indo-Pacific region, which can counter China's influence in the region.
  • Quad Alliance: The Quad Alliance between India, the US, Japan, and Australia can be a force to reckon with in the region. The alliance can work towards developing infrastructure projects, provide Covid-19 vaccines, and counter China's aggression in the region.
  • Pharmaceutical industry: India has emerged as the pharmacy of the world during the Covid-19 pandemic. India has supplied Covid-19 vaccines to over 95 countries. This has helped India increase its soft power and influence in the region.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Narendra Modi has the opportunity to follow in Deng Xiaoping's footsteps and seize the geoeconomic possibilities opened up by Joe Biden's policies and the global churn. India can become a major player in geopolitics, increase its soft power, and reduce its reliance on fossil fuels. However, to achieve this, India needs to work towards developing infrastructure, increasing its manufacturing capabilities, and investing in renewable energy.
