History of India vs Pakistan Cricket Rivalry

The history of India vs Pakistan cricket rivalry stretches back to 1952, when Pakistan played its first international match against India in Delhi. Since then, the two teams have played each other in a total of 199 matches, with Pakistan winning 86 and India winning 70. The remaining 43 matches have ended in ties or no results. The intensity of the rivalry between the two teams is unmatched in cricket, and their matches are often described as high-stakes, high-pressure affairs.

Case Studies of India vs Pakistan Matches

There have been countless memorable matches between India and Pakistan, but here are three that stand out:

  1. 1992 World Cup: The 1992 World Cup match between India and Pakistan in Sydney was a historic moment for cricket. It was the first time the two teams had played against each other in a World Cup, and the match was watched by an estimated audience of one billion people. Pakistan won the match by 43 runs, but the real winner was cricket as a whole, which had been given a new lease of life by the riveting encounter.
  2. 2007 T20 World Cup Final: The 2007 T20 World Cup final between India and Pakistan in Johannesburg is widely regarded as one of the greatest matches in cricket history. Batting first, India posted a modest total of 157/5, but their bowlers, particularly Joginder Sharma, who took the last wicket, held their nerve to restrict Pakistan to 152/7 and win the match by 5 runs.
  3. 2011 World Cup Semi-Final: The 2011 World Cup semi-final between India and Pakistan in Mohali was a high-stakes affair, with the winner advancing to the final. India batted first and posted a challenging total of 260/9, thanks to a brilliant century by Sachin Tendulkar. Pakistan fought hard in their reply, but ultimately fell short, losing the match by 29 runs.
