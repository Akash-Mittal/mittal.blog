Why Honduras cutting ties with Taiwan and opening embassy in China matters?



Image Credits https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2023/6/11/honduras-opens-embassy-in-china-after-cutting-ties-with-taiwan

It was a historic moment for Honduras when it opened its embassy in China in August 2019. The move marked the end of the Central American nation's 57-year diplomatic ties with Taiwan as it joined several other countries in establishing formal relations with China.

The decision by Honduras to side with China instead of Taiwan is not just a political move, it has several significant socio-economic implications for the country. Let us delve deeper and explore why Honduras cutting ties with Taiwan and opening embassy in China matters.

The increasing influence of China in Latin America

China is the world's second-largest economy and is the largest trading partner of several Latin American countries. In recent years, China has been increasing its influence in Latin America through its economic diplomacy, whereby it has been investing heavily in the region's infrastructure, mining, and energy sectors.

In fact, China has invested over $165 billion in Latin America in just over a decade, making it the region's second-largest foreign investor after the United States.

Furthermore, China is not just investing in Latin America, it has also been offering development aid, technical cooperation, and debt relief to several countries in the region, including Honduras.

The decision by Honduras to open its embassy in China and cut ties with Taiwan is, therefore, a significant step towards strengthening its relationship with China, which is likely to result in more investment and development aid for the country.

Case study: El Salvador's experience

El Salvador, a Central American nation that broke ties with Taiwan in 2018 to establish diplomatic relations with China, has already witnessed significant socio-economic benefits as a result of its decision.

China has since invested heavily in El Salvador's infrastructure, including the construction of a new stadium in the capital, San Salvador, and the expansion of its airport. Furthermore, China has also offered assistance in the areas of education, health, and technology.

However, the country's decision has not been without its drawbacks. The United States, a long-time ally of El Salvador, has expressed its displeasure with the country's move to establish relations with China, which has strained its relationship with the US.

Taiwan's declining influence in the region

Taiwan, on the other hand, is a small island nation off the coast of China that has been struggling to maintain its international diplomatic recognition in the face of China's growing influence.

In recent years, several countries, including Burkina Faso, the Dominican Republic, and Panama, have severed diplomatic ties with Taiwan to establish formal relations with China.

As a result, Taiwan's international recognition has been declining, which has hampered its ability to participate in international organizations and hindered its diplomatic efforts to promote its interests globally.

Furthermore, as China becomes more influential in the region, Taiwan's capacity to provide development aid and technical assistance to countries like Honduras is likely to diminish.

Case study: Burkina Faso's experience

Burkina Faso, a landlocked African nation, cut ties with Taiwan in 2018 and established relations with China. The move was driven by Burkina Faso's desire to access more investment and development aid from China.

Since then, China has invested heavily in Burkina Faso's infrastructure, including the construction of a new parliament building and several new roads. Furthermore, China has also offered assistance in the areas of agriculture, health, and education, which has had a significant positive impact on the country's socio-economic development.

The geopolitical implications of the decision

The decision by Honduras to open its embassy in China and cut ties with Taiwan also has significant geopolitical implications for the region.

China continues to assert its dominance in the South China Sea, which has resulted in territorial disputes with several countries in the region, including Taiwan. The decision by Honduras to side with China in this dispute is likely to have implications for its relationship with countries like the United States, which has been a vocal opponent of China's actions in the South China Sea.

Furthermore, China's influence in the region has also resulted in concerns over debt-trap diplomacy, whereby China offers loans to countries with unsustainable repayment terms, resulting in these countries falling into a debt trap.

Case study: Sri Lanka's experience

Sri Lanka, a small island nation in South Asia, has been grappling with the consequences of debt-trap diplomacy after China loaned the country billions of dollars for infrastructure development.

However, Sri Lanka was unable to repay its debt, resulting in it surrendering control of a Chinese-built port to China on a 99-year lease. This move has raised concerns over China's intentions in the region and has led to accusations of Chinese neo-colonialism.

Conclusion

The decision by Honduras to open its embassy in China and cut ties with Taiwan is not just a political decision, it has significant socio-economic and geopolitical implications for the region. While the move is likely to result in increased investment and development aid from China, it is also likely to strain Honduras' relationship with the United States. Furthermore, Taiwan's declining international recognition and China's growing influence in the region has raised concerns over debt-trap diplomacy and China's territorial ambitions in the South China Sea.
