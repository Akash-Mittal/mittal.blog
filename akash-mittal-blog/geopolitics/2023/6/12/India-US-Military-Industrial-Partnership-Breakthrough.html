India-US Military-Industrial Partnership Breakthrough



Image Credits https://www.indianarrative.com/opinion-news/are-india-and-us-poised-for-a-breakthrough-in-military-industrial-partnership-145098.html

In 1998, India conducted a series of nuclear tests which led to strained relations with the US that imposed various sanctions on India. However, since then, both countries have made significant efforts to improve their relationship, and one area where progress has been made is the military-industrial partnership.

India, being one of the largest arms importer in the world, has been seeking to reduce its reliance on foreign-made weapons and equipment. With a growing economy and a rising middle class, India has been increasing its defense spending to modernize its armed forces. Meanwhile, the US, being one of the largest arms exporters, has been seeking to expand its defense sales to India as part of its strategy to counter China.

The entire paradigm of India-United States cooperation is headed for quantitative and qualitative transformation as both countries are poised for a breakthrough in military-industrial partnership.

Examples with Case Studies

  • Defense Technology and Trade Initiative (DTTI) - In 2012, India and the US agreed to launch the DTTI to co-develop and co-produce defense equipment. Under this initiative, several joint working groups were established to identify and prioritize specific projects. The first project selected for cooperation was the Raven unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV). The two countries also signed an agreement to develop the next-generation protective gear for soldiers, known as the Integrated Personal Protective System (IPPS).
  • Apache and Chinook Helicopters - In 2013, India signed a $2.5 billion contract with the US for the purchase of ten Apache attack helicopters and fifteen Chinook heavy-lift helicopters. The Apache is one of the most advanced attack helicopters in the world, while the Chinook is a versatile workhorse used for troop transport, logistics, and medical evacuation.
  • Indian Navy and US Navy - In 2016, the US and Indian navies conducted the Malabar exercise, an annual joint naval exercise. This year, the exercise included Japan as well, making it a trilateral exercise. The exercise focused on the maritime security of the Indo-Pacific region and included a wide range of activities such as anti-submarine warfare, air defense, and counter-piracy operations. The exercise demonstrated the growing maritime cooperation between the three countries.

  1. The India-US military-industrial partnership is set for a major transformation as both countries seek to expand cooperation on defense technology and trade.
  2. The Defense Technology and Trade Initiative (DTTI) provides a framework for co-development and co-production of defense equipment, which could lead to significant technological advancements for both countries.
  3. The growing partnership between India and the US, as well as with other countries such as Japan, reflects the importance of the Indo-Pacific region in the global balance of power.
The India-US partnership on defence is one of the most promising aspects of the India-US strategic relationship. It is based on the concept of co-production, co-development of technology, research and development, which will help us in our quest for modernisation and self-reliance. - Narendra Modi, Prime Minister of India

Overall, the India-US military-industrial partnership has the potential to revolutionize the defense industry and bring about significant advancements in defense technology. It could also deepen the strategic ties between the two countries and contribute to the stability and security of the Indo-Pacific region.
