India: A Strategic Partner or an Unreliable Friend?



Image Credits https://moderndiplomacy.eu/2023/06/09/india-a-strategic-partner-or-an-unreliable-friend/

The United States has long had a strategic alliance with India. In containing China's expanding influence in the region, the two nations have been working together to promote economic growth, maintain regional security, and advance political stability.

However, recent events have caused some to question whether India can truly be considered a reliable partner. For example, India's decision to pull out of the Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership (RCEP) in 2019 was seen by some as a move that was not in line with American interests. Additionally, India's decision to continue to do business with Russia and Iran, despite American sanctions, has raised concerns about the strength of the strategic alliance between the two nations.

Case Studies

One case study that highlights the complexity of the relationship between India and the United States is the issue of Pakistan. While the United States sees Pakistan as a critical ally in its fight against terrorism, India views Pakistan as a major threat to its national security. As a result, the United States and India have often been at odds over how to handle Pakistan.

Another case study is the issue of China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI). While the United States views the BRI as a threat to its global influence, India sees it as a way to expand its economic reach in the region. As a result, the two nations have been at odds over how to handle China's growing economic power.

Conclusion

Despite these challenges, the United States and India remain committed to their strategic alliance. Both nations recognize the importance of working together to maintain regional security and promote economic growth. While there may be disagreements on specific issues, the long-term benefits of the alliance outweigh any short-term challenges.

In order to ensure the continued success of the strategic alliance, it is important for both nations to continue to communicate openly and honestly with one another. By working together to address challenges and find common ground, the United States and India can continue to build a strong and lasting partnership.
