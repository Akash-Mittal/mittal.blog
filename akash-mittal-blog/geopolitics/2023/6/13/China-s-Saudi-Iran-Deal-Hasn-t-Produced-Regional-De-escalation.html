China's Saudi-Iran Deal Hasn't Produced Regional De-escalation



Image Credits https://foreignpolicy.com/2023/06/12/saudi-iran-rapprochement-normalization-deal-china-de-escalation-yemen-syria/

The ongoing conflict between Saudi Arabia and Iran has been one of the defining features of the Middle East for the past few years. With both countries vying for regional dominance and supporting opposite sides in the numerous conflicts throughout the region, tensions have been high and violence has been frequent. However, in recent months, there has been talk of a possible breakthrough in the conflict, with China playing a leading role in brokering a deal between the two rival nations. Unfortunately, so far, this deal has failed to produce the desired de-escalation in the region.

Case Studies

One of the main reasons why the China-brokered deal between Saudi Arabia and Iran has failed to produce regional de-escalation is the fact that the underlying issues that have caused the conflict in the first place have not been addressed.

  • Yemen Conflict: The conflict in Yemen is one of the key battlegrounds in the Saudi-Iran rivalry. Saudi Arabia supports the internationally recognized government of Yemen, while Iran backs the Houthi rebels. Despite the China-brokered deal, the conflict in Yemen has continued unabated, with both sides continuing to support their respective proxies.
  • Lebanon Crisis: The recent political crisis in Lebanon, which saw the Prime Minister resign and Hezbollah gain more power, has been largely attributed to the Saudi-Iran rivalry. Despite the China-brokered deal, the situation in Lebanon remains tense and there is a risk of further escalation.
  • Syrian Conflict: The conflict in Syria has been one of the deadliest and most complex in the region, with both Saudi Arabia and Iran backing different sides. While the China-brokered deal may have led to a slight reduction in violence, the conflict is far from over, with both sides continuing to support their proxies.

Conclusion

While the China-brokered deal between Saudi Arabia and Iran was seen as a potential game-changer in the region, it has so far failed to produce the desired results. The underlying issues that have caused the conflict in the first place have not been addressed, and both sides continue to support their respective proxies in the various conflicts throughout the region. As a result, the risk of further escalation and violence remains high.

In conclusion, the China-brokered deal between Saudi Arabia and Iran has not produced regional de-escalation, and it is unlikely to do so unless the underlying issues are properly addressed.
