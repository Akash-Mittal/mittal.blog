China Asks Lone Indian Journalist to Leave Amid Diplomatic Tensions



Image Credits https://www.wionews.com/india-news/china-tells-lone-indian-journalist-to-leave-as-diplomatic-tensions-soar-603405

Diplomatic tensions between China and India have reached a new high after Beijing officials asked a journalist from the Press Trust of India (PTI) to leave the country earlier this month. This recent development comes after India expelled two Chinese journalists in 2018. The move has sparked concerns about press freedom in China and the rising tensions between the two countries.

The Story: PTI Correspondent Expulsion from China

The PTI correspondent was asked to leave China earlier this month, citing people familiar with the matter. The reasons for his expulsion are currently unclear. However, the incident comes amid escalating tensions between China and India over a series of border disputes.

According to reports, the Indian government is yet to make any comments on the matter. The move, however, has sparked concerns about press freedom in China. China is infamous for suppressing independent media outlets, and the recent incident has only added to these fears.

Case Studies: China's Press Freedom Violations

1. Detention of Journalists

The Chinese government is notorious for detaining journalists who speak out against its policies. Reporters Without Borders, a global organization that advocates for press freedom, has ranked China 177 out of 180 countries in its 2020 World Press Freedom Index. According to the organization, Chinese authorities have arrested 117 journalists in the past decade.

2. Cyber Attacks on Media Outlets

China has also been accused of hacking into several media outlets to monitor their content and prevent its citizens from accessing uncensored news. In 2015, Chinese hackers allegedly attacked the New York Times and stole the personal data of its employees. Similarly, Chinese hackers have also targeted the Voice of America and Radio Free Asia in the past.

3. Censorship of Social Media and News Websites

China's "Great Firewall of China" blocks access to several international news websites, including the New York Times and the BBC. In addition, China's communist party monitors social media platforms and messaging apps like WeChat for dissenting voices. The country has also been accused of using artificial intelligence systems to censor online content.

Conclusion: Crackdown on Press Freedom in China

The Chinese government's move to expel an Indian journalist is a worrying sign of the country's growing crackdown on press freedom. The incident also highlights the tense relationship between China and India over border disputes. The international community should take note of China's press freedom violations and work towards holding the country accountable for its actions.
