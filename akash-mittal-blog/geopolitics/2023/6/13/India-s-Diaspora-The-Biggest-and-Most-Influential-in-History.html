How Indian expats are making their mark on the global stage.

As a young girl growing up in the United States, Indra Nooyi had big dreams. She wanted to become a business leader, and she wanted to make her mark on the world. But in the 1970s, there weren't many Indian women in corporate America. Nooyi knew she would have to work twice as hard to succeed.

Despite this challenge, Nooyi persevered. She went to Yale University and earned a degree in public and private management. She landed a job at strategy consulting firm Boston Consulting Group, and then moved on to work for several large corporations, including Motorola and ABB. But her biggest achievement came in 2006, when she was appointed CEO of PepsiCo, becoming the first woman of color to lead a Fortune 500 company.

Examples of Indian Diaspora in Action

Nooyi's story is just one example of how Indian expats are making their mark on the global stage. In fact, India's diaspora is now bigger and more influential than any in history. Here are just a few more examples of Indians who have made it big abroad:

Adobe - Shantanu Narayen

Shantanu Narayen is the CEO of Adobe, one of the world's most successful tech companies. He was born in Hyderabad, India, and later moved to the United States to study engineering at the University of California, Berkeley. After graduation, he worked for Apple and Silicon Graphics before joining Adobe in 1998. Under his leadership, Adobe has grown into a global powerhouse, with more than 21,000 employees and over $12 billion in annual revenue.

Britain - Priti Patel

Priti Patel is a British politician who currently serves as the Home Secretary of the United Kingdom. She was born in London to Ugandan Indian parents and grew up in a working-class family. Before entering politics, Patel worked in public relations and lobbying. She was first elected to parliament in 2010, and has since served in various ministerial roles, including Secretary of State for International Development. In 2019, she was appointed Home Secretary by Prime Minister Boris Johnson.

Chanel - Anand Ahuja

Anand Ahuja is a successful entrepreneur and the husband of Bollywood actress Sonam Kapoor. He was born in Delhi, India, and later moved to the United States to study at the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania. After graduation, he founded a streetwear brand called Bhane, which has since become one of India's most popular fashion labels. In 2017, he launched a sneaker store called VegNonVeg in Delhi, and has since gone on to invest in several other startups. Most recently, he was appointed as a board member of luxury fashion brand Chanel.

Case Studies

It's not just high-profile CEOs and politicians who are making their mark abroad. Indian expats are succeeding in a wide range of industries and professions. Here are a few more examples:

Medicine - Dr. Vivek Murthy

Dr. Vivek Murthy was born in Karnataka, India, and moved to the United States when he was just three years old. He studied at Harvard University and later became a physician, specializing in internal medicine and infectious diseases. In 2014, he was appointed as the 19th Surgeon General of the United States by President Barack Obama. During his tenure, he focused on public health issues such as mental health, addiction, and obesity.

Engineering - Sundar Pichai

Sundar Pichai is the CEO of Google, one of the world's largest tech companies. He was born in Tamil Nadu, India, and later moved to the United States to study at Stanford University. After graduation, he worked at McKinsey & Company and then joined Google in 2004. Since then, he has held several executive positions at the company, including leading the development of the popular Google Chrome web browser.

Entertainment - Mindy Kaling

Mindy Kaling is an actress, writer, and producer who has appeared in numerous films and TV shows, including "The Office," "The Mindy Project," and "Ocean's 8." She was born in Massachusetts to Indian parents, and has spoken extensively about the challenges she faced growing up as an Indian-American in a predominantly white community. Despite these hurdles, she has become one of Hollywood's most influential comedians, and has inspired countless young South Asian women to pursue careers in the arts.

Conclusion

India's diaspora is larger and more influential than ever before, and Indians are making their mark in virtually every country and industry around the world. From CEOs to doctors, politicians to entertainers, Indian expats are achieving great things and contributing to the global community in countless ways. As India continues to grow and prosper, we can expect to see even more Indians succeeding on the world stage.
