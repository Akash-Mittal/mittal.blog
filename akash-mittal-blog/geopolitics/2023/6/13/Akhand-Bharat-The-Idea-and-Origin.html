Akhand Bharat: The Idea and Origin



Image Credits https://www.newslaundry.com/2023/06/12/explained-what-is-the-idea-and-origin-of-akhand-bharat

The concept of Akhand Bharat, meaning "Undivided India," has been around for decades and has been a source of debate and controversy in the South Asian subcontinent. At its core, the idea of Akhand Bharat seeks to unify the Indian subcontinent under one flag, with India as its leader, and return the region to its pre-colonial boundaries.

The Historical Context

The concept of Akhand Bharat has its roots in ancient Hindu texts and mythology. It was believed that the Indian subcontinent was once a united landmass that extended from Afghanistan to Myanmar, with the Himalayas as its northern border. This idea of a united India persisted throughout history, with various empires and dynasties seeking to assert their dominance over the entire subcontinent.

However, it wasn't until the arrival of European colonial powers in the 16th century that the subcontinent was officially divided into different territories. The British, in particular, played a significant role in carving up the Indian subcontinent based on religious and ethnic lines, resulting in the creation of modern-day India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and other smaller countries.

The Idea of Akhand Bharat

The idea of Akhand Bharat seeks to reverse the British-imposed divisions and unite the subcontinent under one flag. Its proponents argue that the division of India was artificial and has led to ongoing tensions and conflicts within the region. They believe that a united India would be a more potent force on the global stage and would better reflect the cultural and historical unity of the subcontinent.

However, the concept of Akhand Bharat is not without its critics. Many argue that it is a Hindu nationalist agenda and seeks to marginalize minority groups within the subcontinent, particularly Muslims. They also point out that the idea of a united India ignores the ethnic and linguistic diversity of the region and could lead to further tensions and conflicts.

Case Studies

One country where the concept of Akhand Bharat has gained considerable political traction is Nepal. In 2015, Nepal adopted a new constitution that many Indians viewed as discriminatory towards the Madhesi people, who have cultural ties to India. In response, some Indian politicians called for India to reclaim Nepal as part of Akhand Bharat, arguing that it was India's duty to protect the Madhesi people.

Similarly, the ongoing conflict in Kashmir has also been linked to the idea of Akhand Bharat. Some Indian politicians and activists argue that Kashmir should be part of a united India and that its division is an artificial and temporary measure. This viewpoint is not shared by many Kashmiris, who consider themselves a distinct ethnic and linguistic group and do not want to be part of India at all.

Conclusion

The concept of Akhand Bharat is a complex and contentious issue that has divided opinions across the South Asian subcontinent. While its supporters argue that it represents unity and a return to a pre-colonial way of life, its critics believe that it is a Hindu nationalist agenda that seeks to marginalize minority groups and ignores the diversity of the region. Ultimately, the fate of the Indian subcontinent will be determined by its people, who must navigate the challenges of living in a region with such rich and diverse cultural and historical heritage.
