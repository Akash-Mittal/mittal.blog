Global Technological Standards and Geopolitics: A Hudson Institute Event



Image Credits https://www.ntd.com/global-technological-standards-and-geopolitics-a-hudson-institute-event_924903.html

Have you ever wondered how the products you use everyday, like smartphones, cars, and even medical equipment, are able to work seamlessly across different countries and regions around the world?

The answer lies in technological standards, or agreed upon specifications and protocols that ensure interoperability between different products and systems. These standards are developed and maintained by international organizations like the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE).

However, the development and implementation of these standards are not without geopolitical implications. In a discussion held by Hudson Institute on June 12, experts on trade, manufacturing, economics, and foreign policy came together to discuss the impact of global technological standards and geopolitics on these fields.

Case Studies

  • 5G Technology

    "Whoever leads in 5G will rule the world," said a senior US official, reflecting the high-stakes race between the US and China to develop and implement 5G technology, which promises faster data transfer, reduced latency, and greater reliability.

    However, the two countries have different approaches to 5G. While the US is pushing for an open, market-based approach that emphasizes security and privacy, China is pursuing a state-led approach that places less emphasis on these concerns.

    The differences in approach have put pressure on other countries to choose which side to align with, with implications for trade and foreign policy. For example, the US has banned the use of Chinese 5G equipment over national security concerns, while some European countries have expressed willingness to work with both US and Chinese companies.

  • Artificial Intelligence

    "AI will be one of the most important things humanity will ever work on," said Google CEO Sundar Pichai, highlighting the importance of this technology in shaping the future.

    However, the development and implementation of AI are also subject to geopolitical concerns, especially in the areas of ethics and regulation. For example, the US, China, and the EU have different approaches to regulating AI, with the US emphasizing a light-touch approach, China adopting a more permissive approach, and the EU taking a more cautious approach.

    The development of AI also has implications for trade and manufacturing, as countries work to establish themselves as leaders in the field. For example, China has made AI a key part of its Made in China 2025 initiative, while the US has launched the American AI Initiative to maintain its leadership in the field.

  • Data Privacy and Security

    "I actually think most people don't want privacy. They want privacy in the areas they want it," said Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg, highlighting the tensions between privacy and convenience in the digital age.

    The issue of data privacy and security has become increasingly prominent in recent years, with major data breaches and controversies involving companies like Facebook and Cambridge Analytica.

    The development of technological standards around data privacy and security has important implications for trade, as companies seek to comply with different regulations around the world. For example, the EU's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) has had a significant impact on how companies handle data, with companies facing hefty fines for non-compliance.

Conclusion

The development and implementation of global technological standards may seem like a technical issue, but it has far-reaching implications for trade, manufacturing, economics, and foreign policy. As countries and companies compete to establish themselves as leaders in emerging technologies like 5G and AI, the geopolitical implications of technological standards will become increasingly important.

  1. The race to establish technological standards will shape the competitive landscape of emerging industries, with winners and losers determined by their ability to influence these standards.
  2. The role of government in establishing and regulating technological standards will become increasingly important, with geopolitical tensions playing a key role in shaping these policies.
  3. The development and implementation of technological standards around issues like privacy and security will have major implications for consumer trust and confidence, as well as the competitiveness of companies that operate in different regions around the world.
