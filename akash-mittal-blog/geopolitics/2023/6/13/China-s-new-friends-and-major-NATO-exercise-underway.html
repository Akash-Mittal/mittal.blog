As China continues to expand its global reach, the country is finding new friends in unexpected places. At the 10th Arab-China Business Conference in Riyadh, 30 investment agreements worth a total of $10 billion were signed on day one. This is just one example of China's growing economic influence around the world.

The rise of China

Over the past few years, China has become an increasingly important player on the global stage. Its economy is now the second-largest in the world, and it has made significant investments in infrastructure projects around the globe. China has even launched its own development bank, the Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank, which currently has over 100 member countries.

China's growing influence has not gone unnoticed. The country has faced criticism from some quarters, particularly in the West, for its approach to human rights and its economic practices. However, many countries are eager to partner with China, attracted by the promise of investment and economic growth.

Case studies: China's new friends

Pakistan

Pakistan is one country that has embraced China's overtures. China has invested heavily in Pakistan's infrastructure, particularly in its Gwadar Port. This investment is part of China's ambitious Belt and Road Initiative, which seeks to build infrastructure and increase trade between Asia, Europe, and Africa.

China's investment in Pakistan has not been without controversy. Some local residents have raised concerns about the environmental impact of the projects, while others worry about the long-term implications of growing economic dependence on China.

Uganda

Uganda is another country where China has made significant investments. In 2018, China announced plans to build a new airport in Uganda's capital, Kampala, at a cost of $1.4 billion. This project is part of China's efforts to expand its presence in Africa and establish new trade routes.

The airport project has been warmly welcomed by the Ugandan government, which sees it as a key driver of economic growth. However, some Ugandans have expressed concerns about the project's cost and the potential for corruption.

Conclusion: The pros and cons of partnering with China

China's growing economic influence presents both opportunities and challenges for countries around the world. On the one hand, Chinese investment can help to spur economic growth and create new jobs. On the other hand, there are concerns about the environmental and social impact of some of these projects, as well as the potential for economic dependence on China.

Ultimately, each country must weigh the benefits and risks of partnering with China. As China continues to expand its global reach, it will be interesting to see how this dynamic plays out.
