China Alleges Discrimination Against Journalists in India



Image Credits https://www.theweek.in/news/world/2023/06/12/china-accuses-india-of-discriminatory-treatment-against-its-journalists.html

India and China have been engaged in a diplomatic spat over the treatment of journalists in each other's country. China alleges that Indian authorities have been discriminatory towards Chinese journalists, causing them inconvenience and hindering their work. In response, Beijing has asked Indian journalists working in China to leave once their visas expire.

Discrimination Against Chinese Journalists

Chinese journalists working in India have reported instances of discrimination and harassment by Indian authorities. In some cases, they have been denied access to events and interviews, forced to wait for extended periods for clearance, and even had their equipment confiscated. Some have even been physically assaulted.

Case Study: Wang Xiang

Wang Xiang, a journalist with China's state-run Xinhua News Agency, was covering the Indian general elections in 2019 when he was allegedly assaulted by a group of men. Xiang said that he was filming a polling booth in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh when he was attacked. He suffered injuries to his head and back and required medical attention.

Retaliation by Beijing

In response to what it sees as discrimination against its journalists in India, Beijing has reportedly asked Indian journalists working in China to leave the country once their visas expire. This move is seen as a tit-for-tat retaliation against India's treatment of Chinese journalists.

Impact on Media Freedom

The diplomatic spat between India and China over the treatment of journalists has raised concerns about media freedom in both countries. Journalists play a crucial role in keeping people informed and holding those in power accountable. Any attempts to constrain their work through unjustified restrictions or physical violence are a threat to press freedom.

Conclusion

The allegations of discrimination against Chinese journalists in India and the retaliatory move by Beijing against Indian journalists in China have further strained the already-tense bilateral relations between the two countries. However, it is important to remember that journalists are not pawns in a geopolitical game. They have a right to work without fear of discrimination or violence and their freedom should be protected at all costs.
