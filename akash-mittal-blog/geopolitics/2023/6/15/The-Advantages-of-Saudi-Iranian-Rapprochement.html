The Advantages of Saudi-Iranian Rapprochement

Once upon a time, there were two powerful countries in the Middle East: Saudi Arabia and Iran. They were constantly at each other's throats, competing for influence and resources in the region. But then something remarkable happened.

It was a hot summer day in Riyadh, the capital of Saudi Arabia. The new king, Salman, had just ascended to the throne and was eager to make a name for himself. He knew that the animosity between his country and Iran was not only dangerous but also holding back the development of the region.

So he picked up the phone and called the Iranian president, Hassan Rouhani. To everyone's surprise, Rouhani picked up the phone and agreed to a meeting. That meeting would change the course of history.

The Benefits of Rapprochement

There are many benefits to a Saudi-Iranian rapprochement, or a reconciliation of hostile parties. Here are just a few:

  • Peace: The most obvious benefit of any rapprochement is peace. The hostility between Saudi Arabia and Iran has resulted in proxy wars, terrorist attacks, and instability in the region. By reconciling their differences, the two countries could put an end to the bloodshed and begin to build a more stable future.
  • Cooperation: Saudi Arabia and Iran are both major players in the energy industry, with vast reserves of oil and gas. By working together, they could overcome market volatility and ensure stable supplies for their own populations and the world at large.
  • International Influence: A Saudi-Iranian rapprochement could have a ripple effect throughout the region and the world. It could serve as an example for other countries in conflict, showing that diplomacy and compromise are viable options for resolving disputes.

Case Studies

There have been many successful rapprochements in history, including:

  • Germany and France: After centuries of warfare, Germany and France signed the Élysée Treaty in 1963, which established close cooperation between the two countries in economics, culture, and defense. Today, Germany and France are at the core of the European Union.
  • U.S. and Cuba: After more than 50 years of enmity, the United States and Cuba restored diplomatic ties in 2015. Since then, there has been increased trade and tourism between the two countries, as well as greater cultural exchange.
  • Eritrea and Ethiopia: After a brutal border war in the late 1990s, Eritrea and Ethiopia agreed to a peace deal in 2018. The two countries have since reopened borders and resumed flights between their capitals.

Conclusion

A Saudi-Iranian rapprochement is not impossible. It may require painful compromises and concessions, but the benefits are worth it. By ending their hostility, Saudi Arabia and Iran could become leaders in regional stability and international diplomacy.

To summarize, the advantages of a Saudi-Iranian rapprochement are:

  • Peace
  • Cooperation
  • International Influence
