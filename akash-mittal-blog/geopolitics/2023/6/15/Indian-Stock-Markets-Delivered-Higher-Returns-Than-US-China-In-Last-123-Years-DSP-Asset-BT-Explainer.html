Story

Imagine investing your money in the stock market 123 years ago, in 1898. You have three choices: India, the United States, and China. Which market would you choose?

According to a recent analysis by financial firm DSP Asset Management, the Indian stock market would have provided the highest returns over the last century compared to the other two.

This analysis is based on data from 1898 to 2020, which includes the colonial era, independence, and modern times. It shows that the Indian stock market outperformed the US and China by a significant margin, with an average annual growth rate of 6.5% compared to 5.5% and 5.1% for the US and China, respectively.

Analysis

The higher returns in the Indian stock market can be attributed to several factors:

  1. Economic Growth: India has seen significant economic growth over the last few decades, with a population of over 1 billion and steady GDP growth. This growth has led to a rise in disposable income, which has driven demand for securities and led to higher stock prices.
  2. Political Stability: Despite some periods of instability, India has generally maintained a stable government since gaining independence in 1947. This political stability has helped to create an environment conducive to economic growth and investment.
  3. Demographics: India has a large and growing workforce, which makes it an attractive market for businesses. As the population grows and becomes more affluent, this trend is likely to continue.

Case Studies

Let's take a look at some specific examples of Indian stocks that have delivered impressive returns over the years:

  1. Tata Consultancy Services (TCS): TCS is a multinational IT services company based in India. It went public in 2004 and has seen incredible growth since then, with its stock price increasing by over 2400% as of 2021.
  2. Reliance Industries: Reliance Industries is a conglomerate that operates in several industries, including petrochemicals, refining, and telecommunications. Its founder, Dhirubhai Ambani, started the company with a small amount of capital in the 1960s and built it into one of the largest companies in India. Its stock price has increased by over 800% since 2001.
  3. HDFC Bank: HDFC Bank is one of the largest private sector banks in India. It went public in 1995 and has since seen its stock price increase by over 6000%.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Indian stock market has delivered higher returns than the US and China over the last 123 years. This can be attributed to factors such as economic growth, political stability, and demographics.

Investors can take advantage of these trends by investing in Indian stocks and funds. However, as with any investment, it is important to do thorough research and consider the risks involved before making a decision.
