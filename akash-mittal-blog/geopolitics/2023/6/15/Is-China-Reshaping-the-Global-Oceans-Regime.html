Is China Reshaping the Global Oceans Regime?



Image Credits https://foreignpolicy.com/2023/06/10/china-sea-south-east-maritime-claims-law-oceans-us-disputes/

Imagine you are a fisherman who has been fishing in the South China Sea for generations. Your family's livelihood depends on the catch you bring home every day. One day, you notice that a fleet of fishing boats flying the Chinese flag has appeared in your fishing grounds. They are using large trawlers that scoop up everything in sight, destroying the coral reefs and sea grass beds that are essential to the ecosystem. You are left with almost nothing to catch.

This scenario is not fiction. It is a reality for many fishermen in Southeast Asia whose fishing grounds are being encroached upon by Chinese fishing fleets. This is just one example of how China's growing influence in the oceans regime is causing concerns in the international community.

China's Quest for Maritime Dominance

China's rise as a global superpower has been accompanied by its quest for maritime dominance. The country's ambitious Belt and Road Initiative involves expanding its maritime influence in the Indian Ocean and beyond. This has raised concerns in India and other countries that see China's maritime expansion as a threat to their own national security.

China has also been expanding its military presence in the South China Sea, where it has territorial disputes with several Southeast Asian countries. The country has built military bases on artificial islands and has been using its coast guard and fishing fleets to assert its claims.

China's Approach to International Law

China's growing influence in the oceans regime is not just about military and economic power. It is also about the country's approach to international law. China has been accused of ignoring international law and rejecting the decisions of international tribunals.

For example, in 2016, the Permanent Court of Arbitration in The Hague ruled that China's claims to historic rights and maritime entitlements in the South China Sea were not compatible with the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea. China rejected the ruling and refused to abide by it.

Consequences for the International Community

China's actions in the oceans regime have consequences for the international community. Here are three examples:

  1. Maritime Security: China's expansion of its military presence and use of coast guard and fishing fleets to assert its claims increase the risk of conflict in the region.

  2. Sea Lanes: The South China Sea is a major sea lane that connects East Asia to the rest of the world. China's assertiveness in the region could disrupt the flow of goods and increase the cost of shipping.

  3. Environmental Protection: China's fishing fleets have been accused of engaging in Illegal, Unreported and Unregulated (IUU) fishing, which depletes fish stocks and harms the marine ecosystem. This has led to conflicts with other countries and raised concerns about global food security.

Conclusion

China's growing influence in the oceans regime is a complex issue that requires a multifaceted solution. However, here are three points that can guide the international community:

  1. The importance of following international law: The international community needs to uphold the rule of law and encourage China to recognize the decisions of international tribunals.

  2. The need for dialogue: Diplomatic dialogue is key to resolving disputes and reducing the risk of conflict in the region. Dialogue should include issues related to maritime security, territorial disputes, and environmental protection.

  3. The importance of regional cooperation: Countries in the region need to work together on issues related to maritime security, environmental protection, and economic cooperation. This can be achieved through regional organizations such as ASEAN and the Indian Ocean Rim Association.

Imagine a world where the vast oceans are ruled by a single superpower. This is the reality that we are currently facing as China continues to expand its influence on the global oceans regime. With its rapidly growing economy and military, China has become a major player in the international sphere, and its impact on the world's oceans is undeniable.

The Expansive Reach of China

China is the world's largest consumer of seafood, and its fishing industry has been rapidly expanding in recent years. This has led to disputes with neighboring countries over fishing rights, with China often accused of overfishing in international waters. The country has also been involved in territorial disputes in the South China Sea, where it has built artificial islands and claimed exclusive economic zones.

China is also investing heavily in its navy, with plans to build a powerful blue-water fleet that can operate globally. This includes the construction of aircraft carriers and other advanced warships, which will enable China to project its military power far beyond its borders. This has raised concerns among other countries in the region, particularly those with competing territorial claims in the South China Sea.

Case Studies

South China Sea Dispute

China's claims to various islands, reefs, and rocks in the South China Sea have been a source of tension with its neighbors, particularly Vietnam and the Philippines. China has built artificial islands and installed military facilities on them, raising concerns about its intentions in the region. The United States has also become involved in the dispute, conducting freedom of navigation operations to challenge China's claims.

Fishing Disputes

China's fishing industry has come under fire for its practices in international waters, particularly in areas where fish populations are dwindling. In 2019, Ecuador seized a Chinese fishing vessel operating within its exclusive economic zone, and later caught several more Chinese boats engaging in illegal fishing. In response, China has dispatched its coast guard to protect its fishing fleets, leading to further tensions with neighboring countries.

Conclusion

  1. China's expanding influence on the global oceans regime is a cause for concern for many countries, particularly those in the Asia-Pacific region.
  2. The country's aggressive territorial claims and overfishing practices have led to disputes with neighboring countries, and its investment in a blue-water navy has raised alarm among other maritime powers.
  3. In order to maintain peace and stability in the world's oceans, it is crucial that all countries abide by international law and work together to find solutions to these complex issues.
