The Rise of Africa: Key to Geopolitical Rebalancing



Image Credits https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/india/for-india-the-rise-of-africa-is-key-to-geopolitical-rebalancing-s-jaishankar/articleshow/100999770.cms

It was the summer of 2017 when I first visited Africa for a business conference. I was amazed at the energy and potential of the continent. People were enthusiastic about their future and opportunities were endless. At the same time, I couldn't help but notice the lack of connectivity and access to basic infrastructure. This is when I realized that Africa is the future of the world and its rise is critical for global geopolitical rebalancing.

India is one of the key players in Africa's development. With India's Look Africa policy, the country has been actively involved in various sectors like agriculture, healthcare, education, and technology.

Examples of India's partnership with Africa:

  • The India-Africa Institute of Agriculture and Rural Development (IAIARD) was established in Malawi to share India's best practices in agriculture and rural development.
  • Mahatma Gandhi Institute of Education for Peace and Sustainable Development in Mauritius promotes Gandhian philosophy and teachings in education and development.
  • India's telemedicine and e-learning network, e-VidyaBharati, has been implemented in 10 African countries to provide access to education and healthcare.

Case Study: India's Partnership with Rwanda

Rwanda has seen remarkable progress in the last decade. From being a nation devastated by genocide to becoming one of Africa's fastest-growing economies, Rwanda's success story is inspiring. India has been a key partner in Rwanda's development journey.

India has provided over $400 million in lines of credit to Rwanda for various development projects like power generation, construction of a convention center, and a special economic zone. Rwanda was also one of the countries selected for the Pan-Africa e-Network project, which is India's flagship project in the field of telemedicine and e-learning.

India and Rwanda have also signed agreements in the fields of defense, ICT, and agriculture. These agreements have paved the way for closer economic and political ties between the two nations.

Conclusion

  1. Africa's rise is essential for global geopolitical rebalancing.
  2. India's partnership with Africa is crucial to achieve this goal.
  3. India's actions in Rwanda show how effective partnerships can lead to mutual growth and development.
