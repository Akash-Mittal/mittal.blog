Rahul Dravid

Former Indian Cricketer

Rahul Dravid, also known as 'The Wall', is considered one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket. He scored over 24,000 runs in international cricket and was known for his unmatched technique and solid temperament.

Shoaib Akhtar

Former Pakistan Cricketer

Shoaib Akhtar, also known as the 'Rawalpindi Express', was one of the fastest bowlers in the history of cricket. He terrorized batsmen with his blistering pace and picked up over 400 wickets in international cricket.

WTC Final

India vs New Zealand

The World Test Championship Final between India and New Zealand is currently underway at the Rose Bowl in Southampton, England. Both teams are vying for the title of the best Test team in the world.

Strong Criticism From Shoaib Akhtar



Image Credits https://sports.ndtv.com/cricket/as-a-coach-rahul-dravid-is-zero-ex-pakistan-stars-strong-take-on-ex-india-captain-4110315

Former Pakistan cricketer Shoaib Akhtar has come out with a scathing attack on Rahul Dravid, who is currently the head coach of the Indian team. Speaking to a news channel, Akhtar said, "As a coach, Rahul Dravid is zero. He has been a great cricketer but coaching is a different ball game."

Decision Making in the WTC Final



Image Credits https://sports.ndtv.com/cricket/as-a-coach-rahul-dravid-is-zero-ex-pakistan-stars-strong-take-on-ex-india-captain-4110315

Akhtar's comments come in the wake of India's poor showing in the ongoing World Test Championship Final against New Zealand. He criticized the Indian team management for their decision making and said that Dravid had failed as a coach to prepare the team for such an important match.

Case Study - India's Batting Collapse

India won the toss and elected to bat first in the WTC Final, but their decision backfired as the team was bowled out for just 217 runs. The top order batsmen failed to apply themselves and were dismissed cheaply, putting immense pressure on the middle order. The team was eventually bowled out in just 92.1 overs.

Akhtar pointed out that Dravid should have prepared his batsmen better for the conditions in England and said that the team management had made the wrong decision to bat first on a pitch that was showing signs of movement. He also criticized India's middle order for failing to read the conditions and adjust their game accordingly.

Case Study - Team Selection

Akhtar also criticized the Indian team management for their selection decisions. He questioned the decision to play both Ravindra Jadeja and Ravichandran Ashwin in the playing XI, saying that it was a wrong move to play two spinners in English conditions. He also said that picking Ishant Sharma ahead of Mohammed Siraj was a mistake and that the team lacked the firepower to take wickets consistently.

Case Study - Over-Reliance on Virat Kohli and Cheteshwar Pujara

Akhtar pointed out that India's over-reliance on Virat Kohli and Cheteshwar Pujara had been exposed in the WTC Final. He said that the team lacked depth in their batting lineup and that the other batsmen had failed to perform when it mattered the most. He also criticized the Indian bowlers for their lackluster performance in the match.

Conclusion
