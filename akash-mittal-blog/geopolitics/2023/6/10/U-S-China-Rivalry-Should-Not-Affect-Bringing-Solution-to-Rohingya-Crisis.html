U.S.-China Rivalry Should Not Affect Bringing Solution to Rohingya Crisis



Image Credits https://intpolicydigest.org/the-platform/u-s-china-rivalry-should-not-affect-bringing-solution-to-rohingya-crisis/

Once upon a time, in a small village in Myanmar, there lived a group of Rohingya Muslims who were constantly persecuted by the Myanmar military. They were forced to flee their homes and take shelter in neighboring countries. There are countless stories like this in the current Rohingya crisis, where millions of people are living in refugee camps due to ethnic cleansing.

The international community has been trying to solve the crisis for years, but the rivalry between the U.S. and China has been hindering the efforts. Both countries have their own agenda, and they are using the Rohingya crisis as a pawn in their power struggle.

U.S. and China in the Rohingya Crisis

The United States has been actively involved in advocating for the rights of the Rohingya people. In 2017, when the crisis was at its peak, the U.S. imposed sanctions on Myanmar's military leaders and halted military aid. The U.S. has also been pushing for an international inquiry into the atrocities committed against the Rohingya people.

On the other hand, China has been supporting the Myanmar government and has opposed any intervention from the international community. China has invested heavily in Myanmar's infrastructure projects, and the Rohingya crisis is seen as a disturbance to their plans.

Why solving the Rohingya crisis is important

The Rohingya crisis is not just a humanitarian crisis, but it also has political and economic implications. The Rohingya people have been deprived of basic human rights, including citizenship and the right to education and healthcare. This has led to a loss of potential manpower and human capital for Myanmar and neighboring countries.

The crisis has also impacted the political stability of the region. It has led to a rise in extremism and insurgency, which can threaten regional and international security. With the influence of China and the U.S. in the region, there is a risk that the crisis can escalate into a larger conflict.

The way forward

The Rohingya crisis needs a unified effort from the international community. While the U.S. and China may have their own interests, they need to put aside their rivalry and work towards a common goal of bringing justice and peace to the Rohingya people.

Firstly, the United Nations needs to play a more active role in the crisis. The UN should initiate an international investigation into the crimes committed against the Rohingya people and hold the perpetrators accountable. The UN should also provide humanitarian aid to the refugees and work towards a sustainable solution for their resettlement.

Secondly, neighboring countries need to play a more significant role in the solution. Countries like Bangladesh, India, and Thailand have been hosting millions of refugees and need to work together towards a solution that provides the Rohingya people with basic rights and dignity.

Lastly, the U.S. and China need to recognize the impact of their rivalry and resolve their differences through dialogue and cooperation. The Rohingya crisis should not be a victim of their power struggle. Instead, they should work together to support a sustainable solution for the people affected by the crisis.
