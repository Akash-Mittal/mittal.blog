India's Message to China: Violation of UNCLOS Damages Trust Immensely

Introduction

India and China have had a long-standing border dispute, which has recently escalated due to China's violation of the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS). The UNCLOS is an international treaty that defines the legal rights and responsibilities of countries with respect to their use of the world's oceans, establishing guidelines for businesses, the environment, and the management of marine natural resources. China's expansionist policies in the South China Sea have been a major source of contention between India and China, and have caused immense damage to the trust between the two countries.

The UNCLOS violation by China is not only an issue of territorial rights but also a matter of maintaining the global order of the seas. China's behavior could potentially impact other countries' access to the seas, resulting in economic and environmental consequences.

Examples of UNCLOS Violation by China

China has been expanding its territorial claims in the South China Sea by building artificial islands and placing military bases on them. This is in direct violation of the UNCLOS, which states that artificial islands, installations, and structures do not possess the status of islands and have no territorial sea of their own. The Permanent Court of Arbitration, which hears disputes related to the UNCLOS, ruled in 2016 that China's claims to the South China Sea were invalid under international law.

The UNCLOS also states that ships have the right to sail freely through the territorial seas of other countries. However, China has been attempting to restrict the movement of ships through the South China Sea, claiming that it is their territorial waters. This has caused friction with other countries, including India, which conduct trade through the sea routes in the region.

Another example of China's violation of the UNCLOS is its exploitation of marine resources. China has been overfishing in the South China Sea, with some estimates suggesting that up to 80% of the fish stocks in the region have been depleted. This not only harms the environment but also impacts the livelihood of local fishermen who depend on the marine resources for their survival.

Case Studies

India and China have been engaging in a border dispute for several decades now, with both sides accusing the other of violating their respective territorial rights. In June 2020, a violent clash erupted between the two armies in the Galwan Valley, resulting in the deaths of 20 Indian soldiers and an unknown number of Chinese soldiers. The incident has caused a major strain in the relationship between the two countries.

In another case, an Indian navy ship conducting a routine patrol in the South China Sea was recently chased away by Chinese vessels claiming that it was in their territorial waters. India maintains that it has the right to conduct patrols in the region, as it is important for their national security and economic interests.

The Philippines, which has a territorial dispute with China in the South China Sea, filed a case against China in the Permanent Court of Arbitration in 2013. In 2016, the court ruled in favor of the Philippines, invalidating China's claims to the South China Sea. Despite the ruling, China has continued to expand its territorial claims in the region.

Conclusion

The violation of the UNCLOS by China is a matter of great concern for the global community. It not only affects India's territorial rights but also impacts the global order of the seas. The violation damages the trust between two powerful nations that could lead to war and uncertainty in the region.

The UNCLOS provides a framework of rules and guidelines for the use and protection of marine natural resources that can help prevent conflict between nations. Therefore, it is important for countries to abide by these rules and regulations to maintain peace and security in the region.

Key Points

  • China's violation of the UNCLOS damages the trust between India and China.
  • The violation impacts the global order of the seas.
  • Abiding by the UNCLOS rules and guidelines helps maintain peace and security in the region.
