The Plight of Indian Fishermen in Pakistani Custody

On 30th December 2021, Pakistan handed over 200 Indian fishermen to India at the Attari-Wagah Border. These fishermen were arrested at various times in the past few years and had been languishing in Pakistani jails. While this news may seem like a piece of great relief for the fishermen and their families, it puts a spotlight on the plight of the Indian fishermen who are often arrested by the Pakistani authorities.

From a Fishing Net to a Prison Cell:

It's a common sight to see fishermen out at sea, throwing their nets in the water, hoping for a good catch. However, for many Indian fishermen, this could be the start of a nightmare. According to reports, every year, Pakistan maritime forces arrest hundreds of Indian fishermen who accidentally cross the maritime boundary between India and Pakistan while fishing.

In 2019 alone, Pakistan arrested more than 380 Indian fishermen and seized their boats, according to the Indian media. A majority of these fishermen are hailing from coastal cities like Gujarat and Maharashtra and are the sole earning members of their families. Their arrests not only put them through a harrowing ordeal but also leave their families in a state of shock and despair.

Plight of Indian Fishermen:

Indian fishermen face several problems while out at sea. They have to contend with rough weather, unpredictable currents, lack of modern equipment, and a low catch. Despite all this, they are willing to take the risk for their livelihoods. However, what they did not anticipate was the possibility of getting arrested for straying into Pakistani waters.

Once arrested, they find themselves in a legal limbo. There are several cases of fishermen being held hostage for several years, waiting for their cases to be heard. They are often denied access to legal aid, and their families have no idea where they are. In many cases, fishermen are forced to pay hefty fines or endure years of imprisonment.

Case Studies:

One such case is that of Umar Sheikh. Umar was arrested by the Pakistani authorities in 2017 for straying into Pakistani waters while fishing. He was sentenced to three years of imprisonment and was not allowed to contact his family. After a long legal battle, he was finally released in 2020. His family had lost contact with him, and they only realized that he was back home when he called them from the Indian Embassy in Pakistan.

Another case is that of Bhupen Chauhan. Bhupen, a fisherman from Gujarat, was arrested by the Pakistani authorities in 2018 and was sentenced to two years of imprisonment. He was released in 2020 and finally reunited with his family. However, the trauma of his imprisonment had left him a broken man. He lost his fishing boat, which was his only source of income, and now he struggles to make ends meet.

The Humanitarian Angle:

The plight of these fishermen highlights the humanitarian angle that needs to be considered. Beyond the politics of the India-Pakistan relationship, it's the innocent fishermen who are being caught in the crossfire. At the heart of this issue is the need for collaboration between the two countries to ensure the safety and welfare of these fishermen.

A possible solution could be a treaty between India and Pakistan, defining the maritime boundaries and giving access to fishermen to both the countries. There could also be a mechanism in place for the prompt release of the arrested fishermen, taking into account their humanitarian concerns. Such a treaty could go a long way in ensuring the welfare of the fishermen and their families and reduce the strain on the already stressed bilateral relationship between India and Pakistan.

In Conclusion:

The recent release of 200 Indian fishermen is a step in the right direction. However, it does not address the root causes of the issue. The plight of these fishermen and their families must be considered with a humanitarian perspective. India and Pakistan must work together to ensure the safety and welfare of the fishermen, who are often the forgotten victims of the turbulent India-Pakistan relationship.
