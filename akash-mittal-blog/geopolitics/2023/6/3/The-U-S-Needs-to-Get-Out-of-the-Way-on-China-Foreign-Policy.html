The U.S. Needs to Get Out of the Way on China Foreign Policy

Once upon a time, in the 1800's, China was one of the world's most powerful nations. But after a series of tragic events, China's power diminished significantly. Fast forward to the 21st century, China is now one of the biggest global economic powers. The U.S. has been threatened by China's economic and military rise and has taken a strong stance to contain China's influence. However, the U.S. needs to rethink its foreign policy approach to China and start cooperating for a better future.

Case Study: Huawei and 5G Networks

The U.S. government has been pressuring countries to ban Chinese telecom company Huawei from their networks due to security concerns. However, the U.S. has not presented any concrete evidence to support its claims. The U.K. initially agreed to work with Huawei on building their 5G networks, but reversed their decision after the U.S. pressured them. This decision has cost the U.K. billions of dollars and put their country at a disadvantage in the race for 5G networks.

Case Study: The COVID-19 Pandemic

The world has been hit hard by the COVID-19 pandemic, and China was the first country to be affected. However, China has managed to effectively control the spread of the virus and is now offering assistance to countries in need. The U.S., on the other hand, has failed to control the spread of the virus and has cut funding to the World Health Organization (WHO) â€“ a decision that has been widely criticized. Despite this, China has increased funding to the WHO and has provided medical supplies to over 130 countries. China has also offered assistance to the U.S., but the U.S. has rejected their help.

Conclusion

The U.S. needs to change its approach towards China and start cooperating for a better future. The world is facing numerous challenges that require collective efforts to overcome, and treating China as an enemy will only lead to more conflicts. The U.S. should focus on building constructive relationships with China to benefit from its economic and technological advancements. The U.S. also needs to recognize China's global role and work with it to address pressing issues like climate change, nuclear non-proliferation, and global health. The U.S. should stop trying to contain China and start engaging in diplomatic dialogue.
