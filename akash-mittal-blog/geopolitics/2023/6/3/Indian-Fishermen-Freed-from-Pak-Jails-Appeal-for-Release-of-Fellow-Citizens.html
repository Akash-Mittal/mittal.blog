Indian Fishermen Freed from Pak Jails Appeal for Release of Fellow Citizens

On October 22, 2020, eighteen Indian fishermen who were held in Pakistan's jails for violating the country's territorial waters, were released and sent back to India. They were freed after spending over two years in prison.

Now, these freed fishermen are calling on the Indian government to take steps to secure the release of their fellow citizens who are still behind bars in Pakistan.

The fishermen have cited the example of the Sri Lankan government, which carried out a prisoner swap with the Tamil Tigers in the past to secure the release of their citizens held by the group.

Case Studies

The Indian fishermen's appeal for the release of their fellow citizens is not without reason. Over the years, several Indian fishermen have been apprehended by Pakistani authorities for violating the country's territorial waters. Many of them have been held in jails for months and even years, without adequate legal representation or access to consular officials.

One such case is of seven Indian fishermen who were apprehended by Pakistani authorities in January 2020. They were accused of fishing in Pakistani waters and were charged with espionage.

The fishermen's families and human rights groups have refuted the charges, stating that they were innocent and had strayed into Pakistani waters due to a navigational error.

Similarly, in November 2019, nineteen Indian fishermen were arrested by Pakistani authorities for fishing in the country's territorial waters. They were charged with violation of the country's maritime laws and were sent to jail.

The fishermen's families have been running from pillar to post to secure their release, but to no avail.

Conclusion

The plight of the Indian fishermen who are held in Pakistani jails has been an ongoing issue between the two countries. Over the years, several rounds of talks have been held between the two sides to find a solution to the problem, but so far, nothing concrete has emerged.

The fishermen's appeal for their release is not just a humanitarian issue, but also a matter of bilateral trust and understanding between India and Pakistan. It is high time that the two countries take steps to secure the release of these fishermen and ensure that they are able to return to their families and loved ones.

It is important that the Indian government takes up this matter on a priority basis and carries out negotiations with their Pakistani counterparts to secure the release of the Indian fishermen held in Pakistani jails.
