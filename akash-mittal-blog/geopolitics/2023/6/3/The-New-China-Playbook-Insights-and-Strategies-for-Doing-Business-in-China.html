The New China Playbook: Insights and Strategies for Doing Business in China

Imagine you are a business owner, and you want to expand your business to China. You've heard that China is a huge market, full of opportunities, but you are not sure where to start. You start doing some research and you find a book called The New China Playbook, written by Keyu Jin, a professor of economics at the London School of Economics and Political Science.

Insights and Strategies

The New China Playbook provides insights and strategies for doing business in China. It is a comprehensive guide that covers everything from the economic and political landscape, to cultural and legal considerations, to practical advice on how to navigate the market and build successful partnerships.

Cultural Considerations

One of the most important aspects of doing business in China is understanding the culture. Keyu Jin provides in-depth analysis of Chinese culture, including the importance of guanxi, or personal relationships, in business. She also explains how to navigate the complex hierarchy and etiquette of Chinese business meetings and negotiations.

For example, let's say you are attending a business meeting in China. You arrive on time, dressed in formal business attire, but the person you are meeting with is not there yet. You wait patiently, but after an hour, you start to get antsy. You decide to check your phone, but as soon as you do, the person finally arrives. They are offended that you did not give them your full attention and it could harm your business relationship. Keyu Jin advises to always be patient and courteous, and to never do anything that could be seen as disrespectful to your Chinese counterpart.

Legal Considerations

Another crucial aspect of doing business in China is understanding the legal landscape. China has a complex and ever-changing regulatory environment, and it is crucial to stay up-to-date on the latest laws and regulations. Keyu Jin provides practical advice on how to navigate the legal system, including tips on finding a good lawyer and avoiding common legal pitfalls.

For example, let's say you are starting a joint venture with a Chinese partner. You sign a contract that you think is straightforward and fair, but after a few months, you realize that your partner has been violating the terms of the agreement. You take them to court, but the court system is slow and unpredictable, and you are not sure if you will win. Keyu Jin advises to always do due diligence on your potential partner and to have a clear and enforceable contract that protects your interests.

Case Studies

Throughout The New China Playbook, Keyu Jin provides case studies of successful and unsuccessful businesses in China. These case studies illustrate the practical applications of the insights and strategies she provides, and offer invaluable lessons for anyone looking to do business in China.

Successful Case Study: Starbucks

For example, one of the successful case studies is Starbucks. Despite stiff competition from local coffee shops, Starbucks has become one of the most successful foreign businesses in China, with over 4,000 stores and a market share of over 50%. Keyu Jin attributes Starbucks' success to their sensitivity to Chinese culture and their willingness to adapt to local tastes and preferences. For example, Starbucks offers a variety of Chinese teas and snacks, and their stores feature Chinese decor, like calligraphy and traditional art.

Unsuccessful Case Study: Uber

On the other hand, one of the unsuccessful case studies is Uber. Despite a strong start in China, Uber ultimately failed to gain a foothold in the market, and eventually sold their Chinese operation to local competitor Didi Chuxing. Keyu Jin attributes Uber's failure to their lack of understanding of Chinese culture and their failure to adapt to local regulations and consumer preferences. For example, Uber's app was not user-friendly for Chinese consumers, and they failed to build strong relationships with local regulators and taxi companies.

Conclusion

Doing business in China can be challenging, but with the right insights and strategies, it can also be highly rewarding. The New China Playbook, by Keyu Jin, offers invaluable advice for anyone looking to do business in China. By understanding the cultural and legal landscape, and by learning from successful and unsuccessful case studies, you can build strong and successful partnerships in China.
