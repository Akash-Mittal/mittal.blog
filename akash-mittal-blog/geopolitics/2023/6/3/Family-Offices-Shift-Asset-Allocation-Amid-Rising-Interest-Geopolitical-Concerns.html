Family Offices Shift Asset Allocation Amid Rising Interest Geopolitical Concerns

A family office is an organization or a group of people that manage the wealth and investments of a high-net-worth family. These offices are typically responsible for handling everything from taxes to investments to legal matters. With enormous amounts of money to manage, it is no wonder that family offices are closely monitoring geopolitical concerns around the world. In recent years, we have seen these offices shift their asset allocation to hedge against risks associated with such concerns.

The Story of the Family Office

Consider the story of the Johnson family. The Johnsons were one of the wealthiest families in the country, with a net worth of over $2 billion. For years, they had relied on a variety of investment strategies, including a heavy allocation to tech stocks and real estate. They had worked with the same financial advisors for decades and trusted them implicitly.

Then, in 2014, the world changed. Russian troops entered Ukraine, and tensions between the United States and Russia began to escalate. Meanwhile, violence in the Middle East was on the rise, and the Ebola outbreak in West Africa was causing concern among investors. The Johnsons began to worry that their investments were too heavily reliant on a few key industries, and that they were not adequately prepared for the geopolitical risks that could arise.

They turned to their financial advisors for guidance, and the advisors suggested that they consider shifting their asset allocation. They recommended investing in gold, which historically has been a safe-haven asset during times of geopolitical turmoil. They also suggested investing in bonds, which tend to be less volatile than stocks and can provide a steady stream of income. Finally, they recommended diversifying their investments across a range of countries and industries, in order to minimize risk.

Case Studies

The Johnsons are not alone in their concerns. Family offices around the world have been taking steps to hedge against geopolitical risks, including those posed by the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic. Here are some examples:

Case Study 1: The Li Family Office

The Li Family Office is based in Hong Kong and manages the wealth of the Li family, one of the wealthiest families in Asia. In recent years, the office has been reducing its exposure to Chinese stocks and investing more heavily in U.S. and European stocks, as well as in gold. The office is also investing in alternative assets, including private equity and real estate.

The move comes as tensions between China and the United States have been rising, due in part to the ongoing trade war and the issues surrounding the Hong Kong protests. The Li Family Office is concerned that these tensions could spill over into other industries and cause a global economic slowdown.

Case Study 2: The Smith Family Office

The Smith Family Office is based in the United States and manages the wealth of the Smith family, which has a net worth of over $1 billion. In recent years, the office has been reducing its exposure to U.S. stocks and investing more heavily in emerging markets, including India and Brazil. The office is also investing in infrastructure projects around the world, such as the construction of highways and airports.

The move comes as the Smith Family Office is concerned about the stability of the U.S. economy, due in part to the ongoing political polarization and the rising national debt. The office believes that emerging markets and infrastructure projects offer greater growth potential and diversification, while also providing a hedge against geopolitical risks.

Conclusion

Family offices are closely monitoring geopolitical concerns around the world, and are taking steps to hedge against risks associated with these concerns. They are diversifying their investments, investing in safe-haven assets such as gold and bonds, and reducing their exposure to industries and countries that are seen as more vulnerable to geopolitical risks. As geopolitical concerns continue to rise, we can expect family offices to continue to be vigilant and proactive in managing their investments.
