The Role of Indian State Governments in Furthering India UAE Ties

India and UAE share a long-standing history of diplomatic, cultural, and economic ties. Over the years, these ties have only grown stronger. As a result, today India and UAE have emerged as key partners in various sectors like trade, investment, tourism, technology, and more.

While the cooperation between the two nations has been supported at the national level, the role played by the Indian state governments in furthering India UAE ties cannot be overlooked. In this article, we will explore the various ways Indian state governments have contributed to strengthening the bilateral relationship between India and UAE.

The Story of Maharashtra's Sister City Agreement

In 2019, Maharashtra Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis signed a sister city agreement with Sharjah, a city in the UAE. The agreement aimed at promoting cultural, economic, and social ties between Mumbai and Sharjah.

The agreement focused on specific areas of collaboration like trade, tourism, and infrastructure. As a result of this agreement, several collaborations like student and employee exchange programs, tourism promotion, and cultural exchanges have taken place between the two cities.

One of the most significant outcomes of this agreement has been the establishment of a Maharashtra Chamber of Commerce office in Sharjah. This has enabled better business ties between the two regions, leading to job creation and economic growth.

Case Study: Andhra Pradesh's Port Development Projects

Andhra Pradesh, one of the fastest-growing states in India, has been working with UAE to develop its ports and improve its maritime infrastructure. In 2017, the state signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Abu Dhabi Ports to develop a greenfield port at Bhavanapadu in Srikakulam district.

The agreement aimed at creating a world-class port facility that would boost economic growth in the state. The port development project is expected to create employment opportunities for the local population and attract large-scale investments in the region.

Additionally, the state has also been working on the development of a deep-water port at Machilipatnam in collaboration with Dubai Ports World. This project is expected to bring significant investments to the state, leading to employment generation and overall economic growth.

Conclusion: The Way Forward

The bilateral relationship between India and UAE has tremendous potential for growth and development. While the national governments of both countries have played a crucial role in strengthening this relationship, the role of Indian state governments cannot be overlooked.

Indian states have been actively working with UAE in various sectors like trade, investment, tourism, and infrastructure. The collaborations between the Indian states and UAE have resulted in job creation, economic growth, and overall development of the region.

Going forward, it is essential that the Indian state governments continue to work closely with UAE to build on the existing ties and explore new opportunities for collaboration. By doing so, India and UAE can unlock their full potential as partners and take their relationship to new heights.
