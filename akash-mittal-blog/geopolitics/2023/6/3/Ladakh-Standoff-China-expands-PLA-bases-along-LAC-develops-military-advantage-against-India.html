Ladakh Standoff: China expands PLA bases along LAC, develops military advantage against India

Once upon a time, there was a dispute between two countries over a piece of land. The two countries, India and China, both claimed the land as their own and tensions rose between the two. This dispute has been ongoing for decades, but it was escalated in 2020 when Chinese troops crossed the Line of Actual Control (LAC) and occupied Indian territory in the Ladakh region.

Since then, the situation has remained tense, with both sides engaging in talks to resolve the issue but no concrete solution has been reached. In the meantime, China has taken advantage of the situation to expand its People's Liberation Army (PLA) bases along the LAC, providing it with a military advantage against India.

Examples of China's Expansion of PLA Bases

China has been steadily expanding its military presence in the border areas near India over the last few years. However, after the Ladakh standoff, it has accelerated its efforts and is actively building new bases and infrastructure. Here are a few examples:

Doklam Plateau

The Doklam plateau is a disputed area between China and Bhutan, but India has strategic interests in it as well. In 2017, Chinese troops tried to construct a road in the area, leading to a standoff between the two countries. After a 73-day standoff, China backed down but has continued to build infrastructure in the area since then. Satellite images show that it has expanded its military presence and built a new PLA base in the Doklam plateau, giving it a strategic advantage over India in that region.

Gilgit-Baltistan

Gilgit-Baltistan is a region in Pakistan-administered Kashmir that is claimed by India. It is strategically important for both countries as it provides access to the Karakoram highway, which connects China with Pakistan. China has been investing heavily in infrastructure development in the region, including building a new airport and upgrading the Karakoram highway. It has also established a military base in the region, reportedly with the help of Pakistan. This gives China a strategic advantage in the region and allows it to monitor Indian military activities in the area.

South Tibet

South Tibet, also known as Arunachal Pradesh, is a disputed region between India and China. China claims the region as its own and has been building military infrastructure in the area for several years. It has built new roads, bridges, and tunnels, and reportedly has a large number of troops stationed in the area. This gives China a significant advantage over India in that region and allows it to quickly mobilize troops and supplies in case of a conflict.

Impact of China's Expansion of PLA Bases

China's expansion of PLA bases along the LAC has several implications for India and the region as a whole. Here are a few:

Military Advantage

By building new bases and infrastructure in the border areas, China has gained a significant military advantage over India. It has the ability to quickly mobilize troops and supplies in case of a conflict and can monitor Indian military activities in the area. This makes it difficult for India to match China's military capabilities in the region.

Strategic Encirclement

China's expansion of military infrastructure in the region has also led to concerns about strategic encirclement. By building bases in Pakistan-administered Kashmir, it has effectively surrounded India from the west. India has also expressed concerns about China's involvement in the Indian Ocean region and fears that it could be used to cut off India's sea lanes of communication.

Diplomatic Pressure

China's expansion of military infrastructure in the region has also put pressure on India diplomatically. India has been trying to build strategic partnerships with other countries in the region, such as Japan and Australia, to counter China's influence. However, China's military expansion in the region has made it difficult for India to build these partnerships and has threatened its strategic interests in the region.

Conclusion

The Ladakh standoff has brought the India-China dispute to the forefront of international attention. While both countries have engaged in talks to resolve the issue, China has taken advantage of the situation to expand its military presence along the LAC. This has provided it with a military advantage over India and has put pressure on India diplomatically. As the two countries continue to engage in talks to resolve the issue, it remains to be seen how the situation will unfold.
