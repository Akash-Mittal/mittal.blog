Former Pakistani PM Imran Khan granted bail by anti-terrorism court

In a dramatic turn of events, former Pakistani Prime Minister Imran Khan has been granted bail by an anti-terrorism court in Pakistan. This comes as a big relief for the opposition leader who has been facing serious charges of terrorism and sedition. The decision has been welcomed by his supporters who have been protesting his arrest for several weeks now.

Imran Khan was arrested on 30th October 2021 when he was leading a massive rally in Islamabad to demand resignation of the current government led by Prime Minister, Nawaz Sharif. The government accused Imran Khan of inciting violence and creating chaos in the city. His arrest sparked massive protests across the country and led to a complete shutdown of the major cities of Pakistan. The international community also expressed concern over the situation and urged the Pakistani government to release Imran Khan and restore peace.

The decision to grant bail by the anti-terrorism court has come as a major relief for the opposition leader and his supporters. The court has instructed Imran Khan to deposit a hefty amount as a surety before he can be released. His release is expected to take place in the next few days, and his supporters are planning a massive rally to welcome him back.

Quantifiable examples

The case of Imran Khan's arrest and subsequent release is just one example of the political turmoil that Pakistan is facing. The country has been plagued by violence, corruption, and instability for decades. According to some estimates, more than 70,000 people have been killed in terrorist attacks in Pakistan since 2001. The country is also facing an economic crisis, with high inflation, unemployment, and debt. The current government led by Prime Minister Nawaz Sharif has been accused of corruption and mismanagement, and the opposition parties have been demanding his resignation.
