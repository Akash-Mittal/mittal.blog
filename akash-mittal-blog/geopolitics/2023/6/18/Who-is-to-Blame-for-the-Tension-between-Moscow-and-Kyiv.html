Who is to Blame for the Tension between Moscow and Kyiv?



Image Credits https://geopoliticalfutures.com/daily-memo-moscow-points-the-blame-at-kyiv-brussels-looks-for-migrant-deal/

The tension between Moscow and Kyiv has been ongoing for years, with both sides pointing fingers at the other for instigating the conflict. However, the recent Daily Memo from Geopolitical Futures highlights Moscow's blame towards Kyiv, which raises further questions about the root cause of the conflict.

The Story of the Conflict

The conflict between Moscow and Kyiv can be traced back to the fall of the Soviet Union, which led to the formation of independent states in the region. Ukraine gained independence in 1991, which was a significant blow to Moscow's power and influence. Since then, Moscow has been trying to maintain its control over the region by using various methods, including propaganda, military intervention, and economic pressure.

The conflict escalated in 2014 when Moscow annexed Crimea, a move that Kyiv did not recognize. This led to a civil war in eastern Ukraine, where pro-Russian separatists took control of several regions. The conflict has resulted in thousands of deaths and has displaced millions of people.

Moscow Points the Blame at Kyiv

According to the Daily Memo from Geopolitical Futures, Moscow has been blaming Kyiv for the tension, citing Ukraine's cooperation with NATO and the presence of far-right groups in the country. Moscow has also accused Kyiv of violating the ceasefire agreements and not implementing the Minsk agreements, which were meant to resolve the conflict.

However, many experts believe that Moscow is using these excuses to divert attention from its actions in the region. Moscow has been accused of providing military support to the separatists, violating the ceasefire agreements, and attempting to destabilize the region.

Case Studies

The conflict between Moscow and Kyiv has had a severe impact on the region, with significant economic and social implications. Here are a few case studies that highlight the effects of the conflict:

1. Donetsk

Donetsk, a major city in eastern Ukraine, has been at the center of the conflict since 2014. The city has been heavily damaged by the fighting, with many buildings destroyed or damaged. The city's population has also been greatly affected, with over a million people displaced.

2. Mariupol

Mariupol, a port city on the coast of the Sea of Azov, has been a strategic target for both sides. The city has been under constant shelling, resulting in several deaths and injuries. The conflict has also had an impact on the city's economy, with many businesses closing down due to the instability.

3. Crimea

After the annexation of Crimea, the region has faced significant economic and political challenges. The annexation was not recognized by the international community, which led to economic sanctions imposed on Russia. The tourism industry, which was a significant source of income for the region, has also been greatly affected.

Conclusion

The tension between Moscow and Kyiv has had severe implications for the region, with no clear solution in sight. However, it is essential to note that both sides are responsible for the conflict, and blaming each other will not lead to a resolution. Here are three critical points to remember:

  1. The conflict has resulted from a complex set of factors, including historical, political, and economic issues.
  2. Both Moscow and Kyiv have engaged in actions that have further escalated the conflict, including military intervention and propaganda.
  3. A peaceful resolution to the conflict can only be achieved through dialogue and compromise from both sides.
