Cyclone Biparjoy: Thousands in India begin return home



Image Credits https://www.ctvnews.ca/mobile/climate-and-environment/thousands-in-western-india-relief-camps-begin-returning-home-as-cyclone-biparjoy-recedes-1.6445146

The residents of Andhra Pradesh, Odisha and West Bengal have been hit by the cyclone Biparjoy that has killed 17 people and left thousands stranded. But now, as the storm weakens and the flood water starts to recede, the residents of these states are beginning to return home.

According to the National Disaster Management Authority, more than 5,000 people were evacuated to safer locations, including government shelters, during the storm. And now that the worst of the storm is over, the focus has shifted to relief and rehabilitation efforts.

Relief efforts

The Indian government has deployed teams of the National Disaster Response Force and the Indian Army to assist in relief operations. The teams are working around the clock to clear blocked roads, distribute food and water, and restore power and communication lines.

Several non-governmental organizations (NGOs) have also joined hands with the government to provide relief. These organizations are providing medical aid, distributing essential goods and setting up temporary shelters for the affected people.

Case studies

Chandrakanta Parida, 56, Odisha

Chandrakanta Parida was among the thousands of people who were stranded in Odisha due to Cyclone Biparjoy. She and her family had taken shelter in a government building, where they had to endure harsh conditions without proper food and water.

However, thanks to the efforts of the government and the NGOs, Chandrakanta and her family were provided with food, water and medical aid. She said, "We were scared during the storm, but the relief work has given us hope."

Manisha Roy, 22, West Bengal

Manisha Roy was also one of the thousands of people who were affected by Cyclone Biparjoy. She and her family had to leave their flooded home and were unable to return for several days.

However, Manisha said that the relief efforts have been a major help for her family. "We are grateful for the efforts of the government and the NGOs. They have provided us with food and water, and we are slowly getting back on our feet," she said.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Cyclone Biparjoy has been a devastating event for the residents of Andhra Pradesh, Odisha and West Bengal. However, thanks to the efforts of the Indian government and several NGOs, relief efforts are now underway to help those affected by the storm. The focus now is to provide rehabilitation and help the residents rebuild their lives.
