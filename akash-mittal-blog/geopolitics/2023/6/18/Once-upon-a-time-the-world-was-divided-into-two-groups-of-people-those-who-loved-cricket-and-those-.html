However, organizing such a highly anticipated match is not an easy task. From security measures to transportation and accommodation – there are many factors that need to be taken care of. And that's where technology comes in.

Technology has played a significant role in the organization of cricket matches worldwide. With the help of internet and mobile apps, fans can book tickets, get live scores, and even watch matches online. Venue management systems help organizers manage the crowd and ensure smooth flow of people in and out of the stadium. Biometric security systems and facial recognition software help ensure the safety of the players and fans.

One such example of technology being used in cricket matches is the use of drones. Drones have become an essential tool in cricket matches as they provide a bird's-eye view of the field. They are used to capture live footage, which is then sent to television viewers. Drones are also used to monitor the crowd and manage security.

Another excellent example of technology being used in cricket matches is the use of virtual reality (VR). VR is used to give viewers a more immersive experience of the match. Using VR, viewers can feel like they are in the stadium, and they can get a 360-degree view of the field. VR is still in its early stages, but it is expected to revolutionize the way fans watch cricket.
