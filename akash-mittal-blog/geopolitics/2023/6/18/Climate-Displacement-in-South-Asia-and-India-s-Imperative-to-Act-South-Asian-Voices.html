A Story of a Displaced Family

It was a hot summer day in Bangladesh. The family of five was sitting outside their small hut, trying to get some relief from the scorching sun. They were farmers by profession but due to the frequent flooding caused by the changing climate, their crops had failed for the third year in a row. They couldn't afford to buy food and were surviving on the little aid they were receiving from the government and NGOs. They knew they had to move to a safer place, but they had no idea where to go.

This family is just one of many affected by climate displacement in South Asia. The changing climate is causing droughts, flooding, and other natural disasters, which are rendering many people homeless.

Some Examples of Climate Displacement in South Asia

According to the Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre (IDMC), over 17 million people were displaced due to natural disasters in South Asia in 2018 alone. In India, the northern state of Uttarakhand witnessed one of the worst floods in history in 2013, which resulted in the displacement of over 100,000 people.

In Bangladesh, which is one of the most vulnerable countries to climate change, rising sea levels are threatening the livelihoods and homes of millions of people. The country is also facing frequent cyclones, flooding, and other disasters, which are causing displacement on a massive scale.

Case Studies of Climate Displacement in South Asia

Let's take a look at some case studies to understand the impact of climate displacement on people in South Asia.

The Sundarbans

The Sundarbans is a mangrove forest located on the coast of Bangladesh and India. It is home to over 4 million people, who are mostly dependent on fishing and agriculture. Due to rising sea levels, saltwater intrusion is destroying the fresh groundwater resources, which is making agriculture impossible in many areas. The increasing salinity is also affecting the fish population, making it difficult for fishermen to earn a living. As a result, many people are leaving their homes in search of a better life.

The Maldives

The Maldives is a small island nation located in the Indian Ocean, which is at high risk of disappearing due to the rising sea levels caused by climate change. The country is already experiencing frequent flooding and erosion, which is rendering many people homeless. The government of the Maldives has already started a program to relocate its citizens to safer islands, but the process is slow, and many people are still waiting for help.

The Niyamgiri Hills

The Niyamgiri hills are located in the state of Odisha in India and are home to the Dongria Kondh tribe. The hills are rich in bauxite, a mineral used to make aluminum, which has attracted mining companies to the area. The Dongria Kondh tribe has been resisting the mining companies for years, arguing that their way of life and culture are closely linked to the hills. In 2014, the Supreme Court of India ruled in favor of the tribe and banned the mining in the area. However, the mining companies are still trying to gain access to the hills, which is causing anxiety and displacement among the Dongria Kondh people.

Conclusion

In conclusion, climate displacement is a looming threat in South Asia, and India needs to act urgently to address this issue. Here are three things that India can do:

  1. Invest in adaptation measures: India needs to invest in measures that can help people adapt to the changing climate. This includes building climate-resistant homes, providing education and training on climate-resilient agriculture, and developing early warning systems for disasters.
  2. Reduce greenhouse gas emissions: India is one of the biggest emitters of greenhouse gases in the world, and it needs to take concrete steps to reduce its emissions. This includes shifting to renewable energy sources, investing in public transportation, and promoting sustainable practices.
  3. Protect the rights of vulnerable communities: The most vulnerable communities, such as indigenous people and women, are the ones who are most affected by climate displacement. India needs to take steps to protect their rights and ensure that they are not left behind in the fight against climate change.
