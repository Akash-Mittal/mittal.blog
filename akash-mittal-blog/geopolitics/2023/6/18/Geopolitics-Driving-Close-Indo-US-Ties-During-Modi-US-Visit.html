Geopolitics Driving Close Indo-US Ties During Modi US Visit

Interesting Story

When Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi embarked on his visit to the United States in 2014, it was touted as a historic trip to strengthen economic ties between the two nations. However, as the visit progressed, it became clear that there was more on the agenda than just business deals. The visit was a reflection of the geopolitics at play, with India and the US forging a closer alliance amid rising tensions between China and the US.

Examples

The Indo-US relationship is not a new one, but it has seen significant growth in recent years, with both nations identifying common interests and goals. One such example is the Quadrilateral Security Dialogue, or the Quad, which comprises India, the US, Japan, and Australia and is seen as a counter to China's growing military and economic influence in the region.

Another example is the US support for India's bid for a permanent seat on the UN Security Council. The US has recognized India as a rising global power and sees its partnership with India as a crucial element in maintaining peace and stability in the region.

Economic ties have also played a major role in the growing Indo-US relationship. The US is India's second-largest trading partner and the two nations have established various trade agreements to promote greater cooperation.

Case Studies

1. The Chabahar Port Project

The Chabahar Port Project in Iran is one of the most significant examples of the Indo-US partnership. The port provides India with an alternate route to Afghanistan and Central Asia, bypassing Pakistan. The project is being jointly developed by India, Iran, and Afghanistan, with the US providing strategic support and waiver for sanctions.

2. Joint Military Exercises

The Indo-US defense ties have been growing in recent years, with joint military exercises being a major component of the partnership. The two nations have conducted numerous exercises, including the Malabar exercise in the Indian Ocean, aimed at strengthening maritime security and operations.

3. Cooperation in Space

The Indo-US partnership in space has also seen significant growth, with NASA and ISRO (Indian Space Research Organization) collaborating on various projects. The two nations have also signed an agreement to cooperate on lunar exploration, with the US providing support to India's Chandrayaan-2 mission.

Conclusion

The close relationship between India and the US may have started with economic ties, but it has evolved into a strategic partnership aimed at maintaining regional and global stability. Geopolitics has played a significant role in this partnership, with both nations identifying common interests in the face of a rising China. The alliance has seen significant growth with various examples and case studies to back it up.

In conclusion, the Indo-US partnership is vital in today's changing world, and it is crucial for both nations to maintain and strengthen this relationship to achieve their strategic goals.
