It was a hot summer day in Delhi. The prime minister of India, Narendra Modi, was preparing to board his plane to the United States for an important summit with the American president. As he walked towards the aircraft, a reporter shouted a question at him, "Sir, India is facing a rising China. What do you think the future holds?". Modi paused for a moment, looked at the reporter, and smiled. "The future holds what we make of it", he replied, and boarded the plane.

India's Rise and China's Challenge

India and China are two of the world's fastest-growing economies. Over the past decade, India's economy has expanded at an average annual rate of about 7%, while China's has grown at an average of about 9%. However, China is still much larger in terms of GDP and per capita income. India faces several challenges in catching up with its neighbor, including infrastructure deficits, bureaucratic red tape, and political instability. But India also has several advantages, such as a young and tech-savvy workforce, a vibrant democratic system, and a growing middle class.

India's rise is not only an economic phenomenon but also a geopolitical one. India is increasingly asserting its influence in the region and beyond, challenging China's dominance. For example, India has joined the Quad alliance with the US, Japan, and Australia, aimed at countering China's assertiveness in the Indo-Pacific. India has also increased its engagement with Southeast Asia and Africa, offering development assistance and seeking closer ties.

India's rise and China's challenge will be one of the defining rivalries of the 21st century. Both countries have distinct visions of their role in the world and are jostling for power and influence. The US, as a global superpower, has a key role to play in deciding the outcome of this rivalry.

PM Modi's US Visit as a Turning Point

PM Modi's upcoming visit to the US is a turning point for India's rise. It comes at a crucial juncture when India is seeking to leverage its growing economic and geopolitical clout to enhance its global influence. The visit is expected to focus on several areas, such as trade, investment, defense, and technology cooperation. The two countries are likely to sign several agreements and announce new initiatives to deepen their strategic partnership.

The visit is also significant because of the broader context in which it takes place. The US is currently engaged in a strategic competition with China, and India is seen as a key partner in this effort. The US has been trying to woo India away from its traditional policy of non-alignment and towards a closer partnership. The Quad alliance, which includes India, is one of the manifestations of this strategy.

PM Modi's US visit is an opportunity for India to showcase its capabilities and ambitions to the world. It is also an opportunity for India to shape the strategic dynamics of the region and beyond. However, the visit also carries risks, such as growing dependence on the US, alienating China, and internal political opposition. PM Modi will need to navigate these risks carefully and strike a balance between India's interests and its relations with the US and China.

Case Studies on India's Rise

Case Study 1: Make in India

Make in India is a flagship program launched by the Indian government in 2014 to promote manufacturing and investment in the country. The program aims to create jobs, boost exports, and increase the share of manufacturing in GDP. Several initiatives have been taken under this program, such as ease of doing business, infrastructure development, and skill development. The program has attracted significant foreign investment and has helped India become a manufacturing hub in several sectors, such as automobiles, electronics, and pharmaceuticals.

Case Study 2: Digital India

Digital India is another flagship program launched by the Indian government in 2015 to promote the adoption of digital technology and services in the country. The program aims to create a digitally empowered society and economy, improve governance and public services, and enhance digital infrastructure. Several initiatives have been taken under this program, such as Aadhaar, e-governance, and broadband connectivity. The program has helped India become a leader in digital innovation and has improved access to services and information for millions of people.
