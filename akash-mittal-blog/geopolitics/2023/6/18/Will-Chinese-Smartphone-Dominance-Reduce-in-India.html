Will Chinese Smartphone Dominance Reduce in India?



Image Credits https://telecom.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/devices/telecom-diary-will-chinese-smartphone-dominance-reduce-in-india/101069335

Once upon a time, in a small village in rural India, there lived a man who had never used a smartphone before. One day, his son brought home a Chinese smartphone. The man was amazed by the features and functionalities of the device but was also worried about its safety and privacy.

This story reflects the current state of the Indian smartphone market, where Chinese smartphones dominate with over 75% market share. However, recent events have brought into question whether this dominance will reduce or not.

Examples of Chinese Smartphone Dominance

China's Xiaomi has been the leading smartphone brand in India for the past three years, capturing over 30% market share in Q2 2021, according to Counterpoint Research. Other Chinese brands such as Vivo, Realme, and Oppo also hold a significant market share, showcasing the overall dominance of Chinese smartphones in India.

Case Studies

Government Ban on Chinese Apps

In mid-2020, the Indian government put a ban on several Chinese apps, including the widely popular TikTok. This move was made due to security concerns as these apps had access to large amounts of user data that could be shared with the Chinese government. This ban not only affected Chinese app companies but also had a ripple effect on Chinese smartphone brands as these apps were pre-installed on many of their devices. This led to a decline in sales for these brands as Indian consumers moved away from Chinese products.

Anti-China Sentiment among Consumers

Due to recent border conflicts between India and China, anti-China sentiment has been on the rise among Indian consumers. This has led to a boycott of Chinese products, including smartphones. Indian consumers have started to prefer homegrown brands such as Micromax and Lava over Chinese brands, leading to a small decline in market share for Chinese brands.

Conclusion: Will Chinese Smartphone Dominance Reduce in India?

Based on recent events, it is safe to say that Chinese smartphone dominance in India may reduce over time. However, it is unlikely that Chinese brands will completely lose their market share anytime soon. Here are three points that summarize the current state of the Indian smartphone market:

  1. The anti-China sentiment among Indian consumers may lead to a decline in market share for Chinese brands, but this decline may not be significant.
  2. The shift towards homegrown brands such as Micromax and Lava may further reduce the market share of Chinese brands, but these brands have a long way to go to compete with established players.
  3. Chinese brands such as Xiaomi, Vivo, and Realme have already established a dominant presence in the Indian smartphone market with strong brand recognition and popular product lines. These brands are likely to maintain their market share over the coming years.
