China Polysilicon Prices In Freefall

A Grim Tale Of Oversupply & Falling Demand

Once upon a time, the global solar industry was considered to be the savior of the planet, thanks to its ability to generate renewable energy and reduce carbon footprint. However, that fairy tale may soon come to an end, as the industry is currently facing a major crisis in the form of oversupply and falling demand, especially in China.

China is the largest producer and consumer of solar polysilicon, which is a key component of solar panels. Over the past few years, China has invested heavily in polysilicon manufacturing technologies and facilities. It has also encouraged local companies to enter the industry, with generous subsidies and policies.

As a result, China's polysilicon capacity has surged to a record high, while demand has failed to keep up, due to a combination of factors, including policy changes, project delays, and declining prices of coal and other fossil fuels.

As of now, the prices of solar polysilicon in China have fallen by around 30% from their peak in 2018, and are expected to drop further. This has put immense pressure on Chinese polysilicon producers, many of whom are struggling to survive and facing bankruptcy.

Examples Of The Crisis

One such company is Tongwei Group, which is the world's largest solar cell manufacturer and a major polysilicon producer. Tongwei has announced that it will temporarily suspend production at its two Chinese polysilicon plants, due to the weak demand and low prices. This move is expected to affect more than 10% of China's total polysilicon output.

Another company in trouble is Daqo New Energy, which is another major polysilicon producer. Daqo has reported a net loss for the first quarter of 2020, due to the sharp decline in polysilicon prices.

Several other Chinese polysilicon producers, including GCL-Poly and Xinte Energy, have also reported losses or reduced profits, and have announced plans to cut down production or delay expansion projects.

The Case Of Trina Solar

Trina Solar, which is one of the largest solar panel manufacturers in the world, has also been affected by the crisis. Trina has reported a decline in revenue and profits for the first quarter of 2020, due to lower sales volume and falling prices.

Trina has attributed the decline to the COVID-19 pandemic, which has disrupted global supply chains and impacted demand. However, analysts say that the oversupply and falling prices of polysilicon are also major factors.

Trina has responded to the crisis by reducing costs and increasing efficiency, but it remains to be seen how the company will weather the storm.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the China polysilicon crisis is a grim tale of oversupply and falling demand, which has put immense pressure on the solar industry and its stakeholders. Some of the key takeaways from the crisis are:

  • China's polysilicon industry needs to consolidate and rationalize, to avoid further losses and bankruptcies.
  • The solar industry needs to diversify its supply chain and reduce its dependence on a single country or region.
  • The global community needs to recognize the importance of renewable energy and take proactive steps to support the industry.
