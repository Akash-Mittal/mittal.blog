NUST Think Tank Holds Webinar on 'Saudi-Iranian Rapprochement'



Image Credits https://dailytimes.com.pk/1102697/nust-think-tank-holds-webinar-on-saudi-iranian-rapprochement/

Once upon a time, in the Middle East, there were two powerful nations - Saudi Arabia and Iran. These two nations were once friends, but as time passed, their friendship turned into enmity. The rivalry between Saudi Arabia and Iran, labelled as the 'cold war of the Middle East,' has been one of the longest-standing conflicts in modern times. However, recent developments have given some hope for a possible reconciliation between the two nations. To discuss these developments and their implications, the National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST) think tank held a webinar on 'Saudi-Iranian Rapprochement.'

The Recent Developments

The tension between Saudi Arabia and Iran started in 1979, when a revolution in Iran resulted in the establishment of an Islamic Republic. This led to a clash of ideologies between the Sunni-dominated Saudi Arabia and the Shia-dominated Iran. In recent years, several events have taken place that have raised hopes for a possible reconciliation between the two nations.

  1. In 2015, Iran signed a nuclear deal with the United States and other world powers. The deal lifted some of the economic sanctions imposed on Iran, which was a significant boost to its economy.
  2. In 2021, Joe Biden became the President of the United States and expressed his willingness to re-join the Iran nuclear deal. This could pave the way for the lifting of more sanctions on Iran.
  3. In May 2021, secret talks were held between Saudi Arabia and Iran in Iraq. This was the first high-level contact between the two nations in years.

The Implications

If Saudi Arabia and Iran can restore their relationship, it could potentially have several positive implications for the region. Firstly, it could reduce tension in the Middle East and lead to the resolution of many conflicts. For example, the civil war in Yemen is often described as a proxy war between Saudi Arabia and Iran. If these two nations can reconcile, they could work together to bring an end to this conflict. Secondly, it could boost economic activity in the region. Iran is a significant producer of oil and gas, and Saudi Arabia is the largest oil exporter in the world. If the two nations can cooperate, they could increase oil production and potentially lower oil prices. Finally, it could reduce the influence of outside powers in the region. The United States, Russia, and China have all been involved in the Middle East, and if Saudi Arabia and Iran can reconcile, they could potentially limit the influence of these outside powers.

Case Studies

There are several examples of nations that were once enemies but eventually reconciled their differences. One of the most famous examples is the reconciliation between France and Germany after World War II. These two nations were bitter enemies, but they eventually reconciled their differences and formed the European Union. Another example is the reconciliation between the United States and Vietnam. After the Vietnam War, the United States and Vietnam were enemies, but they eventually reconciled their differences and established diplomatic relations. These examples show that even the most bitter enemies can reconcile their differences if they are committed to doing so.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the recent developments in Saudi Arabia and Iran have given some hope for a possible reconciliation between the two nations. If they can restore their relationship, it could potentially have several positive implications for the region. Firstly, it could reduce tension and conflicts in the Middle East. Secondly, it could boost economic activity in the region. Finally, it could reduce the influence of outside powers in the region. However, reconciling the differences between these two nations is easier said than done. It will require patience, diplomacy, and compromise from both sides.
