Cyclone Biparjoy Churns Toward India and Pakistan

It was the year 1999 and I was only 5 years old when the super cyclone hit the eastern coast of India. I still remember how my family had to evacuate our home and take refuge in a nearby school. The powerful winds and heavy rains had caused severe damage to our neighborhood. That was my first encounter with the fury of a cyclone.

Now, more than two decades later, another cyclone named Biparjoy is heading towards India and Pakistan. The India Meteorological Department has warned of heavy rainfall, strong winds, and storm surges in several coastal regions. The authorities are preparing for the worst-case scenario and urging people to take necessary precautions.

Cyclones are not uncommon in this part of the world. The Bay of Bengal and Arabian Sea are prone to tropical cyclones, which can cause widespread destruction and loss of life. In recent years, we have seen the devastating effects of cyclones like Fani, Hudhud, Phailin, and Amphan. These cyclones have left a trail of destruction and suffering in their wake.

But what causes these cyclones and how can we prepare for them?

Causes of Cyclones

Cyclones, also known as hurricanes or typhoons, are caused by a combination of factors, including:

  • Warm ocean temperatures
  • Low atmospheric pressure
  • High humidity
  • Wind shear

When these conditions are met, a low-pressure area is formed over the ocean, which sucks in air and moisture from surrounding areas. As the air rises and cools, it forms clouds and precipitation, which is accompanied by strong winds. The cyclone can then travel over the ocean, gaining strength and intensity, until it makes landfall and causes havoc.

Climate change is also playing a role in the frequency and intensity of cyclones. Warmer ocean temperatures, caused by global warming, are providing more fuel for cyclones to grow and intensify. This means that we can expect more frequent and more severe cyclones in the future.

Preparing for Cyclones

Given the destructive power of cyclones, it is essential that we take steps to prepare for them. Here are a few examples of what can be done:

  • Building robust infrastructure that can withstand strong winds and heavy rains
  • Developing early warning systems that can help people evacuate in time
  • Stockpiling essential supplies, such as food, water, and medicines, in advance
  • Training communities on how to respond to emergencies and avoid risks
  • Creating awareness about the dangers of climate change and the need to reduce greenhouse gas emissions

Several organizations and governments are already working towards these goals. For instance, the Indian government has launched a disaster management program that aims to minimize the impact of natural disasters. The United Nations Development Programme has provided technical assistance to the government of Bangladesh to help them prepare for cyclones, including the installation of early warning systems and evacuation centers.

However, there is still a lot more that needs to be done, especially in vulnerable communities that are often the hardest hit by cyclones.

Case Studies

Let's take a look at a few case studies to understand the impact of cyclones and the efforts to mitigate them:

Cyclone Fani, India (2019)

Cyclone Fani was one of the strongest cyclones to hit the Indian state of Odisha in recent years. The cyclone caused extensive damage to infrastructure and homes, and disrupted power and communication services. However, the death toll was kept relatively low due to the timely evacuation of over 1 million people. The Indian government's disaster management efforts were widely praised for their effectiveness.

Cyclone Idai, Mozambique (2019)

Cyclone Idai was a devastating cyclone that hit Mozambique, Zimbabwe, and Malawi in 2019. The cyclone caused widespread flooding and landslides, which destroyed homes, farms, and infrastructure. The death toll in the three countries exceeded 1,000, and over 2 million were left homeless. International aid organizations like Oxfam and Red Cross provided emergency assistance, but the recovery efforts are still ongoing.

Cyclone Sidr, Bangladesh (2007)

Cyclone Sidr was a powerful cyclone that hit the southwestern coast of Bangladesh in 2007. The cyclone caused extensive damage to infrastructure and homes, and disrupted power and communication services. However, the death toll was kept relatively low due to the effective evacuation of over 2 million people. The government of Bangladesh and international organizations like UNDP and USAID provided extensive support for the recovery efforts.

Conclusion:

  1. Cyclones are becoming more frequent and more severe due to climate change.
  2. Preparations need to be made to minimize the impact of cyclones, including building resilient infrastructure, developing early warning systems, and providing training and awareness to vulnerable communities.
  3. Collaborative efforts between governments, organizations, and communities are needed to effectively mitigate the impact of cyclones.
