**Blinken arrives Beijing on mission to cool soaring US-China tensions**



Image Credits https://www.business-standard.com/world-news/blinken-arrives-beijing-on-mission-to-cool-soaring-us-china-tensions-123061800035_1.html

Once upon a time, two giants emerged in the world of trade and economy, one was the United States of America and the other, China. Both nations had their unique strengths and weaknesses; however, over the years, they had built a symbiotic relationship that benefited both of them. China supplied the US with cheap products, and the US sent technology to China to produce these products cheaply. However, as time passed, the relationship between the two nations started to sour, and today, the US-China trade war is one of the most critical issues in the world of international politics.

On Thursday, March 18, 2021, United States Secretary of State, Antony Blinken, arrived in Beijing to meet with top Chinese officials as part of his diplomatic mission to ease the escalating tensions between the two countries. Blinken took this step after Biden's administration held its first high-level talks with China in Alaska, which turned out to be a diplomatic storm.

The US-China trade war began in 2018 when the US imposed a series of tariffs on Chinese goods, and in retaliation, China increased tariffs on US products. Since then, tensions between the two countries have only escalated, and with Trump's hard-line policy towards China, the situation had deteriorated to a point of no return.

So, how did we get here, and what is at stake?

The US-China relationship is essential to the global economy, politics, and societal values. It is a complex relationship, founded on intertwined interests and strategic priorities. However, various issues have resulted in the souring of the relationship between these two nations. Here are a few examples:

1. TECHNOLOGY WAR:

One of the major issues that have contributed to the US-China trade war is technology. Trump administration believed that China had been stealing American intellectual property and technology. China has been accused of hacking and stealing sensitive information from US government agencies, corporations, and research institutions. Additionally, the US believes that China has been using these stolen technologies to develop its military power and to challenge the US's global dominance.

2. TRADE IMBALANCES:

The slanted trade balance between the US and China has also been a crucial issue. The US has accused China of running a massive trade surplus with America by exporting more goods to the US than it imports from the US. The US view this trade imbalance as a massive loss to the American economy.

3. HUMAN RIGHTS ABUSES:

The human rights situation in China has been another contentious issue. The US has accused China of suppressing religious and minority groups, including Muslims in western China. Additionally, China has been accused of violating human rights in Hong Kong and Taiwan. The US sanctions and sanctions from other western nations have been put in place to punish China for these alleged violations.

What is at stake?

The US-China trade war has major implications for the global economy. The two nations have a vital role to play in world trade, and any disruption could have a severe impact on the world economy. The trade war could also spark a recession by disrupting global supply chains and increasing the cost of goods.

Case Studies

The US-China trade war has affected various industries globally, and here are a few examples of its impact:

1. AUTOMOBILE INDUSTRY:

The US-China trade war has caused a significant impact on the automobile industry. The US increased tariffs on Chinese auto parts, leading to a significant increase in the cost of assembling cars in America. Additionally, China imposed a 25% tariff on American-made cars, further denting the US automobile industry.

2. AGRICULTURAL INDUSTRY:

The agricultural industry in the United States has been hit hard by the US-China trade war. China is the largest importer of US farm products. The tariffs levied by China on US soybeans, corn, and other products have resulted in a significant loss of market share for US farmers. The US government has had to provide subsidies to farmers to prevent them from going out of business.

3. TECHNOLOGY INDUSTRY:

The technology industry, which plays a vital role in the US economy, has also suffered as a result of the trade war. The US, as part of its efforts to counter Chinese technology, banned Chinese tech giants such as Huawei from doing business with US companies. Additionally, the US government blocked Chinese investment in US companies that develop technology with strategic importance.

Conclusion:

1. The US-China trade war is one of the most significant issues in the world of international relations and politics.

2. The trade war has significant implications for the global economy, and any disruption could have severe consequences for industries worldwide.

3. Efforts to ease tensions between the two nations are critical to prevent the further escalation of the situation.
