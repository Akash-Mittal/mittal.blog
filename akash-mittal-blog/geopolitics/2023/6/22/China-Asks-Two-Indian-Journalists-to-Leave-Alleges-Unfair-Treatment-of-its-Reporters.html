China Asks Two Indian Journalists to Leave: Alleges ‘Unfair Treatment' of its Reporters



Image Credits https://www.thehindu.com/news/national/china-asks-two-indian-journalists-to-leave-alleges-unfair-treatment-of-its-reporters/article66961490.ece

Once upon a time, in a land far away, there were two journalists - one from India and one from China. The Indian journalist had come to China to report on the country's economic growth, while the Chinese journalist had been sent to India to cover its political scenario.

However, their journeys were not entirely smooth. The Indian journalist faced several challenges in getting access to information and sources, while the Chinese journalist felt that she was subjected to racial slurs and discrimination in India.

The issue of treatment of journalists is a crucial one - it affects not just the individuals involved, but also the larger arena of journalism and media. The recent incident of China asking two Indian journalists to leave is a case in point.

Unfair Treatment of Chinese Reporters in India?

In April 2021, China asked two Indian journalists working with Xinhua news agency to leave the country, citing "unfriendly" reporting and alleged "unfair treatment" of its reporters in India.

The move came just a month after India had asked three Chinese journalists working with China's state-run media to leave the country. India had accused them of engaging in "activities beyond their journalistic capacities" and "unauthorized travel" to a restricted area.

The tit-for-tat expulsions of journalists has further strained the already tense relations between India and China.

Journalism under Fire

The treatment of journalists has become a major issue in many countries, with several incidents of harassment, intimidation, and attacks on journalists being reported.

According to the World Press Freedom Index 2021, India ranks 142nd out of 180 countries in terms of press freedom - a decline of two places from its 2020 ranking. The report stated that "India' press freedom is in grave danger" and that "journalists are exposed to every kind of attack, including police violence against reporters, ambushes by political activists, and reprisals instigated by criminal groups or corrupt local officials."

In China, the government exercises strict control over the media, with journalists facing restrictions on what they can report and what sources they can use. Foreign journalists are often subjected to surveillance and harassment by Chinese authorities.

The situation is not much better in other countries. In the United States, journalists have been labeled as "fake news" and "enemies of the people" by former President Donald Trump. In Myanmar, the military junta has cracked down on journalists following the coup in February 2021.

Case Studies: Treatment of Journalists in India and China

India

In February 2021, a journalist named Mandeep Punia was arrested by Delhi police while covering a farmers' protest. Punia was accused of "obstructing a public servant in discharge of public functions" and assaulting a police officer. However, media watchdogs and journalists claimed that Punia was targeted for his reporting on police brutality against protesters.

In June 2021, two journalists from Assam - Manoj Kumar Nath and Azhar Iqubal - were arrested by the Manipur police while reporting on an alleged encounter between the police and militants. The journalists were accused of "trespassing" and "obstructing police duties."

China

In February 2021, Chinese journalist Zhang Zhan was sentenced to four years in prison for her reporting from Wuhan during the early days of the COVID-19 pandemic. Zhang had reported on the government's handling of the pandemic and the lockdown measures imposed on Wuhan.

In March 2021, three citizen journalists were jailed for reporting on the early days of the COVID-19 pandemic in Wuhan. Chen Qiushi, Fang Bin, and Li Zehua were charged with "picking quarrels and provoking trouble." Chen had reported on the plight of Wuhan residents during the pandemic, while Fang had posted videos from inside a Wuhan hospital.

Conclusion

The treatment of journalists is a crucial issue that needs to be addressed if we want to ensure the free flow of information and the protection of press freedom. Here are three key points to keep in mind:

  • Journalists play a critical role in informing the public and holding those in power accountable. Any attempts to suppress or intimidate journalists have far-reaching consequences for society as a whole.
  • The rise of social media and digital platforms has given journalists unprecedented tools and opportunities for reporting, but it has also led to new challenges and risks. It is essential to develop new strategies and safeguards to protect journalists in the digital age.
  • The treatment of journalists is also linked to wider issues of democracy, human rights, and freedom of expression. It is a reminder that the struggle for press freedom is an ongoing one, and we need to remain vigilant and committed in defending this fundamental right.
