Arjun is not alone in this. The relationship between India and its neighbours has always been complicated, with continuous skirmishes and tensions at the border. However, despite these challenges, India has come a long way in terms of strengthening its defence capabilities and forging alliances with other countries for support. The US is one such country that India has been collaborating with for a long time now to enhance its defence capabilities. The latest initiative by the US Department of Defense (DoD) to balance its complex relationship with India is a testament to this collaboration.

Examples of US-India Defence Collaboration

The US and India have collaborated on several projects to strengthen their defence capabilities over the years. Some of the notable examples are:

  • Defence Technology and Trade Initiative (DTTI): DTTI was launched in 2012 to strengthen the defence ties between India and the US. The initiative aims to focus on co-development and co-production of defence products and technologies.
  • Logistics Exchange Memorandum of Agreement (LEMOA): LEMOA was signed between India and the US in 2016 to provide access to each other's military bases for refuelling and repairs. The agreement also allows the two countries to share classified information with each other.
  • Defence Innovation Unit (DIU): DIU was launched by the US Department of Defense in 2015 with a mission to accelerate the adoption of commercial technologies into the US military. The unit has been working closely with India's Ministry of Defence to identify key technologies and startups that can help enhance India's defence capabilities.

Case Studies

The US-India defence collaboration has yielded some successful case studies in recent years. Here are a few examples:

1. Apache Helicopters

In 2015, India agreed to purchase 22 Apache helicopters from the US. The deal worth $2.5 billion was aimed at bolstering India's defence capabilities by providing advanced attack helicopters. The Apache helicopters are equipped with Hellfire missiles, rockets and other advanced avionics that make them lethal weapons. The delivery of the helicopters began in 2019 and will continue till 2022.

2. C-130J Super Hercules

The C-130J Super Hercules is a military transport aircraft that is used for a variety of missions including troop transport, cargo transport and humanitarian missions. In 2008, India signed a deal with the US to purchase six C-130J Super Hercules aircraft for $1.1 billion. The aircraft have been instrumental in enhancing India's humanitarian and disaster relief capabilities.

3. P-8I Poseidon

The P-8I Poseidon is a maritime patrol aircraft that is used for anti-submarine warfare, intelligence, surveillance and reconnaissance (ISR) missions. India signed a contract with the US in 2009 to purchase eight P-8I Poseidon aircraft for $2.1 billion. The aircraft have been deployed in the Indian Ocean region to ensure maritime security.

Conclusion

The US-India defence collaboration has come a long way in the past decade. Several initiatives and collaborations have been undertaken to enhance India's defence capabilities and strengthen its relationship with the US. The US recognizes the strategic importance of India in the Indo-Pacific region and is committed to forging a stronger partnership with India to ensure regional security and stability.
