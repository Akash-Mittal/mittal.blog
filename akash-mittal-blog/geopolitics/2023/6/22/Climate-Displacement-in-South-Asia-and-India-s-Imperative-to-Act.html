Climate Displacement in South Asia and India's Imperative to Act



Image Credits https://southasianvoices.org/climate-displacement-in-south-asia-and-indias-imperative-to-act/

An alarming revolution is taking place in the South Asian region. Climate change is posing a huge threat to people's safety and security. The rising sea level, erratic monsoons, cyclones, floods, droughts, and heatwaves are among the many issues they face. More than ever, people are facing forced displacement and migration due to climate change.

Let's take a moment to look at the following scenario to understand the gravity of the situation:

The story revolves around a small island village in the Bay of Bengal, off the coast of India. People of that village were fisherfolks and were happy in their old ways of living. But since the last few years, things started to change. The seawater started to rise and came into their homes. Cyclones became a frequent occurrence. And the once-fruitful fishing trips were not that fruitful anymore.

Finally, the day arrived when they had to make a difficult choice to abandon the only home they ever knew. They packed their belongings and left for the safety of the mainland, not knowing what the future holds for them. This is a very real and binding crisis, wreaking massive devastation on innocent lives.

Climate Displacement and its Impact

The story mentioned above is just one of the many examples of the impact of climate change on human lives. The increasing frequency and severity of natural disasters have displaced millions of people in South Asia. The situation is only getting worse with time.

According to the Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre (IDMC), India alone accounts for almost a third of the world's newly displaced people due to disasters. Between 2008 and 2016, India saw around 21.3 million people displaced by climate-related disasters, such as floods, cyclones, and landslides.

However, it is not just natural disasters that are causing displacement. Climate change is leading to food insecurity, water scarcity, and loss of livelihoods, especially for farmers and fisherfolk. Such displacement is not just dangerous but takes a toll on the physical and mental health of the affected individuals and communities.

Case Studies of Climate Displacement

1. Chennai, India

Chennai faced the most extensive flooding in history in November-December 2015, resulting in the displacement of around 1.8 million people. The flooding was due to a convergence of various factors, such as poor land-use planning, illegal encroachment on water bodies and inadequate drainage systems.

2. Bangladesh's Coastal Communities

Bangladesh is one of the most vulnerable countries to climate change. Every year, around 10,000 hectares of land is lost due to rising sea levels. As a result, many coastal communities have been displaced and have had to migrate to the cities to avoid the devastating impacts of climate change.

3. Thar Desert, India

The Thar Desert, also known as the Great Indian Desert, faces severe water scarcity due to climate change. The desert is home to numerous nomadic communities that rely on water wells for survival. However, climate change has led to a decrease in rainfall, leading to the drying up of water wells. As a result, many nomadic communities have had to leave their homes in search of water and livelihood.

India's Imperative to Act

India is one of the most vulnerable countries to climate change and must act urgently to address the issue of climate displacement. As a developing country, India is facing multiple challenges, such as poverty, illiteracy, and healthcare, but India cannot afford to ignore climate change. The following are some of the steps that India can take to address the issue:

1. Policy Formulation

India needs to formulate and implement policies that address the issues of climate migration, such as housing, education, and livelihoods, to ensure that the displaced communities have a chance to rebuild their lives.

2. Addressing Root Causes of Displacement

Another crucial step that India can take is to address the root causes of climate displacement, such as deforestation, unplanned urbanization, and pollution. India must take the necessary steps to reduce its carbon footprint and help mitigate the impacts of climate change.

3. International Cooperation

India should work closely with its South Asian neighbors to address the issue of climate change. Countries must cooperate and collaborate to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and mitigate the impacts of climate change. India can also seek international support and funding to help the displaced communities.

Conclusion

Climate displacement is a humanitarian crisis that demands immediate attention and action from India. As a country that is highly vulnerable to climate change, India must take the necessary steps to mitigate the impacts of climate change and support its displaced communities. India should work closely with its South Asian neighbors and the international community to address this issue.

The three key takeaways are:

  1. Climate displacement is an alarming issue, and India must act urgently to address the issue.
  2. India must formulate and implement policies that address the root causes of displacement and ensure that the displaced communities have a chance to rebuild their lives.
  3. India should work closely with its South Asian neighbors and the international community to address this issue.
