How to Understand the U.S.-Indian Relationship

An Interesting Story

Think about a scenario where two individuals of diverse cultural backgrounds and customs unite to work and build trust. Their professional or personal bond grows stronger and intensifies, from a working relationship to a strategic alliance. That is the fundamentals of the relationship between the United States and India.

Examples of the U.S.-Indian Relationship

The U.S.-India relationship has witnessed many positive developments in the past decade, including strategic partnerships in technology, innovation, healthcare, education, and defense. These collaborations represent an increasingly strong bond between the two nations.

  • Trade and Economic Partnerships: The United States is India's largest trading partner, with trade exceeding $120 billion. India is also the United States' ninth-largest goods trading partner. General Electric and McDonald's are two U.S. businesses that have become household names in India.
  • Cultural Exchange: The U.S.-India relationship has been strengthened through cultural exchanges. Indian music, fashion, dance, and food are now familiar throughout the United States. Similarly, American movies, music, and popular culture have made significant impacts in India.
  • Defense Ties: The two nations share a mutual interest in global security and stability. The United States views India as a strategic defense partner, given its location and military power. The two countries conduct joint naval exercises and engage in intelligence-sharing, and defense technology transfers.

Case Studies

Let's examine three excellent examples of the U.S.-India strategic partnership, illustrating the benefits and advantages of a close relationship.

  • COVID-19 Joint Task Force: The two countries established a Joint Task Force on COVID-19 in March 2020 to learn from each other's successes and share best practices in responding to the pandemic. This partnership has resulted in the pooling of medical resources and greatly benefited both countries in their fight against the pandemic.
  • Education and Technology Collaboration: The U.S. and India have many joint ventures in higher education, research, and technology. For instance, Stanford University has launched a study abroad program in India, and NASA and the Indian Space Research Organization have collaborated on numerous space missions.
  • The Indo-Pacific Vision: The United States and India share a vision of a free, open, and prosperous Indo-Pacific region. In 2018, the two nations conducted the inaugural 2+2 Dialogue on foreign and defense affairs, where they pledged to cooperate on maritime security, counterterrorism, and trade.

  1. The U.S.-India relationship is critical in the international context due to shared interests and values.
  2. The partnership between India and the United States has grown stronger in recent years and has the potential to become more robust in the future, contributing to economic growth, scientific advancement, and global stability.
  3. Although disagreements between the two nations are likely to arise, dialogue and negotiation are necessary to preserve the strong U.S.-India relationship.
