Examples of Strong India-U.S. Ties

The United States and India have enjoyed a strong relationship for several decades now. This has been reflected in the close cooperation between the two countries in various fields like trade, defense, and technology. Here are a few examples:

Trade

The U.S. and India have been each other's top trading partners in recent years. In 2020, despite the pandemic, the two countries saw a trade exchange of $88.75 billion. This is indicative of the growing importance that each country places on the other as a trading partner.

Defense

The U.S. has been a key supplier of defense equipment to India over the years. In 2020, the U.S. approved the sale of $3.5 billion worth of military equipment to India. Additionally, the two countries have engaged in joint military exercises and technology sharing.

Case Studies of the India-U.S. Partnership

COVID-19 Vaccine Partnership

In 2021, India and the U.S. partnered to produce COVID-19 vaccines to be distributed throughout the world. This was a significant bilateral collaboration that highlighted the benefits of a strong India-U.S. relationship. The U.S. provided raw materials and technical assistance to India's Serum Institute of India, which enabled it to ramp up production of the AstraZeneca vaccine.

The Civil Nuclear Deal

The U.S.-India nuclear deal signed in 2008 was a significant landmark in the bilateral relationship. The deal allowed India access to nuclear technology and fuel on the condition that it opened up its civilian nuclear facilities to international inspection. This deal had been in the works for several years and its success showed the commitment of both countries to a strong partnership.

Conclusion: Three Key Points

In conclusion, the India-U.S. relationship has been marked by close cooperation in various fields. The upcoming state visit of Prime Minister Narendra Modi is likely to further strengthen the partnership between the two countries. Here are three key takeaways:

  1. The U.S. and India have been each other's top trading partners in recent years, indicating a growing importance of each country to the other as a trading partner.
  2. The U.S. has been a key supplier of defense equipment to India, with joint military exercises and technology sharing taking place.
  3. The India-U.S. partnership has resulted in significant bilateral collaborations like the COVID-19 vaccine partnership and the civil nuclear deal.
