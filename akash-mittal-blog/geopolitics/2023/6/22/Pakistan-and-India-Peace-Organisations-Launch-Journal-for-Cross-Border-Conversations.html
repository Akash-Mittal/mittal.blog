Pakistan and India Peace Organisations Launch Journal for Cross Border Conversations



Image Credits https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/peace-organisations-launch-journal-for-cross-border-conversations-between-india-and-pakistan/articleshow/101080929.cms

It all started thirty years ago, when a young Pakistani boy named Ali met an Indian girl named Rani at a peace conference in New Zealand. They became friends, and over the years, they stayed in touch. They exchanged letters, emails and phone calls, sharing their experiences of life in Pakistan and India.

Ali and Rani knew that there was a lot of misunderstanding and mistrust between the people of Pakistan and India. They understood that the media, the politicians and the extremists on both sides were only interested in promoting hate and division. They decided to do something about it.

They realised that the key to bridging the divide was communication. They believed that if the people of Pakistan and India could talk to each other, they could learn to understand and respect each other. So they set up a peace organisation called "Friends Across the Border" and started organising conferences, seminars and cultural exchanges.

Over the years, "Friends Across the Border" grew, and other peace organisations joined them. They organised peace walks, sports events, music concerts and art exhibitions. They created a network of people who believed in peace and who were willing to work together to make it happen.

The Launch of the Journal

Recently, the peace organisations of Pakistan and India launched a journal called "Cross Border Conversations". The aim of the journal is to provide a platform for people to share their views, experiences and ideas about peace and cross-border relations.

The journal has articles, interviews, case studies and opinion pieces from people from all walks of life. It covers a wide range of topics, including trade, travel, culture, politics, environment and security. The journal is published both in print and online, and it is available in both English and Urdu languages.

Case Studies

"Cross Border Conversations" has published several case studies that demonstrate the power of people-to-people communication in promoting peace and understanding.

Case Study 1: The Sari Connection

Shikha and Nida are two women from India and Pakistan respectively. They met on Facebook, where they discovered their mutual love for saris. They started sharing pictures and stories of their saris and soon became good friends.

One day, Shikha invited Nida to her home in Delhi to see her collection of saris. Nida was hesitant at first, but eventually she agreed. She travelled to Delhi, and Shikha welcomed her with open arms. She showed her the sights of the city and took her to her favourite sari shops. Nida was amazed at the hospitality and warmth of Shikha and her family.

Later, Shikha visited Nida in Lahore, and they went sari shopping there. They also visited the Golden Temple in Amritsar together. The two women have now become advocates of peace and cross-border friendship. They have organised several joint sari exhibitions in both India and Pakistan, and they have been featured in several media outlets.

Case Study 2: The Bicycle Peace Ride

A group of cyclists from Pakistan and India organised a "peace ride" from Lahore to Amritsar. They rode their bicycles for three days, stopping at villages and towns along the way. They distributed peace messages, sang songs and played games with the locals. They even had a cricket match between the two teams.

The cyclists were surprised at the amount of goodwill they received from the people they met. They were offered food, drinks and shelter by the locals. They were interviewed by local media, and their message of peace and friendship reached a wider audience.

Conclusion

The launch of "Cross Border Conversations" is a significant step towards promoting peace and understanding between the people of Pakistan and India. It is a testament to the power of people-to-people communication and the importance of creating platforms for dialogue and exchange.

Here are three key takeaways from this article:

  1. Peace can only be achieved through communication and understanding
  2. People-to-people initiatives are more effective than top-down approaches
  3. Small actions can have a big impact in promoting peace and cross-border friendship
