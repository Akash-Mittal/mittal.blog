China's Petty Geopolitics



Image Credits https://ahmedabadmirror.com/chinas-petty-geopolitics/81858646.html

Once upon a time, there was a small fishing village in the South China Sea that belonged to the Philippines. The locals went about their daily lives, fishing and farming, until one day, a group of Chinese ships arrived and claimed the entire island as their own.

This is just one example of China's petty geopolitics, where they use their economic and military power to bully and intimidate smaller countries to get what they want.

Examples of Pettypolitics in Action

In addition to the Philippines, China has ongoing disputes with Vietnam, Taiwan, Malaysia, and Indonesia over the South China Sea. They have also been known to pressure other countries to switch diplomatic recognition from Taiwan to China.

China's Belt and Road Initiative, which is an infrastructure project spanning across Asia, Africa, and Europe, has also been criticized for causing debt traps in developing countries and undermining their sovereignty.

Case Studies

The Philippines: After occupying the fishing village, China constructed military bases and airstrips in other parts of the South China Sea. In 2013, the Philippines filed a case against China in the Permanent Court of Arbitration in The Hague, which ruled in favor of the Philippines in 2016. However, China refused to recognize the ruling and continued its militarization efforts.

Taiwan: China has been putting pressure on countries to switch diplomatic recognition from Taiwan to China. In 2019, the Solomon Islands and Kiribati did just that. China also uses its economic leverage to try to isolate Taiwan from the international community, such as excluding them from the World Health Assembly during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Australia: After Australia called for an independent investigation into the origins of the COVID-19 virus, China retaliated by imposing tariffs on Australian goods, suspending imports, and encouraging boycotts of Australian products. This has had a significant impact on the Australian economy.

Conclusion

In conclusion, China's petty geopolitics is a major concern for the international community. Their use of economic and military power to bully smaller countries violates international law and undermines global stability. In order to address this issue, the following steps need to be taken:

  1. Stronger enforcement of international law and norms
  2. Greater transparency in China's foreign policy
  3. Closer cooperation among countries to counter China's aggression
