China Blocks Proposal to Blacklist Pak-Based LeT Terrorist Sajid Mir



Image Credits https://www.livemint.com/news/india/china-blocks-proposal-by-india-us-to-blacklist-pak-based-let-terrorist-and-26-11-accused-sajid-mir-11687272411744.html

Recently, India and the United States proposed to blacklist Pak-based Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT) terrorist Sajid Mir, who is also one of the prime accused in the 26/11 Mumbai terror attacks. However, China blocked the proposal citing "insufficient evidence."

This move has caused outrage among Indians and Americans alike, who believe that China is protecting a notorious terrorist who participated in the killing of innocent civilians and caused widespread destruction.

The Importance of Blacklisting Terrorists

Blacklisting a terrorist is a common practice employed by nations to counter terrorism. It involves imposing economic sanctions, travel bans, arms embargoes, and freezing of assets on individuals or entities linked to terrorism.

It sends a strong message to terrorists and their sponsors that their actions have consequences and that the international community does not tolerate such heinous acts.

Moreover, blacklisting makes it harder for terrorists to access funds and weapons, which in turn disrupts their operations and reduces their ability to inflict harm.

The Case of Sajid Mir

Sajid Mir is a notorious terrorist who belongs to the LeT, a Pakistan-based jihadist group that has been responsible for several terrorist attacks in India, including the 2008 Mumbai attacks that killed 166 people and injured over 300 others.

Mir is believed to be one of the masterminds behind the Mumbai attacks, with Indian authorities alleging that he trained and directed the 10 terrorists who carried out the strikes.

Besides the Mumbai attacks, Mir is also linked to other terror attacks in India, including the 2006 Mumbai train bombings, the 2016 Pathankot airbase attack, and the 2019 Pulwama attack.

Despite all this, China blocked India and the United States' proposal to blacklist Mir, citing insufficient evidence. This move has been criticized by many as being a blatant attempt by China to shield one of its terrorist allies.

Conclusion

  1. Blacklisting terrorists is an important step in countering terrorism as it sends a strong message to terrorists and their sponsors that their actions have consequences.
  2. Sajid Mir is a notorious terrorist who is linked to several terror attacks in India and should have been blacklisted by the international community.
  3. China's move to block the proposal to blacklist Mir has been criticized as being an attempt to protect one of its terrorist allies and is a cause for concern.
