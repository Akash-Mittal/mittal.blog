Germany Labels China as a Growing Threat to Security: Article



Image Credits https://theprint.in/world/germany-calls-china-a-growing-threat-as-it-steps-up-focus-on-security/1626640/

As Germany increases its focus on security, it has labeled China as a growing threat. The relationship between these two nations has always been somewhat complicated, but recent events have made it more tense than ever before.

The Interesting Story: The Huawei Experience

Huawei has been a global leader in telecommunications and infrastructure. In Germany, Huawei has been selected as a partner in many telecommunications projects in the past. However, in 2020, the German government changed its stance on Huawei. They declared that Huawei's involvement could in fact present a security threat. This declaration, while mainly directed at Huawei itself, is a sign of Germany's concern about China as a whole.

Examples of Growing Tension

Germany labeling China as a threat is not one-sided. China has also shown an increase in aggression towards Germany. Here are a few examples:

  • China recently imposed sanctions on German politicians and researchers over the issue of Uighur Muslims. Germany responded by labeling the sanctions as unfounded and ultimately targeted against German freedom of expression.
  • In 2019, a Hong Kong pro-democracy activist was attacked while visiting Hamburg. The activist accused the Chinese government of being involved.
  • The German military has accused China of increasing cyber attacks on their computer systems.

Case Studies of Aggression

There are a few notable cases where China's aggression has been aimed specifically at Germany, proving that the tension is not just generalized:

The Case of Tu Yoyo

Tu Yoyo was a German citizen who was detained by China while visiting her family. The charge against her was subverting state power, a common accusation for those who speak against China's censorship and human rights violations. Germany demanded her release and called China out for their inhumane treatment of people.

The Case of Deutsche Welle

Deutsche Welle, the German news outlet, was banned in China in retaliation for their critical reporting on the Chinese government. The German government denounced this prohibition and accused China of trying to control the narrative and suppress free speech.

Conclusion: Germany's Concerns on China

Based on the examples and case studies discussed above, it is clear that Germany's labeling of China as a threat to security is not unfounded. The Huawei controversy was merely a symptom of a larger issue that has been brewing between these two nations. While tension between countries is not uncommon, Germany's concern about China's actions goes far beyond what can be classified as a mere diplomatic disagreement. The three main points of concern are:

  1. China's human rights violations and censorship that affect German citizens and companies
  2. China's cyber-attacks and espionage against German infrastructure and institutions
  3. China's attempt to control the narrative and suppress free speech in Germany and other countries
