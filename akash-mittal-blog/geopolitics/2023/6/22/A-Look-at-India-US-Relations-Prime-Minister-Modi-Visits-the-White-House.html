A Look at India-US Relations: Prime Minister Modi Visits the White House



Image Credits https://www.brookings.edu/podcast-episode/a-look-at-india-us-relations-as-prime-minister-modi-visits-the-white-house/

It's a sunny day in Washington, D.C. and the White House is buzzing with excitement as it prepares to welcome one of the most influential leaders of the world, Prime Minister Narendra Modi of India. The visit is highly anticipated and the media is abuzz with speculation about what the two leaders will discuss during their meeting. While there is much to talk about, the focus is on strengthening the relationship between the two countries, which has been historically complex.

India and the United States have a long and complicated history, dating back to India's independence from British rule in 1947. The two nations have shared a rollercoaster ride of cooperation and competition, with occasional clashes over geopolitical issues.

However, in recent years, the relationship has been on an upswing. As the world's two largest democracies, India and the US have many shared values and interests. The Modi government has been proactive in reaching out to the US, both diplomatically and economically. The US, on its part, has recognized India's growing importance in the world and has been keen to deepen its strategic partnership with India.

Examples of India-US Cooperation

India and the US have collaborated on many fronts over the years, ranging from security to technology. Here are a few examples of the cooperation between the two nations:

  • Defense: India is one of the largest buyers of US defense equipment, with the US being India's second-largest arms supplier. The two nations have also conducted several military exercises together, including the Malabar Exercise that also involves Japan. In 2016, India was designated as a 'Major Defense Partner' of the US, which paved the way for closer defense ties between the two nations.
  • Economy: The US is India's second-largest trading partner, with the bilateral trade between the two countries reaching $149 billion in 2019. The two nations have also signed several economic agreements, including the US-India Business Council and the US-India Strategic Partnership Forum.
  • Technology: India and the US have partnered in several tech-driven initiatives, including the Digital India program and the Startup India initiative. The US has also been a leading destination for Indian students, with over 200,000 Indian students currently studying in the US.

Case Studies of India-US Relations

Let's take a look at a few case studies that highlight the India-US relationship:

  • The Civil Nuclear Deal: The India-US Civil Nuclear Agreement, signed in 2008, was a major breakthrough in India-US relations. The deal allowed India access to civilian nuclear technology and fuel, which had been denied to India due to its nuclear weapons program. The deal faced strong opposition from some US lawmakers and non-proliferation advocates, but it was eventually passed by the US Congress and paved the way for closer nuclear cooperation between the two nations.
  • Terrorism: India and the US have had a shared interest in combating terrorism. The 2008 Mumbai attacks, which killed 166 people including six Americans, brought the two nations even closer. Indian intelligence agencies have been cooperating with their US counterparts, and the US has designated several Pakistan-based terror groups as 'Foreign Terrorist Organizations.'
  • Climate Change: Climate change has been a major area of cooperation between India and the US. During the Obama administration, India and the US signed the Paris Climate Agreement, which aims to reduce global greenhouse gas emissions. However, the Trump administration withdrew the US from the agreement, causing some strain in the India-US relationship.

Conclusion: The Future of India-US Relations

The relationship between India and the US is complex, but it has the potential to be transformative. As the world's two largest democracies, India and the US share many values and interests. However, the relationship is not without its challenges, especially with the US-China rivalry and the increasing polarization of US politics. Nevertheless, there are a few key takeaways:

  • Strategic Partnership: India and the US have a strategic partnership that covers a wide range of areas, from defense to technology. Both nations have expressed a desire to deepen this partnership, and it is important for both nations to work towards this goal.
  • Mutual Benefits: India-US relations should be based on mutual benefits, rather than just geopolitical considerations. Both nations have much to gain from closer cooperation, and this should be the driving force behind the relationship.
  • Multilateralism: India and the US should work together to promote multilateralism and international institutions. As the world becomes more interconnected, it is important for nations to work together to address global challenges.
