China Fashion Giant Shein To Return To India On Stringent Terms



Image Credits https://www.ndtv.com/india-news/shein-deal-to-re-enter-india-comes-with-strict-licensing-rules-4140397

Once upon a time, Shein was one of the most loved fashion e-commerce platforms in India. The brand provided affordable and trendy clothes, shoes, accessories, and beauty products to young customers across the country. It offered a wide range of styles and sizes, making it popular among plus-size people as well. However, in June 2020, the Indian government banned Shein and 58 other Chinese apps, citing national security and privacy concerns. This move was a significant blow to Shein's business since India was one of its largest markets.

After almost a year, there is good news for Shein's fans in India. According to a report by Business Standard, the brand is set to make a comeback in the country, but under strict conditions. Shein has tied up with local logistics partners, which will enable it to comply with the Indian government's regulations on data storage and sharing. This move shows that Shein is serious about regaining its status in the Indian market, which is expected to touch $19.6 billion by 2024, according to a report by India Brand Equity Foundation (IBEF).

Examples of Shein's Popularity in India

At its peak, Shein had over 10 million downloads in India and was the most downloaded shopping app on the Apple App Store and Google Play Store, according to a report by Yahoo News India. The brand's success can be attributed to its low prices, fast shipping, and social media marketing strategy. Shein collaborated with popular Indian influencers, fashion bloggers, and content creators to promote its products to their large followings. This led to a surge in sales and a loyal customer base.

Case Studies on Shein's Resurgence in Other Countries

Shein's comeback strategy is not limited to India. The brand has successfully returned to other markets that previously banned it, such as Indonesia and the United States. In 2019, Shein was temporarily banned in Indonesia for selling clothes that were deemed offensive and disrespectful to the country's flag. The brand apologized and removed the products from its website, and worked with the Indonesian government to resolve the issue. Shein also opened a physical store in Jakarta, which allowed customers to try on clothes and shoes before purchasing them online. This move helped Shein regain its popularity and trust among Indonesian customers.

A similar trend was seen in the US market, where Shein was banned by the US Customs and Border Protection (CBP) for importing clothes made from forced labor in Xinjiang, China. Shein denied the allegations and provided evidence of its supply chain's compliance with international labor laws. It also launched a sustainable fashion line called Shein Premium, which uses eco-friendly fabrics and production methods. Shein's transparent communication and commitment to ethical practices helped it overcome the ban and regain its position as a top fashion e-commerce platform in the US.

Conclusion

Shein's return to India is a much-awaited event for its loyal customers, who have missed the brand's affordable and fashionable products. However, the brand will need to work hard to regain the trust of Indian consumers, who are becoming increasingly conscious of data privacy and security. Shein's tie-up with local logistics partners is a step in the right direction, as it shows its commitment to complying with the Indian government's regulations. Shein can also leverage its previous success and social media presence to promote its products to Indian customers.
