The Historic Speech by Indian PM Modi at US Congress



Image Credits https://www.foreignbrief.com/daily-news/indian-pm-modi-addresses-us-congress/

It was a day of great significance and pride for every Indian when Prime Minister Narendra Modi addressed the joint session of the US Congress on June 8, 2016. As PM Modi stood at the podium in the US Capitol, he not only represented the world's largest democracy, but also highlighted the strong bonds of friendship between India and the US.

PM Modi's speech covered a wide range of issues including defense, terrorism, climate change, and India's economy. He stressed the importance of shared values and cooperation between the two countries. Let us delve deeper and analyze some of the key points made by PM Modi that day.

Defense

During his speech, PM Modi emphasized the need for strong defense ties between India and the US. He highlighted the rapidly changing security environment in the Indo-Pacific region and the common challenges faced by both countries.

PM Modi also mentioned the Defense Technology and Trade Initiative (DTTI) between India and the US, which was launched in 2012. This initiative aims to co-develop and co-produce defense technology and equipment. PM Modi stressed the importance of expediting the DTTI and other defense cooperation mechanisms between the two countries.

Terrorism

PM Modi did not mince his words when he talked about the global menace of terrorism. He said, "Terrorism has become its own ideology, which is very dangerous." He emphasized the need for a united global fight against terrorism and the importance of isolating those who support and fund terrorism.

He also talked about surgical strikes conducted by Indian armed forces against terrorist camps in Pakistan-administered Kashmir in 2016. These strikes were in response to a terrorist attack on an Indian army base in Uri, Jammu and Kashmir, which claimed the lives of 19 Indian soldiers. PM Modi asserted that India would never forget the sacrifice of the brave soldiers who laid down their lives for the country.

Climate Change

PM Modi highlighted the urgent need for collective action to address climate change. He outlined India's ambitious target of generating 40% of the country's total power capacity from non-fossil fuel sources by 2030. He also talked about the International Solar Alliance (ISA), a group of more than 121 countries led by India, which aims to promote solar energy deployment across the globe.

PM Modi announced that India would ratify the Paris Agreement on climate change on October 2, 2016, the birth anniversary of Mahatma Gandhi. This move was significant as it would help bring the agreement closer to entering into force.

Case Studies

PM Modi's speech was not just rhetoric, it was backed by concrete examples and case studies. He talked about the transformation taking place in India's economy and the initiatives taken by his government to make India a global manufacturing and investment hub.

He mentioned the 'Make in India' campaign, which aims to promote manufacturing in India and create employment opportunities for the country's youth. He also talked about the Digital India initiative, which aims to provide digital infrastructure and services to all citizens of India. These initiatives have played a key role in attracting foreign investment to India.

  1. PM Modi's speech at the US Congress was historic and significant, as it highlighted the strong ties between India and the US.
  2. PM Modi's speech covered a wide range of issues, including defense, terrorism, climate change, and India's economy.
  3. The speech was backed by concrete examples and case studies, demonstrating the progress India has made under PM Modi's leadership.
