The Controversial Chinese Firm: An article on its No Extramarital Affairs or Divorce Policy



Image Credits https://www.businesstoday.in/latest/world/story/no-extramarital-affairs-or-divorce-chinese-firm-threatens-to-fire-employees-issues-new-rule-386036-2023-06-18

Interesting Story

The Chinese firm, Hangzhou Shunwang Technology, recently issued a new rule that strictly prohibits its employees from indulging in extramarital affairs or getting divorced. The company has threatened to fire employees who do not follow the policy, which has triggered a huge controversy in China.

According to the company's spokesperson, this new rule was implemented to maintain a healthy and stable work environment. The spokesperson also claimed that the policy was in line with China's traditional cultural values, where marriage and family are considered important.

Examples

Hangzhou Shunwang Technology is not the only Chinese company that has implemented such policies. In 2018, a company in southwestern China's Sichuan province announced that it would give a bonus to employees who got married and penalize those who got divorced. Similarly, a company in eastern China's Jiangsu province, in 2015, ordered its employees to get married or face a deductions in their bonuses.

Case Studies

The implementation of such policies has raised several concerns, including privacy invasion and discrimination. Moreover, it raises the question of whether it is the company's place to dictate the personal lives of its employees.

A case in point is that of Guangzhou Yuexiu Financial Holdings Group, which was criticized for its policy that allowed only married people with children to be promoted. The company was sued for discrimination and eventually had to abandon its policy.

Conclusion

  1. The implementation of such policies can have negative consequences, including legal implications.
  2. Companies must recognize that personal lives and relationships cannot be "regulated" by the company.
  3. The focus should be on creating a healthy and supportive work environment that respects employees' personal lives and choices.
