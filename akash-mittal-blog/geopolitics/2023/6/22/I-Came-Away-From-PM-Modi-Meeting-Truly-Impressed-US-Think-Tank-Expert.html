It was nothing but a Late-Night Call That Came in as a Surprise:

It was a regular afternoon as usual, and I was wrapping up my work to get a good night's sleep. It was then that my phone rang and displayed "Unknown Number." I hesitated at first, but soon the curiosity took over, and I answered the call. To my surprise, it was somebody from the Indian Embassy, enquiring if I was interested in meeting Mr. Narendra Modi, the Prime Minister of India, who was on his US visit. I was thrilled and surprised at the same time as it was nothing less than a dream-come-true opportunity for me as a foreign policy analyst and a research fellow at a renowned US Think Tank.

India-US Relations:

It is not an unknown fact that India and the US, two of the world's largest democracies, share a strong bond. The relationship between the countries has seen an upward trajectory for the last few decades, which peaked during the tenure of the Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi. Mr. Modi's visit to the US, meeting with the US President, and delivering speeches addressing Indians living in America, have made a significant impact on the relationship between the countries and have received immense media attention.

The Importance of Think-Tanks:

Think-tanks are an integral part of the political landscape, and they play an essential role in shaping public policies and debates. The Think Tank that I am a part of is well-known for its research and advocacy, and we have played a significant role in shaping the public discourse on foreign policy issues, including India-US relations and regional security dynamics. We often interact with policymakers, the diplomats, and other stakeholders to draw their attention to credible foreign policy perspectives on various issues.

Acknowledging the Prime Minister's Vision and Leadership:

The meeting with the Prime Minister was a surreal experience for me. Even though I am an Indian-American, it was my first time attending a high-level meeting with the Prime Minister of India. He was incredibly approachable and had a clear vision of India's future, which he articulated passionately. The Prime Minister was well-prepared, and he addressed the issues that were of concern to the think tank, including India's role in the Indo-Pacific, regional security dynamics, bilateral trade, and the global political situation. He shared his thoughts on how India could contribute to creating an open, inclusive, and cooperative world order that would be beneficial to all.

Strengthening the Relationship between India and The United States:

The Prime Minister's visit to the US and his interaction with various stakeholders, including the Indian diaspora, leading tech CEOs, and political representatives, have contributed immensely to strengthening the relationship between India and the United States. His visit also marks the renewed focus on economic and strategic cooperation, particularly in the areas of defence, trade, and energy. The think tank that I am a part of has been of great help in establishing a sustained and constructive dialogue between India and the United States, focusing on areas of mutual interest and shared values.

Conclusions:

We live in a dynamic world where the complexity and diversity of our challenges require innovative and pragmatic solutions. As foreign policy specialists, we are committed to supporting constructive and impactful policies that address the world's most pressing issues. Meeting with the Indian Prime Minister has provided us with a clearer understanding of the challenges faced by the Indian Government and its vision for the future. Here are three key takeaways from my meeting with the Prime Minister:

  • India's commitment to an open, inclusive, and cooperative world order is commendable and deserves to be supported;
  • The Indo-US relationship is critical to achieving our shared objectives of global peace, security, and prosperity;
  • Foreign policy continuity between India and the United States is essential, regardless of political differences.
