From Laggard to Leader: Bangladesh Achieves Remarkable Growth in SDG Index



Image Credits https://www.tbsnews.net/economy/sdg-index-2023-bangladesh-ahead-india-pakistan-654006

We often come across success stories of people or nations who turned the tables overnight with grit, determination, and hard work. Bangladesh is one such nation that has set an inspiring example by racing ahead of its neighbors, India, and Pakistan, in the SDG Index 2023. Notably, in the last five years, Bangladesh has witnessed a steep rise in its SDG scores, making it one of the fastest-growing countries in the world in terms of sustainable development. So, what has been the driving force behind this transformative journey? Let's explore.

Examples of Bangladesh's Remarkable Growth in SDG Index

SDG Index measures the progress of a country towards Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) which encompass 17 goals and 169 targets laid out by the United Nations. In recent years, Bangladesh has shown remarkable progress in several SDGs. Here are a few examples:

  • Goal 1: No Poverty - Bangladesh succeeded in reducing extreme poverty rate by 20% from 2011 to 2018. A recent report by the World Bank estimates that the overall poverty rate in the country dropped to 24.3% in 2020, from a staggering 44.2% in 1991.
  • Goal 2: Zero Hunger - Bangladesh's agriculture sector has emerged as a major contributor to food security. Between 2012 and 2020, the country's agricultural production increased by 16%, with a 20% increase in rice production, the staple crop of the country.
  • Goal 5: Gender Equality - Bangladesh has made remarkable progress in addressing gender inequality in education enrollment, workforce participation, and political representation. The country's female workforce participation increased from 29% (in 2000) to 36% (in 2020), while the number of female MPs in parliament reached 21% (in 2019). Bangladesh is now ranked 50th globally in gender parity (according to the Global Gender Gap Index 2020).

Case Studies on Bangladesh's SDG Achievements

Several initiatives have contributed to Bangladesh's SDG achievements. Here are two case studies:

Agriculture Extension Services - A Key to Zero Hunger Goal

The government of Bangladesh has implemented a nationwide Agriculture Extension Services program that provides technical education and agricultural training to farmers. The program, launched in the 1980s, has been instrumental in improving crop yields, reducing crop-related risks, and addressing food insecurity in the country. By 2015, over 12 million farmers had been trained under the program, leading to a significant increase in rice, wheat, and vegetable production in the country.

Women Empowerment through Microfinance - A Stepping Stone to Gender Equality

The Grameen Bank, founded by Nobel laureate Muhammad Yunus, is a pioneer of microfinance in Bangladesh. The bank offers small loans to poor women in rural areas, enabling them to start small businesses and become financially independent. The bank has disbursed over $30 billion loans since it was founded, with a repayment rate of over 98%. The small loans have helped reduce poverty levels and have given rural women much-needed financial autonomy, contributing to their empowerment and gender equality.

Conclusion in Three Points

In conclusion, Bangladesh's remarkable growth in the SDG Index has been a result of a concerted effort by the government, NGOs, civil society, and the private sector. The country has shown that even with scarce resources and limited opportunities, a nation can still achieve unimaginable progress through effective governance, policy implementation, and a strong commitment to sustainable development. Here are three takeaway points:

  1. Effective Governance - Bangladesh's progress in SDG index is a testament to the country's improved governance, social policies, and economic planning, which have created an enabling environment for sustainable development.
  2. Collaborative Efforts - Bangladesh's success is the result of collaborative efforts between the government, private sector, and civil society that have worked together to address social, political, and economic challenges, and to achieve the SDGs.
  3. Micro-level Interventions - Bangladesh's micro-level interventions, such as agricultural extension services, microfinance, and women empowerment, have been instrumental in achieving specific SDGs and have created a ripple effect in reducing poverty, hunger, and gender inequality.
