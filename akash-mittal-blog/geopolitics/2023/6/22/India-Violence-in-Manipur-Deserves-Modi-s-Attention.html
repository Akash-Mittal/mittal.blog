India: Violence in Manipur Deserves Modi's Attention



Image Credits https://foreignpolicy.com/2023/06/13/india-manipur-ethnic-violence-modi-china-myanmar/

On a warm summer evening, a group of young boys were playing cricket in a small village in Manipur. Suddenly, the peace was shattered as a loud explosion ripped through the air. The boys quickly scattered as bullets began to rain down on them. The sound of gunfire echoed through the village as the armed men stormed in, firing indiscriminately.

This is a scene that has been repeated all too often in Manipur, a state in northeastern India that has been plagued by violence for decades. The region is home to a number of insurgent groups that have been fighting for autonomy or independence from India. These groups have engaged in not only armed conflict with the Indian government but also inter-tribal clashes that have resulted in countless deaths and injuries.

Examples of Violence in Manipur

One of the deadliest incidents in recent history occurred in 2018, when a bomb exploded at a market in Imphal, the capital city of Manipur. The bomb was planted by a militant group and killed two people, injuring many more.

In 2019, a group of armed militants attacked a convoy of security forces in Chandel district, killing at least four soldiers and injuring many more.

These incidents are just the tip of the iceberg, however. The violence in Manipur has been ongoing for decades and has resulted in the deaths of thousands of people.

Case Studies

The Extrajudicial Killings of 2009

In July 2009, an incident occurred that brought the issue of human rights abuses in Manipur to the forefront of national attention. A group of security forces killed several people in an alleged encounter with militants. However, it was later revealed that the victims were actually innocent civilians who had been picked up arbitrarily by the security forces. The incident, known as the "Manipur Fake Encounter Killings," sparked widespread protests and demands for justice.

The Death of Irom Sharmila

Irom Sharmila was a Manipuri activist who went on a hunger strike for over 16 years to protest against the Armed Forces Special Powers Act (AFSPA), which gives the security forces immunity from prosecution for human rights violations. Although her hunger strike gained worldwide attention, it ultimately failed to bring about the change she was seeking. Sharmila ended her fast in 2016 and later entered politics, but her efforts to repeal AFSPA have been largely unsuccessful.

Why Modi Needs to Address the Violence in Manipur

  1. The violence in Manipur has a devastating impact on the lives of the people who live there, particularly those who are caught in the crossfire or who have lost loved ones. It is crucial that the Indian government takes steps to protect the human rights of all people in the region.
  2. Manipur's economy has been severely impacted by the violence, with many businesses shutting down and investors hesitant to invest in the region. This has resulted in high levels of unemployment and poverty, exacerbating the already dire situation in the state.
  3. The ongoing violence in Manipur poses a threat not only to the people of the region but to national security as well. The state is located in a strategic location bordering Myanmar and is home to several insurgent groups that pose a threat to India's national security. Addressing the violence in Manipur is therefore not only a humanitarian issue but also a matter of national security.
