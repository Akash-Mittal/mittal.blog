Bangladesh's Advancement on HDI Parameters



Image Credits https://www.weeklyblitz.net/news/bangladesh-has-forged-way-ahead-on-parameters-of-hdi-compared-to-most-countries-including-india-jitendra-kumar-ojha/

Once considered a "basket case" for its chronic poverty and hunger, Bangladesh has made a remarkable turnaround in recent years. It has consistently outperformed many of its South Asian neighbors, including India, on Human Development Index (HDI) parameters such as life expectancy, literacy, and poverty reduction. How did Bangladesh achieve this progress, and what lessons can other countries learn?

To answer these questions, we need to look beyond the numbers and statistics and explore the human factors behind this development. It's a story of resilience, innovation, and community empowerment.

Examples

Let's start with some examples of how Bangladesh has moved ahead on HDI parameters:

  • Life Expectancy: Bangladesh's life expectancy has risen from 45 in the 1970s to over 72 now, surpassing India's average of 69. This is due to a range of factors such as improved healthcare, sanitation, and nutrition. For instance, the government's community health workers have played a critical role in reducing child mortality by providing vaccinations, maternal care, and nutrition advice.
  • Literacy: Bangladesh has achieved nearly universal primary education, with 98.5% of children enrolled in school, compared to India's 93%. The government's innovative program of "non-formal education" has also made strides in teaching basic literacy and life skills to underprivileged adults and children who missed out on formal education.
  • Poverty Reduction: Bangladesh has reduced its poverty rate from over 80% in the 1970s to under 20% now, largely due to its vibrant microfinance industry that has empowered millions of poor women to start their own businesses and break the cycle of dependency. Organizations such as Grameen Bank and BRAC have helped to create a culture of entrepreneurship and self-reliance.

Case Studies

Let's now look at some case studies that illustrate the factors behind Bangladesh's progress:

Community Empowerment

One of the key drivers of Bangladesh's success has been its emphasis on community empowerment, particularly of women. This is evidenced by the work of Grameen Bank, which was founded by economist Muhammad Yunus in 1983. Grameen Bank pioneered the concept of "microcredit", which involves providing small loans to poor women to start businesses.

Since then, Grameen Bank has helped millions of women to become entrepreneurs and break the cycle of poverty. For instance, one such woman is Hosne Ara, who started a small poultry business with a loan of $120. Today, she earns $1,000 a year from her business, which has enabled her to send her children to school and improve her family's living standards. Hosne Ara's story is just one of many such success stories that demonstrate the power of community empowerment and entrepreneurship.

Innovation

Another factor behind Bangladesh's progress has been its innovative solutions to development challenges. One such solution is the government's non-formal education program, which targets the millions of adults and children who have missed out on formal education. This program uses a range of innovative teaching methods such as visual aids, storytelling, and games to make learning fun and accessible.

As a result of this program, many rural women like Fazila Khatun have been able to learn basic literacy and numeracy skills that have improved their livelihoods and boosted their confidence. Fazila, who was married at the age of 14 and never attended school, learned how to read and write through this program and is now able to communicate with her clients and manage her own small business selling vegetables.

Resilience

Finally, Bangladesh's progress is a story of resilience in the face of natural disasters and other challenges. Bangladesh is one of the most disaster-prone countries in the world, with frequent cyclones, floods, and droughts. However, the government and the community have developed innovative approaches to coping with these disasters, such as early warning systems, flood-resistant housing, and disaster preparedness training.

For instance, after the devastating Cyclone Sidr in 2007, which killed over 3,000 people, the government and NGOs worked together to provide emergency relief and long-term recovery support. They provided shelter, food, and medical care to the affected communities and also helped them to rebuild their homes and businesses.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Bangladesh has made remarkable progress on HDI parameters due to a range of factors such as community empowerment, innovation, and resilience. The country has overcome many challenges such as poverty, illiteracy, and natural disasters through a combination of government policies, community initiatives, and NGO support.

The lessons that other countries can learn from Bangladesh's success are to invest in human development, particularly in healthcare, education, and entrepreneurship, and to empower communities, particularly women, to lead their own development. These factors have contributed to Bangladesh's turnaround and can serve as models for other countries seeking to achieve similar progress.

Key Takeaways

  1. Community empowerment, particularly of women, is a key driver of Bangladesh's success.
  2. Innovation in development solutions such as non-formal education has helped to reach marginalized groups.
  3. Resilience in the face of natural disasters and other challenges has been critical to Bangladesh's progress.
