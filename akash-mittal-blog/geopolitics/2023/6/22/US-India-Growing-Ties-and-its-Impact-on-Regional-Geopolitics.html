US-India Growing Ties and its Impact on Regional Geopolitics



Image Credits https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XE1c5vUELrI

Introduction

When it comes to international geopolitics, relationships between countries can have a domino effect on other nations in that region. The US-India relationship has grown significantly and has the potential to impact not only the two countries' economies but also the political dynamics of neighboring regions. Let me tell you a story.

Once upon a time, India was under pressure from its neighboring country China, as the latter tried to assert its dominance in the region. But the US saw India as a strategic ally in countering China's growing influence. The US provided training to India's military, while India opened up its markets to US companies. This was the beginning of a beautiful friendship.

Examples of US-India Growing Ties

The US and India have begun to work together on various issues such as counter-terrorism, cybersecurity, trade and commerce, education, and renewable energy solutions. For instance, US President Joe Biden on his first day in office signed an executive order to rejoin the Paris Climate Agreement, which India has been a part of since its inception in 2015. Together, they can motivate other countries to follow the climate accord.

Another example is the Quad, a strategic alliance between the United States, India, Japan, and Australia. The main goal of the Quad is to promote a free and open Indo-Pacific region, with a focus on trade, security, and infrastructure development. The group has held several meetings to discuss issues such as the COVID-19 pandemic, climate change, and regional security concerns.

Case Studies

Impact on the South Asian Region

The US-India alliance has had a significant impact on the geopolitics of the South Asian region. Pakistan, India's arch-rival, feels threatened by the alliance and its implications. Pakistan has asked China for help in countering this sensitive situation.

However, the US and India's partnership has been beneficial for both countries as they have strengthened economic, political, and military ties. The US has helped India with modernizing its military and has provided intelligence sharing to counter terrorism, while India has opened up its markets to US companies. The two countries share a common goal to counter China's domination in the region.

Impact on the Indo-Pacific Region

The US-India partnership has become a crucial part of the US' Indo-Pacific strategy. The region has become a crucial battleground for the world's superpowers, as China aggressively tries to assert its control over the South China Sea.

The Quad, which includes India, has been formed in response to China's aggressive behavior. The infrastructure projects in the region and, by extension, China's Belt and Road Initiative allow China to create a trade route without interference. The Quad countries, including India, have proposed to build an alternative network of infrastructure to counter China's Belt and Road Initiative.

Conclusion in Three Points

  1. The US-India relationship can significantly impact the geopolitical dynamics of neighboring regions.
  2. The US-India partnership has strengthened economic, political, and military ties, countered terrorism and climate change, and motivated other countries to follow suit.
  3. The partnership between the US and India is essential in countering China's growing influence in the Indo-Pacific region.
