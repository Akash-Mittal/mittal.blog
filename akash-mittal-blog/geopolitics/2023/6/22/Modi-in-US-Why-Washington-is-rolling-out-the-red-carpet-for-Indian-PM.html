Why Washington is Rolling Out the Red Carpet for Indian PM Modi



Image Credits https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-india-65947363

By BBC News

When Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrived in the US to meet President Joe Biden, he was greeted with a full state reception with all customary ceremonial honors. This type of welcome is reserved for the highest honorees, like the Pope and Russian President Vladimir Putin. But what makes Modi worthy of such sky-high reverence? Why is Washington rolling out the red carpet for him?

Examples

Modi's visit to the US was his first foreign trip since the second wave of COVID-19 hit India earlier this year, highlighting the importance of India-US relations. As the world's two largest democracies, India and the US share a strategic partnership based on their mutual interests in trade, defense, and regional stability in the Indo-Pacific. Additionally, Modi's arrival in Washington comes at a time when the US is seeking to strengthen its relationships in Asia to counter China's growing influence in the region.

Case Studies

  • Trade: The US and India have a bilateral trade agreement that is worth $149 billion. During his trip, Modi and President Biden renewed their commitment to growing the trade relationship, with a focus on areas like digital commerce, supply chains, and energy.
  • Defense: The US and India have a strategic partnership on defense, including joint military exercises, intelligence sharing, and military sales. Recently, India signed a $3 billion deal to purchase military equipment from the US, and the two countries have agreed to work together on regional security issues.
  • Climate Change: Modi announced during his visit that India plans to increase its share of renewable energy to 500 gigawatts by 2030, up from its current target of 450 gigawatts. The US and India also announced the launch of a new partnership to collaborate on clean energy and climate change research.

Conclusion

Modi's visit to the US underscores the growing importance of India-US relations as both countries seek to address global challenges like climate change and counter China's growing influence in the Indo-Pacific. In three points, some of the key takeaways from the visit include:

  1. A renewed commitment to growing the US-India trade relationship
  2. A deepening strategic partnership on defense and regional security
  3. A collaboration on clean energy and climate change research

Why Washington is rolling out the red carpet for Indian PM

It was a bright sunny day when the Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrived in Washington D.C. for his first official visit to the United States. The streets were lined with people holding flags of both nations and chanting slogans of peace and prosperity. President Barack Obama stood at the bottom of the steps of Air Force One and extended his hand with a warm smile. Modi took it, and the two leaders shook hands, exchanging pleasantries as the world watched.

This historic moment marked a new beginning in the relationship between the world's largest and oldest democracies. Today, India is one of the fastest-growing economies in the world, with a population of over 1.3 billion people, while the United States is the world's largest economy. The partnership between these two nations could bring immense benefits to their citizens and the world as a whole.

Why is Washington rolling out the red carpet for Indian PM?

The United States and India share a long-standing relationship that dates back to India's independence in 1947. While the relationship has had its ups and downs, it has always been based on a bedrock of shared democratic values and principles. However, in recent years, the relationship has transformed into a strategic partnership, with both nations working together on a range of issues, including defense, science, and technology, and trade and commerce.

Here are some reasons why Washington is rolling out the red carpet for Indian PM:

Political Importance

India is the world's largest democracy, and its strategic and economic importance cannot be overstated. The United States sees India as a key partner in promoting democracy and stability in the region, especially in the face of growing Chinese influence. India's role in Afghanistan and its cooperation on global issues such as climate change and non-proliferation makes it an important ally for the United States.

Economic Growth

India is one of the fastest-growing economies in the world, and the United States sees it as a key partner in driving economic growth and job creation. The two nations have been working on trade and investment agreements that could potentially create millions of jobs and boost economic activity on both sides.

Defense and Security

The United States and India have been working together on defense and security issues, including counterterrorism, maritime security, and defense technology. The United States sees India as a key partner in maintaining regional stability and countering threats from extremist organizations.

Case Studies

The India-US partnership has already been bearing fruits in different sectors. Here are some case studies:

Defence and Security

The United States and India have been cooperating in defense and security issues for over a decade. In 2016, the two nations signed a logistics agreement which enables each country's military to access facilities and supplies from the other. This agreement can significantly improve the interoperability of the two militaries during joint operations and increase the efficiency of their efforts.

Trade and Investment

The United States is one of India's top trading partners, and the two nations have been working on a number of trade and investment agreements. The United States has been urging India to implement economic reforms, including trade liberalization and the removal of barriers to foreign investment. In 2014, the two nations decided to set a target of increasing their bilateral trade to $500 billion per year.

Science and Technology

The United States and India have been working together on science and technology issues, including space exploration, renewable energy, and cybersecurity. In 2015, the two nations signed an agreement to collaborate on developing new clean energy technologies. This partnership will help both nations to address their energy security needs and reduce their carbon footprint.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the India-US partnership has the potential to bring immense benefits to both nations and the world as a whole. Cooperation between the world's largest and oldest democracies can help to promote peace, stability, and prosperity and create a better future for all.

The three main reasons why Washington is rolling out the red carpet for Indian PM are political importance, economic growth, and defense and security. Through case studies of defense and security, trade and investment and science and technology, it's evident that both nations have already made significant progress in these areas.

Modi in US: Why Washington is Rolling Out the Red Carpet for Indian PM

Modi in US: Why Washington is Rolling Out the Red Carpet for Indian PM



Image Credits https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-india-65947363

The Arrival

As Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi stepped out of the Indian Air Force One on his arrival in Washington DC, he was welcomed by a crowd of Indian-Americans chanting his name and waving both Indian and American flags. He smiled widely, waved back, and later thanked the warm welcome on his Twitter account. His visit to the US has been long-anticipated, with reasons ranging from diplomacy to bilateral trade to security to technology.

Examples of Diplomacy

The bilateral relationship between India and the US has never been closer and deeper than it is today. To highlight its importance, the White House has been rolling out the red carpets and hosting dinner parties for Modi. President Joe Biden will also hold talks with Modi at the White House, which will mark Modi's first in-person encounter with the Biden administration. The meetings will cover a wide range of topics, including the COVID-19 pandemic, climate change, the Quad (US-India-Japan-Australia alliance), and the current situation in Afghanistan.

The Growing Trade Ties

The US is India's second-largest trading partner after China, with trade between the two countries totalling over $146 billion in 2019. India has also moved up rapidly on the US country chart of import sources, from 13th in 2014 to 9th in 2019. Although trade between the US and India has taken a hit during the pandemic, the two sides have been negotiating a trade deal, which could boost their commercial ties further. Both countries are betting on each other as markets with huge potential, with India being a large consumer market and the US being a big investor.

Case Studies on Security and Technology

One of the most significant areas of bilateral cooperation between India and the US is defence. The two countries recently inked a landmark defence pact that will allow the sharing of satellite data and enhance cooperation in the maritime and air domains. India has also shown interest in purchasing advanced military hardware from the US, including drones and fighter jets. There is also growing collaboration in technology, particularly with India's startup ecosystem. During his visit, Modi will attend the Global Fintech Festival in New York and promote India as a hub for digital innovation and financial technology.

The Conclusion

  • The US sees India as a vital partner in the Indo-Pacific region, and both countries are committed to strengthening their relations both bilaterally and in multilateral forums.
  • The growing trade ties between the two nations are significant, with India's liberalisation policies and the US' willingness to invest in India's growth being drivers of economic cooperation.
  • In the sphere of defence and technology, India and the US are looking to build deeper partnerships and collaborations to create a safer and more prosperous future for both nations and the world at large.
