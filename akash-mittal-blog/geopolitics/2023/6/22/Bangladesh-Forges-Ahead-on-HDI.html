Bangladesh Forges Ahead on HDI



Image Credits https://www.weeklyblitz.net/news/bangladesh-has-forged-way-ahead-on-parameters-of-hdi-compared-to-most-countries-including-india-jitendra-kumar-ojha/

Once upon a time, Bangladesh was considered one of the poorest countries in the world. It was a land where famine, natural disasters, and political strife dominated the headlines. However, over the past few decades, the country has made significant strides in areas such as education, healthcare, and economic growth. These efforts have led to impressive gains in human development, as measured by the Human Development Index (HDI).

The HDI is a composite indicator of human development that is based on three dimensions of well-being: a long and healthy life, access to knowledge, and a decent standard of living. By looking at these measures, we can better understand how countries are improving the lives of their citizens over time.

Examples of Progress in Bangladesh

Here are just a few examples of how Bangladesh has improved its HDI ranking:

  • Life expectancy has increased from 50 years in 1971 to 72 years in 2019.
  • The percentage of people living in extreme poverty has decreased from 44% in 1990 to 14% in 2018.
  • Literacy rates for adults age 15 and over have increased from 25% in 1974 to 73% in 2019.
  • Gross National Income (GNI) per capita has increased from $360 in 1990 to $1,890 in 2019.

Case Studies

To understand the impact of these improvements, let's look at a few case studies:

Shafiqul Islam: A Farmer in Rural Bangladesh

Shafiqul Islam is a farmer who lives in a rural village in Bangladesh. A few years ago, he struggled to earn enough money to support his family and send his children to school. However, he was able to access a small business loan through a government program. With this money, he purchased new seeds, fertilizer, and a small irrigation system for his farm. As a result, his crop yields increased substantially, and he was able to sell his rice, vegetables, and eggs at a higher price in the local market. With the extra income, he was able to invest in his children's education and improve his family's standard of living.

Sumaiya Shefa: A College Student in Dhaka

Sumaiya Shefa is a college student who lives in Dhaka, the capital city of Bangladesh. She is studying to become a doctor, and she hopes to work in a rural area where access to healthcare is limited. As a child, Sumaiya was able to attend school thanks to a government program that provided free textbooks and a stipend for poor families. Without these resources, she might not have been able to continue her education. By investing in education, Bangladesh has been able to create more opportunities for students like Sumaiya, who can now pursue their dreams and contribute to the country's development.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Bangladesh has made impressive progress in improving the well-being of its citizens. By investing in education, healthcare, and economic growth, the country has been able to reduce poverty, increase literacy rates, and improve life expectancy. Although there is still much work to be done, these achievements should be celebrated as a testament to the resilience and determination of the Bangladeshi people.

To summarize, here are three key takeaways:

  1. Bangladesh has made significant improvements in its HDI ranking over the past few decades.
  2. Investments in education, healthcare, and economic growth have led to tangible improvements in the lives of citizens.
  3. Although there is more work to be done, these achievements should be celebrated as a testament to the resilience and determination of the Bangladeshi people.
