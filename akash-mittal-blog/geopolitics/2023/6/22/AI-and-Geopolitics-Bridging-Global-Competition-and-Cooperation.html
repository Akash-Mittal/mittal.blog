AI and Geopolitics: Bridging Global Competition and Cooperation



Image Credits https://www.ifri.org/en/debates/ai-and-geopolitics-bridging-global-competition-and-cooperation

On a chilly morning in the year 2017, an unusual sight came to pass at the world headquarters of the World Bank in Washington D.C. A group of talented developers and researchers were gathered together around a table, pouring over complex code, data sets, and algorithms more advanced than anything ever seen before. What made this moment particularly interesting was the fact that these individuals hailed from every corner of the world and were brought together in a spirit of cooperation and innovation, and the technology they were working on was the latest breakthrough in Artificial Intelligence (AI) that was designed to help solve some of the world's most pressing problems.

The integration of AI into international relations and geopolitics is rapidly changing the way the world functions. It has the potential to greatly impact political relationships between countries and change the ways that countries interact in the international community. With the rise of AI comes new challenges, but also new opportunities for global competition and cooperation.

Examples of Global Competition

The advances in AI technology have led to a race between countries to become the global leader in innovation and development. Countries are investing heavily in AI research to achieve this goal.

  • China has launched the "Next Generation Artificial Intelligence Plan" which is aimed at making the country the leading AI superpower by 2030 by investing over $150 billion.
  • Canada, with its booming tech industry and talented workforce, is quickly becoming a leading AI hub by attracting top talent in the field and investing in research and development.
  • The United States, with its major tech companies, universities and government agencies, has been a longstanding global leader in AI research and continues to make strides in the field.

Case Studies of Cooperation

AI also has the power to bring countries together in collaboration to address global issues that require the pooling of resources and expertise.

  • Climate Change Mitigation - The Paris Agreement on climate change, which seeks to limit the increase in global temperatures to below 2 degrees Celsius, could be aided by AI. The governments of France, Canada, and China have launched an AI-driven initiative to monitor and track greenhouse gas emissions worldwide. The initiative uses satellite images and machine learning to analyze the data and identify sources of emissions.
  • Healthcare Advancements - Countries with extensive healthcare systems, such as the United States, are partnering with developing nations to use AI to improve access to quality healthcare. IBM's Watson is already being used in healthcare systems globally to diagnose diseases and develop treatment options. With AI's potential to analyze big data, it can also help governments to make informed decisions on resource allocation to address healthcare needs.
  • International Security - AI technology can help governments combat international security threats such as cyber-attacks and terrorism. The United States and the United Kingdom are already collaborating with other governments to develop AI-powered tools to prevent cyber threats and detect terrorists and other high-risk individuals.

Conclusion

As AI technology continues to advance and become more integrated into international relations and geopolitics, it will be critical for countries to find a balance between competition and cooperation. The following are key takeaways for countries to consider:

  1. Countries must collaborate to address global challenges, such as climate change and international security, that require pooling of resources and expertise.
  2. Countries must carefully consider the potential implications of AI on international trade, diplomacy, and security, and take steps to mitigate any negative impacts.
  3. Countries must prioritize investments in AI research and development to remain globally competitive.
