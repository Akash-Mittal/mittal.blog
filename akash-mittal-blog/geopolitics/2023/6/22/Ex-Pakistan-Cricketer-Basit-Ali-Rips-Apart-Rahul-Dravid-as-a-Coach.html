Ex-Pakistan Cricketer Basit Ali Rips Apart Rahul Dravid as a Coach

When it comes to cricket, India and Pakistan have always shared a fierce rivalry on the field. However, the rivalry has often boiled over off the field as well. Recently, former Pakistan cricketer Basit Ali made headlines when he lashed out at Indian cricket team's head coach Rahul Dravid.

Basit Ali, who has been known to make controversial statements in the past, did not hold back this time either. "As a coach, he is absolutely zero," Ali said of Dravid.

While this statement might come as a surprise to many, it is important to examine the reasons behind Ali's criticism of Rahul Dravid as a coach.

Examples of Rahul Dravid's Coaching

Rahul Dravid, who is often referred to as 'The Wall', is known for his impeccable technique and incredible discipline on the cricket field. His success as a player has earned him the respect of his peers and fans alike. However, his success as a coach has been a subject of debate.

Dravid has been the head coach of India's Under-19 and A teams since 2015. Under his guidance, the Under-19 team won the 2018 World Cup and the A team has performed well in tour games. However, the Indian team's performance in the senior team has not been up to the mark. India lost the Test series to England and failed to qualify for the final of the World Test Championship.

While Dravid's fans argue that he has not been given enough time to make an impact, critics like Basit Ali argue that he is not cut out for coaching.

Case Studies of Successful and Unsuccessful Coaches

The debate around Rahul Dravid's coaching abilities is not new. Similar debates have surrounded other legendary cricketers who became coaches. Some have been successful, while others have not.

For example, Gary Kirsten, South Africa's former captain, led India to the 2011 World Cup victory as head coach. On the other hand, Sourav Ganguly, India's former captain, had a brief stint as the team's head coach but was not able to replicate his success as a player.

Similarly, in the case of football, Maradona, a legendary player, was not able to make a mark as a coach, but Zinedine Zidane, another legendary player, won three consecutive Champions League titles as coach of Real Madrid.

These case studies show that the success of a legendary player as a coach depends on several factors, including their ability to adapt to the changing dynamics of the game, their man-management skills, and their strategic thinking.

Three Points to Consider Before Passing Judgment on Rahul Dravid

While Basit Ali's statement might seem harsh, it is important to consider the following points before passing judgment on Rahul Dravid as a coach:

  • Time: Dravid has been the head coach of India's Under-19 and A teams for six years. While he has not yet replicated his success with the senior team, it is important to give him more time to make an impact.
  • Support Staff: A coach's success depends not only on their abilities but also on the quality of their supporting staff. Dravid's performance as a coach can only be evaluated in context with the rest of the support staff.
  • Player Performance: While a coach's role is important, the players on the field ultimately determine the outcome of a match. It is important to consider the performance of the Indian team as a whole rather than solely blaming the coach for their shortcomings.
