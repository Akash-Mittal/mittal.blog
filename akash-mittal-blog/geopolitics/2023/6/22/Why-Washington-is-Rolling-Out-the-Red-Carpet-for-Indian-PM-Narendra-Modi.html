Why Washington is Rolling Out the Red Carpet for Indian PM Narendra Modi



Image Credits https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-india-65947363
Sept 1, 2022 by BBC News

On a warm September day, Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrived in Washington D.C. for a crucial bilateral meeting with US President Joe Biden. As the motorcade drives through the streets, the sound of Indian drums and the sight of dancers in colorful costumes greet them. For Modi, it's a welcome fit for a superstar and a sign of the growing importance of India in the eyes of the US political establishment.

The Importance of India-US Relations

The US-India relationship is seen as crucial to both countries and has grown substantially in recent years. The two nations are home to some of the world's largest democracies and economic powerhouses. The US views India as a counterweight to China in Asia, while India views the US as a crucial strategic partner to counteract China's influence in the region and the world.

Case Studies of Bilateral Cooperation

1. Defense Cooperation

The US and India have rapidly expanded their defense partnership through the signing of multiple agreements and billions of dollars in arms sales. In 2020, India signed a $2.6 billion deal for 24 MH-60R Seahawk maritime helicopters from Lockheed Martin, a deal that saw the US company beat out bids from European and Indian firms. Furthermore, the US and India have conducted joint military exercises, such as the Malabar series, to improve interoperability between their armed forces.

2. Climate Action Partnership

Climate change is one of the major challenges facing the planet, and the US and India have taken some strides towards cooperation. In April 2021, the US and India launched the Climate Action and Finance Mobilization Dialogue to promote clean energy and climate-resilient infrastructure. The two countries also committed to working together to achieve the Paris Agreement goals and to collaborate on climate finance efforts.

3. COVID-19 Vaccine Partnership

The COVID-19 pandemic exposed the interconnectedness of the world and the need for international collaboration to deal with health crises. The US and India have collaborated on vaccine production and distribution. The US has donated millions of vaccine doses to India to help deal with its devastating second wave of infections, while India has been a major manufacturer of COVID-19 vaccines, including the AstraZeneca vaccine, which is being distributed globally through COVAX.

Conclusion

As Modi's visit to the US demonstrates, the US-India partnership continues to grow deeper and stronger. The two nations recognize the challenges facing them and have taken concrete steps towards cooperation in areas such as defense, climate action, and public health. The importance of this relationship will only grow in the years to come.

  2. Case studies of bilateral cooperation include defense, climate action, and public health.
  3. The partnership will continue to grow in importance in the years to come.
