UAE Group Inks Deal with Cash-Strapped Pakistan to Operate Terminal at Its Largest Seaport



Image Credits https://www.arabnews.pk/node/2326246/pakistan

In a recent development, a UAE-based group has signed an agreement with the Pakistani government to operate a terminal at Gwadar, the country's largest seaport. This comes as a relief news for the Pakistani government, given that the country has been struggling financially in recent years. The multi-billion dollar project is expected to bring in foreign investments and further promote economic growth in the region.

Gwadar Seaport and Its Importance to Pakistan

Gwadar is a port city situated on the Arabian Sea, near the Iranian border. It lies at the entrance of the Strait of Hormuz, which is a critical point for oil transportation. The location of Gwadar makes it an essential point for trade and commerce in the region, connecting Pakistan to other major ports in Asia, the Middle East, and Africa.

However, despite its strategic location, the port has been underutilized for a long time due to lack of funds and infrastructure. The Pakistani government aimed to develop Gwadar in 2002, and after years of planning and construction, the port finally became operational in 2007. Since then, the government has been looking for ways to attract investments and foreign companies to utilize the port's full potential.

The Contribution of the UAE-based Group

The signing of the deal with the UAE group is a critical development for Gwadar and the Pakistani government. The group will finance, construct, and operate the terminal at the port for a period of forty years. This is expected to bring in foreign investments worth billions of dollars, which can further enhance the economic growth of Pakistan.

Moreover, the deal with the UAE group is also expected to provide job opportunities for local Pakistani workers, streamline the port's operations, and boost the supply chain initiatives of Pakistani businesses. The increase in port's operation will further promote the import and export of goods, resulting in increased economic activity in the region.

Case Studies: Similar Developments in Other Countries

The deal made by the UAE-based group with Pakistan is not an isolated event. In recent years, several other countries have also witnessed such developments, with foreign companies and investors making their mark in ports and seaports around the world.

For instance, China has been working on its ‘Belt and Road Initiative', which aims to connect countries in Asia, Africa, and Europe, promoting trade and commerce and further enhancing economic growth. Similarly, in Africa, the government of Djibouti has made agreements with foreign companies, including China, to operate their ports and develop them for business and trade purposes.

These developments have helped countries to utilize their strategic location and promote their economic growth. With the increase in exports and imports, these countries have been able to develop their infrastructure, provide employment to their citizens, and build national economies.

Conclusion

The agreement signed by the UAE-based group with Pakistani government is a significant development for the country and the region. The project is expected to bring in foreign investments worth billions and further promote economic growth in the area. The deal will provide job opportunities for local workers and streamline the port's operations, resulting in increased economic activity.

The development is not an isolated event and follows similar developments in other countries such as China and Djibouti, who have utilized their strategic locations to promote business and commerce. This further highlights the importance of connectivity and interdependence in today's world, where countries can benefit from each other's expertise and resources to enhance their growth.
