Why Europe Should Chart New Relations with China?



Image Credits https://news.cgtn.com/news/2023-06-26/Why-should-Europe-chart-new-relations-with-China--1kWJBusONW0/index.html

Story

Imagine a world where Europe and China have cordial relations and work together to resolve global issues. A world where trade flows freely between them and both cooperate for mutual benefit. This might seem like a distant dream, but it is achievable if Europe comes out of its traditional Cold War mindset and reorients its policies towards China. In this article, we will address why Europe needs to chart new relations with China.

Examples

China has emerged as the world's second-largest economy and a major global player. The country is investing heavily in infrastructure projects under its Belt and Road Initiative (BRI), which aims to improve connectivity and foster trade between Asia and Europe. The BRI provides considerable opportunities for European companies to participate in these projects and access new markets.

Moreover, China has become a hub for technology and innovation. Many European firms look towards China for joint ventures and investment to enhance their technological capabilities. Huawei's advanced technological advancements in 5G and its subsequent attempts to find a foothold in European markets underscore China's technological prowess.

On the other hand, Europe has much to offer to China. Europe has a robust services sector and a mature capital market. China recognizes the potential of the European market and has sought to invest in European companies in the automotive, energy and technology sectors. An example of this is the investment of China's Geely in Volvo and Daimler.

Case Studies

Italy-China Cooperation

Italy was the first G7 country to sign up for the BRI, and as a result, has enhanced its partnership with China. In 2019, China's state-owned China Communications Construction Company (CCCC) acquired a majority stake in the port of Trieste. The port is strategically located at the head of the Adriatic Sea, making it an important gateway for Chinese goods entering Europe. Italy hopes that this collaboration will boost its economy and create jobs.

Germany-China Cooperation

Germany and China share strong economic bonds, and many German companies have invested in China. However, with China's growing military and economic power, Germany has become increasingly wary of its intentions. Yet, in 2020, Germany signed a massive investment deal with China. The agreement, despite facing criticism, provides German companies with access to the Chinese market and aims to improve commercial relations between the two countries.

Conclusion

To sum up, Europe must chart new relations with China for mutually beneficial trade and geopolitical cooperation. The BRI presents an opportunity for Europe to access new markets and engage in infrastructure projects of the future. Europe's established capital markets and robust services sector provide China with immense potential for investment and access to new technologies.

  1. Europe must recognize China's ascent and its role as a major global player.
  2. Europe should embrace China's technological advancements and look for collaboration opportunities.
  3. Europe and China need to work together to resolve global issues, and trade should be a cornerstone of their relationship.
