The Art of Diplomacy: Saudi-Iranian Rapprochement

Once upon a time, there were two neighboring countries named Saudi Arabia and Iran. Their relationship had always been tense, marked with political confrontations and proxy wars. But, one day, the leaders of these two countries decided to put an end to this animosity. They sat together and talked about common interests, aspirations, and challenges. After a few months of intense negotiations, they agreed to restore mutual trust and respect, paving the way for a new era of cooperation and stability in the region. This was a historic moment that many thought was impossible. However, it happened because both sides listened to each other, acknowledged their differences, and embraced their shared humanity.

The above story may sound idealistic and naive to some readers, but it reflects the essence of the recent webinar organized by NUST Think Tank, which discussed the prospects and challenges of Saudi-Iranian rapprochement. This topic is not only relevant to the two countries in question but also to the whole Middle East, which is still grappling with the aftermath of decades of instability, violence, and sectarianism. The main question is whether it is possible to break the vicious cycle of hostilities and build a new paradigm based on cooperation, mutual respect, and dialogue.

Why Does Saudi-Iranian Rapprochement Matter?

There are several reasons why the issue of Saudi-Iranian rapprochement matters, both regionally and globally. One of the most important reasons is that Saudi Arabia and Iran are the two major powers in the Middle East, with significant economic, political, and religious clout. Any conflict or tension between them would have serious ramifications, not only for their own citizens but also for their neighboring countries and the world at large.

For instance, the ongoing war in Yemen, which is fueled by Saudi-Iranian rivalry, has caused a humanitarian crisis and a strategic stalemate that affects millions of people. Similarly, the Syrian civil war, which has been fueled by regional and international actors, including Saudi Arabia and Iran, has claimed hundreds of thousands of lives and displaced millions of people, making it one of the worst humanitarian disasters of the 21st century.

In addition, Saudi-Iranian tensions also impact the global economy and the energy market, given that both countries are major oil producers and exporters. Any disruption or instability in the Gulf region would affect the supply and demand of oil and gas, leading to price hikes, inflation, and geopolitical risks.

What Are the Challenges to Saudi-Iranian Rapprochement?

Despite the potential benefits of Saudi-Iranian rapprochement, there are also significant challenges that need to be addressed in order to make it a reality. These challenges include:

  • History of animosity and mistrust: Saudi Arabia and Iran have a long history of rivalry and confrontation, dating back to the Islamic Revolution in 1979, which overthrew the pro-Western Shah of Iran and established an Islamic republic. Since then, the two countries have been supporting opposing factions, militias, and proxies in various conflicts, such as Iraq, Lebanon, Syria, Bahrain, and Yemen.
  • Geopolitical interests and alliances: Saudi Arabia and Iran are also part of different geopolitical alliances and spheres of influence, which often clash with each other. For example, Saudi Arabia is an ally of the United States and Israel, while Iran is an ally of Russia and China. This makes it harder to find common ground and compromise on critical issues, such as nuclear proliferation, terrorism, and regime change.
  • Domestic challenges and popular sentiments: Saudi Arabia and Iran also face domestic challenges and popular sentiments that shape their foreign policies. In Saudi Arabia, the ruling monarchy has to balance between modernization and traditionalism, reformism and conservatism, as well as the demands of its citizens, including women, youth, and minorities. In Iran, the government has to deal with economic sanctions, political dissidents, and social unrest, while maintaining its revolutionary ideology and Islamic identity.

What Are the Prospects for Saudi-Iranian Rapprochement?

Despite these challenges, there are also some prospects for Saudi-Iranian rapprochement, especially under the current regional and international dynamics. Some of these prospects are:

  • The changing geopolitical landscape: The Middle East is witnessing a profound transformation, driven by the rise of new powers, such as Turkey, Qatar, and the United Arab Emirates, as well as the declining influence of former powers, such as the United States and Russia. This makes it possible for other actors, including Saudi Arabia and Iran, to redefine their roles and agendas, without being constrained by external pressures or interests.
  • The impact of COVID-19: The global pandemic has exposed the vulnerabilities and interdependencies of all countries, including Saudi Arabia and Iran. Both countries have suffered from the pandemic, in terms of health, economy, and social stability. This has made them realize the importance of collaboration, coordination, and solidarity, in order to overcome this common threat and to build a sustainable future.
  • The role of diplomacy and dialogue: Diplomacy and dialogue are the key tools to resolve conflicts and to build trust. Saudi Arabia and Iran have already taken some steps towards this direction, such as hosting direct talks in Baghdad, which focused on regional security. They have also released some prisoners, exchanged some messages, and toned down their rhetoric. This shows that there is a willingness from both sides to explore the possibilities of rapprochement.

The Conclusion: Three Points to Remember

In conclusion, Saudi-Iranian rapprochement is a complex and momentous issue that requires careful analysis, dialogue, and action. It is not an easy or immediate process, but it is a necessary and feasible one, given the stakes and the opportunities. Here are three points to remember:

  1. Peace is possible, but it requires courage, wisdom, and empathy from all parties.
  2. Regional and international cooperation is essential, but it should not overlook the national interests and aspirations of Saudi Arabia and Iran.
  3. Dialogue and diplomacy are the best antidotes to enmity and conflict, but they should be based on mutual respect, equality, and non-interference.
