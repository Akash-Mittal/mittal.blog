With No IMF Deal, Pakistan Struggles to Prevent Default



Image Credits https://www.arabnews.pk/node/2328096

The Story

In August 2018, Pakistan reached an agreement with the International Monetary Fund (IMF) for a $6 billion bailout loan to help stabilize the economy. However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic and subsequent economic downturn, Pakistan failed to meet the IMF's conditions for the loan. As a result, Pakistan is now struggling to prevent default.

Without this financial support, Pakistan is facing a severe financial crisis with looming debt payments, a plunging currency, and high inflation rates. The country's economy is in a perilous state, and the situation is exacerbating poverty and unemployment rates, leading to social unrest and political instability.

Examples

The impact of Pakistan's financial crisis is not just limited to the economy but is also affecting its people.

  • Inflation: With the fall of the Pakistani rupee, the cost of imports has increased, and this is leading to a rise in inflation rates in the country. Basic necessities like food, petrol, and electricity are becoming unaffordable for many people. As a result, Pakistanis are finding it difficult to make ends meet.
  • Jobs: Due to the economic downturn, many businesses are shutting down, leading to a significant loss of jobs. With unemployment rates soaring, people are finding it difficult to sustain their livelihoods.
  • Education: As a result of the financial crisis, many schools and universities are facing financial difficulties, leading to a deteriorating quality of education in the country.

Case Studies

Let's take a look at some case studies to understand the impact of the financial crisis on individuals in Pakistan.

  • Aqeel's Story: Aqeel is a small business owner who runs a grocery shop in Lahore. Due to the increase in the prices of basic necessities, his customers are unable to afford groceries, leading to a significant drop in sales. As a result, Aqeel is finding it difficult to keep his shop running.
  • Rukhsar's Story: Rukhsar is a mother of two who works as a maid to provide for her family. Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, many of her employers have lost their jobs, and as a result, Rukhsar is finding it difficult to find work. Her family is struggling to make ends meet, and they are unable to afford basic necessities like food and medicine.

Conclusion

The financial crisis in Pakistan is having a severe impact on the country and its people. Without financial support from the IMF, Pakistan is struggling to prevent default. As a result, the economy is in a perilous state, and poverty and unemployment rates are rising, leading to social unrest and political instability.

Three key takeaways from the article are:

  1. Pakistan's financial crisis is having a severe impact on its people, leading to rising poverty and unemployment rates.
  2. Without financial support from the IMF, Pakistan is struggling to prevent default, leading to a further escalation of the crisis.
  3. The situation in Pakistan highlights the importance of international financial institutions in supporting countries during economic downturns.
