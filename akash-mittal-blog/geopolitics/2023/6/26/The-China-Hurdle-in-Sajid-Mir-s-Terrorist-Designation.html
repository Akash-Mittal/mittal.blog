The China Hurdle in Sajid Mir's Terrorist Designation



Image Credits https://indianexpress.com/article/opinion/editorials/the-china-hurdle-by-blocking-sajid-mirs-designation-as-a-global-terrorist-beijing-does-itself-no-favours-8678430/

Story

In 2008, a terrorist attack in Mumbai, India killed over 160 people and injured hundreds more. The attack was planned and carried out by a group linked to the Pakistani militant group Lashkar-e-Taiba. Among the masterminds of the attack was Sajid Mir, a known terrorist who has evaded justice for years.

Recently, the United States and India have been pushing for the designation of Sajid Mir as a terrorist by the United Nations. However, there is one major hurdle in this effort: China's veto power in the UN Security Council.

Examples

China has a long-standing alliance with Pakistan, which has led to its vetoing of several attempts to designate Pakistani terrorists as international terrorists. For example, in 2016, China vetoed the designation of Jaish-e-Mohammed leader Masood Azhar as a terrorist, even though the group was responsible for a deadly attack on an Indian Air Force base.

Similarly, in 2010, China vetoed a proposal to add Lashkar-e-Taiba leader Hafiz Saeed to the UN's list of designated terrorists. Saeed is believed to have planned the Mumbai attacks and continues to be a free man in Pakistan.

China's veto power has also hindered efforts to designate North Korean individuals and entities as terrorists, which has been a major point of contention between China and the United States.

Case Studies

Masood Azhar

In 2016, the United States, Britain, and France proposed to the UN Security Council that Jaish-e-Mohammed leader Masood Azhar be designated as a terrorist. However, China blocked the move by placing a hold on the proposal and eventually vetoing it, citing "technical reasons."

This led to protests in India, with some calling for boycotts of Chinese products and others calling for a review of India's "One China Policy." The move also strained ties between India and China, which were already tense due to border disputes in the Himalayas.

Hafiz Saeed

In 2010, the United States, Britain, and France proposed to the UN Security Council that Lashkar-e-Taiba leader Hafiz Saeed be designated as a terrorist. However, China and some other council members blocked the move, claiming that the evidence against Saeed was not strong enough and that due process had not been followed.

Saeed remains a free man in Pakistan, where he continues to operate openly and lead Lashkar-e-Taiba's activities. This has led to criticism of Pakistan's failure to crack down on terrorism and calls for international pressure to be exerted on the country.

Conclusion

  1. China's veto power in the UN Security Council has been a major obstacle in efforts to designate known terrorists as international terrorists.
  2. This has led to criticism of China's close ties with countries like Pakistan and North Korea, which have been accused of supporting terrorism.
  3. The international community must work together to find alternate means of putting pressure on these countries and individuals to ensure justice is served.
