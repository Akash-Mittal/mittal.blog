The Curious Case of US-China Meets: Russia suffers Economically



Image Credits https://tfiglobalnews.com/2023/06/26/the-curious-case-of-us-china-meets-russia-suffers-economically/

Once upon a time, there was a country called Russia. It was once a superpower, but due to various reasons, it lost its glory. The country was struggling to revive its economy, and in this quest, it found a new ally - China. China was willing to invest in Russia, and Russia was happy to receive the investment. The two countries became close partners, and it seemed like everything was going well.

However, something unexpected happened. The United States, Russia's long-time rival, and China's biggest trading partner, started having a trade war. The two countries imposed tariffs on each other's goods, and this led to a significant impact on the global economy. Russia, being an ally of China, was also affected by this trade war.

Examples:

One of the sectors that were affected the most was the energy sector. Russia is one of the world's largest producers of oil and gas, and China is one of the world's largest consumers. Russia was exporting a significant amount of oil and gas to China, but due to the trade war, the demand for oil and gas decreased. This led to a decrease in the price of oil and gas, which was a significant blow to Russia's economy.

Another sector that was affected was the automobile industry. China is one of the world's largest automobile markets, and many automakers were exporting cars to China. However, due to the trade war, the demand for cars decreased, and this led to a decrease in the number of cars exported. Russia, being a significant exporter of cars, was also affected by this decrease in demand.

Case Studies:

A case in point is the cancellation of Russia's $400 billion gas deal with China. In 2014, Russia and China signed a deal in which Russia agreed to supply gas to China for 30 years. The deal was seen as a significant boost to Russia's economy, but due to the trade war, China backed out of the deal. Russia suffered a significant loss, and the cancellation of the deal was a significant blow to the country's economy.

Another example is the impact on Russia's aluminum industry. Russia is one of the world's largest producers of aluminum, and China is one of the world's largest consumers. Due to the trade war, the price of aluminum dropped, and this led to a significant decrease in revenue for Russian aluminum producers. The aluminum industry was already struggling due to other factors, and the trade war added to its woes.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the US-China relations have a significant impact on the global economy, and the trade war between the two countries has had a significant impact on Russia. The energy and automobile sectors were the worst affected, and the cancellation of the gas deal was a significant blow to Russia's economy. The aluminum industry was also affected due to the drop in the price of aluminum. The case of Russia shows that even countries that are not directly involved in the trade war can suffer economically.

3 Key Takeaways:

  1. The US-China trade war has had a significant impact on the global economy.
  2. Russia, being an ally of China, has also been affected by the trade war.
  3. The energy, automobile, and aluminum sectors were the worst affected in Russia.
