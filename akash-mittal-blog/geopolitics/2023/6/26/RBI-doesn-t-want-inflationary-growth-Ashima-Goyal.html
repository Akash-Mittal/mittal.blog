RBI doesn't want inflationary growth: Ashima Goyal, RBI MPC external member

Once upon a time, in a small town, there were two grocery store owners. Both of them wanted to increase their profits, so they decided to increase the prices of their products. However, one of them failed to understand the concept of inflation and increased the prices of his products to an unreasonable level. As a result, his customers stopped coming, and his sales started to drop. On the other hand, the other store owner increased the prices of his products but kept it at a reasonable level, which helped him to maintain his customer base and increase his profits.

Just like the second store owner, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) also believes in maintaining a balance between inflation and growth. Inflation can be defined as a general increase in prices and a decrease in the purchasing value of money. High inflation can lead to an unfavorable business environment, and it can have a negative impact on the country's economy.

One of the external members of the RBI Monetary Policy Committee (MPC), Ashima Goyal, recently stated that the central bank doesn't want inflationary growth in India. In this article, we will discuss why inflation is a concern for the RBI and how it affects the country's economy.

Why is inflation a concern for the RBI?

The RBI is India's central bank, responsible for managing the country's monetary policy. Inflation is one of the major concerns for the RBI as it affects the economy in various ways. Here are some reasons why inflation is a concern for the RBI:

  1. Impact on purchasing power: High inflation decreases the purchasing power of money. When prices increase, consumers have to pay more for goods and services, and they have less money left for other expenses. This can lead to a decrease in consumer spending, which affects businesses and the overall economy.
  2. Impact on interest rates: High inflation can lead to an increase in interest rates. This happens because the RBI wants to control inflation by reducing the money supply in the economy. When interest rates are high, it becomes more expensive for businesses to borrow money, which affects their profitability and investments.
  3. Impact on foreign investments: Inflation can also affect foreign investments in the country. When inflation is high, it reduces the value of the local currency, which can make foreign investors hesitant to invest in the country. This can lead to a decrease in foreign investments, which affects the country's economy.

Examples of inflation in India

Inflation has been a concern for the Indian economy for a long time. In recent years, the inflation rate has been under control, but there have been instances of high inflation in the past. Here are some examples of inflation in India:

  • High inflation in 2013-14: In 2013-14, India faced high inflation due to various reasons, including a decrease in the supply of food and fuel. The inflation rate reached a high of 11% in November 2013, which led to a decrease in consumer spending and affected the economy.
  • Inflation targeting framework: In 2016, the RBI introduced an inflation targeting framework to control inflation in the economy. The framework aims to keep the inflation rate at 4% with a +/- 2% tolerance band. The RBI uses various monetary policy tools to achieve this target.
  • Impact of COVID-19: The COVID-19 pandemic had a significant impact on the Indian economy, including inflation. The inflation rate increased to 7.34% in September 2020 due to supply chain disruptions and high food prices. However, the inflation rate has since decreased to 4.06% in January 2021.

Case studies on inflation in India

Let's take a look at some case studies on how inflation has affected the Indian economy:

Impact on the real estate sector

The real estate sector is one of the major sectors affected by inflation. When inflation is high, the cost of construction materials and labor increases, which leads to an increase in property prices. This can affect the demand for real estate, as consumers have to pay more for properties. In addition, high-interest rates can also affect the demand for home loans, which affects the real estate sector.

For example, during the high inflation period of 2013-14, the real estate sector in India was affected, as property prices increased due to high construction costs. This led to a decrease in demand for properties, which affected the business of real estate developers.

Impact on small businesses

Small businesses are also affected by inflation, as it affects the cost of raw materials and other inputs. When prices increase, businesses have to increase the prices of their products to maintain profitability. However, this may affect the demand for their products, as consumers have less money to spend. In addition, high-interest rates can also affect the profitability of small businesses, as it becomes more expensive for them to borrow money.

For example, during the high inflation period of 2013-14, small businesses in India faced difficulties as their costs increased due to high inflation. This affected their profitability and made it difficult for them to sustain their businesses.

Conclusion

Inflation is a major concern for the RBI, as it affects the economy in various ways. It decreases the purchasing power of money, affects interest rates, and can impact foreign investments in the country. However, the RBI aims to maintain a balance between inflation and growth to ensure a favorable business environment.

In conclusion, inflation can have a significant impact on businesses, consumers, and the overall economy. The RBI's efforts to control inflation through various monetary policy tools are crucial to maintaining a stable and sustainable economy.
