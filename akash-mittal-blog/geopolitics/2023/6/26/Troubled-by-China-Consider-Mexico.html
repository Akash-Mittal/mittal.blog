Troubled by China? Consider Mexico



Image Credits https://harrisbricken.com/chinalawblog/troubled-by-china-consider-mexico/

Why doing business in Mexico can be a better option for you.

Here's an interesting story for you.

Jack, a small business owner based in the United States, was looking to expand his production line and reduce his dependency on China. He had heard that Mexico could be a good option for his business, but he wasn't sure if it was the right move. Jack had been doing business with China for years and was familiar with the culture and the way things worked there. He was uncertain about whether he could replicate the same success in Mexico.

Eventually, after a lot of research and consultation, Jack decided to take the plunge and set up a factory in Mexico. Within a year, he saw significant cost savings and an increase in his production efficiency. He also found that doing business in Mexico was much easier and less bureaucratic compared to China, which saved him a lot of time and hassle.

Why should you consider Mexico?

There are several reasons why doing business in Mexico can be a better option for you than China.

  1. Location: Mexico is located right next to the United States, which makes it a great option for businesses that want to have a presence in North America. It has a well-connected transport infrastructure, including road, rail, and air links, which makes it easy to move goods around the country and beyond.
  2. Cultural Similarities: Mexico shares many similarities with the United States in terms of culture, language, and business practices. This makes it easier for American businesses to understand and navigate the Mexican market.
  3. Costs: Mexico offers significantly lower costs for labor, production, and transportation than China. This means that businesses can save a lot on their expenses while still maintaining the quality of their products and services.

Here are a few examples of companies that have successfully made the switch from China to Mexico:

  • Ford: The American automaker has been operating in Mexico since 1925 and currently has 11 manufacturing facilities in the country. It employs over 40,000 people and produces a range of vehicles, including the F-150 pickup truck, which is the best-selling vehicle in the United States.
  • Nike: The athletic apparel giant has been sourcing products from Mexico for over three decades. It operates several factories in the country that produce shoes, apparel, and other goods.
  • Apple: The tech giant has been using Mexico as a base for its production operations since 2014. It has several manufacturing partners in the country that produce components for its products, including Foxconn and Pegatron.

These companies are just a few examples of how doing business in Mexico can be a great option for American businesses.

Conclusion

If you're troubled by China, it's time to consider Mexico. With its strategic location, cultural similarities, and lower costs, Mexico can be a better option for American businesses that want to expand their operations and reduce their dependency on China. With several success stories and real-world examples, it's clear that Mexico is a viable alternative that businesses should seriously consider.

In summary, here are the top three reasons why you should consider Mexico:

  1. Location
  2. Cultural Similarities
  3. Costs
