Russia-China-Pak Triumvirate Worrying for India



Image Credits https://www.heraldgoa.in/Edit/Editorial/RussiaChinaPak-triumvirate-worrying-for-India/206651

Once upon a time, there was a land called India, a country with a rich history and diverse culture. India had faced many challenges in the past, but it always emerged stronger and resilient. However, in recent years, India is facing a grave challenge - the Russia-China-Pak Triumvirate.

The Russia-China-Pak alliance is a strategic partnership between Russia, China, and Pakistan. The alliance poses a significant threat to India's national security as it aims to create a new world order that will challenge the current global status quo. India is concerned about this alliance as it is seen as a move to encircle India and limit its influence in the region.

  • Russia: Russia is an old friend of India, but in recent years, it has been moving closer to China and Pakistan. Russia is now providing military aid and technology to Pakistan, including supplying it with modern fighter jets and submarines. This has raised India's concerns as Pakistan is India's traditional enemy and is involved in a proxy war with India in the state of Jammu and Kashmir.
  • China: China is India's biggest nemesis. The two countries have fought a war in 1962, and their border dispute still remains unresolved. China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) aims to create a new world order that will give China the dominant role in the global economy. Pakistan is a crucial partner in the BRI as it provides China with access to the Indian Ocean, and it is also helping China to build the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC). CPEC passes through the Pakistan-occupied Kashmir region, which is a disputed territory between India and Pakistan. India has opposed the BRI, and it has boycotted China's flagship project, the Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB).
  • Pakistan: Pakistan is India's traditional enemy. It has fought four wars with India, and it is involved in a proxy war with India in the state of Jammu and Kashmir. Pakistan has been providing safe haven to terrorist organizations that are attacking India. Pakistan is also a crucial partner in China's BRI, and it is helping China to build the CPEC. The CPEC passes through the Pakistan-occupied Kashmir region, which is a disputed territory between India and Pakistan.

The Russia-China-Pak alliance poses a grave challenge to India's national security. Here are some case studies that highlight the concerns:

  • Case Study 1: In 2016, India conducted a surgical strike against terrorist camps in Pakistan-occupied Kashmir. While India expected support from Russia, Russia chose to remain neutral. This was a clear indication that Russia's relationship with Pakistan has gained more importance than its relationship with India.
  • Case Study 2: In 2020, China and Pakistan held joint military exercises in the Pakistan-occupied Kashmir region. The exercises involved over 70,000 troops, and it was a clear message to India that China and Pakistan could pose a formidable force against India.
  • Case Study 3: In 2021, Russia chose to ignore India's concerns about the supply of military equipment to Pakistan. Russia supplied Pakistan with the Mi-35 attack helicopters, which are capable of carrying out both transportation and combat operations. This move has raised India's concerns about the changing dynamics of the Russia-India relationship.

In conclusion, the Russia-China-Pak alliance poses a grave challenge to India's national security. India needs to take concrete steps to counter this alliance. Here are three points that could help India:

  1. India needs to build closer ties with the United States, Japan, and Australia to counter the Russia-China-Pak alliance.
  2. India needs to strengthen its military capabilities to deter any aggression from the Russia-China-Pak alliance.
  3. India needs to engage in sustained and meaningful dialogue with China and Pakistan to resolve the outstanding issues and build trust.
