India v Pakistan: A Potential Classic Football Rivalry?



Image Credits https://www.theguardian.com/football/2023/jun/26/pakistan-india-football-south-asian-cup

Sitting in the stands amidst the sea of blue and green, I couldn't help but feel a sense of excitement and anticipation as India faced off against Pakistan in the football field. The cheers, the jeers, the flags all around, and the passion on display made it seem like a classic football rivalry, similar to those seen in Europe and South America. However, as I looked around, I realized that this was not the case. It was then that I started wondering: Could India and Pakistan be a potential classic football rivalry?

The Unique Nature of India v Pakistan

Firstly, it is essential to understand the unique nature of the India v Pakistan rivalry. Although the two countries share a long and troubled history, it is predominantly focused on cricket and geopolitics, with little emphasis on football. Football, which is the most popular sport in the world, is relatively underdeveloped in both India and Pakistan.

Despite having a population of 1.3 billion people, India's football team currently ranks 104th in the world, while Pakistan ranks 200th, making them one of the weakest footballing nations. This lack of success in football has led to little interest in the sport among the masses, resulting in minimal funding, inadequate infrastructure, and lack of exposure for the players. On the other hand, the popularity of cricket in both countries has seen them become powerhouses in the sport, with an intense rivalry that has produced numerous memorable moments over the years.

The Potential of India v Pakistan as a Classic Football Rivalry

Despite the lack of success in football, India and Pakistan's potential to become a classic rivalry is undeniable. Football has the power to bring people together, and nothing exemplifies this better than a well-contested, high-intensity game between two passionate nations.

A classic football rivalry requires several factors to come together, such as history, geography, culture, and competitiveness. Although India and Pakistan's football history and competitiveness are currently lacking, they share a long and complex history, with many cultural similarities and differences. Additionally, their proximity means that the potential for games to be held frequently exists, creating an opportunity for the rivalry to grow over time.

Case Studies

India v Pakistan in the SAFF Championship 2013 Final

The SAFF Championship 2013 final saw India face off against Pakistan in Kathmandu, Nepal. The game was highly anticipated, with numerous political tensions surrounding the fixture. The game itself was a thrilling encounter, with India eventually emerging victorious after a 3-1 win. The match saw an attendance of over 20,000 people and was watched by millions across India and Pakistan, showcasing the potential the rivalry has to offer.

India v Pakistan in the AFC Cup 2021 Qualifiers

In June 2021, India and Pakistan faced off in the AFC Cup qualifiers, with the match held in Doha, Qatar. The game was played behind closed doors, and neither country was at full strength, with several players missing due to COVID-19 protocols. Despite this, the match was highly anticipated, with both countries hoping to gain an edge over their opponents. India emerged victorious, winning the match 2-0, showcasing their dominance in the rivalry.

Conclusion

The India v Pakistan football rivalry may not be a classic rivalry yet, but the potential exists. Despite the lack of footballing success in both countries, the history, geography, and culture that they share, coupled with the passionate fan bases, mean that there is an opportunity for the rivalry to grow over time.

Three Key Takeaways:

  • The India v Pakistan rivalry is unique, with most of the tension concentrated on cricket and geopolitics.
  • Despite football's underdevelopment in both countries, the potential for a classic football rivalry between the two nations exists.
  • History, geography, and culture, coupled with passionate fans, can make a classic football rivalry.
