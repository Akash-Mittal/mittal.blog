Interest vs Alignment - SA's Place in the Geopolitical Maelstrom

It was a hot summer day in Johannesburg, South Africa, when two diplomats from different countries accidentally met each other in a famous coffee shop in the city center. They started talking about the current political situation in South Africa and how their respective countries were involved in it. As they talked, one of them said, "It's always a fine line between our interest and our alignment in this country". The other diplomat nodded in agreement, knowing that this was a true reflection of the geopolitical reality not only in South Africa but across the world.

Examples of Interest vs Alignment in South Africa

South Africa's geopolitical position makes it a crucial player in the global arena. However, the country's internal dynamics often impact its ability to align with the interests of certain countries.

  • China and South Africa have a strategic partnership agreement that covers areas such as trade, investment, and finance. However, since South Africa's ruling party came to power in 1994, it has maintained strong ties with Taiwan, which is a major source of irritation to China.
  • South Africa and Israel have had a complex relationship over the years, with South Africa condemning Israel for its treatment of the Palestinians. However, the two countries have also maintained economic and diplomatic ties, with Israel being one of South Africa's major trading partners in the Middle East.
  • South Africa's relationship with the United States has also been complicated by issues such as the country's human rights record and its ties with countries like Cuba and Venezuela.

Case Studies

Two case studies illustrate the complexity of South Africa's geopolitical position.

South Africa and China

Since the establishment of diplomatic relations between China and South Africa in 1998, the two countries have developed a strategic partnership that covers areas such as trade, investment, and finance. However, South Africa's ties with Taiwan have caused tensions between the two countries. In 2018, China was angered by a visit to Taiwan by South African Ambassador to Taiwan, Mr. Mninwa Mahlangu, and called on South Africa to "correct its mistake". South Africa responded by stating that it did not recognize Taiwan as a state and that it respected China's "One China" policy. However, this incident highlighted the challenge that South Africa faces in balancing its interest with its alignment when it comes to China.

South Africa and the United States

South Africa's relationship with the United States has been complicated by issues such as the country's human rights record and its ties with countries like Cuba and Venezuela. In 2016, South Africa announced that it was withdrawing from the International Criminal Court, a move that was strongly criticized by the United States. However, both countries have also maintained ties in areas such as trade and investment. For example, in 2019, the United States and South Africa signed a trade agreement that is expected to increase trade between the two countries by over $1 billion.

Conclusion

In conclusion, South Africa's geopolitical position makes it a crucial player in the global arena, but its internal dynamics often impact its ability to align with the interests of certain countries. The country's complex relationships with China, Israel, and the United States are just some examples of the challenges it faces in balancing its interest with its alignment. In order to navigate the geopolitical maelstrom, South Africa needs to adopt a strategic approach that takes into account its internal dynamics as well as the interests of countries that it engages with.
