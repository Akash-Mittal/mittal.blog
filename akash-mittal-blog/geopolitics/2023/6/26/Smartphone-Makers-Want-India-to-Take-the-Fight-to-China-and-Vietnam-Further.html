A Step Up in the Smartphone Game: Indian Manufacturers Prepare to Take on China and Vietnam



Image Credits https://www.businessinsider.in/tech/mobile/news/smartphone-makers-urge-indian-government-to-reduce-input-tariffs-to-increase-competitiveness/articleshow/101272037.cms

India has been making strides in the smartphone industry over the past few years. However, now the country is taking it to the next level. Smartphone manufacturers in the country are ready to compete on the global stage, specifically with behemoths like China and Vietnam.

The Current State of the Smartphone Market in India

India is the world's fastest-growing smartphone market. There are over 500 million smartphone users in the country, and that number is predicted to double by 2022. With average revenue per user being one of the lowest in the world, smartphone manufacturers have to create affordable devices that pack a punch.

The country has made strides in recent years, with Indian manufacturers gaining significant market share. Companies like Micromax, Intex, and Lava have been in the mix for a while. However, now the game has changed. Foreign entrants like Xiaomi, Oppo, and Vivo have taken over with their affordable and feature-rich devices.

The Push to Compete Globally

Indian manufacturers are now itching to take the fight to foreign competitors. They want to go beyond the Indian market and make a global impact. Smartphone makers are investing time and money in research and development, creating devices that can stand alongside the best.

One such example is Micromax. The company has been around since 2000, but it wasn't until 2014 that they gained significant ground in the Indian market. However, in 2021, Micromax is set to launch two new devices - the "In note 1" and "In 1b". These devices have been specifically designed for Indian users and aim to offer great performance at an affordable price. Both devices feature MediaTek Helio chipsets, offering great performance at a low cost.

Another example is Lava. The company has been around since 2009 and has been a consistent player in the Indian market. However, now they are stepping up their game. The company has announced the launch of their new "BeU" smartphone. The device features a 6.08-inch HD+ display, a 1.6GHz octa-core processor, and a 4,060mAh battery, all at an affordable price.

Case Studies: Successful Indian Smartphone Manufacturers

Let's take a look at two successful Indian smartphone manufacturers who have made a significant impact on the market.

Xolo

Xolo is an Indian smartphone manufacturer that has gained a lot of traction over the past few years. The company's focus on the budget segment has helped it become a household name in the country. Xolo has been able to provide great value for money and has managed to create a loyal fan base.

One of the devices that put Xolo on the map was the Xolo Q700. The device featured a quad-core processor, an 8MP camera, and was priced extremely competitively. The Q700 was able to compete with devices from Samsung and Micromax, and it helped Xolo gain significant market share.

Intex

Intex is another Indian smartphone manufacturer that has made a name for itself. The company has been around since 1996 but only entered the smartphone market in 2012. However, since then, Intex has been able to establish itself as a significant player in the market.

One of Intex's successful devices is the Aqua Lions 4G. The device features a 5-inch HD display, a 1.3GHz quad-core processor, and 1GB of RAM. The phone is priced extremely competitively and has helped Intex gain significant market share.

Conclusion: Indian Smartphone Manufacturers are Ready to Compete Globally

  1. India is the fastest-growing smartphone market, with over 500 million users.
  2. Indian manufacturers are now creating devices that can compete with global giants like China and Vietnam.
  3. Companies like Micromax, Lava, Xolo, and Intex are making strides and gaining significant market share.
