Germany calls China a growing threat as it steps up focus on security



Image Credits https://theprint.in/world/germany-calls-china-a-growing-threat-as-it-steps-up-focus-on-security/1626640/

Germany has raised concern over China's growing threat as it steps up focus on its security. The two countries have had a long-standing trade relationship, with China being Germany's largest trading partner outside of Europe. However, recent developments have raised alarm bells for Germany, which has taken steps to strengthen its national security measures.

The Story of Germany and China's Trade Relationship

The story of Germany and China's trade relationship dates back to the early 1970s when the two countries established diplomatic relations. China's rapid economic growth over the past few decades has presented an opportunity for German businesses to expand their operations in the Chinese market. Many German companies, including BMW, Volkswagen, and Siemens, have established a strong presence in China, contributing significantly to their overall growth and success.

However, the relationship has not been without its challenges. German businesses have faced various hurdles in the Chinese market, including intellectual property theft, market access restrictions, and unfair competition. Despite these challenges, the trade relationship between the two countries has continued to grow.

The Growing Threat of China

In recent years, China's aggressive foreign policy has raised concerns about its growing threat to national security. German officials have expressed concerns over China's expanding influence in Europe. China has invested heavily in European infrastructure, including ports, railways, and airports, raising concerns about potential national security risks.

Furthermore, China's Belt and Road Initiative, which aims to expand Chinese influence around the world through infrastructure development projects, has raised concerns about the country's intentions. Many European countries, including Germany, have declined to participate in the initiative due to concerns over transparency and legal standards.

Case Study: Huawei

The case of Huawei, the Chinese telecommunications company, has brought the issue of national security to the forefront. The United States has banned Huawei from its 5G network due to concerns about potential espionage and national security risks. Australia, Japan, and the United Kingdom have also taken steps to limit Huawei's involvement in their 5G networks. Germany has not yet made a decision on whether to allow Huawei to participate in the country's 5G network.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Germany's concerns about China's growing threat to national security are justified. The relationship between the two countries has been built on a strong foundation of trade and economic cooperation. However, recent developments have raised serious concerns about China's intentions and its potential impact on national security. Germany has taken steps to strengthen its national security measures, but it remains to be seen how the relationship between the two countries will evolve in the future.
