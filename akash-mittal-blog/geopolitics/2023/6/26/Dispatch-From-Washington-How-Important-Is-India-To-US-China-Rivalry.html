Dispatch From Washington: How Important Is India To US-China Rivalry?

The rivalry between the United States and China is one of the defining global dynamics of the 21st century. As the two superpowers compete for economic, political, and military influence, India has emerged as a key player in their strategic calculations. This article will explore the reasons why India is important to the US-China rivalry, and what implications this has for the future of the region and the world.

An interesting story

In September 2019, Chinese President Xi Jinping paid a visit to India's southern state of Tamil Nadu. The visit was seen as a chance to mend strained ties between the two countries, which had been worsened by China's support for Pakistan on the issue of Kashmir. However, the mood quickly turned sour when Indian troops clashed with Chinese soldiers in the Ladakh region of the Himalayas. The clash, which left 20 Indian soldiers dead and an unknown number of Chinese casualties, was the deadliest border incident between the two countries in more than four decades. It also raised questions about India's ability to balance its relations with China and the United States, which had been growing closer under the Trump administration.

Examples of India's importance

  • Trade: India is a major market for both the US and China, with a population of over 1.3 billion people. In 2019, the US exported over $33 billion worth of goods to India, making it one of the top trading partners in Asia. China, on the other hand, sees India as a key destination for its Belt and Road Initiative, a massive infrastructure project aimed at boosting connectivity across Asia and beyond.
  • Politics: India's democracy and its growing regional influence make it an attractive partner for both the US and China. India has been a strong voice in promoting the rules-based order and free trade, which aligns with US interests. On the other hand, China has sought to cultivate ties with India's neighbors, such as Pakistan, Sri Lanka, and Nepal, in an effort to encircle and contain India.
  • Defense: India's strategic location and growing military capabilities have made it a top priority for both the US and China. India has been modernizing its armed forces and expanding its naval presence in the Indian Ocean, which has raised concerns in China about encirclement. The US has sought to deepen its defense ties with India, including through joint military exercises and the sale of advanced weapons systems.

Case studies

India-US relations

The US has been increasingly looking to India as a key partner in the Indo-Pacific region, as part of its efforts to counterbalance China's growing influence. In 2018, the US renamed its Pacific Command as the Indo-Pacific Command, highlighting India's strategic importance. The US has also sought to deepen defense ties with India, including through the sale of advanced weapons systems such as the Apache attack helicopter and the F-18 fighter jet. The two countries have also held joint military exercises such as the Malabar and Tiger Triumph, which have enhanced interoperability and cooperation between their armed forces.

However, the US-India relationship has faced its share of challenges. India has sought to maintain its strategic autonomy and avoid getting drawn into any Cold War-style alignments. India has also been wary of US sanctions against countries such as Iran and Russia, which are important partners for India's energy and defense needs. The Trump administration's focus on immigration and trade has also raised concerns in India about protectionism and nationalist sentiment in the US.

India-China relations

India and China have had fraught relations dating back to the 1962 border war. The two countries share a long and contested border, which has seen frequent skirmishes and standoffs. China's growing assertiveness in the region, particularly with its Belt and Road Initiative and its military buildup in the South China Sea, has raised concerns in India about China's intentions. India has also been wary of China's support for Pakistan, which has fueled tensions over the disputed region of Kashmir.

Despite these challenges, India and China have sought to engage diplomatically and expand economic ties. Chinese President Xi Jinping held an informal summit with Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi in Wuhan in 2018, which was seen as a breakthrough in their relations. The two countries have also sought to boost trade and investment, although there are concerns in India about the trade deficit with China. However, the clash in Ladakh in 2020 has raised tensions once again, and it remains to be seen how the relationship will evolve going forward.

Conclusion

India's importance to the US-China rivalry is multifaceted and complex. India's growing economic and military clout, as well as its democratic values and regional influence, make it an attractive partner for both superpowers. However, India's strategic autonomy and its own interests must also be taken into account. The US and China must navigate this delicate balance carefully if they wish to maintain their influence in the region and avoid conflict.

  • India's growing economic and military clout make it a key player in the US-China rivalry.
  • The US and China both see India as an attractive partner, but must balance their interests with India's own strategic autonomy.
  • The future of the region depends on how the US-China-India triangle evolves, and whether conflict can be avoided.
