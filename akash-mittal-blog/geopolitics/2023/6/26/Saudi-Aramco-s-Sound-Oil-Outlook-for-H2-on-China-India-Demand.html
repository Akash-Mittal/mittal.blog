Saudi Aramco's 'Sound' Oil Outlook for H2 on China, India Demand



Image Credits https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/global-oil-market-fundamentals-sound-rest-2023-aramco-ceo-2023-06-26/

There was a man who lived in a small town with no electricity. He used a kerosene lamp for light, a wood stove for heat, and a horse and cart for transportation. One day, someone introduced him to electricity. He was amazed at the convenience and simplicity of turning a switch on and off rather than lighting a lamp. As time went on, he learned about the many things electricity could do and how it improved his life.

The same can be said for oil. Oil has been a vital part of our lives for over a century, powering everything from cars to planes to the manufacturing of goods. However, as technology advances and alternative energy sources become more prevalent, the demand for oil has fluctuated.

Despite this, Saudi Aramco, the world's largest oil producer, has a positive outlook for the second half of 2021 on the strength of demand from China and India.

The Sound Outlook

According to a top official at Aramco, demand for oil has continued to rebound since the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, with the second half of 2021 expected to see further growth.

The company cites the economic rebound of China and India as a main factor in the expected rise in demand. Both countries have seen an increase in industrial activity, which in turn leads to higher demand for oil. In addition, the recent surge in travel as restrictions ease will lead to an upturn in aviation fuel demand after a prolonged lull. This outlook sees demand for oil returning to pre-pandemic levels by the end of 2022.

Real World Examples

The positive outlook for oil demand is not just theory, as there are several real-world examples that support Aramco's forecast.

Firstly, China, the world's largest importer of crude oil, has emerged from the pandemic relatively unscathed. In addition, the country has seen significant growth in its manufacturing sector, which uses oil as a main energy source. For instance, in June, Chinese factory output rose at its fastest pace in four months, indicating strong demand for oil.

Secondly, India, a close second to China in oil demand, has also seen a rebound in its economic activity. The country's oil consumption has increased for the third straight month in July, resulting in a 9.3% growth since the same period last year. This trend is expected to continue as India aims to increase its industrial output and grow its economy at a faster rate.

Lastly, the aviation industry is also set for a strong recovery as travel restrictions ease. The International Air Transport Association (IATA) expects air travel to return to pre-COVID levels by 2023. This will result in an increase in aviation fuel demand and a positive impact on the oil industry as a whole.

Case Studies

Despite the positive outlook for demand, there are still challenges ahead for the oil industry. One such challenge is the shift towards renewable energy sources.

In response, Aramco has invested heavily in research and development to find more efficient and environmentally-friendly ways to extract oil. For example, it has reduced the amount of water used in extraction by more than 50%, which has a positive impact on the environment.

Another example of a shift towards renewable energy sources is the recent decision by Royal Dutch Shell to accelerate its move towards clean energy. The company aims to reduce its oil production by half by 2030 and achieve net-zero carbon emissions by 2050. This shows that even some of the largest oil companies are looking towards a more sustainable future.

Conclusion

In conclusion, despite the challenges faced by the oil industry, Aramco's positive outlook for the second half of 2021 shows that there is still a demand for oil. The economic rebound of China and India, as well as the expected revival of the aviation industry, are key factors in this demand.

However, the oil industry also needs to address the challenges of sustainability and efficiency to ensure its future. This can be seen in the examples of Aramco's investment in R&D and Shell's move towards clean energy.

In summary, the oil industry is not going away anytime soon, but it must adapt to changing times to ensure its long-term viability.
