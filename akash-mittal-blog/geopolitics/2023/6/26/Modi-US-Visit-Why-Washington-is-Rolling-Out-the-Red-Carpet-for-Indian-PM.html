It was a warm afternoon in New York City, and the Indian Prime Minister, Narendra Modi, was scheduled to address a packed crowd at Madison Square Garden. I was in the audience, along with over 18,000 other people, eagerly waiting for Modi to take the stage.

The energy was palpable. People were chanting, cheering, and waving Indian flags. There was a sense of pride and excitement in the air that was hard to ignore.

When Modi finally appeared, the crowd erupted in cheers. He spoke for over an hour, covering a range of topics from India's economic growth to its cultural heritage. But it was his charisma and passion that really stood out. It was clear that he had a vision for India and was determined to make it a reality.

Why Washington is Rolling Out the Red Carpet for Modi

Modi's visit to the United States in September 2021 is his first since Joe Biden took office as the President of the United States. The two leaders are meeting for the first time, and the stakes are high.

So why is Washington rolling out the red carpet for Modi? There are several reasons:

Economic Ties

The United States and India have a strong economic relationship. India is the world's sixth-largest economy, and the United States is one of its largest trading partners. In 2019, the two countries traded goods and services worth over $146 billion. The United States is also the largest foreign investor in India, with a cumulative investment of over $42 billion.

The economic relationship between the two countries has the potential to grow even stronger. Modi's visit to the United States is an opportunity for the two leaders to discuss ways to boost trade and investment between the two countries.

Geo-political Cooperation

India is an important strategic partner for the United States in the Indo-Pacific region. The two countries share a vision of a free, open, and inclusive Indo-Pacific, and they have been working together to counter China's growing influence in the region.

In recent years, the United States has been strengthening its ties with India's neighbours, including Japan, Australia, and South Korea. Modi's visit to the United States is an opportunity for the two countries to discuss ways to deepen their strategic partnership and work together to promote regional stability and security.

Climate Change

Climate change is one of the biggest challenges facing the world today. The United States and India are two of the world's largest emitters of greenhouse gases, and their cooperation is essential to combatting the effects of climate change.

Modi has made climate change a priority for India. In 2015, he launched the International Solar Alliance, a coalition of countries working to promote solar energy. The United States and India have also been working together on the Clean Energy Finance Forum, which aims to mobilize private investment in clean energy.

Modi's visit to the United States is an opportunity for the two countries to discuss ways to deepen their cooperation on climate change and work together to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions.

Case Studies

Modi's visit to the United States comes at a time when the two countries are looking to deepen their partnership. Here are a few examples of their current collaborations:

Vaccine Partnership

The United States and India have been working together on vaccine distribution. In April 2021, the United States sent medical supplies to India to help the country combat a surge in COVID-19 cases. In return, India supplied the United States with hydroxychloroquine, a drug used to treat COVID-19.

The United States has also pledged to donate over 500 million doses of the Pfizer vaccine to India. Modi's visit to the United States is an opportunity for the two countries to discuss ways to deepen their partnership on vaccine distribution.

Defense Cooperation

The United States and India have been working together to strengthen their defense cooperation. In 2020, the two countries signed the Basic Exchange and Cooperation Agreement for Geospatial Cooperation (BECA), which allows the two countries to share military information, including satellite data.

Modi's visit to the United States is an opportunity for the two countries to discuss ways to deepen their defense cooperation and work together to promote regional stability and security.

Education and Research

The United States and India have a strong partnership in education and research. In 2020, the two countries launched the U.S.-India Higher Education Dialogue, which aims to increase student mobility between the two countries and promote research collaboration.

Modi's visit to the United States is an opportunity for the two countries to discuss ways to deepen their partnership in education and research and increase student mobility between the two countries.
