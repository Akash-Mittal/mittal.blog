India and Indonesia: The Rising Global Players



Image Credits https://www.heraldmailmedia.com/story/opinion/columns/2023/06/26/india-indonesia-are-becoming-the-key-players-on-the-world-stage-tim-rowland/70345610007/

There is an interesting story about two neighbors, India and Indonesia. These two countries, although separated by the Indian Ocean, share many similarities such as their rich history, diverse cultures, and growing economies. They also share a common goal to become key players on the world stage.

In recent years, India and Indonesia have been making headlines for their impressive economic growth and increasing influence in global affairs. Let's take a closer look at what's driving their rise.

Economic Growth

Both India and Indonesia have been experiencing rapid economic growth over the past decade. In 2019, India was named the world's fastest-growing major economy by the International Monetary Fund (IMF) with a growth rate of 6.1%. Indonesia wasn't far behind, with a growth rate of 5%.

This growth is largely due to the countries' large and young populations, which provide a strong foundation for consumer spending and labor markets. India's population is expected to surpass China's by 2027, while Indonesia's population is projected to reach 305 million by 2035.

Another factor contributing to their economic growth is their strategic location. Both countries are situated in the heart of Asia, making them ideal hubs for international trade and investment. India's proximity to the Middle East and Africa, and Indonesia's location at the intersection of the Indian and Pacific Oceans, make them strategic gateways for businesses looking to expand in these regions.

Increasing Influence

India and Indonesia's growing economies have also translated into increased political and cultural influence on the global stage. Both countries are members of the G20, a group of the world's largest economies, and have been playing active roles in shaping international policies on issues such as climate change, trade, and security.

India and Indonesia have also been strengthening their bilateral and multilateral relationships with other countries. India has been working closely with Japan and the United States to promote an Indo-Pacific strategy, which aims to increase security and economic cooperation in the region. Meanwhile, Indonesia has been playing a leading role in the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN), a regional organization of ten countries that promotes economic integration and political cooperation.

Case Studies

One example of India's growing influence is its success in the information technology (IT) sector. India has become a global hub for software development with companies such as Tata Consultancy Services and Infosys leading the way. These firms have also expanded their operations to other countries, such as the United States, where they have created thousands of jobs and added billions of dollars to the economy.

Indonesia's growing influence can be seen in the tourism industry. In recent years, Indonesia has become a popular destination for tourists from around the world. The government has made significant investments in infrastructure and marketing campaigns to promote tourism. As a result, the industry has grown more than 9% annually, contributing to the country's economic growth.

Conclusion

In conclusion, India and Indonesia are rising global players that have a lot to offer. Their impressive economic growth and increasing influence in global affairs are creating new opportunities for businesses and investors. As they continue to expand their relationships with other countries and strengthen their positions in international organizations, we can expect to see more of India and Indonesia on the world stage.

Three key takeaways from this article are:

  1. India and Indonesia are experiencing rapid economic growth due to their large and young populations, as well as their strategic locations.
  2. The two countries are playing active roles in shaping international policies and strengthening their relationships with other countries.
  3. India's success in the IT sector and Indonesia's growth in tourism are examples of their increasing influence in global industries.
