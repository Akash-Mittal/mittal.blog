GE to Jointly Produce Fighter Jet Engines in India



Image Credits https://sg.news.yahoo.com/ge-fighter-jet-engines-indian-100830819.html

India, a developing nation, has always aimed towards becoming a global superpower in the aviation industry. The aviation industry in India is one of the fastest-growing industries in the world. The Indian aviation market has been expanding rapidly with a significant increase in passenger traffic. In 2018, the Indian aviation market recorded a growth rate of 18.6% compared to the previous year.

Currently, India heavily relies on foreign suppliers for military aircraft, and fighter jets are no exception. India's military aviation sector has always been heavily dependent on other countries for fighter jets and engines. However, the Indian government is now looking to change the situation and has taken a significant step towards achieving its goal.

The Indian government recently announced that General Electric (GE) would collaborate with Hindustan Aeronautics Ltd (HAL) and produce fighter jet engines in India. This collaboration is a step towards India's goal to become a global aviation hub. It is a significant milestone in the Indian aviation industry's history.

The Collaboration - A Significant Step towards India's Goal

The collaboration between GE and HAL is a significant step towards India's goal of achieving self-reliance in the aerospace industry. This step will not only provide a boost to India's domestic aerospace industry but also help India become a global aviation hub.

This collaboration between GE and HAL is also expected to contribute to India's Make in India vision, an initiative launched by the Indian government to promote indigenous manufacturing and production in India. The initiative aims to increase domestic manufacturing and attract foreign investment in the country.

GE's experience and expertise in producing fighter jet engines are unparalleled. Its engines have powered some of the most advanced fighter jets in the world, including the F-16, F-18, and F-35. The collaboration with HAL will help GE tap into India's experienced workforce and advanced manufacturing facilities. India's aerospace industry is expected to provide a low-cost and highly skilled workforce to GE, making it a win-win situation for both entities.

The GE-HAL collaboration is expected to create significant job opportunities in India's aerospace industry. The partnership is expected to generate more than 1000 jobs and contribute to the development of the country's aerospace industry. This collaboration is also expected to bring in investments, which will contribute to the growth of the Indian economy.

Case Studies - Collaborations that Helped Countries Achieve Self-Reliance

The collaboration between GE and HAL is not the first of its kind. Several countries have entered into similar collaborations with foreign companies to achieve self-reliance in different sectors of the economy. Here are some examples of such collaborations and their impact on the economy of the countries involved.

India and France - Rafale Fighter Jets

India and France entered into a defense collaboration, which led to the procurement of Rafale fighter jets by the Indian Air Force. The deal between the two countries involved the transfer of technology to India, enabling the country to produce some of the systems and sub-systems for the fighter jets. The deal also included the training of Indian Air Force personnel by French experts. This collaboration is expected to help India achieve greater self-reliance in the aviation industry.

Brazil and Embraer

Brazil is one of the world's largest producers of aircraft, and Embraer is one of Brazil's leading aerospace companies. Embraer has collaborated with several countries, including China, to produce aircraft. In the case of China, Embraer partnered with the Commercial Aircraft Corporation of China (COMAC) to produce the E190-E2 aircraft. This collaboration helped China achieve self-reliance in the aviation industry and contributed to the growth of its economy.

Singapore and Rolls-Royce

Singapore is a small island nation that has limited natural resources. The country has focused on developing its manufacturing and service sectors to drive economic growth. Rolls-Royce, a leading British engineering company, has set up a manufacturing facility in Singapore to produce aircraft engines. This collaboration has helped Singapore become a global hub for aerospace manufacturing and contributed to the growth of Singapore's economy.
