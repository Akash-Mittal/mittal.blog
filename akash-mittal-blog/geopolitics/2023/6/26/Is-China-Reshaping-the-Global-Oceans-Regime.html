Is China Reshaping the Global Oceans Regime?



Image Credits https://foreignpolicy.com/2023/06/10/china-sea-south-east-maritime-claims-law-oceans-us-disputes/

Our oceans cover more than 70% of the Earth's surface and are home to countless species. However, they are also a source of conflict and competition among nations as they contain valuable resources such as fish, oil, and gas. In recent years, China's assertiveness in the South China Sea has raised concerns about its impact on the global oceans regime.

A Story of Sea Turtles

Sea turtles are a beloved and endangered species. Every year, they migrate thousands of miles to lay their eggs on beaches around the world. However, their survival is threatened by human activity, including overfishing, pollution, and habitat destruction. In recent years, Chinese fishermen have been catching large numbers of sea turtles in the South China Sea, despite a ban on the practice.

According to a report by TRAFFIC, an international wildlife trade monitoring network, Chinese demand for sea turtle meat and eggs has fueled the illegal trade in many countries. The report also notes that Chinese fishing boats have been spotted in the waters of Indonesia and Malaysia, which are known for their sea turtle nesting sites.

China's Impact on the Global Oceans Regime

China's actions in the South China Sea have raised concerns about its impact on the global oceans regime. China claims sovereignty over most of the South China Sea, despite conflicting claims by neighboring countries. It has built artificial islands and deployed military assets, leading to tensions with other countries in the region and the United States.

China's assertiveness in the South China Sea has also led to concerns about its impact on the freedom of navigation and the rule of law. The United States and other countries have sent military vessels and planes to challenge China's claims, leading to several close encounters and tense incidents.

Case Studies

Illegal Fishing

China is the world's largest consumer, producer, and importer of seafood. However, its fishing practices have been criticized for their impact on the environment and other countries' fishing industries. Chinese fishing vessels have been caught illegally fishing in the waters of other countries, such as Argentina, Ghana, and Guinea.

In addition to overfishing, Chinese vessels have been accused of using illegal methods such as bottom trawling, which can damage the seabed and marine life. Some countries have responded by seizing Chinese fishing vessels or banning imports of Chinese seafood.

Marine Pollution

China is also a major contributor to marine pollution. A report by the United Nations found that China is the largest source of plastic waste in the world's oceans, accounting for up to 28% of the total. China's rapid economic growth has led to increased consumption and waste generation, as well as lax enforcement of environmental regulations.

The pollution from China's rivers and coastal areas has also affected neighboring countries such as Vietnam and South Korea. In addition to plastic waste, China's industrial discharges and agricultural runoff have led to the spread of harmful algal blooms and other waterborne diseases.

Acquisition of Marine Resources

China's hunger for marine resources has led it to seek access to other countries' waters and minerals. China has invested heavily in deep-sea mining technology and has explored for minerals such as cobalt and manganese nodules in the Pacific Ocean. It has also signed several fishing agreements with African countries, granting Chinese vessels access to their waters.

China's acquisition of marine resources has raised concerns about its impact on the environment and local communities. Some countries have accused China of engaging in neo-colonialism and disregarding their sovereignty and rights.

Conclusion

China's impact on the global oceans regime is complex and multifaceted. Its actions in the South China Sea have raised concerns about its adherence to international laws and norms, as well as its impact on regional stability. Its fishing practices and pollution have also had a negative impact on the environment and other countries. However, China's technological advances and investment in marine resources could also contribute to sustainable development and scientific progress.

Overall, it is important for the international community to engage with China on ocean issues and encourage responsible and respectful behavior. This could include promoting transparency, accountability, and cooperation, as well as enforcing international laws and regulations. Only by working together can we preserve and protect our oceans for future generations.
