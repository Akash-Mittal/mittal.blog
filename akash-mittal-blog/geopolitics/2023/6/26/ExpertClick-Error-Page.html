The Importance of Error Pages



Image Credits https://www.expertclick.com/NewsRelease/China-The-Warmongering-Peacemaker

Have you ever landed on an error page while browsing a website? If so, you probably felt frustrated and may have even left the site entirely. Error pages can have a big impact on user experience and can even affect a website's traffic and search engine ranking. In this article, we'll explore the importance of error pages and how they can impact your website and business.

Examples of Error Pages

There are several different types of error pages that users can come across while browsing the web. Here are a couple of examples:

  • 404 Not Found Error - This is the most common error users may encounter. It happens when a user attempts to visit a page or URL that doesn't exist or has been removed.
  • 500 Internal Server Error - This error occurs when the server is unable to fulfill a request.

Case Studies

Let's take a look at a few examples of how error pages have affected different businesses:

Case Study 1: Etsy

In 2017, Etsy experienced a major outage that resulted in their website being down for several hours. During this time, users who attempted to visit the site were met with a 502 error page. This had a significant impact on Etsy's business, causing them to lose thousands of dollars in sales and damaging their reputation among customers.

Case Study 2: Blue Fountain Media

Digital agency Blue Fountain Media found that improving their error pages resulted in a 17% decrease in bounce rate and a 7% increase in time spent on their site. By adding a clear call-to-action and helpful resources for users who encountered an error page, they were able to improve user experience and reduce the number of users leaving the site entirely.

Conclusion

As we've seen, error pages can have a significant impact on user experience and website performance. Here are a few key takeaways to keep in mind:

  1. Ensure your error pages are user-friendly and provide clear, helpful information for users who encounter them.
  2. Track your website's error rates and address any recurring issues to improve your site's overall performance.
  3. Consider using humorous or creative error pages to alleviate user frustration and turn a negative experience into a positive one.
