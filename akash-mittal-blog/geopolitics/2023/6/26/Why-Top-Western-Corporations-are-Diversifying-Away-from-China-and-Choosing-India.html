Why Top Western Corporations are Diversifying Away from China and Choosing India?



Image Credits https://swarajyamag.com/business/move-to-india-here-are-the-top-western-corporations-diversifying-away-from-china

Once, a wise man said, "Don't put all your eggs in one basket." It is true not only in our personal lives but also in the corporate world. In 2020, we witnessed how the world was at a standstill due to the coronavirus pandemic. It disrupted not only our daily lives but also the global economy. During the lockdown, many of us turned to online shopping, and it led to a surge in demand for consumer goods. However, manufacturers worldwide faced a challenge in fulfilling this demand as their supply chains were severely affected due to China's production halt.

It was a wake-up call for many Western corporations to diversify their manufacturing bases and reduce their dependency on China, which is said to be the "World's Factory." For years, China has been a go-to destination for manufacturing due to its low labor costs and large-scale production capability. However, with the recent geopolitical tensions between China and the West and the impact of the pandemic, many companies are exploring alternatives.

Examples of Western Corporations Diversifying Away from China

Let's take a look at some of the top Western corporations that are diversifying away from China and expanding their operations into India:

Apple

Apple is one of the largest tech companies in the world and a dominant force in the consumer electronics market. As tensions escalated between China and the US, Apple started exploring alternative production sites to avoid disruptions. In 2020, Apple began shifting its production of iPads and MacBook to some of its suppliers in India. The move is expected to create jobs and boost the Indian economy.

Samsung

Samsung, the South Korean tech giant, is also diversifying away from China. In 2020, Samsung announced its plans to invest $500 million to build a smartphone display manufacturing plant in India. The move is expected to create more than 1,300 jobs and reduce Samsung's dependency on China.

Intel

Intel, the world's largest chipmaker, is also looking to reduce its dependence on China. In 2020, Intel announced its plans to invest $253.5 million to expand its manufacturing operations in India. The move is expected to create 1,500 jobs and boost India's tech industry.

Case Studies of Corporations Investing in India

Xiaomi

Xiaomi is a Chinese tech company that started its operations in India in 2014. Since then, it has become the leading smartphone company in the Indian market, surpassing Samsung and Apple. The company has invested heavily in India and has set up multiple manufacturing plants. Xiaomi has also created more than 50,000 jobs in India, making it one of the largest employers in the country's tech industry.

Tesla

Tesla, the American electric vehicle company, is also looking to expand its operations in India. In 2020, Tesla's CEO, Elon Musk, confirmed that the company will enter the Indian market soon. The move is expected to create jobs and boost India's electric vehicle industry.

Conclusion: Why India is a Potential Destination for Western Corporations

India has emerged as a potential destination for Western corporations looking to diversify away from China. The Indian government has been proactive in implementing policies to attract foreign investment and has created a conducive business environment. India's large consumer market, low labor costs, and skilled workforce make it an attractive destination for Western corporations.

To sum up, here are three points why India is a potential destination for Western corporations:

  1. India offers a large consumer market and a young demographic, making it an attractive investment destination.
  2. India has a skilled workforce that is proficient in English, making it easier for Western corporations to operate in the country.
  3. The Indian government has been reducing bureaucratic hurdles and implementing policies to attract foreign investment.
