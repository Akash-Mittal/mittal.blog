When PM Modi met Egyptian thought leaders



Image Credits https://theprint.in/world/pm-modi-meets-thought-leaders-in-egypt/1641504/

It was a sunny Sunday afternoon when Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi arrived in Cairo. He had been invited by his counterpart, Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi, to discuss trade relations and regional security. However, PM Modi had another agenda as well – to meet some of Egypt's leading intellectuals and intellectuals and discuss ideas.

Modi's team had reached out to several prominent figures in Egypt's cultural and political circles, including Mohamed ElBaradei, a Nobel laureate and former head of the International Atomic Energy Agency, and Hany Shukrallah, a renowned journalist and human rights activist. The response was overwhelming – everyone was eager to meet Modi and exchange ideas with him.

A meeting of minds

The meeting took place in the iconic Cairo Tower, which provided a beautiful view of the city. Modi was accompanied by his trusted aides, including National Security Advisor Ajit Doval and Foreign Secretary S. Jaishankar. The Egyptian side was represented by senior officials from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs as well as several academics and intellectuals.

The discussions were wide-ranging and covered topics such as education, culture, science, and technology. The Egyptian thinkers praised India's rich cultural heritage and emphasized the need for greater cooperation between the two countries in fields such as archaeology, energy, and medicine. Modi, for his part, highlighted India's achievements in space exploration, biotechnology, and renewable energy.

Examples of successful bilateral cooperation

Both sides also gave examples of successful bilateral cooperation in the past. For instance, India and Egypt have collaborated on several archaeological projects, including the restoration of the 5,000-year-old Step Pyramid of Djoser. They have also worked together on solar energy projects, with Indian firms investing in Egyptian solar plants.

Modi's team also took the opportunity to invite more Egyptian students to study in India. Several scholarships were announced, and an agreement was reached to launch joint research projects between Indian and Egyptian universities.

Case Studies on Idea Exchange

The meeting was widely seen as a success by both Indian and Egyptian media. It was also hailed as an example of how leaders can engage with intellectuals and exchange useful ideas.

One recent example of such an engagement is the "Tête-à-Tête with the Prime Minister" series launched by the Indian government in 2021. As part of this initiative, Modi has been meeting with leading thinkers from various fields to discuss important issues facing the country.

Similarly, in 2014, Egyptian President el-Sisi held a series of meetings with intellectuals and cultural figures to discuss ways to reform Egypt's education system and promote scientific research.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the meeting between PM Modi and Egyptian thought leaders was a prime example of how leaders can draw inspiration and ideas from intellectuals and cultural figures. The exchange of ideas proved to be beneficial for both sides, and it is hoped that such initiatives will continue in the future. Some of the main takeaways from the meeting include:

  • Intellectuals and cultural figures can provide valuable insights that can help shape policies and initiatives
  • Engaging with intellectuals can help leaders stay in touch with the pulse of society and the changing needs of the people
  • International partnerships and cooperation in the areas of education, culture, and technology can lead to tangible benefits for both countries
