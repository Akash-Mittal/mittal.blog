Impressions from Modi Meeting



Image Credits https://theprint.in/world/i-came-away-from-the-modi-meeting-truly-impressed-says-max-abrahms-expert-from-us-think-tank/1636814/

Max Abrahms, an expert from the US think-tank, recently attended a meeting with the Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi. Abrahms came away from the meeting truly impressed with the Prime Minister and his vision for India.

Abrahms was struck by Modi's energy and his commitment to improving the lives of the Indian people. He was also impressed by Modi's ability to engage with people from all walks of life, from rural farmers to international leaders.

During the meeting, Modi discussed several key initiatives aimed at improving India's economy, including the "Make in India" campaign, which is designed to encourage foreign investment in India's manufacturing sector. Modi also discussed his vision for creating a "Digital India," which would use technology to connect every Indian citizen to the internet and improve communication and access to information.

Examples of Modi's Vision in Action

Modi's "Make in India" campaign has already resulted in significant investment from foreign companies. For example, in 2018, Samsung announced plans to invest $700 million in a smartphone manufacturing plant in Uttar Pradesh, India. This investment is expected to create thousands of jobs and bring in much-needed tax revenue for the state.

The "Digital India" initiative is also making progress. The government has launched several programs aimed at improving internet connectivity and digital literacy among the Indian population. For example, the government has launched a program to provide free wifi hotspots in over 500 railway stations across India. They have also launched a mobile app called "Umang," which provides access to over 1,000 government services, including passport applications and tax filings.

Case Studies

One of the key challenges facing India is the lack of access to clean water, particularly in rural areas. Modi's government has launched several programs aimed at remedying this situation. For example, the government has launched a program to build 11 million toilets in rural areas and provide access to safe drinking water for every household by 2024.

Another challenge facing India is the issue of female infanticide. Modi's government has launched a program to promote the education and empowerment of women and girls. The Beti Bachao Beti Padhao program aims to address the issue of gender-based discrimination and encourage families to value their daughters as much as their sons.

Conclusion

Modi's vision for India is ambitious, but it is also realistic and achievable. His focus on improving the lives of ordinary citizens and creating a more prosperous and modern India is inspiring. There is no doubt that his efforts are already making a positive impact on the country, and with continued support, his vision can be fully realized.

In summary, the three key takeaways from Max Abrahms' meeting with Modi are:

  • Modi's energy and commitment to improving the lives of the Indian people is impressive
  • Modi's "Make in India" and "Digital India" initiatives are making real progress
  • Modi's government is tackling some of India's biggest challenges with innovative programs and policies
