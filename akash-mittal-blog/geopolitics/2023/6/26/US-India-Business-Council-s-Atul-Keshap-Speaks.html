Once upon a time, in a small town in India, a young boy dreamed of studying and working in the United States. He worked hard, got a scholarship, and eventually made it to America. After years of struggle, he became one of the top executives of a multinational company, and used his position to promote business ties between the two countries. His name was Atul Keshap, and his story is a prime example of the India-US partnership.

Examples of India-US Partnership

  • Trade: According to the US-India Business Council, bilateral trade between the two countries reached $146.1 billion in 2019, making India the 9th largest trading partner of the United States.
  • Investment: US-based companies have invested over $40 billion in India since 2000, and Indian companies have created more than 100,000 jobs in the US, according to the Council.
  • Defense: The two countries have cooperated on defense and security matters for decades, and the US has become one of the largest suppliers of military equipment to India.

Case Studies of India-US Partnership

1. Coca-Cola

The global beverage giant Coca-Cola has been operating in India since 1950s. In recent years, it has invested heavily in the Indian market, such as launching new products like Minute Maid Pulpy Mosambi juice, and partnering with local producers to source ingredients like mango pulp. Coca-Cola India has also set ambitious sustainability goals, such as replenishing 100% of the water it uses and collecting and recycling 100% of the plastic it produces.

2. Walmart

The American retail giant Walmart entered the Indian market in 2009 through a joint venture with Bharti Enterprises, but the partnership did not go smoothly and was eventually dissolved. However, Walmart did not give up on India, and instead acquired Flipkart, one of the largest e-commerce platforms in the country, in 2018. This move allowed Walmart to gain a foothold in the booming digital retail market in India and expand its portfolio of products and services.

3. General Electric

The US industrial conglomerate General Electric has been present in India for over a century. In recent years, it has focused on developing renewable energy solutions for the Indian market, such as wind turbines and solar panels. GE has also partnered with Indian companies to provide healthcare solutions, such as MRIs and CT scans, in rural areas where access to medical facilities is limited.

Conclusion: India-US Partnership in 3 Points

  1. Economic ties between the two countries are strong and mutually beneficial.
  2. Defense and security cooperation is crucial for both sides.
  3. Businesses from both countries are finding innovative ways to collaborate and create value.
