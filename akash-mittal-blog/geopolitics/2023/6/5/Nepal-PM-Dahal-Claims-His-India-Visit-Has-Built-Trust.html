How Nepal PM Dahal's India Visit Strengthened Bilateral Ties?

Prime Minister Pushpa Kamal Dahal of Nepal recently concluded his four-day visit to India, marking his first official overseas trip after being appointed as the Prime Minister of Nepal. PM Dahal's visit aimed at enhancing the bilateral ties between India and Nepal, which had witnessed some strains due to various reasons in the past few years. During his visit, PM Dahal had several rounds of talks with the top Indian leadership and expressed his hope of strengthening the existing bonds of friendship and cooperation between the two countries.

An interesting anecdote related to PM Dahal's visit is the exchange of postal stamps by the two countries. India's External Affairs Minister, Sushma Swaraj, presented a set of commemorative stamps to PM Dahal, featuring the Pashupatinath Temple in Kathmandu and Muktinath Temple in Mustang. Likewise, PM Dahal handed over a set of stamps to Indian Prime Minister, Narendra Modi, featuring Lumbini, the birthplace of Lord Buddha, and Janakpur, the birthplace of Sita. This exchange of stamps symbolizes the cultural and spiritual connect of the two nations, which transcends time and geography.

Examples of Strengthening of Bilateral Ties:

The visit of PM Dahal to India has several implications for the Nepal-India relationship. Here are some of the examples of the strengthening of bilateral ties between the two countries:

  • Building Trust: PM Dahal's visit has generated a fresh wave of goodwill and trust between the two countries, who share a unique bond of culture, religion, and history. The joint statement issued at the end of the visit highlights the shared commitment of the two countries to strengthen the existing relationship and work towards mutual benefit and development. Some of the areas of cooperation identified in the joint statement include hydropower, trade and investment, tourism, people-to-people contacts, and connectivity.
  • Addressing Border Issues: One of the main issues between India and Nepal has been the ongoing border dispute between the two countries. During his visit, PM Dahal held talks with the Indian leadership on the issue and expressed his government's commitment towards finding a lasting resolution to the problem through peaceful means. The joint statement reiterates the need for resolving the border issues through dialogue and mutual understanding.
  • Reviving SAARC: Another important aspect of PM Dahal's visit was his emphasis on reviving the stalled process of South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC). The SAARC process has been stagnant due to differences between India and Pakistan, the two largest members of the bloc. PM Dahal expressed his hope of working towards revitalizing SAARC, which has the potential to promote regional economic integration and enhance people-to-people contacts in South Asia.

Case Studies:

The strengthening of Nepal-India relationship can be further illustrated through some of the case studies:

Hydropower Cooperation:

One of the key areas of cooperation identified during PM Dahal's visit was hydropower. India is Nepal's largest trading partner and has been investing in Nepal's hydropower sector. The Upper Karnali hydroelectric project, which is being built by an Indian company, is one of the flagship projects in this regard. During his visit, PM Dahal expressed his government's commitment towards addressing the concerns of the agitating Madhesi groups in Nepal, who have been demanding greater inclusion in the political process and benefits from the hydropower projects. The joint statement issued at the end of the visit reiterates the need for addressing the concerns of all segments of society while implementing hydropower projects.

Tourism Cooperation:

Tourism is another potential area of cooperation between Nepal and India. Nepal is home to several UNESCO world heritage sites, including the Lumbini, Pashupatinath Temple, and Swayambhunath Stupa, which are major tourist attractions. During his visit, PM Dahal emphasized the need for promoting tourism cooperation between the two countries, which has the potential to generate employment and revenue for the local people. The joint statement issued at the end of the visit identifies tourism as one of the key areas for enhancing people-to-people contacts and cultural exchanges between the two countries.

Connectivity Cooperation:

Connectivity is another important aspect of the Nepal-India relationship, given the geographical proximity of the two countries. The India-Nepal border stretches over 1,850 km and is connected through 26 border points. PM Dahal expressed his government's commitment towards enhancing connectivity between the two countries through the development of roads, railways, and airways. The joint statement issued at the end of the visit highlights the need for improving connectivity as a means of promoting economic integration and regional cooperation.

Conclusion:

PM Dahal's visit to India has been a significant milestone in the Nepal-India relationship. The visit has strengthened the existing bonds of friendship and cooperation between the two countries and generated a fresh wave of goodwill and trust. The joint statement issued at the end of the visit identifies several areas of cooperation between the two countries, including hydropower, trade and investment, tourism, people-to-people contacts, and connectivity. The visit has also highlighted the need for addressing the border issues through dialogue and mutual understanding and for revitalizing the stalled process of SAARC. The strengthening of the Nepal-India relationship has the potential to promote regional economic integration and enhance people-to-people contacts in South Asia.
