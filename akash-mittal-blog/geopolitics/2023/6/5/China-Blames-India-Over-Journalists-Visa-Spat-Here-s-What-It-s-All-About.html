China Blames India Over Journalists Visa Spat: Here's What It's All About

China recently accused India of intentionally blocking the visa applications of journalists from China's state-owned media organization, Xinhua News Agency. The accusation came after Indian authorities reportedly stalled the visa applications of at least five journalists working for Xinhua News Agency, setting off a diplomatic tussle between the two nations.

The Background

The journalists in question had applied for visa extensions, which are typically renewed a few weeks before their expiration. However, the applications were put on hold by the Indian authorities, who cited "technical issues." China accused India of deliberately delaying the visas to intimidate the journalists and limit their access to information.

Indian officials, on the other hand, claimed that the delay was due to a backlog of visa applications, which they have been trying to clear up. They asserted that the visa applications of the Xinhua journalists were being reviewed, and there was no intentional delay or prejudice against them.

The Case Studies

The current spat between China and India over the Xinhua journalists' visa applications is not the first instance of a tussle between the two nations over media access. In 2019, China denied a visa to an Indian journalist who worked for The Hindu. This was believed to be a retaliation against India for denying visas to a few Chinese journalists the previous year.

The spat between China and India has also spilled over into the digital space. In 2020, India banned over 200 Chinese mobile apps, including the popular social media platform TikTok, citing national security concerns. In retaliation, China banned several Indian mobile apps and websites, including the news aggregator app Inshorts and the e-commerce platform Shein.

The Conclusion

The recent tussle between China and India over the visa applications of journalists is just the latest in a long list of diplomatic skirmishes between the two nations. While India has denied any intentional delay or bias against Xinhua journalists, the incident highlights the growing mistrust and suspicion between the two nations.

It is important for both nations to communicate openly and transparently to avoid misunderstandings and prevent the escalation of any conflict in the future. It is also essential that both nations respect the role and freedom of the media to report the news objectively and independently.
