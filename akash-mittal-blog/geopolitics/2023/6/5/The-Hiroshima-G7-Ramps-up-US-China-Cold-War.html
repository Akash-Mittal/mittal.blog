The Hiroshima G7 Ramps up US-China Cold War

It was mid-September and the G7 leaders were gathered in Hiroshima for their annual meeting. The expectations were high, especially amid the ongoing trade war between the United States and China. However, what unfolded during the summit was beyond anyone's imagination.

Rising Tensions

The trade war between the US and China had already escalated to new heights, with both countries imposing tariffs on several goods. However, the tensions between the two nations have now spilled over to other areas, including technology, intellectual property, and even national security.

Both countries have accused each other of espionage and theft of intellectual property, leading to the US blacklisting Chinese technology giant Huawei. China, in turn, has sanctioned US companies like Apple, Boeing, and Lockheed Martin.

Case Studies

South China Sea Dispute

China's territorial claims in the South China Sea have been a point of contention between Beijing and Washington. China has been building military installations on artificial islands in the region, which the US considers a threat to its national security.

The US has been sending naval patrols in the region to assert its freedom of navigation rights, which China sees as a provocation. The situation reached a boiling point in 2018 when the US and China imposed tariffs on each other's goods.

TikTok Ban

In August 2020, President Trump signed an executive order to ban Chinese social media app TikTok unless it was sold to a US company within 45 days. The move was in response to concerns that the app was sharing user data with the Chinese government.

However, the ban was temporarily blocked by a federal judge and negotiations are ongoing for a potential sale to US companies like Microsoft or Oracle.

COVID-19 Pandemic

The COVID-19 pandemic has further worsened the relationship between the US and China. President Trump has repeatedly blamed China for not handling the outbreak properly and for hiding information from the world.

China, on the other hand, has accused the US of politicizing the virus and spreading misinformation. The distrust between the two nations has hindered global cooperation in fighting the pandemic.

Conclusion

The G7 leaders agreed to take a harsher stance towards China, condemning its human rights abuses in Hong Kong and Xinjiang, and calling for Taiwan's inclusion in the World Health Organization (WHO).

The US-China Cold War has now enveloped the entire world, with other nations being forced to pick sides. The consequences of this conflict could be catastrophic for the global economy and could even lead to military conflict.

Three key takeaway points:

  1. The US-China Cold War has escalated to new heights, affecting various areas like trade, technology, and national security.
  2. The situation has worsened due to issues like the South China Sea dispute, TikTok ban, and COVID-19 pandemic.
  3. The Hiroshima G7 summit has further increased tensions, with the G7 leaders condemning China's human rights abuses and calling for Taiwan's inclusion in the WHO.
