What Caused India's Deadly Train Accident in Balasore, Odisha?

On June 09, 2022, India witnessed yet another deadly train accident, this time in Balasore district of Odisha. The accident occurred when the Hirakhand Express train derailed and collided with a cargo train, resulting in the death of at least 32 people and injuring more than 100 others. The cause of the accident is still under investigation, but initial reports suggest that it could be due to a combination of human error, faulty equipment, and poor maintenance.

Human Error

Human error is one of the leading causes of train accidents in India. The railway system in India is vast and complex, and it requires a lot of skilled and experienced personnel to run it efficiently. However, in recent years, there has been a shortage of such personnel, and many of the existing ones are overworked and underpaid. This often leads to fatigue and lack of concentration, which can cause accidents.

For example, in 2019, the Kashi Vishwanath Express train collided with a stationary train near Prayagraj, killing at least 12 people and injuring more than 45 others. The investigation revealed that the driver of the Kashi Vishwanath Express train was fatigued and had failed to apply the brakes on time.

In another instance, in 2018, the Amritsar-Howrah Express train derailed in Bihar, killing at least 7 people and injuring more than 20 others. The investigation revealed that the driver of the train was under the influence of alcohol and had overspeed the train.

Faulty Equipment

The malfunctioning of equipment is another significant cause of train accidents in India. The railway system in India is old and outdated, and many of the trains and tracks have not been upgraded in years. This can lead to equipment failure, which can cause accidents. Moreover, the lack of adequate monitoring and maintenance of these equipment further exacerbates the problem.

For example, in 2020, the Bhubaneswar-New Delhi Rajdhani Express train derailed in Jharkhand, injuring at least 40 passengers. The investigation revealed that the derailment was caused due to a crack in the rail track.

In another instance, in 2016, the Indore-Patna Express train derailed in Kanpur, killing at least 146 people and injuring more than 300 others. The investigation revealed that the accident was caused due to a fracture in the track that had gone undetected during inspection.

Poor Maintenance

Poor maintenance is another crucial cause of train accidents in India. The railway system in India is vast and requires regular maintenance to ensure its smooth functioning. However, the lack of adequate funding and resources has led to poor maintenance of the trains and tracks, which can cause accidents.

For example, in 2017, the Utkal Express train derailed in Uttar Pradesh, killing at least 20 people and injuring more than 90 others. The investigation revealed that the accident was caused due to a lack of maintenance of the railway tracks.

In another instance, in 2015, the Patna-Indore Express train derailed in Madhya Pradesh, killing at least 150 people and injuring more than 250 others. The investigation revealed that the accident was caused due to the poor maintenance of the tracks and the train.

"India needs to invest in its railway system and take appropriate measures to ensure the safety of its passengers. This requires adequate funding, skilled personnel, and a rigorous monitoring and maintenance system."

In conclusion, train accidents in India are not uncommon, and they are often caused due to a combination of human error, faulty equipment, and poor maintenance. To prevent such accidents, it is essential to address each of these factors and take appropriate measures to ensure the safety of the passengers.
