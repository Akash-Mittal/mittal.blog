Don't Trust Pakistan with Western Weapons: India Tells US at Bilateral Meet

India has raised concerns about the US providing advanced weapons to Pakistan, citing its history of misuse and support for terrorism. During a bilateral meeting, Indian officials cautioned their US counterparts about the risks of arming Pakistan, and urged them to reconsider any plans to provide Pakistan with sophisticated military hardware. India has long accused Pakistan of supporting terrorism, and has pointed to incidents in which weapons provided to Pakistan were diverted to terrorist groups.

The issue was first raised by India's External Affairs Minister, who stated that "there is a high risk of these weapons falling into the wrong hands, and being used against India." India has also pointed out that Pakistan has previously used weapons supplied by the US against India, including during the Kargil war in 1999.

Furthermore, there have been numerous cases in which weapons provided to Pakistan have been diverted to militants or used against US forces. In 2011, it was revealed that the US had accidentally provided Pakistan with technology to make advanced heat-seeking missiles, which could potentially be used against American aircraft. The missiles were later found in the possession of Taliban fighters in Afghanistan.

"India's concerns are well-founded. Pakistan has a long history of misusing military aid, and supporting terrorist groups that target India and Afghanistan."

India has also pointed to the recent conflict in Nagorno-Karabakh, where Pakistan provided military support to Azerbaijan, including advanced drones and missiles. India has accused Pakistan of destabilizing the region by supporting Azerbaijan, and has warned that providing Pakistan with advanced weapons will only fuel regional tensions and instability.

Case Studies

There are several cases where Pakistan has misused weapons provided by the US and other western countries:

  • Kargil War: Pakistan used US-made Stinger missiles to target Indian aircraft during the Kargil war in 1999.
  • Taliban support: The US provided Pakistan with military aid to combat the Taliban, but some of the weapons and funds were later diverted to the Taliban.
  • Mumbai attacks: The terrorists who carried out the 2008 Mumbai attacks were using US-made GPS systems to navigate their way to their targets.

Conclusion

India's concerns about Pakistan's use of western weapons are not unfounded, given the country's history of supporting terrorism and misuse of military aid. Providing Pakistan with advanced weapons will only exacerbate regional tensions and instability, and could potentially lead to the weapons falling into the wrong hands. The US should reconsider its plans to provide Pakistan with sophisticated military hardware, and instead focus on addressing the root causes of terrorism in the region.
