1. The BrahMos Missile System

The BrahMos missile system is a joint venture between India's Defense Research and Development Organization and Russia's NPO Mashinostroyenia. The missile is named after the Brahmaputra River in India and the Moskva River in Russia. The US Department of Defense has praised the missile for its precision-guided capabilities, and it has been used successfully in a number of operations.

2. COVID-19 Response

During the COVID-19 pandemic, the US and India collaborated on a number of fronts, including the development of vaccines and the sharing of medical supplies. The two nations have also worked together to address the economic impacts of the pandemic.

3. The Malabar Exercise

The Malabar exercise is an annual naval exercise between India, the US, and Japan. The exercise focuses on enhancing cooperation and interoperability between the three nations in the Indo-Pacific region. The exercise has been held annually since 1992, but Japan only joined in recent years.
