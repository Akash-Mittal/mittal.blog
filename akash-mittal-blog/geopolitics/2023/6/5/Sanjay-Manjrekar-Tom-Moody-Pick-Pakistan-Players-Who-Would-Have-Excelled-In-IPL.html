Sanjay Manjrekar Tom Moody Pick Pakistan Players Who Would Have Excelled In IPL

Indian Premier League (IPL) is undoubtedly one of the biggest cricketing events in the world. The competition attracts top cricketers from around the globe who want to showcase their skills in the T20 format. Over the years, IPL has unearthed many talented players from different countries and given them a platform to shine and earn worldwide recognition.

Pakistan, on the other hand, has been unable to participate in IPL due to political tensions between India and Pakistan. However, many cricket experts, including former Indian cricketers Sanjay Manjrekar and Tom Moody, believe that Pakistani players would have excelled in the IPL. Here are some of the Pakistani players that could have made a significant impact in the IPL:

Shahid Afridi

Shahid Afridi is arguably one of the most explosive all-rounders in the history of cricket. He is capable of changing the course of a game with his powerful hitting and lethal leg-spin. Afridi has played in numerous T20 leagues around the world, and his performances have always been impressive. He has a strike rate of over 150 in T20 cricket and has taken more than 100 wickets in 100 matches, which is a testament to his skills. If he had been able to play in the IPL, he could have become one of the most sought-after players in the league.

Saeed Ajmal

Saeed Ajmal is one of the best spinners Pakistan has produced in recent times. He has played a vital role in Pakistan's success in international cricket. Ajmal has a vast array of variations, and he can deceive even the best batsmen with his doosra. In his prime, he was virtually unplayable, and many batsmen struggled to read his deliveries. Ajmal has played in T20 leagues around the world, and his performances have always been impressive. He could have been a valuable asset to any IPL team and could have taken wickets on tracks that assist spinners.

Babar Azam

Babar Azam is one of the most talented batsmen in the world. He has already established himself as one of Pakistan's finest batsmen and has been consistent in all formats of the game. Azam's batting style is elegant, and he can score runs at a brisk pace without taking unnecessary risks. He has a strike rate of over 130 in T20 cricket and has scored more than 1700 runs in 50 matches. If he had been able to play in the IPL, he could have been a valuable asset to any team and could have taken IPL by storm with his batting skills.

Conclusion

Despite being unable to participate in IPL, Pakistani players have made a significant impact in T20 cricket around the world. The likes of Afridi, Ajmal, and Azam have been amongst the best T20 players in the world and have played crucial roles in their team's success. If given a chance, Pakistani players could have become a vital part of the IPL and could have added more diversity and excitement to the competition. Hopefully, in the future, the political tensions between India and Pakistan will be resolved, and Pakistani players will have a chance to showcase their skills in IPL.
