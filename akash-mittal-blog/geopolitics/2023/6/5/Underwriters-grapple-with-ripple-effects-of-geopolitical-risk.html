Underwriters grapple with ripple effects of geopolitical risk

Geopolitical risks have always been a challenging issue for underwriters as the uncertainty introduced by these risks can have ripple effects across multiple industries and economies. From trade tensions between major players to proxy wars in smaller countries, geopolitical events have direct and indirect consequences that can impact insurance policies and premium rates across the board.

The Story of Geopolitical Risk

One of the most prominent examples of geopolitical risk is the ongoing trade war between the United States and China. The trade war between the world's two largest economies has had a significant impact on global markets and supply chains. As a result, the insurance industry has seen financial losses due to trade disruptions and changing political landscapes.

In another case, the proxy war in Yemen has also had ripple effects on the global market. Yemen is located in a strategic location that connects Europe to Asia and it controls the Bab-el-Mandeb strait - a crucial route for oil shipments. The protracted conflict between Saudi Arabia and Iran-backed Houthi rebels has created instability in the region and impacted insurance policies for shipping companies as they have had to re-evaluate the risks of navigating through the strait.

Additionally, geopolitical risks also create indirect effects that can impact underwriters and insurers. For instance, political unrest and social tensions in a country could lead to business interruptions, forcing companies to file for insurance claims.

Case Studies

Trade War between US and China

The ongoing trade war between the US and China has had significant impacts on the global economy, including the insurance industry. In the initial stages of the trade war, marine cargo and trade credit insurers were the most impacted as they covered losses resulting from disrupted or delayed shipments. Moreover, these insurers also witnessed increased demand for their services as companies looked to protect themselves against future risks.

However, as the trade issues have escalated, the impact has trickled down to other industries as well. For instance, property and casualty insurers covering supply chains have experienced higher levels of risk due to the trade uncertainty. Additionally, political risk insurers have also seen an increase in demand for their services, as businesses look to protect themselves against losses resulting from direct or indirect consequences of the trade war.

Geopolitical Risk in the Oil and Gas Industry

The oil and gas industry is one of the most vulnerable sectors to geopolitical risks. Events such as wars, revolutions, and outbreaks of political violence, all have a direct impact on the industry. Over the years, the industry has witnessed an increase in demand for political risk insurance coverage as companies look to mitigate the risks of supply disruptions and business interruption.

For instance, after the Arab Spring, the political situation in certain countries became volatile, and companies operating in those regions had to reassess their strategies and acquire political risk insurance coverage to mitigate their risks. Additionally, the attack on Saudi Arabia's oil infrastructure in 2019 also highlights the importance of political risk insurance coverage as it protects businesses against unforeseen events that can result in significant financial losses.

Conclusion

Geopolitical risks are complex and unpredictable, making it difficult for underwriters to assess and manage the risks. However, as the world continues to undergo rapid changes and as geopolitical tensions continue to rise, insurers and underwriters must stay vigilant and adapt to the new reality.

In conclusion, geopolitics can have far-reaching consequences that impact multiple industries and sectors. Insurers and underwriters need to be proactive and adapt their risk management strategies to mitigate these risks. The geopolitical landscape will continue to evolve, and it is essential to stay ahead of the curve to manage risk effectively.
