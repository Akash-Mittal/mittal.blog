Erdogan Won But Turkiye May Lose

Story

In the early 2021, Turkey faced a crisis when the United States imposed sanctions over its purchase of Russian S-400 missile defense systems. However, Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdogan remained defiant and refused to back down from the deal. While he won the battle, Turkey may now lose the war as this decision may have serious implications on the country's long-term interests.

Examples

  • In 2017, Turkey signed an agreement with Russia to purchase S-400 missile defense systems. The deal was criticized by the United States and NATO, who argued that the system was incompatible with the alliance's defense network.
  • In December 2020, the United States imposed sanctions on Turkey over the S-400 deal. The sanctions included a ban on all US export licenses and loans to Turkey's Presidency of Defense Industries (SSB) and blocks on the assets of SSB's head and three other officials.
  • Turkey responded by accusing the United States of interfering in its domestic affairs and remaining defiant on the S-400 deal. Turkish President Erdogan stated that Turkey would not back down from its decision and that the country's security concerns were a top priority.
  • The S-400 deal has strained Turkey's relations with the United States and its other NATO allies. The United States has threatened to impose further sanctions if Turkey does not abandon the S-400 deal, and there are concerns that Turkey's membership in NATO may be at risk.
  • Turkey has also faced criticism over its human rights record, including its crackdown on political dissent and freedom of the press. The country's recent withdrawal from the Istanbul Convention, an international treaty to combat violence against women, has raised further concerns about its commitment to human rights and gender equality.

Case Studies

Let us take a look at some case studies to understand the possible implications of Turkey's decision to purchase the S-400 missile defense system:

  1. Impact on Turkey's economy: Turkey's economy has already been facing challenges due to the COVID-19 pandemic, and the US sanctions over the S-400 deal have further deteriorated the situation. The sanctions have resulted in a significant drop in Turkey's currency and have affected the country's ability to access international markets. If the situation persists, it could lead to a major economic crisis in Turkey.
  2. Impact on Turkey's defense capabilities: While the S-400 missile defense system is a powerful weapon, it is not compatible with NATO's defense network, which Turkey is a part of. This means that Turkey's ability to work with its NATO allies on defense matters is limited. Furthermore, if Turkey faces a threat from a NATO member, the S-400 system may not be effective in providing adequate defense.
  3. Impact on Turkey's relations with other countries: Turkey's decision to purchase the S-400 missile defense system has strained its relations with the United States and other NATO allies. This not only affects Turkey's ability to work with its allies on defense matters but also its ability to cooperate on other issues such as trade, diplomacy, and regional stability.

Conclusion

Turkey's decision to purchase the S-400 missile defense system may have been a victory for President Erdogan in the short term, but it may have serious implications for the country in the long run. The decision has already strained Turkey's relations with the United States and its NATO allies, affected its economy, and limited its defense capabilities. In order to secure its interests, Turkey will need to carefully evaluate its decisions and work towards building positive relations with its allies.
