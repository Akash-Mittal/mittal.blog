Templeton Dragon Fund: Time to Nibble Again

China's economy has come a long way since the last global financial crisis. Over the past decade, it has transitioned from an export-driven economy to a consumer-driven one, with growing middle class and increasing purchasing power.

For investors looking to capitalize on China's long-term growth prospects, the Templeton Dragon Fund (TDF) provides a compelling opportunity. With a diversified portfolio of Chinese equities, the TDF offers exposure to a range of sectors and industries poised for growth.

Time to Nibble

In recent months, the TDF has seen a pullback in its share price due to concerns over global trade tensions and slowing economic growth in China. However, we believe that this short-term volatility presents a buying opportunity for long-term investors.

As quoted by Franklin Templeton Investments portfolio manager, Chetan Sehgal, "For us, it's a matter of remaining disciplined and focused on our long-term investment approach. We believe China has a lot of that long-term growth potential that we're looking for in different sectors."

Case Studies

Case Study 1: Alibaba (BABA)

Alibaba is China's largest e-commerce company, with a market cap of over $430 billion. Despite the recent trade tensions and economic slowdown in China, Alibaba has continued to grow at an impressive rate. In its most recent quarterly earnings report, the company reported a 41% year-over-year increase in revenue.

Furthermore, Alibaba has been aggressively expanding into new markets and sectors, such as cloud computing and digital payments. Its recent partnership with Starbucks to open "Starbucks Delivery Kitchens" in China is a clear example of the company's ambition to become a dominant player in every aspect of Chinese consumers' lives.

Case Study 2: China Mobile (CHL)

China Mobile is the largest telecommunications company in China, with over 900 million subscribers. The company has benefited from the rapid growth in China's mobile internet market, which is expected to reach $223 billion by 2022.

China Mobile's financials remain strong, with steady revenue growth and a healthy dividend yield. The company is also investing heavily in new technologies, such as 5G mobile networks and artificial intelligence, which should drive future growth.

Conclusion

Despite recent market volatility, we believe that the long-term growth prospects for China remain strong. The TDF provides investors with a diversified portfolio of Chinese equities, including companies like Alibaba and China Mobile, that are poised for growth.

As always, investors should perform their due diligence and consider their own risk tolerance and investment objectives before making any investment decisions.
