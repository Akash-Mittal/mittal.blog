India and US Discuss Upgrading Defense Ties to Counter China

India and the United States have been engaged in a series of talks to enhance their defense cooperation in a bid to counter China's growing assertiveness in the region. This move comes as China continues to flex its military muscles in the Indo-Pacific, where it has long-standing territorial disputes with several countries, including India. Both countries have been looking to deepen their strategic partnership over the past few years, with the US referring to India as its "major defense partner."

Why India-US Defense Ties Are Gaining Importance?

The Indo-Pacific region is witnessing a significant push by China to expand its military presence in the region, which is becoming a source of concern for other countries. Countries like India oppose China's assertiveness as they see it as an attempt by China to dominate the region and challenge India's interests. With this backdrop, India and the US are looking to cooperate closely to safeguard their interests, and this has led to several meetings between the two countries' defense ministers.

Recent Developments in India-US Defense Cooperation

In October 2020, India and the US conducted the third round of the 2+2 ministerial dialogue, where they explored ways to deepen their defense ties. During the talks, the two sides signed an agreement called BECA (Basic Exchange and Cooperation Agreement). The agreement enables the two countries to exchange geospatial data, which will help the Indian military to increase its accuracy of missiles and drones. It will also enable the US to share advanced satellite and sensor technology with India.

BECA is the fourth major defense agreement that India has signed with the US, after the Logistics Exchange Memorandum of Agreement (LEMOA), the Communications Compatibility and Security Agreement (COMCASA), and the Industrial Security Annex (ISA).

The Future of India-US Defense Ties

The future looks promising for India-US defense ties as both countries are looking to cement their partnership further. One of the main areas of cooperation is likely to be the development of India's defense manufacturing sector. With India's intention to significantly increase the number of defense equipment manufactured within the country, it is looking for partnerships with countries that can provide the necessary technology and expertise for joint ventures.

The two countries are also exploring opportunities to collaborate on new and emerging technologies such as Artificial Intelligence, Quantum Computing, and Cybersecurity. These areas have become critical in the defense sector, and India is keen to build its capabilities in these areas.

Conclusion

India and the US have been working to upgrade their defense cooperation for a few years now, but recent developments have taken it to a new level. The Chinese threat and the emergence of new technologies have made it imperative for the two countries to collaborate closely to keep their interests secure. The signing of BECA and the talks on emerging technologies show that the two countries are keen on taking the partnership to the next level.
