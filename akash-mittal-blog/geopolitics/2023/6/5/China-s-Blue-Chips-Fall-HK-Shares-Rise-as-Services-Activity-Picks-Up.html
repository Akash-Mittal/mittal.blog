China's Blue Chips Fall, HK Shares Rise as Services Activity Picks Up

Investors are closely watching the stock market in China, as its blue chips experienced a fall while Hong Kong shares enjoyed a rise due to services activity picking up.

Case Studies

Case Study 1

In September 2021, China's largest steelmaker, China Baowu Steel Group, announced a profit drop of 61% due to China's regulatory crackdown on the steel industry.

Case Study 2

On the other hand, Hong Kong's Hang Seng Index rose by 2.25%, driven by optimistic services activity in the city. For instance, gaming giant, Melco Resorts & Entertainment, saw its shares rise by 10% as it resumed full operations at its City of Dreams Manila casino in the Philippines.

Conclusion

China's regulatory crackdown is having a significant impact on its largest companies, causing a drop in blue chips. However, Hong Kong shares are flourishing thanks to are picking up in services activity. It is important for investors to monitor these changes and act accordingly.
