Upgrading Ties with India: A Key Defense Strategy Against China

The US Defense Secretary recently discussed the importance of upgrading ties with India as a strategy to counter China's military and economic influence in the region. In a speech at the Atlantic Council virtual event, Secretary Lloyd Austin emphasized the need to partner with India in defense and security matters.

Let's explore the reasons why upgrading ties with India is a vital defense strategy against China:

India's Strategic Importance

India's geography is strategically significant in the Indo-Pacific region. Located at the crossroads of the Indian Ocean and the South China Sea, India holds the key to the sea lanes of communication, through which trillions of dollars' worth of global trade flows. India's location has made it a critical ally for both the US and Japan, who are looking to limit China's expansion in the region.

Moreover, India has the world's second-largest population and the fifth-largest economy in terms of nominal GDP. Its market potential is enormous, making it a desirable economic partner for the US.

Case Studies

Two recent developments showcase the growing partnership between the US and India:

  • The QUAD: The Quad is the informal alliance between the US, India, Japan, and Australia, formed in 2007 to counter China's growing influence in the Indo-Pacific region. The Quad's primary objective is to maintain a free, open and rules-based order, which China is actively challenging. The Quad's defense ministers recently held their first in-person meeting in Tokyo, where they discussed strengthening defense cooperation in the region.
  • Defense Partnership: In October 2021, the US and India signed a new defense pact, expanding their defense partnership. Under the pact, the US will provide India with advanced military technology and strengthen its economic and security relationship with the country. The agreement includes cooperation in areas such as maritime security, counter-terrorism, and defense trade.

Conclusion

Upgrading ties with India is a vital defense strategy against China. India's strategic location, massive population, and growing economy make it a critical ally for the US, both militarily and economically. The QUAD and the new defense pact between the US and India showcase the growing partnership between the two countries, which is likely to further strengthen in the coming years.
