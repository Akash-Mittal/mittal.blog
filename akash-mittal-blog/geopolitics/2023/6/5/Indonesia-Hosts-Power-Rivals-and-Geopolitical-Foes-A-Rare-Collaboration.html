Indonesia Hosts Power Rivals and Geopolitical Foes - A Rare Collaboration

Indonesia, an archipelago of over 17,000 islands and a rising power in Southeast Asia, has found itself to be a neutral ground for global power rivals and geopolitical foes to come together in rare joint efforts for the greater good. Indonesia has been able to use its unique position and diplomatic skills to bring long-standing rivals to the negotiation table and foster cooperation between them. Here are some examples:

China and the US - Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) Collaboration

The US and China, the world's largest economy and the rising power, respectively, have been engaged in a trade war that has been going on for several years. The two countries have been engaged in a geopolitical struggle in the South China Sea, with both asserting sovereignty over the contested waters. However, the US and China have come together to protect the marine environment through the establishment of Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) in the South China Sea. Indonesia serves as a hub for the project, with MPAs being established in Raja Ampat and in the Bird's Head Seascape. The initiative is one of the few remaining areas of cooperation between the two countries and highlights the potential for collaboration even among rivals.

India and Pakistan - Tsunami Relief Efforts

India and Pakistan are two South Asian neighbors who have long been geopolitical foes. The two countries have engaged in several wars and have a tense relationship. However, in 2004, a devastating tsunami hit the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, which are territories of India and were severely affected by the disaster. Pakistan, despite its diplomatic tensions with India, was one of the first countries to offer assistance to India. The Pakistani Navy provided medical aid and sent helicopters to the islands to help with relief efforts. The assistance led to a positive shift in relations between the two countries and demonstrated the power of collaboration even between sworn enemies.

Conclusion

Indonesia has proven itself to be a valuable mediator in bridging the gap between geopolitical foes and global power rivals. The examples of cooperation between China and the US, as well as India and Pakistan, demonstrate the potential for even the most unlikely of alliances to work together. Through its unique position and diplomatic skills, Indonesia continues to foster collaboration among nations and promote peace and stability in the region.
