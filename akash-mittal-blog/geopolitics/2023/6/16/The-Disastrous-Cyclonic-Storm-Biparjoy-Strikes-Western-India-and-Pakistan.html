The Disastrous Cyclonic Storm "Biparjoy" Strikes Western India and Pakistan



Image Credits https://tvpworld.com/70519614/seven-die-as-severe-biparjoy-barrels-towards-western-india-pakistan

As the world battles the Covid-19 pandemic, a new disaster threatens the lives of millions of people in western India and Pakistan - the severe cyclonic storm "Biparjoy". The storm has already claimed seven lives and is expected to land around Thursday evening between Mandvi in India's Gujarat state and Karachi in southern Pakistan.

"Biparjoy" is a powerful cyclonic storm that originated from the Arabian Sea. It has a wind speed of 185-195 kmph and is expected to bring heavy rains, strong winds, and flooding in its wake. The Indian Meteorological Department (IMD) has issued a red alert and warned people to stay indoors and follow safety measures.

The Impact of "Biparjoy"

The cyclonic storm "Biparjoy" has already caused significant damage in its path. Seven people have lost their lives, and many others have been injured or displaced. The storm is expected to impact several districts in Gujarat, including Bhavnagar, Amreli, Gir-Somnath, and Junagadh. The coastal areas of Saurashtra are also likely to be affected.

In Pakistan, the storm is expected to hit several coastal areas resulting in power outages, flooding, and damage to property and infrastructure. Karachi, the country's largest city, is likely to be severely impacted by the cyclone.

Case Studies of Previous Cyclonic Storms

Cyclonic storms are not new to India and Pakistan. Both countries are prone to cyclones due to their proximity to the Bay of Bengal and the Arabian Sea. Here are a few case studies of previous cyclonic storms in the region:

Cyclone Amphan

Cyclone Amphan hit the eastern coast of India in May 2020, causing extensive damage in West Bengal and Odisha. The storm claimed over 100 lives, displaced over 4 million people, and caused damage worth billions of dollars.

Cyclone Hudhud

Cyclone Hudhud hit the eastern coast of India in October 2014. The storm caused massive damage in the states of Andhra Pradesh and Odisha, claiming over 100 lives and leaving millions of people homeless.

Cyclone Phailin

Cyclone Phailin hit the eastern coast of India in October 2013, causing significant damage in Odisha and Andhra Pradesh. The storm claimed over 40 lives and left millions of people displaced.

The Need for Precautions and Preparedness

Cyclonic storms can cause massive damage and loss of life. Therefore, it is essential to take precautions and be prepared to face the storm's impact. Here are a few safety measures that can help during a cyclonic storm:

  • Stay indoors and avoid going out unless necessary.
  • Keep essential items like food, water, medicines, and torches handy.
  • Stay informed about the latest weather updates and advisories.
  • Evacuate to a safe place if necessary.
  • Switch off electrical appliances and keep them unplugged to avoid electrical shocks.

Conclusion

The cyclonic storm "Biparjoy" is a reminder of the need for preparedness and precautions during natural disasters. The impact of the storm can be catastrophic, and it is essential to stay informed, stay safe, and be prepared for any eventuality.
