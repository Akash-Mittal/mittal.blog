Examples of America's Interest in India

John's story is not unique. Many American businesses and policymakers have recognized the potential of India as a strategic partner in the global economy. Some examples of this interest include:

  • Increased trade and investment: In recent years, the US and India have strengthened their economic ties, with bilateral trade growing rapidly and mutual investments reaching new heights. According to the US-India Business Council, US companies have invested over $45 billion in India since 2000, and Indian companies have invested over $15 billion in the US. This trend is expected to continue, as both countries seek to expand their markets and diversify their supply chains.
  • Technology cooperation: India has emerged as a hub for innovation and entrepreneurship, with its vibrant start-up ecosystem attracting global attention. Many US tech giants, such as Google, Microsoft, and Amazon, have established their presence in India and collaborated with local partners to develop new products and services. In addition, the two countries have signed several agreements on cybersecurity, space exploration, and artificial intelligence, which aim to enhance their technological capabilities and address common challenges.
  • Strategic alignment: The US and India share common values and interests, such as democracy, human rights, and regional stability. Both countries have supported each other in many international forums, such as the United Nations, and cooperated on issues such as counterterrorism, climate change, and defense. The US has also recognized India as a major defense partner, and signed several defense deals, such as the purchase of Apache helicopters and C-17 transport planes.

General Electric (GE) has partnered with Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), an Indian IT services company, to transform GE's global operations and supply chain through digital technologies. Furthermore, this initiative has enhanced Coca-Cola's brand value and social impact in India, and promoted the UN Sustainable Development Goals.
