Surviving Cyclone Biparjoy: Lessons from Yesterday

The moment we thought we were safe and sound from natural calamities, we have been proven wrong, yet again. Cyclone Biparjoy has hit the coasts of India and Pakistan bringing havoc and chaos in its wake. The South Asian countries have been battling the severe storm with mass evacuations and coastal warnings. However, this is not the first time that the region has dealt with such disaster, and it surely won't be the last.

Remembering the Past with Case Studies

History is our best teacher. Throughout the years, the South Asian countries have been hit with hurricanes, cyclones, and tsunamis taking countless lives and leaving a trail of destruction. Looking back, there are several case studies that we can learn from and apply to present situations such as Cyclone Biparjoy.

The Great Bhola Cyclone of 1970

This cyclone hit East Pakistan (present-day Bangladesh) on November 12, 1970, and took the lives of more than 500,000 people, making it the deadliest storm in history. The government had neither prepared nor taken any preventive measures to counter this natural disaster. The lack of communication systems, proper evacuation plans and shelters, and awareness campaigns resulted in such a massive loss of life.

Super Cyclonic Storm Amphan of 2020

Amphan was one of the strongest cyclonic storms in the Bay of Bengal region, which hit India and Bangladesh in May 2020. The storm caused extensive damage to properties and crops, and the death toll was 128 in India and 26 in Bangladesh. However, due to the better preparation and early warning systems employed by the government, the total destruction and loss could've been much worse.

Pakistan Floods of 2010

Pakistan's worst natural disaster in recent times was a series of floods that hit the country in 2010. The floods affected 20 million people, killed 1,700, and caused USD 10 billion worth of damages. The reason for the disaster was heavy rains that overwhelmed the dams, ultimately causing bursts and massive water inundation. The government had failed to regulate and maintain the dams and other water reservoirs despite severe weather predictions, which resulted in such colossal damage.

Lessons Learned

These case studies have highlighted the importance of being prepared and proactive before, during, and after such natural disasters. Here are three significant lessons that we can all take back home amidst Cyclone Biparjoy:

Early Warning and Communication is Key

One of the most crucial factors in mitigating the effects of hurricanes and cyclones is early warning systems. The government and disaster management teams must have established communication lines and networks to provide information to the public as soon as possible. The general public must also be alert and responsive in receiving and following these early warnings to ensure timely evacuations and safe sheltering.

Preparing for Disaster

Preparing for the worst-case scenario must be a top priority of the government and citizens. The authorities must have adequate preparedness and response systems in place to minimize loss as much as possible. This includes training of local communities, stockpiling of emergency supplies like food, water, and medicine, and having efficient evacuation routes and shelter facilities. Citizens must also take responsibility for their safety by staying informed and following all emergency protocols.

Environment Conservation as the Key to Healthier and Resilient Communities

Human activities have adversely affected the environment leading to climate change. This has resulted in a higher incidence of natural disasters, including hurricanes, cyclones, and tsunamis. Therefore, the final lesson is that we must invest in reducing the carbon footprint, protecting ecosystems, planting trees, supporting organic farming, and taking other environment-friendly measures. A healthy environment means a better future with more robust and resilient communities.
