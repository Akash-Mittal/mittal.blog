India's Oil Demand Growth to Overtake China's by 2027: IEA



Image Credits https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/economy/indias-oil-demand-growth-to-overtake-chinas-by-2027-iea/article66968776.ece

Once upon a time, China was dominating the oil market with its tremendous demand growth. However, according to a recent report by the International Energy Agency (IEA), India is expected to overtake China in oil demand growth by the year 2027.

This news is not a surprise given India's growing economy, heavy industrialization, urbanization, rising disposable income, and rapidly expanding transportation sector. India is already the third biggest oil consumer after the US and China, and its rising oil demand growth is further strengthening its position in the global oil market.

Let's take a look at some examples of India's soaring oil demand:

  • In 2017-18, India's oil demand growth was 5.3%, nearly double the average growth rate of other major oil consumers such as the US, China, and Japan.
  • India is the world's fourth-largest importer of crude oil and petroleum products, and its net oil imports account for approximately 80% of its total oil consumption.
  • India's gasoline demand growth is expected to rise by 7.2% in 2019, the highest among all major economies in the world.

These examples clearly indicate the magnitude of India's growing oil consumption. But, what are the factors behind this surge?

Factors Driving India's Oil Demand Growth

India's oil demand growth is chiefly driven by the following factors:

  • Economic Growth: India's economy has been growing at an impressive rate of over 6%, and is projected to grow at 7.5% in the next fiscal year. This growth has led to increased demand for various products and services, particularly in the transportation and manufacturing sectors.
  • Urbanization: India is rapidly urbanizing, and this has increased the concentration of population in cities. With more people living in cities, transportation demand has grown, leading to increased oil consumption.
  • Rising Disposable Income: With the growth in the economy, incomes have also risen. This has led to more people owning cars and driving farther distances, thereby increasing the demand for fuel.
  • Rapidly Expanding Transportation Sector: India's transportation sector is expanding at a breakneck pace, with the government investing heavily in the development of roads, highways, airports, and seaports. This is leading to increased demand for fuel, both for personal and commercial transportation.

All these factors combine to make India's oil demand growth a force to reckon with in the global energy market. There are also some case studies that highlight the impact of India's oil demand growth on the global energy sector.

Case Studies

Case Study 1: Oil Prices Spike Due to India's Growing Demand

India's growing oil demand has led to a spike in global oil prices. According to analysts, the rise in oil prices in 2018 was led by demand growth from India. In fact, India's oil demand growth was the main reason behind the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) decision to cut production in order to stabilize prices.

Case Study 2: India's Electric Vehicle Plan Hinges on Growing Oil Demand

India has made an ambitious plan to have all-electric cars on its roads by 2030. However, according to experts, this plan hinges on the growth of India's oil demand. This is because India currently lacks the infrastructure and technology needed to support a massive shift to electric vehicles. In the short term, India's oil demand growth is necessary to fund the development of this infrastructure and technology.

Case Study 3: India's Crude Oil Import Bill Swells Due to Rising Demand

India's crude oil import bill has been swelling due to its growing oil demand. In the financial year 2017-2018, India paid $87.7 billion for crude oil imports, up almost 25% from the previous year. This rise in import bill puts pressure on India's current account deficit and exchange rates, leading to macroeconomic imbalances.

These case studies clearly indicate that India's oil demand growth has far-reaching implications for the global energy sector. In light of this, what can be the key takeaways?

Conclusion

  1. India's Growing Oil Demand is a Challenge and an Opportunity: India's growing oil demand is both a challenge and an opportunity for the global energy sector. While it puts pressure on oil prices and energy security, it also presents the opportunity for investment and innovation in the sector.
  2. India Needs Diversified Sources of Energy: Given the rising oil import bill and other macroeconomic challenges, India needs to diversify its sources of energy. This means investing in renewable energy and other non-fossil fuel-based sources of energy.
  3. India Needs Sustainable and Inclusive Growth: While India's oil demand growth is impressive, it cannot come at the cost of sustainability and inclusivity. India needs to adopt a sustainable and inclusive growth model that addresses issues such as climate change, energy access, and social inequality.
