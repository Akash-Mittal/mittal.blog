India emerges as a major beneficiary as supply chains migrate from China



Image Credits https://odishatv.in/news/business/india-seen-as-major-beneficiary-as-supply-chains-migrate-from-china-206979

It was in the year 2020 when the world witnessed a pandemic that affected every country in the world. The outbreak of COVID-19 disrupted global supply chains, bringing to light the vulnerability of organizations that were heavily reliant on a single source of supply. Many companies were forced to shut down their production lines, and various economies suffered severe consequences. It was a wakeup call for organizations to look for alternatives and diversify their supply chains.

In the post-COVID world, companies started doing just that, and India emerged as a potential beneficiary. India's geography, along with its large market and skilled workforce, makes it an attractive option for companies that are looking to move their supply chains out of China. The Indian government is also making efforts to attract significant foreign investments and build a favorable ecosystem for businesses.

Let's take a look at some companies that have moved their supply chains to India:

Apple

In July 2020, Apple began moving its production of iPad tablets and MacBook laptops from China to India. This move came after the Indian government implemented some favorable policies for electronics manufacturing. Apple is using one of its key suppliers, Foxconn, to manufacture products in India, creating significant job opportunities for the locals.

Samsung

Samsung, the South Korean multinational conglomerate, also has a significant presence in India. The company produces over 60% of its smartphones in India, with plans to increase this number to 90% in the coming years. Samsung is investing heavily in India, setting up a manufacturing plant in Uttar Pradesh and focusing on assembling its flagship phones in the country.

Xiaomi

Xiaomi, the Chinese smartphone and electronics company, is another major player in the Indian market. The company manufactures most of its phones in India, with the aim of making India a global manufacturing hub. Xiaomi's investments in India exceed USD $1 billion, and the company is expanding its supply chain and workforce in the country.

These are just a few examples of how India is emerging as a major beneficiary as supply chains migrate from China. Let's take a closer look at India's advantages:

India's Advantages in Supply Chain Diversification

Large Market

India has a population of over 1.3 billion, making it one of the largest markets in the world. Companies that move their supply chains to India can tap into this vast market and expand their customer base. With a growing middle class, the demand for goods and services in India is ever-increasing, making it a profitable destination for companies.

Skilled Workforce

India is known for its skilled workforce, with a vast pool of engineers, technicians, and other professionals. Indian professionals are highly sought after for their technical expertise and problem-solving skills, making them ideal for manufacturing and supply chain operations. Companies that relocate their supply chains to India can take advantage of this skilled workforce to drive innovation and growth.

Government Support

The Indian government is making efforts to attract foreign investments and build a favorable ecosystem for businesses. The government has implemented several policies to promote electronics manufacturing, such as the Production-Linked Incentive Scheme, which offers incentives for electronic manufacturing companies. The government is also improving infrastructure, simplifying procedures, and reducing bureaucracy to ease the process of doing business in India.

Conclusion

COVID-19 has highlighted the importance of diversification in the supply chain, and India is emerging as a major beneficiary of this shift. With its large market, skilled workforce, and favorable government policies, India is becoming an increasingly attractive destination for companies that are looking to move their supply chains out of China. Companies that move to India can access a growing market, a skilled workforce, and a favorable business environment, making it a win-win situation for all.
