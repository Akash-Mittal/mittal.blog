Bangladesh Leads On HDI Parameters: A Closer Look



Image Credits https://www.weeklyblitz.net/news/bangladesh-has-forged-way-ahead-on-parameters-of-hdi-compared-to-most-countries-including-india-jitendra-kumar-ojha/

Once considered a basket case, Bangladesh has come a long way in terms of human development. From reducing its infant mortality rate to increasing life expectancy and literacy rates, the country has made substantial improvements on various parameters of human development index (HDI).

The country's success in improving HDI has been recognized by various global agencies, including the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP). According to the latest Human Development Report by UNDP, Bangladesh ranked 133rd out of 189 countries in terms of overall HDI score. The country's HDI score of 0.632 is classified as medium human development, which is higher than the average for South Asia as a region (0.642).

So, how did Bangladesh achieve this remarkable feat? Let's take a closer look at some of the key factors that contributed to it:

Investing in Education

Education is often considered a key component of human development. Bangladesh has made significant investments in education over the years, which has led to a considerable increase in literacy rates. According to the World Bank, the country's literacy rate increased from 23% in 1971 to 73% in 2018.

One of the key initiatives that helped improve education in Bangladesh was the government's decision to make primary education free and compulsory in 1990. This move not only increased enrollment rates but also helped reduce gender disparities in education. The country has also invested in teacher training programs, curriculum development, and infrastructure improvement to ensure quality education for its citizens.

Case Study: BRAC's Education Program

BRAC, one of the world's largest NGOs, has played a significant role in improving education outcomes in Bangladesh. The organization runs a comprehensive education program that targets disadvantaged children in rural areas. The program not only provides formal education but also offers life skills training, health education, and other support services to ensure the holistic development of children. According to BRAC, their education program has reached over 12 million children since its inception.

Improving Healthcare

Access to quality healthcare is essential for human development. Bangladesh has made significant progress in improving its healthcare infrastructure over the years. The country has been successful in reducing its maternal mortality rate, infant mortality rate, and under-five mortality rate.

One of the key initiatives that helped improve healthcare in Bangladesh was the government's decision to increase healthcare spending. The country's healthcare spending as a percentage of GDP increased from 3.7% in 2010 to 7.9% in 2018.

Case Study: Grameen Health Program

Grameen Health Program, a subsidiary of Grameen Bank, provides healthcare services to the poor in rural areas of Bangladesh. The program offers affordable health services, health education, and health insurance to ensure that the poor have access to quality healthcare. According to Grameen Health, their services have reached over 2 million people since its inception.

Promoting Gender Equality

Gender inequality is a significant barrier to human development. Bangladesh has made substantial progress in reducing gender gaps in education, healthcare, and political representation. The country has also implemented policies and programs to empower women and promote women's rights.

One of the significant milestones in the country's journey towards gender equality was the passage of the Domestic Violence (Prevention and Protection) Act in 2010. The law criminalizes various forms of violence against women, including physical, sexual, and psychological abuse.

Case Study: The Garment Industry

The garment industry is a significant employer in Bangladesh, providing jobs to millions of women. The industry has played a crucial role in promoting gender equality by empowering women workers and providing them with economic opportunities. According to a study by International Labour Organization (ILO), women workers constitute 80% of the country's garment industry workforce.

Conclusion

Bangladesh's success in improving HDI is a testament to the country's resilience and determination. The country has overcome various challenges, including natural disasters, political instability, and poverty, to achieve significant progress in human development. Bangladesh's experience offers valuable lessons for other developing countries that are striving to improve their HDI scores. Investing in education, healthcare, and promoting gender equality are essential components of any strategy aimed at improving human development.
