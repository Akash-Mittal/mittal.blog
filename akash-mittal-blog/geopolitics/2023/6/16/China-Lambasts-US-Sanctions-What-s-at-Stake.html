China Lambasts US Sanctions: What's at Stake?



Image Credits https://asianews.network/china-slams-us-sanctions-on-certain-enterprises-individuals/

As the United States continues to impose sanctions on certain enterprises and individuals, China voices its disapproval and anger towards this move. The latest round of sanctions include Chinese telecom giant Huawei and several Chinese officials believed to be involved in human rights abuses in Xinjiang. These sanctions have far-reaching consequences, not just for the sanctioned entities, but also for the larger geo-political climate between the US and China.

Case Studies

One of the most prominent cases of US sanctions on China is the ongoing saga of Huawei. The US government has accused Huawei of stealing trade secrets, violating sanctions on Iran, and posing a national security threat due to its alleged connections to the Chinese government. As a result, Huawei has been banned from doing business with US companies and the US has been pressuring its allies to exclude Huawei from their 5G networks.

Another case is the recent sanctions on Chinese officials involved in human rights abuses in Xinjiang, where China has been accused of detaining over one million Uighur Muslims in internment camps. The sanctioned officials include Chen Quanguo, the Communist Party secretary in Xinjiang, and Zhu Hailun, a former deputy Communist Party secretary in Xinjiang.

Consequences of Sanctions

The sanctions have a number of consequences for both China and the US. China has accused the US of interfering in its internal affairs and violating international law. The US, meanwhile, sees the sanctions as a way to hold China accountable for its actions.

For Huawei, the sanctions have led to a decline in its global market share and revenue. The company has been forced to find alternatives to US-made components, which has resulted in delays and higher costs. The sanctions on Xinjiang officials, on the other hand, have limited their travel and access to financial resources, but have not stopped the alleged human rights abuses.

Conclusion

The US sanctions on China have far-reaching consequences, both for the sanctioned entities and for the larger geo-political climate between the two countries. China's response to the sanctions will play a significant role in shaping the future of US-China relations.
