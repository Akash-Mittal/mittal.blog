Are India and US Poised for a Breakthrough in Military-Industrial Partnership?



Image Credits https://www.indianarrative.com/opinion-news/are-india-and-us-poised-for-a-breakthrough-in-military-industrial-partnership-145098.html

The relationship between India and the United States has come a long way since the days of the Cold War. From being on opposite sides of the ideological spectrum, the two countries have moved towards closer diplomatic, economic, and military ties. As India's economic ascent continues and China's aggressive expansionism in the region grows, the relationship between India and US is expected to play a significant role in shaping the strategic balance in the Indo-Pacific region.

While there have been cooperation in various sectors, the defense and security sector has emerged as a particularly promising area for collaboration. As India looks to modernize its armed forces and reduce its reliance on imports, the US defense industry presents itself as a reliable partner. The US has been pushing for deeper defense ties with India, highlighting the strategic convergence between the two countries and the shared concerns about China's rising power.

The entire paradigm of India-United States cooperation is headed for quantitative and qualitative transformation when it comes to the defense sector. Let's explore this in detail:

Quantitative Transformation

In recent years, the US has become one of India's top arms suppliers. According to the Stockholm International Peace Research Institute, between 2015 and 2019, the US accounted for 16% of India's arms imports, making it the second-largest supplier after Russia. In 2020, the US and India signed a $3 billion defense deal for the purchase of military helicopters and other equipment. The US has also offered to sell India advanced fighter jets, missile defense systems, and unmanned aerial vehicles.

As India's economy grows and defense spending increases, there is a huge potential for the US defense industry to tap into the Indian market. India's defense budget is expected to reach $130 billion by 2025, and the US is keen to capture a significant share of this market. The US defense industry can offer India advanced technology and equipment that can enhance the capabilities of its armed forces and reduce its reliance on imports from Russia and other countries.

The increased defense cooperation is not limited to arms sales. The two countries have also been conducting joint military exercises and sharing intelligence and technology. In 2019, the US and India held their first tri-service exercise, codenamed Tiger Triumph, which involved their armies, navies, and air forces. Such joint exercises help in interoperability and mutual learning, which can be crucial in times of crisis.

Qualitative Transformation

The transformation in the defense partnership between India and the US is not just about buying and selling arms. It is also about co-development and co-production of advanced defense technologies. The US has been pushing for a transfer of technology and joint development of defense equipment, which can help in building a robust defense partnership between the two countries.

One successful example of such cooperation is the BrahMos missile. This supersonic missile is a joint venture between India and Russia, with the propulsion system provided by the Russians and the guidance system developed by India. The missile has been inducted into the Indian armed forces and has been a major success story in India's defense acquisition program. The US has been looking to replicate this model of co-development and co-production with India in other areas as well.

In recent years, there has been a focus on developing defense technologies in areas like unmanned aerial vehicles, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity. The US has been offering India access to its advanced technology and expertise in these areas, which can help in building India's indigenous defense capabilities. Such cooperation can also lead to joint research and development, which can be useful for both the countries.

Conclusion

The India-US defense partnership is at a crucial juncture. The strategic convergence and shared concerns about China's rising power present a huge opportunity for deeper defense cooperation between the two countries. The increased defense sales, joint exercises, and technology transfers are just the beginning of a much deeper partnership in the defense and security sector. The entire paradigm of India-United States cooperation is headed for a transformation that can have a significant impact on the strategic balance in the Indo-Pacific region.

In conclusion, let us summarize the key points of the article in three points:

  • The US defense industry presents itself as a reliable partner to India, as the latter looks to modernize its armed forces and reduce its reliance on imports from Russia and other countries.

  • The strategic convergence and shared concerns about China's rising power present a huge opportunity for deeper defense cooperation between the two countries, which can have a significant impact on the strategic balance in the Indo-Pacific region.
