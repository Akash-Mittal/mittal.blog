Once upon a time, there was a small village in India where a man named Raj worked as a silver smith. He had learned his craft from his father and grandfather, and had become quite skilled at creating beautiful pieces of jewelry and other decorative items out of silver. Raj had always been able to sell his wares in the local market, but he dreamed of expanding his business beyond the village.

Raj knew that his success depended on the price of silver, which was influenced by global demand. So, when he heard that there was weakening demand for silver from China and India, he became worried. How would he be able to make a living if people didn't want to buy silver anymore? Fortunately, he read an ANZ report that suggested that the price of silver would reach $26/oz by the end of the year. This gave him hope that his business would continue to thrive.

Raj's story is just one example of how the global silver market affects people's lives. The ANZ report that Raj read is based on a wealth of data and analysis that can help us understand what's happening in the market. Let's explore the report in more detail.

Weakening Demand from China and India

According to the ANZ report, China and India together account for roughly two thirds of global silver demand, so any changes in their economies can have a big impact on the market. In recent years, both countries have experienced slower economic growth, which has led to a decrease in demand for silver. For example, China's industrial production has slowed down, which means that fewer silver-based products are being manufactured.

Similarly, India's jewelry market is facing some challenges due to changing consumer preferences and the introduction of a goods and services tax (GST). In the past, silver jewelry was very popular in India, but now people are more interested in gold and diamond jewelry. Additionally, the GST has made silver jewelry more expensive, which has led to lower sales.

Despite these challenges, the ANZ report suggests that the overall outlook for the silver market is positive, thanks to other factors that are driving demand.

Positive Factors Driving Demand for Silver

One of the main factors driving demand for silver is the growth of the solar panel industry. Silver is used in the manufacturing of solar panels, which convert sunlight into electricity. As more and more countries invest in renewable energy, the demand for solar panels is expected to increase, which will in turn boost demand for silver.

Another important factor is the ongoing global push for electrification, particularly in the automotive industry. As more people switch to electric vehicles, the demand for batteries will increase. Silver is used in the conductive paste that's applied to the electrodes of some batteries, so this will also drive demand.

Finally, the ANZ report points out that there's often a "safe haven" effect that happens during times of economic uncertainty. When people are worried about the stock market, for example, they may turn to investing in precious metals like silver as a way to protect their wealth. This can lead to a temporary increase in demand.

Case Studies of Companies Affected by Silver Prices

To illustrate how fluctuations in the silver market can affect companies, let's look at two case studies: Pan American Silver and Fresnillo.

Pan American Silver is a mining company that operates in several countries throughout the Americas. They produce silver, gold, and other metals, and their financial performance is strongly tied to the prices of those commodities. In 2018, Pan American Silver reported a net loss of $27.6 million, largely due to lower silver prices. However, they remained optimistic about the future and continued to invest in exploration and development projects.

Fresnillo is another mining company that specializes in silver and gold. They operate primarily in Mexico, where they have several mines and processing facilities. In 2018, Fresnillo reported a slight increase in revenue, but their net profit was down 43% from the previous year. The company cited lower silver prices, higher operating costs, and declining ore grades as some of the challenges they faced.

Conclusion

Based on the ANZ report and the case studies we've discussed, we can draw several conclusions about the silver market:

1. Weakening demand from China and India is a concern, but there are other factors that are driving demand for silver.

2. Companies that rely on silver prices, such as mining companies, may experience financial difficulties during periods of low prices.

3. Despite these challenges, the overall outlook for the silver market is positive, thanks to trends like solar panel manufacturing and the push for electrification.
