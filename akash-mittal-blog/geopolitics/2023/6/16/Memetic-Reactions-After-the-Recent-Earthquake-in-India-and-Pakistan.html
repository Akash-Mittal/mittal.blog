Memetic Reactions After the Recent Earthquake in India and Pakistan

How social media users reacted to an unexpected natural disaster

About

On September 22, 2020, a 5.8 magnitude earthquake hit the northeastern region of Pakistan, affecting various parts of India and Pakistan. While natural disasters garner serious attention, social media users have found creative ways to cope with the situation through humor and memes. This article aims to explore the different memetic reactions that flooded various social media platforms in the aftermath of the earthquake.

Examples

One popular meme that circulated on Twitter and Facebook was an image of a football player falling to the ground, with the caption "How I survived the earthquake." This image was shared numerous times and shows how people use humor as a coping mechanism during times of stress. Another example of memetic reaction is the use of popular Bollywood movie clips and songs to depict the earthquake. A scene from the Bollywood movie Lagaan was used to show how the earthquake affected cricket matches. The song "Kabhi Khushi Kabhi Gham" was also remixed to relate to the earthquake, with the new version titled "Kabhi Earthquake Kabhi Aftershock."

Case Studies

Instagram Influencer

An Instagram influencer with a large following in India received backlash for posting a selfie with a caption that read "Earthquake... but first, let me take a selfie." The post was deemed insensitive and sparked outrage online. In response, the influencer apologized and deleted the post.

Brands on Twitter

Several brands took advantage of the trending hashtag #earthquake to promote their products or services on Twitter. However, this approach was met with criticism and deemed inappropriate by many users. One brand, in particular, faced backlash for using the earthquake as a marketing strategy.

Conclusion

Natural disasters can evoke various emotional responses and humor is sometimes used as a coping mechanism. Social media provides a platform for users to express themselves in creative ways, and memes are one such example. However, it is important to be mindful of the impact certain posts may have on others, and refrain from insensitive or inappropriate content. Brands should also exercise caution and avoid using natural disasters as a marketing tool.
