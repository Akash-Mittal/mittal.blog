Chinese Handset Companies & Indian Equity Partners



Image Credits https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/cons-products/electronics/government-tells-chinese-handset-companies-to-get-indian-equity-partners/articleshow/100950387.cms

China and India have been rivals for years, competing against each other in various aspects of life. However, a recent development in the technology industry has brought the two countries closer together.

As the Indian government has put forth a new policy, requiring Chinese handset companies to have Indian equity partners and appoint Indian executives in key roles such as CEO, COO, CFO and CTO, there has been a rise in joint ventures between Chinese and Indian companies.

This move is believed to be a step towards promoting India's 'Make in India' initiative and attracting foreign manufacturers to set up factories in India.

Case Studies

1. Xiaomi

Xiaomi, one of the largest Chinese smartphone manufacturers, has already complied with the Indian government's policy and appointed Manu Jain, an Indian, as its Vice President and Managing Director for the Indian operations.

As a result of this move, Xiaomi has seen a tremendous rise in its Indian market share, with a growth rate of 47% in the first quarter of 2018, compared to the same period in the previous year.

2. Oppo

Oppo, another Chinese smartphone manufacturer, has also formed a joint venture with India's biggest retailer, Flipkart, to introduce its products in the Indian market. This joint venture has helped Oppo to strengthen its foothold in India and increase its market share.

3. Vivo

Vivo, yet another Chinese smartphone manufacturer, has signed an MoU with the state government of Uttar Pradesh to set up a manufacturing unit in the state, under the 'Make in India' initiative. This move is expected to create job opportunities in the state and boost the local economy.

Conclusion

The Indian government's policy of requiring Chinese handset companies to have Indian equity partners and appoint Indian executives is a smart move towards promoting India's 'Make in India' initiative and creating job opportunities in the country.

It is evident from the case studies mentioned above that this policy has already started to yield positive results and has brought Chinese and Indian companies closer together.

Therefore, it can be concluded that this policy has the potential to improve the economic ties between India and China and promote peaceful co-existence between the two countries.
