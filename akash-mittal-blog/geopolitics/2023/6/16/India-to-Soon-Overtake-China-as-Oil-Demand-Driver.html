India to Soon Overtake China as Oil Demand Driver



Image Credits https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/energy/oil-gas/india-to-soon-overtake-china-as-largest-driver-of-oil-demand-iea-chief/articleshow/100994908.cms

Once upon a time, India was a country known for its agriculture and handicraft industries. However, as the world evolved, so did India, and today it is one of the fastest-growing economies in the world. With a population of over 1.3 billion people, India's energy demand is growing rapidly. It is predicted that by 2040, India will replace China as the leading driver of oil demand in the world.

The IEA's prediction

The International Energy Agency (IEA) recently released a report that predicts that India's oil demand will rise to 10 million barrels per day (bpd) by 2029. This is up from the current demand of 4.4 million bpd. The report also suggests that India's energy consumption will grow by 4.2% per year for the next two decades, making it the fastest-growing energy consumer in the world.

India's potential for green hydrogen

While the rise in oil demand may seem like a cause for concern, India also has the potential to become a leader in the green hydrogen market. Green hydrogen is produced from renewable energy sources and is considered to be a key player in the future of the energy industry. India has set a target of generating 40% of its energy needs from renewable sources by 2030, and green hydrogen can play a crucial role in achieving this goal.

Case Studies

One example of India's potential in the green hydrogen market is the Joint Initiative on Hydrogen between India and Australia. This initiative aims to develop a hydrogen supply chain between the two countries, with a focus on green hydrogen. In addition, India's largest refiner, Indian Oil Corporation, has signed an agreement with Israel's Phinergy to develop metal-air batteries that can be used to power electric vehicles. Metal-air batteries are a type of battery that can be recharged using a fuel source, such as hydrogen.

Conclusion

In conclusion, India's rise as a leading driver of oil demand is both a challenge and an opportunity. While the demand for oil may increase, India also has the potential to become a leader in the green hydrogen market. By investing in renewable energy sources and developing innovative technologies, India can lead the world in the transition to a greener future.

India to soon overtake China as oil demand driver

India to soon overtake China as oil demand driver; can be leader in green hydrogen: IEA chief



Image Credits https://www.moneycontrol.com/news/india/india-to-soon-overtake-china-as-oil-demand-driver-can-be-leader-in-green-hydrogen-iea-chief-10798081.html

There was a time when India was considered to be a developing country with little hope of catching up to China, the world's most populous country and the fastest-growing economy. But times have changed, and today, India is emerging as a major player in the global energy market.

India's demand for oil is projected to grow by 3.9 percent annually over the next decade, according to the International Energy Agency (IEA). This will make India the world's top driver of oil demand growth by 2030, overtaking China.

The IEA chief, Fatih Birol, has also said that India has the potential to become a leader in green hydrogen, a clean energy source that can be used to power transport and industry.

Examples of India's Rise in the Energy Market

India's energy market is evolving rapidly, with several developments indicating its growing importance:

  • India has become the world's second-largest importer of coal, after China.
  • India has been the world's largest renewable energy auction market since 2017, with more than 80 GW of renewable energy capacity auctioned off to date.
  • India is building the world's largest refinery complex, which will have a capacity of 1.2 million barrels per day.
  • India is expanding its gas pipeline network to increase the share of gas in its energy mix from 6 percent to 15 percent by 2030.

Case Studies

Let's take a closer look at two case studies that highlight India's growing prominence in the energy market:

Renewable Energy

In 2020, India hosted the world's largest renewable energy auction, with 2 GW of solar power and 1 GW of wind power capacity being auctioned off. This was the first hybrid renewable energy auction in India. The auction attracted bids from leading global players such as Softbank, ReNew Power, and Adani Green Energy.

The auction, which received 1.2 times the capacity that was being offered, resulted in a record-low tariff of INR 2.41 per kWh ($0.03 per kWh) for the solar power capacity and INR 2.43 per kWh ($0.03 per kWh) for wind power capacity. This is one of the lowest tariffs in the world and shows the competitiveness of renewable energy in India.

Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG)

India has been importing LNG since 2004 and is now one of the world's largest importers of the fuel. The country has steadily increased its LNG imports, with a target of importing 70 million tonnes per annum (mtpa) by 2030. India is now the world's 4th largest LNG importer, after Japan, China, and South Korea.

One of the most significant recent developments in India's LNG market is the construction of the country's first east coast LNG import terminal in Kakinada, Andhra Pradesh. The terminal has a capacity of 5 mtpa and is strategically located to serve the demand for gas in the southern and eastern regions of India.

Conclusion

In conclusion, India's rise in the energy market is driven by several factors, including its rapidly growing economy, its huge population, and its ambitious renewable energy and gas expansion plans. India's demand for oil is also set to increase rapidly over the next decade, making it the world's top driver of oil demand growth by 2030.

Furthermore, India has the potential to become a leader in green hydrogen, which could help the country move towards a cleaner and greener future. If India invests in green hydrogen technologies, it could emerge as a global leader in this space, which would provide a significant boost to its economy and its reputation in the international community.
