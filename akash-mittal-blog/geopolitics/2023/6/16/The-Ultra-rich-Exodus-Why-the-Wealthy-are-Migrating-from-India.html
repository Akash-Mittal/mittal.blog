Many other high-net-worth individuals (HNWIs) have been migrating from India in recent years.

Why are the rich leaving India?

The exodus of wealthy individuals from India is not new. According to the latest report of New World Wealth, around 22,000 HNWIs have migrated from India between 2014 and 2019. The trend is expected to continue, and the number is projected to reach around 23,000 by 2023.

But what is driving this mass exodus of HNWIs from India? There are several reasons why the ultra-rich are moving out of India, such as:

1. Higher Taxation:

One of the most significant reasons why HNWIs are leaving India is to avoid the high taxes levied by the government. India has one of the highest tax rates in the world, and the wealthy pay a large portion of taxes. The high taxes are not only a burden on their income but also their overall wealth.

Several tax laws and amnesties introduced by the government have also failed to provide any respite to the rich and resulted in a lack of confidence in the tax system.

2. Unstable Economic and Political Conditions:

The economic and political conditions of a country can determine the prosperity of the people living there. However, India is facing a severe economic slowdown and an unstable political environment, which is making the wealthy rethink their decision to stay.

The Indian government's policies are viewed as unfavorable for businesses, and bureaucratic hurdles have increased, making it difficult for the wealthy to operate their businesses smoothly. The instability of the government also makes investors hesitant to put their money in India, which has resulted in a lack of confidence in the economy.

3. Quality of Life:

The wealthy also migrate for a better quality of life, which is not adequately met by India's amenities. The pollution levels in many metropolitan areas are among the highest globally, causing severe health hazards, and the infrastructure is often inadequate and outdated. Besides this, concerns over safety issues and the quality of education and healthcare also drive the wealthy to look for greener pastures outside India.

Case Studies:

1. Anil Ambani:

One of the most recent and high-profile cases of wealthy individuals leaving India is that of Anil Ambani, a prominent India businessman. Amidst the severe economic slowdown in India, Anil Ambani had a net worth of around $42 billion in 2007 but has seen it dwindle over the years to around $300 million in 2020.

With several of his companies under heavy debt and the Indian government taking legal action against him, Anil Ambani decided to leave India and relocate to the UK, where he holds citizenship.

2. Lakshmi Mittal:

Lakshmi Mittal, the CEO of ArcelorMittal (the world's largest steel producer), decided to shift his base from India to the UK in 1995. He believed that the UK provided a better business climate and opportunities to expand his business.

The decision has been fruitful for him, and he has since become one of the wealthiest Indians globally, with a net worth of around $12 billion in 2020. He remains committed to India and continues to invest in the country.

Conclusion:

There is no denying the fact that the exodus of HNWIs from India is a worrying trend. The reasons are not limited to high taxation, economic and political uncertainties, and quality of life, but also extend to the lack of business-friendly policies and bureaucratic hurdles.

If the Indian government fails to address these concerns and create an environment that is conducive to growth, India may lose more of its wealthy individuals in the coming years. The government must re-evaluate its policies and create a favorable business environment that is both stable and attractive to entrepreneurs.
