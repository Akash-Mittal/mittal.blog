China's Expulsion of Indian Journalist: A New Low in Ties

Introduction

On August 20th, China asked KJM Varma, a reporter for Press Trust of India, to leave Beijing by the end of the month. This is the latest expulsion of an Indian journalist, leaving Indian media organisations in a blind state and leading to a new low in ties between the two nations.

Examples with Case Studies

This is not the first time China has targeted Indian journalists. In 2020, Hindustan Times journalist Sutirtho Patranobis was expelled from China. In 2016, two journalists from The Hindu were denied visa extensions due to their critical coverage of China. In 2014, The Economist's correspondent was denied a visa due to an article critical of Chinese leader Xi Jinping.

Similarly, China has also targeted journalists from other countries, especially those who report on human rights abuses and political dissent. In 2021, Australian journalist Cheng Lei was detained by Chinese authorities and charged with "endangering national security." In 2020, American journalists from The New York Times, The Wall Street Journal, and The Washington Post were expelled from China due to their critical coverage of the Chinese government's handling of the COVID-19 pandemic.

These incidents point to China's growing intolerance of critical reporting and dissenting voices, both domestically and internationally. As its global influence expands, China wants to control the narrative and suppress any criticism of its policies or actions.

Conclusion

The expulsion of KJM Varma is a worrying development for press freedom and diplomatic ties between India and China. It reflects China's increasing aggression towards journalists and its disregard for international norms and values. India must take a firm stance and demand an explanation for this move, while also highlighting the need for press freedom and protecting the rights of journalists worldwide.

Key Takeaways:

  • China's expulsion of Indian journalist KJM Varma is a disturbing trend that points to its growing intolerance of critical reporting and dissenting voices.
  • This move also reflects the deteriorating diplomatic ties between India and China.
  • Press freedom and protecting the rights of journalists must be a priority for both nations and the international community at large.
