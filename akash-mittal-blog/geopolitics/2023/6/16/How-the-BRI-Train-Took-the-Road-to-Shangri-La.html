How the BRI Train Took the Road to Shangri-La



Image Credits https://thecradle.co/article-view/25792/columns

Once upon a time in China, there was a dream to revive the ancient Silk Road for the modern era. The Belt and Road Initiative (BRI), also known as One Belt One Road, was born. It seeks to connect Asia, Europe, and Africa through a network of railways, highways, ports, and energy pipelines. The BRI aims to boost trade, investment, and cultural exchange among participating countries. One of the notable BRI projects is the China-Laos Railway, which links the capital city Vientiane to the border town of Boten.

The BRI Train in Laos

The China-Laos Railway spans over 400 kilometers and crosses 75 tunnels and over 160 bridges. It connects China's southern Yunnan Province to Laos, providing a faster and safer mode of transportation compared to the existing bumpy roads. The railway has a designed speed of 160 kilometers per hour and can carry both passengers and cargo. The BRI train has hired local workers and employed Chinese technologies and standards to build the railway.

The Benefits of the BRI Train

The China-Laos Railway has brought tangible benefits to both China and Laos. For China, the railway provides a new route for trade and investment in Southeast Asia and beyond. It helps to diversify China's transportation options and reduce its reliance on maritime trade, which can be vulnerable to geopolitical tensions and natural disasters. The railway also creates new business opportunities for Chinese companies involved in construction, engineering, and logistics. Moreover, the BRI train enhances China's image as a global leader and promotes its soft power through cultural exchange and people-to-people diplomacy.

For Laos, the railway is a game-changer in terms of economic development and regional integration. The BRI train connects Laos to the broader regional market and allows it to access China's vast consumer base. It also boosts tourism and facilitates the flow of people and goods across the border. Additionally, the railway creates jobs and stimulates local industries, such as agriculture, manufacturing, and services. Moreover, the BRI train enhances Laos' connectivity and reduces its landlocked status, which has been a hindrance to its trade and development.

The Challenges of the BRI Train

Despite the benefits of the China-Laos Railway, there are also challenges and risks involved. One of them is the financing of the project, which requires huge investments from both China and Laos. The railway is estimated to cost around $6 billion, and it is funded mainly by loans from Chinese banks. However, there are concerns about the debt sustainability of Laos, which has a small economy and limited resources to repay the loans. Moreover, there are concerns about the environmental and social impacts of the railway, such as deforestation, displacement of communities, and cultural homogenization. Therefore, it is crucial to ensure that the BRI train is implemented in a responsible and sustainable manner, with proper risk management and stakeholder engagement.

Conclusion

The Belt and Road Initiative, represented by the China-Laos Railway, is a major international project that has significant implications for global trade, investment, and development. The BRI train provides new opportunities and challenges for both China and Laos, and it requires careful planning and execution to maximize its benefits and minimize its risks. Therefore, it is essential to adopt a holistic and inclusive approach to the BRI train, one that takes into account the economic, social, environmental, and geopolitical dimensions of the project. By doing so, the BRI train can truly take the road to Shangri-La, a place of harmony, prosperity, and sustainability.

How the BRI train took the road to Shangri-La



Image Credits https://thecradle.co/article-view/25792/how-the-bri-train-took-the-road-to-shangri-la

The Belt and Road Initiative, also known as the New Silk Road, is a development strategy proposed by China's President Xi Jinping in 2013 to enhance connectivity and cooperation between Asia, Europe, and Africa. The initiative aims to utilize China's sprawling infrastructure to build trade and commercial links with over 100 countries, covering almost 70% of the world's population and around 40% of global GDP.

The BRI train has played a pivotal role in reshaping the transportation infrastructure and enhancing trade amongst the nations, leading towards the progress of Shangri-La. Let's explore how.

Case Studies

China-Kyrgyzstan-Uzbekistan Railway

The railway network connecting China, Kyrgyzstan, and Uzbekistan is a part of the BRI's Central Asia Regional Economic Cooperation program. It covers a distance of 986 km and connects several Chinese cities with Tashkent, the capital of Uzbekistan. It has significantly reduced the transportation costs of goods between the countries, and the journey time has decreased from nearly 20 days by road to around five days by train. The increased trade and commerce have helped in the development and growth of Shangri-La.

Mombasa-Nairobi Standard Gauge Railway

The Mombasa-Nairobi Standard Gauge Railway is a part of the BRI's Maritime Silk Road program and connects the port city of Mombasa in Kenya to the capital city of Nairobi. The railway network covers a distance of 609 km and has reduced the transportation costs of goods in Kenya by up to 40%. The railway line has also contributed to the development of Shangri-La by connecting the country's major industrial hubs with the port of Mombasa.

Pakistan-China Economic Corridor

The Pakistan-China Economic Corridor is a part of the BRI's flagship project and connects China's western city of Kashgar with Pakistan's Gwadar port via a network of highways, railways, and pipelines. The corridor is expected to cover a distance of 2,700 km and is considered a game-changer for the development of Shangri-La. The project has already created new jobs and industries in Pakistan, and the increased trade and commerce are expected to lead to long-term economic benefits.

Conclusion

The BRI train has proved to be a game-changer for the transportation infrastructure and trade amongst nations. The initiative has led to the development and growth of Shangri-La, as evident from the case studies mentioned above. The increased connectivity and cooperation between countries have brought new opportunities for economic growth and development that will lead to long-term benefits for the region.

In conclusion, the BRI train has taken the road to Shangri-La, and it has paved the way for the progress of the region.
