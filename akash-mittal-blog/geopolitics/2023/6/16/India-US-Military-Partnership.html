Are India and US poised for a breakthrough in military-industrial partnership?



Image Credits https://www.indianarrative.com/opinion-news/are-india-and-us-poised-for-a-breakthrough-in-military-industrial-partnership-145098.html

India and the United States share a long-standing relationship that traces back to the Cold War era. From being adversaries in the past, the two nations have come a long way in developing strategic and economic ties. And now, the entire paradigm of India-United States cooperation is headed for quantitative and qualitative transformation, especially in the military-industrial sector.

A key driver of this shift is the growing geopolitical and strategic convergence between India and the US. Both nations are concerned about China's assertive behavior in Asia, especially in the Indo-Pacific region. To counter China's influence, India and the US have been strengthening security cooperation through various defense agreements and joint military exercises. But, there is much more potential in this partnership.

Examples of India-US Military-Industrial Cooperation:

  • Defense Trade and Technology Initiative (DTTI): Launched in 2012, DTTI is a flagship program that aims to promote co-development and co-production of defense equipment between India and the US. Under this initiative, several projects are being pursued, including the development of a next-generation Raven unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) and the production of a lightweight small arms technology.
  • Apache Attack Helicopters: In 2015, India signed a $3 billion deal with the US to buy 22 AH-64E Apache attack helicopters. The manufacture and delivery of these choppers are being carried out by Boeing, under the 'Make in India' initiative. This deal marked a significant milestone in India-US military cooperation, as it was the first time that India bought US-made primary military equipment.
  • Naval Cooperation: The US and India are also working closely in the naval domain, especially in anti-submarine warfare and maritime security. In 2019, the US Navy participated in India's largest naval exercise 'Milan' for the first time, along with several other nations. The participation was aimed at enhancing interoperability and cooperation among the navies of the Indo-Pacific region.

Case Studies of India-US Military Partnership:

Case Study 1: Boeing and Tata Advanced Systems Limited (TASL): In 2016, Boeing and TASL signed a strategic agreement to co-produce Apache fuselages, which made India the sole global producer of this critical structural component. This partnership not only supported the 'Make in India' initiative but also created employment opportunities and developed the domestic aerospace industry's capabilities.

Case Study 2: Lockheed Martin and Tata Advanced Systems Limited (TASL): In 2018, Lockheed Martin and TASL announced the production of F-16 fighter jets in India, under the 'Make in India' initiative. The project is expected to create thousands of direct and indirect jobs in India and contribute to building the country's aerospace ecosystem. Additionally, it will enable the transfer of advanced technologies from the US to India and strengthen defense ties between the two nations.

Conclusion:

India-US military-industrial partnership is poised for a breakthrough in the coming years. With the growing geopolitical and strategic convergence between the two nations, there is much potential for cooperation in defense research and development, co-production, and technology transfer. The recently launched Defense Technology and Trade Initiative (DTTI) provides a framework for collaboration across a range of defense technologies, including emerging and dual-use technologies. This partnership not only promotes India-US economic ties but also supports India's national security interests and strategic objectives.
