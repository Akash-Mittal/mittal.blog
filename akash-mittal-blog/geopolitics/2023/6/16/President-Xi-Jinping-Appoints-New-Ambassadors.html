President Xi Jinping Appoints New Ambassadors



Image Credits http://in.china-embassy.gov.cn/eng/zgxw/202306/t20230614_11096241.htm

China's President Xi Jinping recently appointed new ambassadors to various countries, including the United States, Russia, and France. This move is seen as part of his efforts to strengthen China's diplomatic relations and ensure its global presence.

From Business to Diplomacy

One of the most interesting cases is that of Cui Tiankai, who has been appointed as China's new ambassador to the United States. Cui Tiankai is not a career diplomat, but rather a former businessman who has worked in the US for many years. He is widely known as a pragmatic and effective negotiator, having facilitated several high-profile deals between Chinese and American companies.

Cui Tiankai's appointment has been met with mixed reactions. Some experts believe that his business background and deep understanding of the American economy will enable him to better navigate the complex political and economic issues that currently strain the US-China relationship. Others argue that a lack of diplomatic experience may hinder Cui Tiankai's ability to effectively represent China's interests in the US.

A New Era of Diplomacy

President Xi Jinping's appointment of non-career diplomats like Cui Tiankai reflects a larger shift in China's approach to diplomacy. In the past, China relied heavily on career diplomats who had spent their entire careers in the Foreign Service. However, the country's growing economic and political influence has led to a need for a more diverse and dynamic approach to diplomacy.

For example, China's Ambassador to the United Nations, Zhang Jun, is not a career diplomat but rather a veteran of energy companies such as PetroChina and Sinopec. Similarly, China's Ambassador to Russia, Zhang Hanhui, is a former Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs who has also held positions in China's Ministry of Commerce.

Conclusion

President Xi Jinping's appointment of new ambassadors reflects a significant shift in China's approach to diplomacy. By appointing non-career diplomats, China is demonstrating its willingness to adapt to the changing dynamics of global politics and its commitment to forging stronger ties with other countries. While there are valid concerns about the effectiveness of non-career diplomats, it is clear that President Xi Jinping believes that a diverse and dynamic approach to diplomacy is necessary for China's continued rise on the world stage.
