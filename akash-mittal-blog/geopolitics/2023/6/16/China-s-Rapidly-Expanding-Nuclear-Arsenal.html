China's Rapidly Expanding Nuclear Arsenal: Impact on India's Strategic Deterrence



Image Credits https://m.timesofindia.com/india/china-fast-expanding-nuclear-arsenal-but-india-confident-of-its-strategic-deterrence/articleshow/100946496.cms

China has been rapidly expanding its nuclear weapons arsenal in recent years, which poses a significant threat to India's strategic deterrence capabilities. While China's nuclear ambitions are not new, its aggressive actions in the region have heightened concerns about a potential nuclear conflict. However, India remains confident in its own strategic deterrence capabilities despite China's growing nuclear might.

The Story of Nuclear Arms Race between India and China

The nuclear arms race between India and China dates back to the 1950s when China tested its first nuclear bomb in 1964. India responded by conducting its first nuclear test in 1974 to catch up with China's nuclear capability. Since then, both countries have been actively investing in their nuclear arsenals to protect their national security interests.

China's nuclear weapons inventory has significantly expanded in recent years, with estimates suggesting it has around 320 nuclear warheads compared to India's 150. Additionally, China has also been developing its ballistic missile capabilities to enhance its ability to strike targets, including the United States, from long-range. These developments have intensified concerns about China's aggressive military expansion in the region.

India, on the other hand, has taken a more strategic approach to its nuclear programme. It has placed great emphasis on ensuring that its nuclear deterrence capabilities are credible, effective and reliable. India's nuclear doctrine emphasises "no-first use" which means India will not use nuclear weapons unless it is attacked with them first.

Case Studies: China's Nuclear Ambitions in the Region

The South China Sea Dispute and its Nuclear Implications

The South China Sea dispute remains a flashpoint in the region, with multiple countries competing for territorial control. China has built military bases on artificial islands in the disputed waters, including airstrips capable of handling fighter jets. This has led to concerns about China's military expansion in the region and its nuclear implications.

While China maintains that its nuclear doctrine is purely defensive, there are fears that its aggressive actions in the South China Sea could escalate into a nuclear conflict. Should a military conflict arise, China's nuclear arsenal would be a significant factor on the battlefield.

The Doklam Standoff and its Nuclear Implications

The Doklam standoff between India and China in 2017 was a significant escalation of tensions between the two countries. The dispute arose when Chinese troops entered Doklam, a disputed territory that is claimed by both China and Bhutan. India sent troops to the area to protect its ally, sparking a military standoff that lasted for several weeks.

During the standoff, there were concerns that the situation could escalate into a nuclear conflict. While both countries managed to resolve the dispute peacefully, it highlighted the potential for nuclear tensions between India and China.

Conclusion

China's rapidly expanding nuclear arsenal remains a major concern for India's strategic deterrence capabilities. While India has tried to keep pace by modernising its nuclear arsenal and building better delivery systems, there is still a significant asymmetry in the nuclear capabilities of the two countries. However, India's no-first-use policy and emphasis on credible deterrence should serve as a reassuring factor in the region.

In conclusion, India must continue to invest in its strategic capabilities to counter China's nuclear expansion. At the same time, it should maintain a strong commitment to multilateralism and peaceful resolution of disputes to avoid any potential nuclear conflict in the region.
