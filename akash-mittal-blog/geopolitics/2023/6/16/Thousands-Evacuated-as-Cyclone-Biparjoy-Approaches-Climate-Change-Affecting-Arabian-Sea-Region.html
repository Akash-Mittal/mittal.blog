Thousands Evacuated in India and Pakistan as Cyclone Biparjoy Approaches - Climate Change Affecting Arabian Sea Region



Image Credits https://thediplomat.com/2023/06/thousands-evacuated-in-india-and-pakistan-as-cyclone-biparjoy-approaches/

With winds gusting up to 185 kilometers per hour, Cyclone Biparjoy is expected to hit the coasts of India and Pakistan in the coming days. Authorities have ordered thousands of people to evacuate from the coastal areas as a precautionary measure.

Unfortunately, this is not an isolated incident. In recent years, we have seen a rise in the frequency and intensity of cyclones in the Arabian Sea region. Experts are warning that this is due to the effects of climate change.

Climate Change and Cyclones

Cyclones are weather phenomena that are caused by large-scale atmospheric disturbances. These disturbances are fueled by warm ocean waters and occur mainly in tropical regions. As the waters in the Arabian Sea continue to warm up, cyclones are becoming more frequent and intense.

According to a report published by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), there is a high degree of certainty that the frequency of cyclones will increase in the future due to climate change. The report notes that the Arabian Sea is expected to see an increase in both the frequency and intensity of cyclones.

This is not just a theoretical concept. In recent years, we have seen several examples of the devastating impact of cyclones in the Arabian Sea region.

Case Studies

Cyclone Fani

In May 2019, Cyclone Fani hit the eastern coast of India, killing more than 80 people and causing widespread damage. The storm was the strongest to hit the region in over 20 years and was fueled by the warm waters of the Bay of Bengal.

Cyclone Mekunu

In May 2018, Cyclone Mekunu hit the southern coast of Oman and Yemen, causing significant damage and killing several people. The storm was one of the strongest to hit the region in decades and was fueled by warm waters in the Arabian Sea.

Cyclone Nilofar

In October 2014, Cyclone Nilofar threatened to hit the western coast of India, prompting the authorities to evacuate thousands of people from the coastal areas. The storm weakened before making landfall, but it highlighted the dangers of cyclones in the Arabian Sea.

Conclusion

Cyclones are a natural phenomenon, but their impact is becoming increasingly severe due to climate change. Rising ocean temperatures in the Arabian Sea are fueling the intensity and frequency of cyclones in the region. This is a threat not just to the coastal population, but also to the economy and infrastructure of the affected countries. It is crucial that we take steps to mitigate the impact of climate change and address the root causes of this global crisis.
