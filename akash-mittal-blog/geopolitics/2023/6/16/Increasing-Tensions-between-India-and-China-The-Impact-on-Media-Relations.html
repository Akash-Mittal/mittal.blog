Increasing Tensions between India and China: The Impact on Media Relations



Image Credits https://english.alarabiya.net/News/world/2023/06/12/In-deepening-rift-China-orders-last-Indian-journalist-in-country-to-leave

As tensions increase between India and China, media relations between the two countries have taken a hit. The recent expulsion of Indian journalists from China, and the subsequent expulsion of Chinese journalists from India, has raised concerns about the impact of these actions on journalism and press freedom. This situation is not unique, as other countries have faced similar challenges in recent years. Let us take a closer look at how these actions impact the media using case studies and examples.

Case Study 1: China and India

China's decision to expel the last remaining Indian journalist from their country is an indication of the growing tensions between the two countries. The journalist, Biresh Banerjee, had been working for Kolkata's Ananda Bazar Patrika and was told to leave China by the end of August. The reason given by the Chinese government was that his visa application was not approved, though some speculate it may be retaliation for India's decision to expel Chinese journalists earlier this year. Banerjee's expulsion from China is an example of the retaliation and escalation that can occur in such situations, and highlights the potential impact on press freedom and journalism.

Case Study 2: Russia and the US

Russia and the US have faced similar challenges when it comes to the relationship between their governments and the media. In 2016, the Russian government passed a law that requires foreign media organizations to register as "foreign agents." This move was seen as retaliation for the US government's decision to require the same of Russian media outlets in the US. This has had a significant impact on the ability of foreign media to operate in Russia, and has raised concerns about press freedom and the ability of journalists to do their jobs without fear of retribution.

Conclusion

The expulsion of journalists from one country by another is not a productive or effective means of addressing disputes between governments. Rather, it can have far-reaching implications for press freedom, the ability of journalists to do their jobs, and the wider public's ability to access accurate and reliable information. Governments need to recognize the importance of a free press in a democratic society, and work to support, rather than hinder, the work of journalists. In order to do so, it is essential that we work to build relationships between journalists, media organizations, and governments that are based on mutual respect, openness, and transparency.
