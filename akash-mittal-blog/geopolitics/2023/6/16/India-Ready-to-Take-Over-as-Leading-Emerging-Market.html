India Ready to Take Over as Leading Emerging Market



Image Credits https://portfolio-adviser.com/india-poised-to-replace-china-as-core-emerging-market-allocation/

Once considered a nation plagued by corruption and bureaucracy, India is now emerging as one of the world's fastest-growing economies. With a population of over 1.3 billion, a young workforce, and a burgeoning middle class, it is poised to replace China as the core emerging market for asset allocation.

Take the example of pharmaceuticals, one of India's fastest-growing sectors. The Indian pharmaceutical industry is the third largest in the world by volume and fourteenth largest in terms of value. Companies such as Sun Pharmaceutical and Cipla have become global players, exporting to over 200 countries.

Another example is the Indian IT industry, which has grown at an average rate of 7.5% per year and is expected to continue growing. Companies such as Tata Consultancy Services and Infosys have become household names in the tech industry, providing IT services to clients all over the world.

Case Studies

Case Study 1: The Rise of Flipkart

Flipkart is India's largest e-commerce company, worth over $20 billion. Founded by two former Amazon employees in 2007, it has grown into a behemoth, selling everything from books to electronics to fashion. In 2018, Walmart paid $16 billion for a 77% stake in the company, making it the largest e-commerce acquisition in history.

This acquisition highlights the potential of the Indian e-commerce market. With a population of over 1.3 billion, and over 500 million smartphone users, India's e-commerce market is expected to be worth $200 billion by 2026. This presents a huge opportunity for investors looking to tap into India's rapidly growing consumer market.

Case Study 2: The Growth of Reliance Industries

Reliance Industries, founded by India's richest man Mukesh Ambani, has grown into a conglomerate worth over $100 billion. It is involved in a range of industries, from oil and gas to telecom to retail. Its subsidiary, Jio, has disrupted the Indian telecom market by offering dirt-cheap data plans, forcing competitors to follow suit.

Reliance's growth highlights the potential of India's domestic market. With over 1.3 billion people, and a rapidly growing middle class, companies that cater to the Indian market are poised for explosive growth.

Why India is the New China for Asset Allocation

India has a number of advantages over China when it comes to asset allocation:

  1. Demographics: India has a young and growing workforce, with a median age of 28.7 years. China, on the other hand, has an aging population, with a median age of 38.4 years. This means that India has a higher potential for growth in the long term.
  2. Regulatory Environment: India has been making strides to improve its regulatory environment, making it easier for foreign investors to invest in the country. China, on the other hand, has a highly regulated market, which can be difficult for foreign investors to navigate.
  3. Valuation: India's equity market is currently trading at a premium to its historical average, but it is still cheaper than China. This means that there is still potential for growth, whereas China's equity market is nearing its peak.

Conclusion

India is emerging as one of the world's fastest-growing economies, presenting a huge opportunity for asset allocators. With a young and growing workforce, a burgeoning middle class, and an improving regulatory environment, it is poised to replace China as the core emerging market for asset allocation. India's domestic market presents a huge opportunity for growth, as companies that cater to the Indian market are poised for explosive growth.
