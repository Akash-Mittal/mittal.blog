Soft Power, Hard Ball: Why The U.S. Wants Back In UNESCO



Image Credits https://worldcrunch.com/world-affairs/us-unesco

When it comes to international relations, soft power and hard ball are often at play.

Case in point: the United States' recent decision to rejoin UNESCO, the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization, after pulling out of it in 2017.

Imagine you're a young student in a remote village in India. You are eager to learn and grow, but you don't have access to quality education or resources. However, thanks to UNESCO's efforts, a school has been established in your village with trained teachers, updated curriculum and adequate infrastructure.

This has not only improved the quality of education in your village, but it has also opened doors for you and others like you to access higher education and better job opportunities.

Now, imagine the impact this has had on your village and other nearby communities, all due to UNESCO's work in promoting education and learning across the world.

Case Studies

Artificial Intelligence

One of the key reasons for the U.S.'s return to UNESCO is its focus on promoting the development of artificial intelligence (AI) worldwide.

As the world becomes increasingly digital and AI-driven, the U.S. recognizes the need to collaborate with other countries and organizations to develop ethical and equitable AI policies and regulations that benefit all.

UNESCO's work on AI ethics and AI for social good aligns with the U.S.'s goals in this area, making rejoining UNESCO a strategic move for the country.

Moreover, the U.S. hopes to lead the discourse on AI development and standards within UNESCO, which could give it a competitive edge in the global AI race.

Geopolitics

The U.S.'s decision to rejoin UNESCO is also driven by geopolitical considerations.

As China's influence grows across the world, the U.S. sees UNESCO as a way to counter China's soft power diplomacy and build alliances with other countries.

Rejoining UNESCO could also help the U.S. in promoting democratic values and human rights, particularly in countries where China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) has a substantial presence.

The U.S. hopes that by being an active member of UNESCO, it can build stronger partnerships with like-minded countries and counter China's growing global influence.

Conclusion

The U.S.'s decision to rejoin UNESCO is a nuanced one, driven by a combination of strategic, technological, and geopolitical factors.

By collaborating with UNESCO on important issues like AI ethics and promoting democratic values, the U.S. hopes to reassert its global leadership and counter China's growing influence.

Ultimately, the U.S.'s return to UNESCO can be seen as a move towards greater international cooperation and diplomacy, which can benefit the world as a whole.
