IMF's Treatment of Pakistan: An Unfair Deal?



Image Credits https://www.arabnews.pk/node/2318861/pakistan

Analyze the impacts of the IMF's delay on the bailout program for Pakistan.

June 25,

As Pakistan's finance minister, Ishaq Dar, put it, the International Monetary Fund's (IMF) decision to delay the bailout program for the country was "totally unfair." Dar argued that the global lender had also contributed to the economic turmoil in his country, specifically by imposing harsh conditions and demanding strict austerity measures.

This isn't the first time that Pakistan has been stuck in the economic quagmire. The country's history is rife with examples of political instability, corruption, and a lack of governance that have hindered Pakistan's growth prospects. The COVID-19 pandemic has only exacerbated the issue, creating a revenue shortfall and hampering economic activity.

Given these difficulties, Pakistan has been seeking aid from multilateral institutions, including the IMF. However, the delay in the program has undoubtedly created further uncertainty for the country.

IMF's Demands

One of the primary reasons for the delay in the bailout program is the IMF's insistence on structural reforms and austerity measures. The global lender has demanded that Pakistan reduce its budget deficit, increase tax revenue, and improve its energy sector.

While these demands may be reasonable, they have also raised concerns regarding the impact on the country's most vulnerable populations. In a country where poverty and unemployment rates are already high, further austerity measures could lead to immense social unrest. Additionally, the IMF's demands may place too much burden on Pakistan's economy, leading to further stagnation.

Case Study: Greece

The IMF's approach towards Pakistan echoes its handling of Greece's debt crisis in 2010. Greece was also forced to accept harsh austerity measures in exchange for a bailout, which had significant social and economic repercussions. The country's output fell by a quarter, unemployment soared, and poverty rates increased.

The Greek example highlights the IMF's tendency to prioritize the repayment of debt over the welfare of a country's citizens. While debt repayment is undoubtedly important, it cannot come at the cost of the most vulnerable populations.

Conclusion

The delay in the IMF's bailout program has undoubtedly created further uncertainty for Pakistan. However, it is also important to question the IMF's demands and the impact they will have on the country's economy and society. The IMF's approach towards Pakistan must take into account the country's unique circumstances and needs, rather than imposing a one-size-fits-all solution.
