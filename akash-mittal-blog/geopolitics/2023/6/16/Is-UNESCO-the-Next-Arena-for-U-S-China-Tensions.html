Is UNESCO the Next Arena for U.S.-China Tensions?



Image Credits https://foreignpolicy.com/2023/06/13/unesco-china-us-rejoin-multilateral-un/

As the rivalry between the United States and China continues to heat up, the two superpowers are looking for new arenas in which to compete. One of these areas is UNESCO, the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization. The organization, which is dedicated to promoting international cooperation in education, science, and culture, has become a battleground for U.S.-China tensions in recent years. Here are a few examples of how UNESCO is becoming embroiled in this geopolitical struggle.

Examples and Case Studies

The U.S. Withdrawal from UNESCO

In 2017, the United States announced that it would withdraw from UNESCO, citing a need for "fundamental reform" within the organization. The decision, which took effect in 2018, was met with widespread criticism from other member countries, who accused the U.S. of turning its back on international cooperation and undermining efforts to promote peace and security.

China's Growing Influence within UNESCO

As the U.S. has pulled back from UNESCO, China has become increasingly involved in the organization. In 2017, China was elected to UNESCO's executive board, a move that was seen as a significant boost to its global standing. China has also been investing heavily in UNESCO projects, particularly in developing countries, which has helped to increase its influence within the organization.

The Battle Over Taiwan's Participation in UNESCO

One of the most contentious issues within UNESCO is the question of Taiwan's participation in the organization. China regards Taiwan as a province of the mainland and opposes its participation in any international organization as an independent state. In recent years, China has used its influence within UNESCO to block Taiwan's participation, which has led to tensions between the two countries as well as the U.S..

Conclusion

Despite being focused on education, science, and culture, UNESCO has become a new arena for U.S.-China tensions. The U.S. withdrawal from the organization and China's growing influence within it have heightened tensions between the two countries, and issues such as Taiwan's participation have made UNESCO a battleground for geopolitical rivalry. As such, it is clear that UNESCO will continue to be caught up in the wider struggle between the U.S. and China for power and influence in the world.
