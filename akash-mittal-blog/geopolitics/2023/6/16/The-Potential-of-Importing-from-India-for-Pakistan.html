The Potential of Importing from India for Pakistan

Once upon a time, a Pakistani businessman was desperately searching for a supplier of quality apples at an affordable price. He tried sourcing from various countries, but the cost was too high for him to make a profit. Then, he stumbled upon an Indian supplier who not only offered the same quality of apples but at a fraction of the price. The Pakistani businessman couldn't believe his luck and quickly placed an order. That was the moment he realized the potential of importing from India for Pakistan.

India - Pakistan Trade Relations

India and Pakistan share a long history of trade relations. Despite political tensions between the two countries, India remains one of Pakistan's top trading partners. In recent years, however, Pakistan's imports from India have declined due to various factors, including political tensions and lack of trust. However, it's time for Pakistan to re-think its trade policies and consider importing more goods from India.

The Advantage of Importing from India

India is known for its diverse range of products and services. From textiles to technology, India has a lot to offer at competitive prices. By importing from India, Pakistan can not only save costs but also access better quality products. Here are some examples:

Pharmaceuticals:

India is the world's third-largest producer of pharmaceutical drugs and has an established reputation for producing high-quality generic drugs at low prices. Pakistan, on the other hand, imports a significant amount of pharmaceuticals every year, mostly from European countries such as Germany, France, and Switzerland. However, by importing from India, Pakistan can save up to 80% of its pharmaceutical import costs.

Automobile Parts:

The Indian auto component industry is a major supplier of auto parts globally, including to some of the world's leading car manufacturers. Pakistani automobile manufacturers currently import most of their parts from China, Japan, and South Korea. However, importing from India can reduce costs significantly, and it's worth exploring options for collaboration in this sector.

Case Studies

There are many examples of successful trade partnerships between India and Pakistan. Here are two case studies:

Textiles:

In 2017, Pakistani textile manufacturers imported yarn from India worth approximately $822 million. According to industry experts, if Pakistan sourced more yarn from India, it could help boost the country's textile sector. Moreover, Indian textile manufacturers also have a growing demand for Pakistani cotton and could benefit from importing it.

Cement:

Pakistan is the world's ninth-largest cement producer, exporting to countries such as Sri Lanka, Tanzania, and the UAE. However, Pakistan also imports cement from India, which started in 2010 after the earthquake in Kashmir damaged bridges, making it impossible to transport cement from other regions of Pakistan. Since then, India has become a regular supplier of cement to Pakistan, with Pakistani businessmen recognizing the cost advantages of importing from India.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Pakistan has much to gain by importing from India. From pharmaceuticals to automobiles, India has a diverse range of products and services that could benefit the Pakistani economy. By exploring more options for collaboration, the two countries could facilitate trade that would be mutually beneficial.
