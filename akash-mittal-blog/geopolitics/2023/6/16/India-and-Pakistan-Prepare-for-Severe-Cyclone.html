India and Pakistan Prepare for Severe Cyclone



Image Credits https://abc17news.com/news/2023/06/12/india-pakistan-deploy-rescuers-and-plan-evacuations-ahead-of-severe-cyclone/

Rescuers are mobilizing and evacuations are being planned as a dangerous cyclone is expected to hit India and Pakistan in the next few days.

What is Happening?

A severe cyclone called Tauktae, with winds of up to 175 kilometers per hour, is expected to make landfall along the western coast of India on Monday, May 17th. The storm is also likely to hit the southern coast of Pakistan. The Indian Meteorological Department has issued a red alert for several coastal districts, warning of possible damage to homes, roads, and power lines, as well as a danger to human life. In preparation, both India and Pakistan have deployed rescue teams and started evacuating people from vulnerable areas.

Examples of Preparations

  • The Indian Navy has deployed multiple ships and helicopters for rescue and relief operations. They are also coordinating with the Coast Guard and other agencies to ensure quick response.
  • Pakistan's National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) has put its units on high alert and started evacuating people from low-lying areas and nearby islands in Sindh and Balochistan provinces.
  • In Gujarat state, India, authorities have shifted hundreds of COVID-19 patients from hospitals near the coast to facilities further inland.
  • In Mumbai, India's financial capital, the local municipal corporation has cancelled the vaccination drive for Monday and Tuesday due to the expected cyclone.

Case Studies

India's Experience with Cyclones

India frequently faces severe cyclones along its eastern and western coasts. In November 2020, Cyclone Nivar hit the southern state of Tamil Nadu, causing heavy rainfall and flooding. The state government had deployed thousands of personnel for disaster response and evacuated nearly 2 lakh (200,000) people from vulnerable areas. Thanks to these preparations, the death toll was low and the damages were limited.

However, not all cyclones in India end up having a positive outcome. In 1999, Cyclone super-ally hit the eastern state of Odisha, causing over 10,000 deaths and extensive damages to property and infrastructure. The disaster was largely attributed to poor preparation and coordination between the state and central authorities.

Pakistan's Floods and Droughts

Pakistan is no stranger to natural disasters, having faced several major floods and droughts in the past. In 2010, the country was hit by the worst floods in its history, affecting over 20 million people and causing billions of dollars in damages. The government's response was criticized for being slow and inadequate, with many people left without food, water, or shelter for days.

Since then, Pakistan has strengthened its disaster management and response systems, including the creation of the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) in 2007. However, it still faces challenges in dealing with disasters, especially in remote and hard-to-reach areas.

Conclusion

As India and Pakistan prepare for Cyclone Tauktae, the focus is on minimizing the loss of life and property. While it is difficult to predict the exact impact of the cyclone, early preparations and timely response can make a significant difference. Both countries have learned lessons from past disasters and are putting that knowledge into practice. In the end, successful disaster management is not just about dealing with emergencies, but also about building resilience and preparedness for future events.
