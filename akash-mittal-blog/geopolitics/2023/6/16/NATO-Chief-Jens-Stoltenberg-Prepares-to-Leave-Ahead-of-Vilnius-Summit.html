NATO Chief Jens Stoltenberg Prepares to Leave Ahead of Vilnius Summit

On September 30, 2021, Jens Stoltenberg will complete his term as the Secretary-General of NATO, marking the end of a seven-year tenure as the Chief of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization.

Under Stoltenberg's leadership, NATO has seen many challenges and successes. From the annexation of Crimea by Russia in 2014, to the fight against ISIS in Iraq and Syria, to the global COVID-19 pandemic, Stoltenberg has worked tirelessly to ensure that NATO remains a strong and united alliance.

Case Studies

1. Response to Russian aggression

One of the most significant challenges that Stoltenberg faced during his tenure was the annexation of Crimea by Russia in 2014. This act of aggression by Russia violated international law and threatened the stability of Europe. Stoltenberg's response was immediate and resolute. He condemned Russia's actions and called for a collective response from NATO members. The alliance responded by increasing its presence in the Eastern part of Europe, where it deployed troops and equipment to deter Russian aggression. This move demonstrated the alliance's commitment to collective defense and sent a strong message to Moscow.

2. Fight against ISIS

In 2015, Stoltenberg led NATO's efforts against ISIS in Iraq and Syria. NATO deployed its AWACS surveillance aircraft to provide situational awareness to the anti-ISIS coalition, and also provided training and support to Iraqi security forces. Stoltenberg's leadership played a crucial role in the coalition's success against ISIS, which was ultimately defeated in 2019. This victory demonstrated NATO's capacity to engage in operations beyond its traditional area of responsibility.

3. Response to COVID-19 pandemic

In 2020, NATO faced a new challenge in the form of the global COVID-19 pandemic. Stoltenberg responded by leading NATO's efforts to support its member states in their response to the pandemic. NATO provided logistical support, such as the transportation of medical supplies, and also facilitated the sharing of best practices among its member states. Stoltenberg emphasized the importance of solidarity and cooperation in the face of the pandemic, and his leadership helped to ensure that NATO remained a relevant and effective organization.

Conclusion

Jens Stoltenberg's tenure as the Secretary-General of NATO has been marked by significant challenges and achievements. From the response to Russian aggression in Eastern Europe, to the fight against ISIS in Iraq and Syria, to the global COVID-19 pandemic, Stoltenberg has demonstrated his leadership and commitment to a strong and united alliance. His departure leaves big shoes to fill, but the work he has done to modernize NATO and ensure its relevance in the 21st century will continue to benefit the alliance in the years to come.
