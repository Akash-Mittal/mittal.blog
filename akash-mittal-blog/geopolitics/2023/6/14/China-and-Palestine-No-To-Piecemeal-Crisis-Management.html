China and Palestine: No To 'Piecemeal Crisis Management'



Image Credits https://scheerpost.com/2023/06/13/china-and-palestine-no-to-piecemeal-crisis-management/

It was a warm summer day in the small town of Beit Jala, just outside of Bethlehem in Palestine. The sun was shining and the birds were chirping. Sarah and her family had just sat down to enjoy a meal when they heard the sound of tanks rolling into their town. They quickly shut all the windows and locked the doors. They could hear shouting, gunfire and explosions in the distance. They waited in silence, hoping the tanks would not reach their home.

This scenario may seem fictional, but it is a very real experience for many Palestinians living in the West Bank. The Israeli-Palestinian conflict has been ongoing for decades and has resulted in the displacement and suffering of countless innocent civilians. However, it is not just Palestine that is facing crisis management issues. China too is grappling with its own set of challenges. But both these countries have chosen to say 'No' to piecemeal crisis management strategies and are instead taking a more comprehensive approach to drive change.

Case Studies: China's Belt and Road Initiative and Palestine's National Policy Agenda

China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI)

In 2013, China launched the Belt and Road Initiative, a global infrastructure development strategy aimed at promoting economic cooperation among nations. The initiative originated in China and aims to connect China to Asia, Europe and Africa through a network of trade routes. The initiative has been praised for its potential to address infrastructure gaps, promote trade and investment, and boost economic development.

One of the biggest challenges that China faces is the income disparity between urban and rural areas. The BRI aims to address this by investing in infrastructure projects in underdeveloped areas, creating jobs and spurring economic growth. The initiative also aims to promote connectivity and cooperation between countries and regions, addressing issues of geopolitics and promoting economic stability.

The BRI has not been without criticism. Some have highlighted the environmental impact of the initiative, while others have raised concerns over the impact of China's growing international influence.

Palestine's National Policy Agenda (NPA)

In 2017, the State of Palestine launched its National Policy Agenda, a comprehensive plan for national development that aims to address the social, economic and environmental challenges facing the country. The NPA focuses on six main areas: governance and democracy, economic development, social development, environmental sustainability, gender equality, and youth and sports.

The NPA is a response to the ongoing conflict and political instability in Palestine, and aims to provide a framework for long-term development. The plan prioritizes the needs and aspirations of the Palestinian people, and aims to promote sustainable economic growth, investment in human capital, and the protection and preservation of the environment.

The NPA has been praised for its comprehensive approach to addressing the challenges facing Palestine. However, it faces significant challenges due to the political instability and ongoing conflict in the region, as well as a lack of funding and resources.

Conclusion: A Comprehensive Approach Is Key

China's BRI and Palestine's NPA are just two examples of how countries can take a comprehensive approach to addressing their challenges. Rather than focusing on piecemeal crisis management strategies, these initiatives provide a long-term framework for addressing social, economic and environmental challenges.

Such comprehensive initiatives may not be perfect and may face their own set of challenges, but they provide a more sustainable and holistic approach to driving change. By addressing the root causes of problems and promoting economic development, these initiatives can help create more stable and prosperous societies.

It is important for countries to recognize that short-term fixes may provide temporary relief but will not address the underlying issues. A comprehensive approach that prioritizes the needs and aspirations of the people is key to driving sustainable change.
