Understanding India-China Conflict: 7 Must-Read Books



Image Credits https://m.timesofindia.com/life-style/books/features/7-books-to-understand-the-india-china-conflict/photostory/100965716.cms

By The Times of India - Oct 1,

The India-China Conflict has been making headlines for years now. Despite numerous meetings and diplomatic efforts, tensions continue to run high between the two countries. In order to get a better understanding of this ongoing conflict, there are various books that provide in-depth insights into the history, geography, and political aspects of this issue.

Here we have listed down seven must-read books that can help you comprehend the India-China Conflict:

  1. The India-China War: An Analysis of Military, Diplomatic and Political Dimensions by Dipankar Banerjee

    2. This book is a comprehensive analysis of the India-China War of 1962. It provides a detailed account of the events that led to the war, the military strategies employed by both sides, and the diplomatic and political implications of the war. The author also explores the aftermath of the war, its impact on India-China relations, and the lessons learned from the conflict.

  2. The Dragon and the Elephant: China, India and the New World Order by David Smith and Bingbing Wu

    3. This book offers a fresh perspective on the India-China Conflict by examining the economic, political, and strategic implications of the rising powers of China and India. The authors explore the interactions and mutual perceptions of the two countries, the geopolitical factors that influence their relationship, and the opportunities and challenges that arise from their growing economic and military power.

  3. India China Relations: The Border Issue and Beyond by Avtar Singh Bhasin

    4. This book provides a detailed account of the history and evolution of the India-China Border Issue. It delves into the various aspects of the border dispute, including the political, historical, and geographical factors that have contributed to the conflict. The author also examines the economic and strategic implications of the border issue and offers insights into possible solutions for resolving the dispute.

  4. The India-China Borderlands: Conversations beyond the Centre edited by Nimmi Kurian, Alka Acharya, and Manjeet S. Pardesi

    5. This book is a compilation of essays that explore the diverse perspectives on the India-China Conflict from various borderland regions. The contributors, who come from different academic and professional backgrounds, provide insights into the local, national, and international dimensions of the conflict. The book offers a multidimensional view of the India-China Conflict that goes beyond the usual geopolitical analysis.

  5. China's India War: Collision Course on the Roof of the World by Bertil Lintner

    6. This book offers a gripping account of the India-China War of 1962, based on extensive research and interviews with participants and eyewitnesses. The author examines the military strategies employed by both sides, the geopolitical factors that influenced the conflict, and the aftermath of the war. The book also provides insights into the broader implications of the conflict for the region and the world.

  6. The Sino-Indian Border in Ladakh: A Geopolitical Analysis by A.S. Bhalla

    7. This book provides a geopolitical analysis of the India-China Border Issue in the Ladakh region. The author examines the historical, political, and strategic factors that have contributed to the conflict, and offers insights into the possible solutions for resolving the border dispute. The book also explores the economic and strategic implications of the border dispute for India and China.

  7. India and China: The Battle between Soft and Hard Power by Manoj Joshi

    8. This book examines the India-China Conflict from the perspective of soft power and hard power. It explores the various ways in which India and China exercise their influence in the region and the world, and how the two countries compete for power and influence. The author provides insights into the cultural, economic, and political dimensions of the India-China Conflict, and offers an analysis of the future prospects for the two countries.

The Conclusion: Why You Should Read These Books

The India-China Conflict is a complex and multifaceted issue that requires a nuanced understanding of its historical, geopolitical, and strategic dimensions. These books offer valuable insights and perspectives on the conflict, allowing readers to gain a deeper understanding of the issues at stake. By reading these books, you can broaden your understanding of the India-China Conflict and develop informed opinions on this important issue.
