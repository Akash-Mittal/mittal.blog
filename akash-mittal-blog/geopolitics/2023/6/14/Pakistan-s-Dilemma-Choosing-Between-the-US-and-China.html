Pakistan's Dilemma: Choosing Between the US and China



Image Credits https://www.politico.eu/article/pakistan-choose-us-china-global-power-bloc-hina-rabbani-khar/

Why Pakistan cannot afford to choose sides in the emerging global power struggle

The Threat of Two Rival Power Blocs

The emergence of the US-China rivalry as two power blocs has become a major threat to global peace and stability. Both countries are the top two economic and military powers in the world. While the US has been a traditional ally of Pakistan, the rise of China as a major player in the region has thrown a spanner in the works for the South Asian nation.

For Pakistan, the choice between the US and China is not an easy one. The country has deep economic and strategic ties with both countries. However, with the US citing Pakistan's failure to crack down on terrorism and China's growing influence in the region, Pakistan is stuck in the middle of a geopolitical power struggle.

The Case for China

China has become Pakistan's largest trading partner and the biggest investor in the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC) project. The CPEC project involves improving infrastructure, energy and communications links between the two countries. The project provides a much-needed boost for Pakistan's struggling economy, creating jobs and increasing economic growth.

China has also been a strong ally of Pakistan on the international stage. It has blocked efforts by India to isolate Pakistan after the 2019 Pulwama attack. In addition, China has supported Pakistan's stance on the Kashmir issue, which has helped to create a united front against India's territorial ambitions in the region.

China's support for Pakistan is not only strategic but also emotional. The two countries share a bond that goes beyond just economic and political ties. This is reflected in the popular sentiment in Pakistan towards China which is overwhelmingly positive.

The Case for the US

The US has been a major ally of Pakistan since the Cold War. It has provided economic and military aid to Pakistan, especially during times of crisis. The US has also played an important role in moderating regional conflicts, including the ongoing conflict between India and Pakistan over Kashmir.

Despite recent tensions between the two countries, the US remains a big market for Pakistan's exports. In addition, Pakistan's military relies heavily on the US for training and equipment. The two countries also share intelligence in the fight against terrorism, which remains a major concern for both.

The US also provides a platform for Pakistan to engage with the global community. It is a member of various international organizations, including the UN and the World Trade Organization, which allows it to have a voice in the global arena.

The Way Forward

Pakistan cannot afford to choose between the US and China. The country needs to balance its relationship with both countries while protecting its national interests. Pakistan should continue to engage with both countries and use its unique position to bridge the gap between the US and China.

Pakistan should also focus on enhancing its own economic and military capabilities. This would reduce its dependence on external aid and allow it to be more self-sufficient in the long run. In addition, Pakistan should work towards resolving its internal issues, including terrorism and corruption, which has affected its standing in the international community.

In conclusion, Pakistan's dilemma of choosing between the US and China is a complex issue that requires a nuanced approach. Pakistan should continue to engage with both countries while prioritizing its national interests. By doing so, Pakistan can emerge as a key player in the emerging global power struggle rather than a pawn caught in the middle.
