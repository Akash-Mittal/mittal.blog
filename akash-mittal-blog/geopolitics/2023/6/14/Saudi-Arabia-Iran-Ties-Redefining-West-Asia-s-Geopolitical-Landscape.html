Saudi Arabia-Iran Ties: Redefining West Asia's Geopolitical Landscape



Image Credits https://www.deccanherald.com/international/world-news-politics/saudi-arabia-iran-ties-set-to-redefine-west-asia-s-geopolitical-landscape-1226718.html

West Asia has always been a volatile region that sprang surprises from time to time. But, in recent years, the major regional powers are consciously toning down the rhetoric and differences to align with the common areas of interest through expanding reapproachments. The relationship between Saudi Arabia and Iran, the two regional heavyweights, has always been strained, but there have been some interesting stories of cooperation and collaboration that have come to the fore.

The Story of a New Beginning

In the year 2017, Saudi Arabia and Iran played a friendly game of football for the first time in 40 years. The match, played in Tehran, ended in a 1-0 victory for Iran. While the result was not of much significance, the fact that these two countries, which have been at loggerheads for decades, came together on the football field, raised hopes of a new beginning.

This was followed by a joint statement by Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman and Iranian President Hassan Rouhani, expressing their willingness to improve bilateral relations and reduce tensions. The statement was issued after the two leaders held a historic phone call, the first of its kind in more than three decades.

Examples of Cooperation and Collaboration

After years of tension, there have been some signs of cooperation and collaboration between Saudi Arabia and Iran, which could redefine the geopolitical landscape of West Asia. Here are some of the examples:

1. Yemen Crisis

Saudi Arabia and Iran have been backing opposite sides in the Yemen conflict, with Saudi Arabia leading a coalition of Arab states against the Iran-backed Houthi rebels. However, in 2019, both countries engaged in secret talks in Baghdad to end the Yemen crisis. The talks were the first direct high-level meetings between the two countries in more than four years. While the talks did not lead to an immediate solution, they raised hopes of a peaceful settlement to the crisis.

2. Oman Mediation

Oman, a neutral country in the region, has been playing a crucial role in mediating between Saudi Arabia and Iran. In 2018, Oman's Foreign Minister, Yusuf bin Alawi, made a rare visit to Tehran, where he held talks with Iranian officials. This was followed by a visit to Saudi Arabia, where he met with Saudi King Salman and Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman. The visits were seen as an attempt to ease tension between the two countries.

3. Hajj Agreement

Every year, millions of Muslims from all over the world travel to Saudi Arabia to perform the Hajj pilgrimage. In 2017, Iran boycotted the Hajj following a diplomatic row with Saudi Arabia. However, in 2019, the two countries reached an agreement that allowed Iranian pilgrims to participate in the Hajj. The agreement was seen as a positive step towards improving bilateral relations between the two countries.

Conclusion

The recent thaw in Saudi Arabia-Iran ties could have far-reaching implications for the geopolitical landscape of West Asia. While it is too early to predict the outcome of the ongoing talks and negotiations, the fact that these two countries are willing to engage in dialogue and cooperation is a positive sign. The world will be watching closely as these two regional heavyweights strive to redefine their relationship for the better.
