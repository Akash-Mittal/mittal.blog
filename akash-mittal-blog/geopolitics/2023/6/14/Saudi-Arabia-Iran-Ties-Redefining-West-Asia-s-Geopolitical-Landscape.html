West Asia has always been a volatile region that sprang surprises from time to time. But, in recent years, the major regional powers are consciously toning down the rhetoric and differences to align with the common areas of interest through expanding reapproachments. The relationship between Saudi Arabia and Iran, the two regional heavyweights, has always been strained, but there have been some interesting stories of cooperation and collaboration that have come to the fore.

The Story of a New Beginning

In the year 2017, Saudi Arabia and Iran played a friendly game of football for the first time in 40 years. The match, played in Tehran, ended in a 1-0 victory for Iran. While the result was not of much significance, the fact that these two countries, which have been at loggerheads for decades, came together on the football field, raised hopes of a new beginning.

This was followed by a joint statement by Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman and Iranian President Hassan Rouhani, expressing their willingness to improve bilateral relations and reduce tensions. The statement was issued after the two leaders held a historic phone call, the first of its kind in more than three decades.

Examples of Cooperation and Collaboration

After years of tension, there have been some signs of cooperation and collaboration between Saudi Arabia and Iran, which could redefine the geopolitical landscape of West Asia. Here are some of the examples:

1. Yemen Crisis

Saudi Arabia and Iran have been backing opposite sides in the Yemen conflict, with Saudi Arabia leading a coalition of Arab states against the Iran-backed Houthi rebels. However, in 2019, both countries engaged in secret talks in Baghdad to end the Yemen crisis. The talks were the first direct high-level meetings between the two countries in more than four years. While the talks did not lead to an immediate solution, they raised hopes of a peaceful settlement to the crisis.

2. Oman Mediation

Oman, a neutral country in the region, has been playing a crucial role in mediating between Saudi Arabia and Iran. In 2018, Oman's Foreign Minister, Yusuf bin Alawi, made a rare visit to Tehran, where he held talks with Iranian officials. This was followed by a visit to Saudi Arabia, where he met with Saudi King Salman and Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman. The visits were seen as an attempt to ease tension between the two countries.

3. Hajj Agreement

Every year, millions of Muslims from all over the world travel to Saudi Arabia to perform the Hajj pilgrimage. In 2017, Iran boycotted the Hajj following a diplomatic row with Saudi Arabia. However, in 2019, the two countries reached an agreement that allowed Iranian pilgrims to participate in the Hajj. The agreement was seen as a positive step towards improving bilateral relations between the two countries.

Conclusion

The recent thaw in Saudi Arabia-Iran ties could have far-reaching implications for the geopolitical landscape of West Asia. While it is too early to predict the outcome of the ongoing talks and negotiations, the fact that these two countries are willing to engage in dialogue and cooperation is a positive sign. The world will be watching closely as these two regional heavyweights strive to redefine their relationship for the better.

West Asia has always been a region of surprises, with its volatile nature and complex geopolitical landscape. This region has witnessed numerous conflicts, rivalries and alliances, making it one of the most challenging regions for global powers to navigate. However, in recent years, the major regional powers - Saudi Arabia and Iran - are trying to tone down their differences and work on aligning their common areas of interest. This article will examine how the rapprochement between Saudi Arabia and Iran can redefine West Asia's geopolitical landscape.

The Story So Far

The rivalry between Saudi Arabia and Iran is not new, and it has deep roots in history. Both countries have different religious and political ideologies and have been involved in a power struggle for decades. Their rivalry has played out in conflicts across the region, including the civil war in Syria, Yemen's war and instability in Iraq. Saudi Arabia and its Sunni allies have long accused Iran of supporting terrorist groups and interfering in the internal affairs of Arab states, while Iran has accused Saudi Arabia of sowing sectarianism and promoting extremism.

However, there has been a subtle shift in their approach in recent years. The election of Hassan Rouhani as President of Iran in 2013 and his pledge to open up Iran to the world has created opportunities for dialogue and engagement. In 2015, Iran and six world powers, including the US, signed a historic deal to curb Iran's nuclear program in exchange for lifting economic sanctions on Iran. This deal was seen as a major breakthrough in easing tensions between Iran and the West and opened up the possibility of Iran playing a more constructive role in the region.

Meanwhile, Saudi Arabia is undergoing a transformation under Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman, who has launched an ambitious plan to modernize the economy and society. As part of this plan, he has pledged to reduce the country's dependence on oil and diversify the economy. He has also introduced social reforms, such as allowing women to drive and attend public events. These reforms are aimed at projecting a more liberal image of Saudi Arabia and gaining support from the younger generation.

Case Studies

Syria

Syria has been at the forefront of the Saudi-Iranian rivalry. Iran has been a staunch supporter of Syrian President Bashar al-Assad, while Saudi Arabia has been backing the opposition forces. However, in recent years, there have been talks of a possible rapprochement. In 2018, Saudi Arabia reopened its embassy in Damascus, marking a significant shift in its position. Though the embassy was closed again in 2020, the move was seen as a positive step towards reconciliation. Similarly, Iran has called for a political solution to the crisis in Syria and has been willing to engage in talks with Saudi Arabia to find a way forward.

Yemen

Yemen's war has been one of the most devastating conflicts in the region, with thousands of people killed and millions displaced. Saudi Arabia has been leading a coalition of Arab states in a military campaign against Iran-backed Houthi rebels in Yemen. Iran has been accused of providing weapons and support to the rebels. However, there have been efforts to find a political solution to the conflict. In 2018, the UN brokered talks between the warring parties in Sweden, which resulted in a ceasefire agreement. While the ceasefire has been fragile, it has given some hope for a possible end to the conflict.

Conclusion

The rapprochement between Saudi Arabia and Iran is a positive development for the region and the world. It has the potential to reduce tensions and conflicts and create opportunities for cooperation and dialogue. However, there are still several challenges that need to be addressed, including the deep mistrust and ideological differences between the two countries. The international community can play a constructive role in facilitating dialogue and building trust between the two countries. Ultimately, the fate of the region depends on the willingness of its major players to find common ground and work towards a more peaceful and prosperous future.
