As the CEO of US-India Strategic Partnership Forum, I have witnessed a tremendous growth in the relationship between the two countries. India and the US have come closer than ever before and there are several reasons behind it. In this article, we will explore some interesting stories, case studies, and examples to understand the concrete relationship between the two nations.

India and the US have always had their differences just like any other countries. India was never the first choice for the US when it came to business and trade, especially during the cold war. But things have drastically changed since then. Today, we see several US companies investing and setting up their business in India. What created this change?

The answer lies in the political environment of the two countries. India has been through a lot of turmoil in the past decades, but it has also experienced several positive changes since the last decade. The Indian government has been taking measures to improve the ease of doing business in India while striving towards a more open economy. Likewise, the US has also seen a significant shift in its political approach towards India.

Case Studies and Examples

Trade and Investment

Over the past few years, the US and India have significantly strengthened their trade and investment relationship. For instance, in 2020, the US surpassed China as India's top trading partner and the bilateral trade between the two countries stood at $146 billion. This reflects an increase of over $100 billion since 2006.

Another example of this partnership is Walmart, which acquired Flipkart, one of India's largest e-commerce companies, for $16 billion. This has created a significant impact on the Indian economy, and the number of Flipkart employees has increased by 45% since the acquisition.

Defense Cooperation

The US and India have also been working closely when it comes to defense cooperation. In 2018, the US and India signed the Communications Compatibility and Security Agreement (COMCASA) and the Basic Exchange and Cooperation Agreement (BECA) in 2020. These agreements have helped in improving the interoperability of the military forces of both the countries.

One of the notable examples of this cooperation is the purchase of 30 MQ-9 Reaper drones by India from the US. This marked the first time the US has sold armed drones to a non-NATO ally.

Educational Exchange

One of the critical aspects of global partnership is educational exchange. The US and India have made significant strides in this regard. The Fulbright program, which started in India in 1950, has enabled thousands of students to study in the US, and vice versa.

Several US universities have also been setting up their campuses in India. For instance, the University of Chicago established the Center in Delhi in 2014, which offers various academic programs for students from India and around the world.

Conclusion

The relationship between India and the US has come a long way, and it is not just political but also commercial, cultural, and social. The partnership has brought benefits for both the countries, and the future looks promising with new collaborations and investments on the horizon.

This concrete relationship between the US and India can be summarized in three key points:

  • Trade and Investment
  • Defense Cooperation
  • Educational Exchange
