Once upon a time, there was a small island nation called Mauritius, located in the Indian Ocean. With a population of just over one million people, the country did not have significant political or economic influence on the global stage. However, in recent years, Mauritius has been able to enhance its geopolitical power and influence through strategic partnerships and coalitions.



Image Credits https://www.nation.com.pk/09-Jun-2023/i2u2-a-geopolitical-perspective

One such partnership is the Indian Ocean Rim Association (IORA), which is a regional forum that promotes economic cooperation and political dialogue among its member nations. Mauritius, as a member of IORA, has been able to leverage this platform to advance its own interests and enhance its geopolitical influence in the region. For example, Mauritius has been advocating for a sustainable blue economy in the Indian Ocean, which has gained traction among IORA member states.

Another way Mauritius has been able to enhance its geopolitical power is by strengthening its relationships with major world powers. For example, the country has a close relationship with India, which has been a key partner in supporting Mauritius' efforts to become a hub for finance, trade, and investment in the region. Mauritius has also been able to leverage its relationship with China to secure investments and development assistance for its infrastructure projects.

In addition to strategic partnerships and alliances, Mauritius has also been able to enhance its geopolitical power by actively participating in multilateral organizations and forums. For example, the country sits on the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) and has been able to use this platform to promote its development agenda and influence global policy discussions on issues such as climate change and sustainable development.

In conclusion, the case of Mauritius highlights the importance of strategic partnerships, alliances, and multilateral engagements in enhancing geopolitical power and influence. By leveraging these platforms, countries can advance their own interests, promote their agendas, and shape global policy discussions. Ultimately, the ability to enhance geopolitical power and influence is essential for countries to protect their national security, advance their economic interests, and secure their place in the world.
