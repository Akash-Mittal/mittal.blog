Once upon a time, two neighboring countries, India and China, had a friendly relationship. They shared cultural and economic ties with each other, and many Indians considered China as their friend and ally. However, things have changed drastically in recent times, and the relationship between the two countries has hit an all-time low. Let's explore why.



Image Credits https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DVPE2LIJN6E

One of the major reasons for the souring of relations between India and China is the border dispute. The border between India and China has been a contentious issue for many years, with both countries claiming sovereignty over certain regions. In 2020, tensions between the two countries boiled over, leading to a military standoff in the Ladakh region. The skirmish resulted in the deaths of several soldiers on both sides and a severe strain on diplomatic relations.

Another issue that has soured the relationship between India and China is trade. India has been facing a huge trade deficit with China for many years, which has fueled resentment among Indian policymakers and the public. The Indian government has sought to reduce the trade deficit by enacting strict regulations on Chinese products and investments. For instance, the Indian government has recently introduced new rules for Chinese apps and has banned several of them, citing security concerns.

The COVID-19 pandemic has also played a part in the worsening of the relationship between India and China. Many Indians believe that China is responsible for the spread of the virus and the subsequent loss of life and economic damage. Moreover, China's lack of transparency and initial cover-up of the outbreak has further eroded trust between the two countries.

In conclusion, the relationship between India and China has hit an all-time low, with contentious issues such as the border dispute, trade deficit, and COVID-19 pandemic exacerbating tensions. It remains to be seen whether the two countries can resolve their differences and restore friendly ties. Nevertheless, it is essential to recognize that the strained relationship between India and China has significant political, economic, and social implications for both countries and the region at large.
