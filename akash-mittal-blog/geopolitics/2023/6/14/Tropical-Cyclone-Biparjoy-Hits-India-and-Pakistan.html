Tropical Cyclone Biparjoy Hits India and Pakistan



Image Credits https://reliefweb.int/report/india/india-pakistan-tropical-cyclone-biparjoy-gdacs-jtwc-pmd-imd-echo-daily-flash-12-june-2023

It was an ordinary day in the coastal village of Chhota Udepur, located in the state of Gujarat, India. The sun was shining bright, birds were chirping, and fishermen had set sail to the sea. Suddenly, the weather took a turn for the worse. The sky turned dark and ominous, and strong winds started blowing. Within hours, the village was hit by tropical cyclone Biparjoy, causing massive destruction.

Tropical cyclone Biparjoy hit India and Pakistan on June 12, 2023, bringing with it heavy rain, strong winds, and storm surges. The cyclone made landfall in the state of Gujarat, India, and then moved towards Pakistan, causing extensive damage to both countries.

Impact on India

In India, the cyclone affected several states, including Gujarat, Maharashtra, and Madhya Pradesh. According to the India Meteorological Department (IMD), the cyclone caused 26 deaths and injured more than 100 people. The strong winds uprooted trees, damaged buildings, and disrupted power supply. Many villages in Gujarat were inundated by floodwaters, and a large number of people had to be evacuated to safer places.

The cyclone also severely impacted the agricultural sector, with crops like cotton, groundnut, and sugarcane getting severely damaged. The loss to the agricultural sector is estimated to be over INR 1000 crore.

Impact on Pakistan

In Pakistan, the cyclone hit the coastal areas of Sindh and Balochistan, causing widespread damage. According to the Pakistan Meteorological Department (PMD), the cyclone caused at least 15 deaths and injured many others. The strong winds damaged houses, uprooted trees, and caused power outages. The storm surges caused flooding in several areas, and many people had to be rescued from the floodwaters.

The cyclone also affected the fishing industry, which is a major source of livelihood for the coastal communities in Pakistan. The damage to the fishing boats, nets, and other equipment is estimated to be over PKR 100 crore.

Lessons Learned

The impact of tropical cyclone Biparjoy highlights the urgent need for better disaster preparedness and response in India and Pakistan. Both countries need to invest in early warning systems, evacuation plans, and improved infrastructure to mitigate the impact of future disasters. The agricultural and fishing sectors also need to be made more resilient to climate-related risks.
