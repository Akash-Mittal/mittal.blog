Cambodia: Candlelight Election Ban Shows Paris Peace Agreements More Important Than Ever



Image Credits https://thegeopolitics.com/cambodia-candlelight-election-ban-shows-paris-peace-agreements-more-important-than-ever/

On the evening of July 29, 2018, Phnom Penh, Cambodia was filled with the light of candles as people peacefully protested the election ban. The ban, which prevented the country's main opposition party, the Cambodia National Rescue Party (CNRP), from participating in the 2018 elections, was yet another blow to the country's fragile democracy.

The Paris Peace Agreements, signed on October 23, 1991, aimed to end decades of conflict in Cambodia and establish a democratic country through free and fair elections and neutrality. However, nearly 30 years later, those goals remain unfulfilled.

The Banning of Opposition Parties

One of the biggest obstacles to achieving a democratic Cambodia has been the banning of opposition parties. In November 2017, the Constitutional Court of Cambodia dissolved the CNRP, the main opposition party, on charges of seeking to topple the government with the backing of the United States.

The decision was widely criticized by the international community, including the United Nations and the European Union, as a blow to democracy in Cambodia and a violation of human rights. The CNRP's leader, Kem Sokha, was also arrested in September 2017 and is still awaiting trial.

The ban on the CNRP effectively eliminated any real opposition to the ruling Cambodian People's Party (CPP) and ensured its victory in the 2018 elections. While CPP officials claim that the election was free and fair, many Cambodians boycotted the vote or cast blank ballots in protest.

China's Influence

China's growing influence in Cambodia has also hindered progress towards a democratic country. China has been Cambodia's biggest aid donor and investor in recent years, providing loans and grants for infrastructure projects and supporting the government's crackdown on dissent.

Human rights groups have accused the Cambodian government of using Chinese aid to suppress opposition and silence critics. In particular, the government has been accused of using Chinese technology to monitor and control internet access, thereby limiting freedom of expression.

Moreover, China's support of the Cambodian government has allowed it to resist international pressure to adhere to democratic principles and human rights. As the world increasingly turns towards China, Cambodia risks becoming a pawn in its geostrategic game.

Conclusion

The banning of opposition parties and China's influence pose significant challenges to achieving a democratic Cambodia. However, the Paris Peace Agreements remain a beacon of hope that democratic ideals can be achieved. It is imperative that the international community continues to pressure the Cambodian government to adhere to the agreements and respect human rights.

In conclusion, the situation in Cambodia highlights the importance of upholding democratic values and the need for vigilance in protecting such values. The Paris Peace Agreements offer a roadmap for democratic governance, and it is up to both the Cambodian government and the international community to ensure that those goals are finally achieved.
