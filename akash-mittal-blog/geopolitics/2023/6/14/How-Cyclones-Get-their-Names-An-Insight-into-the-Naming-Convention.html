How Cyclones Get their Names: An Insight into the Naming Convention



Image Credits https://www.wionews.com/photos/as-cyclone-biparjoy-threatens-india-and-pakistan-lets-find-out-how-cyclones-get-their-names-603938

Whenever a cyclone strikes, it becomes a matter of concern for the authorities as well as the masses. The severity of the situation aggravates when the natural disaster hits densely populated regions. In such cases, prompt action is necessary to mitigate the impact of the cyclonic storm.

The convention of naming a cyclone follows the alphabetical order. The first cyclone of the year is assigned a name beginning with the letter 'A' followed by 'B', 'C', and so on. But have you ever wondered why we name a cyclone?

The idea behind giving names is to simplify communication among the meteorologists, disaster management bodies, and the public. In the case of extreme weather conditions, accurate and prompt communication is crucial for effective disaster management.

The Reason Behind Naming Cyclones

The simple reason for naming a cyclone is to create a channel for effective communication. In the era of digitalization and social media, a cyclone's name is the quickest way to spread awareness and disseminate information to the masses. A clear and concise naming structure enables people to remember the name and take prompt action in case of a natural disaster.

The History and Evolution of Cyclone Naming Convention

The beginning of the 19th century witnessed a shift in the way hurricanes were tracked. It was in 1953 when the US Weather Bureau deployed women's names for the hurricanes. The trend caught up, and the World Meteorological Organization (WMO) initiated the practice of naming the cyclonic storms in the year 1955.

The WMO divided the world into nine regions, headed by different countries' meteorological departments. The countries belonging to the specific region are responsible for naming the tropical cyclones. For example, India belongs to the Bay of Bengal and Arabian Sea region and hence is responsible for naming the cyclones formed in these regions.

Since 2000, the WMO has maintained an updated list of cyclonic storm names. The organization allocates a list of names to each region, and the cycle repeats every six years. The WMO also avoids assigning the names of people so that there is no disrespect or confusion.

The Importance of Naming Cyclones

Naming a cyclone helps promote awareness and prepares people in advance to minimize the loss during the disaster. For instance, in 2013, the 'Phailin' cyclone happened to be the worst cyclonic storm to hit the Indian peninsula in over a decade. The timely evaluation and prompt process of evacuating the people were the prime reasons for a minimal loss of life.

Again in 2020, Cyclone Amphan struck the eastern coast of India, killing over 100 people and leaving millions displaced. Reliable and accurate information dissemination about the disaster gave the people an idea of what to expect and how to prepare.

Hence, the naming of cyclonic storms is a useful tool to spread awareness and ensure that people remain vigilant. The easy, simple, and clear structure of cyclone names makes it easy for the masses to remember and understand the information.

Conclusion

Naming a cyclone is the first step towards preparing oneself for the impending natural disaster. It is an effective communication tool that helps in easy communication, spreading awareness, and promoting timely action. The clear and simple naming structure ensures that people remain informed about the natural calamity in advance and take the required precautionary measures.
