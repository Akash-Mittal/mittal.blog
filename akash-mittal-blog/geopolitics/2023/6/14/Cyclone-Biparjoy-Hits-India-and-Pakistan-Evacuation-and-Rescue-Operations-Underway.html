Cyclone Biparjoy Hits India and Pakistan: Evacuation and Rescue Operations Underway



Image Credits https://www.scmp.com/news/asia/south-asia/article/3223954/7-dead-including-4-boys-cyclone-biparjoy-barrels-towards-india-pakistan-thousands-evacuated

By South China Morning Post

Every year, India and Pakistan experience a number of cyclones and other natural disasters that wreak havoc on the lives of millions of people. These disasters often come with little warning, leaving people with little time to evacuate or prepare for the worst.

However, in recent years, both countries have made significant strides in their disaster preparedness and response efforts. From building cyclone shelters and strengthening infrastructure to investing in early warning systems and conducting regular drills and training programs, India and Pakistan have demonstrated a commitment to keeping their citizens safe in the face of natural disasters.

Cyclone Biparjoy

On October 30, 2021, the India Meteorological Department (IMD) issued a warning about a cyclonic storm named Biparjoy that was expected to hit the coasts of India and Pakistan in the coming days.

To minimize the impact of the storm, both countries immediately began evacuating people from coastal areas and deploying rescue teams to ensure that those who remained were safe and secure.

Evacuation and Rescue Operations

By October 31, more than 40,000 people had been evacuated from vulnerable areas in both countries. India's National Disaster Response Force (NDRF) and Pakistan's National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) were working around the clock to rescue people who were still trapped in their homes or in other dangerous areas.

Rescue teams are also using technology such as drones and satellite imagery to identify and locate people who may be stranded or in need of assistance.

Case Studies

One of the key factors in the success of India and Pakistan's disaster preparedness and response efforts is the willingness of their citizens to participate in rescue operations and help those in need. Case in point:

  • In 2019, Cyclone Fani hit the Indian state of Odisha, causing widespread damage and resulting in the evacuation of over 1.2 million people. However, the state's disaster response efforts were hailed as a success, largely due to the preparedness of the local communities and their willingness to collaborate with the authorities.
  • In August 2020, several districts in Pakistan were hit by flooding due to heavy monsoon rains. Despite the challenging conditions, local communities and volunteers stepped forward to assist the authorities in rescue and relief efforts.

Conclusion

As Cyclone Biparjoy continues to barrel towards India and Pakistan, the two countries are working together to ensure the safety of their citizens. The success of their evacuation and rescue operations demonstrate the effectiveness of their disaster preparedness plans and the resilience of their communities.
