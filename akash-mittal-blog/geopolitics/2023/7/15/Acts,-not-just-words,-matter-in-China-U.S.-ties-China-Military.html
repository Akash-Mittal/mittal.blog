Acts, not just words, matter in China-U.S. ties

China Military

China and the United States have long had a complex relationship. The two countries, with their different ideologies and systems, have been engaged in a series of actions and negotiations that shape the bilateral ties. In recent times, the focus has shifted from words to actions as both nations take concrete steps to demonstrate their intentions and assert their power.

The Trump administration had adopted a robust approach towards China, labeling it as a strategic competitor. The rapid rise of China's military and economic prowess has fueled concerns among U.S. policymakers. The implementation of policies like trade tariffs, technology restrictions, and travel bans was aimed at curbing China's influence.

However, the Biden administration has continued to strengthen the stance against China, emphasizing the need for a more competitive approach. The shift in strategy is driven by the recognition that actions, not mere words, are essential for effectively managing the China-U.S. relationship. The United States has taken several noteworthy measures to address its concerns.

Trade and Economy

The trade war initiated by the Trump administration had significant impacts on the global economy, affecting both China and the United States. The Biden administration has continued to maintain certain restrictions on trade with China, particularly with regard to critical technologies and intellectual property. These measures are seen as an attempt to protect American businesses and prevent the transfer of sensitive information to China.

Additionally, the United States has been actively seeking to diversify its supply chains and reduce dependency on Chinese manufacturing capabilities. This move is aimed at reducing vulnerabilities and ensuring national security. Strengthening ties with like-minded countries in the Indo-Pacific region has become a priority for the Biden administration.

Technology and Security

Technological advancements play a crucial role in shaping the China-U.S. relationship. Both countries have been involved in fierce competition, especially in emerging technologies like 5G, artificial intelligence, and quantum computing. The United States has imposed restrictions on several Chinese tech companies, citing concerns over national security and data privacy.

The recently established 'Clean Network' initiative by the United States aims at securing the information and communication technology (ICT) infrastructure from untrusted vendors, with a specific focus on Chinese companies. This initiative has gained traction among like-minded countries and poses challenges for China's technology ambitions.

Military and Defense

The military dimension of China-U.S. ties has been crucial in maintaining regional stability and balance of power. The United States has continually reassured its allies in the Indo-Pacific region of its commitment to their security. Joint military exercises, increased military presence, and enhanced defense cooperation have become prominent features of the bilateral relationship.

China, on the other hand, has been modernizing its military capabilities and expanding its naval presence in the South China Sea. These actions have raised concerns among the U.S. and its allies, leading to increased military readiness and cooperation in the region.

The Way Forward

The China-U.S. relationship is unquestionably complex, with numerous areas of competition and cooperation. While words and rhetoric can set the tone, it is the actions taken by both nations that truly shape the relationship. Both China and the United States need to focus on building mutual trust, fostering open dialogue, and finding common ground on issues of strategic importance.

As the international order evolves, it is essential for both countries to understand that acts of collaboration, not just confrontation, are vital for achieving long-term stability and prosperity. Only through constructive actions can China and the United States navigate their differences and build a future that benefits not only their own people but also the international community as a whole.
