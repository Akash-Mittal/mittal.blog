Trade Between India and China Declines For First Time After Record High in 2022



Image Credits https://thewire.in/trade/india-china-trade-decreases-for-first-time-in-over-two-years

In a surprising turn of events, trade between India and China has experienced a decline for the first time after reaching a record high in 2022. This news has raised concerns among experts as the economic cooperation between the two Asian giants plays a significant role in the global market.

The bilateral trade volume between India and China stood at a staggering $110 billion in 2022, marking an impressive growth compared to previous years. However, recent data from trade agencies reveals a decline of 5% in the first quarter of 2023, amounting to $104.5 billion in total trade. This downturn can be attributed to several factors that have impacted the trade relationship between the two nations.

One factor that has contributed to the decline is the ongoing geopolitical tensions between India and China. The border disputes, particularly along the Line of Actual Control (LAC), have strained diplomatic relations and hindered trade flows. Both countries imposed trade restrictions and tariffs, limiting the movement of goods and leading to decreased export and import activities.

Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on global trade, including the trade between India and China. The pandemic disrupted supply chains, resulted in lockdowns, and led to a decrease in consumer demand. These factors combined have resulted in a decline in trade volume.

Furthermore, changes in political and economic policies of both countries have affected trade dynamics. India's focus on self-reliance and the promotion of domestic industries, as showcased through schemes like "Make in India," has reduced its dependence on Chinese imports. Simultaneously, China has been exploring alternative trade partnerships in regions like Southeast Asia and Africa, diversifying its trade portfolio.

It is important to note that the decline in trade does not necessarily indicate a fractured relationship between India and China. Both countries still recognize the importance of economic cooperation and have expressed their willingness to address the challenges and find mutually beneficial solutions. Several bilateral talks have taken place to discuss trade-related issues and explore opportunities for growth.

The decline in trade volume between India and China has led to concerns among businesses and investors in both countries. The two nations are major players in the global supply chain, and any disruption in their trade relationship can have far-reaching consequences. It is hoped that through dialogue and cooperation, the trade between India and China can rebound and continue its growth trajectory.
