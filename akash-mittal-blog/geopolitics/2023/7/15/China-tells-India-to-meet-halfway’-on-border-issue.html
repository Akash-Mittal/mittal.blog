China tells India to meet ‘halfway' on border issue



Image Credits https://www.thesundaily.my/world/china-tells-india-to-meet-halfway-on-border-issue-BB11236241

Beijing, China - In a diplomatic move to ease tensions between China and India, the Chinese government has called on India to meet "halfway" on the longstanding border issue that has strained relations between the two Asian giants for decades. The call for compromise comes as both sides continue military build-up near the Line of Actual Control (LAC) in the eastern Ladakh region.

The border issue between China and India dates back to the 1960s, following a brief but bloody war. Since then, both nations have made attempts to resolve the territorial dispute, but without significant progress. The latest round of tensions flared up in 2020 when violent clashes erupted in the Galwan Valley.

In a press conference held at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Beijing, Chinese spokesperson Zhao Lijian stated, "China urges India to meet us halfway and jointly maintain peace and stability in the border areas. The only way to resolve this issue is through negotiation and dialogue."

The Indian government, in its response, acknowledged the importance of dialogue but emphasized the need for both parties to strictly adhere to previously agreed-upon protocols and disengagement processes. "While we remain committed to resolving the issue peacefully, any resolution must be based on mutual trust and respect, and in accordance with the well-established principles of international law," stated Indian Foreign Ministry spokesperson Anurag Srivastava.

Analysts believe that China's call for compromise reflects a desire to de-escalate tensions and improve its international image, particularly in the aftermath of the border clashes and rising global scrutiny of its policies in various areas. On the other hand, India has adopted a cautious approach, insisting that any resolution must safeguard its territorial integrity and honor past agreements.

The border issue between China and India not only has implications on regional stability but also affects their bilateral economic ties. Both countries have invested heavily in fostering economic cooperation, with China being India's largest trading partner. The prolonged border dispute has, however, hindered the full potential of economic collaboration.

With both China and India being nuclear-armed nations, there is a significant concern within the international community regarding the potential escalation of the border conflict. Global leaders have called for restraint and peaceful resolution to avoid any military confrontation that could have serious ramifications.

Experts suggest that while compromise is necessary, it will require strong leadership and political will from both sides to reach a lasting solution. Confidence-building measures, increased diplomatic engagement, and sustained dialogue at multiple levels can help create an environment conducive to resolving the border issue peacefully.
