PM Modi concludes State visit to Egypt, emplanes for India

Prime Minister Narendra Modi concluded his successful State visit to Egypt today and took off from the Cairo International Airport to return to India. This visit marks an important milestone in the bilateral relations between the two countries, fostering cooperation and strengthening ties in various sectors.

During his visit, PM Modi engaged in high-level talks with Egyptian President Abdel Fattah El-Sisi, discussing mutual interests, regional security, and issues of global concern. Both leaders expressed their commitment to enhancing trade relations and economic cooperation between India and Egypt.

Around 10 MoUs have been signed between the two nations, ranging from cultural exchanges to technical collaborations. These agreements will facilitate the exchange of knowledge, technology, and expertise in fields such as agriculture, healthcare, renewable energy, and education.

One of the key highlights of PM Modi's visit was his address at the Al Azhar University in Cairo, renowned as one of the highest seats of learning in the Islamic world. In his speech, Modi emphasized the importance of unity and peace, promoting religious harmony and countering extremism. He called for a collective effort from all nations to eliminate terrorism.

The Indian community in Egypt also had the opportunity to interact with PM Modi at a special event organized to strengthen cultural ties and address their concerns. Modi acknowledged the contributions of the Indian diaspora in Egypt and assured them of continued support from the Indian government.

Furthermore, PM Modi visited historical sites such as the Great Sphinx of Giza and the Egyptian Museum, showcasing the deep cultural connect between India and Egypt. He expressed admiration for Egypt's rich heritage and its significant contributions to humanity throughout history.

The successful outcome of PM Modi's visit to Egypt sets the stage for further cooperation, particularly in the fields of defense, tourism, and trade. Both nations aim to increase bilateral trade and investments, exploring new avenues to boost economic growth.

As PM Modi emplanes for India, he expressed gratitude towards President El-Sisi and the people of Egypt for their warm hospitality. He reiterated his commitment to nurturing the strong bond between India and Egypt and emphasized the potential for greater collaboration.
