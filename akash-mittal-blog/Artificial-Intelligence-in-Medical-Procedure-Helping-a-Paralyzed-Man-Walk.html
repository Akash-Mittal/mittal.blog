Artificial Intelligence in Medical Procedure | Helping a Paralyzed Man Walk

Have you ever imagined the day when technology would help a paralyzed individual to walk again? With the development of artificial intelligence (AI), this has become a reality. AI is helping to revolutionize medical procedures around the world, including the possibility for paralyzed individuals to walk again. Let's explore how.

Meet James, a young man who was paralyzed after a tragic car accident. James had always been an active person, and the thought of never walking again devastated him. However, with the help of technology and medical professionals, James was able to undergo a procedure that completely changed his life.

Doctors implanted a device into James's spinal cord that, with the help of artificial intelligence, was able to send signals from his brain to his legs. After a few months of rehabilitation and training, James was able to stand up and walk again.

The use of artificial intelligence in medical procedures has shown incredible results across the board. For example, in one study, paralyzed patients who received a spinal cord implant with AI technology, were able to regain partial or complete movement. The study reported that 70% of patients regained significant function after a year of use, while 80% retained the ability to walk with crutches or a walker.

Another example is from a medical institution that developed a robotic exoskeleton assistance suit called HAL. In clinical trials, patients who used HAL were able to walk up to 200 meters without assistance after just a few months of training.
