Freedom Is Not Just an Idea: It's a State of Being



Image Credits Akash Mittal

From the moment we enter this world, we are granted the right to freedom. Freedom can take many forms- the freedom to speak our mind, the freedom to worship, the freedom to pursue our dreams. Unfortunately, throughout history, we have seen that freedom must always be defended. As Edward R. Murrow once said, "we cannot defend freedom abroad by deserting it at home."

The truth is that freedom is not just an idea; it's a state of being. It's what allows us to be who we are, to make choices that reflect our values and beliefs, and to live our lives on our terms. But when we fail to defend these rights, we risk losing them altogether.

Let's consider a story that illustrates the importance of defending freedom both at home and abroad. In the late 1980s, Nelson Mandela was released from prison after serving 27 years for his role in fighting against apartheid in South Africa. He had become a symbol of hope for millions around the world who believed in the fight for human rights and equality.

Upon his release, Mandela traveled to the United States to meet with political leaders, including President George H.W. Bush. The visit was a chance for Mandela to thank those who had supported the anti-apartheid movement, and to inspire people to continue the fight against oppressive regimes.

During his visit, Mandela was asked about the state of freedom in America. He replied that while America had made significant strides in the fight for civil rights, there was still much work to be done. He pointed out the disparities in education, healthcare, and economic opportunity that can have a disproportionate impact on minority communities.

Mandela's point was simple- the fight for freedom is ongoing, and it cannot be limited by borders or ideologies. If we truly believe in the universality of human rights, we must be willing to fight for them wherever they are threatened.

So, how do we defend freedom at home and abroad? Here are a few practical tips:

1. Speak out against oppression and injustice: One of the most powerful tools we have as advocates for freedom is our voice. Whether it's through social media, protests, or conversations with friends and family, we can raise awareness about the issues that matter.

2. Support organizations that promote freedom: There are countless organizations working to promote human rights and democracy around the world. Consider donating your time or money to support their efforts.

3. Hold leaders accountable: Politicians and other leaders must be held accountable for their actions, both at home and abroad. We must demand that they uphold the values of freedom and human dignity that are so vital to our society.

In conclusion, defending freedom is not only a responsibility, but also a privilege. We must cherish our right to freedom and actively work to preserve it, both at home and abroad. Together, we can create a world where freedom truly reigns.
