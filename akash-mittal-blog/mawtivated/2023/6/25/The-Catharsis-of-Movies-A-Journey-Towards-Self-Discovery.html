The Catharsis of Movies: A Journey Towards Self-Discovery



An Inspiring Story by Edward Zwick

When Edward Zwick was a teenager, he stumbled upon a movie that changed his life forever. It was "The Graduate" directed by Mike Nichols. Watching the movie made Zwick realize that he wanted to become a filmmaker and tell stories that would move people. It was a pivotal moment in his life that inspired him to pursue his dreams, and he went on to become one of the most acclaimed directors in Hollywood.

For Zwick, movies are not just a form of entertainment, but a tool for self-discovery and catharsis. In one of his interviews, he said:

"Sometimes when we weep in the movies we weep for ourselves or for a life unlived. Or we even go to the movies because we want to resist the emotion that's there in front of us. I think there is always a catharsis that I look for and that makes the movie experience worthwhile."

Indeed, movies have the power to move us, to make us laugh and cry, to inspire us and to challenge us. They can transport us to different worlds and perspectives, and make us see things in a new light. But what is it about movies that make them so cathartic and transformative?

1. Movies Speak to Our Innermost Desires

Movies have the ability to speak to our innermost desires and fears, and to tap into the universal human experience. Whether it's the longing for love and connection, the fear of death and loss, or the search for meaning and purpose, movies have a way of touching on the deeper aspects of our psyche.

Take for example the movie "The Wizard of Oz". On the surface, it's a whimsical fairy tale about a girl who finds herself in a magical land. But underneath the veneer of fantasy lies a powerful allegory about self-discovery and empowerment. Dorothy's journey is not just about finding her way back home, but also about realizing her own strength and potential.

  • Tip: When you watch a movie, pay attention to the underlying themes and messages. Try to identify what resonates with you on a personal level, and reflect on how it relates to your own life.

2. Movies Allow Us to Release Emotions

Another reason why movies can be cathartic is that they allow us to release pent-up emotions in a safe and controlled environment. We may not always feel comfortable expressing our feelings in real life, but when we see them mirrored on the screen, we may find it easier to let go.

For example, the movie "Gravity" directed by Alfonso Cuaron is a harrowing tale of survival in outer space. If you watch it in a theater on a big screen with surround sound, you may feel like you're right there with the characters, experiencing their fear and desperation. By the end of the movie, you may feel emotionally drained, but also liberated and empowered by the sheer force of the human spirit.

  • Tip: If you're going through a tough time in your life, watching a movie that speaks to your emotions may help you release some of the tension and find closure.

3. Movies Inspire Us to Take Action

Finally, movies can be cathartic because they inspire us to take action and make positive changes in our lives. Whether it's standing up to injustice, pursuing our dreams, or simply being more present in the moment, movies have the power to incite us to act.

For example, the movie "Erin Brockovich" directed by Steven Soderbergh is based on the true story of a single mother who takes on a powerful corporation for polluting a small town's water supply. The movie shows how one person can make a difference, and how persistence and determination can lead to justice and change.

  • Tip: If you're feeling uninspired or stuck in your life, watching a movie that portrays a character who overcomes obstacles may motivate you to take action in your own life.

The catharsis of movies is a powerful tool for self-discovery and transformation. By tapping into our emotions, desires, and fears, movies can help us become more aware of ourselves and the world around us. Whether it's finding inspiration, release, or action, movies have the ability to move us and change us for the better.
