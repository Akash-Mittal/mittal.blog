Bequeathing the Spirit of Reverence to Children



Story has it that once there was an old man who was on a journey with his grandson. As they were walking along the road, they came across a small plant that the old man picked up and showed it to the little boy.

"What is it, Grandpa?" the boy asked curiously.

The old man replied with a smile, "It's a plant, my child. In many ways, it is like you. It needs nurturing, care, and protection to grow to its fullest potential."

The little boy then asked, "Will it grow as big and strong as that tree over there?"

The old man nodded gently. "Yes, it will. But only if we provide for it. That is why we must take care of it and water it every day. Just like we should take care of our bodies and our souls."

This story is a poignant reminder that the spirit of reverence is not something that can be bought or inherited. Rather, it is a quality that must be nurtured and cultivated.

Who is Plato?

Plato was a philosopher in ancient Greece who advocated for the importance of a well-rounded education. He believed that children should be taught not just reading, writing, and arithmetic, but also philosophy, ethics, and metaphysics.

What is the Spirit of Reverence?

The spirit of reverence is an attitude of respect and awe toward the world around us. It is a deep appreciation for the beauty and wonder of the universe, as well as a recognition of our own place within it.

Why is the Spirit of Reverence important?

In a world that often values material possessions and fame over all else, the spirit of reverence can provide a much-needed sense of perspective. It reminds us that there are things in life that are more important than money, possessions, or status. It also encourages us to take care of the world around us, both for our own benefit and for the benefit of future generations.

Examples of the Spirit of Reverence in Action

  • A child kneeling in awe before a beautiful sunset
  • A family taking a walk in the woods and marveling at the natural world around them
  • A group of friends volunteering at a local charity, giving back to the community and helping those in need

How Can You Bequeath the Spirit of Reverence to Your Children?

Here are a few practical tips:

  1. Encourage curiosity and a sense of wonder. Children are natural explorers and are fascinated by the world around them. Encourage this curiosity by pointing out interesting things and asking open-ended questions.
  2. Talk openly about your values and beliefs. Children are like sponges, absorbing everything they see and hear. By sharing your own thoughts and feelings about the world, you can help instill a sense of reverence and respect in your children.
  3. Lead by example. Children learn more from what you do than what you say. Model the behavior you want to see in your children, whether it's taking care of the environment or treating others with kindness and respect.

Conclusion:

In summary, the spirit of reverence is a vital quality that can help us appreciate the beauty and wonder of the world around us. By nurturing this spirit in our children, we can help them grow into thoughtful, compassionate, and well-rounded individuals who will make the world a better place.

  2. Talk openly about your values and beliefs
  3. Lead by example
