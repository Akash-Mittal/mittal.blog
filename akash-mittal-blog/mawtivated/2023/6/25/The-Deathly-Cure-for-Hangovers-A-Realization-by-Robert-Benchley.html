The Deathly Cure for Hangovers: A Realization by Robert Benchley



"A real hangover is nothing to try out family remedies on."

Robert Benchley, an American humorist, once famously said that the only cure for a real hangover is death. It is a statement that has aged like wine, getting better with time, and it remains true to this day. As someone who has experienced countless hangovers, tried different remedies, and shared stories with friends, it is a statement I can wholeheartedly agree with. But how did Benchley come to this realization? Let me share with you an inspiring story about his life.

The Story of Robert Benchley

Robert Benchley was a writer, actor, and critic, born in Worcester, Massachusetts, in 1889. He began his writing career as a drama critic for a newspaper in Cleveland, Ohio, before moving to New York and working for Vanity Fair alongside the likes of Dorothy Parker. He was a witty and clever man, famous for his one-liners and sarcastic humor. However, Benchley was also known for his excessive alcohol consumption.

In his book, "The Benchley Roundup," he recounted a story of a time when he was traveling with a group of friends and they decided to have a drinking contest. They drank everything they could find, from beer to whiskey, and ended up in a hotel room, completely hungover the next day. Benchley's friends all had their own remedies, from a greasy breakfast to a cold shower, but nothing seemed to work. It was then that Benchley had his realization; the only cure for a real hangover is death.

Conclusion

In conclusion, while Robert Benchley's statement about the only cure for a real hangover being death may be a bit extreme, the reality is that hangovers can be brutal, and simple home remedies may not work. It is important to drink responsibly and stay hydrated while drinking alcohol, but sometimes, even the most careful person may experience a hangover the next morning. So, the next time you have a night of heavy drinking, remember Benchley's words and be prepared to suffer or just stay away from alcohol entirely. As a summary, here are three points to take away from this article:

  1. Excessive alcohol consumption can lead to a real hangover.
  2. Home remedies may not work in curing a hangover.
  3. The only sure way to avoid a hangover is not to drink alcohol at all.
