The Power of Holding Onto Something that Makes You Whole



Everyone has experienced loss in their lives. Some have lost loved ones, pets, jobs, or even relationships. And sometimes, the loss can be so devastating that it can leave us feeling empty and lost without a sense of direction. Reese Hoffa, a professional shot putter, knows this feeling too well. Losing his birth mother at a young age, he realized the importance of holding onto something that makes you feel whole.

Reese Hoffa's Story

Reese Hoffa is a three-time Olympian and a two-time World Indoor Champion in the men's shot put. He has won numerous medals and accolades in his career. But his success does not come without challenges. Reese was adopted at the age of four and grew up with his new family, who supported him in his athletic pursuits. However, when he was 16, his birth mother passed away from colon cancer. This tragedy left him feeling lost and vulnerable, unsure of how to navigate through life without his birth mother.

When you have lost people like I lost my birth mom at a young age and you remember the whole process of losing her, you want to grab on to something that makes you whole. - Reese Hoffa

Reese knew he needed to find something that would help fill the void left by his mother's death. He turned to sports, where he discovered a passion that would sustain him through the years. He found a sense of purpose and direction in striving to become the best shot putter he could be, dedicating himself to practicing and improving his skills.

The Importance of Holding Onto Something That Makes You Whole

Reese's story highlights the importance of holding onto something that makes you feel whole. When we experience loss, it can be easy to feel hopeless and lost. We may feel that our lives have lost meaning and that we are directionless. But holding onto something that brings us joy and a sense of purpose can help us navigate through the difficult times.

Personal anecdotes help illustrate this point. For example, one person may find solace in writing, while another may find comfort in painting. Some may turn to fitness or travel to help them through a tough time. Whatever it may be, finding something that makes you feel whole and helps you feel connected to yourself is crucial in moving through loss and adversity.

Examples of How to Hold Onto Something That Makes You Whole

Here are some examples of how you can hold onto something that makes you feel whole:

  • Find a hobby or interest that brings you joy and purpose.
  • Connect with friends and family who support and encourage you.
  • Learn a new skill or take up a new challenge.
  • Practice mindfulness and meditation to center yourself and connect with your emotions.

Conclusion

In conclusion, holding onto something that makes you feel whole is essential in navigating through loss and adversity. Reese Hoffa's story is an inspiration, demonstrating the power of passion and determination in overcoming challenges. By finding something that brings us joy and purpose, we can move forward with a greater sense of purpose and fulfillment.

Remember:

  1. Find what makes you feel whole and dedicated yourself to it.
  2. Surround yourself with a support system of friends and family.
  3. Stay positive and committed to your goals.
