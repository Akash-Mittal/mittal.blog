The Power of Touring Alone: Adventures of Jane Siberry



Inspiring Story of Jane Siberry

Jane Siberry, a Canadian singer-songwriter, is a true inspiration for many musicians who dream of having a successful career while staying true to their authentic self. Siberry is known for her unique voice, experimental music style, and her willingness to tour alone, without any sound check or back up. She has been living this way for over 20 years, and her touring style has become her trademark.

Despite being in the music industry for decades, Siberry did not compromise her way of touring. She said in an interview, "I tour alone. There's no sound check, no back up. I stay with the hosts, I am in a family home, and it's really nourishing. I just have to remember after the show not to run out into the living room in my pyjamas. Every day, it's a new relationship being built. It's odd and wonderful."

Her approach to touring alone has been an inspiration for many aspiring artists who struggle to find their place in the industry. Siberry has shown that being true to oneself and maintaining creative independence can lead to great success.

Jane Siberry's Story and Background

Jane Siberry, born in Toronto in 1955, started her music career as a folk singer in the 1980s. She became a sensation with her album "The Speckless Sky," which earned her a Juno Award in 1985.

She continued to release several albums that explored different music styles, including jazz, classical, and folk. Siberry's career reached its peak in the 1990s when she signed with Warner Bros. Records and was praised for her experimental approach to music.

However, despite her success in the industry, Siberry did not compromise her artistic independence. She started touring alone in the early 2000s, and since then, has been able to connect with her fans in a more intimate way.

Why Touring Alone is Powerful

Jane Siberry's approach to touring alone has several benefits. Here are some reasons why it can be a powerful way to build a career as a musician:

  • Authenticity: Touring alone allows artists to stay true to their music and creative vision without any external pressure or influence.
  • Intimacy: Performing in small venues and staying with the hosts allows artists to connect with their fans in a more personal way and build lasting relationships.
  • Creative Freedom: Performing without backing tracks or sound checks gives artists the freedom to experiment and improvise with their music.
  • Cost-Effective: Touring alone can be more affordable for independent artists who cannot afford to hire a full band or production team.

Practical Tips for Touring Alone

If you are considering touring alone, here are some practical tips to help you get started:

  1. Plan ahead: Make sure to plan your tour route, accommodations, and transportation in advance to avoid any last-minute stress.
  2. Build relationships: Connect with local hosts, venues, or promoters ahead of time and build relationships to ensure a successful tour.
  3. Stay organized: Keep track of your finances, set up online bookings and payments, and maintain a schedule to stay organized throughout the tour.
  4. Stay safe: While touring alone can be a great experience, it's essential to take safety precautions, such as letting someone know your itinerary and having a backup plan in case of emergencies.

  3. If you are considering touring alone, make sure to plan ahead, build relationships, stay organized, and stay safe.
