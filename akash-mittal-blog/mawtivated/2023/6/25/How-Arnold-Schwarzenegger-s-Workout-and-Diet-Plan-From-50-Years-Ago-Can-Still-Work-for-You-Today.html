How Arnold Schwarzenegger's Workout and Diet Plan From 50 Years Ago Can Still Work for You Today



When Arnold Schwarzenegger said, "I do the same exercises I did 50 years ago and they still work. I eat the same food I ate 50 years ago and it still works," he was not only referring to his past achievements but also to a valuable lesson about fitness and nutrition.

His statement is the perfect reminder that sometimes, the old ways are still the best. In this article, we will explore how Arnold's workout and diet plan from 50 years ago can still work for you today.

Inspiring Story

Arnold Schwarzenegger is a living legend in bodybuilding, and he continues to showcase the power of sticking to what works. At the age of 15, he discovered weightlifting and was immediately hooked. However, living in post-World War II Austria, the resources were limited.

Arnold did not have access to advanced gym equipment or supplements, so he had to rely on basic weights, bodyweight exercises, and a simple diet. Despite this, Arnold became one of the greatest bodybuilders of all time, winning Mr. Universe at the age of 20 and Mr. Olympia seven times.

Arnold's story is inspiring because it highlights the power of determination, consistency, and simplicity. He did not have access to the latest trends or techniques, but he believed in his method and trained hard every day. His results speak for themselves.

Arnold Schwarzenegger's Story and Background

Arnold Schwarzenegger was born in Austria in 1947 and started weightlifting at the age of 15. In 1965, he won the Mr. Austria competition and moved to the United States to pursue his dream of becoming a professional bodybuilder.

Over the next few years, Arnold won numerous bodybuilding titles, including Mr. Universe, Mr. World, and Mr. Olympia. He also appeared in movies, such as Conan the Barbarian and Terminator, and became the Governor of California from 2003 to 2011.

Throughout his career, Arnold has always emphasized the importance of hard work and dedication. He has inspired millions of people to pursue their fitness goals and lead a healthy lifestyle.

Arnold Schwarzenegger's Workout Plan

Arnold's workout plan from 50 years ago was simple, yet effective. It consisted of six days a week of weightlifting, targeting one or two muscle groups per day. Here is a breakdown of his weekly schedule:

Monday: Chest and Back

- Bench Press

- Incline Dumbbell Press

- Dumbbell Flyes

- Barbell Rows

- T-Bar Rows

- Chin-Ups

Tuesday: Shoulders and Arms

- Overhead Press

- Lateral Raises

- Rear Delt Raises

- Barbell Curls

- Dumbbell Curls

- Tricep Pushdowns

Wednesday: Legs

- Squats

- Leg Press

- Leg Extensions

- Hamstring Curls

- Standing Calf Raises

- Seated Calf Raises

Thursday: Chest and Back (light workout)

- Dumbbell Bench Press

- Dumbbell Flyes

- Lat Pulldowns

- Bent-Over Rows

- Seated Cable Rows

- Dumbbell Pullovers

Friday: Shoulders and Arms (light workout)

- Military Press

- Dumbbell Lateral Raises

- Rear Delt Raises

- Barbell Curls

- Hammer Curls

- Tricep Extensions

Saturday: Legs (light workout)

- Leg Press

- Leg Extensions

- Hamstring Curls

- Calf Raises

Arnold's workout plan focused on compound exercises, which involve multiple muscle groups at once. This approach allows you to lift heavier weights and build more muscle. He also used high-volume training, doing multiple sets of each exercise with a moderate weight.

His workout plan is still relevant today because it emphasizes the importance of consistency and progressive overload. By lifting weights regularly and gradually increasing the weight or reps, you can build muscle and improve your fitness level.

Arnold Schwarzenegger's Diet Plan

Arnold's diet plan from 50 years ago was also simple. He ate six meals a day, consisting of whole foods and lean protein. Here is a breakdown of his daily diet:

Meal 1: Omelet with vegetables and whole-grain toast

Meal 2: Protein shake and fruit

Meal 3: Grilled chicken or fish with brown rice and vegetables

Meal 4: Protein shake and almonds

Meal 5: Beef or poultry with sweet potato and vegetables

Meal 6: Cottage cheese or Greek yogurt with fruit

Arnold's diet plan is still relevant today because it emphasizes the importance of whole, nutrient-dense foods. By eating a balanced diet that includes lean protein, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats, you can fuel your body and optimize your health.

Conclusion

Arnold Schwarzenegger's workout and diet plan from 50 years ago may seem outdated, but they are still relevant today. By focusing on basic weightlifting exercises and whole, nutrient-dense foods, you can build muscle, lose weight, and improve your overall health.
