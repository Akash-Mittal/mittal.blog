I'm in Control of My Life



An Inspiring Story by Drew Barrymore

Who would have ever thought that a young girl who had so many personal issues would later prove to the world that she's undoubtedly the one in control of her life?

At the age of 13, Drew Barrymore had already become a household name, thanks to her lead role in the movie E.T. fame. However, things took a sharp turn when she started engaging in risky behaviors such as drugs and alcohol.

"I knew my life had to turn around. No one was going to take care of me but myself," Barrymore said during an interview.

It's been years since Drew's big turnaround, and today, she has nothing but an abundance of inspiration to share. She's an accomplished actress, a successful producer, and a mom of two adorable daughters. Barrymore's life serves as an inspiration to people all over the world, and her quote, "I'm so in control of my life, you shouldn't dislike anything I do," is proof that she's living her best life.

The Power of Taking Control

Each day, life presents us with numerous challenges that make it easy for us to feel like we're not in control. It's at these times that we need to rise up and take control of our lives. Taking control isn't just about empowering yourself; it's also about setting the pace for how your life plays out.

In Drew Barrymore's case, she took control by staying away from drugs and alcohol and focusing on living a healthy life. For each of us, it's different, but the power is in your hands to make the changes that you need to take control of your life.

Practical Tips to Take Control

  • Identify areas where you're losing control - this is the first step to making necessary changes.
  • Set achievable and realistic goals - break down your goals into manageable pieces that you can attain and monitor your progress.
  • Stay accountable - have someone you trust to keep you accountable and encourage you when things get tough. It could be a therapist, mentor, or close friend.
  • Focus on what you can control - stop focusing on things that are beyond your control. Spend time and effort on things that you can change.

The Benefits of Taking Control

When you take control of your life, you open yourself up to an array of benefits, such as:

  1. Increased happiness and fulfillment as you steer your life toward your goals.
  2. A sense of empowerment, which encourages you to be more confident in the decisions you make.
  3. Reduced stress levels as you tackle challenges head-on and stay in control of your life.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Drew Barrymore's quote, "I'm so in control of my life, you shouldn't dislike anything I do," is a testament to the power of taking control of your life. When you're in control, you feel empowered, confident, and fulfilled. By identifying areas that you need to work on, setting achievable goals, staying accountable, and focusing on the things you can control, you, too, can be in control of your life.
