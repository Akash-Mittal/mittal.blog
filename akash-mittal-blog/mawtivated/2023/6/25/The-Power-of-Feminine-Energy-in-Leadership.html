The Power of Feminine Energy in Leadership



An Inspiring Story

In the early 1970s, a young girl from Chile was forced to flee her home after her uncle was assassinated. She had to leave behind everything she knew and loved. After living as a refugee in Venezuela for years, she moved to the United States and started a new life. Despite facing numerous challenges, she never gave up on her dreams. Today, she is one of the most influential writers of our time. Her name is Isabel Allende.

Why We Need Feminine Energy in Leadership

"For real change, we need feminine energy in the management of the world. We need a critical number of women in positions of power, and we need to nurture the feminine energy in men." These were the words spoken by Isabel Allende in her TED Talk in 2014. And she could not be more right. Feminine energy is the nurturing, compassionate, intuitive, and collaborative energy that exists in all of us, regardless of gender. It is what the world needs right now to face the challenges we are all facing.

For centuries, our societies have been dominated by masculine energy - the energy of competition, power, and control. This energy has led us to where we are today - a world that is plagued by violence, inequality, and environmental destruction. It is time for a change.

Bringing more feminine energy into leadership means bringing more empathy, compassion, and collaboration into the way we make decisions and solve problems. It means creating a more balanced and harmonious world, where everyone has the opportunity to thrive. This is not just a matter of social justice or moral values - it is a matter of survival.

Examples of Feminine Energy in Leadership

  • Angela Merkel, the Chancellor of Germany, who has been praised for her empathetic leadership during the COVID-19 crisis.
  • Jacinda Ardern, the Prime Minister of New Zealand, who has championed policies that prioritize well-being and sustainability over economic growth.
  • Greta Thunberg, the young climate activist who has inspired millions of people around the world to take action against climate change.

When I was working at a tech company a few years ago, I noticed that most of the managers and executives were men. The work culture was highly competitive and individualistic, and it often felt like everyone was trying to prove their worth. As a woman, I found it difficult to fit in, and I often felt like my ideas were not being heard.

Things changed when a new CEO was hired - a woman who embodied feminine energy. She valued collaboration and empathy, and she encouraged everyone to contribute to the company's vision. She created a more inclusive work culture, and she made sure that everyone felt heard and valued. As a result, the company became more innovative and successful.

The Three Benefits of Feminine Energy in Leadership

  1. Increased well-being: When leaders prioritize the well-being of their employees and stakeholders, they create a more positive and supportive work environment, which leads to better mental and physical health for everyone involved.
  2. More innovation: When leaders encourage collaboration, empathy, and diversity of thought, they create a space for new ideas and perspectives to emerge, which leads to more innovative solutions to complex problems.
  3. Greater sustainability: When leaders prioritize the long-term health of the planet and its inhabitants, they create a more sustainable and resilient world, where everyone has the opportunity to thrive.

Practical Tips for Embodying Feminine Energy in Leadership

  • Practice active listening: Make sure that everyone's ideas and perspectives are heard and valued.
  • Empower others: Encourage your team members to take on leadership roles and to contribute to the decision-making process.
  • Create a positive work culture: Foster a sense of community, support, and collaboration among your team members.
  • Lead with empathy: Consider the feelings and needs of your employees and stakeholders, and make decisions that prioritize their well-being.
