The Power of Language: How Ken Follet Became a Best-Selling Author in Germany



An Inspiring Story of Success

It was the early 1990s and Ken Follet, a British novelist, had already established himself as a popular author in English-speaking markets. His books - which spanned across various genres including historical fiction and thriller - were beloved by readers around the world. However, there was one country where his work was struggling to make an impact: Germany.

"It's great that in the German language I've sold almost 30 million books. Isn't that amazing?" - Ken Follet

Despite his previous successes, Follet's books were initially met with little enthusiasm in Germany. His first novel, "Eye of the Needle," had been translated and published in German, but it failed to catch on with readers. Nevertheless, Follet remained determined and knew that if he could crack the German market, it would open up enormous opportunities for him.

Language is Key

As someone who spoke several languages, Follet understood the importance of language in connecting with readers. He also knew that a good translation could make or break a book's success in a foreign market. So, he set about finding a new translator who could capture the essence of his work and help him gain a foothold in the German market.

He eventually found the perfect collaborator in Ruth Keen, a translator with a deep understanding of German culture and language. Together, Follet and Keen began a fruitful partnership that would see his books become bestsellers in Germany and beyond.

The Power of Persistence

However, even with Keen's expert translation skills, Follet's books still faced an uphill battle in Germany. It took years of hard work, marketing campaigns, and personal appearances at book signings before his name was finally recognised in the country.

But Ken Follet never gave up. He persisted, knowing that his stories had the potential to connect with readers no matter what language they were written in. And eventually, his persistence paid off. His books began to sell by the millions in Germany, making him one of the country's most beloved authors.

The Takeaway

So, what can we learn from Ken Follet's success in the German market? Here are three key takeaways:

  1. Language matters. If you're looking to expand your reach into new markets, take the time to understand the language and cultural nuances of your target audience.
  2. Persistence pays off. Success rarely comes overnight. Keep pushing and advocating for your work, even when it feels like you're not making any progress.
  3. Collaborate with experts. Whether it's a translator, marketer, or literary agent, surround yourself with people who understand the needs of your target audience.
