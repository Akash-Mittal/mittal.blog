Living with an Open Heart: The Jane Seymour Story



An inspiring tale of hope, resilience, and generosity

Once upon a time, there was a young girl named Jane Seymour who dreamed of becoming an artist. She spent hours on end sketching, painting, and doodling, ignoring the world around her and losing herself in the colors and shapes that filled her mind. One day, while she was wandering in the woods, she stumbled upon a wounded bird that lay helplessly on the ground, its wings broken and its eyes pleading for mercy.

"I remember feeling so helpless and scared," Seymour later recalled. "I didn't know what to do or how to help. But then something inside me told me not to give up, not to turn away. So I picked up the bird, held it close to my heart, and prayed for it to heal."

To her amazement, the bird not only survived but also flew away, soaring into the sky and leaving Seymour with a newfound sense of purpose and power. From that day on, she vowed to live with an open heart, spreading kindness and compassion wherever she went. And that's exactly what she did, becoming not only a successful artist but also a philanthropist, a jewelry designer, an actress, a mother, and a symbol of hope for millions of people around the world.

The Power of Open Hearts

What does it mean to live with an open heart? For Seymour, it means being vulnerable, authentic, and empathetic. It means acknowledging your own pain and struggles, but also recognizing that others are going through their own challenges and need your support. It means not being afraid to love and be loved, to give and receive, to trust and be trusted. It means embracing life with all its beauty and messiness, and finding meaning and purpose in every moment.

  • Open hearts are resilient. They bounce back from setbacks, failures, and disappointments, learning from them and growing stronger.
  • Open hearts are generous. They give without expecting anything in return, knowing that the act of giving itself is a gift.
  • Open hearts are loving. They connect with others on a deep level, sharing their joys and sorrows, and creating meaningful relationships.
  • Open hearts are creative. They unleash their imagination, curiosity, and passion, expressing themselves in unique and inspiring ways.

The Open Hearts Jewelry Line

In 2004, Seymour launched her Open Hearts collection of jewelry, inspired by her mother's advice to always keep her heart open. The design features an abstract heart shape that resembles two open wings, symbolizing the idea that love can lift us up and carry us through difficult times. The collection includes necklaces, rings, bracelets, and earrings, all made of gold or silver and adorned with diamonds or gemstones. Each piece is a tangible reminder of the power of open hearts, and a way to honor and celebrate the people and moments that have touched our lives.

"Creating the Open Hearts design was a true labor of love," Seymour said. "I wanted to capture the essence of what my mom taught me and share it with the world. I never imagined that it would become such a beloved and iconic symbol, but I'm thrilled and humbled by the response it has received."

Living with an Open Heart: Practical Tips

How can we cultivate and nurture our own open hearts? Here are three practical tips:

  1. Practice self-awareness. Be honest with yourself about your thoughts, feelings, and needs. Reflect on your past experiences and how they have shaped you. Ask for help when you need it, and offer help when you can.
  2. Practice empathy. Put yourself in other people's shoes. Listen to them without judgment. Try to understand their perspectives and feelings. Show compassion and kindness, even when it's hard.
  3. Practice gratitude. Focus on the good things in your life. Appreciate the people, places, and things that make you happy. Say thank you often. Give back to others, and pay it forward.

With these simple but powerful practices, we can open our hearts to ourselves and others, and create a more loving and meaningful world.
