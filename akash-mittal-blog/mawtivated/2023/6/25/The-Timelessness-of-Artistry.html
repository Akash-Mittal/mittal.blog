The Timelessness of Artistry



Once upon a time, there was an artist named Edgard Varese. He was born in 1883 in France and studied music in Paris. However, Varese's unconventional approach to music didn't sit well with his professors, and he clashed with them frequently.

He moved to the United States in 1915 and suffered from extreme poverty. Nevertheless, he continued to experiment with sound and music, creating pieces that were considered too radical for their time. He coined the term "organized sound" to describe his compositions, which often included electronically produced noises.

"It is not enough to teach man a specialty," Varese once said. "In teaching man knowledge, we must teach him to seek for himself, we must inspire him to that thirst for knowledge."

Varese's music would go on to influence generations of composers, and he's now considered one of the most significant figures in the development of electronic and experimental music.

But it wasn't always that way. When he was alive, Varese was often dismissed by the critics and audiences of his time. People couldn't comprehend his vision, and many believed he was simply making a bunch of noise. Yet, decades later, his music sounds fresh and contemporary, as if it was made yesterday.

Being Ahead of Your Time

Varese's story is not unique. Many artists, writers, musicians, and other creatives have been ahead of their time, producing work that challenged conventions and pushed boundaries. These visionaries were often misunderstood and ridiculed, but they persevered.

  • Vincent van Gogh's paintings were rejected by the art establishment of his day, but his post-impressionist style would go on to inspire generations of artists.
  • Jane Austen's novels were considered too feminist and critical of society, but today she's recognized as one of the greatest writers in English literature.
  • The Beatles were initially ignored by record labels and dismissed as a passing fad, but their music remains iconic, beloved, and influential.

The lesson here is that true artistry often exists outside of the mainstream and challenges our preconceived notions of what is possible. It's not enough to simply replicate what has come before. The most creative individuals are those who are willing to take risks, to delve into the unknown, and to fail before they succeed.

How to Embrace Timelessness

If we want to truly appreciate the work of artists who are ahead of their time, we need to adopt a different mindset. Here are some ideas to consider:

  • Be open-minded. Don't dismiss something just because it doesn't fit the mold.
  • Be curious. Learn about the context in which the work was created and what the artist was trying to achieve.
  • Be patient. Sometimes it takes time to appreciate something that's different from what we're used to.

By doing these things, we can broaden our understanding of art and culture and recognize the value of work that may have been overlooked in its time.

Conclusion

  1. Artists who are ahead of their time often face rejection and criticism.
  2. True artistry challenges conventions and pushes boundaries.
  3. We can embrace timelessness by being open-minded, curious, and patient.
