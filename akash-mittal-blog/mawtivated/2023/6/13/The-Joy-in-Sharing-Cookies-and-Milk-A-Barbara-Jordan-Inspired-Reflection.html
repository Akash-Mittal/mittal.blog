# The Joy in Sharing Cookies and Milk - A Barbara Jordan Inspired Reflection



Image Credits Akash Mittal

Imagine a world where every single person can look forward to cookies and milk, followed by a peaceful nap at 3 o'clock in the afternoon. It may seem like a simple and trivial idea, but in reality, this vision carries with it a profound message that can inspire us all to become better versions of ourselves.

Barbara Jordan, an American politician who served as a congresswoman from 1973 to 1979, was known for her eloquence and inspiring speeches that championed social justice and equality. Her words, both spoken and written, continue to resonate with generations of people who strive to make the world a better place.

One of her most beloved quotes is the one we mentioned earlier: "Think what a better world it would be if we all, the whole world, had cookies and milk about three o'clock every afternoon and then lay down on our blankets for a nap." At first glance, it may seem like a whimsical and fanciful thought, but take a closer look and you'll see that it carries within it a message of hope, unity, and care.

Why Cookies and Milk Matter

So, why cookies and milk? At its essence, this humble snack and the accompanying nap time represents a simple act of kindness that we can all engage in. It's a moment of pause, a time to recharge ourselves and connect with others over something as simple as a shared snack.

But beyond this, cookies and milk can also symbolize the power of commonality and inclusivity. It's a snack that transcends age, gender, race, and social status. No matter who you are or where you come from, you can sit down and enjoy a cookie and milk with someone else.

This act of sharing and enjoying something together can foster a sense of community and togetherness, something that is sorely lacking in many parts of our world today. Whether it's through a shared snack, a conversation, or a common cause, we can all contribute to making the world a better place by building bridges between each other, one cookie at a time.

The Power of Rest

Another important aspect of Barbara Jordan's quote is the idea of taking a nap. In our fast-paced and busy world, taking a moment to rest and recharge is often seen as a luxury or an indulgence. However, science has shown time and again that rest is essential for our physical, mental, and emotional health.

Taking a nap can help improve memory, boost productivity, reduce stress, and even lower the risk of heart disease. Beyond these benefits, though, taking a nap gives us the chance to disconnect from the demands of everyday life and focus on ourselves for a moment.

When we're well-rested, we're better able to show up for the people and causes that matter to us. We're more patient, compassionate, and resilient in the face of challenges. By prioritizing rest in our lives, we can become better versions of ourselves and contribute to a more peaceful and harmonious world.

How To Live Barbara Jordan's Message

So, how can we take Barbara Jordan's vision and make it a reality in our own lives? Here are some practical tips to consider:

1. Share Cookies and Milk with Someone You Love

The next time you have a free afternoon, invite a friend, family member, or neighbor over for cookies and milk. It doesn't have to be anything fancy or elaborate - just a simple snack and a chat can go a long way in building stronger relationships and fostering a sense of community.

2. Take Time to Rest and Recharge

Make rest a non-negotiable part of your daily routine. Whether it's a quick catnap after lunch or a longer rest period on the weekends, prioritize rest and give yourself the time and space you need to recharge.

3. Look for Ways to Build Bridges

Lastly, look for ways to connect with people who are different from you. Whether it's through volunteering, attending events, or reaching out to someone you've never met before, making connections and building relationships can help us all feel more connected and valued.

Barbara Jordan's simple yet profound message reminds us that we all have the power to make positive changes in the world. By sharing cookies and milk, taking time to rest and recharge, and building bridges with those around us, we can all contribute to a more hopeful and harmonious world.
