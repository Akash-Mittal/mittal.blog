Being an Artist: Dragging Your Innermost Feelings Out



An Inspiring Story

Years ago, a struggling artist named Jane was feeling frustrated with her lack of success. She had been pouring all of her emotions into her art, but no one seemed to be paying attention. She decided to enter one of her paintings into a local art show, hoping to finally get noticed.

The day of the show arrived, and Jane anxiously made her way around the room, looking at all the other pieces on display. She was in awe of the talent and creativity she saw on the walls, and began to doubt her own abilities. But then, something amazing happened.

"Your painting is incredible," a stranger said to Jane. "It's like you captured a piece of your soul on canvas."

Jane was overwhelmed with emotion. That was exactly what she had been trying to do with her art - capture her innermost feelings and share them with the world. From that moment on, she never lost sight of that goal.

About the Author - Henry Rollins

Henry Rollins is a musician, spoken-word artist, and former frontman of the punk rock band Black Flag. He is known for his intense, raw performances and his commitment to expressing his feelings through his art.

Rollins once said, "Being an artist is dragging your innermost feelings out, giving a piece of yourself, no matter in which art form, in which medium." This quote perfectly encapsulates the essence of being an artist.

Examples of Dragging Your Innermost Feelings Out

  • Writing a deeply personal memoir
  • Pouring your heart into a song or album
  • Creating expressive choreography for a dance performance
  • Painting a portrait that captures the subject's essence
  • Performing a one-man show that explores your own experiences

As an artist myself, I can relate to Rollins' quote on a deep level. One of my first paintings was a self-portrait that I created during a period of intense emotional turmoil. I poured all of my sadness, fear, and uncertainty onto the canvas, and the result was a raw, emotional piece that revealed so much about me. It was scary to put such a vulnerable part of myself out into the world, but it was also incredibly freeing.

I also have a friend who is a dancer, and she once told me about a performance she choreographed based on her experience with anxiety. She used sharp, jagged movements to represent the feeling of panic, and then gradually transitioned into smooth, fluid movements as a way of finding peace. Watching her perform this piece was both moving and inspiring.

Conclusion

  1. Being an artist means being willing to express your innermost feelings.
  2. Art is a powerful tool for self-expression and can help us connect with others on a deeper level.
  3. Don't be afraid to be vulnerable - it's often the most honest and compelling art that resonates the most with others.
