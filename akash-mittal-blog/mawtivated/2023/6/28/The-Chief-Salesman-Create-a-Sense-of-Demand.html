The Chief Salesman: Create a Sense of Demand



An Inspiring Story of a Businesswoman

People are often waiting for some kind of demand to create a business or start an entrepreneurial venture. Countless individuals strive to come up with an idea for a product or service that will naturally have people lining up to utilize it. However, Barbara Corcoran, founder of the Corcoran Group and renowned investor on the TV show Shark Tank, firmly believed that this is a faulty way of thinking. In business, you're the Chief Salesman. Create a sense of demand, rather than waiting to have demand.

In the 1970s, Barbara was a struggling real estate agent in New York City. While showing a property to a potential buyer, she told them a white lie to try and make the space appear more valuable. She told her buyer that another interested party had put in a substantially higher offer than the property was actually worth. The buyer was worried that they would lose the property, and ended up agreeing to the higher price that Barbara had falsely advertised. Barbara's ingenious move helped her make the sale and cemented the idea of creating demand in her mind.

  • Tip 1: Be proactive in creating demand for your business and products.
  • Tip 2: Don't be afraid to use creative tactics to show the value of your offerings.
  • Tip 3: The key to creating demand is in being able to identify what your market needs and wants.
The thing that separates the winners from the losers, in business, is one simple thing. The winners walk into a room, and the first thing they think is, 'How do I sell everybody in this room?' The losers walk into the same room and think, 'Who do I know in this room?'

This quote from fashion guru Andre Leon Talley speaks volumes about the mindset of a successful businessperson. To be a Chief Salesman, one needs to have the confidence to believe in their own abilities and believe that they have the power to create demand. It is a mentality that is laser-focused on strategizing and executing plans that are meant to develop and maintain a constant flow of clients and profits.

Examples of Creating Demand

Apple is a company that is renowned for creating demand for its products. Apple makes it a priority to showcase its unique design concepts and technologies in order to make people want their products. In 2007, when Apple announced the release of the iPhone, it created a wave of excitement among Apple fans even though the technology was still not available for purchase. Apple's catchy slogans, "Think Different" and "Designed by Apple in California" have become world-famous advertising lines that draw people to their brand. They create a culture and lifestyle that people want to be a part of, and this is what leads to massive demand for their products.

Similarly, the brand Nike has been able to create demand by positioning itself as a premium sports and fitness brand. By partnering with athletes and popular social media influencers, Nike has established itself as a symbol of athleticism and success. When people wear Nike, they feel like they are part of a select group of people who are determined to achieve their fitness goals. This mindset helps create a constant demand for their products.

Conclusion

In conclusion, creating demand is not something that can be left to chance, nor is it something that can be expected to naturally happen. It involves identifying the needs and wants of your target market and then employing various creative tactics, marketing strategies, and building a unique brand that people want to be a part of. By being proactive and constantly refining your approach to creating demand, any entrepreneur can successfully build a successful business.

  • Be proactive: Take the initiative in the marketplace.
  • Identify demand: Recognize the needs and wants of your target market.
  • Create a unique brand: Build a brand that people can identify with and aspire to.
