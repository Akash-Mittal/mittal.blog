Why Judge Roberts is a Judicial Mind, not an Ideological Puppet



An Inspiring Story

As a child, Judge John Roberts had a unique hobby. He would study the United States Constitution and memorize its text. It was this passion that fueled his interest in becoming a lawyer, and eventually a judge.

But what sets Judge Roberts apart from many other judges is his commitment to impartiality and reasoned decision making. He is one of the few judges who puts the law and the Constitution above any political agenda.

and Case Studies

Senator Ron Wyden has worked with Judge Roberts for years and believes that he is one of the most thoughtful and careful judges in the country.

"It is hard to see Judge Roberts as a judicial activist who would place ideological purity or a particular agenda above or ahead the need for thoughtful legal reasoning."

This sentiment is echoed by many legal experts who have studied Judge Roberts' decisions and opinions. He is not afraid to take unpopular stances if he believes they are required by the law.

For example, in the case of Citizens United v. FEC, Judge Roberts wrote the majority opinion that allowed corporations to spend unlimited amounts of money in political campaigns. This decision was highly criticized by many, but Judge Roberts believed it was in keeping with the First Amendment's guarantee of free speech.

Similarly, in the case of King v. Burwell, Judge Roberts was the deciding vote that upheld the Affordable Care Act. This decision was unexpected and controversial, but Judge Roberts believed it was the right decision based on the principles of legal interpretation.

Examples of Judge Roberts' Judicial Philosophy

  • Judge Roberts is known for his commitment to originalism, which means he interprets the Constitution according to its original meaning when it was written. This approach ensures that the Constitution remains relevant and applied in a way that reflects the intentions of the framers.
  • Judge Roberts often emphasized that he is not a policy maker. He believes that the legislature is responsible for making laws and judges are only responsible for interpreting them.
  • Judge Roberts has also emphasized the importance of separation of powers. He believes that the role of the judiciary is to provide a check on the power of the legislative and executive branches, while respecting the boundaries of each branch.

Conclusion: Why Judge Roberts is a Judicial Mind, Not an Ideological Puppet

  1. Judge Roberts is committed to impartiality and reasoned decision making, and does not allow political agendas to influence his decisions.
  2. Judge Roberts uses originalism to interpret the Constitution and believes that the role of the judiciary is to provide a check on the power of the legislative and executive branches.
  3. Judge Roberts is not a policy maker, but an interpreter of the law who upholds the principles of legal interpretation.
