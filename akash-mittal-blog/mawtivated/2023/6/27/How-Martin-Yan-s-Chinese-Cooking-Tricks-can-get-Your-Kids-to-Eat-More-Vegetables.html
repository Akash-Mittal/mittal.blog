How Martin Yan's Chinese Cooking Tricks can get Your Kids to Eat More Vegetables



An Inspiring Story

As a busy parent, you know how hard it can be to get your kids to eat their vegetables. But what if we told you there was a way to make vegetables so delicious that your kids begged for more?

Enter Martin Yan, famous chef and host of the popular cooking show "Yan Can Cook." Martin has been cooking up delicious Chinese meals for over 40 years, and he has a special trick for getting kids to eat their vegetables.

When Martin cooks Chinese food, he mixes everything together, including the vegetables. This means that kids can't pick out the veggies without also getting some of the other delicious ingredients. Before they know it, they've eaten their entire serving of vegetables without even realizing it!

"So when I do Chinese cooking, I mix everything together, then the kids have to eat their vegetables. They won't have the patience to pick them out." - Martin Yan

By following Martin Yan's tricks, you can make vegetables a delicious and irresistible part of your family's meals.

Martin Yan's Story

Martin Yan was born in Guangzhou, China, and grew up in a family of chefs. He began cooking at a young age and went on to study at the Overseas Institute of Cookery in Hong Kong. In 1978, Martin moved to the United States and began teaching cooking classes and hosting cooking shows. Today, he is known all over the world for his dynamic personality and delicious Chinese cuisine.

Martin's approach to cooking is all about making it accessible and fun for everyone. He believes that cooking is a way of sharing love and kindness with those around us, and his recipes reflect this philosophy.

Examples of Martin Yan's Tricks

Here are some examples of how you can use Martin Yan's Chinese cooking tricks to get your kids to eat more vegetables:

  • Stir fry: Stir frying is a great way to mix everything together and make vegetables part of the meal. Add veggies like bell peppers, snap peas, and broccoli to your dish, and your kids will love the variety of colors, textures, and flavors.
  • Spring rolls: Spring rolls are a fun and easy way to get your kids to eat their vegetables. Add a variety of veggies to the filling, and let your kids assemble their own rolls for added fun.
  • Fried rice: Fried rice is a classic Chinese dish that's perfect for mixing everything together. Add a variety of veggies to your fried rice, like carrots, peas, and corn, and your kids will love the tasty combination.

Conclusion

  1. Mixing vegetables in with other ingredients is a great way to get your kids to eat more of them.
  2. Chinese cooking is a fun and delicious way to make vegetables part of your family's meals.
  3. By following Martin Yan's tricks, you can make vegetables a tasty and irresistible part of your family's diet.
