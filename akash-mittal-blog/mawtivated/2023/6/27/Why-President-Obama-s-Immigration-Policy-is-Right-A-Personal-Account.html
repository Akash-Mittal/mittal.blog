Why President Obama's Immigration Policy is Right: A Personal Account



By Eliot Spitzer

An Inspiring Story

Meet Sofia. Sofia was brought to the United States by her parents when she was just two years old. She has grown up in the US, has always identified as an American, and has excelled in school. Unfortunately, Sofia has been living in the shadows, knowing that at any moment, she could be deported to a country she doesn't even remember.

But then President Obama implemented the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program. Sofia was able to apply for and receive protection from deportation, and she was even able to obtain a work permit. Today, Sofia is able to live and work openly in the US, free from the constant fear of being separated from her family and deported to a foreign country. Thanks to DACA, Sofia can pursue her dreams and contribute to society without the fear of being punished for something she had no control over.

Why President Obama's Immigration Policy is Right

President Obama is doing the right thing by offering young immigrants, most often in this country through no action of their own, a chance to live and work openly, free from the fear of deportation. Here are just a few reasons why:

  • DACA allows Dreamers, young immigrants who were brought to the US as children, to come out of the shadows and contribute to society without fear of deportation.
  • Many Dreamers have grown up in the US and consider themselves American. It is only fair that they should be able to stay in the country they know as home.
  • Allowing Dreamers to work legally means they can contribute to the economy and pay taxes, which benefits everyone.
"We are a nation of immigrants, and we should be proud of that fact. Offering a path to citizenship for Dreamers is the right thing to do morally and economically."

and Case Studies

While many people think of immigration policy in abstract terms, it is important to remember that real people's lives are impacted by these policies. Here are just a few personal anecdotes and case studies:

  1. As a former governor of New York, I had the privilege of meeting many Dreamers who lived in my state. These were young people who had grown up in New York, had graduated from high school, and were now attending college or working. They were bright, ambitious, and wanted nothing more than to be able to fully participate in society.
  2. I also spoke with many business owners who were thrilled to be able to hire Dreamers who had obtained work permits through DACA. These were hardworking and talented individuals who were eager to contribute to their communities and build better lives for themselves and their families.

These stories illustrate just how important DACA is to so many people. It is a lifeline for Dreamers who want nothing more than to be able to live and work without fear of deportation.

Conclusion

In conclusion, President Obama's immigration policy is right because it allows young immigrants, most often in the country through no action of their own, a chance to live and work openly without fear of being deported. Here are three key takeaways:

  1. DACA allows Dreamers to come out of the shadows and contribute to society.
  2. Many Dreamers have grown up in the US and consider themselves American, so it is only fair that they should be able to stay in the country they know as home.
  3. Allowing Dreamers to work legally means they can contribute to the economy and pay taxes, which benefits everyone.

It is important to remember that behind every immigration statistic is a real person with a story. By supporting policies like DACA, we are enabling people like Sofia to fully participate in society and achieve their dreams.
