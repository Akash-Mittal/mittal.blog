Adolescence: Just One Big Walking Pimple



Written by Carol Burnett

An Inspiring Story

When I was in junior high, I had a terrible case of acne. I was embarrassed to go to school and face my friends every day. I tried all the OTC remedies my mother could afford, but nothing seemed to work. I was losing hope until one day, a girl in my class noticed my struggle and offered some advice. She recommended I see a dermatologist, and that's when things started to turn around. The dermatologist prescribed me with some medication, and within a few months, my skin cleared up.

That experience changed my life. It made me realize that people can be kind and supportive, even when things are tough. It also taught me the importance of seeking help when needed.

Adolescence: A Difficult Journey

Adolescence can be a challenging phase in life. It's a time when we're trying to figure out who we are while facing societal pressures to conform. Our bodies are changing, and we're dealing with emotions that we're not always equipped to handle. To make matters worse, acne can add to our already complex struggles.

As comedian Phyllis Diller once said, "Adolescence is the age at which children stop asking questions because they know all the answers."

Adolescence is a time when we feel invincible yet vulnerable. It's a time when we think we know everything but are still trying to figure out life. It's a time of contradictions and confusion.

Dealing with Acne: Tips and Strategies

1. Seek Professional Help

As my personal story illustrates, seeking professional help can make a world of difference. Dermatologists are experts in their field and can prescribe medication that works. They can also provide advice on how to take care of your skin and manage your acne.

2. Practice Good Hygiene

Washing your face twice a day with a gentle cleanser can help remove excess oils and prevent acne. Avoid touching your face since your hands can transfer dirt and bacteria. Also, if you wear makeup, make sure to remove it before going to bed.

3. Avoid Picking Your Pimples

It's tempting to pick at your pimples, but that can lead to scarring and further inflammation. Instead, use acne creams that contain benzoyl peroxide or salicylic acid, which can help dry out pimples.

4. Stay Positive

Acne can take a toll on your self-esteem, but it's essential to remember that it's a common condition that affects many people. Don't let your acne define who you are. Instead, focus on your strengths and the things you enjoy doing.

5. Support from Friends and Family

It's okay to ask for support from your loved ones. Your family and friends can be valuable allies in your journey to clear skin.

Conclusion

  1. Adolescence is a time of change and confusion. Acne can add to the complexity of this phase.
  2. Seeking professional help, practicing good hygiene, avoiding picking pimples, staying positive, and seeking support from friends and family are effective ways of dealing with acne.
  3. Remember, you are not alone, and acne does not define who you are.
