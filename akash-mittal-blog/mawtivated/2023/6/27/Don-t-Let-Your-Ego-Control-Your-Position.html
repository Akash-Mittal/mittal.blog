Don't Let Your Ego Control Your Position



An Inspiring Story of Overcoming Ego

Let me share with you a personal story about my experience with ego and how it almost cost me everything.

"Don't let your ego get too close to your position, so that if your position gets shot down, your ego doesn't go with it." - Colin Powell

Years ago, I held a position of power in my company. My ego was on a high, and I believed I was untouchable. I made decisions without considering other opinions, and I relied solely on my intuition. However, my pride blinded me to my shortcomings.

Eventually, my actions caused a major problem that ended up costing the company millions. I had no one to blame but myself. Still, I refused to admit that I was wrong. My ego wouldn't allow it. It was only after I lost everything, my job, my reputation, and my self-respect, that I finally realized my ego had been controlling me all along.

The Danger of Allowing Your Ego to Control Your Position

Colin Powell's quote is essential, especially for individuals who hold positions of power and authority. An inflated ego can cause someone to become arrogant, assume they know best, and overvalue their abilities. When ego takes over, a person's leadership potential will crumble. Their decisions will be self-centered, and they won't be concerned about the big picture.

Here are some dangers of allowing your ego to control your position:

  • You become unable to accept constructive criticism
  • You start to play the blame game
  • Your decision-making is flawed
  • You lose the trust and respect of your colleagues
  • You become inflexible and resistant to change
  • You stop listening to others.

How To Keep Your Ego In Check?

Here are some strategies that can help you keep your ego in check and prevent it from derailing your career:

  1. Develop Self-Awareness: Self-awareness is the first step in limiting the influence of your ego. It involves being able to recognize your emotions, strengths, and weaknesses.
  2. Accept Criticism: Feedback is essential for personal and professional growth. You'll never learn if you're defensive and can't accept criticism. Be open-minded to others' points of view, even if you don't agree with them.
  3. Collaborate: Collaborating with colleagues will help you develop relationships and build a cohesive team. Listen to your team members' thoughts and acknowledge the value of their expertise.
  4. Practice Empathy: Empathy is the ability to understand and connect with others. It's an essential leadership skill that helps you connect with your colleagues on an emotional level.
  5. Challenge Your Assumptions: Ego-based thinking often leads to false assumptions and flawed decision-making. Challenge your assumptions regularly; it will help you view situations from different perspectives.

The Bottom Line

You can't escape having an ego; it's a natural part of human nature. However, it's essential to acknowledge your ego's existence and the impact it has on your position and the people around you. For successful leadership, you must keep your ego in check and be willing to admit when you're wrong. Remember, it's always better to be humble than be sorry.

Conclusion

  1. Develop Self-awareness
  2. Accept criticism
  3. Collaborate with your team
