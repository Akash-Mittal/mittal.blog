From Music to Film: How Star Wars Destroyed the Science Fiction Genre



It was a warm summer night when I first saw Star Wars. I was a kid then, and like any other kid in the late 70s, I was mesmerized by the space battles, the lightsabers, and the epic story of good versus evil. But as I grew up and started to appreciate the art of music and film, I began to see how Star Wars, despite its undeniable success, has had a negative impact on the science fiction genre.

The Rise of the Music Scores

One of the things that made science fiction films stand out in the past was their music scores. From the haunting soundtracks of Blade Runner and Alien to the iconic themes of Star Trek and Close Encounters of the Third Kind, these films were a treasure trove of musical inspiration for composers.

But with Star Wars came the reign of John Williams, who created one of the most recognizable soundtracks in film history. While there is no denying the brilliance of Williams' work, it overshadowed the other scores that came before and after it. Suddenly, science fiction films became synonymous with bombastic orchestral music, and composers who tried to explore other genres or experiment with new sounds were often dismissed.

"Star Wars seems really to have destroyed the genre, which at one time offered great musical opportunities." - Carter Burwell

The Impact on Filmmaking

Star Wars also had a major impact on the way science fiction films were made. Before Star Wars, science fiction films were often slow, moody, and thought-provoking, with an emphasis on atmosphere and imagery. But after the success of Star Wars, studios realized that they could make a lot of money by producing big-budget, action-packed science fiction films.

While there is nothing wrong with making money, this shift in focus meant that the science fiction genre became increasingly shallow and formulaic. Gone were the days of films like 2001: A Space Odyssey and Solaris, replaced by films like Independence Day and Transformers.

What Can We Learn?

Despite its flaws, Star Wars is still a beloved film, and its impact on popular culture cannot be denied. But as we move forward, it is essential that we learn from its mistakes and try to create a more diverse and nuanced science fiction genre.

  • Embrace different types of music. While orchestral music can certainly be powerful and inspiring, there is no reason why science fiction films can't explore other genres or collaborate with artists from different backgrounds.
  • Focus on storytelling. The best science fiction films are the ones that make you think, that challenge your beliefs and make you question the world around you. It is essential that we continue to create films that have a deeper meaning and purpose.
  • Experiment with different styles. Science fiction is a genre that is all about exploring the unknown and pushing the boundaries of what is possible. It is essential that we continue to experiment with different styles and techniques, and not be afraid to take risks.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Star Wars may have had a negative impact on the science fiction genre, but it has also inspired countless filmmakers and musicians over the years. As we move forward, it is essential that we continue to learn from both its successes and failures, and strive to create a more diverse and engaging genre that can inspire the next generation of storytellers and artists.
