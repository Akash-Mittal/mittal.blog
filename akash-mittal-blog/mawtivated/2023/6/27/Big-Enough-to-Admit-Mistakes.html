Big Enough to Admit Mistakes



"It is not the strongest of the species that survives, nor the most intelligent that survives. It is the one that is most adaptable to change," said Charles Darwin.

Adapting to change requires acknowledging mistakes. But admitting your mistakes takes guts. It's hard to swallow your pride when everything inside tells you otherwise. However, the truth is, being big enough to admit your mistakes can lead to positive change.

A Story of Admitting Mistakes:

It was a Sunday evening, and Mark had forgotten to pick up a paintbrush for the community project on Monday. He realized this after all the stores had closed. Mark was the lead of the project, and everything was ready except for the paintbrush.

The only option was to ask for help. Mark reached out to his friend, who could easily have refused to help, given how late it was. But, his friend was happy to lend him the paintbrush.

The next day the project was a success, and at the end of the day, while packing, Mark's friend thanked Mark for giving him the opportunity to contribute. Mark realized his mistake and that he should have asked for help earlier. He felt embarrassed that he had let his ego block him from asking for help when he really needed it.

Author - John C. Maxwell:

John C. Maxwell, an American author, speaker, and pastor, has written many books on leadership. The quote mentioned here, "A man must be big enough to admit his mistakes, smart enough to profit from them, and strong enough to correct them," is a testament to his emphasis on always striving to improve.

Examples:

  • When a CEO admits they have made a mistake, their employees become more trusting and develop more respect for that leader. Instead of hiding mistakes or blaming others, they admit the mistake and work on finding a solution.
  • When a software developer explains an error, they quickly gain the trust of the customer. It can foster communication and help improve the software.
  • When a student acknowledges a mistake they made in class, it shows their interest in learning and being open to constructive feedback.

Three Tips for Admitting Your Mistakes:

  1. Be honest with yourself: Admitting your mistake to yourself is the first step to acknowledging it.
  2. Be open to feedback: Feedback can be ugly, but it can be a valuable tool in improving your mistakes.
  3. Take action: Admitting your mistake is great, but only if you take action to correct it.

Conclusion:

Acknowledging mistakes can be hard. It requires a lot of humility but doing so can lead to growth and positive change. Remember to be honest with yourself, open to feedback, and ready to take action.

As John C. Maxwell said, "A man must be big enough to admit his mistakes, smart enough to profit from them, and strong enough to correct them."
