The Pursuit of Happiness: A Planet Nourished by Human Rights and Nuclear Survival



Image Credits Akash Mittal

As a child, Jimmy Carter once saw a black man being beaten by a group of white men. Disturbed by this incident, he later confessed to his mother that he had lost faith in the fairness of the American system. His mother instilled in him a sense of hope and justice, telling him that he could make a difference in the world. It was this early experience that led him to become a fierce advocate for human rights and global peace as the 39th President of the United States.

For Carter, the pursuit of happiness was not just about individual pleasure, but about the well-being of the entire planet. In a world threatened by nuclear war, climate change, and poverty, he advocated for a path based on nuclear survival, human rights, and the nourishment of our physical and spiritual selves.

Nuclear Survival

In the 21st century, the threat of nuclear war continues to loom over the world. In order to pursue happiness, we must first ensure our survival. This means working towards a world without nuclear weapons. We must support policies and agreements that reduce and ultimately eliminate the risk of nuclear war. The International Campaign to Abolish Nuclear Weapons (ICAN) won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2017, and their goal is exactly that - to prohibit and eliminate nuclear weapons worldwide. The world saw the grave dangers of a nuclear arms race in the Cold War era, but the threat of nuclear weapons is still very real. Nuclear weapons should never be used again on people, as they are a crime against humanity.

Human Rights

As President, Carter placed human rights at the center of his foreign policy. He established the U.S. Commission on International Religious Freedom, which monitors the right to religious freedom abroad. He condemned the use of torture and advocated for fair elections, free speech, and the right to assemble. His commitment to human rights led to the historic Camp David Accords between Israel and Egypt, which have held as a peace agreement for over 40 years.

Today, human rights continue to be essential to the pursuit of happiness. We must protect the rights of all people, regardless of their race, gender, sexuality, or beliefs. This means standing up against discrimination and actively promoting equality. As Nelson Mandela famously said, "To be free is not merely to cast off one's chains, but to live in a way that respects and enhances the freedom of others."

The Nourishment of Our Planet

Carter believed that the pursuit of happiness also means ensuring that our planet is nourished both physically and spiritually. This involves protecting our natural environment by reducing carbon emissions and promoting clean energy, but it also involves feeding the hungry and supporting those in need. Carter's involvement in the Habitat for Humanity movement, which builds affordable housing for low-income families, exemplifies this commitment to nourishing our planet.

In addition to reducing emissions and feeding the hungry, we also need to care for our spiritual selves. This means making time for self-care and finding ways to connect with others in a meaningful way. Practices such as meditation, yoga, and mindfulness can help us to find inner peace and cultivate empathy and compassion for others.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the pursuit of happiness involves nuclear survival, human rights, and the nourishment of our planet. By working towards a world without nuclear weapons, protecting human rights, and caring for both our physical and spiritual selves, we can pave the way towards a happier, healthier, and more just world.

Three key takeaways for pursuing happiness:

  1. Work towards a nuclear-free world to ensure our survival
  2. Protect human rights for all people, no matter who they are
  3. Nourish both the physical and spiritual aspects of our planet
