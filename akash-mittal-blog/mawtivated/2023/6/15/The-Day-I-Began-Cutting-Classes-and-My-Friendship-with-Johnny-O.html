The Day I Began Cutting Classes and My Friendship with Johnny O

By: Randy West

An Inspiring Story

It all started with a love for television shows. I remember being captivated by the bright lights, cameras, and action that takes place during a TV taping. This love eventually had me cutting classes just to attend these tapings. It wasn't long before I met Johnny O, who was a production assistant at the time. He showed me the ropes and reminded me of the importance of staying in school, but also pursuing my passion. This moment changed my life, and it ultimately led me to where I am today.

  • During one of the TV tapings, I got to meet the director who has been in the industry for over 20 years. He offered me a job as a runner to help with the production, which eventually led to a permanent position.
  • Johnny O and I eventually became close friends and shared a common interest in writing for television shows. We spent countless nights brainstorming ideas and pitching concepts to production companies.

Increasing Fascination and Respect

"I discovered that producing a television show is not just about filming actors, it is about creating a world that is believable and engaging."

As I spent more time attending TV tapings, I gained an increased fascination and respect for what goes into producing a successful show. It's not just about what happens on camera, but the entire production process. Every detail, no matter how small, plays a crucial role in creating a believable and engaging world. It takes a highly coordinated team effort to bring a show to life.

Examples of Successful Shows

  • Breaking Bad: This show's success is attributed to the strong writing and character development. The showrunners had a clear idea of where they wanted the story to go, and the result was a gripping, emotionally charged television drama.
  • Boardwalk Empire: Apart from the brilliant cast and memorable characters, the intricate sets and attention to detail made the show more convincing and realistic.

Conclusion

  1. Follow your passion, but don't forget the importance of education.
  2. It takes a team effort and attention to detail to create a successful television show.
  3. Never stop learning and exploring new opportunities.
