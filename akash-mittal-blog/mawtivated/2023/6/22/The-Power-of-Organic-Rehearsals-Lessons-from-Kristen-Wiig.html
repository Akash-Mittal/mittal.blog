The Power of Organic Rehearsals: Lessons from Kristen Wiig



A story of how one actress became a master of her craft.

An Inspiring Story

Imagine you're a struggling actress, trying to make it in Hollywood. You've had some small roles, but nothing that really showcases your talent. You go to audition after audition, but the parts always seem to go to someone else.

But then, one day, you get the chance of a lifetime. You're asked to audition for a new sketch comedy show that's being developed by a young up-and-coming producer. You're nervous, but excited. This could be your big break.

You go into the audition room, and there's Kristen Wiig. She's one of the producers, and she's going to be performing opposite you in the scene. You've seen her on SNL, and you're a little intimidated. But then something amazing happens. She starts improvising with you, and it's like magic. The scene comes to life, and you both start cracking up.

Afterwards, Kristen tells you that you're perfect for the part. You're over the moon. But then she says something that surprises you. She says that she doesn't like to rehearse too much. She likes to keep things organic. And you realize that that's exactly what made the scene so great.

Kristen Wiig's Story

Kristen Wiig is one of the most talented comedic actresses of her generation. She's known for her unique characters and her ability to improvise on the spot. But how did she get to be so good?

Well, it turns out that Wiig didn't start out as a performer. She grew up in a small town in upstate New York, and went to college to study art. After graduation, she moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career as a designer. But then she stumbled upon a class in improv, and everything changed.

Wiig fell in love with improv, and started taking classes wherever she could. She eventually joined the famed Los Angeles-based improv troupe The Groundlings, and honed her skills there. It wasn't long before she was discovered by SNL, and the rest is history.

The Power of Organic Rehearsals

So what is it about organic rehearsals that makes them so effective? Well, there are a few things:

  • They keep things fresh. When you rehearse a scene too much, it can start to feel stale. But when you keep things organic, you never know what's going to happen. This keeps you on your toes, and makes the performance more exciting.
  • They encourage improvisation. When you're not locked into a specific script or blocking, you're free to improvise. This can lead to some truly amazing moments that you wouldn't have thought of otherwise.
  • They build trust and rapport. When you're improvising with someone, you're building a connection with them. You're trusting them to go along with your ideas, and they're trusting you with theirs. This can lead to a stronger performance overall.

Of course, organic rehearsals aren't right for every situation. If you're doing a highly scripted play or a complex dance routine, you'll need a lot of rehearsing to get everything just right. But for more improvisational work, like sketch comedy or stand-up, organic rehearsals can be a powerful tool.

Practical Tips

So how can you incorporate organic rehearsals into your own work? Here are a few practical tips:

  • Don't be afraid to improvise. Even if you have a script, try to be open to improvise and be spontaneous. You might be surprised at what you come up with.
  • Trust your instincts. When you're in the moment, trust your gut. If you feel like something isn't working, speak up and try to change it.
  • Be open to feedback. When you're working with others, be open to their ideas and suggestions. Even if it's not exactly what you had in mind, it might lead to something great.
"I don't rehearse a lot. I try to keep it organic. Even in movies, the less I rehearse, the better I am." - Kristen Wiig
