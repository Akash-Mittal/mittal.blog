Statistics: A Form of Wish Fulfillment



Statistics are everywhere. From the charts shared on Instagram to the graphs on your textbooks, they are ubiquitous in our lives. However, they are not just mere data points. Statistics, like dreams, are a form of wish fulfillment.

An Inspiring Story

Let me give you an example. Growing up, I always dreamed of becoming a writer. However, the statistics showed that the job market for writers was highly competitive and that only a handful of people were able to support themselves through writing alone. So, I gave up on my dream and pursued a more conventional career path.

Years went by and I found myself unhappy in my job. I missed the creativity and passion that I had aspired to in my youth. One day, I decided to take a leap of faith and start writing again. Despite the statistics, I became a published author and was able to turn my passion into a career.

Author - Jean Baudrillard

Jean Baudrillard is a French philosopher, cultural critic, and author known for his work on postmodernism, consumer culture, and simulation theory. He argued that society has replaced all reality and meaning with symbols and signs, and that our world has become a simulation of itself.

One of the most significant statistics that Jean Baudrillard commented on was the idea of the "obesity epidemic." He believed that the concept of obesity had become a symbol of the decay of Western society and that it was used to distract us from larger, more significant issues. However, as someone who struggled with her weight, I have always found that statistic to be discouraging and judgmental.

It wasn't until I discovered the body positivity movement that I realized the harm that statistics and societal norms can do to our self-esteem and self-worth. I began to embrace my body as it was, and I learned to appreciate the diversity and beauty in all body types. Now, I feel empowered to speak out against the harmful stereotypes and statistics that hold us back as a society.

Examples

  • The unemployment rate in the US is at its lowest in 50 years
  • The divorce rate in the US has declined for the third year in a row
  • 69% of American adults use social media
  • 81% of people support a ban on smoking in public places

Conclusion

  1. Statistics can be limiting and discouraging, but they do not define us. We have the power to shape our own lives and destinies.
  2. We must be critical of the statistics that are presented to us and question their validity and biases.
  3. We should strive to embrace diversity and individuality, rather than relying on statistics to define who we are and what we are capable of.
