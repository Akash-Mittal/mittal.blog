Encounter with Christmas: The Decisive Encounter between God and Mankind



Christmas is not only a day of festivity and gift-giving but also a time for spiritual reflection, forgiveness, and renewal. In the hustle and bustle of the holiday season, we often forget the true meaning of this joyous occasion. Christmas is an encounter between God and mankind, which rekindles faith and hope and inspires us to live a life of charity, generosity, and kindness. Let me share an inspiring story with you to understand the importance of this encounter.

An Inspiring Encounter

For many years, a homeless man named Bob used to sit outside a church in downtown New York and watch the bustling crowds of people rushing past him on their way to work. Bob was an alcoholic and had lost all his family, friends, and possessions due to his addiction. He had no hope and saw no purpose in life except for the occasional handout from strangers or churches that showed him pity.

One day, as Bob was sitting outside the church, a little girl walked up to him and offered him a homemade cookie and a smile. Bob was taken aback by this kind gesture and said, "Why are you being so nice to me, little girl? Don't you know that I'm a bum and a disgrace?"

The little girl looked at Bob with innocent eyes and said, "My mommy taught me that Christmas is about love and sharing. We have so much and you have so little. So, I thought I could share my cookie with you and see you smile. Merry Christmas!"

Bob was moved to tears by this simple act of kindness and felt a glimmer of hope in his heart. He realized that he had been looking for love and comfort in all the wrong places, but he had never thought about the true meaning of Christmas. He went inside the church that day and met the pastor, who welcomed him with open arms and listened to his story. Bob found solace and redemption in the church, and he never forgot the little girl who showed him the way to the true encounter with Christmas.

Pope Paul VI and the Encounter with Christmas

The concept of encounter has been an integral part of Christian theology and spirituality. Pope Paul VI, who served as the head of the Catholic Church from 1963 to 1978, highlighted the significance of encounter in his teachings. He believed that encounter was the foundation of evangelization, which meant spreading the Good News of Jesus Christ to all nations.

"We must regard Christmas as an encounter, the great encounter, the historical encounter, the decisive encounter, between God and mankind. It is this encounter that gives us hope and joy, and it is this encounter that transforms our lives and the world."

Pope Paul VI emphasized that Christmas was not just a cultural or sentimental event but a profound encounter between God and humankind that changed the course of history and the destiny of humanity. He urged Christians to rediscover the true meaning of Christmas and to share the love and mercy of God with all those who had lost their way.

Examples of Encounter with Christmas

Encounter with Christmas can take many forms, depending on our cultural, social, and personal background. Here are some examples of how people around the world connect with the true meaning of Christmas:

  • Volunteering at soup kitchens, homeless shelters, or nursing homes
  • Visiting sick or elderly relatives and friends
  • Donating money, clothes, or food to charities or organizations
  • Attending midnight Mass or religious services
  • Sharing stories, songs, or traditions with family members
  • Forgiving or reconciling with estranged or hurtful people
  • Making handmade gifts or cards for loved ones

There are many ways to encounter Christmas, but the key is to open our hearts and minds to the presence of God and to see the world through the eyes of love and compassion. As Pope Paul VI said, "He who has faith knows this truly let him rejoice."

Conclusion

Encounter with Christmas is not a one-time event or a superficial gesture but a lifelong journey of faith and conversion. We are called to encounter God in our daily lives through prayer, service, and worship, and to encounter our fellow human beings with dignity, respect, and love. This encounter transforms us and empowers us to be agents of positive change in the world, spreading hope and joy to those who need it the most.

In conclusion, let us remember that Christmas is not about what we get or what we give but about who we encounter and how we encounter them. Let us be open to the miraculous encounter between God and humanity that took place on that first Christmas night, and let us share the light of that encounter with others.
