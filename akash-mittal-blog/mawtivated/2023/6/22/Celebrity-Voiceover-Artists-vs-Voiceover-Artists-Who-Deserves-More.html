The Voiceover Artist Dilemma: Who Deserves More?



Once upon a time in Hollywood...

David Duchovny was your typical struggling actor in Hollywood, taking any job that could pay his bills. One day he stumbled upon a voiceover job for a commercial and decided to give it a try.

Years later, Duchovny would be known as the voice behind some of the most iconic commercials, audiobooks, and animations - a true voiceover artist in the industry.

Voiceover Artists vs. Celebrity Voiceover Artists

The voiceover industry has evolved over the years. It used to be a space for talented voice actors who needed the job opportunities. Nowadays, more and more celebrities are dipping their toes in the voiceover industry and taking up jobs that could have otherwise gone to the voiceover artists.

"At one time there were voiceover artists, now there are celebrity voiceover artists. It's unfortunate because these people need the money less than the voiceover artist." - Rob Paulsen, Voiceover Artist.

  • One example is Beyonce, who voiced Nala in the Lion King remake.
  • Ariana Grande, who voiced Moly in the Angry Birds 2 movie.
  • Even Kevin Hart, who voiced Snowball in the Secret Life of Pets.

Even though these celebrities have impressive resumes in the entertainment industry, they don't necessarily have the same experience or talent as trained voiceover artists. It could be argued that these celebrities are taking away jobs from professionals in the industry who rely on them to pay their bills.

David Duchovny Story

David Duchovny is a prime example of the transformation of the voiceover industry. Starting as a struggling actor, he ultimately built his name and reputation as a voiceover artist through his craft. The industry has come a long way since then, with celebrities now taking center stage in the voiceover space, leaving less opportunities for talented and trained professionals like Duchovny.

The Reality of the Situation

Celebrity voiceovers, as opposed to a trained voiceover artist, might come with a higher price tag. Companies tend to prioritize working with these celebrities to market their products or movies as a form of brand recognition. In some cases, these celebrities might not be worth their pay compared to the quality and authenticity professional voiceover artists bring to the table.

The Conclusion
  1. Celebrity voiceover artists are taking opportunities from trained and experienced voiceover artists.
  2. Celebrities might not be the best fit for every voiceover job, but they are often favored by companies due to their recognition and brand value.
  3. The voiceover industry is ever-evolving, and it is up to industry professionals to adapt and advocate for their work and craft.
Some Practical Tips for Voiceover Artists
  • Focus on your specialty and work hard to get the best out of it. Be the best in what you do and focus on delivering quality.
  • Be creative. Always bring something new and unique to the table. In an industry that's constantly changing, staying current and ahead of trends is key to staying relevant.
  • Become self-promoting. Utilize social media and participate in public speaking engagements to showcase your skills and expertise. You never know when the next big opportunity will come your way.
