Love: It's All About Giving



One day, a kind-hearted traveler was walking through a remote village when he saw an old man who was struggling to plant a tree in his backyard. The traveler stopped and asked the old man: "Would you like some help?" The old man replied: "Yes, if you don't mind." And so the traveler helped the old man plant the tree.

As they were working together, the old man said: "You know, this tree won't bear fruit until after I'm dead. But I'm planting it anyway, because I believe in giving back to the next generation." The traveler was moved by the old man's words, and he realized that love was not just about receiving, but about giving as well.

A bell's not a bell 'til you ring it, A song's not a song 'til you sing it, Love in your heart wasn't put there to stay, Love isn't love 'til you give it away! - Oscar Hammerstein II

Oscar Hammerstein II, the famous lyricist and playwright, knew the importance of giving love. He wrote some of the most memorable songs in history, including classics like "My Favorite Things" from The Sound of Music. And he believed that love was a powerful force that could change the world.

Love is not just a feeling or an emotion. It is a choice that we make every day. It is the decision to put others before ourselves, to give more than we receive, and to make the world a better place. And when we give love, we receive it in return.

Examples of Giving Love

  • Volunteering at a local soup kitchen or homeless shelter
  • Donating to a charity or nonprofit organization
  • Visiting a nursing home or assisted living facility
  • Mentoring a student or young person
  • Offering a listening ear or a kind word to someone in need

When I was in college, I volunteered at a local food bank. One day, a woman came in with her two young children, and I could see the desperation in her eyes. She was struggling to make ends meet, and she didn't know how she was going to feed her family. But when she left, she had bags of food and a smile on her face. That experience taught me the power of giving love, even in small ways.

Another time, I was walking through the park when I saw an elderly man sitting alone on a bench. I could tell that he was upset, so I sat down next to him and asked him if he was okay. He told me that his wife had passed away recently, and he didn't know how to go on without her. We talked for a long time, and I listened to him as he shared his memories and his pain. When I left, he hugged me and thanked me for being there for him.

Practical Tips for Giving Love

  1. Look for opportunities to volunteer or donate to causes that you care about.
  2. Be present and attentive when you are with someone who needs your love and support.
  3. Practice empathy and try to see the world from others' perspectives.
  4. Say thank you and show appreciation for the people in your life who give you love.
  5. Show kindness and compassion to strangers, even if it's just a smile or a friendly hello.

Conclusion

In conclusion, love is all about giving. Whether we are volunteering at a local shelter or simply offering a kind word to someone in need, every act of love makes a difference. And when we choose to give love freely and without expectation, we create a world that is more compassionate, more understanding, and more beautiful.

  1. Love is a choice that we make every day.
  2. By giving love, we receive it in return.
  3. Every act of love makes a difference.
