Why Poetry is Worth the Effort



An Inspiring Story

About a decade ago, when I was studying literature in college, I came across a poem by Emily Dickinson. It was a simple yet beautiful poem, but I didn't quite understand it at the time. However, something about the rhythm and the words stayed with me. Fast forward a few years, and I found myself going through a tough time. I remembered that Dickinson poem and decided to revisit it. This time, it spoke to me in a way that it never had before. It was like I was seeing the world through fresh eyes, and the poem gave me the words to articulate my thoughts and emotions.

That experience made me realize the power of poetry, and it's something that has stayed with me ever since.

Background of Jane Campion

Jane Campion is an award-winning filmmaker and screenwriter hailing from New Zealand. She's known for her unique storytelling style that often combines poetry and cinema. When asked about her relationship with poetry, she said:

"Poetry has always been important to me. I vastly prefer to be with poets than with politicians."

Campion's work is a testament to the influence that poetry can have on other creative forms.

Why Poetry Needs to be Met

As Campion said, poetry does need to be met to some extent. It's not always an easy task - especially when we're talking about 19th-century poets like Dickinson and Keats, whose works can sometimes feel daunting and unfamiliar. But the effort is worth it.

Here are a few reasons why:

  • It cultivates empathy: Poetry often puts us in someone else's shoes, making us more understanding and compassionate towards the human experience.
  • It improves our language skills: The study and appreciation of poetry helps us become better writers and communicators, as it introduces us to new vocabulary, syntax, and rhetorical devices.
  • It expands our worldview: Poetry can take us to places and times we've never been, allowing us to explore different cultures, histories, and perspectives.

Planting a Garden in Your Head

As I mentioned earlier, revisiting that Dickinson poem was like planting a garden in my head. Poetry has a way of growing and blossoming in unexpected ways, long after we've first encountered it. Here are a few examples of how poetry can continue to enrich our lives:

  • It can become a source of comfort: When we're feeling lost or alone, poetry can offer us a sense of connection and solace.
  • It can inspire us to create: Many great artists, from Bob Dylan to Sylvia Plath, have cited poetry as a major influence on their work.
  • It can help us make sense of the world: Poetry has a way of distilling complex emotions and ideas into simple yet profound statements.

Conclusion

To sum up, here are three key takeaways:

  1. Poetry has the power to cultivate empathy, improve language skills, and expand our worldview.
  2. Metting poetry - even if it takes some effort - is worth it because it can become a source of comfort, inspiration, and understanding.
  3. As Jane Campion said, poetry is an important part of the human experience, and we should make an effort to seek it out and appreciate it.

So the next time you come across a poem that speaks to you, take a moment to savor it and let it grow in your mind. You never know how it might bloom.
