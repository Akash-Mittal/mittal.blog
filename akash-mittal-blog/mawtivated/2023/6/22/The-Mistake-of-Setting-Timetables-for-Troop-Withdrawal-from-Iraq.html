The Mistake of Setting Timetables for Troop Withdrawal from Iraq



An Inspiring Story

When a small village in Iraq was liberated from the grips of ISIS, the people rejoiced. However, it wasn't until American soldiers returned to the village some time later that they truly understood the impact their actions had. The village elder thanked them for their service and then said something that stuck with the soldiers: "You gave us our freedom, but you also gave us hope."

This story highlights the importance and complexity of the situation in Iraq. While American troops have accomplished much in the fight against terrorism, there is still much work to be done to ensure lasting peace and stability in the region.

The Myth of Timetables

Conventional wisdom holds that setting a timetable for getting American troops out of Iraq would be a mistake. This belief is based on the idea that it will signal weakness to our enemies, undermine our efforts to stabilize the region, and ultimately lead to more violence and instability.

However, the reality is that timetables are often necessary to push for progress in negotiations and to ensure accountability on the part of all parties involved. Without deadlines, there is no sense of urgency to make the difficult decisions that peace requires.

In addition, timetables can actually help to build trust and promote long-term stability by showing a commitment to a peaceful resolution. They can also help to reduce the human and financial costs of war by setting clear goals and objectives for all parties involved.

"Without deadlines, there is no sense of urgency to make the difficult decisions that peace requires."

Of course, timetables must be approached with caution and flexibility. They should be realistic and take into account the complex political and social dynamics in the region. They should also be open to negotiation and adjustment as circumstances change.

Examples of Successful Timetables

  • The Dayton Accords, which ended the Bosnian war, included a deadline for withdrawing all foreign troops within one year.
  • The Iran nuclear deal included a timetable for the removal of sanctions in exchange for Iran's compliance with nuclear inspections.
  • The Paris climate agreement included a goal of limiting global warming to below 2 degrees Celsius by 2100, with regular reviews and updates to ensure progress towards this goal.

Practical Tips

  • Approach timetables with caution and flexibility, taking into account the specific circumstances of the conflict.
  • Ensure that all parties involved are committed to the timetable and that it is enforceable.
  • Regularly review and adjust the timetable as needed to ensure progress towards peace.

Conclusion

  1. Timetables can be a useful tool in conflict resolution, but they must be approached with caution and flexibility.
  2. Successful timetables have been used in a variety of conflicts, including Bosnia, Iran, and climate change.
  3. Ultimately, the key to lasting peace is a commitment to diplomacy, negotiation, and empathy for all those affected by conflict.
