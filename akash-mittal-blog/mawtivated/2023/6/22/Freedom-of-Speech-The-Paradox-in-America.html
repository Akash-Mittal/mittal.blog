Freedom of Speech: The Paradox in America



How Restrictions on Free Expression are Impacting Our Society

It was a sunny day in July when Rosa Parks refused to give up her seat on a Montgomery bus. She knew that by standing up for what she believed in - her right to sit where she chose - she was breaking the law. But she also knew that she needed to do something to bring attention to the racial segregation that was rampant in America at the time. By sitting down, she sparked a movement that would ultimately lead to the Civil Rights Act of 1964.

The story of Rosa Parks serves as a reminder of the power of free speech and the impact it can have on society. But as Dr. Benjamin Carson, renowned neurosurgeon and former United States Secretary of Housing and Urban Development, has pointed out, restrictions on free expression are increasingly becoming a problem in America.

"Here's a nation, one of the founding pillars was freedom of speech and freedom of expression. And yet, we have imposed upon people restrictions on what they can say, on what they can think. And the media is the largest proponent of this, crucifying people who say things really quite innocently."

Dr. Carson's observations are not unfounded. In recent years, we have seen numerous cases where individuals have faced consequences for expressing their opinions, even if they were not intended to be hurtful or offensive.

  • In 2018, Harvard University rescinded the acceptance of a student who had posted racist memes in a private Facebook group.
  • In 2019, a high school student in California was prevented from speaking at her graduation because she insisted on mentioning the sexual assault she had experienced on campus.
  • Also in 2019, a teacher in Georgia was fired for posting a racist comment on Facebook, even though she apologized and expressed remorse for her actions.

So what can we do to ensure that freedom of speech remains a cornerstone of our democracy while also acknowledging the harm that hurtful language can cause?

  1. Think before you speak. Consider the impact your words may have on others and whether they align with your values and beliefs.
  2. Engage in civil discourse. Instead of resorting to name-calling or other ad hominem attacks, focus on the issues at hand and seek out common ground where possible.
  3. Be open to learning. If someone tells you that something you said was hurtful or offensive, listen to them and try to understand where they are coming from. Apologize if necessary and make an effort to do better next time.

Summary

In conclusion, while freedom of speech is a fundamental right enshrined in the First Amendment to the U.S. Constitution, it is not without limits. As Americans, we must balance the need for robust debate with the need to protect individuals from harm and discrimination. By thinking carefully about our words and engaging in respectful dialogue, we can work towards a more inclusive society where everyone's voices are heard.

  1. We must remember the power of free speech to effect positive change.
  2. We must acknowledge the harm that hurtful language can cause and make an effort to avoid it.
  3. We must work towards a society where everyone's voices are heard and respected.
