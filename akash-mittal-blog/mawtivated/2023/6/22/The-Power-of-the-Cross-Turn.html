What is the Cross-Turn?

The simplest way to do something cool is the cross-turn. Like in the '80s - Michael Jackson did it. You jump and cross your legs together at the same time, and then spin out of it. That's it.

The cross-turn is a popular dance move that originated in the 80s and has made a comeback in recent years thanks to social media and popular culture. It involves jumping and crossing your legs while spinning out of it, creating a visually stunning effect.

But the cross-turn isn't just a cool dance move. It's a testament to the power of simplicity and innovation. Sometimes, the simplest ideas can have the biggest impact.

Practical Tips for Mastering the Cross-Turn

Here are some tips for mastering the cross-turn:

  1. Start slow and practice the footwork without the spin. Make sure you have the coordination down before adding in the jump and spin.
  2. Focus on your timing and count out the beats in your head. The key to a successful cross-turn is precise timing.
  3. Once you have the basic move down, experiment with adding your own flair and style. The cross-turn is a versatile move that can be adapted to fit any dance style.
