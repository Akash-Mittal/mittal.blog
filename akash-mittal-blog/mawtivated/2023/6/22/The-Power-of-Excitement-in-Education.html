The Power of Excitement in Education



It was the first day of the new school year. Mrs. Johnson, a high school English teacher, stood at the front of the room and looked at her students. They were all slumped in their seats, looking disinterested and bored. She knew she had a big task ahead of her to get them excited about learning.

She decided to start the class with a game. She divided the students into teams and asked them to come up with a story, one sentence at a time. The students were hesitant at first, but soon they were laughing and shouting out ideas. Before she knew it, the entire class was engaged and excited.

This story illustrates the power of excitement in education. When students are excited about learning, they are more engaged, productive, and willing to take risks. In this article, we will explore this concept in detail, and share some tips on how you can use excitement to improve student productivity.

What is Excitement in Education?

Excitement in education is the feeling of enthusiasm, engagement, and energy that students experience when they are learning something new and challenging. Excitement can come from many sources, including:

  • Interesting topics and subjects
  • Challenging assignments and projects
  • Innovative teaching methods
  • Supportive and encouraging teachers

Students who are excited about learning are more likely to be productive, motivated, and engaged in their studies. They are also more likely to succeed academically and professionally.

The Story of Major Owens

Author and former congressman Major Owens is an excellent example of how excitement in education can lead to success. Owens was born and raised in poverty in the segregated South. However, he was always eager to learn, and he credits his early love of reading with inspiring him to succeed.

"I think the excitement of books got me started," Owens said in an interview. "I read the world before I ever saw it."
- Major Owens

Owens went on to earn a bachelor's degree from Morehouse College and a master's degree in library science from Atlanta University. He then became a teacher, working to inspire a love of learning in his students.

Later in life, Owens became a congressman, using his position to advocate for educational reform and increased funding for schools. He believed that education was the key to upward mobility, and that excitement in education was the key to unlocking that potential.

How to Create Excitement in Education

Creating excitement in education requires a combination of factors, including interesting topics, engaging teaching methods, and supportive teachers. Here are some tips on how you can create excitement in your classroom:

  • Start with a hook: Begin your lessons with a question, video, or story that grabs your students' attention and gets them excited about learning.
  • Make it relevant: Show your students how the material you are teaching is relevant to their lives, their future careers, or the world around them.
  • Use innovative teaching methods: Don't be afraid to try new teaching methods, such as project-based learning, games, or group work.
  • Provide feedback and support: Give your students feedback on their work, and encourage them to take risks and try new things.

Conclusion

In conclusion, excitement in education is a powerful force that can lead to increased student productivity, engagement, and success. By creating a classroom environment that is exciting and challenging, teachers can inspire their students to reach their full potential and achieve their goals.

Remember the three keys to creating excitement in education: start with a hook, make it relevant, and use innovative teaching methods. By doing so, you can create an environment that is full of energy, enthusiasm, and learning.
