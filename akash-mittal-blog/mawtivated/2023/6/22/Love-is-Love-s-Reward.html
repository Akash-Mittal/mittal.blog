Love is Love's Reward



Once upon a time, there was a young man named John. He was a hopeless romantic, always seeking for true love. But every time he thought he found it, he was disappointed. He had his heart broken many times. One day, he met a wise old man who listened to his story and said:

"My dear boy, what you seek is not just any love, but unconditional love. Love that doesn't expect anything in return. Love that gives and gives without expecting reward. Love that is patient, kind, and forgiving. Love that is selfless. That is true love. And when you find it, you will receive the greatest reward of all."

John was skeptical, but he decided to take the old man's advice and seek for unconditional love. He learned that true love is not about finding someone who meets all your expectations, but about loving someone even with their flaws and imperfections. He learned that true love is not just a feeling, but a choice to love, no matter what. And he learned that true love is not just for receiving, but for giving. And so, he began his journey to find true love.

Author - John Dryden

John Dryden was a famous English poet, playwright, and critic who lived in the 17th century. He was known for his satirical and heroic poems, as well as his influential critiques of literary works. But he was also known for his views on love and relationships. He believed that true love is not just a passion or a whim, but a commitment and a sacrifice. He believed that love is not self-seeking, but selfless. And he believed that love is not just for temporary pleasure, but for eternal happiness.

Examples of Unconditional Love

  • A mother's love for her child, even when the child is disobedient or rebellious.
  • A dog's love for its owner, even when the owner is neglectful or abusive.
  • A friend's love for a friend, even when the friend is in trouble or in need.
  • A spouse's love for a spouse, even when the spouse is sick or dying.

The Rewards of Unconditional Love

What are the rewards of unconditional love? Here are some of them:

  1. Happiness - When you love someone unconditionally, you feel a sense of joy and fulfillment that cannot be replaced by anything else. You become less concerned about your own needs and more concerned about the needs of others. You find meaning and purpose in life.
  2. Peace - When you love someone unconditionally, you experience a sense of calmness and serenity that cannot be disturbed by external circumstances. You learn to accept things as they are and find contentment in every situation. You become less anxious and less stressed.
  3. Freedom - When you love someone unconditionally, you free yourself from the bondage of selfishness and egoism. You become more generous and more compassionate. You learn to forgive and forget. You become a better person.

Conclusion

In conclusion, love is love's reward. When you love someone unconditionally, you receive the greatest reward of all - the reward of love itself. Love is not just a feeling, but an action. Love is not just for receiving, but for giving. Love is not just for the moment, but for eternity. So, let us love one another unconditionally, and let us receive the greatest reward of all - the reward of love.

Practical Tips

  • Practice kindness, patience, and forgiveness with your loved ones.
  • Be generous and giving without expecting anything in return.
  • Focus on the positive qualities of your loved ones and show appreciation for them.
  • Communicate openly and honestly with your loved ones and listen to their needs.
