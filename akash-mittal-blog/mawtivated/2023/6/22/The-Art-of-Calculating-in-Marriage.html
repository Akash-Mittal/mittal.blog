The Art of Calculating in Marriage



An Inspiring Story

Carol was once a young woman who dreamed of having a family of her own. She was in love with a charming man who promised her the world. They eventually got married, but it didn't take long for Carol to realize that her husband was not the man of her dreams. He spent most of their savings on gambling and was not interested in building a family.

After years of trying to change him, Carol decided to take matters into her own hands. She went back to school, got a degree, and landed a high-paying job. She saved as much money as she could, and when she felt financially capable, she divorced her husband and started a new life.

Today, Carol is happily married to a man who shares her values and ambitions. They have two beautiful children and a comfortable life, all because Carol learned the art of calculating when it comes to marriage.

The Importance of Calculation

Carol's story is a perfect example of how crucial it is to be calculating in marriage. Being "calculating" does not necessarily mean being cold and manipulative. It means making rational decisions based on your priorities, values, and goals. Marriage is not just about romance and emotions, it is a lifelong partnership that requires careful planning and commitment.

Author - Linda McCartney

Linda McCartney was an American photographer, musician, and activist who is best known for her marriage to Paul McCartney of the Beatles. She was a trailblazer for women who wanted to pursue their dreams while balancing family life. Linda was candid about her struggles with balancing her career and family, but she never gave up on either. She left a legacy of unapologetic determination and creativity that inspires people to this day.

Practical Tips

  • Prioritize your goals and values - Before getting married or making any decisions in your marriage, take the time to think about what you truly want to achieve in life. This will help you make informed decisions that are aligned with your goals.
  • Communicate effectively - Communication is key in any relationship. Be honest and clear about your needs and expectations, and listen actively to your partner's point of view.
  • Plan your finances - Money is one of the biggest sources of conflict in marriage. Make sure you and your partner are on the same page when it comes to financial planning. Don't hesitate to seek professional help if you need it.

Conclusion

  1. Being calculating in marriage does not mean being manipulative or selfish. It means making informed decisions based on your priorities and values.
  2. Effective communication and financial planning are key to building a strong and lasting marriage.
  3. Your marriage is a partnership that requires commitment, dedication, and hard work. Don't be afraid to make bold decisions that are aligned with your goals and values.
