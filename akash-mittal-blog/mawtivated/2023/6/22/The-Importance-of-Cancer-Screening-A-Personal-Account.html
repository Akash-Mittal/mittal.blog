The Importance of Cancer Screening: A Personal Account



By Meghan O'Rourke

It was a typical summer day in 2013 when I received a phone call that changed my life. The doctor on the other end of the line informed me that the biopsy I had done on a lump in my breast tested positive for cancer. My heart sank and I felt a wave of terror. How could I have cancer? I was only 42 and had never smoked, and had been healthy all my life.

As the reality sank in, I started to do my research. I read about the importance of cancer screening and how early detection could make all the difference. I wished I had gone for the mammogram sooner. But that's the thing about cancer- you never think it will happen to you until it does.

I underwent a mastectomy followed by chemotherapy and radiation. It was a challenging time in my life, but I learned a lot about the importance of cancer screening and self-care. Here are some practical tips to keep in mind:

  • Get regular checkups, and don't skip the recommended cancer screenings, such as mammograms and colonoscopies.
  • Pay attention to your body and report any abnormal symptoms to your doctor, such as lumps, persistent pain, or bleeding.
  • Make healthy lifestyle choices, such as quitting smoking, eating a balanced diet, and exercising regularly. These habits can reduce your risk of developing cancer and other chronic diseases.

It's been seven years since my diagnosis, and I'm happy to say that I'm cancer-free. But I always remind myself of the experience and use it as a reminder to take care of myself. Too often, we take our health for granted until something goes wrong. Don't wait for a scare to prioritize your well-being.

"Cancer is a word, not a sentence." ~ John Diamond

In conclusion, cancer can be a scary and difficult experience to navigate. But with early detection and proper care, many people are able to survive and thrive. Don't underestimate the importance of cancer screening and self-care. Take care of yourself and your loved ones, and don't wait until it's too late.
