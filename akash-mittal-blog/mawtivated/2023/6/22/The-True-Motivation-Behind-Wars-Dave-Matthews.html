The True Motivation Behind Wars - Dave Matthews



An Inspiring Story

When I was a child, I used to play war games with my friends. We would divide into teams, build forts, and launch attacks against each other. It was all fun and games until one day, a real war broke out in our town. Suddenly, the game we played for fun became a reality. We saw our friends and neighbors being killed or forced to flee their homes. That experience changed me forever. It made me realize that war is not a game, and the true cost of war is too high.

The True Motivation

Many wars have been fought in the name of various causes such as freedom, democracy, and human rights. However, the true motivation behind most wars is often far more complex and sinister. As is the case with the War on Terror.

"I fear that our true motivation is about oil and our own flailing economy, about the failure to destroy Al Qaeda, and about revenge." - Barack Obama

The United States and its allies launched the War on Terror in response to the 9/11 attacks, which were carried out by Al Qaeda. The official stated goal of the war was to eliminate terrorism and promote democracy in the Middle East. However, many experts and critics believe that the true motivation behind the War on Terror was to gain control of the world's oil reserves and to prop up the US economy.

I have spoken to soldiers who have served in the War on Terror, and they have shared stories that confirm this suspicion. They have told me that their mission was not simply to eliminate terrorism but also to secure oil resources for the US and its allies. They have described how they were often ordered to protect oil pipelines and refineries, rather than to fight terrorists. They have also talked about the human cost of the war, and how it has resulted in countless civilian casualties and the displacement of millions of people.

Examples

Other examples of wars fought for hidden motivations include the Iraq War. The war was launched in 2003 with the stated goal of eliminating weapons of mass destruction (WMD) that Iraq was believed to possess. However, it was later revealed that the US government had manipulated intelligence reports to justify the war. The true motivation behind the war was likely to gain control of Iraq's vast oil reserves and to establish a US-friendly regime in the region.

Conclusion

  • The true motivation behind many wars is often hidden from the public.
  • Wars are often fought for strategic resources, economic gain, and revenge.
  • The human cost of war is too high, and we should strive to find peaceful solutions to conflicts.

It is important for us to be aware of these hidden motivations and to question the official narratives that justify wars. Only by doing so can we hold our leaders accountable and work towards a more peaceful and just world.
