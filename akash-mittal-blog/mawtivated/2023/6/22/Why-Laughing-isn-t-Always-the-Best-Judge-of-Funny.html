Why Laughing isn't Always the Best Judge of Funny



An article by Tim Heidecker

Laughter is often seen as the ultimate validation of humor. But as a comedian, I've come to realize that not everything that's funny is going to make you laugh out loud. Let me share with you a personal story that illustrates this point.

The Power of Humor

Before I became a comedian, I worked as a sales clerk in a menswear store. One day, a couple came in looking to buy a suit for their son's wedding. They were a lovely couple, but the husband had a noticeable limp. He would drag his left leg with every step and it was clear that he was in pain.

As I helped the couple pick out a suit, I couldn't help but notice the husband's discomfort. I wanted to lighten the mood and make them feel better, so I improvised a joke. I said, "I hope your son is marrying a woman with a bad right ear. Then he can lean in the right direction while he walks down the aisle!"

The couple looked at me in shock at first, but then they burst out laughing. Tears streamed down their faces as they thanked me for making their day a little brighter. It was a simple joke, but it had the power to bring some joy into their lives.

Not All Humor is Audible

As I reflected on that experience, I realized that not all funny moments provoke laughter. Sometimes humor can be more subtle, more nuanced. It can be a clever turn of phrase or a clever reference that only a select few will understand. It can be a physical gesture or facial expression that conveys more humor than any joke ever could.

And yet, these moments can be just as powerful as belly laughs. They can create a connection between people, a shared understanding of what it means to be human. They can bring people closer together even when they're not making a sound.

The Importance of Humor

Humor is an essential part of the human experience. It helps us to connect with others, to find joy in the midst of hardship, and to appreciate the absurdity of life. It's a valuable tool that can be used to break down barriers and build bridges between people from different backgrounds.

So the next time you come across something that's funny but doesn't make you laugh, don't discount it. Take a moment to appreciate the cleverness or the nuance, and consider how it's adding value to your life in ways you might not have even realized.

My Top 3 Tips for Finding Humor in Unexpected Places

  1. Pay attention to the details. Sometimes the smallest things can be the funniest.
  2. Be willing to take risks. Not every joke will land, but you never know until you try.
  3. Look for the humor in difficult situations. Sometimes laughter really is the best medicine.
As comedian Steve Martin once said, "Comedy is the art of making people laugh without making them puke." And sometimes, the best way to do that is to find humor in unexpected places.

Conclusion

To sum it up, laughing isn't always the best judge of funny. Humor can come in many forms, and not all of them are audible. So take a moment to appreciate the subtle nuances of humor, and you might just find yourself laughing more than ever before.
