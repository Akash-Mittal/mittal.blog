The Power of Immigrants in Creating America's Economic Growth



The Inspiring Story of Eric Ries

Eric Ries is a Silicon Valley entrepreneur and author of the book The Lean Startup, which has quickly become a must-read for entrepreneurs around the world. In his book, Ries talks about the importance of creating a "minimum viable product" (MVP) as the first step in launching a startup. By creating an MVP, entrepreneurs can quickly test their ideas and get feedback from early adopters, which will help them to iterate and improve their product.

But what is perhaps even more inspiring about Ries is his personal story. Ries comes from a family of immigrants who fled Iran during the Islamic Revolution. As a child, Ries saw his parents struggle to build a new life in America, including struggling to find work and adapting to a new culture. But despite these challenges, Ries was raised with a sense of determination and grit that has served him well in his career.

Why Immigrants Are Key to Job Creation

As Ries's story illustrates, immigrants have played a key role in America's economy for generations. But what does the data say? According to a report by the National Foundation for American Policy, immigrants have founded or cofounded over 55% of the unicorn startups in America (startups with a valuation of over $1 billion). This is a staggering number when you consider that immigrants represent just 14% of the population.

But what about job creation? Substantial research shows that immigrants play a key role in American job creation. According to a report by the National Bureau of Economic Research, immigrant-owned businesses are more likely to hire other immigrants, and these businesses generate billions of dollars in economic activity each year. In fact, immigrant-owned businesses generate over $775 billion in revenue each year and employ over 4.7 million Americans.

Examples of Immigrant Success Stories

  • Elon Musk: Tesla and SpaceX founder Elon Musk was born in South Africa and came to America as a young man. Today, he is one of the most successful and well-known entrepreneurs of our time.
  • Arianna Huffington: The founder and former editor-in-chief of the Huffington Post, Arianna Huffington was born in Greece and came to America in 1980. Today, she is a successful author, entrepreneur, and public speaker.
  • Garrett Gee: The founder of the mobile app Scan, Garrett Gee was born in California to a family of immigrants from China. Scan was eventually acquired by Snapchat for $54 million.

The Immigration Solution

The United States is locked in a new arms race for that most precious resource - the future entrepreneurs upon whom economic growth depends. In order to continue to grow and innovate, America needs to attract and retain the best and brightest from around the world. But what can be done to make this happen?

First, we need to reform our immigration policies to make it easier for entrepreneurs and innovators to come to America. This means creating a new visa category for startup founders and easing the visa process for qualified individuals in STEM fields.

Second, we need to invest in education and training programs that will help American workers adapt to new industries and technologies. By doing this, we can ensure that our workforce is prepared for the jobs of the future.

Finally, we need to embrace diversity and give immigrants the opportunity to contribute their unique perspectives and experiences to our society. By doing this, we can not only create economic growth, but also build a stronger, more vibrant America.

Conclusion: How You Can Make A Difference

As an individual, there are several things you can do to help promote immigration as a solution for economic growth:

  1. Support political candidates who are in favor of immigration reform.
  2. Hire immigrants and support immigrant-owned businesses in your community.
  3. Speak out against xenophobia and racism, and support diversity and inclusion in all aspects of life.
