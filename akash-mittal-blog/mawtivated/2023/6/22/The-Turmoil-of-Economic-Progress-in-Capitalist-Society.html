The Turmoil of Economic Progress in Capitalist Society



Once upon a time in a small village, there lived a farmer named John. He owned a small piece of land and a few cattle. However, he dreamt of giving his family a better life. One day, he decided to take a loan and start a poultry farm. After months of hard work, his business prospered. He hired a few people to help him and even expanded his farm. Despite facing initial difficulties, John was satisfied with the progress he had made.

This story beautifully illustrates the concept of economic progress. Economic progress is the hallmark of any capitalist society. It is the manifestation of a society's ability to create wealth and improve its standard of living. However, as Joseph A. Schumpeter, the famous Austrian economist, proposed in his theory of 'creative destruction,' economic progress can only be achieved when old ideas, technologies, and businesses are destroyed to make way for new and better ones. This constant process of innovation leads to the creation of new industries, products, and ways of doing things.

However, this creative destruction is not without its pitfalls. As new industries are created, old industries decline, leading to job losses and financial difficulties for those employed in the dying industries. This leads to turmoil and unrest in society. Furthermore, these changes are not limited to individual companies but can also affect entire industries and even the economy as a whole.

In the early 2000s, the rise of e-commerce giant Amazon and online streaming service Netflix revolutionized the retail and entertainment industries. This led to the decline of many brick-and-mortar stores and traditional cable TV services, causing thousands of job losses and financial difficulties for employees and businesses in these sectors.

However, economic progress cannot be halted, nor should it be. Instead, measures can be taken to ensure a smooth transition during this period of turmoil. Governments can provide training and financial support for workers to acquire new skills and enter other industries. Businesses can invest in research and development to create new products and services and remain competitive. Adaptability is critical during periods of economic progress.

Conclusion

  • Economic progress is an inevitable outcome of capitalist society.
  • The process of creative destruction inevitably leads to turmoil and unrest.
  • However, society can take measures to ensure a smooth transition during this period of change.

As John's poultry farm demonstrated, taking a risk and embracing change can lead to benefits and progress. To thrive in today's society, individuals and businesses must adapt and evolve, accepting that turbulence is an inevitable part of achieving economic progress.
