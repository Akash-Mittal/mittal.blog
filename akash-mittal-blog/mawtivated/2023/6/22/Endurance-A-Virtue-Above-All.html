Endurance: A Virtue Above All



It was a cold winter morning when John Ruskin, an accomplished artist, writer and philosopher, was walking to his regular painting class. As he walked through the streets, he saw a little girl shivering in the cold, trying her best to sell matches. Moved by her plight, Ruskin approached her and bought all the matches she was carrying, handing her back the money. As he walked away, he realized the importance of endurance and patience in life. Since then, he always treasured these virtues above all.

The Value of Endurance

Endurance is a rare and valuable virtue in today's fast-paced world. It means the ability to persist through difficulties and hardships, to stick to one's commitments, and to stay focused on long-term goals even when the going gets tough.

Endurance is not a simple trait that you can develop overnight; it requires practice and perseverance. It is a skill that can be learned through experience. Patience, perseverance, and resilience are crucial components of endurance. Enduring individuals stay committed to their goals, regardless of the negative events or setbacks they may face, and maintain a positive outlook towards the future.

Endurance vs. Strength, and Patience vs. Beauty

Strength and beauty have always been an essential part of human society. They have been glorified in tales of epic heroes and heroines. But according to Ruskin, endurance and patience should be valued more than strength and beauty.

Physical strength can only take a person so far. It may help them overcome some challenges, but when it comes to life's fragility, it fails. A compromising situation hits you, all the strength won't help unless you have the patience to look for a solution calmly and find the oasis amid the chaos.

Similarly, beauty is a fleeting attribute. What one considers beautiful, another may disdain. It fades and changes with time. But with endurance, one can build something permanent to look forward to, which empowers them.

Examples of Endurance

Throughout history and culture, endurance has been a highly respected trait. Here are a few examples of people demonstrating endurance:

  • Malala Yousafzai - Malala, a Pakistani student, is a worldwide icon of endurance. She faced adversity since childhood when she worked hard to gain an education in a society where girls were banned from attending school. Even after being shot by terrorists, she returned to continue her education and work towards women's empowerment.
  • Nelson Mandela - Nelson Mandela spent 27 years in jail for his fight against apartheid in South Africa. Despite the harsh conditions of confinement, he remained strong, won the support of many people globally, and brought about a change of heart among South Africans.
  • JK Rowling - JK Rowling, the author of the Harry Potter series, went through a lot of struggles before she became a best-selling author. She faced rejection after rejection from publishers but persisted and persevered with her writing, finally achieving the success that she deserved.

The Role of Endurance in Everyday Life

Endurance is not limited to exceptional individuals, nor is it reserved for extraordinary circumstances alone. In our everyday lives, we all face various challenges, from the smallest to the most significant. It's up to us to choose how we want to react to them. Endurance can help us prevail through tough times, make better decisions, and enjoy a more fulfilling life.

The Conclusion

In conclusion, endurance is a noble quality that deserves recognition and attention. It's a skill that anyone can develop, with willpower, persistence, and perseverance. In our everyday lives, with the many challenges we face, being patient and enduring will allow us to achieve our goals and reach our fullest potential. So, don't underestimate the virtue of endurance in your life as it can be your most valuable ally.

The 3 Key Points of Endurance:

  1. Endurance means the ability to persist through difficulties and hardships, to stick to one's commitments and to stay focused on long-term goals even when the going gets tough.
  2. Endurance is not a simple trait that you can develop overnight; it requires practice and perseverance.
  3. Endurance is a noble quality that deserves recognition and attention, and it can help us prevail through tough times, make better decisions, and enjoy a more fulfilling life.
