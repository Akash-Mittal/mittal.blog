The Fear of Beauty in Scotland: A Personal Perspective



Author - Anne Boyd

When I was a young girl, my parents took me on a trip to Scotland. As we drove through the stunning landscapes, I was in awe of the beauty that surrounded me. However, I couldn't help but feel a sense of dread. I was afraid that if I lived in a place as beautiful as Scotland, I would never be content.

This fear stayed with me throughout my life, and as I grew older, I realized that it was not just me. Many people feel overwhelmed by beauty, and they worry that they will never be able to appreciate it fully without constantly longing for more.

The Fear of Beauty

The fear of beauty is not a new concept. It has been explored by philosophers and artists for centuries. The Greek myth of Narcissus, who fell in love with his own reflection in a pool of water, is a perfect example of the danger of beauty. Narcissus was so captivated by his own beauty that he lost touch with the real world and eventually died.

Similarly, many people fear that they will become consumed by the beauty around them and lose touch with reality. They worry that they will never be able to find happiness because they will always be searching for something more beautiful.

As someone who has struggled with the fear of beauty, I have found that it helps to focus on the present moment. When I am surrounded by beautiful scenery, I try to savor the moment and appreciate it for what it is. I also remind myself that beauty comes in many forms, and that I don't need to constantly seek out the most dramatic landscapes to find joy.

Another approach that has helped me is to view beauty as a source of inspiration. Instead of feeling overwhelmed by it, I try to use it as a motivation to create something of my own. For example, if I see a beautiful sunset, I might write a poem or paint a picture inspired by it.

Conclusion

  1. Focus on the present moment.
  2. View beauty as a source of inspiration.
  3. Remember that beauty comes in many forms.
