The Power of Experience: Learning from Roger Ascham



An Inspiring Story

Have you ever heard of Roger Ascham? He was an English scholar and teacher during the 16th century. Ascham was best known for his innovative teaching methods and his emphasis on practical learning.

One of Ascham's most famous students was a young Princess Elizabeth, later known as Queen Elizabeth I. According to legend, Ascham found Elizabeth sitting under a tree, reading a book as she enjoyed the outdoors.

"Madam, there is no royal road to learning," Ascham reportedly told her. "Do not allow yourself to be led by the idea that reading is enough. Experience is the only sure guide in life."

Elizabeth took Ascham's words to heart and went on to become one of the most educated and successful monarchs in history.

The Importance of Experience

Ascham's advice to Elizabeth holds true today. Experience is a powerful teacher, and it can lead us down paths we never thought possible.

By experiencing things for ourselves, we gain a deeper understanding of the world around us. We also learn valuable lessons that we wouldn't have otherwise. You can only read so much about a subject before you have to get your hands dirty and dive in.

Most successful people will tell you that they didn't get to where they are by following a straight and narrow path. They made mistakes, took risks, and learned from their experiences. This is why many employers value experience over formal education. They know that someone who has been in the trenches has a unique perspective and the skills to get the job done.

Examples of Learning from Experience

Let's look at some examples of how experience can be a powerful tool for learning:

  1. Learning a new language: You can read all the textbooks and watch all the videos you want, but the only way to truly learn a new language is by speaking it. Immersing yourself in a new culture and interacting with native speakers is the best way to become fluent.
  2. Starting a business: Entrepreneurship is all about taking risks and learning from your failures. You may have a great business plan, but until you actually launch your product or service, you won't know what works and what doesn't.
  3. Becoming a parent: Even with all the parenting books and classes available, nothing can fully prepare you for being a parent. The only way to truly learn is by experiencing it firsthand.

The Three Keys to Learning from Experience

  1. Stay open-minded: When we encounter new experiences, it's easy to cling to preconceived notions and assumptions. However, by staying open-minded and curious, we can learn more and discover new things.
  2. Reflect on your experiences: After you've gone through an experience, take some time to reflect on what you learned. What did you do well? What could you have done differently?
  3. Take action: Experience is only valuable if we use it to inform our future decisions. Don't just file away your experiences as anecdotes. Instead, use them to make better choices in the future.
