The Power of Sweetness: Lessons from My Mom



An Inspiring Story

When I was a little girl, my mother used to tell me a tale about a magical garden where kindness and love bloomed all year round. As I grew older, I realized that the garden was not a place, but a state of being – a way of living that my mother embodied every day.

My mom is from Nebraska, the heartland of America, where people are known for their hardworking, humble, and friendly nature. She grew up on a farm and learned early on how to be resourceful, compassionate, and optimistic. Despite facing many challenges in her life, my mom always remained sweet – and it has made all the difference.

Lessons from Gabrielle Union

One of my favorite actresses, Gabrielle Union, has a similar story. In her memoir, "We're Going to Need More Wine", she writes about her mother's influence on her life:

"My mother taught me to be sweet, but not weak; strong, but not rude; bold, but not a bully; humble, but not timid; proud, but not arrogant; have humor, but without folly."

These words resonate with me, as they capture the essence of what it means to be sweet – not just as a trait, but as a way of navigating the world.

The Power of Sweetness

Sweetness is often dismissed as a frivolous or weak quality, but in reality, it is a powerful force that can transform our lives and our relationships. Here are some examples:

  • Sweetness can diffuse conflict and bring people together.
  • Sweetness can inspire others and make them feel valued.
  • Sweetness can create a positive atmosphere and uplift everyone around you.
  • Sweetness can help you connect with your inner sense of calm and contentment.

How to Cultivate Sweetness

If you want to cultivate more sweetness in your own life, here are some practical tips:

  1. Practice gratitude: Focus on what you have rather than what you lack.
  2. Show empathy: Put yourself in other people's shoes and try to understand their perspective.
  3. Be sincere: Speak from your heart and let your actions match your words.
  4. Savor the moment: Enjoy the simple pleasures of life and appreciate the beauty around you.
  5. Be kind to yourself: Treat yourself with the same loving care that you would give to a dear friend.

Conclusion

In conclusion, sweetness is a quality that can enrich our lives in many ways. By following the examples of my mom and Gabrielle Union, we can learn how to embody sweetness and create a more harmonious and joyful world.

  1. Sweetness is not weakness, but a source of strength.
  2. Sweetness is contagious, and can spread love and compassion.
  3. Sweetness is a choice, and we can all cultivate it in our own lives.
