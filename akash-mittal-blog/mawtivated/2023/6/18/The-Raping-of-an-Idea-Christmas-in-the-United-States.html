The Raping of an Idea: Christmas in the United States



It was a cold winter night, and Richard Bach was walking down the streets of his hometown. The snow was gently falling, and the lights of the Christmas decorations illuminated the street. For Bach, it was a beautiful sight, but something felt wrong. He realized that what he was experiencing was not the true essence of Christmas.

Today, Christmas in the United States has become the rape of an idea. The holiday has transformed from a simple celebration of joy and love to an excessive consumerist frenzy. It has lost its true essence, and instead, it has become a symbol of our endless desire for material things.

The Origins of Christmas

Christmas is a holiday that originated from the Christian celebration of the birth of Jesus Christ. It was a time of religious reflection and giving, and the emphasis was on the spiritual rather than the material. Today, however, Christmas has become a symbol of capitalism and consumerism. People are encouraged to spend more and more money, buying gifts and indulging in excessive feasts. What happened to the simple rejoicing and reflection of the true essence of Christmas?

The Consumerist Frenzy

Christmas has transformed into a commercialized holiday that requires people to spend money in order to show their love and appreciation for each other. The focus is no longer on the act of giving, but instead on the value of the gift. The larger and more expensive the gift, the more one is perceived to care. It's a trend that has driven many people into debt and leaves them feeling disillusioned with the holiday.

The holiday season has also become a time of over-indulgence. People indulge in excessive feasting and drinking, which often leads to health problems. In the United States, the holiday season is known for its high rates of heart disease and obesity, which shows the harsh reality of the consumerist frenzy that is Christmas.

Personal Reflections and Practical Tips

As much as we may criticize the commercialization of Christmas, we must also accept that it is possible to enjoy the holiday while maintaining our sense of ethics. Here are some personal reflections and tips to make the most out of the holiday season:

  • Focus on giving: Rather than focusing on the value of the gift, focus on the act of giving. A thoughtful, personalized gift will always bring more joy than an expensive one.
  • Limit spending: Set a budget and stick to it. Don't let the hype of the holiday season dictate your spending habits.
  • Practice self-care: Take time to take care of yourself amidst the holiday frenzy. Prioritize your health and well-being.

Conclusion

  1. Christmas in the United States has become a symbol of capitalist excess and consumerism.
  2. It is possible to enjoy the holiday while maintaining our sense of ethics.
  3. Focusing on the true essence of the holiday – giving, reflection and joy – can help make the most out of the holiday season.
