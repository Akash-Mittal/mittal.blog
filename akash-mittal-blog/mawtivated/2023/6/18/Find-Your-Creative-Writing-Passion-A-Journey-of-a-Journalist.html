Find Your Creative Writing Passion: A Journey of a Journalist



Tony was a journalist for over 10 years. He enjoyed his job and loved meeting new people and uncovering stories. However, one day he realized that he wanted to do more with his writing. He was inspired by the stories he uncovered and wanted to create his own works of fiction. The only problem was he had never had any creative writing experience, except for one class he took as a sophomore in college.

The Journey Begins

Like Tony, many journalists and non-writers often have a desire to write creatively but do not know how to begin. Dave Eggers, the author of "A Heartbreaking Work of Staggering Genius," was in a similar position when he decided to start writing. He had worked as a journalist for many years and one day decided he had a story to tell.

"After working as a journalist for much of my adult life, both as a reporter and an editor, I realized that what I really wanted to do was write fiction. Journalism taught me the nuts and bolts of storytelling, how to get to the point, how to communicate quickly and efficiently. And those skills are incredibly important in starting to write fiction."

Like Eggers, Tony began his creative writing journey by building on the writing skills he learned as a journalist. He used his knowledge of storytelling and efficient communication to start writing his first short story.

However, finding the time and motivation to write creatively can be challenging. With deadlines and daily schedules, carving out time for creative writing can seem impossible. Tony found that setting aside specific times to write and establishing a routine helped him stay on track. He also suggests finding a writing community or group for support and motivation.

Discover Your Creative Writing Passion

When beginning to write creatively, it is important to explore different genres and forms of writing to find what inspires you. Tony took different online courses and workshops in poetry, short stories, and even screenplays, which helped him develop his style and find what he enjoyed writing the most.

  • Tip: Do not limit yourself to just one genre. Explore different forms of writing to find your own unique style and voice.

Further, finding inspiration for creative writing can come from everyday experiences and emotions. Tony suggests keeping a journal to jot down ideas and inspiration that may come unexpectedly.

  • Tip: Keep a journal or notebook nearby to catch any sparks of inspiration that come your way. These can inspire future creative writing pieces.

The Importance of Editing and Revising

One of the biggest challenges of creative writing is the revision process. However, this stage is crucial for refining the work and making it the best it can be. Tony emphasizes the importance of taking a break from the work to come back to it with fresh eyes. He also suggests finding trusted beta readers or joining writing groups for feedback and critiques.

  • Tip: Take breaks between drafting and revising. This will give you fresh eyes and encourage more objective feedback on your work.
  • Tip: Join writing groups to get feedback on your work. Use critiques to identify strengths and weaknesses in your writing.

Conclusion

Starting your creative writing journey may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and mindset, it can be a fulfilling experience. Dave Eggers and Tony were both journalists without any creative writing experience, yet they were able to find their voice and create engaging works of fiction.

  • Conclusion Point 1: Building on the writing skills you already have is a great starting point for creative writing.
  • Conclusion Point 2: Explore different genres and forms of writing to find what you are passionate about.
  • Conclusion Point 3: Editing and revising will make your writing the best it can be. Use trusted beta readers and writing groups to get feedback on your work.
