The Power of Humor as a Survival Tool



In times of adversity, people often turn to different coping mechanisms to help them get through tough situations. One of the most effective and overlooked tools in this regard is the power of humor. As the famous quote goes, "humor is one of the best ingredients of survival." In this article, we will explore the different ways humor can help people cope with difficult times and share practical tips on how to incorporate humor into your own life.

An Inspiring Story

Before we delve into the benefits of humor, let's start with an inspiring story that showcases the true power of humor. The story revolves around a man named Nathan, who lost his job, his wife, and his home all in a matter of weeks. Devastated and on the brink of a breakdown, Nathan decided to take a trip to visit his brother in another state to clear his head. During his visit, Nathan was introduced to a group of people who all shared his love for stand-up comedy. He started attending their comedy nights and even tried his hand at a few open mics. To Nathan's surprise, he was actually quite funny. Not only did he find laughter in his own jokes, but he also started making new friends, which helped him through his difficult time. Eventually, Nathan landed a job and was able to get back on his feet. He credited his newfound love for comedy as the key factor in his survival.

Aung San Suu Kyi's Story

Aung San Suu Kyi, the Burmese politician and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, is another example of how humor can be used as a survival tool. Suu Kyi was placed under house arrest by the Burmese government from 1989-1995 and then again from 2000-2010. During this time, she used humor as a way to connect with her guards and fellow prisoners, often joking and making light of the situation. In her book, "Freedom From Fear," Suu Kyi writes, "Laughter was our medicine, and every day we had a ration of humor that kept us in good spirits." Her ability to find humor in the darkest of situations helped her to keep a positive outlook and stay mentally resilient.

Benefits of Humor

  • Stress Reduction: Humor is an excellent stress reliever. Laughter releases endorphins, the body's natural feel-good chemicals, which can help reduce anxiety and make you feel relaxed.
  • Better Resilience: Humor can help you bounce back faster from difficult situations. When you find the humor in a difficult situation, it gives you a different perspective and makes it easier to move forward.
  • Improved Relationships: Humor can also strengthen relationships by creating a shared sense of joy and camaraderie between people. Sharing a laugh with someone can create a bond and help you connect with them on a deeper level.

Practical Tips

Now that we know the benefits of humor, let's look at some practical tips on how to incorporate humor into your own life to help you get through tough times.

  1. Watch a Comedy: This is an easy way to inject some humor into your life. Whether it's a stand-up special or a funny movie, laughing along with others can help you feel better.
  2. Find the Funny: Try to find the humor in everyday situations. For example, if you're stuck in traffic, instead of getting angry, try making up funny stories about the other drivers.
  3. Create Something: If you have a creative side, try channeling it into something funny. You could make a funny video, write a comedic blog post, or even draw some funny cartoons.

Conclusion

In summary, humor is one of the most effective tools for coping with difficult situations. Whether it's watching a comedy, finding the funny in everyday situations, or creating something funny, there are many ways to incorporate humor into your life. With stress reduction, better resilience, and improved relationships being just some of the benefits, it's clear that humor should be a part of everyone's survival toolkit.
