Truth Surpasses the Imitation and Copy



There was a man who lived in a small village in India. He was known for his unique pottery skills and was admired by everyone in the village. One day, a potter from a nearby town visited the man and offered him a deal. The deal was to copy the man's pottery designs and sell them in the market for profit.

The man thought about the offer for a while and finally decided to reject it. He believed that his pottery was unique and could not be imitated. The potter from the nearby town was disappointed but left the village.

Years passed by, and the man continued to make his unique pottery designs. His pottery became famous not just in the village but in nearby towns and cities as well. He was even invited to exhibit his pottery at an international event.

The potter from the nearby town, on the other hand, copied the man's designs and sold them in the market. However, he soon realized that his pottery was not as popular as the man's. His pottery lacked the uniqueness and originality that the man's pottery had. He regretted his decision to copy the man's designs and wished that he had created something of his own.

In everything truth surpasses the imitation and copy.

This story beautifully illustrates the power of originality. The man in the story knew the value of his unique pottery designs and did not compromise on it. His originality not only earned him recognition but also gave him the satisfaction that he had created something unique and valuable.

Author - Marcus Tullius Cicero

Marcus Tullius Cicero was a Roman philosopher, statesman, and orator who lived in the first century BC. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest orators in the history of the Roman Republic. Cicero was also a staunch believer in the power of truth and originality. He believed that one should always strive to create something original and unique rather than copying others.

Examples of Originality

  • Apple Inc.: Apple Inc. is known for its innovative and original products such as the iPhone and the Mac. Apple products have a unique design and features that set them apart from their competitors.
  • J.K. Rowling: J.K. Rowling is the author of the Harry Potter book series, which is one of the most successful book series in the world. Rowling's originality and creativity in creating the Harry Potter universe have captured the imaginations of millions of people around the world.
  • Walt Disney: Walt Disney was known for his creativity and originality in creating cartoons and animated films. His most famous creation, Mickey Mouse, is still loved by millions of people around the world.

The Power of Originality

Originality is the key to success in today's world. In a world full of copycats, originality is what sets you apart and gives you a competitive edge. People value originality and uniqueness, and they are willing to pay a premium for it. Here are three reasons why originality is important:

  1. Originality sets you apart from the competition. In today's crowded marketplace, it's not enough to just be good. You need to be unique and original to capture people's attention.
  2. Originality adds value to your work. When you create something original, you are creating something valuable. People are willing to pay for things that are valuable.
  3. Originality gives you satisfaction. There is no greater satisfaction than creating something original and unique. It's a feeling that cannot be matched by anything else.
