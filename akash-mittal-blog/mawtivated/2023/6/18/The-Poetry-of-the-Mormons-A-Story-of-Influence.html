The Poetry of the Mormons: A Story of Influence



How Joseph Smith and the Mormons Inspired Marguerite Young

An Inspiring Story

It all started with a visit to Salt Lake City. Marguerite Young, a young writer from Indiana, was struck by the beauty of the city and the kindness of its people. She started exploring the history of the Mormons and was fascinated by the story of Joseph Smith and his followers.

Young was particularly drawn to the poetry of the Mormons, which she found to be both inspiring and unconventional. She studied their work and started experimenting with her own style, blending the traditional with the modern, the spiritual with the secular.

The Influence of Joseph Smith and the Mormons

Young later remarked that her writing was as much influenced by Joseph Smith and the Mormons as by any modern writer, including Eliot. She was drawn to their use of metaphor and symbolism, as well as their emphasis on individual experience and spiritual quest.

One of Young's most famous works, Angel in the Forest, is a prime example of her Mormon-inspired poetry. The poem tells the story of a woman who has a vision of an angel in a forest and is transformed by the encounter. The imagery and language of the poem are deeply influenced by the Mormon faith, yet Young takes the story in her own direction, exploring the themes of love, beauty, and transcendence.

"I'm as much influenced by Joseph Smith and the Mormons as I am, more so, than by Eliot. Actually, I'm much more influenced by the poetry of the Mormons."

Young's work has been praised for its originality and depth, and many scholars have recognized the influence of the Mormons on her poetry. Today, her legacy lives on through her writing, as well as through the young writers who are inspired by her example.

Practical Tips for Writing Inspired Poetry

  • Explore different spiritual traditions and find the ones that speak to you.
  • Study the work of poets who have been influenced by these traditions and analyze what makes their writing effective.
  • Experiment with language and imagery, blending the traditional with the modern, the spiritual with the secular.
  • Find your own voice and style, and don't be afraid to take your work in new directions.
  • Share your writing with others and be open to feedback and critique.

Conclusion

  1. Marguerite Young was deeply influenced by Joseph Smith and the Mormons, and her poetry reflects this influence.
  2. The Mormon faith inspired Young's use of metaphor, symbolism, and individual experience, as well as her exploration of love, beauty, and transcendence.
  3. Young's example offers practical tips for writers who seek to develop their own style and voice, and who want to explore the spiritual dimensions of language.
