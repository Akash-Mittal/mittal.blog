Introduction

As society evolves, so does the way we gather and process information. In today's highly developed society, we are constantly bombarded with information from various sources. The question then arises, what methodological representation should we apply to this vast amount of knowledge to make sense of it all?

"If we wish to discuss knowledge in the most highly developed contemporary society, we must answer the preliminary question of what methodological representation to apply to that society." - Jean-Francois Lyotard

In this article, we will explore the different approaches to understanding information in contemporary society, and how we can use it to our advantage.

Background of Jean-Francois Lyotard

Jean-Francois Lyotard was a French philosopher who played a significant role in shaping postmodernist thought. His works focused on the changes in society brought about by technology, and the impact it had on our perceptions of knowledge. He believed that knowledge should not be restricted to the traditional institutional structures, but rather should be seen as a fluid and dynamic concept.

Examples of Methodological Representation

  • Scientific Method: This approach relies on empirical evidence and experimentation to establish knowledge. It is often used in scientific fields such as physics, chemistry, and biology.
  • Social Science Research: This approach involves the study of human behavior and social interactions, and relies on various research methods such as surveys, interviews, and case studies.
  • Critical Theory: This approach is used to analyze and critique society and culture, and is often used in fields such as literature and media studies.
  • Postmodernism: This approach challenges the idea of a universal knowledge and instead sees knowledge as subjective and context-dependent.

Each approach has its strengths and weaknesses, and can be used depending on the context and purpose of the study.
