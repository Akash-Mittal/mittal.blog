The Power of Struggle in Education



How hardship can pave the way to success

When we think of education, we often picture a classroom filled with students and a teacher, or a library stacked with books. However, one of the most valuable education experiences is often overlooked - struggling to make a living. The lessons we learn through hardship can be some of the most powerful and long-lasting, shaping our character and giving us strength to overcome challenges. Author Wendell Phillips known for his advocacy work for the abolition of slavery in the United States, is a prime example of how struggle can lead to greater success.

The Story of Wendell Phillips

"What is defeat? Nothing but education; nothing but the first step to something better."

Wendell Phillips was born into a wealthy family and had a comfortable upbringing. However, after graduating Harvard, he struggled to find his path in life and was hit hard by the financial crisis of 1837. With little job prospects, Phillips turned to law and found himself advocating for the oppressed, especially those suffering under the cruelty of slavery. By dedicating himself to the cause of abolitionism, Phillips became one of the most influential speakers and change-makers of his time, eventually playing a major role in the abolition of slavery in the United States. Through his struggles, Phillips found his calling and his passion, and made a lasting impact on history.

The Power of Struggle

Wendell Phillips' story is a testament to the power of struggle, and how it can lead to greater growth and success. Here are a few reasons why:

  • Strengthens resilience and perseverance: Going through difficult times builds resilience and strengthens our ability to face adversity. We learn to keep pushing forward even when things get tough.
  • Develops problem-solving skills: Struggling forces us to find solutions to problems, which can improve our ability to think critically and creatively.
  • Teaches valuable life skills: From learning how to budget and live frugally to developing resourcefulness, the lessons we learn through hardship can be invaluable in life and in career.

Examples of Struggle Leading to Success

There are countless examples of people who have achieved great success after going through difficult times. Here are a few:

  1. Oprah Winfrey: Oprah grew up in poverty and suffered abuse, but turned to education as a way out. Today she is a media mogul, philanthropist, and one of the most powerful women in the world.
  2. Steve Jobs: Jobs was fired from the company he co-founded, but went on to create Pixar and revolutionize the technology industry with Apple.
  3. Malala Yousafzai: Malala faced threats and violence because of her advocacy for girls' education in Pakistan, but has continued to be a voice for change and a Nobel Peace Prize winner.

Conclusion

While it's true that the classroom and traditional forms of education have value, we should not underestimate the power of struggle. Going through difficult times can teach us invaluable lessons, strengthen our character, and lead to long-term success. So when the road gets tough, remember that it can be the first step to something better.

Here are the key takeaways:

  1. Struggling can lead to resilience, problem-solving skills, and valuable life skills.
  2. People who have gone through hardship have achieved success and made a lasting impact on the world.
  3. The next time you face adversity, remember that it can be an opportunity for growth and learning.
