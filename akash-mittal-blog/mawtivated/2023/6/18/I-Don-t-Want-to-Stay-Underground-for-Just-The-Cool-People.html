The Power of Inclusivity



Why We Shouldn't Limit Ourselves to Coolness or Elitism

Author: Buzz Osborne

There was a time in my life where I only wanted to associate with like-minded individuals, those who were as passionate about music and underground culture as I was. I found comfort in being part of an exclusive group, where only a select few were privy to our shared interests and experiences. But as I progressed through my career as a musician and interacted with more people from varying backgrounds, I realized the power of inclusivity.

One of the most inspiring stories that comes to mind is that of my friend, a fellow musician who was involved in a local organization that aimed to bring music education to underprivileged communities. He had always been passionate about music, having grown up in a musically inclined family, and wanted to share that passion with others. Through this organization, he not only imparted his knowledge to children who might not have had access to musical education otherwise, but he also learned from them. He opened himself up to different perspectives and saw the value in diversity.

This experience made me reflect on my own actions and the limited mindset I had previously held. By only wanting to be associated with "cool" people or those who shared my same interests, I was closing myself off from learning and growing. I was missing out on potential friendships and missed opportunities to connect with people from different backgrounds.

The Danger of Elitism

The idea of exclusivity or elitism may seem appealing at first, but it is ultimately harmful. When we only associate with individuals who fit a specific mold or share the same beliefs as us, we create an echo chamber where only one perspective is heard. This can lead to a narrow-minded mentality and limit our growth as individuals. In addition, by only accepting certain individuals into our circles, we perpetuate harmful stereotypes and reinforce divisions within our communities.

The Power of Inclusivity

On the other hand, when we embrace inclusivity, we create a space where people from all walks of life are welcome. We open ourselves up to different perspectives, experiences, and knowledge. This leads to a richer, more diverse community and fosters growth and understanding. Through inclusivity, we can break down barriers and create meaningful connections with people we might not have otherwise encountered.

Personal anecdotes or case studies can illustrate this point. Many of us have experienced moments where we were on the outside looking in, whether it be at a social gathering, a professional setting, or even within our own families. These moments can be isolating and discouraging, but they can also be opportunities for growth. By embracing inclusivity and seeking out new connections, we can expand our horizons and gain new perspectives.

Practical Tips for Embracing Inclusivity

  • Challenge your assumptions: Be aware of any preconceived notions you may have about certain groups of people and challenge them. Try to approach every interaction with an open mind.
  • Seek out new experiences: Attend events, workshops, or classes that are outside of your comfort zone. This can expose you to new people and perspectives.
  • Listen actively: When engaging with others, make a conscious effort to listen actively and understand their perspective.
"Inclusivity is not just a buzzword, it is a mindset. It requires a willingness to step outside of our comfort zones and embrace new experiences and perspectives." - Buzz Osborne
