Work as a Prayer: A Daily Dedication to our Creator



Author - Joe Murray

Imagine waking up at 5 AM every morning, before the sun rises and the world is still asleep. The first thing you do is light a candle in front of your altar and kneel down to pray. You dedicate your day to your Creator, asking for guidance and strength in everything you do. With a clear mind and a heart full of gratitude, you stand up and start your work, knowing that every task, big or small, is a prayer in itself.

This is the routine of Ravi, a small business owner in India who runs a spice shop in a bustling market. For him, work is not just a means to an end, but a way to connect with the divine. He believes that every action we take, every word we speak, and every thought we have can either create positive or negative energy in the world. By infusing his work with intention and purpose, he hopes to contribute to the greater good and make a meaningful difference in the lives of his customers.

Why Work as a Prayer?

"Work is a prayer, and I start off every morning dedicating it to our Creator."

This statement by Ravi holds a deeper truth that we often overlook in our daily lives. We tend to see work as a necessary chore, something we have to do to earn a living and pay the bills. We might even resent it, complain about it, or avoid it altogether. However, this attitude can create a negative cycle of stress, anxiety, and disconnection from our values and purpose.

On the other hand, when we view work as a prayer, we shift our perspective from a transactional mindset to a transformational mindset. We see work not as a burden, but as a blessing. We recognize that every moment we spend working is an opportunity to express our creativity, share our talents, and serve others. We also acknowledge that our work does not define us, but rather reflects our innermost values and aspirations.

How to Practice Work as a Prayer?

  • Take a few moments every morning to set an intention for your work. You can light a candle, say a prayer, or simply take a deep breath and connect with your inner self.
  • Cultivate a mindset of gratitude and humility. Instead of focusing on what you lack or what you could do better, appreciate what you have and what you have already accomplished. Recognize that your work is not solely your own, but a collaboration with others and a gift from the divine.
  • Infuse your work with positive energy and purpose. Whether you are a CEO, a freelancer, a volunteer, or a stay-at-home parent, you can find ways to align your work with your values and make a difference in your community. You can also seek feedback, learn from your mistakes, and celebrate your successes.

Benefits of Work as a Prayer

The benefits of practicing work as a prayer are manifold, both for yourself and for others. Here are some examples:

  • Better mental and emotional health: When you approach your work with a positive and purposeful mindset, you are less likely to experience stress, burnout, or boredom. You are also more likely to develop resilience, creativity, and empathy.
  • Higher productivity and quality: When you are focused, motivated, and aligned with your values, you are more likely to perform well and deliver high-quality results. You are also more likely to inspire others and create a culture of positivity and growth.
  • Greater sense of meaning and fulfillment: When you see your work as a way to express your unique gifts, contribute to a greater cause, and make a difference in the world, you are more likely to feel fulfilled and satisfied with your life. You are also more likely to attract like-minded people and opportunities.

Conclusion

In conclusion, work as a prayer is a powerful practice that can transform your life and your work. By dedicating your work to your Creator, infusing it with intention and purpose, and aligning it with your values and aspirations, you can create a positive and meaningful impact in the world. As Rumi once said, "Work as though you are serving the most high, and expect no reward for yourself." May your work be a prayer that uplifts yourself and others, and may you find joy and fulfillment in every moment.
