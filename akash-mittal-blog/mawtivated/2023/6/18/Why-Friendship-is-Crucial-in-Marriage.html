The Power of Friendship in Marriage



Marriage is a beautiful thing, but it also requires work and commitment. It's easy to think that love is all you need to make a marriage successful, but according to Friedrich Nietzsche, it's not a lack of love that makes unhappy marriages, but a lack of friendship.

The Importance of Friendship in Marriage

Consider John and Sarah. They met in college and fell in love. They were inseparable and spent all their time together. But after a few years of marriage, they started to drift apart. They no longer had the same interests and hobbies, and they stopped talking and confiding in each other.

Despite their love for each other, their marriage was in trouble. They didn't realize that a lack of friendship was the root of their problems. Friendship is what keeps a marriage strong and healthy. It's what allows partners to communicate, share their thoughts and feelings, and work through challenges together.

Author - Friedrich Nietzsche

"It is not a lack of love, but a lack of friendship that makes unhappy marriages."

Friedrich Nietzsche was a German philosopher and cultural critic who lived in the 19th century. He is best known for his philosophy of nihilism and his critique of traditional moral values and Christianity. Despite his controversial ideas, Nietzsche's insights on marriage and relationships are still relevant today.

Examples of Friendship in Marriage

  • Spending quality time together
  • Supporting each other's goals and dreams
  • Being each other's confidant and best friend
  • Laughing and having fun together
  • Going on adventures and exploring new things together
  • Being there for each other during tough times

I've been married for ten years, and I can say from personal experience that friendship is crucial in a marriage. My husband and I are each other's best friend. We share everything with each other and support each other in everything we do.

One time, my husband decided to quit his job and start his own business. It was a risky move, but I believed in him and his dreams. I supported him every step of the way and helped him whenever he needed it. And when I went through a tough time with my own career, he was there for me, giving me advice and encouragement.

Our friendship has kept our marriage strong and happy, and I know that we can get through anything together.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the key to a successful marriage is not just love, but also friendship. Partners who are best friends have a strong foundation of trust, communication, and support that allows them to face challenges together and grow closer over time. Here are some practical tips for building friendship in your marriage:

  1. Take time to do things you both enjoy
  2. Communicate openly and honestly with each other
  3. Support each other's dreams and goals
  4. Laugh and have fun together
  5. Be there for each other during tough times
