From Subnormal Education to Outstanding Professionalism



Let me tell you a story about Loretta Young. She was a beautiful young woman born in 1913 in Utah, USA. Her family moved to Hollywood when she was a teenager and, shortly after, she landed her first movie role. But Loretta was not just any actress, she was determined to learn all she could about the craft and the industry. She spent hours observing other actors, reading scripts, and studying what made a successful movie. As she grew in her profession, so did her thirst for knowledge. She took classes, attended workshops and seminars, and surrounded herself with mentors who challenged her to become a better actress. Her hard work paid off, and she won numerous awards for her performances.

Loretta's story is a reminder of the power of education in any profession, including the creative field. In the quote "In common with many others in the varied branches of our profession, my academic education is subnormal," we can see that not everyone has the luxury of traditional academic education to prepare them for their profession. However, that does not mean they cannot achieve outstanding results. The key is to be intentional and proactive about learning and growing in each stage of your professional journey.

Why Academic Education Matters

Although not everyone has a traditional academic education, it is important to understand the value these programs offer. Academic education provides a foundation of knowledge and skills in a specific field. It equips students with a comprehensive understanding of the concepts, theories, and practical applications of their chosen area. In addition, academic institutions often offer research opportunities, access to experienced professionals, and networking events that can aid in career development.

However, academic education is not the only path to success. Many professionals have been able to achieve great things with alternative education such as vocational training, apprenticeships, mentorship, and self-study. As long as there is a willingness to learn, grow and develop new skills, there are a variety of paths to success.

and Case Studies

When it comes to learning and growing in our profession, real-life examples can offer valuable insight. Various successful professionals have achieved great things despite or because of their academic background. Let's take a look at some examples.

  1. Richard Branson: Despite dropping out of school at 16, Branson is a successful entrepreneur, investor, and philanthropist. His unconventional attitude and refusal to adhere to traditional business models have been key factors in his success. Branson attributes his success to his natural curiosity, willingness to learn, and eagerness to take risks.
  2. Sir Ken Robinson: Robinson is an internationally-respected author, speaker, and advisor on education. His views on creativity and the importance of education have garnered him a global following. Despite his success, Robinson has shared that he struggled academically in his youth due to his undiagnosed ADHD. However, he credits his lack of success to the educational system rather than his intelligence, and advocates for a change in the way we approach education.
  3. Anne Hathaway: Hathaway is an Oscar winner and has received numerous other accolades for her acting career. She attended Vassar College, a prestigious liberal arts institution in the United States, where she studied English and Women's Studies. Hathaway's academic background has given her a depth of knowledge and understanding of the arts, literature, and social issues that often come through in her performances.

The Importance of Continual Learning

No matter what your academic background may be, it is important to understand the value of continual learning. The world and industries are constantly evolving, and professionals who do not keep up with these changes run the risk of becoming obsolete. Continual learning can take many forms, from attending workshops and webinars to pursuing additional academic degrees and certifications.

In the digital age, learning can be easier than ever. Online courses, apps, and other digital platforms can make it possible for anyone to learn a new skill or deepen their knowledge in their field. The key is to find learning opportunities that match your personal style and learning goals.

Conclusion

In conclusion, education—whether traditional or alternative—is a vital component to success in any profession. Professionals who prioritize learning and development are more likely to succeed, no matter their academic background. There are many ways to continue learning throughout your career, and the most important thing is to stay curious, stay engaged, and never stop growing.

Summary Key Points:

  1. Academic education provides a foundation of knowledge and skills in a specific field.
  2. Academic institutions often offer research opportunities, access to experienced professionals, and networking events that can aid in career development.
  3. Alternative education such as vocational training, apprenticeships, mentorship, and self-study can be valuable paths to success.
  4. Continuous learning is essential to stay relevant and excel in any field.

Practical Tips:

  • Stay informed of industry trends and changes
  • Find new ways to apply your skills and knowledge
  • Take courses, attend webinars, and other learning opportunities
  • Find a mentor or peer who can challenge you and offer insights
