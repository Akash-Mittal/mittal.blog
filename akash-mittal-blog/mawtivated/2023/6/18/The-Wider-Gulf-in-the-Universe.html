The Wider Gulf in the Universe



It was a hot summer day when Susan, a successful businesswoman, met a homeless man named John. She saw John on the side of the road, hungry and thirsty. Without hesitation, she offered him some food and a cold drink. As soon as John realized what Susan was doing, he broke down in tears. He had not imagined that someone would help him in his most desperate hour.

Susan sat down with John, listened to his story, and offered assistance in getting him back on his feet. As they talked, Susan realized that she had never experienced deep poverty, and John had never experienced great wealth. They came from different worlds, but their human connection helped bridge the gap between them.

This inspiring story illustrates the quote by Rebecca West, "There is no wider gulf in the universe than yawns between those on the hither and thither side of vital experience." The gap between the rich and poor, the educated and uneducated, the healthy and sick, is vast. But, it's essential to learn about each other's experiences and perspectives to understand each other better.

Author - Rebecca West

Rebecca West was a British author who wrote novels, essays, and literary criticism. She was a feminist and contributed a lot to the Women's Suffrage Movement. West was born in 1892 in London, England, and started her career as a journalist before moving into authorship.

Examples

Let us consider two examples to understand the quote better:

  • When wealthy people talk about their problems, their difficulties may seem insignificant to someone who is struggling to afford basic needs, like food, shelter, and healthcare.
  • Similarly, when someone with good health talks about their stress, it could seem petty to someone who is struggling with a terminal illness.

Personal stories help to illustrate the point that there is a vast gulf between those with different experiences. When we share our experiences, we allow others to relate to us and understand our perspective. For example:

  • A friend of mine who grew up in a low-income household shared how difficult it was to make ends meet each month. She expressed frustration that some people overlook the significance of financial security.
  • Likewise, when I share my experience of living with anxiety, some people comment that I should relax and not worry. They can't understand that anxiety is not a choice, and it's not easy to control.

Conclusions

The quote by Rebecca West is profound, and it's essential to consider its significance in our daily lives. The conclusions we can draw are:

  1. We need to practice empathy and listen to each other's stories to bridge the gap between different experiences.
  2. We need to be mindful of the language we use to talk about our experiences and be sensitive to others' perspectives.
  3. We should not judge others because they have different experiences and perspectives. Instead, we should try to learn from each other.
