A Great Relationship is Beyond Lucky



By Julianne Hough

My name is Julianne, and I'm fortunate enough to say that I have a great relationship. It was not always the case, though. A few years ago, I found myself in a toxic relationship that was draining my energy and self-esteem. But through my personal growth journey, I learned that finding and nurturing a healthy, loving partnership is not a matter of luck but a deliberate choice. And once you do, it can transform your life in ways you never imagined.

Why a Great Relationship is Worth the Effort

There are many reasons why having a fulfilling and supportive relationship is one of the most valuable experiences in life. Here are some of the benefits, based on research and personal experience:

  • It boosts your physical and mental health. Studies have shown that people who are in happy relationships have lower blood pressure, stronger immune systems, and better mental well-being than those who are not. This is because having someone who cares for you and shares your life can reduce stress, improve sleep, and provide a sense of purpose and belonging.
  • It enhances your personal growth and happiness. A healthy relationship is not just about finding someone who completes you but someone who challenges you to be your best self. When you're in a supportive partnership, you have the freedom to explore your interests, pursue your goals, and learn from your mistakes with someone who has your back. This can help you cultivate self-awareness, resilience, and confidence, which are crucial for overall life satisfaction.
  • It strengthens your social connections. Being in a happy couple can improve your relationships with other people, too. When you have a partner who values communication, empathy, and respect, you're more likely to practice those same skills with your friends, family, and colleagues. Additionally, having a stable and supportive relationship can provide a buffer against loneliness, isolation, and social anxiety.
"...finding and nurturing a healthy, loving partnership is not a matter of luck but a deliberate choice."

How to Cultivate a Great Relationship

Now that you know why a great relationship is worth striving for, let's explore some practical tips for how to make it happen:

  • Communicate openly and honestly. One of the most important aspects of a healthy relationship is effective communication. This means being willing to express your feelings, needs, and boundaries in a clear and respectful way, and also listening actively and empathetically to your partner's perspective. Remember that communication is not just about words but also about body language, tone of voice, and nonverbal cues.
  • Nurture intimacy and connection. Intimacy is more than just physical attraction or sexual desire; it's the sense of deep emotional closeness and trust that comes from sharing your vulnerabilities, dreams, and values with someone. To build intimacy with your partner, practice being present and attentive when you're together, showing affection and appreciation regularly, and exploring new experiences and adventures that bond you together.
  • Respect each other's autonomy and individuality. While a great relationship involves a lot of compromise and collaboration, it's also important to uphold each other's independence and uniqueness. This means not trying to control or change your partner, but rather supporting their growth and development as an individual. It also means taking time for your own hobbies, passions, and self-care practices, which can enhance your sense of self and make you more interesting and attractive to your partner.

A Final Word

If you're reading this article, chances are you're already interested in having a great relationship, which is a fantastic starting point. Remember that a strong and loving partnership takes time, effort, and commitment, but the rewards are immeasurable. By choosing to prioritize your relationship and implement some of the tips we discussed, you can create a bond that enriches your life and helps you become the best version of yourself.
