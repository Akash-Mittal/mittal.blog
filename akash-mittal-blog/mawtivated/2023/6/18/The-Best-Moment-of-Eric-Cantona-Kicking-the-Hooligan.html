The Best Moment of Eric Cantona: Kicking the Hooligan



The Inspiring Story

Eric Cantona, a French former professional footballer, had many good moments during his career, but his best moment is one that has become legendary. In January 1995, during a match against Crystal Palace, Cantona was sent off after receiving a red card. As he was walking towards the tunnel, a fan started shouting insults at him. Cantona couldn't take it anymore and jumped over the advertising boards to kick the hooligan in the chest.

"My best moment? I have a lot of good moments but the one I prefer is when I kicked the hooligan." - Eric Cantona

This act of violence might seem unprofessional and inappropriate, but it was also a moment of empowerment for Cantona. He was not just a puppet of the football industry anymore; he was a human being who could no longer tolerate abuse from someone he didn't even know. This act also made Cantona even more popular with the fans, who saw him as a hero who stood up against the injustice of hooliganism.

Eric Cantona Background

Eric Cantona was born in Marseille, France, in 1966. He started his football career at age 16, playing for Auxerre. Throughout his career, Cantona played for several leading football clubs, including Leeds United and Manchester United. He was known for his exceptional skill, vision, and creativity on the field, but also for his temperamental personality and unconventional behavior off the field.

"I am not a man, I am Cantona." - Eric Cantona

After retiring from football at the age of 30, Cantona pursued a career in acting and became a cultural icon in France. He also became known for his philosophical and poetic musings, which have inspired many people to this day.
