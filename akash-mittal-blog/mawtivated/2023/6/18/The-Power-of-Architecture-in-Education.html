The Power of Architecture in Education



Richard Rogers, a renowned architect and urban designer, once said:

"I believe very strongly, and have fought since many years ago - at least over 30 years ago - to get architecture not just within schools, but architecture talked about under history, geography, science, technology, art."

Indeed, the inclusion of architecture in the education system is a topic that deserves more attention. Architecture not only shapes our cities and landscapes but also influences our way of living and thinking. By teaching architecture at a young age, we can empower students to become critical thinkers and innovators who can transform their communities for the better.

An Inspiring Story

In 2017, a group of students from the University of Wollongong in Australia won the Solar Decathlon China, a global competition that challenges teams to design and build sustainable and energy-efficient houses. Their entry, dubbed the "Desert Rose," was a beautiful and high-tech home that used renewable energy sources and advanced building materials.

What was remarkable about this project was that it was not just a feat of engineering or design, but a product of a multidisciplinary collaboration that involved students from different fields, such as architecture, engineering, and business. The team worked together for more than two years to create a house that not only showcased their technical skills but also reflected their vision of a more sustainable and equitable future.

For these students, architecture wasn't just a profession or a hobby - it was a way of thinking and problem-solving that transcended traditional boundaries. By learning about architecture in a holistic way, they were able to apply their knowledge to real-world challenges and make a meaningful impact in their community.

The Importance of Teaching Architecture in Education

Richard Rogers is not alone in his belief that architecture should be part of the education system. Many educators and practitioners have advocated for the inclusion of architecture in various subjects, such as history, geography, science, technology, and art. Here are some reasons why:

  • Architecture fosters creativity and imagination. By learning about architecture, students can develop their visual and spatial thinking skills, as well as their ability to generate and communicate ideas.
  • Architecture promotes interdisciplinary learning. Architecture is an inherently interdisciplinary field that requires collaboration among experts from different fields. By teaching architecture, we can encourage students to think beyond their own disciplines and work together to solve complex problems.
  • Architecture raises awareness of social and environmental issues. Architecture has a profound impact on our society and environment, and by teaching architecture, we can raise awareness of issues such as sustainability, social justice, and cultural heritage.
  • Architecture empowers students to make a difference. Architecture is not just about designing buildings - it is about creating spaces that enhance the quality of life for people. By teaching architecture, we can inspire students to use their skills and knowledge to make a positive impact in their communities.

Practical Tips for Incorporating Architecture into Education

If you are an educator or a parent who wants to introduce architecture to your students or children, here are some practical tips:

  1. Start with the basics. Teach students about the elements of architecture, such as form, space, light, and material. You can use simple exercises, such as drawing or modeling, to help students understand these concepts.
  2. Integrate architecture into other subjects. Show students how architecture intersects with other fields, such as history, geography, science, and art. For example, you can teach about the architecture of different cultures or the physics of structures.
  3. Use real-world examples. Show students how architecture is applied in real-life projects, such as buildings, parks, or public spaces. You can take students on field trips or invite guest speakers who work in the architecture field.
  4. Encourage experimentation and collaboration. Give students opportunities to experiment with different materials, shapes, and ideas. You can also encourage them to work in groups and share their ideas with each other.
  5. Show the impact of architecture. Show students how architecture can make a difference in people's lives, such as by creating more livable neighborhoods, improving public health, or preserving cultural heritage.

Conclusion

In summary, teaching architecture in education is not just a matter of enriching the curriculum but a way of preparing students for the challenges of the future. By learning about architecture, students can develop their creativity, critical thinking, and social awareness, as well as their technical skills. If we want to create a more sustainable, equitable, and beautiful world, we need to start by teaching the next generation about architecture.

Therefore, to sum up, here are three main points from this article:

  1. Architecture is a vital subject that should be included in the education system.
  2. Architecture promotes creativity, interdisciplinary learning, and social and environmental awareness.
  3. Practical tips for incorporating architecture into education include starting with the basics, integrating it into other subjects, using real-world examples, encouraging experimentation and collaboration, and showing the impact of architecture.
