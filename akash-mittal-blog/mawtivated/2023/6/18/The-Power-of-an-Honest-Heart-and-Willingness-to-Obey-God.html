The Power of an Honest Heart and Willingness to Obey God



A young boy once asked his father, "Dad, you say that we can ask God for anything and He will give it to us. Is that really true?" The father replied in the affirmative. The boy thought for a moment and asked, "Dad, why don't we ask God to help us obey Him?"

This short story underscores what it takes to be a Christian. It does not require great learning or deep philosophical understanding. What it does require is an honest heart and a willingness to obey God.

The Story of Albert Barnes

Albert Barnes (1798-1870) was a Presbyterian minister, theologian, and author. He wrote many books, including his famous commentary on the Bible, which is still widely used today.

Barnes grew up in a devout Christian family and was educated at Hamilton College and Princeton Theological Seminary. He became a pastor at the age of 22 and served several churches throughout his life.

Barnes was known for his ability to explain complex theological concepts in simple language that ordinary people could understand. He believed that the Bible was the true word of God and that it contained everything a person needed to know in order to live a successful and fulfilling life.

Barnes also believed that obedience to God was the key to spiritual growth and maturity. He wrote, "The principle of obedience is fundamental in religion. It lies at the foundation of all true piety, and is the essence of a life devoted to God."

Examples of an Honest Heart and Willingness to Obey

There are many examples in the Bible of people who had an honest heart and a willingness to obey God:

  • Abraham was willing to leave his home and family to go to a new land that God promised him (Genesis 12:1-4).
  • Moses was willing to confront Pharaoh and lead the Israelites out of slavery, even though he was initially reluctant (Exodus 3-4).
  • David was willing to face the giant Goliath in battle, trusting that God would give him the strength to win (1 Samuel 17).
  • The apostles were willing to leave their jobs and families to follow Jesus and spread the gospel (Matthew 4:18-22).

These examples show that an honest heart and a willingness to obey God can lead to great things. They also demonstrate that obedience to God requires faith and trust in His promises, even when they seem impossible to fulfill.

Practical Tips for Honesty and Obedience

Here are some practical tips for developing an honest heart and a willingness to obey God:

  • Spend time reading and studying the Bible. This will help you understand God's will and strengthen your faith.
  • Pray regularly and ask God to help you obey His commands.
  • Seek advice and guidance from wise and mature Christians who can help you grow in your faith.
  • Be honest with yourself and others. Confess your sins and ask for forgiveness when you fall short of God's standards.
  • Be willing to take risks and step out in faith, trusting that God will guide and protect you.

By following these tips, you can develop an honest heart and a willingness to obey God, and experience the blessings and joy that come with a life of faith.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the power of an honest heart and a willingness to obey God cannot be overstated. It is not a matter of intellectual achievement or philosophical understanding, but rather a matter of the heart. When we are honest with ourselves and with God, and when we are willing to obey His commands, we open ourselves up to a life of joy, fulfillment, and purpose.

To develop an honest heart and a willingness to obey God, we must spend time reading and studying the Bible, pray regularly, seek advice from wise and mature Christians, be honest with ourselves and others, and be willing to take risks and step out in faith. By following these tips, we can become the people that God created us to be and experience the blessings of a life devoted to Him.
