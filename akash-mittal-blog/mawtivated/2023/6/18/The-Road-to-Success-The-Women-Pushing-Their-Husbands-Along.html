The Road to Success: The Women Pushing Their Husbands Along



There's an inspiring story by David Tyreman that goes:

In 1947, Hulett C. Smith was running for Governor of West Virginia. During his campaign, he spoke in a town in West Virginia where he met a woman in a wheelbarrow. He stopped to ask the woman why she was in a wheelbarrow. The woman said it was because her husband said if she wanted to come along, she would have to push the wheelbarrow. Hulett C. Smith told the woman, "You and I have a lot in common. We're both pushing our husbands along."

This story highlights the important role of women in men's success. Many great men owe their success to the women who supported them, believed in them, and pushed them to do better. But this doesn't mean that women have to play a passive role in their husbands' or partners' journey to success. Women can be the driving force behind their own success, too.

Thomas R. Dewar's Story

Thomas R. Dewar, a Scottish-born businessman and philanthropist who owned a successful distillery, was once asked about the secret to his success.

"Marry a stout-hearted woman. She'll help you while you're climbing the ladder, and when you fall off, she'll help to pick you up again."

Dewar's answer goes to show that a supportive partner is crucial to one's success. But not just any partner, a stout-hearted one. A stout-hearted woman is strong, unyielding, and unwavering in her support. She's a woman who believes in her husband's or partner's potential and works tirelessly to help him achieve his goals.

Women Can Be the Driving Force Behind Their Own Success, Too

While the examples above are about women pushing their husbands along, women can be the driving force behind their own success, too. Here are some ways women can push themselves along:

  • Set goals and work towards them. Don't be afraid to dream big.
  • Surround yourself with supportive people. Seek out mentors, peers, and friends who will encourage and inspire you.
  • Believe in yourself. Trust your instincts and don't be afraid to take risks. Failure is a natural part of the journey to success.

By being their own driving force, women can achieve great things and make their mark on the world.

Conclusion

In summary, women have been instrumental in men's success throughout history. They've pushed, encouraged, and supported their husbands and partners, helping them to achieve great things. But women can also be the driving force behind their own success. By setting goals, surrounding themselves with supportive people, and believing in themselves, women can achieve anything they set their minds to.
