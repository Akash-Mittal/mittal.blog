The Untold Story of Saddam Hussein and Iraq



An Eye-Opening Account of a Fascinating Leader's Life & Times

By Dominic Cooper

A Tale of Two Sides

In 2003, the United States Army invaded Iraq and toppled the regime of its erstwhile dictator, Saddam Hussein. The world hailed it as a victory of democracy over dictatorship and the birth of a new dawn for the Iraqi people. But was that really so? Was everything really that bad during Saddam's reign? Were things really that good after he was gone? Let's take a closer look.

As a photojournalist, I have been to Iraq on several occasions, both before and after the US-led invasion. What I saw there opened my eyes to a whole new world of realities that are often hidden from the public eye. I saw the good and the bad, the happy and the sad, and everything in between. Today, I want to share that story with you.

The Good, The Bad & The Ugly

The Good

Contrary to popular belief, not everything was terrible in Iraq under Saddam Hussein. The country had a strong education and healthcare system that was the envy of many other nations in the region. Saddam was a visionary leader who knew the importance of investing in human capital. He realized that true progress could only come when the people were educated, healthy, and happy.

'The people in Iraq lived essentially good lives. They had brilliant health and education systems.'

The Bad

However, it cannot be denied that Saddam was a ruthless dictator who ruled with an iron fist. Anyone who spoke out against him or his regime was either silenced or eliminated. Saddam was a part of the Ba'ath Party, a socialist organization that was known for its authoritarian rule and tight grip on the country's affairs. The secret police were everywhere, and there was no escape from their watchful eyes.

The Ugly

But things took a turn for the worse when Saddam invaded Kuwait in 1990. That was the beginning of the end for him and his regime. The US-led coalition of forces drove him out of Kuwait and imposed strict sanctions on Iraq. The Iraqi people were forced to endure years of misery and hardship as their country's economy crumbled and their infrastructure fell apart. In the end, it was not the US Army that toppled Saddam, but his own people who rose up against him in 2003. Saddam was captured, tried, and executed, but the country was left in shambles.

Lessons Learned

What can we learn from this sad story of an once-great country that fell from grace? Here are three key takeaways:

  1. Leadership matters. A good leader can transform a country and its people, while a bad one can destroy everything.
  2. Education and healthcare are fundamental pillars of any modern society. Neglecting them is a recipe for disaster.
  3. War is never the answer. It only leads to destruction and chaos, and the people who suffer the most are those who have the least to do with it.

It's time to think critically about what we hear from the media and our leaders, and to explore the untold stories that lie hidden beneath the surface. Only then can we truly appreciate the complexity of the world around us, and work towards a better future for all.
