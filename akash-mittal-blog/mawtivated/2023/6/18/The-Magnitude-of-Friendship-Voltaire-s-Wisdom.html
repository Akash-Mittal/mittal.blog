The Magnitude of Friendship: Voltaire's Wisdom



Once upon a time, there was a group of friends living in a small village. They were the best of friends, they shared everything, good and bad times. They were inseparable. They were so connected that people in the village envied them and wished to have such friendships. One day, a conflict erupted among them, they found it hard to reconcile, and their bond faded.

This story is just an example of how fragile friendship can be. Friendship is a unique relationship that we develop with another person. It is beautiful, beneficial, and can bring us joy and satisfaction. However, it also has its shortcomings and can be hurtful and disappointing.

Voltaire aptly denotes friendship as the marriage of the soul. When we think of marriage, we know it is a commitment and involves love, patience, understanding, and effort. The same principles apply to friendship. As with every relationship, friendship requires nurturing and care. As we journey through life, we make friends along the way, sometimes we keep them, sometimes we lose them.

Examples of the ups and downs of Friendship

Friendship can bring happiness and comfort, but it also can bring pain and heartache. Here are a few examples:

  • Robin's best friend, Jane, spread rumors about her in school, hurting her reputation terribly.
  • Bob and George have been friends for thirty years, they have supported each other through thick and thin, they are each other's confidante and advisor.
  • Martha's friend, Sarah, won't take responsibility for her actions, never says "sorry" or tries to make amends when she's wrong.
  • John and Tom had a disagreement. John forgave Tom, but Tom never apologized or acknowledged John's hurt feelings.

These examples show the ups and downs of friendship and how it can either nurture our souls or cause us distress.

Practical Tips for Strengthening Friendship

Here are some practical tips for strengthening the bond of friendship:

  1. Communication is Key: Openly and honestly communicating with your friend can go a long way. It can prevent misunderstandings and promote healing when things go wrong.
  2. Mutual respect: A lack of respect can destroy a friendship quickly. It's important to be mindful and respectful of your friend's feelings, needs, opinions, and boundaries.
  3. Empathy: Showing empathy and understanding towards your friend can help you stay connected. Try to see things from your friend's perspective and offer your support when needed.

Conclusion

In conclusion, friendship is a vital part of our lives. It brings us joy, happiness, and support. However, like marriage, it requires constant effort, love, forgiveness, and patience. The ups and downs of friendship are similar to the ups and downs of life. It is not the absence of conflicts that defines friendship, but how we handle them.
