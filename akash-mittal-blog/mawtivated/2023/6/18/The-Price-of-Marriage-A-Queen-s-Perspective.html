The Price of Marriage: A Queen's Perspective



Once upon a time, there was a Queen named Victoria who ruled England for 63 years, from 1837 until her death in 1901. As a young monarch, many people underestimated her, but she proved them wrong. She was intelligent, independent, and strong-willed. She had a deep sense of duty and took her responsibilities seriously. However, Queen Victoria's views on marriage may have surprised some people, given her own successful marriage.

"When I think of a merry, happy, free young girl - and look at the ailing, aching state a young wife generally is doomed to - which you can't deny is the penalty of marriage."

These words, written by Queen Victoria in a letter to her daughter, show her skepticism towards the institution of marriage. In her time, women were expected to marry and have children, and their husbands became their lord and master.

The High Price of Marriage

Queen Victoria was not the only woman who thought this way. Many women of her time knew the high price of marriage. Women had little say in whom they married and few rights within the marriage. They were considered their husband's property and were expected to serve him and bear children. This led to many unhappy marriages and contributed to the growing feminist movement.

Today, things are different. Women have many more rights and freedoms, and marriage is no longer the only path to happiness and fulfillment. However, there are still many obstacles and challenges facing women who choose to get married.

The Challenges Facing Married Women Today

  1. The Expectation of Domestic Duties: Despite the fact that both men and women work outside the home, women are still expected to take on the lion's share of domestic duties. This can lead to burnout and stress, especially if the woman is also trying to balance a career or other responsibilities.
  2. The Marriage Penalty: Many couples find that they pay more in taxes after they get married than they did when they were single. This can be a significant financial burden, especially if one spouse earns significantly more than the other.
  3. The Gender Pay Gap: Women still earn less than men for doing the same job. This can create tension within a marriage if the woman feels that she is not being valued or appreciated for her contributions.

The Benefits of Marriage

Despite these challenges, there are also many benefits to getting married. Some of these include:

  • Legal Protections: Married couples have many legal protections that unmarried couples do not have, such as the ability to make medical decisions for each other if one partner is incapacitated.
  • Financial Benefits: Marriage can provide financial stability and security. Couples can pool their resources and work together towards common financial goals.
  • Emotional Support: Marriage can provide emotional support and companionship. It can be nice to have someone to share your life with and to help you through the ups and downs.

Conclusion

Marriage is not for everyone, and it is important for women to make choices that are best for them. However, for those who do choose to get married, it is important to go into it with realistic expectations and to communicate openly with your partner. Here are three practical tips for a happy marriage:

  1. Communicate openly and honestly with your partner.
  2. Share the domestic responsibilities, and work together as a team.
  3. Respect each other's individual needs and interests, and make time for each other.

Remember, marriage is a journey, not a destination. It requires patience, dedication, and hard work, but it can also be one of the most rewarding relationships of your life.
