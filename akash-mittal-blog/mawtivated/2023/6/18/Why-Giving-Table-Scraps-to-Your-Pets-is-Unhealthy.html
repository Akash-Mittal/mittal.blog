The Number One Mistake Pet Owners Make: Giving Table Scraps



By Alison Sweeney

As a pet owner, I've made my fair share of mistakes. One mistake in particular that I'd like to discuss is the act of giving table scraps to pets. At one point, I thought I was showing my dog love by giving her food and treats during mealtimes. It wasn't until I learned about portion control and proper nutrition that I realized how unhealthy this practice was.

My dog is a Boston Terrier, and as a small breed, she only needs a specific amount of food per day. A tiny 4 oz. piece of cheese might not seem like much to us, but for a dog her size, it's similar to eating one and a half hamburgers. This kind of calorie overload can lead to obesity and other health problems.

The reality is that table scraps are not meant for pets. The food that we eat is often seasoned and cooked in a way that isn't suitable for pets. Additionally, many foods that humans enjoy can be dangerous for animals to consume, such as chocolate, garlic, and onions.

The Risks and Consequences

So what are the risks and consequences of giving your pets table scraps?

  • Obesity: As mentioned earlier, table scraps can cause pets to consume more calories than they need, leading to weight gain and obesity
  • Health problems: Some foods can be toxic to pets, leading to vomiting, diarrhea, and even liver failure
  • Behavioral issues: Giving pets table scraps can create bad habits and reinforce begging behavior during mealtimes
  • Digestive problems: The digestive systems of pets are different from ours, and the introduction of table scraps can lead to digestive problems such as constipation or diarrhea

What Should You Feed Your Pets?

So what should you be feeding your pets instead of table scraps? Ideally, you should stick to a nutritionally-balanced pet food that is appropriate for your pet's age, weight, and health needs. There are many different options available on the market, including dry kibble, wet food, and raw food diets.

Of course, it's important to ensure that your pet is getting the proper amount of exercise and not overeating. You can consult with your veterinarian about the appropriate portion sizes and the best type of food for your pet.

Conclusion

In conclusion, it's important to avoid giving table scraps to your pets. Doing so can lead to a host of health problems, obesity, and behavioral issues. Instead, focus on providing your pet with a nutritionally-balanced diet that meets their specific needs. Remember to consult with your veterinarian for guidance in choosing the right food for your pet.

  1. Stick to a nutritionally-balanced pet food that is appropriate for your pet's age, weight, and health needs
  2. Avoid giving table scraps to your pets to prevent obesity, health problems, and behavioral issues
  3. Consult with your veterinarian for guidance in choosing the right food for your pet and the appropriate portion sizes
