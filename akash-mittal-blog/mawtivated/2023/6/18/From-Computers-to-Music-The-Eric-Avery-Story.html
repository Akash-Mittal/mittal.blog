From Computers to Music: The Eric Avery Story



Who is Eric Avery?

Eric Avery is a musician who rose to fame in the 1980s as the bassist for the alternative rock band, Jane's Addiction. Throughout his career, Avery has been known for his unique style and innovative use of effects pedals.

The Beginning of the Story

Despite his early success with Jane's Addiction, Avery found himself becoming increasingly disillusioned with the music industry as the years went on. He grew tired of the endless touring and felt like he was no longer making the kind of music he wanted to make.

It was around this time that Avery discovered his love for computers and internet technologies. He found himself captivated by the way these technologies were changing the world and began spending more and more time learning about them.

"I wasn't especially interested in being a musician, but I wound up finding my way back to being interested in music through computers." - Eric Avery

Finding His Way Back to Music

As Avery became more involved in the world of computers, he discovered that he could use these technologies to create music in new and exciting ways. He began experimenting with software synthesizers, samplers, and other digital tools.

What started out as a simple curiosity soon turned into a full-blown passion for music once again. Avery found himself staying up late into the night, tinkering with his computer and coming up with new musical ideas.

Examples

Collaboration with nine inch nails

One of Avery's most well-known collaborations from this period was working with Nine Inch Nails on their album, The Fragile. Avery played bass and contributed to the songwriting on several tracks, including the hit song, "We're in This Together."

Solo albums

Avery has also released several solo albums that showcase his love of digital music-making techniques. His album, Help Wanted, is a prime example of the ways in which Avery has embraced new technologies in his music.

  • Passion is the key ingredient in any successful creative endeavor.
