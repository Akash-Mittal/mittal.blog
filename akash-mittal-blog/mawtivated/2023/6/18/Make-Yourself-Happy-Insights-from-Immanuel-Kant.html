Make Yourself Happy: Insights from Immanuel Kant



By John Smith

It is often said that happiness is a state of mind, and that it is up to us to cultivate that state within ourselves. But what does that really mean? How do we go about making ourselves truly happy? In this article, we will explore the ideas of Immanuel Kant and how they can help us discover the true path to happiness.

The Story of Sarah

Let me tell you the story of Sarah, a woman who had everything she thought she wanted. She was successful in her career, had a loving family, and was financially secure. Yet, deep down she felt a sense of emptiness and dissatisfaction that she couldn't quite place. She tried everything from material possessions to therapy, but nothing could fill the void.

One day, a friend recommended that she read the works of Immanuel Kant. Sarah was hesitant at first, but decided to give it a try. As she delved into his writings, she began to realize that her pursuit of happiness was misguided. She had been looking for external sources of happiness, when in reality it was something she had to cultivate within herself.

"It is not God's will merely that we should be happy, but that we should make ourselves happy."

Insights from Kant

Immanuel Kant, a German philosopher from the 18th century, believed that true happiness comes from within. He argued that we must pursue our duties, live morally and ethically, and cultivate relationships based on respect and mutual understanding. It is only when we fulfill these obligations that we can find true happiness.

  • Morality: According to Kant, happiness is not found in pleasure or material possessions. Rather, it is found in fulfilling our moral obligations. By doing what is right, we can achieve a sense of satisfaction and purpose.
  • Relationships: Kant believed that our relationships with others should be based on mutual respect and understanding. By treating others with dignity and valuing their needs and desires, we can build strong and meaningful connections.
  • Inner Freedom: Kant believed that we must cultivate our inner freedom, which is the ability to act according to our own values and principles. By rejecting external pressures, we can live authentically and find true happiness.

Examples of Kantian Principles

Let's take a look at some real-life examples of how Kant's principles can be applied:

  • Choosing a Career: When deciding on a career path, it is important to consider not just financial gain, but also how the work aligns with our values and principles. By choosing a career that aligns with our beliefs, we can find fulfillment and purpose in our work.
  • Respectful Communication: When interacting with others, it is important to listen actively and treat them with respect. By valuing their perspective, we can build stronger relationships and achieve better outcomes.
  • Personal Growth: By focusing on personal growth and taking steps to cultivate our inner freedom, we can live a more authentic and fulfilling life.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Immanuel Kant's philosophy provides a strong foundation for cultivating true happiness. By focusing on our moral obligations, relationships, and inner freedom, we can create a life that is fulfilling and purposeful. Remember:

  • It is not external sources that will make us happy, but rather our commitment to fulfilling our duties.
  • Our relationships should be based on mutual respect and understanding.
  • We must cultivate our inner freedom in order to live authentically and find true happiness.
