Working Harder to Help People Around the World



It was a scorching hot day in the small village of Dembi Dollo in Ethiopia. I was there as a volunteer with a non-profit organization, helping to build a water well for the local community. I remember feeling exhausted and overwhelmed, wondering if anything I did would really make a difference.

But then, as we were digging and hauling buckets of dirt, a young girl named Ayana came up to me with a big smile on her face. She took my hand and led me to the edge of the village, where a group of children were playing a game of soccer with a makeshift ball made from rags.

Even though we couldn't communicate very well due to the language barrier, Ayana made it clear that she was grateful for our work and that it meant something to her and her community. That moment stayed with me and reminded me of why I want to work harder than ever to help people around the world.

The Inspiring Story of Lech Walesa

One person who has inspired me in this pursuit is Lech Walesa, the former president of Poland and a Nobel Peace Prize laureate. Walesa rose to prominence in the 1980s as the leader of the Solidarity movement, which fought for workers' rights and democracy in a country that was under communist rule.

Despite facing oppression and even imprisonment, Walesa refused to give up and remained committed to his cause. His efforts eventually led to free elections in Poland and the fall of communism in Eastern Europe.

"I believe that every person can make a difference, no matter how small. It's not about how much you can do, but about how much you care."

Walesa's story reminds me that even one person can make a significant impact on the world. It's not about the size of the problem or how much resources you have; it's about having the passion and drive to make a difference.

Why We Should Work Harder to Help Others

There are many reasons why we should work harder to help people around the world. Here are a few:

  • It shows empathy and compassion - when we volunteer or donate to a cause, it signals to others that we care about their well-being and that we are willing to help in any way we can.
  • It can have a direct impact on people's lives - whether it's providing clean drinking water or access to education, our efforts can make a tangible difference in the communities we serve.
  • It makes us happier and more fulfilled - studies have shown that helping others can increase our own sense of well-being and purpose in life. It's a win-win situation!

Practical Tips for Helping Others

If you're unsure of how to get started or want to make a bigger impact, here are a few practical tips:

  • Find a cause that you are passionate about - whether it's protecting the environment or fighting poverty, it's important to choose something that resonates with you.
  • Volunteer your time or skills - not everyone can afford to donate money, but we all have something to offer. Consider volunteering at a local non-profit or using your professional skills to help a cause you care about.
  • Spread awareness - social media and word-of-mouth are powerful tools for raising awareness about an issue or cause. Share news articles, write a blog post, or simply talk to your friends about something you're passionate about.

Remember, every little bit helps!
