The Story Behind Thomas Carlyle's Quote

Thomas Carlyle was a Scottish philosopher, writer, and historian who lived in the 19th century. He was known for his influential works on the French Revolution and for his critical stance on the Industrial Revolution.

In his essay, "On Heroes, Hero-Worship, and The Heroic in History," Carlyle argued that history was shaped by great men and women who possessed extraordinary qualities. However, he also recognized that cleverness and intellect alone weren't enough to make someone a true hero.

It is the heart always that sees, before the head can see. - Thomas Carlyle

Carlyle believed that true heroes had a higher purpose and a sense of duty that drove them beyond their own self-interest. They were driven by a passion for excellence and a desire to make a positive impact on the world.
