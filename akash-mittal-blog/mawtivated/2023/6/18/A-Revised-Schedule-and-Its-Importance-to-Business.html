A Revised Schedule: The New Opportunity For Your Business



Have you ever felt stuck in your business and unable to move forward?

It happens to even the most successful entrepreneurs. But what sets them apart is their ability to adapt and revise their schedules like athletes do when faced with a new season, or artists when given a new canvas.

Let me share with you a story that inspired this article.

"When I was the CEO of Lockheed Martin, we were assigned a project with the U.S. government that had some delays, and as a result, the milestones had to be redefined. During a review meeting, my team and I realized that this revised schedule had the potential to turn things around and deliver the project faster than the original one. The only difference was that it required us to change our approach and view the revised schedule as a new opportunity rather than a setback. This experience taught me the importance of having a positive attitude towards revisions and how it can help businesses thrive."

The author of this quote, Norman Ralph Augustine, is a prominent American engineer and businessman who has served as the CEO of various aerospace companies throughout his career.

Why A Revised Schedule Is So Important

Many business owners view a revised schedule as a setback or a failure, but in reality, it is an opportunity to improve. Here are some important reasons why:

  • It Helps To Stay Focused: A revised schedule enables you to prioritize the critical tasks and reassess your resources to meet the new deadlines.
  • It Provides An Opportunity For Creativity: A new schedule isn't just about new deadlines, but it is also an ideal opportunity to be more innovative and creative.
  • It Boosts Confidence: You can view it as an opportunity to correct past mistakes and get one step closer to your goals.

Examples Of Successful Businesses That Embraced A Revised Schedule

There are many examples of businesses that adapted to a revised schedule and have prospered from it. Here are a few:

  1. Apple Inc: In 2017, Apple announced a revised schedule for the release of its latest iPhone X due to production problems. The decision to delay its launch actually created more anticipation and drove up the sales of its previous models.
  2. Amazon: Amazon has been known to adjust its schedules to meet the demands and needs of its customers. The company's ability to evolve and adapt has made it the undisputed leader in the e-commerce industry.
  3. Starbucks: Starbucks' CEO Kevin Johnson revised the company's schedule and announced that the company would be closing 8,000 stores for one afternoon to conduct racial bias training for its employees. The decision was applauded by many, and it reflected the company's commitment to its customers and to be socially responsible.

Practical Tips To Adapt To A Revised Schedule

Although it can be challenging to adapt to a revised schedule, here are some practical tips that can help:

  • Stay Positive: A positive attitude can go a long way in turning a setback into an opportunity.
  • Reassess Your Resources: Take stock of your available resources and re-allocate them according to the new schedule.
  • Communicate Effectively: Make sure everyone involved understands the revised schedule and their roles in achieving the new deadlines.
  • Be Realistic: Be honest about what you can and can't achieve with the revised schedule. Do not overload or over-promise anything that your team cannot deliver.

Conclusion

  1. A revised schedule is an opportunity for your business to improve, be creative, and boost confidence.
  2. Many successful businesses have adapted to revised schedules and have prospered from them.
  3. It can be challenging, but with the right attitude, mindset, and practical tips, you can learn to embrace a new schedule and thrive.
