The Power of Relatability in Hollywood Movies



Have you ever watched a movie and thought to yourself, "I cannot relate to this at all"? This is a problem for many people when it comes to Hollywood movies. The industry is saturated with films featuring larger-than-life characters or unrealistic scenarios that the average person just can't relate to. However, in recent years, there has been a push for more relatable content in Hollywood. Let's explore why relatability is key in Hollywood movies and how it can make a big impact on audiences.

An Inspiring Story

Kim Cattrall is a prime example of why relatable characters are so important in Hollywood. She was known for her role in the popular TV show "Sex and the City," where she played the confident and successful character of Samantha. However, when she was offered the role, she initially turned it down because she didn't relate to the character. It wasn't until she was convinced to look at the character in a different way, that she realized Samantha was a complex character with many layers. This allowed her to give an authentic performance that resonated with audiences.

Why Relatability Matters

Relatability is key in Hollywood movies because it helps to create a connection between the audience and the characters on screen. When people can see themselves in the characters, it allows them to get fully immersed in the story and feel a range of emotions - from sadness to joy. This connection also allows for a deeper understanding of different perspectives and experiences which can promote empathy and understanding.

Examples of Relatable Movies

Some examples of Hollywood movies that have nailed relatability include the following:

  • The Pursuit of Happyness: This movie tells the story of Chris Gardner, a struggling salesman who becomes homeless with his young son. It's a heartwarming story about perseverance and never giving up in the face of adversity. Everyone can relate to the struggles of life and the pursuit of happiness.
  • Little Miss Sunshine: This movie tells the story of a dysfunctional family traveling to a beauty pageant. It's a comedy about embracing your imperfections, and the importance of family.
  • Forrest Gump: This movie tells the story of a simple man living through some of the most historic moments of the 20th century. It's a story about the power of love and how good things can happen to anyone, no matter how unlikely it seems.

Conclusion

In conclusion, relatability is key in Hollywood movies because it helps to create an emotional connection between the audience and the characters on screen. By creating characters and storylines that people can relate to, it allows for a deeper understanding of different perspectives and experiences. The three main takeaways from this article are:

  1. Relatability creates a connection between the audience and the characters on screen.
  2. Empathy can be promoted through relatable content.
  3. There are many examples of relatable movies that have touched the hearts of audiences everywhere.
