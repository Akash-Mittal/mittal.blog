The Enlightening Story of Chris Gardner



Chris Gardner is a prime example of someone who beat the odds in life. Born in the slums of Wisconsin, he faced many hardships such as domestic violence and abandonment. He witnessed his stepfather beating his mother on multiple occasions and was later abandoned by his biological father.

Despite the circumstances, Gardner pushed himself to his limits and became a successful businessman. He is now a motivational speaker and has inspired countless individuals to work hard towards their dreams.


Is Hitting Your Children Ever Justifiable?

As parents, we have to make some of the toughest decisions, and disciplining our children is one such situation. Hitting them in anger should never be one of the options. Yet, it is something that many of us (parents) find ourselves doing.

Many experts recommend that parents instill discipline through non-violent means such as verbal warnings, time-outs, and revoking privileges. However, what happens when you have tried every possible method and it still doesn't get the desired result?

For some parents, hitting their children might seem like the only possible solution. But when is a good time? Perhaps when you're feeling festive? Of course not.

So, when is it ever justifiable? Never!


Violence Begets Violence

As parents, we are expected to lead by example. If you choose violence to resolve an issue, then your children may adopt the same behavior. Therefore, it is essential that you work towards finding a solution to make your child understand their mistake and correct their behavior non-violently.

Practical Tips to Incorporate Non-Violent Discipline

  • Establish clear routines for your child that involve things like homework, chores, meals, and bedtime.
  • Use non-violent punishments like time-outs or loss of privileges.
  • Reinforce positive behaviors through praise, encouragement, and rewards.
  • Listen to your child and take their opinions into consideration.
  • Be consistent with your discipline to avoid any confusion.

A Personal Anecdote - Roseanne Barr's Story

Roseanne Barr, actress, writer, and comedian, became notorious for hitting her children in anger. Her experiences growing up have been documented, and her childhood wasn't exactly smooth sailing.

According to her, she resorted to violence as a means to establish control over her children. But as time went by, she realized that this only mirrored the abuse she had faced from her own mother.

She then admitted to the National Parents' Resource Institute for Drug Education that hitting her children had an adverse effect on them. They became distant, unapproachable, and even developed anxiety. The situation led her to seek help and learn alternative methods to establish discipline and control


Conclusion

Violence should never be a solution to address the behavior of young children. As parents, it's our responsibility to establish discipline non-violently. In conclusion, by following simple tips like establishing routines, communicating effectively, and reinforcing positive behavior, we can raise our children to be well-behaved, confident, and respectful adults.

  • Establishing discipline non-violently is essential to raise well-behaved and confident children.
  • Violence begets more violence; if we hit our children, they may learn hitting is a way to solve problems.
  • Practical tips like establishing routines, non-violent punishments, and reinforcing positive behavior can help establish discipline without the need for violence.
