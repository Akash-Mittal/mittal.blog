Great Ambition: The Key to a Great Character



"I know men and I tell you that Jesus Christ is no mere man. Between Him and every other person in the world there is no possible term of comparison. Alexander, Caesar, Charlemagne, and I have founded empires. But on what did we rest the creation of our genius? Upon force. Jesus Christ founded His empire upon love; and at this hour millions of men would die for Him."

Napoleon Bonaparte

From the words of Napoleon Bonaparte, it is clear that ambition played a crucial role in shaping his character and achieving great success in life. However, as he rightly pointed out, ambition can lead to both good and bad outcomes depending on the principles that govern it. In this article, we will explore the concept of great ambition and how it can help one achieve success in the right way.

An Inspiring Story

Let me begin with an inspiring story that exemplifies the power of great ambition. It is the story of a young girl named Malala Yousafzai, who was born in Pakistan in 1997. Malala grew up in the Swat Valley, which was under the control of the Taliban, an extremist group that believed in suppressing the rights of women and girls. Despite this, Malala was determined to pursue her education and advocate for the rights of girls to go to school.

At the age of 11, Malala started a blog under a pseudonym for the BBC, where she spoke about the challenges faced by girls in getting an education. Her blog became popular and brought international attention to the issue. However, this also made her a target for the Taliban, who threatened to kill her. Despite the danger, Malala continued to speak out and advocate for girls' education.

In 2012, while returning from school, Malala was shot in the head by a Taliban gunman. She survived the attack and went on to become a global advocate for girls' education. She was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2014 at the age of 17, making her the youngest ever Nobel laureate. Malala's story shows us what can be achieved with great ambition, even in the face of extreme adversity.

The Importance of Principles

Malala's story also highlights the importance of principles in guiding our ambition. Malala's ambition was not driven by a desire for personal gain or fame. Instead, it was fueled by a belief in the importance of education for girls and a desire to make a positive impact in the world. This is a crucial distinction, as ambition without principled values can lead to destructive outcomes.

We can see this in the case of individuals like Adolf Hitler or Joseph Stalin, who were driven by a ruthless ambition for power at all costs, leading to unimaginable atrocities. On the other hand, we have leaders like Mahatma Gandhi or Nelson Mandela, whose ambition was guided by principles of non-violence and social justice, leading to the liberation of their nations.

Therefore, the principles that guide our ambition must be rooted in values that prioritize the greater good over personal gain. Principles such as integrity, compassion, empathy, and respect for others are essential in pursuing great ambition.

Let me share with you a personal anecdote that illustrates the power of great ambition. When I was in my early twenties, I had a burning desire to start my own business. I had a vision of creating a company that would make a positive impact in the world while also being financially successful.

However, I faced numerous challenges in realizing this vision. I lacked the resources, skills, and connections needed to start a business. Many people around me thought I was crazy for even considering such a venture. Despite these obstacles, my ambition kept me going.

Over the years, I worked hard to acquire the skills and knowledge needed to start a business. I networked with people who could help me, learned from the experiences of other entrepreneurs, and took risks when necessary. Finally, after several setbacks and failures, I managed to start my own company.

Today, my company is successful and has had a positive impact on the world. Looking back, I realize that it was my great ambition that kept me going during the tough times. However, it was also the principles that guided my ambition that ensured that my company was founded on values such as integrity, social responsibility, and ethical business practices.

Examples of Great Ambition

There are countless examples of individuals and organizations that have achieved great success through their ambition. Here are a few examples:

  • Elon Musk: He founded SpaceX, Tesla, SolarCity, and other companies with a vision of transforming the world through sustainable energy and space exploration.
  • Steve Jobs: He co-founded Apple and revolutionized the technology industry with his innovative products and marketing strategies.
  • Mother Teresa: She dedicated her life to serving the poor and sick, founding the Missionaries of Charity and earning a Nobel Peace Prize for her work.
  • Malcolm X: He was a Civil Rights leader who fought for the rights of African Americans and inspired a generation of activists.
  • Bill Gates: He co-founded Microsoft and used his wealth and influence to fund initiatives such as global health and education.

All of these individuals were driven by great ambition, but their principles and values ensured that their impact on the world was positive.

Conclusion

To summarize, great ambition is a powerful force that can drive individuals and organizations to achieve incredible things. However, the principles and values that guide this ambition are what determine whether its outcomes are good or bad. Therefore, as you pursue your own ambitions, remember to prioritize principles such as integrity, empathy, and compassion. Here are three practical tips to help you in this journey:

  1. Define your values and principles: Take the time to reflect on what matters most to you in life and what principles guide your decisions and actions.
  2. Set ambitious goals: Don't be afraid to dream big and set ambitious goals for yourself and your organization.
  3. Maintain integrity and empathy: Always stay true to your principles and values, and treat others with respect and empathy.
