2,000 Miles in a What?



An Inspiring Journey by Jane Smiley

When I came home for the summer after my first year of college, I told my mother that my best friend and I were driving to California. She laughed out loud - 2,000 miles in a what? Well, my best friend had an old Chevy. What could go wrong?

As a young adult, the prospect of embarking on such a journey can be daunting. But sometimes, when the road ahead appears uncertain, it is the decision to take a leap of faith and to embark on an adventure that can provide the most valuable life lessons. And so, with a thirst for adventure and a spirit of curiosity, my friend and I set out on the open road.

Lessons Learned

  1. The Importance of Planning

    2. While it's important not to get bogged down in the details, a basic plan is necessary. Researching potential routes, mapping out gas and food stops, and having a general sense of the amount of time the journey will take are crucial. In the end, the little things really do make a big difference.

  2. Expect the Unexpected

    3. No matter how much you plan, unexpected situations will arise. Whether it's a flat tire, an unexpected detour, or running low on gas in the middle of nowhere, it's important to remain calm and stay focused on the end goal. When unexpected challenges arise, it is our attitude and resilience that will determine how we respond.

  3. Memories to Last a Lifetime

    4. Looking back on that road trip, I am filled with a sense of nostalgia and gratitude. The memories we created on that journey have lasted a lifetime. Sometimes, it is the experiences outside of our comfort zones that provide the most long-lasting memories.

The Power of Adventure

My journey proved that sometimes the most valuable lessons in life are learned by taking risks and embracing the unknown. The open road provides endless opportunities for adventure and growth, and it is up to us to take advantage of them. So next time you're faced with a seemingly impossible journey, remember, with a little planning, a positive attitude, and a spirit of adventure, anything is possible.
