Prosperity - God's Will for Your Finances



It was a hot summer afternoon and the sun was beating down relentlessly on the dry soil of the maize fields. The farmer was weary and his pockets were empty. He had sown good seed and had done everything he could to ensure a good harvest, but the rains had failed to come. His crops were drying up and he was at the end of his rope. He sat under a tree and prayed, "God, why have you abandoned me? I am your faithful servant, yet you have allowed my crops to fail and my family to go hungry."

Just then, a stranger approached him. He was a well-dressed man with a pleasant smile. "Greetings, my friend," the stranger said. "I've been watching you and your struggle. I couldn't help but overhear your prayer. Why don't you tell me your story?"

The farmer poured out his heart to the stranger. When he had finished, the stranger said, "I believe that God wants you to prosper financially, to have plenty of money, to fulfill the destiny He has laid out for you." The farmer was taken aback. Was it really God's will for him to be wealthy? The stranger continued, "Yes, it is. But you have to do your part. You have to believe that God wants you to prosper and take action accordingly."

The farmer was hesitant. He had always been taught that wealth was sinful and that God's blessings were only for the spiritual realm. But the stranger's words resonated in his heart. Could it be that God really wanted him to prosper financially? He decided to take a leap of faith and put his trust in God's promises.

Years went by, and the farmer became one of the wealthiest men in the village. His crops flourished, his business thrived, and he was able to provide for his family and help others in need. He knew that he had fulfilled his destiny, and that God's will for his life was to prosper abundantly.

God's Will for Your Finances

Many people believe that God is not interested in their finances. They see money as a necessary evil, something that is needed to survive in this world, but not something that is important in the spiritual realm. However, Joel Osteen believes that God wants us to prosper financially, to have plenty of money, to fulfill the destiny He has laid out for us.

According to Joel, there are several reasons why God wants us to prosper financially:

  • God wants us to be a blessing to others. When we have abundance, we are able to help those in need and make a difference in the world.
  • God wants us to have the resources we need to fulfill our destiny. He has a purpose for our lives, and we need the means to carry it out.
  • God wants us to live in abundance. He does not want us to live in poverty, lack, or defeat. He wants us to have the best in life.

Examples of God's Blessings

Joel Osteen shares several examples of God's blessings in his own life.

"When I was younger, I used to work in my father's television repair shop. I would spend hours fixing broken televisions and radios. It was hard work, but I enjoyed it. One day, I felt a prompting from God to start my own business. I was hesitant at first because I had no experience in entrepreneurship, but I trusted that God had a plan for me.
"I started a small business selling Bibles and other Christian books. At first, it was slow going. But as I put my trust in God and worked hard, the business began to grow. Today, I am the senior pastor of the largest church in America, with millions of followers around the world. I never could have achieved this level of success without God's blessings."

Conclusion

To sum up, God wants us to prosper financially, to have plenty of money, to fulfill the destiny He has laid out for us. When we believe in God's promises and take action accordingly, we can achieve abundance in all areas of our lives. Here are three practical tips for achieving financial prosperity:

  1. Visualize your success. Imagine yourself living in abundance and fulfilling your God-given destiny. This will give you the motivation and confidence you need to take action.
  2. Take action. Start taking steps towards your financial goals, whether it's starting a business, investing in stocks, or pursuing a new career. Don't be afraid to take risks and try new things.
  3. Trust in God's timing. Sometimes, we may not see immediate results from our efforts. But if we keep the faith and trust in God's plan, we will eventually reap the rewards of our hard work.
