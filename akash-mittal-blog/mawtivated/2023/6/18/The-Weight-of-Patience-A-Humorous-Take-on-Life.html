The Weight of Patience - A Humorous Take on Life



An article by Emily Johns

One day, I was hurrying to work when I got stuck behind an elderly man at a traffic light. He was moving slowly and seemed to be struggling with his walker. Impatience was building within me as the light turned green, but instead of honking or trying to pass him, I took a deep breath and waited patiently until he made it across the street. As I drove away, I felt a sense of calm and satisfaction that I had probably never experienced before.

Patience is not merely the ability to wait, but the capacity to maintain a good attitude while waiting. This is a valuable life skill that John Updike playfully illustrates in his quote, which reads, "A healthy male adult bore consumes each year one and a half times his own weight in other people's patience."

The Author - John Updike

John Updike was an American novelist, short-story writer, poet, and literary critic. He was born in 1932 and died in 2009. Updike was best known for his "Rabbit" series of novels, which chronicles the life of a man named Harry "Rabbit" Angstrom over the course of several decades. He was also known for his sharp wit, keen observations of human behavior, and his ability to make readers both laugh and think.

Examples of Impatience in Everyday Life

  • Getting frustrated with slow internet speeds
  • Getting angry at traffic jams
  • Getting annoyed with long lines at the grocery store
  • Getting fed up with a coworker who takes too long to explain something
  • Getting impatient with a child who takes too long to tie their shoelaces

As someone who has struggled with impatience for most of my life, I can attest to the fact that it can cause unnecessary stress and tension in relationships, work environments, and daily life. However, I have also learned that cultivating patience can bring about a sense of peace and contentment that is difficult to achieve through any other means.

One way that I have cultivated patience is by practicing mindfulness meditation. It has taught me to become more aware of my thoughts and emotions, and has helped me to become less reactive to external stimuli. As a result, I am better able to deal with stress, anxiety, and impatience in a more constructive way.

Conclusion - 3 Key Takeaways:

  1. Impatience is a common human trait that can cause unnecessary stress and tension in daily life.
  2. Practicing mindfulness and being more aware of our thoughts and emotions can help us to cultivate patience and deal with stress in a more constructive way.
  3. By choosing to be patient in difficult situations, we can create a sense of calm and contentment that is difficult to achieve through any other means.
