Fighting for Liberty: The Harriet Tubman Story



Harriet Tubman, an African-American abolitionist, was born into slavery in Maryland in the early 1820s. She escaped to freedom in 1849, and then returned multiple times, risking her life to help others escape through the Underground Railroad. She was also a spy for the Union Army during the Civil War and an advocate for women's suffrage. Tubman faced immense challenges, but she remained steadfast in her pursuit of freedom, saying "I would fight for my liberty so long as my strength lasted, and if the time came for me to go, the Lord would let them take me."

Tubman's story is both inspiring and humbling. Her unwavering faith in God and her determination to fight for what was right, regardless of the personal cost, serves as a beacon of hope for all those who've experienced oppression or injustice. As we reflect on her life and legacy, here are three key lessons we can all learn from Harriet Tubman:

  • Believe in Yourself: Tubman believed in herself and her ability to overcome obstacles, despite being a slave and a woman in the 19th century. You too can believe in yourself and your ability to make a difference in the world.
  • Be Courageous: Tubman was fearless, even in the face of danger. You too can have courage, even when facing difficult or uncomfortable situations.
  • Never Give Up: Tubman never gave up, even in the face of setbacks or obstacles. You too can persevere, even when things get tough.
