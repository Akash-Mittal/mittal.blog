Why It's Important to Prioritize Time for Your Children



By Mary J. Blige

When I was growing up, my mother worked long hours to provide for our family. As a result, she didn't have much time to spend with us kids. However, she always made sure that whenever she was home, she was present and engaged with us. Even though we didn't have as much time with her as we would have liked, the time we did have was quality time.

As a mother myself, I understand the importance of spending time with your children. It's not just about being physically present, but about being emotionally present as well. As much as I would love to adopt more children, I know that I barely have time for my own children. To adopt more children and not have time for them would be poor parenting on my part.

The Importance of Prioritizing Time with Your Children

  1. Builds Strong Bonds: Spending time with your children helps to build strong emotional bonds between you and your child. It allows you to connect with your child, show them that you care, and create memories that will last a lifetime.
  2. Aids in Development: Time spent with your children aids in their development. It provides opportunities for learning and growth and can help to foster creativity and imagination.
  3. Creates a Sense of Security: Children who spend quality time with their parents feel more secure, confident, and loved. This can help to contribute to their overall wellbeing and happiness.

One of my favorite memories as a mother was when my son and I went on a trip to Hawaii together. We spent a whole week together, just the two of us, exploring the island and having fun. That trip brought us so much closer together and created a bond that I cherish to this day.

Another time, I remember being so busy with work that I missed one of my daughter's school plays. She was so disappointed that I couldn't make it, but I made it up to her by taking her out for a special mother-daughter day. We spent the whole day together doing fun activities and just enjoying each other's company. That day taught me that it's not about the quantity of time you spend with your children, but about the quality of time.

Examples

Here are some examples of activities you can do with your children to spend quality time together:

  • Going for a walk or bike ride
  • Baking together
  • Playing board games or card games
  • Watching a movie or TV show together
  • Having a picnic or BBQ
  • Going on a day trip or vacation together

In Conclusion

To be a good parent, it's important to prioritize time with your children. Being emotionally present and engaged with your children helps to build strong bonds, aids in their development, and creates a sense of security. Remember, it's not about the quantity of time you spend with your children, but about the quality of time.

  1. Make time for your children
  2. Be present and engaged with them
  3. Create memories that will last a lifetime
