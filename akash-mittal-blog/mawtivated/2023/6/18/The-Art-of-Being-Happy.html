The Art of Being Happy



How to Extract Happiness from Common Things

Inspiring Story

Once upon a time, there was a wise man named Henry Ward Beecher. He was a renowned clergyman, social reformer, and speaker who lived in the 19th century. He believed that the art of being happy lies in the power of extracting happiness from common things. It's not about accumulating wealth, possessions, or fame, but about finding joy in the simple pleasures of life.

One day, a young woman came to him and asked for his advice on how to be happy. She told him that she was always unhappy and felt like something was missing in her life. Henry Ward Beecher smiled and said, "My dear, happiness is not something that you can find outside of yourself. It's already within you. You just have to discover it."

He then took out a small rock from his pocket and said, "See this stone? It's just an ordinary stone that you may find on the street. But to me, it's a treasure. Whenever I feel unhappy or stressed, I hold this stone in my hand and feel its texture, weight, and shape. I imagine all the wonders and mysteries of nature that led to its creation, and I feel grateful and blessed to be alive."

The young woman was surprised and asked, "But how can a stone make you happy?" Henry Ward Beecher replied, "It's not the stone that makes me happy. It's my attitude and perspective that make me happy. I choose to see the beauty and goodness in the world instead of the ugliness and evil. That's the secret of happiness."

Background of Henry Ward Beecher

Henry Ward Beecher was born in 1813 in Litchfield, Connecticut, to a prominent religious family. His father was a famous preacher and his sister was the author of the famous novel Uncle Tom's Cabin. He followed in his father's footsteps and became a minister in his early twenties. He was known for his persuasive and entertaining speeches that attracted large crowds of people. He was also a passionate advocate for abolition, women's suffrage, and temperance.

Despite his successes, he suffered from personal tragedies and scandals that tarnished his reputation. His daughter died from scarlet fever, his son was involved in a sex scandal, and he himself was accused of adultery. However, he never lost his faith, humor, or optimism. He continued to preach and write until his death in 1887.

Examples of Extracting Happiness from Common Things

  • Enjoying a cup of coffee or tea in the morning and savoring its aroma and taste.
  • Taking a walk in nature and appreciating its sights, sounds, and smells.
  • Listening to music or singing a song that uplifts your mood and inspires your soul.
  • Reading a book or watching a movie that stimulates your mind and imagination.
  • Playing with a pet or a child that brings joy and laughter to your heart.
  • Cooking a meal or baking a dessert that satisfies your hunger and creativity.
  • Talking to a friend or a family member that connects you with others and deepens your relationships.
  • Doing a hobby or a sport that challenges your skills and enriches your life.
  • Helping someone in need or volunteering for a cause that gives you a sense of purpose and fulfillment.
  • Being grateful for the blessings and opportunities that you have and expressing your appreciation to others.
"Happiness is not a destination, but a journey. It's not a goal that you achieve, but a state of mind that you cultivate. It's not a result of external circumstances, but an internal attitude that you choose."

Conclusion

To sum up, the art of being happy lies in the power of extracting happiness from common things. It's not about the quantity or quality of the things you have, but about the value and meaning you give to them. It's not about the past or the future, but about the present moment and the positive emotions you experience. It's not about the external world, but about the internal world of your thoughts, feelings, and beliefs.

If you want to be happy, you need to:

  1. Cultivate a positive attitude and perspective.
  2. Develop a sense of gratitude and appreciation.
  3. Engage in activities and relationships that bring you joy and fulfillment.

By doing so, you will discover the happiness that is already within you and share it with others. Remember, happiness is not a one-time event, but a lifelong process. Enjoy the journey and make the most of it!
