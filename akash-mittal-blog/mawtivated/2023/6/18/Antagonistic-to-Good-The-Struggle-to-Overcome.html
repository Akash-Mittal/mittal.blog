Antagonistic to Good: The Struggle to Overcome



"There must always remain something that is antagonistic to good." - Plato

Everyone has their own definition of what is good, but one thing is for sure: there will always be obstacles and challenges that prevent us from achieving what we want. It's not always easy to overcome these antagonists, but we must try if we want to lead fulfilling lives.


An Inspiring Story

The story of a young woman who lived in poverty all her life and dreamed of becoming a doctor only to be told that she was not good enough because of her social status. She refused to accept this as a hindrance to her dream and worked hard to prove her worth. She studied tirelessly, worked hard and achieved her goal of becoming a doctor. She is now an inspiration to many, and her struggle shows us that nothing is impossible if we are willing to work for it.


Author - Plato

Plato was a philosopher who lived in Ancient Greece. He believed in the power of reason and learning, and his ideas have influenced many people throughout history. Plato believed that there was a perfect world of ideas, and that this world could be attained through reason and knowledge.


Examples

There are many examples of things that are antagonistic to good, such as:

  • Negative thinking: when we constantly doubt ourselves and our abilities, it becomes difficult to achieve our goals.
  • Procrastination: when we put things off, we are less likely to achieve what we want. We need to set goals and work towards them every day.
  • Perfectionism: when we strive for perfection, we often become disappointed and discouraged. We need to learn when to let go and accept that we are human.

Conclusion

In order to overcome the antagonists that prevent us from achieving what we want, we must:

  1. Believe in ourselves: it all starts with having confidence in our abilities and trusting that we can achieve what we want.
  2. Take action: we need to set goals and work towards them every day. This means setting priorities and managing our time effectively so that we can make progress towards our goals.
  3. Learn to adapt: sometimes life throws us curveballs, but we need to be resilient and adapt to change. This means being open to new perspectives and approaches, and being willing to learn from our mistakes.
