Trusting Yourself and Avoiding the Trap of Luck



Have you ever felt like you were just waiting for luck to come your way? Maybe you were hoping to win the lottery or get a lucky break in your career. Unfortunately, relying on luck isn't a reliable strategy for reaching your goals. As author E. W. Howe once said,

"When a man has no reason to trust himself, he trusts in luck."

So, how can you start trusting yourself and avoid relying on luck? Let's explore some practical tips and examples.

The Power of Trusting Yourself

Trusting yourself is a crucial ingredient for success. When you trust yourself, you make better decisions and take actions that lead to better outcomes. On the other hand, when you doubt yourself, you hesitate and miss opportunities.

One story that demonstrates the power of self-trust is that of entrepreneur Sara Blakely, founder of Spanx. When Blakely first started her company, she had no experience in fashion, retail, or manufacturing. Despite this lack of expertise, she believed in her idea and her ability to figure things out. She trusted herself to learn what she needed to know and make her vision a reality.

Blakely's self-trust paid off. Today, Spanx is a billion-dollar company and Blakely is one of the most successful self-made female billionaires in the world. Her story shows us that when we trust ourselves, we can achieve incredible things.

Avoiding the Trap of Luck

Now, let's talk about why relying on luck is a trap. Luck is unpredictable and out of our control. We can't count on luck to make our dreams come true. Rather than waiting for luck, we need to take action and create our own opportunities.

A great example of this is author J.K. Rowling. Before she became famous for the Harry Potter series, Rowling was a struggling single mother living on government benefits. She didn't have a publisher or an agent, and she faced dozens of rejections from publishers. However, she didn't give up. She trusted her writing skills and kept working on her manuscript.

Eventually, Rowling's persistence paid off. She secured a publishing deal and the Harry Potter series became a worldwide phenomenon. Rowling didn't rely on luck to become successful; she trusted herself and took action.

Tips for Trusting Yourself

So, how can you start trusting yourself and avoid relying on luck? Here are some practical tips:

  • Identify your strengths and talents. What are you good at? What do you enjoy doing?
  • Set realistic goals that align with your values and passions.
  • Take action and make progress towards your goals, even if it's small steps at a time.
  • Embrace failure as a learning opportunity. When things don't go according to plan, reflect on what went wrong and what you can do differently next time.
  • Surround yourself with supportive people who believe in you. Seek out mentors or peers who can offer guidance and feedback.

Remember, trusting yourself is a process that takes time and practice. Be patient with yourself and celebrate your progress along the way. With self-trust and action, you can achieve incredible things.

Conclusion

In summary, relying on luck is a trap that can prevent us from achieving our goals. Instead of waiting for luck, we need to trust ourselves and take action. Here are three key takeaways:

  1. Self-trust is a crucial ingredient for success.
  2. Don't rely on luck; take action and create your own opportunities.
  3. Practical tips for trusting yourself include identifying your strengths, setting realistic goals, and embracing failure as a learning opportunity.

Now, go forth and trust yourself to achieve your dreams!
