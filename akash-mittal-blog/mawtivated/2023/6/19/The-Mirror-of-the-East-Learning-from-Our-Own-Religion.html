The Mirror of the East: Learning from Our Own Religion



A Tale of Two Monks

Once upon a time, there were two monks who lived in a temple at the top of a mountain. They were both wise and learned in the ways of their religion, but one was a Westerner and the other was from the East. One day, they decided to exchange teachings and explore the similarities and differences in their practices.

The Westerner monk was eager to learn from the East and asked the Eastern monk about his traditions and rituals. The Eastern monk smiled and said, "My friend, you do not need to go East to find what you seek. Look within your own heart and you will find the same wisdom and teachings that you seek in the East."

The Westerner monk was initially puzzled by this response, but upon reflection, he realized that the Eastern monk was right. He had been looking outside himself for answers when the teachings he sought were already within him.

The Mirror of the East

Matthew Fox, a Christian theologian and author, once said, "I am a Westerner. We're not going to change the West by going East. The East has a lot to teach us, but essentially it's like a mirror, saying, hey, can't you see what's here in your own religion, what are you, stupid?"

What Fox means by this is that we often overlook the teachings and traditions within our own religions and cultures, thinking that we need to look elsewhere for answers. However, by exploring our own teachings and practices, we can gain greater insight into our own experiences and find the wisdom we seek.

For example, many cultures have traditions of meditation or prayer that help individuals connect with their inner selves and a higher power. By examining and practicing these traditions within our own culture, we can cultivate a deeper sense of spirituality and self-awareness.

Similarly, many religions have teachings of compassion, forgiveness, and loving-kindness. By living these values in our daily lives, we can create a more peaceful and harmonious world.

Examples of Learning From Our Own Religion

  • Christianity has a rich tradition of contemplative prayer, which can help individuals connect with God and cultivate inner peace.
  • Buddhism emphasizes the practice of mindfulness, which can help individuals become more present and connected to their experiences.
  • Islam encourages acts of charity, which can help individuals cultivate compassion and kindness towards others.

By exploring and practicing these teachings within our own lives, we can gain greater insight into our own experiences and become more compassionate, loving, and self-aware individuals.

Conclusion

  1. Remember that the teachings and traditions you seek may already be within your own religion or culture.
  2. Take time to explore and practice these teachings within your own life.
  3. By doing so, you can gain greater insight into your own experiences and become a more compassionate, loving, and self-aware individual.
