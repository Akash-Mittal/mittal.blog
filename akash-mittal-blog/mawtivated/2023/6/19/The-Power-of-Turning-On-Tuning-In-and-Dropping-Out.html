The Power of Turning On, Tuning In, and Dropping Out



When I was growing up, I was always told that success in life meant going to college, getting a good job, and climbing the corporate ladder. And for a time, I believed it. I worked hard, got good grades, and landed a job at a prestigious firm. But the more I worked, the more I realized that this wasn't what I wanted out of life. I wasn't happy, I wasn't fulfilled, and I certainly wasn't taking my nervous system, sense organs, and energy process seriously.

So I decided to take a different path. I turned on, tuned in, and dropped out of the corporate world. I started my own business, traveled the world, and focused on my own personal growth and development. And let me tell you, it has been the best decision I ever made.

The Story of Timothy Leary

"My advice to people today is as follows: if you take the game of life seriously, if you take your nervous system seriously, if you take your sense organs seriously, if you take the energy process seriously, you must turn on, tune in, and drop out."

These words were famously spoken by Timothy Leary, a psychologist and writer who became a countercultural icon in the 1960s. Leary was a firm believer in the power of consciousness-expanding drugs like LSD, and he saw them as a way to break free from the constraints of society and explore new realms of consciousness.

Of course, Leary's ideas were controversial. He was arrested numerous times for drug-related charges, and his philosophy was seen by many as a threat to the social order. But despite this, Leary remains an important figure in the history of psychedelic culture, and his words continue to inspire people to this day.

The Benefits of Turning On, Tuning In, and Dropping Out

So what does it mean to turn on, tune in, and drop out? At its core, this philosophy is about rejecting the status quo and embracing a more authentic and fulfilling way of life. Here are just a few of the benefits that come with adopting this mindset:

1. Increased Creativity

When you turn on, tune in, and drop out, you free your mind from the constraints of convention. This allows you to tap into your own innate creativity and come up with ideas and solutions that you might never have thought of before.

2. Greater Fulfillment

By tuning into your own inner wisdom and desires, you can live a life that truly feels fulfilling. Instead of following a predetermined path laid out by society, you create your own path and chart your own course.

3. Enhanced Awareness

When you take your nervous system, sense organs, and energy process seriously, you become more attuned to the world around you. You see things more clearly, feel things more deeply, and experience a heightened sense of awareness overall.

Examples of Turning On, Tuning In, and Dropping Out

Of course, turning on, tuning in, and dropping out doesn't necessarily mean dropping everything and moving to a commune in the woods (although it could!). Here are a few examples of how this philosophy can manifest in everyday life:

  • Taking up a creative hobby like painting, writing, or woodworking
  • Traveling to new places and immersing yourself in different cultures
  • Meditating or practicing yoga to cultivate inner peace and mindfulness
  • Starting your own business or pursuing a non-traditional career path
  • Volunteering or getting involved with a cause you're passionate about

Conclusion: How to Turn On, Tune In, and Drop Out

So if you're feeling unfulfilled or stuck in your life, consider turning on, tuning in, and dropping out. Here's how:

  1. Experiment. Try new things, explore different paths, and follow your intuition.
  2. Connect. Surround yourself with people who share your values and encourage your growth.
  3. Reflect. Take time to be still, listen to your inner voice, and focus on what truly matters to you.

With these three steps, you can start to embrace this powerful philosophy and unlock your true potential.
