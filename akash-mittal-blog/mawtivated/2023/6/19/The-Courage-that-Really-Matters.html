The Courage that Really Matters



The Story of a Young Girl

When Sarah was diagnosed with leukemia, she was only 8 years old. Her parents were devastated, but they decided to fight for her life, no matter what. Over the next few years, Sarah went through grueling chemotherapy sessions, countless hospital visits, and numerous setbacks. But she never gave up hope.

Her strength and courage inspired everyone around her. She never complained or felt sorry for herself. Instead, she focused on the present moment and found joy and meaning in small things, like drawing, playing with her friends, and listening to music. She showed us that the only courage that really matters is the one that gets you through each moment, one day at a time.

What is Real Courage?

Courage means different things to different people. For some, it's the ability to face danger or risk, like firefighters, soldiers, or police officers. For others, it's the willingness to stand up for their beliefs or values, like activists, leaders, or whistleblowers. These forms of courage are certainly admirable and necessary.

However, there is another kind of courage that is often overlooked or undervalued: the courage to deal with everyday challenges, struggles, and hardships. This kind of courage is not glamorous or heroic, but it's essential for our well-being and growth. It's the courage to:

  • Face your fears and insecurities.
  • Ask for help when you need it.
  • Admit your mistakes and learn from them.
  • Forgive yourself and others.
  • Take responsibility for your life and choices.

"Courage doesn't always roar. Sometimes courage is the quiet voice at the end of the day saying, 'I will try again tomorrow.'" - Mary Anne Radmacher

If you think about it, most of our daily challenges and struggles require some degree of courage. It takes courage to get out of bed in the morning and face the day, especially when you're dealing with pain, grief, or depression. It takes courage to reach out to someone when you feel lonely, isolated, or overwhelmed. It takes courage to keep going when you feel like giving up, whether it's in your work, your relationships, or your personal growth.

So, the question is not whether you have courage or not, but how you can cultivate and strengthen your courage in the face of adversity. Here are some tips:

Tips for Building Everyday Courage

  1. Practice mindfulness and self-awareness. By paying attention to your thoughts, feelings, and sensations, you can better understand your fears, doubts, and limitations, and challenge them with compassion and clarity.
  2. Cultivate a growth mindset. Instead of seeing yourself as fixed and unchangeable, see yourself as capable of learning, growing, and adapting to new challenges and experiences.
  3. Build a support network. Surround yourself with people who encourage, challenge, and inspire you. Share your struggles and successes with them, and ask for their feedback and advice.
  4. Maintain a healthy lifestyle. Take care of your physical, emotional, and mental health by eating well, exercising regularly, sleeping enough, and practicing relaxation techniques, such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing.
  5. Set realistic goals and take action. Break down your goals into small, achievable steps, and take consistent action towards them. Celebrate your progress and learn from your setbacks.

Conclusion: The Power of Small Steps

Being courageous is not an all-or-nothing game. It's a process of taking small steps towards your goals and values, even when you feel scared, uncertain, or uncomfortable. It's the willingness to embrace the present moment, accept yourself as you are, and trust yourself to handle whatever comes your way.

Remember, the only courage that really matters is the kind that gets you from one moment to the next. Whether you're facing a life-changing crisis or a mundane task, you have the inner strength and determination to rise above your fears and doubts and take action towards a better life.
