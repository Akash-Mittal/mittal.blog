The George Best Story: Squandering Money on Booze, Birds, and Fast Cars



Introduction

When we think of the greatest football players of all time, names like Pele, Maradona, and Messi come to mind. But there was a player who was not only a legendary footballer but also a cultural icon in his own right. I'm talking about the one and only George Best - the Belfast Boy.

The George Best Story

George Best was a football superstar in the 1960s and 1970s, but he was also famous for his flamboyant lifestyle. He once famously said, "I spent a lot of money on booze, birds and fast cars. The rest I just squandered."

Best was never the one to shy away from attention, he sported the latest hairstyles and was always dressed in the most fashionable clothes. He lived life to the fullest, spending money on expensive cars, designer clothes, and lavish parties. He had an insatiable appetite for luxury.

Booze

Best was known for his love of booze. He was often seen drinking heavily in nightclubs, pubs, and hotels. It's estimated that he drank around 40 pints of beer a day and at one point drank 14 bottles of champagne on a night out in London.

His alcoholism started to seriously affect his performance on the pitch, and he missed training sessions and matches due to his drinking habits. Eventually, he was fired from Manchester United because of his drinking.

Birds

Best was a ladies' man, and his charisma and good looks attracted many women throughout his career. He dated models, actresses, and even Miss World herself. He was often seen partying with beautiful women, and his reputation as a playboy grew with each passing day.

His relationships often ended badly, and he married and divorced twice. Women were one of his weaknesses, and he spent a substantial amount of his earnings on them.

Fast Cars

Best was a petrol-head, and his love for fast cars was well-known. He owned several sports cars including a Jensen Interceptor, an Aston Martin DB6, and a Ferrari Dino. He loved the thrill of driving and often raced his cars on the track.

However, his love for cars often proved deadly. He was involved in several car accidents, and one of them left him with a broken leg. His love for cars also meant that he spent a considerable amount of his money on them.

Lessons Learned

George Best's life was a cautionary tale of how not to squander your money. However, his life can teach us a thing or two about personal finance. Here are some lessons we can learn from the Belfast Boy:

  • Live within your means: Best's lavish lifestyle caused him to overspend his earnings. He didn't save for the future and ended up broke towards the end of his life. Living within your means is important if you want to have a secure financial future.
  • Avoid vices: Best's addiction to alcohol and women had a negative impact on his life. His drinking habits affected his performance on the pitch, and his relationships suffered because of his philandering behaviour. Vices like smoking, drinking, and gambling can have a serious impact on your financial well-being.
  • Invest and save: Best didn't invest his money, and he didn't save for the future. Investing your money in stocks, bonds, and mutual funds can provide you with a steady income stream in the long run. Additionally, having an emergency fund can help you weather unexpected financial storms.

Conclusion

In conclusion, George Best may have squandered a considerable amount of his money on booze, birds, and fast cars, but his life provides us with important lessons about personal finance. We can learn from his mistakes and avoid the same pitfalls he faced. By living within our means, avoiding vices, and investing and saving our money, we can build a secure financial future for ourselves.
