The Most Important Quality in a Leader: Acknowledgement



Leadership is an essential element of success. From business to personal life, we are all leaders in one way or another. However, what distinguishes a great leader from an average one is the quality of being acknowledged as a leader. Andre Maurois once said, "The most important quality in a leader is that of being acknowledged as such. All leaders whose fitness is questioned are clearly lacking in force."

A Story of Leadership

There is a famous story of leadership that is often shared among motivational speakers. In the early 1900s, a young Winston Churchill was asked to speak at his school's assembly. As he walked to the podium, the headmaster whispered to him, "Remember, young man, you are representing the noblest institution in the country. Don't let us down."

Churchill, only 12 years old at the time, proceeded to deliver an inspiring and powerful speech that left everyone in awe. He was acknowledged as a leader from that moment forward, and his influence only grew as he aged. Churchill became one of the greatest leaders in history, and his legacy still inspires us today.

I have had the privilege of working with many leaders throughout my career. Some were acknowledged as such, while others were not. I noticed a distinct difference in the way their teams responded to them. The leaders who were acknowledged as such had teams that were more motivated, productive, and loyal. On the other hand, the leaders who were not acknowledged struggled to gain the respect and support of their teams.

One case that stands out to me was a leader who was struggling to get his team to achieve their goals. He was smart, experienced, and passionate about his work, but he was not acknowledged as a leader. He was constantly challenged by his team, and they often ignored his instructions. I spoke with him and suggested that he work on building his credibility with his team. He took my advice and started to involve his team in decision making, listen to their concerns, and reward their successes. Within a few months, his team started to perform better than ever before.

Examples of Acknowledged Leaders

There are many examples of acknowledged leaders who have made a significant impact on our world. Here are just a few:

  • Nelson Mandela
  • Steve Jobs
  • Mother Teresa
  • Martin Luther King Jr.
  • Elon Musk

What do all of these leaders have in common? They were acknowledged as leaders by their followers. They had a force that was undeniable and inspired those around them.

Conclusion

In conclusion, being acknowledged as a leader is the most important quality in a leader. Without acknowledgement, a leader lacks the force to inspire and lead their team to success. To be acknowledged as a leader, one must focus on building their credibility, involving their team in decision making, listening to their concerns, and rewarding their successes. In summary, to be a great leader, one must:

  1. Focus on building credibility
  2. Involve the team in decision making
  3. Listen to their concerns and reward their successes
