The Road to Success: How to Overcome Your Fear of Creating Your Own Parts



Author: Kristen Wiig

Fear is a natural human emotion that can either propel you towards success or hold you back from achieving greatness. As an actress and writer, I know what it means to struggle with the fear of taking risks and trying new things - especially when it comes to creating my own parts.

In this article, I'll share my own journey towards overcoming fear and offer practical tips and advice for anyone looking to do the same.

My Story

Like many aspiring actors, I started my career by auditioning for other people's projects. I had some successes, but I also had a lot of rejections - and those rejections left me feeling discouraged and insecure.

It wasn't until I started writing and performing my own sketches that I began to feel a sense of control over my career. I realized that I didn't have to wait for someone else to give me an opportunity - I could create my own.

"I wish I had less fear about creating my own parts."

However, the fear of failure still loomed over me. What if my sketches weren't funny enough? What if nobody liked them? It took a lot of courage to put myself out there and try something new, but I knew that the potential rewards were worth it.

Why You Should Create Your Own Parts

Creating your own parts can be scary, but it's also incredibly empowering. Here are just a few of the benefits:

  • You get to showcase your unique talents and perspective
  • You have control over your own career trajectory
  • You can open doors for yourself and others by creating opportunities

Practical Tips for Overcoming Fear

If you're ready to take the plunge and start creating your own parts, here are a few tips that can help you overcome your fear:

  1. Start small. Don't feel like you have to write a full-length play or screenplay right away. Start with a short sketch or monologue that showcases your strengths.
  2. Collaborate. Don't think that you have to do everything on your own. Find other actors, writers, or directors who share your vision and can help bring your ideas to life.
  3. Don't get discouraged by rejection. Not everything you create will be a hit, and that's okay. Keep pushing yourself to try new things and don't let rejection hold you back.

Conclusion

Creating your own parts can be scary, but it's also incredibly rewarding. By taking control of your career and showcasing your unique talents, you can open doors for yourself and others. Remember to start small, collaborate, and don't get discouraged by rejection. With perseverance and hard work, you can overcome your fear and create something truly great.
