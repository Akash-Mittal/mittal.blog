Trusting Your Director - A Lesson from Ewan McGregor



There is a lot to be said for trust in the workplace. Trusting your team members, trusting your boss, trusting your instincts can all lead to greater success and personal fulfillment in your work life. And yet, when it comes to trusting your director on a film set, it takes on a whole new meaning. This is something that actor Ewan McGregor has recently spoken out about, and his thoughts on the matter are insightful and inspiring.

Ewan McGregor's Story

Ewan McGregor is a Scottish actor who has had an incredible career spanning over three decades. He has starred in iconic films such as Trainspotting, Moulin Rouge!, and the Star Wars prequel trilogy. Despite his success, McGregor is known for being grounded and humble, especially when it comes to his work on set.

In a recent interview, McGregor explained that he has always trusted his directors and their vision for the film he is working on. He firmly believes that it is not his job as an actor to try and alter their style or vision. Instead, his job is to bring his best to the role he is given and to work collaboratively with the director and the rest of the team.

"I think it's really important to trust your director. They are the person who has the vision, who has written the script, who knows how they want the film to look. It's not my job to try and alter that. My job is to give myself over to them and give them everything I have."

For McGregor, this trust extends beyond just the director. He believes in trusting the entire team on set, from the cast to the crew. Everyone has a specific job to do, and when everyone is working together towards a common goal, amazing things can happen.

The Benefits of Trusting Your Director

Trusting your director may seem like a small thing, but it can have a huge impact on your work and the success of the project. Here are just a few of the benefits:

  • Greater Collaboration: When you trust your director, you are more likely to work collaboratively with them and the rest of the team. This can lead to better communication, better ideas, and a better end result.
  • Less Stress: When you don't have to worry about whether or not you are doing the right thing, it can be a huge relief. Trusting your director allows you to focus on your role and not feel like you have to micromanage everything.
  • Increased Creativity: When you trust your director, you are more likely to take risks and push boundaries creatively. This can lead to some truly amazing work.

The Takeaways

So, what can we learn from Ewan McGregor's philosophy on trusting your director? Here are three key takeaways:

  1. Give Yourself Over: When you are working on a project, give yourself over to the director and their vision. Trust that they know what they are doing, and focus on your role in the project.
  2. Collaborate: Work collaboratively with the rest of the team, including the director. This can lead to better communication and more creative work.
  3. Embrace the Unknown: When you trust your director, you are more willing to take risks and try new things. Embrace the unknown and see where it takes you.
