Respect and Tolerance: Insights from Charles de Gaulle



An article on the importance of respecting those who resist us, even if we cannot tolerate them

An Inspiring Story

Imagine you are a leader of a company that is struggling to keep up with the competition. You come up with a bold new strategy that you believe could turn things around, but many of your colleagues are resistant to change and are hesitant to embrace your vision. You could easily dismiss them as obstacles to progress and keep pushing forward, but instead, you take the time to listen to their concerns and address them one by one. Through patience, persistence, and empathy, you are able to win over your team and successfully implement your plan, ultimately leading your company to greater success.

The Wisdom of Charles de Gaulle

Charles de Gaulle was a French general and statesman who led his country through World War II and played a key role in shaping post-war Europe. He was known for his boldness, his intelligence, and his unwavering commitment to his beliefs. He was also a man who understood the value of respect, even in the face of opposition. As he once famously said:

"I respect only those who resist me, but I cannot tolerate them."

What does de Gaulle mean by this statement? Essentially, he was saying that he valued people who were willing to challenge him and offer different perspectives, but that he could not stand people who were completely closed-minded and unwilling to listen. This is an important distinction, particularly for leaders who want to encourage creativity, innovation, and open dialogue within their organizations.

Examples of Respectful Tolerance in Action

One example of respectful tolerance in action comes from the world of medicine. When doctors encounter patients who are hesitant to follow their recommended treatment plans, the temptation can be to dismiss them as noncompliant or difficult. However, a more effective approach is to take the time to understand the patient's concerns and work with them to find a solution that works for both parties. This might involve modifying the treatment plan or involving the patient in the decision-making process. By showing respect for the patient's wants and needs, the doctor can establish a relationship of trust and ultimately improve the patient's health outcomes.

Another example comes from the world of politics. In the United States, there is often a great deal of hostility between members of different political parties. However, some politicians have been able to bridge this divide by engaging in civil discourse and actively listening to opposing viewpoints. One such example is the late Senator John McCain, who famously reached across the aisle to work with members of the opposing party on key issues such as immigration reform and campaign finance reform. McCain's ability to respect and tolerate those who disagreed with him made him a highly respected and effective legislator, even among those who did not share his political affiliation.

Conclusion: Three Key Takeaways

  1. Respect begets respect. If we want others to respect us, we must first show them respect, even if we disagree with them.
  2. Tolerance does not equal capitulation. We can tolerate the views and opinions of others without giving up our own principles or compromising our goals. In fact, being willing to listen to opposing viewpoints can help us strengthen our own arguments and make stronger decisions.
  3. Patience and persistence are key. Building relationships of trust and understanding takes time and effort. It requires us to be patient, empathetic, and willing to put in the work to communicate effectively with those who disagree with us.
