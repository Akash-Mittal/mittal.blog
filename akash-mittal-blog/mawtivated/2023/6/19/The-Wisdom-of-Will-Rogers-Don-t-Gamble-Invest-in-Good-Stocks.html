The Wisdom of Will Rogers: Don't Gamble, Invest in Good Stocks



As a vaudeville performer, radio personality, and Hollywood actor in the early 1900s, Will Rogers was a household name. But he was also a savvy investor who made his fortune in the stock market. His famous quote, "Don't gamble, take all your savings and buy some good stock and hold it till it goes up, then sell it. If it don't go up, don't buy it," has stood the test of time and remains a valuable lesson for investors today.

An Inspiring Story

Will Rogers wasn't born into wealth. He grew up in rural Oklahoma and dropped out of school in the 10th grade to become a cowboy. However, his natural charisma and talent for entertaining people led him to a successful career in show business.

Despite his fame, Rogers remained grounded and practical. He believed in the value of hard work and smart investing, and he was frugal with his money. In fact, he once joked that he was more proud of the money he didn't lose than the money he made.

Why You Should Invest in Good Stocks

Will Rogers' advice to invest in good stocks and hold onto them until they go up is still relevant today. The stock market can be volatile, but investing wisely and strategically can lead to long-term financial gains.

  • When you invest in a good stock, you're buying a share of ownership in a company that has proven to be successful.
  • Over time, the value of that company (and your share) is likely to increase.
  • By holding onto your shares, you avoid transaction fees and the temptation to make impulsive decisions.

Of course, not every stock will go up. That's why Rogers' advice to only buy a stock if it has a strong likelihood of increasing in value is essential. By doing your research and tracking market trends, you can make informed decisions about which stocks to invest in.

Practical Tips for Investing in Stocks

If you're new to investing, the stock market can be overwhelming. Here are some practical tips to keep in mind:

  • Do your research before investing in a stock. Look for a strong history of growth and stability.
  • Diversify your portfolio to minimize risk. Invest in stocks across different industries and sectors.
  • Stay patient and avoid the temptation to make impulsive decisions based on short-term market fluctuations.

The Bottom Line

Will Rogers' advice to invest in good stocks is just as relevant today as it was when he first uttered those words. By doing your research, diversifying your portfolio, and staying patient, you can make smart investments that will pay off in the long run.

Conclusion:

  1. Only invest in stocks that have a strong likelihood of increasing in value. Do your research and track market trends to make informed decisions.
  2. Diversify your portfolio to minimize risk. Investing in stocks across different industries and sectors can help protect your investments.
  3. Stay patient and avoid impulsive decisions based on short-term market fluctuations. By holding onto your shares, you increase your chances of long-term financial gains.
