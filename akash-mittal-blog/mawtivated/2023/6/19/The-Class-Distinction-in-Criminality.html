The Class Distinction in Criminality



Have you ever noticed that the richest people aren't usually counted as criminals? It's because criminality is a class distinction, not an ethical problem. Orson Welles said, "Criminals are never very amusing. It's because they're failures. Those who make real money aren't counted as criminals." Let's dive deeper into this issue.

An Inspiring Story

When Steve Jobs first started Apple, he realized that he couldn't make it alone. So, he teamed up with Steve Wozniak, who was a great programmer. Instead of hiring him, Jobs offered Wozniak 50% of their future profits. That was a smart move because Jobs knew that he needed Wozniak's skills to succeed.

The Class Distinction in Criminality

In many societies, people who are born into privileged families often have an easier time becoming successful. People from poorer backgrounds, on the other hand, have to work harder and may resort to criminal activities to get ahead. This is why criminality is a class distinction. The rich have access to resources and connections that the poor don't have, which can make a huge difference in terms of success.

and Case Studies

  • John was born into a wealthy family and went to a prestigious university. After graduation, he was given a high-paying job at his father's company. He eventually became the CEO and made millions of dollars. He was never in trouble with the law because he had family connections that protected him.
  • Maria, on the other hand, was born into a poor family in a developing country. She had to work multiple jobs just to get by. She eventually started selling drugs to make ends meet. She was caught and sentenced to life in prison. She didn't have the resources or connections that could have kept her out of jail.

Examples of Class Distinction in Criminality

Here are some famous examples of class distinction in criminality:

  • Bernie Madoff - A wealthy stockbroker who orchestrated the largest Ponzi scheme in history, taking billions of dollars from his clients. He was sentenced to 150 years in prison.
  • Martha Stewart - A successful businesswoman who was convicted of insider trading. She served five months in prison and continued her career after her release.
  • Phil Spector - A famous music producer who was convicted of murder. He came from a wealthy family and was able to hire a team of expensive lawyers.

Conclusion

  1. The rich have access to resources and connections that can prevent them from getting in trouble with the law.
  2. The poor are more likely to resort to criminal activities to get ahead because they don't have the same opportunities.
  3. Criminality is a class distinction, not an ethical problem.

If you are facing financial difficulties, try to find legal ways to improve your situation. Consider getting a second job or starting a side hustle. If you are struggling with addiction, seek help from professionals. Remember that breaking the law is not the answer.
