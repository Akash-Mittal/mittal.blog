The Rise of Faith Schools in a Heathen Nation



Do you believe in miracles? I do. I believe the story of Megan Kerr, who despite the odds, became the first person in her family to go to university. She was raised in a working-class family in one of the poorest areas in the country. Her parents struggled to make ends meet, and her school lacked the resources to provide her with the education she needed to succeed.

However, Megan did not give up hope. She attended a faith school, which provided her with the support and guidance she needed to achieve her dreams. The teachers at her school instilled in her a sense of purpose and ambition, and encouraged her to pursue her passions. Through hard work and dedication, she went on to study law at a prestigious university, and is now a successful barrister.

Megan's story is not unique. There are countless others like her who have benefited from attending faith schools. However, this is not a popular view among some, including the author Polly Toynbee, who wrote:

But how odd that in this heathen nation of empty pews, where churches' bare, ruined choirs are converted into luxury loft living, a Labour government - yes, a Labour government - is deliberately creating a huge expansion of faith schools.

While Toynbee's perspective is understandable, it fails to acknowledge the benefits that faith schools can provide to students who might otherwise fall through the cracks. In this article, we will delve deeper into the rise of faith schools in the UK, and examine the reasons behind their increasing popularity.

Author - Polly Toynbee

Polly K Toynbee is a British journalist, columnist, and author who has been writing for The Guardian newspaper since 1998. She is known for her left-leaning political views and progressive opinions on social issues. She has written extensively on the subject of faith schools, which she sees as an affront to secularism and a danger to social cohesion.

The Benefits of Faith Schools

One of the main reasons why faith schools are becoming more popular is that they provide a sense of community and belonging for students and their families. In a country where traditional institutions like churches and community centers are declining, faith schools offer a place for people to come together and connect.

Furthermore, faith schools often have a strong sense of values and ethics that are embedded into their curriculum. This can provide students with a sense of purpose and a moral compass that they might not get from other types of schools. Additionally, faith schools often have a higher standard of discipline and behavior, which can lead to a more positive learning environment.

Case Study: The Rise of Islamic Schools

One of the most notable examples of the rise of faith schools in the UK is the growth of Islamic schools. According to a report by The Guardian, there are now over 200 Islamic schools in the UK, up from just 60 in 2005. This trend is particularly noteworthy given the negative stereotypes that are often associated with the Muslim community in the UK.

However, the growth of Islamic schools is not necessarily problematic. In fact, these schools can help to counter stereotypes and promote greater understanding between different communities. Furthermore, they offer a safe space for Muslim students to learn about their religion in a supportive environment.

Conclusion

  1. Faith schools are becoming more popular in the UK, despite concerns about their impact on social cohesion.
  2. However, these schools can provide a sense of community, values, and discipline that are beneficial to students.
  3. The growth of Islamic schools is particularly noteworthy, and can help to counter stereotypes and promote greater understanding between different communities.
