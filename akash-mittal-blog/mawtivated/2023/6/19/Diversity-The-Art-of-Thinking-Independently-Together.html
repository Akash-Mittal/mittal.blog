Diversity: The Art of Thinking Independently Together



It was a bright and sunny day when I met Sarah, an African American woman with a degree in Computer Science, who worked in a small IT company. When she was assigned to my team, I felt like we didn't have much in common, but as time passed, I realized that her unique perspective on things was an invaluable contribution to our team.

Diversity goes beyond just physical appearances and covers a wide range of experiences, including cultural, social, ethnic, and educational backgrounds. It's about embracing and celebrating our differences and unique qualities that make us who we are, and bringing those qualities to the table to make the world a better place.

As Malcolm Forbes once said, "Diversity: the art of thinking independently together." In other words, diversity allows us to come together as individuals while still embracing our differences and unique perspectives on life. By working together, we can come up with innovative ideas, achieve our goals, and create a better future for ourselves and others.

Why Diversity Matters?

  • Diversity brings together different perspectives and ideas
  • It fosters creativity and innovation
  • It leads to better decision-making
  • It improves problem-solving skills
  • It creates a more inclusive and tolerant society

Here are some examples of how diversity has made an impact in the workplace:

In 2015, Intel Corporation pledged $300 million to increase the diversity of their workforce. They did this by creating recruitment programs for women, underrepresented minorities, and veterans, and partnering with schools and universities to increase the number of diverse candidates in the workforce.
Google created an unconscious bias training program for their employees to raise awareness about how our biases can affect our behavior and judgments. The program aims to create a more inclusive and supportive environment for everyone.

But diversity goes beyond just the workplace. In our daily lives, we can embrace diversity by:

  • Learning about different cultures and customs
  • Having open and honest conversations about different perspectives
  • Recognizing and challenging our biases and assumptions
  • Supporting diverse groups and individuals

Conclusion

To sum up, diversity is about embracing and celebrating our differences to achieve a common goal. It's about coming together as individuals while still preserving our unique qualities and perspectives on life.

My personal experience with Sarah taught me that diversity is not just a buzzword, but a real and valuable attribute that can bring enormous benefits in the workplace and beyond. Here are three practical tips that you can follow to embrace diversity:

  1. Be open to different opinions and perspectives
  2. Challenge your biases and assumptions
  3. Support diversity and inclusivity in your workplace and community
