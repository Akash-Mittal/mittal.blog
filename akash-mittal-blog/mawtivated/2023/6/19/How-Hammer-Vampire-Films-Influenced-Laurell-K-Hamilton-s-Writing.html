How Hammer Vampire Films Influenced Laurell K. Hamilton's Writing



Do you ever wonder where writers draw inspiration from? Sometimes, they find inspiration in the most unexpected places.

Author Background

Laurell K. Hamilton was born in Heber Springs, Arkansas in 1963. She showed an early talent for writing and storytelling, and was an avid reader. However, it wasn't until her college years that she really began to pursue writing as a career.

Hamilton's vampire novels eventually became her most popular works. She created her own unique vampire mythology, and her books have sold millions of copies worldwide.

Hammer Vampire Films

Hammer Films was a British production company that was particularly active in the 1950s, 60s, and 70s. They produced a wide variety of horror movies, but they were particularly known for their vampire films.

One of the films that left a lasting impression on Hamilton was "Vampire Circus" (also known as "Circus of Fear"). The movie centers around a traveling circus that is actually a front for a group of vampires. Hamilton was particularly inspired by the concept of a group of vampires traveling together and using their unique abilities to elude detection.

In fact, Hamilton's own vampire characters in her novels share some similarities with the vampires in "Vampire Circus." They have unique abilities, and often travel together as groups.

Hamilton has spoken publicly about how influential Hammer vampire films were to her writing. In one interview, she recalled watching Hammer films with her grandmother when she was a child, and being fascinated by the monsters on screen.

She said, "I loved the idea that there were these creatures who were both beautiful and deadly at the same time. It was a contradiction that really appealed to me."

Examples of Hamilton's Work

  • In the "Anita Blake" series, the main character is a vampire hunter who often finds herself working alongside vampires.
  • In the "Merry Gentry" series, the main character is a faerie princess who is also half-human, and often has to deal with vampires and other supernatural creatures.
  • In "A Kiss of Shadows," the first book in the "Merry Gentry" series, there is a character named Doyle who is a vampire and has unique abilities similar to the vampires in "Vampire Circus."

Conclusion

In conclusion, Laurell K. Hamilton's writing was greatly influenced by Hammer vampire films, particularly "Vampire Circus." The unique abilities and traveling groups of vampires in the film inspired her own vampire characters, and helped shape her own unique vampire mythology.

Hamilton's story is a reminder that inspiration can come from anywhere, and that the most unexpected sources can lead to the most creative ideas.

Three takeaways from this story:

  1. Inspiration can come from unexpected sources.
  2. Unique abilities and groups of creatures can make for intriguing stories.
  3. Imagination can turn traditional monsters into something completely new.
