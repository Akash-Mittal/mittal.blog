Government: The Answer or the Problem?



When I was a young boy, I had a dream of becoming a successful businessman. I would see my father work hard every day to provide for our family, and I knew that I wanted to be just like him. But I also knew that the path to success was not an easy one. It would require hard work, determination and a little bit of luck. I had all those qualities, but I also had something else: a government that stood in my way.

This administration in Washington that's in power now clearly believes that government is not only the answer to every need, but it's the most qualified to make the most central decisions for every American in every area. This statement by Rick Perry highlights the struggle that many Americans face today. The government, which is supposed to serve the people, has instead become a burden on their lives. This burden manifests itself in many forms, such as excessive regulation, high taxes, and an attitude of entitlement among government officials.

Examples of Government Overreach

One example of government overreach is the Affordable Care Act, commonly known as Obamacare. This law was supposed to make healthcare more affordable for all Americans. However, it has resulted in higher premiums, fewer options, and more bureaucracy.

Another example is the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). While the EPA was created with the intention of protecting the environment, it has instead become a tool for government officials to impose their will on private citizens. The EPA's rules and regulations have become so burdensome that they often prevent businesses and individuals from pursuing their own goals and interests.

The Role of Government

So what is the role of government in our lives? Is it to be a guiding force, or a hindrance? The answer is not a simple one. Government is necessary to protect our freedom, to ensure that laws are followed and that the rights of the individual are respected. However, when government becomes too powerful and too intrusive, it can have a negative impact on our lives.

The key to a successful government is balance. Government must strike a balance between protecting the people and giving them the freedom to pursue their own interests. It must also strike a balance between regulation and innovation, between stability and change.

The Rick Perry Story

Rick Perry is a politician who understands the balance that government must strike. His background in government and business has given him a unique perspective on the role of government. As governor of Texas, Perry was able to create a business-friendly environment that attracted companies and entrepreneurs from around the world. He understood that government should not be an obstacle to success, but a partner in it.

Perry's approach to government is one that is focused on results. He believes that government should be accountable to the people, and that its actions should be based on data and evidence, not just ideology. This approach has earned him the respect of many people in the business community, and has led to the creation of thousands of jobs in Texas.

Conclusion in 3 points

  1. Government can be a guiding force or a hindrance in our lives.
  2. The key to a successful government is balance.
  3. Leaders like Rick Perry understand the role of government and how to create a business-friendly environment.

In conclusion, government is neither the answer nor the problem. It is up to us, as citizens, to ensure that our government is one that serves the people, not the other way around. We must demand accountability, transparency, and results from our elected officials. We must also work to create a culture of innovation and entrepreneurship, one that is not hampered by excessive regulation and bureaucracy. Only then can we truly say that we are living in a country that is free, prosperous, and just.
