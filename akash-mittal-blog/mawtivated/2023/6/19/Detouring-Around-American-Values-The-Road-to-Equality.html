Detouring Around American Values: The Road to Equality



Inspiring Story

Once upon a time, there was a young girl who dreamt of becoming a doctor. Unfortunately, she lived in a society where women were not encouraged to pursue higher education or careers outside of the home. Despite the challenges, she worked hard and eventually became the first woman from her community to graduate from medical school. She went on to become a successful physician, inspiring countless other women to follow in her footsteps.

Detouring Around American Values

As Eleanor Holmes Norton once said, "On the road to equality there is no better place for blacks to detour around American values than in forgoing its example in the treatment of its women and the organization of its family." Indeed, American society has long been plagued by gender inequality and outdated notions of what constitutes a "proper" family structure. It is time for us to take a different path and detour around these values in order to achieve true equality.

Treating Women with Respect

  • Pay women equally for equal work
  • Allow women to pursue careers and education without discrimination or harassment
  • Provide paid family leave, affordable childcare, and other policies that support working mothers

Redefining the Family

"The definition of family must be broad enough to encompass the many different types of families that exist in our society today."
  • Support same-sex marriage and other non-traditional forms of partnership
  • Recognize the importance of extended family and community support networks
  • Provide resources and support for single parents and blended families

As a woman and a mother, I have personally experienced the challenges of navigating a society that doesn't always value women's contributions to the workforce or the family. But I am also heartened by the progress that has been made in recent years, from the #MeToo movement to the legalization of gay marriage. These changes give me hope that we can continue to detour around outdated values and work towards a more just and equal society for all.

Similarly, Eleanor Holmes Norton has dedicated her career to fighting for equity and justice for marginalized communities. As an African American woman in a predominantly white, male-dominated field, Norton has experienced discrimination firsthand. But she has also worked tirelessly to stand up for the rights of others and challenge the status quo.

Examples of Progress

  • The passage of Title IX in 1972, which prohibited discrimination on the basis of sex in education
  • The legalization of gay marriage nationwide in 2015
  • The growing number of women in political leadership roles, such as Kamala Harris, the first female and first Black and Asian American Vice President of the United States

Practical Tips

There are many ways we can all help detour around American values and promote equality:

  • Speak out against sexism and gender-based discrimination in your own life and on a larger scale
  • Support policies and initiatives that promote equality, such as paid family leave and affordable childcare
  • Challenge traditional "gender roles" and embrace diversity in all forms

Conclusion

In order to achieve true equality, we must detour around traditional American values that have perpetuated gender inequality and limited our understanding of what constitutes a "proper" family. By treating women with respect, redefining the family, and embracing diversity in all forms, we can create a more just and equitable society for everyone.

  1. Treat women with respect and support their career and educational goals
  2. Redefine the family to include all types of partnerships and support networks
  3. Embrace diversity and challenge traditional gender roles in all aspects of life
