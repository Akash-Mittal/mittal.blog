The Power of Persistence: My Journey to Medical School



Author: Edwin Moses

I always knew that I wanted to become a doctor. As a child, I would spend countless hours reading books on medicine, anatomy, and biology. I thought that pursuing a career in medicine was the only way I could make a meaningful difference in the world.

However, when I was in high school, I realized that I could not afford to pay for college tuition. My parents were unable to finance my education, and I did not receive any scholarships or bursaries. I was crushed when I realized that I may have to abandon my dreams of becoming a doctor.

But I refused to give up. I was determined to find a way to pay for my education.

Perseverance Pays Off

After graduating from high school, I decided to take a gap year to work and save money. I worked long hours at a part-time job, and I volunteered at a local hospital. I knew that I had to gain experience and build my resume to increase my chances of being accepted into medical school.

After a year of hard work, I was able to save enough money to enroll in college. I took out student loans and applied for financial aid to pay for my tuition. Even though I faced significant financial challenges, I was determined to succeed.

Lessons Learned on the Journey

  • Never give up on your dreams, even in the face of adversity. With hard work and determination, anything is possible.
  • Gain experience in your desired field. Volunteer, intern, or work part-time to build your resume and increase your chances of being accepted into graduate school.
  • Seek out financial assistance. There are many scholarships, bursaries, and financial aid options available for students who demonstrate financial need and academic potential.
