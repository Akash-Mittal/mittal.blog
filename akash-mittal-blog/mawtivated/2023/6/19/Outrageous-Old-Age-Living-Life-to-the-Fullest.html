Outrageous Old Age: Living Life to the Fullest



It was a warm summer day when I met Louis Kronenberger. He was sitting on a bench in the park, surrounded by young children playing and laughing. I couldn't help but notice his sparkling eyes and mischievous grin. As I sat down next to him, he turned to me and said, "Old age is an excellent time for outrage. My goal is to say or do at least one outrageous thing every week."

This statement stuck with me, and I soon learned more about this fascinating man. Kronenberger was a renowned writer and critic, known for his witty commentary and sharp tongue. He wrote for The New Yorker and The New York Times, and was widely respected in literary circles.

Kronenberger's philosophy on aging was unique. He believed that life should be lived to the fullest, no matter how old you are. He saw old age as a time to embrace your eccentricities, speak your mind, and challenge the status quo.

As I reflected on our conversation, I realized that Kronenberger was onto something. Too often, we see old age as a time to slow down and take it easy. We retire, travel, and spend time with family. While these are all wonderful things, they don't necessarily lead to a fulfilling life. What if, instead of simply coasting into old age, we chose to live outrageously?

Living Outrageously: Examples and Tips

What does it mean to live outrageously? Here are a few examples:

  • Joining a protest or political rally
  • Trying a new hobby, like skydiving or painting
  • Cutting your hair into a bright, bold style
  • Speaking your mind, even when it's unpopular
  • Dressing in a way that expresses your personality

Of course, everyone's version of outrageous will be different. What's important is that you're pushing yourself out of your comfort zone and challenging societal norms.

If you're not sure where to start, here are some practical tips:

  1. Make a bucket list of outrageous things you want to try. It could be something small, like dying your hair pink, or something larger, like running a marathon. Having a list will give you something to work towards and keep you motivated.
  2. Surround yourself with like-minded people. Join a club or group that shares your interests, or attend events where you're likely to meet people who value individuality and self-expression.
  3. Be kind to yourself. Living outrageously can be scary, and it's okay to make mistakes or feel unsure. Remember that you're doing something brave by challenging the status quo, and give yourself permission to experiment and learn.

Conclusion

In conclusion, old age doesn't have to be a time of slowing down and settling for the status quo. As Louis Kronenberger showed us, it's a time to live life to the fullest, embracing our individuality and challenging societal norms.

If you're feeling stuck or uninspired, try incorporating some outrageous activities into your routine. Make a bucket list, join a group, or start dressing in a way that reflects your true self. Remember to be kind to yourself and give yourself permission to experiment and learn.

As Bill Watterson once said, "We're so busy watching out for what's just ahead of us that we don't take time to enjoy where we are." So go out and enjoy your life, no matter how old you are.
