The Hooker who made a Fortune: Shirley MacLaine's journey



Shirley MacLaine's quote "I've made so many movies playing a hooker that they don't pay me in the regular way anymore. They leave it on the dresser" may seem humorous to the audience but it represents the real struggle of actors.

The inspiring story of Shirley MacLaine

Shirley MacLaine is an American actress, author, and dancer. Born in Richmond, Virginia, USA, she started her acting career in the 1950s. She is one of the most versatile actors in Hollywood. MacLaine's journey is truly inspiring because she started as a nobody and rose to the top of Hollywood.

MacLaine's life changed when she moved to New York City to try her luck in the entertainment industry. She worked as a chorus girl and performed in Broadway productions like The Pajama Game and Me and Juliet. In 1955, she got her first break in the film industry with the film The Trouble With Harry. She went on to act in various films and television shows, including Steel Magnolias, The Apartment, and Downton Abbey.

Shirley MacLaine's Background

MacLaine came from a conservative family, but she was always interested in spirituality and new age beliefs. She has written several books on this topic, including her memoir Out on a Limb. MacLaine is a firm believer in reincarnation and claims to have had many past lives.
