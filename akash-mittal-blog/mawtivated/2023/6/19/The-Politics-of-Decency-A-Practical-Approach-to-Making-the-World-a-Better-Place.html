Have you ever noticed how the simplest acts of kindness can make someone's day? A smile to a stranger, holding the door open for someone or paying for a coffee for the person behind you in the line-up can make a real difference. Author and former US president Theodore Roosevelt once said, "The most practical kind of politics is the politics of decency." This is still true today and can be applied not only to governments but also in our everyday lives.

What is the Politics of Decency?

The politics of decency is based on the notion that treating others the way you would like to be treated is not only ethical and moral but also practical. It's about showing empathy and compassion toward others and viewing society as a community of individuals working together for the greater good.

Politicians who practice the politics of decency are more likely to appeal to a wider range of people because they are seen as trustworthy and caring. They are also more likely to make decisions that benefit society as a whole rather than just a select few.

Examples of the Politics of Decency in Action

The politics of decency can be demonstrated in many ways, from small acts of kindness to large-scale policies that promote equality and justice. Here are a few examples:

  • A local politician in a small town who takes the time to listen to the concerns of the community and work collaboratively to find a solution to a problem.
  • A government leader who actively promotes policies that reduce social inequality and improve the lives of marginalized communities.
  • A business owner who treats their employees with respect and fairness, offering them good working conditions and a living wage.

During his time as president, Barack Obama showed a commitment to decency and compassion. He worked tirelessly to make healthcare accessible to more Americans, supported marriage equality and was a vocal advocate for gun control.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the politics of decency is the most practical approach to governance and everyday life. Here are three key takeaways for practicing the politics of decency:

  1. Show empathy and compassion towards others.
  2. Work collaboratively to find solutions to problems.
  3. Promote policies that benefit society as a whole.

Let's all try to make the world a better place by practicing the politics of decency, one small act of kindness at a time.
