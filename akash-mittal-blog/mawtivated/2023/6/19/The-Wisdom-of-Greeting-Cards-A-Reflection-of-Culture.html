The Wisdom of Greeting Cards: A Reflection of Culture



Courtney had always been close to her grandmother, but when she was diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease, their relationship became strained. It was hard for Courtney to watch her once-vibrant grandmother struggle to remember her own name, let alone the memories they had shared.

One day, Courtney received a greeting card in the mail from her grandmother. It was a simple card, with a photograph of a sunset on the front and a handwritten message inside:

"Even in the darkness, there is always a glimmer of light. Don't give up hope."

Courtney was moved to tears. She knew that her grandmother had not written those words herself, but they expressed the love and encouragement that her grandmother still wanted to convey.

This experience showed Courtney the power of greeting cards as a reflection of culture. She realized that, even if she didn't turn to greeting cards for wisdom and advice, they were a fine representation of the collective wisdom and advice of society.

The Culture Reflected by Greeting Cards

Greeting cards are an ancient tradition, dating back to the ancient Chinese, who exchanged messages of good will to celebrate the New Year. By the 15th century, Valentine's Day cards had become popular in Europe, and by the 19th century, mass-produced greeting cards were available in America.

Today, greeting cards can be found for almost every occasion, from birthdays to graduations to condolences. While some greeting cards are personalized for individuals, others reflect the broader culture and its values, beliefs, and attitudes.

Examples of cultural reflection in greeting cards include:

  • Messages of inclusion and diversity, such as cards that celebrate LGBTQ+ relationships or feature images of people of many races and ethnicities
  • Expressions of spirituality or faith, such as cards with religious iconography or quotes from religious texts
  • Reflections on the passage of time, such as cards that mark milestone birthdays or anniversaries
  • Encouragement and support during difficult times, such as cards for people going through illness or personal struggles
  • Humor and satire, such as cards that poke fun at popular culture trends or political figures

As Susan Orlean, an American journalist and author, has explained:

"Greeting cards are just one more creation of a society that loves to record itself."

This means that greeting cards are not only a reflection of culture, but also a way for culture to record itself. Greeting cards capture the sentiment, humor, and style of a particular time and place, allowing future generations to glimpse into the cultural landscape of an era.

The Lessons We Can Learn from Greeting Cards

While greeting cards can serve as a fascinating window into the collective wisdom and advice of our society, it is important to remember that they are not always perfect reflections of culture. Like any cultural artifact, they can be shaped by commercial interests, political biases, and other factors.

However, by examining greeting cards and the messages they contain, we can learn some important lessons about ourselves and our culture:

  1. We are not alone. Greeting cards often offer messages of love, encouragement, and support, reminding us that we are part of a broader community that wants us to succeed and thrive.
  2. Our values and beliefs are constantly evolving. Greeting cards that celebrate diversity or challenge traditional gender roles show that society is becoming more accepting and inclusive. Cards that reflect the challenges of aging or managing chronic illness acknowledge the realities of our modern lives.
  3. Words matter. The messages on greeting cards may be brief, but they can have a powerful impact on our emotions and attitudes. By choosing cards with thoughtful, empowering messages, we can enrich our own lives and the lives of others.

Conclusion

While greeting cards are sometimes dismissed as superficial or insincere, they can offer valuable insights into the culture and values of a society. By examining the messages contained in greeting cards, we can learn more about ourselves, our community, and the lessons we can take from the collective wisdom and advice of those who came before us.

In conclusion, here are the three main takeaways from the wisdom of greeting cards:

  1. Greeting cards offer a reflection of the culture and values of a society. By examining the messages contained in greeting cards, we can learn more about ourselves and our community.
  2. Greeting cards remind us that we are part of a broader community that wants us to succeed and thrive. Messages of love, encouragement, and support can have a powerful impact on our emotions and attitudes.
  3. Words matter. The messages on greeting cards may be brief, but they can have a powerful impact on our perceptions and attitudes towards the world around us.
