The Price of Fame: Corey Feldman's Story



An Inspiring Story

Corey Feldman is a well-known name in Hollywood who rose to fame at a very young age. He started his acting career at the age of 3 and became famous before he even knew his own name. His talent was recognized by many and he soon began landing prominent roles in movies and TV shows.

However, fame at such a young age came with a high price. Corey was exposed to the darker side of Hollywood, including drug abuse and sexual exploitation. He struggled to cope with the pressure of fame and the constant scrutiny of his personal life.

Corey Feldman's Story

Corey Feldman was born on July 16, 1971, in Los Angeles, California. His parents were both in the entertainment industry, and his mother managed his early career. He made his film debut in the 1980 comedy "The Fox and the Hound" and soon after landed a prominent role in the classic film "Stand By Me."

Corey's career continued to thrive throughout the 1980s, with notable roles in films such as "The Lost Boys," "License to Drive" and "Dream a Little Dream." However, his childhood was far from perfect. He was exposed to drugs and alcohol at a very young age, and he had to deal with the pressure of being a child star.

In his book "Coreyography," Corey recounts the sexual abuse he and his friend and fellow child star Corey Haim endured in the industry. Corey alleges that they were targeted by several powerful industry figures, and that their abusers were protected by powerful lawyers and agents.

The Price of Fame

Corey's story is a tragic one but it is not unique. The entertainment industry is notorious for its exploitation of young and vulnerable actors. The pressure to succeed coupled with the never-ending demands of the industry can be too much for young actors to handle.

  • The entertainment industry often exploits young actors
  • Drug and alcohol abuse are prevalent in Hollywood

Conclusion

In conclusion, Corey Feldman's story highlights the high price of fame, especially for child stars. The entertainment industry needs to do more to protect young actors from exploitation and abuse. Parents and guardians also play a crucial role in ensuring the wellbeing of young actors.

  • Parents and guardians should carefully evaluate the risks and benefits of their child's career in the entertainment industry.
  • The industry should take steps to protect young actors from exploitation and abuse, including implementing stricter background checks and providing more support for child actors on set.
  • As a society, we should be more aware of the challenges facing child stars and should advocate for their wellbeing.
