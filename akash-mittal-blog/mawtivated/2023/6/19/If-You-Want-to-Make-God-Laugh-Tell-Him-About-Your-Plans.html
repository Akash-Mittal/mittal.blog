If You Want to Make God Laugh, Tell Him About Your Plans



Have you ever made plans just to have them go completely wrong? Maybe you planned on getting a promotion at work, but instead got laid off. Or you planned on traveling the world, but got sick and had to stay home. It's frustrating, and sometimes it feels like God is playing a big joke on us.

As Woody Allen once said, "If you want to make God laugh, tell him about your plans." But what does that really mean? Does it mean that you shouldn't make plans at all? Does it mean that everything is out of your control? Or is there something deeper going on here?

The Truth About Plans

Let's start by looking at what plans really are. Plans are simply our way of trying to control our lives. They give us a sense of security and predictability. When we have a plan, we feel like we know what's going to happen next.

But the truth is, we can never really know what's going to happen next. Life is unpredictable and sometimes things happen that are completely out of our control. That's where the quote from Woody Allen comes in. When we make plans, we're essentially trying to control the future. And as much as we'd like to think we can control everything, we can't.

Why We Still Make Plans

So if plans are just a way of trying to control the future, why do we still make them? The answer is simple: because they work, at least some of the time. When we make plans and take action, we can create the future we want. It may not always work out, but sometimes it does.

For example, let's say you want to start a business. You do your research, come up with a business plan, and start taking action. Maybe you get investors, hire employees, and start selling products. And it works! Your business takes off and becomes a huge success. In this case, your plan worked.

When Plans Go Wrong

But what happens when plans don't work out? What happens when you do everything right and still fail? This is where things get tricky. It's easy to feel discouraged and frustrated when things don't go according to plan.

But here's the thing: just because your plan didn't work out, doesn't mean it was a bad plan. It just means that something unexpected happened. Maybe the market changed, or you got sick, or a competitor emerged. Whatever it is, it's important to remember that failure is not the end. It's just a setback.

Conclusion: What to Do When Plans Fail

So what can you do when plans fail? Here are three things to keep in mind:

  • Don't give up. Failure is not the end of the road. It's just a speed bump.
  • Learn from your mistakes. Take a step back and look at what went wrong. What could you have done differently? What did you learn from the experience?
  • Keep moving forward. Take what you learned and use it to create a better plan. Keep taking action and don't let setbacks stop you.

Author - Woody Allen Story or Background

Woody Allen is a well-known filmmaker, comedian, and writer. He's famous for his dark humor and satirical take on life. The quote "If you want to make God laugh, tell him about your plans" is often attributed to him, although it's unclear if he actually said it. Regardless of who said it, the quote has become a popular way of expressing the idea that life is unpredictable.
