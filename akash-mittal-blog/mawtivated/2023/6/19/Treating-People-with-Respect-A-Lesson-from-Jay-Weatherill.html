Treating People with Respect: A Lesson from Jay Weatherill



An inspiring story of politics and respect

When Jay Weatherill was elected as Premier of the state of South Australia, he had a message for his fellow politicians.

"My government, you can be assured, will be less focused on personalities. It is about treating people with respect. I think complaining about the community not being able to see the wisdom of our ideas is the wrong approach."

This promise was not just a political cliche, but a core principle that Weatherill held in his heart. Growing up in a working-class family, he witnessed firsthand how disrespect could create unnecessary tension, conflict, and suffering. By the time he entered politics, he had already learned that treating people with respect was not only a matter of doing the right thing, but also of achieving real results.

From Personal Anecdote to Political Lesson

In his political career, Weatherill came across many situations in which respect was lacking. But instead of adding fuel to the fire, he used these situations to illustrate the importance of his core principle. For example, he once told a story of how he visited a school that had a terrible reputation for student behavior. When he arrived, he saw a group of students sitting in detention, looking bored and dejected. Weatherill decided to talk to them and hear their side of the story. As it turned out, many of these students felt ignored, belittled, or stereotyped by their teachers and classmates. Instead of punishing them, Weatherill decided to address the root cause of their misbehavior by working with the school and the community to create a more inclusive and supportive environment for all students. In just a few years, the school transformed into a model of student engagement and achievement.

This simple but powerful anecdote illustrates two important points. First, it shows how respectful listening and dialogue can reveal underlying problems and solutions that might not be immediately apparent. Second, it shows how treating people with respect can create a ripple effect of positive change in a community.

Examples of Respectful Politics

Weatherill's commitment to respect did not only show in his personal interactions but also in his policy decisions. Here are some examples:

  • In 2015, Weatherill's government introduced a groundbreaking bill to recognize Aboriginal heritage in the state's constitution. This bill was the result of years of consultation and dialogue with Aboriginal leaders and communities, and aimed to rectify the historical injustice done to the First Nations people.
  • In 2016, Weatherill declared a ban on fracking in the state, citing the concerns of local communities about the environmental and health risks of the practice. This decision was backed by scientific evidence and public opinion, and showed that Weatherill was willing to put the well-being of people ahead of corporate interests.
  • In 2017, Weatherill's government launched a $150 million renewable energy fund to create jobs and reduce carbon emissions. This fund was part of a long-term strategy to transition South Australia to a sustainable and prosperous economy, and was welcomed by environmentalists and industry leaders alike.

These examples show how respectful politics can be both visionary and practical. By listening to the diverse voices of a community and acting on their concerns, a politician can create a fairer, healthier, and more prosperous society.

Conclusion: Three Principles of Respectful Politics

Based on Jay Weatherill's example, we can derive three principles of respectful politics:

  1. Listen actively and respectfully to all stakeholders
  2. Act on the evidence and values that benefit the common good
  3. Communicate honestly and empathetically with transparency

If politicians follow these principles, they can not only win the trust and respect of their constituents but also make a positive impact on their lives. In a world where politics is often seen as divisive and cynical, we can learn from Jay Weatherill that treating people with respect is not only possible but also essential for a better future.
