Power: Not Something You Can Assume or Discard Like Underwear



When we think of power, we often think of the most visible displays of it: the CEO standing at the head of the boardroom, the politician addressing the nation, the celebrity with millions of followers. But power is much more nuanced than that, woven through every aspect of our lives and permeating every interaction we have with others.

Author John Kenneth Galbraith, who served in several US government positions and wrote extensively about economics and public policy, wrote that "power is not something that can be assumed or discarded at will like underwear." In other words, power is not a switch we can turn on or off at our convenience. It is not something we can choose to wield in some situations and not in others. It is always there, always influencing our actions and shaping our perceptions, whether we are aware of it or not.

The Nature of Power

So what exactly is power? At its core, power is the ability to influence or control the behavior of others. This can be done through a wide range of means, from physical force to persuasion to manipulation. Power can take many forms, including:

  • Physical power (strength, speed, athleticism)
  • Intellectual power (knowledge, expertise, critical thinking)
  • Emotional power (charisma, empathy, the ability to connect with others)
  • Social power (status, wealth, influence)

Importantly, no one person possesses all of these types of power, and some people may have greater prowess in one area than another. However, everyone has some degree of power and is capable of using it to affect the world around them.

The Danger of Unexamined Power

Having power is not inherently good or bad. Like any tool, it can be used for constructive or destructive purposes. However, the danger of power lies in its ability to go unnoticed or unquestioned. When we take our power for granted or fail to recognize it, we risk using it in ways that harm others without realizing it.

An example of this can be seen in the case of a manager at a retail store who was known for intimidating and belittling her employees. From her perspective, she was simply using her authority to keep her team in line and ensure they were meeting their sales quotas. However, from the perspective of her subordinates, her behavior was demoralizing and created a hostile work environment. Because she never examined her use of power or considered the impact it was having on those around her, she continued to perpetuate this harmful behavior.

How to Use Power Responsibly

So how can we use power in a way that is responsible and ethical? While there are no easy answers, there are some general principles that can guide us:

  • Recognize the power dynamics at play. Before we can use power effectively, we need to understand who has it, who doesn't, and how it is distributed. This means being aware of the different types of power that exist and recognizing our own biases and privileges.
  • Empathize with others. When we take the time to understand others' perspectives and needs, we can use our power in a way that benefits everyone involved. This means listening attentively, practicing empathy, and being willing to compromise.
  • Be willing to give up power. Just because we have power doesn't mean we need to use it all the time. There may be situations where it is more appropriate to step back and let others take the lead. This means being humble, recognizing our limitations, and having faith in others.
"With great power comes great responsibility." - Uncle Ben (Spider-Man comics)

In the end, power is a complex and multifaceted concept that cannot be easily summed up or dismissed. While it can be tempting to dismiss our own power or underestimate the power of others, doing so only serves to perpetuate harmful power dynamics. Instead, by examining our own power and using it responsibly, we can create a more equitable and just world.

Conclusion

To sum up, power is not something that can be assumed or discarded at will like underwear. Rather, it is a constant force that influences our actions and shapes our interactions with others. If we want to use power in a way that is responsible and ethical, we need to be willing to examine it, understand its nature, and use it in a way that benefits everyone involved. By doing so, we can create a world where power is used for good and where everyone has the opportunity to thrive.
