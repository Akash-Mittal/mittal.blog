The Peace Corps - A Chance to Explore the World



"I never knew how much I needed to see the world until I joined the Peace Corps."

These were the words of a young, bright-eyed volunteer who recently returned from her two-year stint in Tanzania, Africa.

The Peace Corps offers a unique opportunity for young Americans to explore the world and make a difference in the lives of others. Its founding father, Sargent Shriver, believed that this organization would change the way America engaged with the world, and he was right.

Background Story of Sargent Shriver

Sargent Shriver was the driving force behind the creation of the Peace Corps. He was appointed by President Kennedy to head the organization in 1961 and served in that position until 1966. Shriver was a visionary leader who believed that young Americans could make a positive impact on the world, and that through their work, they could promote peace and understanding between nations.

How the Peace Corps works

The Peace Corps sends volunteers to work in developing countries around the world. The volunteers are usually young Americans who have completed their college education, although older applicants are also accepted. They are trained in the language, culture, and customs of the country to which they are assigned. They then work on a wide range of projects, from teaching English to building institutions that will help communities develop sustainably.

Benefits of the Peace Corps

Broadening Horizons

Perhaps the most significant benefit of serving in the Peace Corps is the opportunity to explore the world. Volunteers are sent to some of the most remote and fascinating places on earth, where they learn about new cultures, people, and ways of life. They learn to live with fewer material possessions and to appreciate the simple things in life. They return home with a broader perspective on life, a better understanding of the world, and a renewed sense of purpose.

Making a Difference

The Peace Corps also offers the opportunity to make a tangible difference in the world. Volunteers work on projects that address the most critical needs of the communities they serve. They help build schools, promote health and hygiene, and develop sustainable agriculture practices. They work with local leaders to promote peace and understanding between different groups of people.

Career Benefits

Serving in the Peace Corps can also help kick-start a career. Volunteers learn a wide range of skills, from teaching and construction to project management and community development. They develop the ability to work collaboratively with diverse groups of people, improve their language skills, and gain invaluable experience that can help them stand out in the job market when they return home.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Peace Corps offers a unique opportunity for young Americans to explore the world, make a difference in the lives of others, and gain invaluable skills and experience that can help them succeed in their future careers. From building schools in Africa to promoting peace and understanding in the Middle East, the Peace Corps has helped thousands of young people make a positive impact on the world.

Practical Tips:

  • Begin the application process early, as it can be lengthy.
  • Research the country and culture where you will be placed.
  • Be willing to adapt to new and challenging situations.
