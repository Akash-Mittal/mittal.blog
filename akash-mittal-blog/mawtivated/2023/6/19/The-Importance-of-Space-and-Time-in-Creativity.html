The Importance of Space and Time in Creativity



A Personal Insight from Award-Winning Filmmaker Lasse Hallstrom

As a young boy, Lasse Hallstrom had a vivid imagination and a passion for movies. He would spend countless hours watching films in his hometown in Sweden, dreaming of one day creating his own stories. Yet, little did he know that the biggest lesson he would learn in his career as a filmmaker will not come from the movies he watched, but from the space and time he carved out for himself.

In an interview for MovieMaker Magazine, Hallstrom shared his experience of filming "The Shipping News." He said, "It was important on The Shipping News to have my house far enough away from each location so I had this time in the morning to think about my shots and still remain open to surprises once I got to the set." This statement might seem simple, but the implications are significant.

By intentionally creating physical distance and unstructured time in his routine, Hallstrom allowed himself to find inspiration in unexpected places. He could ponder about his creative vision, experiment with his ideas, and remain flexible to the ever-changing nature of filmmaking. In other words, by prioritizing space and time, he set himself up for success.

The Power of Space and Time for Creativity

It's not just filmmakers who benefit from space and time. As creatives, we all need room to breathe and observe the world around us. We need time to let our ideas percolate and evolve. We need space to experiment and play. Without these essential elements, our work can become stagnant, repetitive, or uninspired.

Here are some practical tips for incorporating space and time into your creative process:

  • Create a designated workspace - whether it's a corner of your room or a separate studio, having a physical space dedicated to your creative pursuits can help you focus and get into the right mindset.
  • Take breaks and switch up your routine - don't feel guilty for taking breaks or doing something unrelated to your project. In fact, doing so can help your brain recharge and spark new ideas.
  • Practice mindfulness and reflection - set aside time to reflect on your progress, goals, and challenges. Use this time to journal, meditate, or simply be present with your thoughts.
  • Embrace spontaneity and play - sometimes the best ideas come from experimenting and playing. Don't be afraid to try something new or take a creative risk.

Conclusion

In summary, space and time are crucial elements in creative work. By intentionally creating distance and unstructured time, we can tap into our best ideas, remain flexible to changes, and stay inspired. Whether you're a filmmaker like Lasse Hallstrom or a writer, designer, or artist, consider how you can incorporate more space and time into your process. Your creative work will thank you for it.
