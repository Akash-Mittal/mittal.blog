When Dad Disapproves: Navigating Family Relationships



As a young man, I went through a phase where I couldn't seem to win the approval of my father. No matter what I did, it never felt like enough to earn his respect and admiration. I felt like I was constantly trying to prove myself to him, and it was exhausting.

But through that experience, I learned a lot about the importance of building strong family relationships, even when it feels like someone in your family doesn't approve of you. Here are a few tips that I hope can help you navigate these tricky situations:

Tip 1: Respect and Validate Their Opinions

It can be tough when a family member doesn't like you, but it's important to remember that they're entitled to their opinion. Instead of trying to change their mind, try to understand where they're coming from. Listen to what they have to say and show them that you hear their concerns.

"If my Dad doesn't like you, you will know."

This sentiment rings true for a lot of families. But just because Dad doesn't approve doesn't mean that you have to give up on building a relationship with him. Try to find common ground, and show him that you're willing to put in the effort to make things work.

One strategy I've found helpful is to ask for their advice. People love to feel like they're helping others, and by asking for their opinion, you can show that you respect their knowledge and expertise. It's a win-win situation.

Tip 2: Don't Take it Personally

It's natural to feel hurt when someone doesn't approve of you, but it's important not to take it personally. Remember that their opinion of you doesn't define who you are. You're still a valuable person, even if they can't see it.

One way to avoid taking it personally is to try to be empathetic. Put yourself in their shoes and try to imagine why they might feel the way they do. Maybe they're going through a tough time, or they've had bad experiences with someone like you in the past. Whatever the reason, try to approach the situation with kindness and understanding.

Tip 3: Focus on Building Positive Relationships

In the end, building positive relationships is the key to navigating family dynamics. Even if one family member disapproves of you, there are likely others who care about you and want to see you succeed. Focus on building those positive relationships, and try not to let the negative ones get you down.

One way to build positive relationships is to focus on shared interests and experiences. Maybe you and Dad don't see eye-to-eye on everything, but you both love basketball. Make an effort to bond over that shared interest, and you might find that other parts of your relationship improve as well.
