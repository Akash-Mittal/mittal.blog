Life is like art - Work hard, keep it simple and meaningful



There was once a young girl, Maria, who was fascinated by a painting at a local museum. She must have stood in front of it for over an hour, taking in every detail and brushstroke of the masterpiece. She was particularly awe-struck by the simplicity of the painting. It was just a blue square on a white canvas, yet it conveyed so much emotion and depth. Maria couldn't help but wonder how the artist managed to create something so profound with such simplicity.

The painting inspired Maria to pursue art. She took classes and practiced tirelessly. But as she progressed, she found herself getting caught up in complexity, trying to create intricate and ornate pieces of art. Despite her best effort, her art lacked the essence and impact of the painting that had originally moved her.

One day, Maria met a wise old artist, who, upon seeing her work, said, "Life is like art. You have to work hard to keep it simple and still have meaning."

These words struck a chord with Maria, and she started a new journey of simplicity. She simplified her art by eliminating unnecessary elements and focusing on the essence. She stopped trying to impress others and started creating from the heart. Her art transformed, and people started to connect with it on a deeper level.

The lesson in this story is applicable to all aspects of our lives. We often make life harder than it has to be by chasing after complexity. But simplicity is the key to finding meaning and depth. Let's explore it further.

Author - Charles de Lint

Charles de Lint is a Canadian author who masterfully blends fantasy, science fiction, and mythology to create a unique and captivating literary style. His writing is layered and rich, yet simple and accessible. Through his novels and short stories, he has taught us that complexity doesn't always equal quality.

Examples of Simplicity in Action:

1. Steve Jobs and Apple

Steve Jobs was a pioneer in using simplicity as a design principle. He believed that simplicity was the ultimate form of sophistication and that it was essential for Apple's products to be simple and intuitive. This philosophy is evident in everything Apple creates, from their iPhones to their retail stores.

2. Marie Kondo and Organizing

Marie Kondo is a Japanese organizing consultant whose "KonMari" method has taken the world by storm. Her philosophy is to eliminate clutter and only keep possessions that "spark joy." By simplifying our material possessions, we can create a more meaningful and joyful life.

Practical Tips for Keeping it Simple:

1. Focus on what's essential

Identify what's critical in your life and focus on those things. Eliminate anything that doesn't add value.

2. Simplify your surroundings

Declutter your home, workspace, and digital devices. Simplify your wardrobe and streamline your daily routines.

3. Embrace minimalism

Simplicity and minimalism go hand in hand. Consider adopting a minimalistic lifestyle by reducing your material possessions and embracing a more simplified way of living.

Conclusion:

1. Simplicity is the key to a meaningful and fulfilling life.

2. Complexity often leads to confusion and overwhelm.

3. Focus on what's essential, simplify your surroundings, and embrace minimalism to find simplicity in your life.
