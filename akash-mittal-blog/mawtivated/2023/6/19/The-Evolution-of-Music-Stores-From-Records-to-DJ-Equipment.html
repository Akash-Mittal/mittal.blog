The Evolution of Music Stores: From Records to DJ Equipment



Growing up, music stores were a haven for those who loved music. Entering one would be like entering a treasure trove of vinyl records, cassette tapes, and CDs. However, with the rise of digital music and the advent of DJing, the purpose of music stores has evolved. In this article, we take a look at the evolution of music stores and how they adapted to changing times.

Jam Master Jay: Pioneer of Modern Music Stores

Jam Master Jay, born Jason Mizell, was a renowned DJ and member of the hip-hop group Run-DMC. In the 90s, he opened up a music store in Queens, New York, called Scratch DJ Academy, which quickly became the go-to place for those interested in DJing. This store was unique in that it wasn't just about selling records, but also about teaching aspiring DJs the craft.

Jam Master Jay's passion for music and DJing led him to create a store that was the first of its kind. He realized that the future of music was in DJing and that it was essential to create a space where aspiring DJs could acquire the tools and knowledge they needed to succeed.

From Records to DJ Equipment

Before the advent of digital music, music stores primarily sold physical records, cassettes, and CDs. However, with the rise of digital music, the need for physical copies of music decreased. As a result, music stores had to adapt. They started stocking DJ equipment, such as mixers and turntables, to cater to the needs of aspiring DJs.

Nowadays, music stores sell a wide array of DJ equipment, from DJ mats and mixers to headphones and speakers. Aspiring DJs can walk into a music store and find everything they need to start their DJing journey. Additionally, music stores also offer DJing lessons and workshops to help aspiring DJs hone their skills.

Examples of Modern Music Stores

  • Guitar Center: Guitar Center is a popular music store that caters to musicians and DJs. They have a wide range of DJ equipment, from turntables to DJ controllers. Additionally, they offer DJing lessons and workshops.
  • Sam Ash: Sam Ash is another music store that offers a wide range of DJ equipment, from DJ controllers to mixers and headphones. They also have knowledgeable staff who can assist customers with their purchases.
  • Sweetwater: Sweetwater is an online music store that specializes in DJ equipment and software. They offer competitive pricing and expert advice on the equipment they sell.

Conclusion: Evolution of Music Stores in 3 Points

  1. Music stores have evolved from selling physical records to stocking DJ equipment.
  2. Modern music stores offer professional DJing lessons and workshops to help aspiring DJs hone their craft.
  3. The future of music stores lies in catering to the needs of DJs and other music enthusiasts.

Practical Tips

If you're an aspiring DJ looking to start your DJing journey, here are some practical tips:

  • Research and compare DJ equipment before making a purchase.
  • Take advantage of DJing lessons and workshops offered by music stores.
  • Practice regularly and experiment with different techniques to develop your unique style.
