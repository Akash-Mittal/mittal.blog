Raised by a Panamanian Man and a Jamaican Woman:
My Fascination with Jamaican Food



When I was a kid, my family lived in a one-bedroom apartment in Northwest D.C. Despite the cramped quarters, my parents always made sure I was fed well. While my mother cooked whatever she could afford, my father, who was Panamanian, would make dishes from his home country and the neighboring Caribbean islands.

It was through him that I first discovered Jamaican food. The savory spices, vibrant colors, and unique flavors were unlike anything I had ever tasted. I was hooked.

As I grew older, my fascination with Jamaican food only grew. I tried to learn as much as I could about the culture and cuisine. I would spend hours watching YouTube videos about different Jamaican dishes and dreaming of the day I would get to try them all.

Why Jamaican Food is Worth Exploring

Jamaican cuisine is a unique blend of flavors and influences from around the world. Due to the country's history, a fusion of African, European, and indigenous ingredients and cooking techniques have created a wide range of dishes. Here are some of my favorite Jamaican dishes:

  • Jerk chicken - a spicy dish made by marinating chicken in a blend of spices and grilling it over a wood fire
  • Curried goat - a flavorful curry made with tender goat meat and Jamaican curry powder
  • Rice and peas - a classic Jamaican side dish made with rice, coconut milk, and kidney beans
  • Coco bread - a sweet and fluffy bread that pairs perfectly with spicy meats or savory fillings

These dishes are just the beginning, and there is so much more to explore in Jamaican cuisine. Whether you're a foodie or just looking for something new, Jamaican food is definitely worth exploring.

My Journey with Jamaican Food

Over the years, I have tried many Jamaican restaurants, and even attempted to cook some dishes myself. While some of my experiments have been less than successful, I have learned a lot about the ingredients and techniques that make Jamaican cuisine so unique.

One of my most memorable experiences with Jamaican food was at a small restaurant in Kingston. The owner, a lovely Jamaican woman, welcomed our group with open arms and introduced us to some of the best dishes I have ever tasted. We shared a plate of jerk chicken, curried goat, and rice and peas. The flavors were so intense and complex, I felt like I was transported to the streets of Kingston.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Jamaican food is a fascinating and delicious cuisine that is worth exploring. Whether you're a seasoned foodie or just looking to try something new, there is something for everyone in Jamaican cuisine. To get started, I recommend trying some classic dishes like jerk chicken, curried goat, and rice and peas. Don't be afraid to experiment with new flavors and ingredients, and before you know it, you'll be cooking up a storm in your own kitchen.

  1. Try classic Jamaican dishes like jerk chicken, curried goat, and rice and peas
  2. Experiment with new flavors and ingredients
  3. Learn more about Jamaican culture and cuisine
