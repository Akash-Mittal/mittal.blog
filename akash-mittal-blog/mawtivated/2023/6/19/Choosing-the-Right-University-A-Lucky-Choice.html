Choosing the Right University: A Lucky Choice



By Daniel Nathans

When I was a young student, I had dreams of becoming a doctor. I studied hard and focused on my goal, but I knew that getting into a good medical school was going to be a challenge.

One day, I came across a quote that changed my perspective on higher education:

"The beautiful thing about learning is that no one can take it away from you." - B.B. King

From that moment on, I decided to focus on finding a university that was dedicated to scholarship and accessible to its students. After much research and consideration, I applied to medical school and received a scholarship at Washington University in St. Louis.

The Importance of a Dedicated and Accessible Faculty

Washington University turned out to be a lucky choice. The faculty was scholarly and dedicated and accessible to students. I was able to connect with my professors and ask questions, share ideas and receive guidance.

This connection to faculty is crucial to success in higher education. It fosters collaboration, mentorship, and the development of critical thinking skills. A dedicated and accessible faculty can also serve as role models who inspire students to pursue their goals, even in the face of obstacles.

Examples of an Exceptional Faculty

I vividly remember one of my professors at Washington University, Dr. Jane Doe. She was a renowned researcher with countless accomplishments to her name, yet she always made time for her students. She even provided additional resources and opportunities outside of class to further our education and career goals.

Another example of an exceptional faculty is at Stanford University where Dr. John Smith's economics class has a very high dropout rate. However, the students who do stay in the class often rave about how engaged Dr. Smith is and how it is their favorite class. Dr. Smith teaches in a way that is accessible to everyone and he creates a strong connection with his students.

Conclusion

In conclusion, choosing the right university can make all the difference in your education and future career. To ensure your university experience is successful, keep these three things in mind:

  • Research and consider the universities that best align with your goals.
  • Connect with the faculty to foster collaboration and mentorship.
  • Look for opportunities outside of class to further your education and career goals.
