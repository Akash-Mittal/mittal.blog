An Inspiring Story

Once upon a time in a strategy game, there was a player who was convinced that placing two home supply centers adjacent to each other was the key to winning. This player believed that having two powerful armies side by side would guarantee their dominance on the game board. However, their strategy failed miserably, and they were defeated by their opponents.

It was after this defeat that the player reached out to me, asking for advice on how to improve their game. I told them that there was one thing that they could change to drastically improve their chances of winning: the placement of their home supply centers.

The Importance of Home Supply Center Placement

As a game designer and enthusiast, I have spent countless hours analyzing and experimenting with different game mechanics and strategies. And one thing that I have learned over the years is the importance of home supply center placement.

Home supply centers are the backbone of any player's empire in strategy games. They provide the necessary resources to build armies, conquer territories, and ultimately win the game. However, the placement of these home supply centers can greatly affect the player's chances of success.

"If at all possible within the context of the game, don't have two home supply centers touching each other."

This quote from my personal game design philosophy highlights the significance of home supply center placement. Having two home supply centers touching each other can make them vulnerable to attack and can limit the player's strategic options. It also makes them more difficult to defend, as a single attack can wipe out multiple home supply centers at once.

On the other hand, strategically placing home supply centers apart from each other can create a strong and resilient empire. It allows the player to cover a wider area with their armies, making it more difficult for their opponents to attack. It also gives them more options for attacking their opponents, as they can approach from multiple angles.

and Case Studies

To illustrate the importance of home supply center placement, let me share with you some personal anecdotes and case studies.

Anecdote 1:

In a game of Total War: Rome II, I once made the mistake of placing two home supply centers adjacent to each other. I had a powerful army guarding them, and I felt confident that my opponent wouldn't be able to breach my defenses. However, my opponent surprised me with a flank attack that easily wiped out both of my home supply centers in a single turn. I was left with no resources and no defense, and ultimately lost the game.

Anecdote 2:

In a game of Risk, I strategically placed my home supply centers apart from each other, making it more difficult for my opponents to attack me. I was able to cover a wider area with my armies, and I had multiple options for attacking my opponents. This allowed me to gain an early lead in the game, which ultimately led to my victory.

Case Study:

In the game of Diplomacy, home supply center placement is critical to the success of a player. A study of the top Diplomacy players revealed that the winning players had a greater average distance between their home supply centers than the losing players. This indicates that strategic placement of home supply centers is a key factor in winning the game.

Examples of Effective Home Supply Center Placement

  • In Civilization VI, placing home supply centers on different sides of a river or mountain range can create a natural barrier that limits the opponent's ability to attack.
  • In Starcraft II, placing home supply centers near chokepoints or defensive structures can make them more difficult to breach.
  • In Axis & Allies, placing home supply centers on different continents can create a strong and resilient empire that is difficult to attack and conquer.

Conclusion

To summarize, the placement of home supply centers is a critical factor in the success of a player in strategy games. Strategic placement of these centers can create a strong and resilient empire that is difficult to attack and conquer. To ensure the best chances of success, follow these three rules:

  1. Avoid placing two home supply centers adjacent to each other.
  2. Strategically place home supply centers apart from each other to cover a wider area and create multiple attacking options.
  3. Create natural barriers or chokepoints near home supply centers to make them more difficult to breach.
