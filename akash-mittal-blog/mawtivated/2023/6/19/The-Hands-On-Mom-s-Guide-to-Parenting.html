The Hands-On Mom's Guide to Parenting



By Brooke Burke

Inspiring Story

I remember a time when I was driving my kids to school and saw a mom walking with her child on the sidewalk. As I got closer, I noticed that they were holding hands and singing a song together. It was such a beautiful moment and I was inspired to be more hands-on with my own kids. From then on, I made a conscious effort to be present and engaged in their lives as much as possible.

The Hands-On Mom

Being a hands-on mom means more than just being physically present. It means actively participating in your child's life and making a genuine effort to understand their needs and interests. As a working mom myself, I know how challenging it can be to balance career and family, but I believe that being hands-on is essential to raising happy, healthy children.

Personal Anecdote

I remember one summer when my family took a road trip across the country. We visited countless museums, historical landmarks, and national parks. It was an incredible experience and I was so grateful to be able to share it with my kids. But what made the trip truly special was that I took the time to research each destination ahead of time so that I could share interesting facts and stories with my children. I could see how engaged and curious they were, and it made all the planning worth it.

Case Study

I have a friend who is also a hands-on mom. She has three children, all of whom are involved in different extracurricular activities. Despite their busy schedules, my friend always makes time to attend their games, performances, and competitions. She also makes an effort to get to know their coaches and fellow teammates. As a result, her children feel supported and valued, and they have developed a strong sense of confidence and independence.

Tips for Being a Hands-On Mom

  • Set aside dedicated time for your children each day. This can be as simple as having a family dinner or playing a board game together.
  • Encourage your child's interests by researching activities or programs that align with their passions.
  • Get to know your child's friends, teachers, and other important people in their lives.
  • Be present and engaged in your child's activities, whether it's attending their soccer game or helping them with a school project.
  • Model positive behavior by showing empathy, kindness, and respect to others.

Conclusion

  1. Being a hands-on mom requires effort, but it is worth it in the end.
  2. Your child will feel loved, supported, and valued when you actively participate in their life.
  3. By being present and engaged, you are setting your child up for success and happiness in the future.
