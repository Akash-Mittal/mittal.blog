Marriage as a Sacrament



The Case for Traditional Values

An Inspiring Story

There was once a couple who had been together for 50 years. They had weathered many storms and overcome many challenges, but through it all, their love had only grown stronger. On their 50th wedding anniversary, they renewed their vows in a beautiful ceremony surrounded by their family and friends. As they looked into each other's eyes and pledged their love once again, it was clear that their marriage was not just a legal contract, but a sacred bond.

The Importance of Marriage as a Sacrament

Marriage is more than just a legal union between two people. It is a sacrament, a sacred bond between two individuals who have chosen to spend their lives together. It is a reflection of the divine love that exists within us all, and it should be treated as such.

"I very much feel that marriage is a sacrament and that sacrament should extend... to that legal entity of a union between what traditionally in our Western values has been defined as between a man and a woman." - Bill Frist

In today's society, the institution of marriage is under threat. Many people view it as outdated or unnecessary, and others believe that it should be redefined to include same-sex couples. However, those who see marriage as a sacrament understand the importance of preserving its traditional definition as a union between one man and one woman.

and Case Studies

There are countless examples of couples who have found joy and fulfillment in their traditional marriages. Take, for example, the story of John and Mary, who have been married for 30 years. They have raised three children together and supported each other through thick and thin. John says that the secret to their happy marriage is that they have always put each other first. "We may not always see eye to eye, but we know that the love we have for each other is more important than any disagreement," he says.

Similarly, studies have shown that children who grow up in traditional households fare better in a number of ways than those who come from single-parent or same-sex households. A 2012 report by the Institute for American Values found that children from intact, married families are more likely to graduate from high school, go to college, and avoid poverty than those from non-intact families.

Practical Tips for a Successful Marriage

  • Prioritize your relationship: Make time for each other, even if it's just a few minutes each day.
  • Communicate effectively: Be honest and open with each other, and don't be afraid to ask for what you need.
  • Practice forgiveness: No one is perfect, and everyone makes mistakes. Learn to forgive each other and move on.
  • Build a strong support system: Surround yourselves with friends and family who support your relationship and can offer guidance and advice when needed.
  • Remember that love is a choice: Even when things are tough, remember why you fell in love in the first place and choose to continue loving each other.

Conclusion

In conclusion, marriage is a sacrament that should be viewed as more than just a legal contract. Traditional values, which define marriage as a union between one man and one woman, should be upheld and protected. By prioritizing our relationships, communicating effectively, practicing forgiveness, building a strong support system, and remembering that love is a choice, we can build successful and fulfilling marriages that endure the test of time.
