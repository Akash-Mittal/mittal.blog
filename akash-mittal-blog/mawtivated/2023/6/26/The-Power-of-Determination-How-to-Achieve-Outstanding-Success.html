The Power of Determination - How to Achieve Outstanding Success



by George Allen, Sr.

An Inspiring Story of Determination

In 2008, a 30-year-old woman named Diana Nyad made her fifth attempt to swim from Cuba to Florida, a distance of 110 miles through shark-infested waters. In previous attempts, she had been stung by jellyfish, suffered from dehydration, and battled hallucinations. But on her fifth try, equipped with a new team and a renewed sense of determination, she finally succeeded. She became the first person to complete the swim without a shark cage, and at the age of 64 she proved that it's never too late to achieve your dreams.

"The journey was difficult, but I would not give up. I had a dream, and I was determined to make it come true."

Diana Nyad's story is just one example of the power of determination. People of all ages, backgrounds, and abilities have accomplished amazing things simply because they refused to quit. As the famous quote goes, "Most men succeed because they are determined to."

What is Determination?

Determination is a quality that allows us to pursue our goals with focus and persistence. It is the ability to overcome obstacles, bounce back from failures, and keep moving forward towards our aspirations despite the challenges we may face.

However, determination is not simply about being stubborn or rigid. It requires a certain level of flexibility and adaptability, as well as a willingness to learn and grow from our experiences.

Examples of Determination

There are countless examples of determination throughout history and in modern times. Here are just a few:

  • Walt Disney, who was fired from a newspaper for "lacking imagination" and went on to create one of the most successful entertainment companies in the world;
  • J.K. Rowling, who was rejected by multiple publishers before she finally found one willing to take a chance on her Harry Potter series, which went on to become a global sensation;
  • Stephen Hawking, who defied a debilitating disease to become one of the most brilliant scientists of our time, and penned best-selling books such as "A Brief History of Time";
  • Malala Yousafzai, who survived an assassination attempt by the Taliban and went on to become an activist for girls' education, a Nobel Peace Prize winner, and an inspiration to millions around the world.

These examples demonstrate that determination can lead to both personal and societal success. It can help us achieve our dreams, but it can also make a difference in the lives of others.

How to Develop Determination

While determination may be innate for some, it is also a quality that can be developed and strengthened over time. Here are some tips:

  • Set clear goals: Know what you want to achieve and break it down into manageable steps.
  • Find your why: Understand the reason behind your goals and connect with it emotionally.
  • Stay positive: Focus on your strengths and accomplishments, and avoid negative self-talk or rumination.
  • Be persistent: Don't give up at the first sign of failure. Use setbacks as learning opportunities and keep trying.
  • Build a support network: Surround yourself with people who believe in you and can offer encouragement and advice.
  • Practice self-care: Take care of your physical and mental health, and make time for activities that bring you joy and relaxation.

By incorporating these strategies into your life, you can increase your own level of determination and achieve more than you ever thought possible.

Conclusion: The Power of Determination in Three Points

  1. Determination is a quality that allows us to pursue our goals with focus and persistence.
  2. Determination can be developed and strengthened over time, with the right mindset and strategies.
  3. With determination, we can achieve our dreams, make an impact on the world, and inspire others to do the same.
