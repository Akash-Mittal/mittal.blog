Yoko Ono: Going with the Flow



An Inspiring Story

When Yoko Ono was studying at Sarah Lawrence College, she had a professor who asked her to write an essay on Beethoven. However, instead of writing about the music, Yoko wrote a tribute to silence. Despite the professor's disappointment, Yoko continued to pursue this unconventional way of thinking and went on to become one of the most influential avant-garde artists of the 20th century.

About the Author: Yoko Ono

Yoko Ono was born in Tokyo, Japan in 1933. She later moved to the United States and studied at Sarah Lawrence College, where she developed her unique artistic style. Yoko is known for her avant-garde work in a variety of fields, including music, art, and film. She is also known for her activism and her ongoing efforts for peace and social justice.

Going with the Flow: Any Style Can Be in My Music

Yoko Ono's philosophy when it comes to music is simple: go with the flow. According to Yoko, any style can be incorporated into her music, which makes it all the more exciting. This approach to music can be seen not only in Yoko's solo work, but also in her collaborations with other artists, including her late husband John Lennon.

Examples of Yoko's Style

  • Yoko's earliest musical work was with a group called the Fluxus movement, which was known for their experimentation with avant-garde art and music. One of Yoko's most well-known pieces from this time was called "Unfinished Music No. 1: Two Virgins," which was a collaboration with John Lennon and featured heavily improvised music.
  • In the 1970s, Yoko released a series of albums that incorporated elements of rock, jazz, and even disco. One of her most successful albums from this period was called "Fly," which featured a mix of experimental and more mainstream sounds.
  • In more recent years, Yoko has continued to collaborate with a variety of musicians and artists. Her 2016 album, "Yes, I'm a Witch Too," featured collaborations with artists as diverse as Sean Lennon, Moby, and Death Cab for Cutie.

Yoko's approach to music is not just about incorporating different styles; it's also about embracing the unexpected. In an interview with Rolling Stone, Yoko talked about how she sometimes creates music by accident:

"In the studio, I sometimes do rhythm tracks, and I do it by accident. I press the wrong button and I say, 'Oh, that's kind of interesting.' Then I try to do it again and I can't do it."

This willingness to embrace mistakes and accidents is a key part of Yoko's creative process.

Conclusion

Yoko Ono's approach to music is a reminder that creativity comes from a willingness to take risks and try new things. Here are three key takeaways:

  1. Embrace the unexpected: Sometimes the most interesting music comes from accidents or mistakes.
  2. Don't be afraid to mix genres: Incorporating elements of different styles can create a unique and exciting sound.
  3. Stay true to your vision: Yoko's work has always been unconventional, but she has remained committed to her artistic vision throughout her career.
