Leadership: The Art of Inspiring and Leading Soldiers



An Inspiring Story

During the War of 1812, General William Henry Harrison commanded a small army tasked with defending the Indiana Territory from British forces. One winter, his army was facing starvation and morale was low. Without hesitation, General Harrison distributed his own personal supplies to his soldiers and boosted their spirits by leading by example. His soldiers followed him into battle with renewed vigor and resilience.

Author - Sun Tzu

Sun Tzu was a Chinese military general, strategist, and philosopher who lived in the Eastern Zhou period of ancient China. His book, The Art of War is considered one of the greatest works on military strategy and tactics.

Regard Your Soldiers as Your Children

Sun Tzu emphasized the importance of treating your soldiers with respect and empathy. By regarding them as your children, you establish a sense of care and concern that will increase their loyalty to you. Soldiers want to be led by someone who cares about their wellbeing and is invested in their success.

  • Be approachable and visible to your soldiers.
  • Show that you care by addressing their needs and concerns.
  • Take time to listen and understand their perspective.
  • Provide support and resources to help them achieve their goals.

Look on Them as Your Own Beloved Sons

Sun Tzu understood the power of familial bonds in motivating soldiers. By looking on your soldiers as your own beloved sons, you establish a sense of pride and belonging that will motivate them to follow you into the toughest of battles. Soldiers want to be a part of something bigger than themselves and feel that their sacrifices are appreciated.

"We love to feel that our lives count for something and that we make a difference in the world. Strong leaders help soldiers believe they are doing something that matters. They help them understand that they are part of a game that is bigger than themselves, and that they stand on the right side of history."

To accomplish this, leaders must:

  • Create a strong sense of mission and purpose.
  • Clearly communicate their appreciation for the soldiers' sacrifices.
  • Encourage and praise the courage and bravery of the soldiers.
  • Maintain a positive attitude and inspire soldiers through their words and actions.

Conclusion

In conclusion, inspiring and leading soldiers requires a combination of empathy, vision, and effective communication skills. By following Sun Tzu's advice to regard your soldiers as your children and look on them as your own beloved sons, you can establish a culture of respect and trust that will increase their loyalty and motivation. Effective leadership inspires soldiers to give their very best and helps them to succeed as individuals and as a team. Remember:

  1. Create a culture of trust and respect.
  2. Provide support and resources for your soldiers to achieve their goals.
  3. Maintain a positive attitude and inspire your soldiers through your words and actions.
