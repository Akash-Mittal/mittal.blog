Leaving Formal Education Behind: A Success Story



By Sarah Zettel

When I graduated from the University of Michigan with a BA in Communications, I thought that was it for me. I was done with formal education and ready to start my career. But little did I know that leaving formal education behind would be the best decision I ever made.

Why I Left Formal Education Behind

I wasn't always sure about leaving formal education behind. Like most students, I thought that getting a degree from a prestigious university was the key to success. But as I progressed through my coursework, I began to feel disillusioned. I realized that I was not excited about the requirements and regulations of formal education.

Moreover, I was deeply dissatisfied with the quality of the education I was receiving. The coursework for my major seemed outdated and impractical. One day, I was discussing my frustrations with a professor and he said: "It sounds like you want to learn what you're passionate about. You don't need a degree for that." That simple statement changed my life.

How I Succeeded Without Formal Education

  • Identify Your Passions: When I left formal education behind, I wasn't sure what I wanted to do. But I knew I was passionate about writing. So I started writing as much as possible. I wrote articles, short stories, and eventually, novels. I learned everything I could about writing by reading books, attending workshops, and practicing my craft.
  • Build Your Network: As important as it was to learn on my own, I found it equally critical to connect with other people who shared my interests. I attended writing conferences, joined writing groups, and became an active member of writing communities. These connections, as much as my ability to write, helped me become a successful author.
  • Create Opportunities: The biggest challenge of leaving formal education behind was creating opportunities for myself. But I started small and eventually built a career writing novels and articles for major publications. I created my own opportunities by taking risks, submitting my work to contests and publishers, and persistently seeking out new connections and resources.

Key Takeaways for Success

Leaving formal education behind may seem daunting, but it is not impossible to succeed. Some key takeaways from my journey include:

  1. Follow your passions: Life is short, so it's important to do what you love. Pursue activities and interests that genuinely excite you.
  2. Bulk up your networks: Meeting new people who share your interests can open up new opportunities and career paths. Join online forums, attend meet-ups or conferences to grow your network.
  3. Take calculated risks: Opportunities don't come to those who wait. Take a chance on yourself and seek out ways to become a doer, not a watcher, get involved in projects and commit to your work.
"The only true way to learn something is through experience."

About Sarah Zettel
