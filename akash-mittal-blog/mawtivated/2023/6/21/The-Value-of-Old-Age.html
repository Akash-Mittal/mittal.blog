The Value of Old Age: It's All in the Person Reaching It



Thomas Hardy, a famous English novelist and poet, once said, "The value of old age depends upon the person who reaches it. To some men of early performance it is useless. To others, who are late to develop, it just enables them to finish the job." This statement holds true even up to today.

An Inspiring Story

Let me introduce you to Richard Glauser, a retired businessman who decided to start his own company at the age of 65. Many people would have opted to travel or spend time with their families during their retirement years, but not Richard. With his years of experience and wealth of knowledge, he confidently ventured into the world of entrepreneurship.

Richard did face some challenges along the way, but nothing he couldn't handle. His company eventually succeeded, and he even won an award for 'Entrepreneur of the Year' at the ripe old age of 73!

The Value of Old Age

As Thomas Hardy pointed out, the value of old age depends on the person who reaches it. Let's take a closer look at some examples:

  • Early Performers: Some people reach their peak at a young age. For them, old age might seem useless. They may have already accomplished everything they dreamed of and feel that they have nothing more to do. They may feel that they have reached the end of the road and that there is nowhere else to go.
  • Late Bloomers: On the other hand, some people are late to develop. For them, reaching old age gives them the opportunity to finish what they started. Old age gives them the chance to achieve what they couldn't earlier in their lives. They have more time and experience to work towards their goals.

Take the example of my grandmother. She was a stay-at-home mom all her life, raising her children and taking care of her family. When she finally had all the free time in the world after retirement, she decided to pursue her passion for painting. She had always been interested in art but never had the chance to pursue it earlier. Now, at the age of 80, she exhibits her paintings and even sells them!

Another example is my neighbor, who was a high school teacher all her life. After retiring, she started volunteering at a local shelter and became the go-to person for animal rescue in the community. She may have spent her life teaching children, but she found her true calling later in life.

Conclusion

  1. Age is just a number. It's never too late to pursue your passions or achieve your dreams.
  2. Don't let age hold you back. Use your experience to your advantage and go after what you want.
  3. Never underestimate the value of old age. It's all in the person who reaches it.

Practical Tips

  • Stay active and stay connected with your community. Join a club or organizations that align with your interests.
  • Always keep learning. Take a class or a course, learn a new skill, practice a new hobby.
  • Take care of your physical and mental health. Exercise regularly and seek support when needed.
