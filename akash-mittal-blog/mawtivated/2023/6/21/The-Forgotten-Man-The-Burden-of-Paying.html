The Forgotten Man: The Burden of Paying



Once upon a time, there was a man who lived in a small village. He was hardworking, honest, and always willing to help his neighbors. However, no one seemed to notice his efforts, and he was often overlooked or forgotten. This man represented the forgotten man in society, the one who works hard, pays taxes, and yet receives little recognition or help.

The forgotten man was not a new concept and had been discussed by many scholars throughout history. However, it was William Graham Sumner, an American sociologist and professor at Yale University, who popularized the term in the late 19th century. Sumner described the forgotten man as the hardworking, tax-paying individual who is burdened with the cost of maintaining society and yet receives little to no benefits in return.

The forgotten man is the one who works long hours to provide for his family and pay for his expenses. He is the one who sacrifices his dreams and ambitions to fulfill his obligations. He is the backbone of society, yet he is often ignored or taken for granted.

  • He pays for the education of his children, yet he has limited access to quality education himself.
  • He pays for healthcare, yet he struggles to afford his own medical bills.
  • He pays for infrastructure and public services, yet he is stuck in traffic and waiting in long lines.
  • He pays for the salaries of government officials, yet he has limited influence on policy decisions.
  • He pays for social programs and aid, yet he often receives criticism and stigma for needing assistance.

The burden of paying falls heavily on the forgotten man, and it is essential to acknowledge his contributions and address his concerns. Here are three practical tips to support the forgotten man:

  1. Invest in education and skills training to enable him to progress and access better-paying jobs.
  2. Provide affordable healthcare and financial assistance to reduce the burden of medical expenses.
  3. Encourage civic engagement and participation to empower him to make informed decisions and raise awareness of his needs.
"The forgotten man... He works, he votes, generally he prays, but his chief business in life is to pay." - William Graham Sumner

It is time to remember and value the forgotten man. Let us recognize his efforts and ensure that he receives the support he needs to thrive in society. By investing in his well-being and potential, we can create a more equitable and prosperous community for all.
