The Silence of Laws in the Time of War



War is a time when chaos reigns. The normal rules that govern society are suspended, and people are forced to fight for their lives and their freedom. In such a time, laws often become silent, as they are ignored or broken in the name of survival. This article will explore the role of laws in a time of war, and how their silence can have both positive and negative effects.

An Inspiring Story

Amidst the devastation of World War II, there were still acts of humanity and compassion. One such story is that of Nicholas Winton, a British humanitarian who saved the lives of 669 Jewish children from Czechoslovakia.

In 1938, Winton was planning to take a skiing trip to Switzerland, but instead he traveled to Prague to help a friend who was involved in refugee work. While there, he was horrified to see the plight of Jewish families desperately trying to escape Nazi oppression. Winton decided to do something about it, and over the next year he arranged for the transportation of hundreds of children to safety in Britain.

Winton's efforts were far from legal - he had to forge documents, bribe officials, and break immigration laws. But he did it because he knew it was the right thing to do. His actions saved the lives of 669 children, many of whom went on to have families of their own. Winton's story is a reminder that even in the midst of war, there are still opportunities to act with compassion and make a difference.

Marcus Tullius Cicero and the Power of Laws

Marcus Tullius Cicero was a Roman statesman, lawyer, and philosopher who lived from 106 BC to 43 BC. He is considered one of the greatest orators in history, and his writings on politics, law, and philosophy continue to be studied today.

Cicero believed in the power of laws to govern society and protect the rights of individuals. He argued that laws should be based on reason and justice, and that they should be applied equally to all citizens. Cicero believed that without laws, society would descend into chaos, and the strong would overpower the weak.

Cicero's belief in the importance of laws is still relevant today, especially in times of war. When laws are silent, chaos can ensue, and those in power may take advantage of the situation to further their own interests. It is important to remember that even in times of war, the rule of law should be upheld as much as possible, in order to protect the rights of all individuals.

Examples of Laws Being Silent in Times of War

  • In World War I, both the Allied and Central powers engaged in unrestricted submarine warfare, which involved sinking enemy ships without warning. This violated international law, but was seen as necessary for military victory.
  • During the Vietnam War, the US military engaged in a campaign of bombing and chemical warfare that caused massive destruction and loss of life. Critics argued that these actions violated the Geneva Convention, but the US government defended them as necessary for national security.
  • After the terrorist attacks of September 11, 2001, the US government authorized the use of torture and indefinite detention without trial in its war on terror. These actions were widely criticized as violations of human rights and the rule of law, but were defended as necessary for national security.

These examples illustrate how the silence of laws in times of war can lead to actions that are illegal or unethical. It is important to remember that just because a law is silent, does not mean that it is ethical or justifiable.

Conclusion

  1. The silence of laws in times of war can lead to actions that are illegal or unethical.
  2. The rule of law should be upheld as much as possible in times of war, in order to protect the rights of all individuals.
  3. Even in times of war, there are still opportunities to act with compassion and make a difference.

Practical tips for upholding the rule of law in times of war include holding those in power accountable for their actions, supporting organizations that protect human rights, and advocating for peaceful solutions to conflicts.
