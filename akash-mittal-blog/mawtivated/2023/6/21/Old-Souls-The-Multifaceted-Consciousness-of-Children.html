Old Souls: The Multifaceted Consciousness of Children



An Inspiring Story

As a mother of three, I have always been in awe of the beauty and wonder of my children. But it wasn't until my youngest son was born that I truly understood the concept of old souls and their multifaceted consciousness. From the moment he came into this world, it was clear that he was not like other children. His eyes seemed to hold a depth of wisdom and knowledge that was far beyond his years. It was almost as if he had lived a thousand lifetimes and had been reborn into the body of a child. As he grew up, his ability to understand complex concepts and ideas only continued to astound me. His mind was a kaleidoscope of thoughts and emotions, and I knew that he was destined for greatness.

The Concept of Multifaceted Consciousness

According to Shirley MacLaine, children are not just blank slates waiting to be filled with knowledge and experience. Instead, they possess a "multipersonhood" that is aware of its own complexity and depth. They have a nucleus at the center of their consciousness that serves as the repository for all the knowledge and experiences they have accumulated over previous lifetimes. This multifaceted consciousness allows children to tap into a wealth of information and insights that go far beyond the realm of what we consider traditional education or learning.

MacLaine argues that it is important for parents, teachers, and caregivers to recognize and honor this multifaceted consciousness in children. Instead of trying to mold them into a certain image or expectation, we should allow them the freedom to explore and grow in their own unique way. This means providing them with the tools and resources they need to expand their knowledge and creativity, but also letting them follow their own intuition and inner guidance.

Examples of Multifaceted Consciousness in Action

  • A three-year-old child who can speak multiple languages fluently, despite never having been formally taught
  • A six-year-old who has an advanced understanding of quantum physics and can explain complex concepts to adults
  • A twelve-year-old artist who can create breathtaking pieces of art that showcase an understanding of color theory, composition, and light that goes beyond their years of experience
  • A nine-year-old musician who can play multiple instruments with virtuosity and compose their own original pieces that evoke a wide range of emotions

While these examples may seem extraordinary, MacLaine believes that they are only the tip of the iceberg when it comes to the potential of multifaceted consciousness in children. When we allow them the space and freedom to explore their own interests and abilities, they can tap into a wellspring of creativity and insight that can change the world.

Practical Tips for Cultivating Multifaceted Consciousness in Children

  • Encourage curiosity and exploration by providing children with a wide variety of toys, books, and other resources that spark their interest
  • Support their natural talents and abilities by providing them with opportunities to develop their skills through lessons, workshops, or other forms of instruction
  • Acknowledge their accomplishments and successes, no matter how small, to boost their confidence and self-esteem
  • Provide opportunities for social interaction and collaboration with other children to foster a sense of community and teamwork
  • Allow them the freedom to make mistakes and learn from them, without fear of judgment or punishment
  • Encourage open communication and dialogue, and provide opportunities for children to express their thoughts and feelings without interruption or judgment

Conclusion

  1. Children are not blank slates, but rather possess a complex and multifaceted consciousness that allows them to tap into a wealth of knowledge and insight
  2. It is important for parents, teachers, and caregivers to recognize and honor this multifaceted consciousness, and provide opportunities for children to explore and grow in their own unique way
  3. By cultivating curiosity, providing support and encouragement, and allowing for open communication, we can help children tap into their full potential and become agents of change in the world
