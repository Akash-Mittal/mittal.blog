How Bad Girls Can Teach Us the Art of Pursuing Happiness



An Inspiring Story of a Bookish Child in Calcutta

As a bookish child in Calcutta, I used to thrill to the adventures of bad girls whose pursuit of happiness swept them outside the bounds of social decency. Tess of the d'Urbervilles, Emma Bovary and Anna Karenina lived large in my imagination. The naughty girls of Hollywood films flirted and knew how to drive.

Author - Bharati Mukherjee Story or Background

Bharati Mukherjee was an award-winning Indian-American writer. Born in Calcutta, she came to the United States in 1961 to pursue graduate studies in creative writing. Her novels and short stories often explore the experiences of immigrants and their struggle to assimilate into American culture. She passed away in 2017, leaving behind a legacy of thought-provoking and inspiring literature.

Why Bad Girls are Good Role Models

Bad girls have been a fascination for generations, and for good reason. They represent a departure from the norm, a rejection of the status quo, and a determination to live life on their own terms. So what can we learn from them?

1. The Importance of Independence

Bad girls are not afraid to stand on their own two feet and make their own way in the world. They value their independence and refuse to let society dictate the course of their lives. This is a valuable lesson for all of us, especially women who may feel pressured to conform to traditional gender roles.

"I am not an angel," Emma proclaimed, "and I will not be one till I die: I will take care to sin boldly. But, really, when I look around me, I feel proud to live in a society where you are free to be whatever you want to be." - Gustave Flaubert, Madame Bovary

2. The Power of Self-Determination

Bad girls also teach us the importance of following our own instincts and passions. They refuse to let others determine their path in life, and instead choose to blaze their own trail. This is a valuable lesson for anyone who has ever felt stuck in a job or a relationship that doesn't fulfill them.

"I am not afraid of storms, for I am learning how to sail my ship." - Louisa May Alcott, Little Women

3. The Joys of Living Life to the Fullest

Finally, bad girls show us that life is meant to be enjoyed to the fullest. They embrace their sexuality, take risks, and live in the moment. This is a valuable lesson for anyone who has ever felt weighed down by the responsibilities of adult life.

"I declare after all there is no enjoyment like reading! How much sooner one tires of any thing than of a book! When I have a house of my own, I shall be miserable if I have not an excellent library." - Jane Austen, Pride and Prejudice

Examples of Bad Girls We Can Look up to

Here are some examples of bad girls who have made their mark on the world:

  • Marilyn Monroe
  • Frida Kahlo
  • Janis Joplin
  • Gloria Steinem
  • Nina Simone

Conclusion

In conclusion, bad girls have a lot to teach us about pursuing happiness. By valuing their independence, following their own passions, and living life to the fullest, they have left a legacy that we can all aspire to. So embrace your inner bad girl and start living life on your own terms!

Three points to remember:

  1. Value your independence and refuse to let society dictate your path in life.
  2. Follow your own instincts and passions, and don't be afraid to take risks.
  3. Embrace your sexuality and live life to the fullest.
