The Ethics in the Art World: A Critical Examination



An Inspiring Story of Artistic Integrity

The story of Marina Abramovic, a Serbian performance artist, serves as a powerful example of the importance of ethical behavior in the art world.

In her work, Abramovic often pushes the boundaries of what is considered acceptable in art, inviting her audience to participate in her performances in various ways. In one work, she sat immobile for over 700 hours while visitors sat across from her, sometimes engaging in eye contact and other times not.

However, despite the controversy that sometimes surrounds her art, Abramovic maintains a strict code of ethics. In 2010, she was approached by wealthy collectors who wanted to commission a private performance piece. Abramovic agreed to the project, but only on the condition that they donate the same amount of money to her institute for preserving performance art.

By sticking to her principles, Abramovic demonstrated that ethics and integrity should be at the forefront of every artist's decision-making process.

The Role of Ethics in the Art World

While the art world isn't typically associated with rules and regulations, ethical considerations are crucial to maintaining the integrity of the industry. Without a code of ethics, artists, gallerists, and academics could easily succumb to the temptations of fame, fortune, and power.

  • Creative Integrity: The first and most important consideration for any artist should be creative integrity. This means staying true to your artistic vision and avoiding the temptation to cater to the demands of galleries or collectors.
  • Transparency: Galleries should be transparent about their financial dealings and avoid engaging in shady or unethical practices, such as price-fixing or hiding information about an artist's work or background.
  • Academic Integrity: Academics should prioritize their duty to teach and educate, rather than pursuing their own personal agendas or using their influence to promote certain artists or movements.
"Of course art world ethics are important. But museums are no purer than any other institution or business. Academics aren't necessarily more high-minded than gallerists." - Jerry Saltz

As Jerry Saltz, a renowned art critic and columnist for New York magazine, points out, the art world is no different from any other industry, and maintaining ethical standards is critical to promoting fairness and preventing corruption.

Throughout his career as an art critic, Jerry Saltz has come across numerous examples of both ethical and unethical behavior in the art world. In one instance, he recounted an experience where a wealthy collector offered him a bribe to promote a particular artist.

Saltz refused the offer, demonstrating his commitment to journalistic integrity. In another instance, he criticized a prominent museum for exhibiting work by an artist who had been accused of sexual assault.

These examples illustrate the importance of ethical decision-making in the art world and the potential consequences of failing to uphold these standards.

Examples of Ethical Behavior in the Art World

  • Theaster Gates: Gates, a Chicago-based artist, has made ethical considerations a central part of his artistic practice. He has revitalized neglected neighborhoods by creating community art centers and training programs for local residents.
  • Pablo Helguera: Helguera, a New York-based artist and educator, has written extensively about the responsibility of artists to create work that is socially and politically engaged. He has also hosted numerous community events and workshops aimed at promoting dialogue and social change through art.

These artists demonstrate that ethical considerations and artistic creativity are not mutually exclusive, and that artists can use their work to make a positive impact on society.

Conclusion:

  1. Creative integrity should be the top priority for every artist. While it can be tempting to cater to the demands of collectors and galleries, compromising your creative vision can ultimately compromise your reputation and your ability to make meaningful contributions to the art world.
  2. Transparency and honesty are crucial for maintaining the integrity of the industry. Galleries, collectors, and museums must be transparent about their financial dealings and avoid engaging in unethical practices that could damage the reputation of the artist and the industry as a whole.
  3. Artists have a responsibility to use their work to promote positive change. Whether it's through community outreach or socially engaged artwork, artists have the power to create meaningful change and contribute to a more ethical and socially just art world.
