The Basic Comforts in Time of Loss



A Tribute to Our Fallen Heroes

It was a sunny day in June when Jenny received the news that changed her life forever. Her husband, Sergeant Tom Davis had been killed in action while on duty overseas. Shock, disbelief, and overwhelming sadness took over her as she tried to come to terms with her loss.

Jenny's family was devastated, and they struggled to find comfort and support in their time of mourning. But through the fog of grief, there was one thing that gave them solace: the knowledge that their country was grateful for Tom's sacrifice and service.

At a family's most difficult time, it is crucial to provide them with the very basic of comforts. These simple acts of kindness can make a world of difference in helping them cope with their loss and move forward with their lives.

What Are the Basic Comforts?

The basic comforts are the minimum requirements to ensure that the grieving families of our fallen heroes have a peaceful environment that allows them to mourn their loss in private.

  • A private and dignified return of the remains of their loved one.
  • A private and respectful notification of their loved one's death.
  • Adequate grief counseling and emotional support services to help them with their healing process.
  • The understanding that their loved one made the ultimate sacrifice for their country and that their service was appreciated and will not be forgotten.

These essential comforts provide a foundation for grieving families to begin to heal and cope with their loss. They allow them to mourn in private and take solace in the knowledge that their loved one's service and sacrifice were not in vain.

Dave Reichert is a former Congressman and sheriff of King County, Washington. His experience in law enforcement and public service has allowed him to witness firsthand the profound impact that the loss of a loved one in the line of duty can have on a family.

"In my time in law enforcement, I have seen how important it is for families to have the support they need when they lose a loved one. This is especially true for our military families who have sacrificed so much for our country. Providing them with the basic comforts is the least we can do to show our gratitude and respect for their service."

Dave's commitment to supporting grieving families is evident in his work with the Gold Star Family Tax Relief Act, which he sponsored to provide tax relief to families of fallen service members.

Another example of the impact of the basic comforts can be seen in the story of Sheila, whose son was killed in Iraq in 2006. She recalls the deep sense of comfort she felt when two soldiers arrived at her doorstep to notify her of her son's death. Their professionalism and compassion made a challenging situation more bearable for Sheila and her family.

How Can We Provide the Basic Comforts?

As a society, we must ensure that the basic comforts are a priority for our fallen heroes' families. Here are some practical ways we can make this a reality:

  1. Support legislation that provides resources for families of fallen service members to receive grief counseling, financial assistance, and emotional support.
  2. Encourage employers to offer flexible work schedules and time off for employees who are supporting a family member after the loss of a loved one.
  3. Volunteer with organizations that support military families or donate your time and resources to provide care packages, meals, or other forms of support.
  4. Use social media to raise awareness and advocate for basic comforts for military families.

By providing basic comforts to our fallen heroes' families, we are showing them that their loved one's sacrifice was appreciated and that we will never forget their service to our country.
