The Importance of Peace in Achieving Economic Aims



Have you ever heard the tale of the two countries?

Once upon a time, there were two neighboring countries - Country A and Country B. Country A was rich in natural resources while Country B lacked them. Country A continuously exported their resources to other countries while Country B struggled to keep up with the demand. However, despite their differences, the leaders of both countries consistently engaged in conflict with each other.

Eventually, the two countries found themselves in a war that lasted for years. The economy of both countries plummeted, and the citizens suffered. It was only when the war ended, and peace was restored, that both countries were able to recover.

This story highlights the importance of peace in achieving economic aims. The attainment of economic growth, development, and stability require a peaceful environment. Disruptions such as wars, civil unrests, and conflicts can lead to the destruction of essential infrastructure, inflation, stagnation, and even economic collapse.

Author - Ludwig von Mises

Ludwig von Mises was a prominent economist and philosopher of the twentieth century. He was a fierce defender of classical liberalism and believed that free-market capitalism was the most efficient and effective economic system.

Examples

  • During World War II, many countries suffered the destruction of their infrastructure, resulting in socio-economic consequences that lasted for years after the war ended.
  • The civil war in Syria has resulted in one of the worst humanitarian crisis in history, with millions of refugees, death, and destruction.
  • The ongoing conflict in Yemen has created a famine that affected millions of people due to the unstable and non-existent economy.

Conclusion

  1. Peace is essential for economic growth, stability, and development.
  2. Conflicts and wars disrupt essential infrastructures and lead to economic collapse.
  3. The promotion of peace is an international issue and requires the cooperation and coordination of everyone.

Practical Tips

  • Engage in peaceful activities and support the promotion of peace.
  • Participate in the political process, and advocate for peaceful solutions.
  • Support organizations that promote peace and help find practical and sustainable solutions to conflicts.
