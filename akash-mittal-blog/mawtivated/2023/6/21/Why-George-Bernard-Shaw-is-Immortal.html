Why George Bernard Shaw is Immortal



An Inspiring Story

George Bernard Shaw was born in Ireland in 1856. Despite coming from a poor family, Shaw was determined to become a writer. He started off as a journalist, but it wasn't until his play "Arms and the Man" was produced in 1894 that he achieved success as a playwright. Over the course of his career, Shaw wrote more than 60 plays, including "Pygmalion" (1912), which was later adapted into the musical "My Fair Lady." In addition to his plays, Shaw was also a prolific essayist and critic, and a passionate advocate for political and social reform.

The Personality of George Bernard Shaw

Max Beerbohm once said, "As a teacher, as a propagandist, Mr. Shaw is no good at all, even in his own generation. But as a personality, he is immortal." This statement is a testament to Shaw's enduring legacy and influence in the world of literature and politics. Shaw was known for his wit and humor, his outspoken opinions on a wide range of topics, and his dedication to causes such as women's rights and socialism.

Shaw's personality was so strong that it often overshadowed his work as a writer. He was a master of the quip and the one-liner, and had a talent for cutting through pretense and exposing the flaws in society. For example, when someone asked him if he believed in free love, Shaw responded, "Certainly, as long as it's not compulsory."

Examples of Shaw's Influence

  • Shaw's plays continue to be performed and studied around the world, and have been adapted for television, film, and the stage.
  • Shaw's essay "The Quintessence of Ibsenism" helped to popularize the works of playwright Henrik Ibsen, who was relatively unknown in England at the time.
  • Shaw's political activism and advocacy for women's rights and socialism influenced many of his contemporaries, as well as future generations of activists and writers.

Conclusion

  1. Shaw's legacy as a writer and political activist continues to inspire people around the world.
  2. Shaw's wit and humor, as well as his dedication to social justice, have made him a beloved figure in popular culture.
  3. Whether you are a fan of Shaw's plays, essays, or political writings, there is no denying the enduring impact that he has had on the literary and political landscape.
