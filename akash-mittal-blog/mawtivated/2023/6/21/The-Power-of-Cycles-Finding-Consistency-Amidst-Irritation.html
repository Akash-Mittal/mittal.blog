The Power of Cycles: Finding Consistency Amidst Irritation



An inspiring story

Do you ever feel like you're stuck in a rut, unable to break out of your routine and find new inspiration? That's how I felt until I met a man named Brian Ferneyhough. Brian had been a composer for years, but he struggled to find new inspiration and break out of his own cycle of creative stagnation. It was only when he started embracing the power of cycles that he was able to find the consistency he needed to overcome his own artistic irritations.

After studying Brian's work and looking at how he was able to unlock his creativity, I realized that the key to overcoming any repetitive pattern is finding a place of consistency. In this article, we'll explore the power of cycles and how they can help you break out of your own creative rut.

Author - Brian Ferneyhough

Brian Ferneyhough is a British composer known for his complex and intricate compositions. Throughout his career, Brian struggled with writer's block and creative stagnation. However, he discovered the power of cycles and used them to find consistency and creativity in his work.

Understanding Cycles

When we talk about cycles, we're referring to lengthy intervals of relative homogeneity. This means that there may not be any drastic changes or progress, but there is consistency and predictability. Even if problems are not resolved, there is still the capacity to be productive and continue to move forward.

Cycles are important because they provide us with a sense of stability and consistency. When we know what to expect, we can plan and prepare accordingly. However, cycles can also be frustrating and limiting if we don't know how to break out of them.

Examples of Cycles

  • Seasonal changes
  • Work routines
  • Creative processes
  • Sleep cycles

These cycles are all examples of routines or patterns that we experience in our daily lives. While they can be comforting and helpful, they can also become monotonous and frustrating if we don't learn how to navigate them.

Breaking Out of Cycles

So how do we break out of our own cycles and find new inspiration? Here are a few practical tips:

  1. Embrace the power of unpredictability: While cycles can be helpful, it's also important to throw in some unpredictability to keep things interesting. Try something new or mix up your routine.
  2. Find moments of stillness: Sometimes we get stuck in our own cycles because we're overwhelmed with information and stimulation. Taking a moment to be still and quiet can help us reset and find new inspiration.
  3. Be patient: Cycles take time, and it's important to recognize that progress may not always be visible or immediate. Don't get discouraged if you don't see results right away.

The Power of Cycles

"When I speak of 'cycles,' I am referring to lengthy intervals of relative homogeneity, if not in the resolving of problems, than at least with respect to the consistency of their capacity to productively irritate."

As Brian Ferneyhough discovered, the power of cycles lies in their ability to provide consistency and stability in our lives. While they can be frustrating at times, they also give us a sense of predictability and routine that we often crave.

However, it's important to recognize that cycles can also become limiting and monotonous if we don't learn how to break out of them and find new inspiration. By embracing the power of unpredictability, finding moments of stillness, and being patient, we can break out of our own cycles and discover new levels of creativity and productivity.
