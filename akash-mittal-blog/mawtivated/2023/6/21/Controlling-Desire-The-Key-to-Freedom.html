Controlling Desire: The Key to Freedom



Freedom is a precious commodity that everyone desires, but not everyone achieves. Many people associate freedom with the ability to pursue their desires and wants. However, the reality is something different - freedom is not procured by a full enjoyment of what is desired, but by controlling the desire. The great philosopher Epictetus once said:

"Freedom is the only worthy goal in life. It is won by disregarding things that lie beyond our control."
Epictetus was born a slave, but he became one of the most influential philosophers in history. He lived his life as an example of how one could achieve freedom in any circumstance.

The Story of Epictetus

Epictetus was born in 50 AD in Hierapolis, Turkey. He was born into slavery and was owned by a wealthy man who recognized his intelligence and potential. The man gave Epictetus an education, and he eventually became a student of philosophy. He studied under the philosopher Musonius Rufus and later opened his own school in Rome.

Epictetus had a simple philosophy - a person can only control their own thoughts and actions. He believed that the pursuit of material wealth and pleasure would lead to suffering and was therefore a waste of time. He advised his students to focus on what they could control and let go of what they could not.

Controlling Desire

Epictetus believed that controlling our desires was the key to freedom. When we desire something intensely, we become attached to it. We become slaves to our desires and lose our freedom. On the other hand, when we learn to control our desires, we become free.

For example, imagine you desire a new car. You may be consumed by thoughts of getting the car, worrying about the cost, and imagining how it will improve your life. But if you learn to control that desire, you won't be attached to it. You can appreciate the car without becoming obsessed with it. You can be free from the desire to have it.

Controlling desire is not easy, but it is necessary. Epictetus believed that by controlling our desires, we could focus on what we can control - our own thoughts and actions. When we focus on what we can control, we can achieve a state of inner peace and freedom.

Practical Tips

Here are some practical tips for controlling desire and achieving freedom:

  • Mindfulness meditation: Practice mindfulness meditation to become aware of your thoughts and desires. This can help you observe and control them.
  • Gratitude journaling: Take time each day to write down what you are grateful for. This can help shift your focus from what you want to what you have.
  • Practice letting go: When you feel attached to something, remind yourself that it is outside of your control. Let go of the attachment and focus on what you can control.

Conclusion

Controlling desire is the key to freedom. Epictetus believed that by focusing on what we can control - our own thoughts and actions - we can achieve a state of inner peace and freedom. By controlling our desires, we become free from attachment and can appreciate life without becoming slaves to our wants.

  2. Practice gratitude journaling
  3. Practice letting go
