The Art of Defending Your Freedom



Freedom, they say, isn't free. It comes with a price, and that price is vigilance. History is replete with examples of peoples and nations who lost their freedom because they thought they could protect it by making themselves inoffensive to their enemies. Perhaps no one said it better than Dean Acheson when he observed that "no people in history have ever survived who thought they could protect their freedom by making themselves inoffensive to their enemies."


Dean Acheson Background

Before we delve into the art of defending freedom, perhaps it's best to understand who Dean Acheson was and what makes his quote so powerful. Born on April 11, 1893, in Middletown, Connecticut, Dean Acheson was an American statesman and lawyer who served as the U.S. secretary of state from 1949–1953. Though he espoused liberal democratic ideals and believed in the power of compromise, he also recognized the dangers of appeasement and the need to stand firm against adversaries who threatened freedom and democracy.


An Inspiring Story

When it comes to standing up for freedom, one of the many stories that come to mind is that of French politician Andre Malraux. While giving a speech at a trade union congress in Les Rois, France, in 1936, Malraux noticed a group of fascists in the audience who were interrupting the proceedings. Rather than cower or appease them, Malraux took off his jacket, rolled up his sleeves, and challenged the fascists to a fistfight. The fascists backed down, and Malraux continued his speech to thunderous applause. While it's not always advisable to resort to physical violence, Malraux's story illustrates that sometimes the best defense is a good offense.


and Case Studies

Personal anecdotes and case studies can also help illustrate the art of defending freedom. Take, for instance, the story of Michal Šimonovský, a Czech journalist who, in 2019, was threatened with arrest after publishing an article criticizing the country's prime minister. Rather than remain silent or back down, Šimonovský doubled down on his criticism and turned himself into police. His brave act of defiance not only put a spotlight on the prime minister's autocratic tendencies but also inspired a groundswell of support for a free press.


Examples of Defending Freedom

  • Protesting against unjust laws and policies
  • Exercising your right to free speech and expression
  • Becoming a whistleblower to expose corruption and wrongdoing
  • Engaging in civil disobedience to challenge unjust authority

The Power of Three

When it comes to the art of defending freedom, here are three key takeaways:

  1. Don't be inoffensive to your enemies
  2. Don't remain silent in the face of injustice
  3. Don't give up your freedom without a fight

Practical Tips

  1. Stay informed and educated about the issues that threaten freedom
  2. Get involved in activism and community organizing
  3. Support organizations and individuals who defend freedom and democracy
  4. Speak out against injustice and oppression
  5. Stay vigilant and be prepared to defend your rights and freedoms
