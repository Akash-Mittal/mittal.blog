From £10 Poms to Australian Icons: The Story of Immigration Down Under



An inspiring journey of immigrants who made Australia their home.

Author's Story: Hugh Jackman

Hugh Jackman, a celebrated actor and producer, was born to English immigrants who moved to Sydney in search of a better life. Growing up in the diverse city of Sydney, Hugh felt a deep connection to Australia and its people. He often shares his experience of growing up in a multicultural environment and the impact it had on him.

The £10 Pom Scheme

In the late 1940s, the Australian government launched the £10 Pom Scheme. The aim of the scheme was to attract people from Britain and Canada to migrate to Australia. The scheme earned its name from the cost of the one-way ticket, which was £10. The scheme ran until 1972 and brought around 1.5 million people to Australia.

Many of the people who migrated to Australia during the scheme were skilled workers who were in demand in the growing economy. The scheme played a significant role in shaping the social, economic, and cultural landscape of Australia.

Immigration Down Under: The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly

Immigration has been a hot topic in Australian politics and society for decades. While immigration has brought many benefits to the country, it has also been the subject of controversies and debates.

The Good

  • Immigration has contributed significantly to the cultural diversity of Australia.
  • Immigrants have played a crucial role in the growth of the Australian economy.
  • Immigration has helped Australia address its skilled labour shortage.

The Bad

  • Immigrants have faced discrimination and prejudice in Australia.
  • Immigration policies have been criticised for being too restrictive and exclusionary.
  • The integration of immigrants into Australian society has been challenging.

The Ugly

Recently, incidents of racist and xenophobic attacks on immigrants in Australia have been reported in the media. The rise of far-right groups in the country has further fuelled the sentiments of hatred and intolerance towards immigrants.

The Way Forward

Immigration will continue to be a critical issue for Australia in the coming years. Here are some practical tips for policymakers and individuals to make immigration work for the country:

  • Adopt an inclusive and non-discriminatory immigration policy that welcomes immigrants from diverse backgrounds.
  • Invest in programs and initiatives that promote the integration of immigrants into Australian society.
  • Encourage intercultural dialogue and interaction to foster social cohesion and harmony.
