The Richest Love: Submitting to the Arbitration of Time



It's easy to get caught up in the rush of emotions that come with falling in love. You feel like you're on top of the world, and nothing can bring you down. But as time passes, you may realize that not all love stories have a happy ending.

Author Lawrence Durrell once said, "The richest love is that which submits to the arbitration of time." What does this mean? It means that true love isn't just about the initial rush of excitement and passion, but it's about weathering the storms that come along the way.

Let me share with you a personal story. I fell deeply in love with my partner while we were both in college. We had so much in common and shared the same dreams and ambitions for our future. But after graduation, we started to realize that life wasn't as easy as we thought it would be. We struggled with our careers, our finances, and our communication with each other. We went through some tough times and even broke up a couple of times. But we always found our way back to each other. We learned to forgive, to compromise, and to grow together. Now, more than 10 years later, we're still together and stronger than ever.

This is what it means to submit to the arbitration of time. Love isn't just about the good times, but it's also about the difficult times that test your bond and your commitment to each other. Here are some practical tips on how to submit to the arbitration of time in your own relationships:

  • Communicate openly and honestly. Don't avoid difficult conversations just because they're uncomfortable. Be willing to listen to each other's perspectives and find common ground.
  • Practice forgiveness. No one is perfect, and we all make mistakes. It's important to learn to forgive each other and move forward.
  • Make time for each other. Life can get busy, but love requires effort and attention. Make sure to prioritize your relationship and carve out time to spend together.
  • Celebrate each other's successes. When one person succeeds, your relationship succeeds. Show your partner that you're proud of them and support their dreams.

As you navigate the ups and downs of your own relationships, remember that true love isn't just about the pleasures of the present moment. It's about submitting to the arbitration of time, building a foundation of trust and understanding, and weathering the storms that come along the way.
