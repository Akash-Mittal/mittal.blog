Discovering Inner Strength:



The Power of Putting Others First

Author: Hoda Kotb

It was a typical day at the NBC studio when a young girl with a big dream stepped into my life. Her name was Madison, and she was battling a rare form of cancer that had stolen her hair and her energy, but not her spirit.

I was in the midst of a busy week, with back-to-back interviews and endless to-do lists, but as soon as I met Madison, I knew my focus had to shift. Suddenly, my own problems and stresses seemed small in comparison to the mountain this brave girl was scaling every day.

"Sometimes you have to take the focus off of you and put it on someone else and it's funny what you can accomplish and how much strength you really have." - Hoda Kotb

Over the next few weeks, I watched as Madison fought to keep a smile on her face despite the pain and exhaustion, and I realized that her spirit was contagious. By putting her needs ahead of my own, I found that I had more energy, more patience, and more joy than I thought possible.

Madison may have been young, but she taught me a lesson that I have carried with me ever since: when you put others first, you discover an inner strength that you never knew existed.

Why Putting Others First is So Powerful

It's easy to get caught up in our own problems and goals, especially in a world that tells us to focus on self-improvement and personal success. But while there is nothing wrong with taking care of yourself, there is also immense power in putting others first.

  • It shifts your focus: When you are constantly thinking about your own needs and wants, it's easy to become myopic and lose perspective. By focusing on someone else's needs, you gain a broader view of the world and the problems and possibilities within it.
  • It builds connection and empathy: When you take the time to listen to someone else's story, to offer a helping hand, or to simply be present with them, you build a connection that transcends words. This connection can create empathy and understanding, which in turn can lead to greater compassion and kindness towards others.
  • It cultivates resilience and gratitude: When you put others first, you may find that you have more perseverance, courage, and gratitude than you thought possible. By seeing the struggles and triumphs of those around you, you gain perspective on your own challenges and blessings in life.

Examples of Putting Others First

Putting others first doesn't have to be a grand gesture or a lifelong mission. Often, it's small acts of kindness that can have the biggest impact. Here are some examples:

  • Listening without interrupting
  • Making time for someone who is struggling
  • Giving a compliment or expressing gratitude
  • Helping someone with a task or project
  • Donating to a worthwhile cause

These actions may seem small, but they can create ripples of positivity that spread far beyond what we can see. When you put others first in even the smallest ways, you can change someone's day, their week, or even their life.

The Power of Putting Others First: A Personal Anecdote

One of the most incredible experiences I have had as a journalist was meeting a man named Chris Norton. Chris was a football player who became paralyzed during a game, but he refused to let his injury define him. Instead, he set an ambitious goal for himself: to walk across the stage at his college graduation.

I had the honor of documenting Chris's journey towards this goal, and it was one of the most inspiring stories I have ever covered. But what struck me most about Chris was not his determination or his physical strength, but his giving spirit.

Though he had every reason to feel angry or bitter about his injury, Chris chose to focus on helping others. He started a foundation to provide support and resources to people with spinal cord injuries, and he became a motivational speaker who travels the world sharing his story of resilience and hope.

What I learned from Chris is that no matter what happens to us in life, we always have a choice about how we respond. By putting others first, by sharing our stories and our struggles, and by opening ourselves up to the world, we can discover an inner strength that transcends our circumstances.

Conclusion: Putting Others First Can Change Your Life

The power of putting others first is not just a feel-good platitude. It is a real, tangible force that can transform your life and the lives of those around you. By shifting your focus, building connection and empathy, and cultivating resilience and gratitude, you can tap into a wellspring of inner strength that you never knew existed.

So the next time you feel overwhelmed by your problems or stressed by your goals, try shifting your focus outward. Listen to someone else's story, lend a helping hand, or simply be present with someone who needs it. You may be surprised at how much strength and joy you discover along the way.
