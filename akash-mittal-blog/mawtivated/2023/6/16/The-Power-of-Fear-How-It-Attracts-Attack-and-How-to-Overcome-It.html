The Power of Fear: How It Attracts Attack and How to Overcome It



By Edwin Louis Cole

An Inspiring Story

There was once a woman who lived in constant fear of losing her job. She had been working at the same company for over a decade, but with recent changes in management, she felt like her job was always on the line. She started to worry about every little mistake she made and second-guessed every decision she took. She couldn't sleep and felt like her anxiety was eating away at her soul.

One day, her boss called her into his office. She braced herself for the worst, thinking she was about to be fired. But to her surprise, he praised her for her outstanding work and gave her a promotion. She realized then that her fear had held her back for so long and that it had attracted the negative energy she was trying to avoid.

"Fear attracts attack. It's like swimming with sharks while wearing a meat suit. You're just asking for trouble."

The Power of Fear

Fear is a natural human emotion. It's the body's way of protecting us from danger, whether real or imagined. But when fear becomes a constant presence in our lives, it can have detrimental effects on our mental and physical health.

  • Fear attracts attack. When we constantly focus on the things we're afraid of, we give them power and energy. This makes us more vulnerable to negative experiences and can attract the very thing we're trying to avoid.
  • Fear clouds our judgment. When we're consumed by fear, we can't think clearly or make rational decisions. We're more likely to act impulsively or make mistakes.
  • Fear limits our potential. When we're afraid of failure or rejection, we're less likely to take risks and pursue our goals. We miss out on opportunities for growth and fulfillment.

Overcoming Fear

If fear has been holding you back, don't despair. You can learn to overcome it and live a more fulfilling life.

  1. Identify your fears. What are you really afraid of? Write down your fears and explore them. Often, the act of putting them on paper and examining them closely can help to diffuse their power.
  2. Challenge your fears. Ask yourself: are these fears rational? What evidence do I have to support them? What evidence contradicts them? Often, we're afraid of things that are unlikely to happen or that we can't control. Challenging our fears with logic and reason can help to put things in perspective.
  3. Take action. The best way to overcome fear is to face it head-on. Start small by taking baby steps towards your goals. The more you do, the more confident you'll become.

Final Thoughts

If you let fear control your life, you'll never reach your full potential. It's important to acknowledge your fears and work towards overcoming them. Remember, fear attracts attack, but courage attracts success.

"Courage is not the absence of fear, but the triumph over it."

So be brave, take risks, and don't let fear hold you back.
