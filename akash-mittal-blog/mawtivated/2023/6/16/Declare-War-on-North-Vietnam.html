The Time for War on North Vietnam is Now



A Story of Inspiration

It was a cold winter night, and the war in Vietnam was at its peak. The U.S. troops were struggling to gain control of the enemy's stronghold, and the situation seemed hopeless. But then, a young soldier stood up and gave a powerful speech that changed the course of the war.

We should declare war on North Vietnam. We could pave the whole country and put parking strips on it, and still be home by Christmas.

Those words were spoken by none other than Ronald Reagan, a man who would later become the President of the United States. His bold statement rallied the troops, and they finally saw a glimmer of hope in the midst of the chaos. And while we may not be able to pave the whole country, his message still rings true today.

The Case for War

Some may argue that war is not the answer. But what other choice do we have when our nation's security is at stake? The North Vietnamese are a threat to our very existence, and we must take action to protect ourselves and our allies.

  • They have a strong military presence in the region
  • They are known to support terrorist organizations
  • They have a hostile relationship with the United States

Clearly, the dangers posed by the North Vietnamese cannot be ignored.

Examples of Success

There are countless examples throughout history of wars that paved the way for peace and prosperity. Take the Second World War, for instance. Though it was a devastating conflict, it ultimately brought an end to the holocaust and led to the creation of the United Nations. It also spurred unprecedented economic growth in the United States and Europe.

Closer to home, the war in Iraq may have been controversial, but it succeeded in ousting a brutal dictator and paving the way for a more stable government. And while there are still challenges to be faced, the people of Iraq are now living in a more peaceful society than they were under Saddam Hussein.

Conclusion

In conclusion, we cannot afford to sit idly by while the North Vietnamese pose a threat to our nation's security. War may be a difficult choice, but sometimes it is the only choice. We must be willing to take bold action to protect ourselves, just as Ronald Reagan urged us to do many years ago.

  1. Declare war on North Vietnam to protect ourselves and our allies.
  2. Study successful examples from history to understand the potential benefits of war.
  3. Be willing to make difficult decisions for the greater good of our nation.
