Becoming God: An Inspiring Journey



Have you ever thought about what it means to become God? It sounds impossible, doesn't it? But it's not. In fact, it's the reason why the Son of God became man. And let me tell you a story that inspired me to write this article.

"I was an atheist, until I met a man who lived his life as if he was God. Not in the sense that he was bossy or controlling, but in the sense that he showed love, compassion, and forgiveness to everyone he met. He was a true embodiment of what it means to become God. And I realized that's what I want for myself."

This story made me think about my own life and what it means for me to become God. And I realized that it's not just about being religious or attending church. It's about living a life of love and compassion, just like that man did.

Author - Athanasius Story or Background

Athanasius was an early Christian theologian and bishop who lived in the 4th century. He played an important role in defining the Christian doctrine of the Trinity and the person of Christ. But perhaps his most famous quote is the one that inspired this article:

"The Son of God became man so that we might become God."

What Athanasius meant by this is that Jesus Christ, as the Son of God, became human in order to show us the way to becoming like God. It's not that we will become God in the same sense as Jesus is God, but rather that we will become like God in our character and our actions.

What Does it Mean to Become God?

So, what does it mean to become God? Let's break it down into a few key points:

  • Love: God is love, and to become like God means to love as God loves.
  • Compassion: God is compassionate, and to become like God means to show compassion to those who are hurting or in need.
  • Forgiveness: God is forgiving, and to become like God means to forgive those who have wronged us.
  • Holiness: God is holy, and to become like God means to live a life of purity and righteousness.

It's important to remember that becoming God is not a one-time event, but rather a lifelong journey. It's something that we work towards every day, through prayer, meditation, and living out our faith.

Examples of Becoming God

Let's look at a few examples of people who have embodied the characteristics of becoming God:

  • Mother Teresa: Mother Teresa is perhaps the most famous example of someone who lived a life of love, compassion, and forgiveness. Her work with the poor and sick in Calcutta has inspired millions around the world.
  • Nelson Mandela: Nelson Mandela's fight against apartheid in South Africa was rooted in his belief in equality and justice, which are key aspects of becoming God.
  • Pope Francis: Pope Francis has been a vocal advocate for the poor and marginalized, and his message of love and compassion has resonated with people of all faiths around the world.

These individuals serve as examples of what it means to become God, and remind us that it is a journey worth striving towards.

Conclusion

In conclusion, becoming God is not about being perfect, but rather about striving towards a life of love, compassion, and forgiveness. It's a lifelong journey that requires patience, commitment, and a willingness to learn and grow. Here are three key takeaways from this article:

  1. Love: To become God, we must love as God loves.
  2. Compassion: To become God, we must show compassion to those who are hurting or in need.
  3. Forgiveness: To become God, we must forgive those who have wronged us.

Remember, becoming God is not a destination, but a journey. It's about living a life of purpose and meaning, and making a positive impact on the world around us.
