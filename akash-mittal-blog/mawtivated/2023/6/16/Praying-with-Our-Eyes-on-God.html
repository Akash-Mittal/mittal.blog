The Power of Prayer: Keeping Our Eyes on God Despite the Challenges



There was once an impoverished farmer who visited a wise man in his village. The farmer told the wise man about his troubles, how his crops were barely growing, and how he was struggling to make ends meet for his family.

The wise man listened attentively and then said, "I know why your crops are not growing. You are focusing too much on the difficulties and not enough on God's blessings." The farmer was puzzled and asked the wise man to explain. The wise man replied, "You are so preoccupied with the problems that weigh you down that you are forgetting to look up and see how God is working in your life. You need to pray with your eyes on God, not on the difficulties."

"We have to pray with our eyes on God, not on the difficulties." - Oswald Chambers

The farmer took these words to heart and began to pray with a renewed fervor, focusing solely on God's presence and power. To his amazement, his crops began to flourish and his life was transformed.

The Importance of Praying with Our Eyes on God

As Oswald Chambers once said, "We have to pray with our eyes on God, not on the difficulties." But why is this so important? When we fix our eyes on our problems, we become overwhelmed and discouraged. We start to doubt that God is with us and that He cares about our struggles. Prayer then becomes a chore or an obligation.

However, when we shift our focus to God, we are reminded of His presence and power. We become filled with faith and hope, trusting that God will provide for our needs according to His will. We are also able to see how He is working in our lives, even in the midst of our difficulties.

Practical Tips for Praying with Our Eyes on God

  • Start with thanksgiving: Begin by thanking God for the blessings in your life, and for His faithfulness and love.
  • Open your heart to God: Be honest with God about your struggles, fears and worries. Pour out your heart to Him and tell Him how you feel.
  • Focus on God's character: Remember who God is, and His attributes of love, mercy, and grace. Focus on these attributes instead of your difficulties.
  • Pray for God's will: Seek God's will in your life, and pray that He will guide your steps according to His purposes and plans.
  • Spend time in silence: After praying, spend a few moments in silence, listening to God's voice and allowing Him to speak to you.

Conclusion: Praying with Faith and Hope

In conclusion, praying with our eyes on God, not on the difficulties, is critical to developing a meaningful and fulfilling prayer life. By focusing on God's presence and power, we are filled with faith and hope, and are able to persevere through the trials and challenges we face. Remember to start with thanksgiving, open your heart to God, focus on God's character, pray for His will, and spend time in silence. Through these practices, we can learn to trust in God's goodness and His plans for our lives.

Author: Oswald Chambers

Oswald Chambers was a British theologian and minister, best known for his devotional writings, including "My Utmost for His Highest." Chambers believed that a relationship with God was vital for daily living, and that prayer was key to deepening that relationship. His teachings and writings continue to inspire millions around the world today.
