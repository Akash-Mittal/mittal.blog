My Experience Hosting 'The Tonight Show'



As a young man growing up in Massachusetts, I never would have imagined that one day I would have the opportunity to host one of the most iconic television shows of all time. But that's exactly what happened to me in 2009, when I was chosen to take over as host of 'The Tonight Show' from Jay Leno.

At the time, it was a dream come true for me. I had spent many years working as a writer and performer on various comedy shows, and hosting 'The Tonight Show' was the ultimate goal. I was thrilled to have the chance to follow in the footsteps of legends like Johnny Carson and Jay Leno, and I couldn't wait to make my mark on the show.

Of course, as with any big opportunity, there were also some nerves and doubts. I knew that taking over a show as beloved and iconic as 'The Tonight Show' was a huge responsibility, and I didn't want to let anyone down. But I also knew that I had worked hard to get to that point, and that I had a great team of writers, producers, and other talented people behind me.

So I took a deep breath, put on my best suit, and stepped out onto that iconic stage for the first time. And for the next seven months, I got to live out my dream in front of millions of viewers every night.

  • There were some amazing moments, like when I interviewed my favorite band, Radiohead, and they played an incredible version of their song "Fake Plastic Trees".
  • There were some hilarious moments, like when I dressed up as a giant taco for a bit about Cinco de Mayo.
  • And there were some truly memorable moments, like when I said goodbye to the show after NBC decided to give it back to Jay Leno.
"Every comedian dreams of hosting 'The Tonight Show' and, for seven months, I got to. I did it my way, with people I love, and I do not regret a second." - Conan O'Brien

Looking back on my time as host of 'The Tonight Show', I have no regrets. I gave it my all, and I had a lot of fun along the way. Of course, things didn't turn out exactly as I had hoped or planned - after just seven months, I was forced to leave the show and start over elsewhere. But even that experience taught me some valuable lessons about resilience, perseverance, and staying true to yourself.

I think that's the key to success in any creative field - whether it's television, comedy, or something else entirely. You have to trust your own instincts, surround yourself with good people, and never give up, even when things get tough. It's not always easy, but it's worth it in the end.

So if you're a young comedian or writer out there, dreaming of one day hosting your own show or making it big in Hollywood, I say go for it. Take your shot, chase your dreams, and don't let anyone tell you that you can't do it. You never know what might happen if you put yourself out there and give it your all.

- Conan O'Brien

My Three Takeaways from Hosting 'The Tonight Show'

  • Follow your dreams, even if they seem impossible or unlikely.
  • Surround yourself with good people who believe in you and your vision.
  • Stay true to yourself and your unique voice, even when faced with challenges or setbacks.
