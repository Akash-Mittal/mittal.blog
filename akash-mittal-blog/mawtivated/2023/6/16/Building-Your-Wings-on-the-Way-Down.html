Building Your Wings on the Way Down



At the age of 27, Amanda was working as a restaurant manager, following a path she thought was certain and stable. However, she was always restless and seeking something more fulfilling. One day, she quit her job and started a bakery from her little kitchen in her apartment. Without any prior experience in the industry and with limited funds, she faced many challenges. But within two years, not only had her bakery grown into a successful business, but she had found new friends, a passion for baking, and a sense of independence she had not known before.

This is a story of someone who had the courage to take a leap of faith, to jump off a cliff without knowing what was at the bottom, and to build her wings on the way down. This is a story that resonates with many of us who have hesitated to pursue our dreams, who have struggled with doubts and fears, who have been told to stay within our comfort zones. But why should we listen to our intellect and be cynical? Why not take that leap and see where it takes us?

The Wisdom of Ray Bradbury

If we listened to our intellect, we'd never have a love affair. We'd never have a friendship. We'd never go into business, because we'd be cynical. Well, that's nonsense. You've got to jump off cliffs all the time and build your wings on the way down.

Ray Bradbury, the acclaimed science fiction author, knew the value of taking risks and embracing uncertainty. Born in Illinois in 1920, he began writing at an early age and contributed stories to his school newspaper. Despite dropping out of college, he pursued his passion for storytelling and eventually became one of the most prolific and influential writers of the 20th century. He wrote classics such as "Fahrenheit 451," "The Martian Chronicles," and "Something Wicked This Way Comes," among others.

Bradbury believed that imagination and creativity were the keys to a fulfilling life, and that taking risks was part of the process. He once said, "I have never listened to anyone who criticized my taste in space travel, sideshows or gorillas. When this occurs, I pack up my dinosaurs and leave the room." In other words, he refused to let others' opinions limit his vision or his actions. He understood that building one's wings on the way down required courage, curiosity, and a willingness to learn from mistakes.

Examples of Building Your Wings on the Way Down

Building your wings on the way down can take different forms, depending on your interests, talents, and circumstances. Here are some examples:

  • Starting your own business, even if you have no prior experience or capital
  • Traveling to a new country and immersing yourself in a different culture
  • Learning a new skill or hobby that challenges you and expands your abilities
  • Volunteering for a cause that resonates with you and makes a positive impact on others

These are just a few examples, but they share a common thread: they require you to step outside your comfort zone, to take a chance on yourself, and to trust that you can figure things out as you go along.

Tips for Building Your Wings on the Way Down

While building your wings on the way down is not always easy or predictable, there are some tips that can help you navigate the process:

  1. Identify your passions and values. Before you can take a leap of faith, you need to know what you care about and what motivates you. Ask yourself what interests you, what causes you to feel alive, and what you want your legacy to be.
  2. Cultivate a growth mindset. Instead of viewing challenges or obstacles as setbacks, see them as opportunities to learn and grow. Embrace failure as part of the process and be open to feedback and constructive criticism.
  3. Network and seek support. Building your wings on the way down can be a lonely road at times. Surround yourself with people who inspire you, challenge you, and support you. Join communities, attend events, and collaborate with others who share your vision.
