The Two Vietnams – A Story of Hope and Peace



Sheila Jackson Lee, a renowned politician and civil rights activist, once said, "Today, we have two Vietnams, side by side, North and South, exchanging and working. We may not agree with all that North Vietnam is doing, but they are living in peace. I would look for a better human rights record for North Vietnam, but they are living side by side."

This statement from a distinguished figure in the United States highlights the progress that has been made in Vietnam. Not too long ago, Vietnam was one of the most war-torn and divided nations in the world. The Vietnam War claimed the lives of millions and left the country shattered and torn. However, today, Vietnam is a country that is emerging as an economic and cultural powerhouse in Southeast Asia. This story of hope and peace is worth telling, and we will discuss it in detail in this article.

Sheila Jackson Lee – A Brief Background

Sheila Jackson Lee is a Democratic member of the United States House of Representatives, representing the 18th congressional district of Texas. She has been a member of Congress since 1995 and is currently serving her thirteenth term. Lee is known for her outspokenness and advocacy on issues related to civil rights, criminal justice, and national security.

The Two Vietnams – A Story of Emerging Peace

As Sheila Jackson Lee pointed out, Vietnam is a country that is divided into two regions – North Vietnam and South Vietnam. The division between the two regions was a result of the Vietnam War that raged on between 1955 and 1975. The war ended with the fall of Saigon, the capital of South Vietnam, and the subsequent unification of the country under communist rule. Since then, the country has made significant strides in rebuilding and healing the wounds caused by the decades of war.

Today, Vietnam is a leading exporter of rice, coffee, and seafood. The country has attracted significant foreign investment, particularly from Asian countries such as Japan, Korea, and Taiwan. This investment has spurred growth in various sectors, including manufacturing, tourism, and services. Consequently, the country's economy has remained resilient despite the global economic slowdown induced by the COVID-19 pandemic.

The Vietnamese people are resourceful and hardworking. Despite the challenges that they have faced over the years, they have remained steadfast in their resolve to build a better tomorrow. The country's political system may not be perfect, and human rights abuses have been reported. However, the Vietnamese government has taken steps to address these issues, and progress has been made towards improving the human rights record.

There are many inspiring stories from Vietnam that illustrate the resilience and determination of the people. For instance, Truong Thi Ngoc Anh, a 27-year-old woman from Quang Ninh province, started her business of making dried jackfruit chips with an initial investment of only $35. She now employs over 20 women from her village and has expanded her product line to include other fruits such as pineapple and banana. Her business has become a source of economic empowerment for her and her employees, and she hopes to continue growing her business.

Another example is the story of Le Van Tri, a farmer from Ninh Thuan province. Tri started farming grapes in 1998 after returning from the war. He faced many challenges along the way, including drought, pests, and lack of access to markets. However, he persisted, and his hard work paid off. Today, Tri is one of the most successful grape farmers in the region, with an annual income of over $50,000. He attributes his success to his perseverance and determination to succeed.

The Importance of Peace and Reconciliation

Vietnam's story of emerging peace is proof that reconciliation and forgiveness are possible even in the most challenging of circumstances. The Vietnamese people have shown that it is possible to move beyond the scars of war and embrace a brighter future. Today, Vietnam is a country that is on the path to becoming a developed nation, and this progress would not have been possible without peace.

Peace and reconciliation do not only benefit nations; they are essential for individuals as well. Forgiving those who have wronged us and reconciling with them is a necessary step towards healing and moving forward. Holding on to bitterness and resentment only fuels negativity and prevents personal growth and progress.

Conclusion – Three Key Takeaways

  1. Vietnam's story of hope and peace is inspiring and worth telling.
  2. The Vietnamese people's resilience and determination are a testament to the human spirit.
  3. Forgiveness and reconciliation are essential for personal growth and progress.

The story of Vietnam is a testimony to the power of resilience, reconciliation, and hope. As individuals, we can learn many lessons from their story and apply them in our lives. Whether it is forgiving someone who wronged us or persisting in the face of adversity, we can make a difference in our lives and the lives of others around us if we choose to take the path of peace and hope.
