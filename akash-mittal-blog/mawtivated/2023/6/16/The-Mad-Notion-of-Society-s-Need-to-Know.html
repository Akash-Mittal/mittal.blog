The Mad Notion of Society's Need to Know



He was just an ordinary man, living an ordinary life. He had a job he didn't particularly like, a small group of friends, and a family he loved. Day in and day out, he followed the same routine, never thinking that there was anything remarkable about him.

Until one day, when he read a book that changed everything.

It was a book that spoke to him on a deep level, that made him see himself and the world around him in a different light. As he read it, he felt a spark ignite within him, a desire to share what he had learned with others.

It wasn't an easy journey, of course. He had doubts and fears, moments when he wondered if he was just being foolish. But he couldn't shake the feeling that he had something important to say. So, he started writing, pouring his heart and soul onto the page.

Eventually, he had a manuscript, a book of his own that he knew, in his heart, would make a difference in the world.

The Mad Notion

John Updike once said, "The essential support and encouragement comes from within, arising out of the mad notion that your society needs to know what only you can tell it." That's the mad notion that drove our ordinary man to write his book. It's the mad notion that drives anyone to share their unique perspective, to create something no one else could.

But that notion can be hard to hold onto. We live in a world that tells us we need to conform, that our ideas aren't valuable unless they fit neatly into the status quo.

But the truth is, it's our idiosyncrasies that make us valuable. It's the things that only we can see or say that make us worth listening to. So, how do we stay true to that mad notion?

Staying True to Your Voice

  • Believe in yourself - this is the most important step. No one else can believe in you until you believe in yourself. Know that your perspective is valuable simply because it's yours.
  • Surround yourself with encouragement - seek out those who support your mad notion. Join a writing group or find a mentor who can help you stay focused and motivated.
  • Share your process - your journey is just as important as the finished product. Don't be afraid to share your struggles and triumphs with others. You never know who might be inspired to pursue their own mad notion.

Examples

There are countless examples of mad notions that have changed the world - from artists like Vincent Van Gogh, who never achieved recognition in his lifetime, to entrepreneurs like Steve Jobs, who were driven by a vision no one else could see.

But you don't have to be famous to make a difference. There are countless individuals who have pursued their mad notions, who have started nonprofits or written books or simply shared their stories with those around them.

Conclusion

  • Believe in yourself - your perspective matters.
  • Surround yourself with encouragement - seek out those who support your mad notion.
  • Share your process - your journey is just as important as the finished product.

Practical Tips:

If you're struggling to stay true to your voice, here are some practical tips:

  • Set aside time every day to write or create.
  • Read or consume media that inspires you.
  • Join a writing group or find a mentor.
