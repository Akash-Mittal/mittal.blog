Poets as Soldiers: The Power of Words



Words are powerful. They have the ability to evoke emotions, inspire action, and change lives. And when it comes to poets, they are soldiers that liberate words from the steadfast possession of definition.

An Inspiring Story

Let me share with you a story that will undoubtedly inspire you. William Ernest Henley, an English poet, wrote a poem titled "Invictus" while he was recovering from tuberculosis. The poem, which means "unconquered" in Latin, became a source of inspiration for many people.

"Out of the night that covers me,
Black as the Pit from pole to pole,
I thank whatever gods may be
For my unconquerable soul."

These opening lines of the poem give you a glimpse of Henley's strength of character. Despite his physical ailment, he remained steadfast in his belief that he was unconquerable. The poem goes on to describe his determination to overcome all the challenges that life throws at him. Truly inspiring!

Case Study #1

A study conducted by the University of Liverpool found that reading poetry can lower stress levels and improve emotional well-being. The study involved a group of people who were asked to read a selection of poems every day for four weeks. At the end of four weeks, the participants reported feeling more calm and relaxed.
