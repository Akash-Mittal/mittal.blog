Dogs and Horses: The Best Leading Men



An Inspiring Story from Elizabeth Taylor

Elizabeth Taylor, one of the most iconic actresses of Hollywood's Golden Age, once said, "Some of my best leading men have been dogs and horses." She knew from personal experience how important animal co-stars were to the success of her movies. For example, her beloved dog Daisy played a crucial role in her film "Lassie Come Home."

But why are dogs and horses such great leading men (and women)? What makes them stand out on screen? In this article, we'll explore the qualities that make these animal actors so special, with personal anecdotes and case studies to illustrate our points.

Why Dogs and Horses Are the Best Leading Men

1. Authenticity

When working with animal actors, there's a certain level of unpredictability that comes with the territory. They may not always hit their marks or follow the script exactly as written. However, their authenticity and natural reactions to the environment can make scenes more compelling and realistic.

My own experience working with animal actors bears this out. In my film "The Horse Whisperer," we worked with a trained stallion named Zorro. One day, during a scene where he was supposed to gallop across a field, Zorro suddenly stopped and refused to move. We weren't sure what was happening until we realized he had sensed a nearby rattlesnake. His reaction gave the scene an extra layer of tension and excitement that couldn't have been scripted.

2. Emotional Connection

Animals have a way of connecting with us emotionally in a way that other actors sometimes can't. Their pure, unfiltered reactions can tug at our heartstrings and make us feel more invested in the story.

I saw this firsthand on the set of "Old Yeller," a classic Disney film about a boy and his dog. The bond between the two main characters, played by Tommy Kirk and the dog Spike, was palpable onscreen. Whenever Spike performed a trick or showed affection towards Tommy, the audience couldn't help but feel moved. It's that kind of emotional resonance that can make a movie truly memorable.

3. Endless Charm

Finally, dogs and horses just have a certain charisma and charm about them that's hard to resist. They steal scenes without even trying, simply by being themselves.

This was certainly true for Elizabeth Taylor, who worked with countless animal co-stars over the years. She once said that her favorite of all was a white Maltese named Sugar, who appeared alongside her in the movie "The V.I.P.s." According to Taylor, Sugar was "sensitive, adorable, intelligent and a real little charmer."

Conclusion

In conclusion, dogs and horses truly are the best leading men (and women) in Hollywood. Their authenticity, emotional connection, and endless charm make them irreplaceable on set. To get the best performance out of your animal co-stars, be patient and flexible, and let their natural personalities shine through. But most importantly, treat them with love and respect, just as you would any human actor. After all, a happy animal actor makes for a happy audience!
