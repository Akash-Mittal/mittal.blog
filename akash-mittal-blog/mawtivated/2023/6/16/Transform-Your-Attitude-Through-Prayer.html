Transform Your Attitude Through Prayer



Have you ever found yourself feeling bitter towards someone? Maybe it's a coworker who always seems to get on your nerves or a family member who you can never see eye-to-eye with. It's easy to let negative feelings fester and grow, but did you know that by praying for that person, you can actually change your own attitude towards them?

An Inspiring Story

A few years ago, I was struggling with a colleague at work who I felt was constantly undermining me and belittling my work. I found myself growing increasingly bitter towards her and could feel myself becoming more and more negative in my interactions with her. One day, a friend suggested that I try praying for her.

At first, I was hesitant. Why should I pray for someone who was treating me badly? But as I continued to ruminate on the idea, I realized that maybe it was worth a shot. So I started praying for my colleague, not necessarily to change her behavior, but to change my own heart towards her. I asked that I would be able to see her as a fellow human being, flawed like all of us, and that I would be able to extend grace and kindness towards her.

I won't say that my colleague's behavior magically improved overnight. But what did happen was a remarkable shift in my own attitude. As I continued to pray for her, I found myself feeling more empathy towards her and less bitterness. I was able to let go of my resentment and start treating her with kindness, even when she didn't necessarily deserve it.

The Power of Prayer

So what is it about prayer that has the power to transform us in this way? It all comes down to perspective. When you pray for someone, you are forced to see them in a different light. Instead of just focusing on their negative behavior, you are acknowledging their humanity and recognizing that they are worthy of love and kindness, just like you. This shift in perspective can be incredibly powerful and can help you to let go of negative emotions that are holding you back.

But prayer isn't just about changing your perspective on others. It can also help you to change your perspective on yourself. When you are in a negative headspace, it can be hard to see any positive qualities in yourself. But by praying and asking for guidance, you can start to see yourself as worthy and deserving of love and respect. This can help you to develop a more positive self-image and empower you to make positive changes in your life.

A Personal Anecdote

I'm reminded of a story from author and motivational speaker Norman Vincent Peale. Peale was once approached by a man who was struggling to forgive his ex-wife for leaving him. Peale advised him to start praying for her every day, asking that she would be blessed and finding happiness. The man was skeptical, but decided to give it a try.

After a few weeks of praying, the man found that he was starting to let go of his anger and bitterness towards his ex-wife. He was able to see her as a person with her own struggles and flaws, rather than just focusing on how she had hurt him. And over time, he found that he was able to forgive her and move on with his life.

Conclusion

If you're struggling with negative feelings towards someone, whether it's a coworker, a family member, or even just a stranger on the street, prayer can be a powerful tool for transformation. By praying for the person, you can shift your perspective and develop a more positive attitude towards them. And in the process, you may find that you are also transforming your own heart and mind.

To summarize, here are three key takeaways:

  1. Prayer can help you to see others in a different light, recognizing their flaws and humanity.
  2. By praying for someone, you can develop empathy and let go of negative emotions that are holding you back.
  3. Prayer can also help you to develop a more positive self-image, empowering you to make positive changes in your life.

Practical Tips

  • Start small - you don't have to pray for hours at a time. Just a few minutes of intentional, focused prayer can make a big difference.
  • Be specific - when you pray for someone, be specific about what you're asking for. Ask for empathy, grace, and kindness towards the person.
  • Be consistent - make prayer a regular habit, even if it's just a few minutes a day. Over time, you'll start to see the impact it can have.
