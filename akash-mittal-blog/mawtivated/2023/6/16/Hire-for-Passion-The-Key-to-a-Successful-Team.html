Hire for Passion: The Key to a Successful Team



Let me start this article with a story that inspired me to write about the importance of passion in hiring:

When I was starting my first business, I hired a man for a junior position in my team. He had a great skill set and impressive experience on his resume. However, after a few weeks, I noticed that he lacked the drive and passion that my other team members had. He did his work just for the salary and didn't show any initiative or eagerness to learn.

After some time, I realized that his lack of passion was affecting the rest of the team's morale. They were all passionate about the work they were doing and expected the same level of enthusiasm from their colleagues. I had to let him go and learned a valuable lesson: It's not about finding the most qualified candidate, but the one who has passion for the work.

The wisdom of Henry David Thoreau

The above story is a testament to the quotation by Henry David Thoreau:

"Do not hire a man who does your work for money, but him who does it for love of it."

Thoreau, an American philosopher, and writer, emphasized the importance of passion and purpose in one's work. He believed that people who work for the love of it are more likely to succeed than those who are just in it for the money.

Thoreau's words still hold true today. In fact, in some industries, passion has become a critical factor in hiring. For example, in the creative industries such as design, advertising, and media, companies look for candidates with a strong passion for their craft.

Why passion matters in hiring

Passionate employees are more engaged, productive, innovative, and committed to their work. They are more likely to go the extra mile, take on extra responsibilities, and contribute to the company's bottom line.

On the other hand, employees who lack passion can affect the team's morale and productivity. They can also hinder the company's growth by being resistant to change or taking a complacent attitude towards their work.

Examples of successful companies that hire for passion

Some of the most successful companies in the world prioritize passion in their hiring process. Here are some examples:

  • Apple: The tech giant looks for people who are passionate about their products and services. In fact, Apple's CEO, Tim Cook, once said, "We want people that are passionate about what they do. We believe that if you're passionate about something, you bring an incredible amount of energy to it."
  • Nike: The sports apparel company values passion in their employees. In job postings, Nike often emphasizes the need for candidates who are passionate about sports and fitness.
  • Google: The tech giant is known for its unique company culture, which values passion and creativity. Google's hiring process focuses on finding candidates who are passionate about technology and have a track record of innovation.

These companies understand that passion is a key factor in creating a successful team. They know that employees who love what they do are more likely to create innovative products, engage customers, and drive revenue.

How to hire for passion

If you're looking to hire passionate employees for your team, here are some practical tips:

  • Assess your company culture: Before you start hiring, evaluate your company culture and values. What kind of work environment do you have, and what kind of employees thrive in that environment? Look for candidates whose values align with your company's mission and culture.
  • Ask the right questions: During job interviews, ask questions that will help you gauge the candidate's level of passion. Some examples include: What made you interested in this field? What motivates you to do your best work? What do you do outside of work that demonstrates your passion for this field?
  • Look for relevant experience: Passionate employees often have experience outside of their day job that demonstrates their commitment to their field. Look for candidates who have participated in industry-related organizations, attended conferences or workshops, or worked on personal projects that showcase their skills and passion.

Conclusion

In conclusion, hiring for passion is a crucial factor in building a successful team. Passionate employees are more engaged, productive, and committed to their work. They also bring energy and innovation to their teams, which can drive revenue and growth for the company. When hiring, assess your company's culture and values, ask the right questions, and look for relevant experience. By doing so, you'll be on your way to building a passionate team that can take your company to the next level.
