When Beauty is Sought for Pleasure



Once upon a time in a small village, there was a young girl named Maria. She was known for her stunning beauty that could turn heads in any direction. However, Maria never took pride in her beauty or flaunted it for pleasure. She respected the gift she possessed and used it to spread kindness and love among people.

As she grew up, she came across people who didn't understand the real purpose of beauty. They saw it as a tool to manipulate or lure someone into doing something. These people were driven by their own pleasure and didn't care about the consequences it brought upon others.

This story is a perfect example of how beauty can be misused when it is sought not from religion and love but for pleasure. The quote by Annie Dillard, "As soon as beauty is sought not from religion and love, but for pleasure, it degrades the seeker," highlights the true essence of beauty and how it should be appreciated.

What is beauty?

Beauty is not just skin deep; it goes beyond that. It is a characteristic that is appreciated for its unique and admirable qualities. It is a quality that is appreciated by all because it is a universal language that transcends through cultures, time and space. Beauty is in everything, from nature to people to art to music.

Why should we respect beauty?

  1. It's a precious gift: Beauty is a gift that not everyone has the privilege to possess. It is unique, and everyone should respect it.
  2. It's a source of joy: Beauty can bring joy to people's lives and can be a source of inspiration.
  3. It evokes emotions: Beauty has the power to evoke emotions in people, whether it is through a stunning sunset or a breathtaking piece of art.

What happens when beauty is sought for pleasure?

When beauty is sought for pleasure, it can lead to the following consequences:

  • Objectification: People who seek beauty for pleasure tend to objectify people. They see them as just a means to an end, rather than a person with their own desires, thoughts and emotions.
  • Pressure to conform: Society has set a standard for beauty, and people who seek it for pleasure tend to conform to these standards. This can lead to low self-esteem and body image issues.
  • Unhealthy competition: People who seek beauty for pleasure tend to compete with each other, leading to an unhealthy and toxic environment. They often use extreme measures such as cosmetic surgery or unhealthy diets to achieve this goal.

How can we appreciate and respect beauty?

There are ways in which we can appreciate and respect beauty, such as:

  • Gratitude: We should be grateful for the beauty around us and appreciate it.
  • Embrace imperfections: We should embrace imperfections and appreciate the uniqueness of everything.
  • Use beauty for good: We should use beauty to spread kindness, love and positivity among people.

Conclusion

In conclusion, beauty is a gift that should be respected and appreciated. When it is sought for pleasure, it can lead to negative consequences such as objectification, pressure to conform and unhealthy competition. However, when beauty is appreciated for its unique and admirable qualities, it can bring joy, inspiration and evoke emotions. Therefore, we should use beauty for good and to spread kindness, love and positivity.
