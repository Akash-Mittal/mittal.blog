The Role of Luck in Success: Guy Kawasaki's Perspective



Success is a combination of great ideas, hard work, and luck. While some may argue that luck does not play any role in achieving success, it is essential to acknowledge the role of luck in success. Often, luck is the differentiator between successful people and those who fail. Guy Kawasaki, a renowned entrepreneur, author, and speaker, shares his perspective on the role of luck in success.

A Story of Luck in Success

Back in 1983, Guy Kawasaki was working for Apple Inc. as the chief evangelist for the Macintosh computer. As part of his role, he was tasked with shipping the Macintosh to retailers with the aim of establishing it as the primary computer in the market. This was a critical time for Apple as the company was struggling to keep up with IBM, which was dominating the personal computer market.

As Kawasaki was working to market the product, he stumbled upon a stroke of luck. The president of a retailer with whom they had been working called him and informed him that they could not accept the shipment of Macintoshes as their store had burned down. While this was bad news for the retailer, it was great news for Apple. The disaster meant that the store had to reorder the entire shipment of Macintoshes, which gave Apple more time to improve the product and build its brand.

Apple took advantage of this lucky break and improved the product they were shipping. They added a better manual, which made it easier for users to understand how to operate the computer, and a general manual fix was done within the machine. When the retailer delivered the new shipment of Macintoshes, they sold out within a week. This luck played a role in establishing Apple as a leader in the personal computer market.

The Role of Hard Work and Implementation in Success

While luck played a role in the success of Apple, it was also essential that the company worked hard to establish its brand and improve the product. As Kawasaki notes, good ideas are just ten percent of what it takes to succeed. The other ninety percent involves implementation and hard work.

When Kawasaki was working for Apple, he and his team had great ideas for the Macintosh product, but it was their hard work and implementation that made the product successful. They worked tirelessly on improving the product's design, marketing strategy, and packaging to ensure that it met the needs of the market. This hard work paid off, and soon the Macintosh became the primary computer in the market, eclipsing IBM's dominance.

Luck Is Essential in Overcoming Challenges

Despite the need for hard work and implementation in achieving success, luck still plays a significant role. As Kawasaki notes, "you can do everything right and still not succeed." Sometimes, even the best-laid plans can fail due to unforeseen circumstances. That is where luck comes into play.

A stroke of luck can help a business overcome unforeseen challenges. For example, a client who was previously disinterested in a company's product may suddenly express interest, providing the break the business needs to succeed. These types of lucky breaks are not planned, but they can significantly impact a business's success.

Conclusion

As highlighted by Guy Kawasaki's story, a combination of good ideas, hard work, and a stroke of luck is essential for success. While a good idea is crucial, it is the implementation and hard work that makes it successful. However, luck can be the differentiator between success and failure.

So always be prepared to take advantage of any opportunities that come your way, and be open to the possibility of luck playing a role in your success story. In conclusion, the three key points to bear in mind are:

  1. Good ideas are just ten percent of what it takes to succeed. The other ninety percent involves implementation and hard work.
  2. You can do everything right and still not succeed, making luck an essential ingredient of success.
  3. Be prepared to take advantage of any opportunities that come your way and be open to the possibility of luck playing a role in your success story.
