Kill It Before It Grows: A Lesson from Bob Marley



Have you ever faced an obstacle in your life that seemed insurmountable? Maybe it was a difficult project at work, a strained relationship, or a personal struggle. Whatever it was, you probably felt overwhelmed and wondered how you could possibly overcome it.

That's where Bob Marley's quote comes in: "Every time I plant a seed, He say kill it before it grow, he say kill it before they grow." At first glance, these words might seem discouraging or defeatist. But if you look a little closer, there's a valuable lesson to be learned.

The Story of the Orchid

Let me tell you a story. A few years ago, I received an orchid as a gift. I was thrilled; it was beautiful and exotic, and I'd always wanted one. I followed the care instructions carefully, giving it just the right amount of sunlight and water. And for a few weeks, it seemed to thrive.

But then, something strange happened. The leaves started turning yellow and falling off. The flowers wilted and died. I tried everything I could think of to save it, but nothing worked. I felt like a failure. I had wanted this orchid so badly, and now it was dead.

But then I remembered Bob Marley's words. "Kill it before it grows." Maybe, just maybe, I had been trying too hard to keep this orchid alive. Maybe I had been neglecting other things in my life in order to focus on it. Maybe it wasn't meant to thrive in my care.

So I took a deep breath, gathered up the dead plant, and threw it away. It was difficult, but it was also liberating. I wasn't a failure, after all. I had just been trying to grow something that wasn't meant to grow in my environment.

What Bob Marley's Quote Really Means

Now, let's take a closer look at Bob Marley's quote. "Every time I plant a seed, He say kill it before it grow, he say kill it before they grow." At first glance, this might seem like a defeatist attitude. If you're constantly killing your own ideas and dreams, how will you ever achieve anything?

But what Marley is really saying is this: Sometimes we need to let go of things that aren't meant to thrive in our environment. Sometimes we need to be ruthless with our own ideas and dreams. Sometimes, we need to kill them before they grow, so that we can make room for things that will truly thrive and flourish.

Think about it this way: if you plant a seed for a palm tree in the middle of a desert, no matter how much you water it or care for it, it will never grow. But if you plant a cactus in that same environment, it will thrive. So it's not about giving up on our dreams altogether; it's about being realistic and finding the right environment for them to grow and flourish.

Examples of "Killing It Before It Grows"

So how can we apply this lesson to our own lives? Let's look at a few examples:

  • You've been working on a project for weeks, but no matter how hard you try, it just isn't coming together. Maybe it's time to let that project go and start fresh with a new idea.
  • You're in a toxic relationship that drains your energy and makes you unhappy. Maybe it's time to end that relationship and focus on finding healthier, more supportive friendships and partnerships.
  • You've always dreamed of being a doctor, but no matter how many times you try to get into medical school, you just can't seem to make it happen. Maybe it's time to consider a different path, like nursing or public health.

These are just a few examples, but the lesson applies to virtually any area of our lives. Sometimes, we need to be willing to let go of things that aren't working in order to make room for things that will.

Conclusion

So what have we learned from Bob Marley's quote? Here are three key takeaways:

  1. It's important to be realistic about what can and cannot thrive in our environment.
  2. Sometimes, we need to be willing to let go of things that aren't working in order to make room for things that will.
  3. Killing our own ideas and dreams isn't necessarily a bad thing; it's about being proactive and making room for growth and progress.

Next time you're faced with an obstacle in your life, remember Bob Marley's words: "Kill it before it grows." Then take a deep breath, let go of what isn't working, and make room for what will.
