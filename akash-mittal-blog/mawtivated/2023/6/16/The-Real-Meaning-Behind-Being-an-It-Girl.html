The Real Meaning Behind Being an "It Girl"



It was in the early 2000s when Amanda Seyfried, then a young and aspiring actress, earned herself the unofficial status of an "it girl." She was fresh out of her teenage years and already making waves in Hollywood with her breakout role in the hit comedy-drama series, "Veronica Mars." Her charm, talent, and striking looks all contributed to her newfound fame and popularity, and it seemed like her star was only ascending from there.

But as she would soon realize, being considered an "it girl" isn't just about being the talk of the town and getting your face plastered on every magazine cover. As Seyfried elegantly put it, there has to be something solid behind that supposed status.

For many young women who have been labeled as "it girls," the road to that title can often be both exciting and daunting. On one hand, it means you have become a recognized figure in your respective field, whether it be in entertainment, fashion, or even politics. You are seen as a trendsetter, an influencer, and a role model to many. But on the other hand, it also means you have a lot to live up to – and that can take a toll on your mental and emotional wellbeing.

What Does It Really Mean to Be an "It Girl?"

Firstly, let's address the elephant in the room – what exactly is an "it girl?" There are many definitions and interpretations of the term, but generally speaking, it is used to describe a young woman who is deemed to be popular, charismatic, and often recognized for her physical beauty or unique personal brand.

However, being an "it girl" is not a predetermined label that one is born with. It is a status that is earned through a combination of hard work, talent, and a certain je ne sais quoi. That said, it is important to acknowledge that the criteria for being seen as an "it girl" can vary depending on the industry or niche one is in.

For example, in the fashion world, an "it girl" may be someone who has a distinct sense of style, is a frequent attendee of high-profile events, and has a sizable following on social media. In politics, an "it girl" could refer to a young woman who is challenging the status quo and making strides in their advocacy work. In entertainment, an "it girl" may be someone who has gained critical acclaim for her performances and has a fan base who adores her.

What Are the Pros and Cons of Being an "It Girl?"

Pros:

  • Increased visibility and exposure
  • The opportunity to influence and inspire others
  • Access to high-profile events and networking opportunities
  • The chance to collaborate with other creatives in the industry
  • Higher earning potential and career growth prospects

Cons:

  • Pressure to maintain a certain image or brand
  • Increased scrutiny from the media and public
  • Difficulty in separating personal and professional life
  • Competition and comparison with other "it girls"
  • Risk of burnout and exhaustion from constant attention and demands

Examples of "It Girls" Throughout History

While the term "it girl" may seem like a recent phenomenon, the concept of a young woman being idolized and admired for her charm and beauty has existed for centuries. Here are just a few examples of iconic "it girls" from different eras and industries:

Louise Brooks

"I have a gift for enraging people, but if I ever bore you, it will be with a knife."

1920s silent movie star Louise Brooks was known for her signature blunt bob haircut and rebellious attitude. She captivated audiences with her performances in films such as "Pandora's Box" and "Diary of a Lost Girl."

Audrey Hepburn

"The beauty of a woman is not in the clothes she wears, the figure that she carries, or the way she combs her hair. The beauty of a woman is seen in her eyes, because that is the doorway to her heart, the place where love resides."

Hollywood icon Audrey Hepburn was a true icon of grace and elegance. Her performances in films like "Roman Holiday" and "Breakfast at Tiffany's" solidified her status as one of the most beloved actresses of all time.

Diana Spencer

"I don't go by the rule book. I lead from the heart, not the head."

Known to the world as Princess Diana, she was not only a member of the British royal family but also a humanitarian and activist. She used her platform to bring attention to issues related to health, education, and poverty, and remains a beloved figure to this day.

What Can We Learn From "It Girls"?

Whether you aspire to be an "it girl" yourself or simply admire those who have achieved that status, there are valuable lessons that can be learned from these women. Here are a few:

  • Stay true to yourself – don't try to mold yourself into what others expect you to be
  • Be passionate and dedicated to your craft – hard work and persistence pays off
  • Use your platform for good – whether it's advocating for a cause you believe in or inspiring others to pursue their dreams
  • Maintain a support system – surround yourself with people who lift you up and encourage you to be your best self
  • Take care of yourself – prioritize self-care and establish healthy boundaries to avoid burnout and exhaustion

3 Key Takeaways:

  1. An "it girl" is a young woman who is deemed to be popular, charismatic, and often recognized for her physical beauty or unique personal brand.
  2. While being an "it girl" can offer increased visibility and exposure, it also comes with the pressure to maintain a certain image and increased scrutiny from the media and public.
  3. Lessons to learn from "it girls" include staying true to yourself, being passionate and dedicated to your craft, using your platform for good, maintaining a support system, and taking care of yourself.
