My Dad Was a Ham, And I Am Proud to Be Like Him



There is nothing more inspiring than seeing someone you love excel at what they do best. For me, that person was my dad. Born in the early 1900s, he was a natural-born salesman who could sell anything to anyone. He started with simple products like fruits and vegetables in his hometown market, but throughout the years, he moved on to more challenging tasks like selling automobiles and real estate. He was always an inspiration to me, and I am proud to say that he taught me everything I know about sales and marketing.

The Legacy of Paul Lynde

When I was just starting out in the sales business, I found myself looking up to a man named Paul Lynde. For those who are not familiar with him, Paul Lynde was an American comedian and actor known for his sarcastic humor and his roles in Broadway productions such as "Bye Bye Birdie" and TV shows like "Bewitched." But what most people don't know is that Paul Lynde was also a skilled salesman. He started his career selling women's clothing and quickly moved up the ladder to become the top salesman in his department. His ability to connect with people and sell them what they wanted was unmatched, and he became a legend in the sales business.

The Art of Selling

As I learned from my dad and from Paul Lynde, the art of selling is not just about convincing someone to buy a product. It's about creating a connection with them, understanding their needs, and showing them how your product can help them solve their problems. It's about building trust and loyalty, and making sure that your customers are always satisfied with their purchases.

Over the years, I have seen many people try to sell products without understanding these principles. They use aggressive tactics, talk too much, and forget to listen to their customers. This approach rarely works, and it can damage your reputation in the long run.

Practical Tips for Sales Success

So how do you become a successful salesperson? Here are a few tips:

  • Listen to your customers: Ask them questions, find out what they are looking for, and show them how your product can help them.
  • Be honest: Don't overpromise or oversell. If your product has limitations, be transparent about them.
  • Build relationships: Follow up with your customers, send them helpful resources, and make sure that they are satisfied with their purchase.
  • Find your niche: Figure out what you are passionate about and focus on selling products that align with your values and interests.
Conclusion: The Art of Being a Great Salesperson

In conclusion, I am proud to have learned from my dad and from legends like Paul Lynde about the art of selling. It's not just about making money or closing deals, but about creating connections with people and helping them solve their problems. By listening to your customers, being honest, building relationships, finding your niche, and understanding the principles of salesmanship, you can become a great salesperson and make a positive impact on the world.

In summary, to become a good salesperson, remember:

  1. Listen to your customers
  2. Be honest
  3. Build relationships
