Choose Your Friends Wisely for a Lasting Friendship



When I was in high school, I made a friend named Sarah. She was fashionable, popular, and seemed to have it all together. I was initially drawn to her because I wanted to be like her. However, as we spent more time together, I realized that we didn't have much in common beyond our love for shopping. After graduation, we lost touch and never spoke again.

The above quote by the famous actress, Renee Olstead, sums up my experience perfectly. Picking your friends based on superficial qualities won't lead to lasting friendships. Instead, you should choose friends based on values, interests, and character.

The Story of Renee Olstead

Renee Olstead is an American actress and singer who first gained fame in the early 2000s as a child actress on the television series "Still Standing." Despite her early success, Renee struggled to find true friends in the entertainment industry. She found that many people only befriended her because of her fame and status rather than for who she was as a person.

After some self-reflection, Renee decided to change her approach to friendships. She began to surround herself with people who had similar values, interests, and passions as her. Through this shift in focus, Renee found lasting friendships and a sense of community that she had been missing.

The Danger of Superficial Friendships

Superficial friendships based on appearance or popularity rarely last beyond high school or college. When the basis of the friendship is built on shallow values, there is little room for growth or genuine connection. As people grow and change, so do their values and interests. If a friendship is only tied to certain superficial qualities, it is unlikely to withstand those changes.

On the other hand, friendships that are built on shared values, interests, and character traits have a much greater chance of lasting. These friendships provide a foundation for genuine connection and understanding that can withstand the test of time and life's challenges.

How to Choose Friends Wisely

Choosing friends wisely requires some effort and intentionality. Here are some practical tips to help you in the process:

  • Assess your own values and interests - before you can choose friends based on shared values and interests, you need to know what yours are.
  • Look for people who share your passions - whether it's a sport, hobby, or career path, finding people who share your passions can provide a strong foundation for friendship.
  • Observe their character - character traits like honesty, kindness, and reliability are important foundations for any friendship.
  • Take your time - building genuine friendships takes time and effort. Don't rush the process or force friendships where there isn't a natural connection.

Conclusion

To summarize, choosing friends based on superficial qualities like appearance or popularity rarely leads to lasting friendships. Instead, you should focus on finding friends who share your values, interests, and character traits. By doing so, you can build genuine connections that can withstand the test of time.
