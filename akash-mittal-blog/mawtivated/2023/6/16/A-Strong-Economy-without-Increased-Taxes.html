A Strong Economy without Increased Taxes



Once upon a time, in a small town in the state of New Jersey, there was a man named John who started a small business selling homemade soap and candles. At first, he struggled to make ends meet and often found himself wondering if he would have to shut down his business and find a new job. However, after years of hard work and dedication, John's business began to flourish and he was able to not only provide for his family but also give back to his community in many ways.

John's story is just one example of the countless entrepreneurs and small business owners who fuel the economy and provide jobs for millions of Americans. However, sometimes these businesses face challenges and obstacles that go beyond the typical ups and downs of entrepreneurship, such as increased taxes.

The Wrong Way to Go

"And I just think that we're at a point in our economic life here in our state - and - and, candidly, across the country, where increased taxes is just the wrong way to go. The people of our state are not convinced that state government, county government, local government has done all they can with the money we already give them, rather than the money that we have before." - Chris Christie

Former Governor Chris Christie of New Jersey is not alone in his belief that increased taxes are not the solution to economic growth. Many Americans believe that the government should focus on being more efficient and effective with the tax money they already receive instead of constantly asking for more from taxpayers.

According to a survey conducted by the National Small Business Association, 47% of small business owners listed taxes as the number one hurdle for their business, followed by economic uncertainty and government regulations. This same survey found that 75% of small business owners believe that tax reform should be a top priority for the government.

The Case for Innovation

If increased taxes are not the answer, what is? One solution is to promote innovation and entrepreneurship. By creating an environment that encourages and supports small businesses, governments can help stimulate economic growth and job creation.

Some practical tips for supporting innovators and entrepreneurs include:

  • Providing affordable access to capital through loans and grants
  • Offering tax incentives for small businesses that create jobs and invest in their communities
  • Supporting education and training programs that promote skills development and entrepreneurship
  • Reducing regulatory burdens that hinder innovation and growth

The Power of Partnership

Another key aspect of promoting economic growth is the power of collaboration. By working together, businesses and government can create policies and initiatives that benefit the local economy. For example, the city of Chattanooga, Tennessee, partnered with the local business community to create a high-speed fiber optic network, which has attracted new startups and businesses to the city.

Other examples of successful public-private partnerships include:

  • The Miami-Dade Beacon Council, which is a partnership between business and government that promotes economic growth and development in the Miami-Dade County region.
  • The Innovate Birmingham Regional Workforce Partnership, which brings together local businesses and educational institutions to train and develop a skilled workforce in the technology industry.

A Strong Economy for All

In conclusion, there are many ways to promote economic growth and job creation without resorting to increased taxes. By supporting innovation, collaboration, and entrepreneurship, governments at all levels can help create a strong economy that benefits everyone.

Three key takeaways include:

  1. Increased taxes are not the answer to promoting economic growth
  2. Supporting innovators and entrepreneurs is a practical solution that can stimulate job creation
  3. Public-private partnerships can create policies and initiatives that benefit the local economy

Let's work together to create a better future and a stronger economy for all.
