Editing Yourself: The Art of Stripping Down for Clarity



It's not easy to edit yourself. As a writer or creator, you're invested in your work and likely attached to certain phrases, sentences, or ideas. But editing is a crucial part of the content creation process. It's what takes your work from good to great, from competent to remarkable.

Consider the story of Vera Farmiga, an accomplished actress, director, and producer. She started her career in the late 1990s and quickly gained acclaim for her nuanced performances in films like Down to the Bone and The Departed. However, as she began taking on more directing and producing roles, she realized that editing was an integral part of the process.

"Editing yourself is like an irksome coin toss," she said in an interview with The Guardian. "You've got to strip yourself of super ego and operate from the id. Maybe I've got my Freud mixed up. It's just hard to trade a beauty shot for the performance with truth and a brightly lit zit."

What Farmiga means is that editing requires you to remove anything extraneous, anything that doesn't serve the story or message you're trying to convey. It means being ruthless with your own work and being willing to make tough choices.

The Benefits of Self-Editing

Self-editing allows you to:

  • Reduce clutter and streamline your message
  • Improve clarity and readability
  • Ensure your work is free of errors and mistakes
  • Focus on what's important and cut out anything extraneous

It can be tempting to believe that your work is perfect as-is. But even the best writers and creators need to edit their work. In fact, it's often the case that the most successful writers are also the most ruthless editors. They understand that editing is what takes their work from good to great.

Practical Tips for Editing Yourself

Here are some practical tips for self-editing:

  1. Take time away from your work. It's hard to be objective about something you just finished. Give yourself time to step away from your work and come back to it with fresh eyes.
  2. Read your work out loud. This will help you catch errors, ensure your sentences flow together smoothly, and identify any confusing phrases.
  3. Cut anything that doesn't serve the story or message. It can be hard to let go of something you worked hard on, but if it doesn't serve your overall goal, it needs to go.
  4. Focus on clarity and simplicity. Use clear, concise language and avoid complex sentence structures or jargon.
  5. Use editing tools. There are numerous online tools available that can help you catch errors, improve readability, and identify areas that need improvement.

The Importance of Honesty

At the end of the day, the most important aspect of self-editing is honesty. You need to be honest with yourself about what's working and what's not. It can be hard to let go of something you're emotionally attached to, but if it's not serving your overall goal, it needs to go.

"Maybe I've got my Freud mixed-up," Vera Farmiga said. "It's just hard to trade a beauty shot for the performance with truth and a brightly lit zit."

Editing requires you to look at your work honestly and critically, identifying areas that need improvement and making the tough choices necessary to improve your overall message and impact.

Conclusion

In conclusion, editing yourself is a crucial part of the content creation process. It allows you to streamline your message, improve clarity, and achieve greater impact. To effectively self-edit, it's important to be honest with yourself, be willing to make tough choices, and focus on clarity and simplicity.

  1. Take time away from your work.
  2. Read your work out loud.
  3. Cut anything that doesn't serve the story or message.

By following these tips and focusing on honesty and clarity, you'll be able to create compelling, impactful content that resonates with your audience.

Examples

For examples of effective self-editing, consider reading the works of acclaimed writers like Ernest Hemingway, F. Scott Fitzgerald, and Joan Didion. These writers are known for their economical, impactful prose and their willingness to make tough choices when it comes to editing.
