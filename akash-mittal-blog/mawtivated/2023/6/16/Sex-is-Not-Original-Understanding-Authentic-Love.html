Sex is Not Original - Understanding Authentic Love



A Story of Jane Campion

Jane Campion is a New Zealand screenwriter, producer, and director who has been recognized for her exceptional talent in storytelling. She has won multiple awards for her films and TV series, including the Palme d'Or at the Cannes Film Festival for "The Piano." Campion's work often explores the complexities and nuances of human relationships, particularly those that are intimate and erotic.

In an interview, Campion once said, "Sex is actually not so original as the way people love or the stories behind each relationship, which is what you remember. Sex is sex in the end." Her words are a powerful reminder that intimacy and connection go beyond physical arousal and pleasure. Authentic love involves a deep understanding and appreciation of the other person, their quirks, flaws, and strengths, as well as a willingness to be vulnerable and honest.

What is Authentic Love?

Authentic love is relationship glue that binds us together. It is genuine, respectful, and selfless, as opposed to the shallow, selfish, and superficial love that often dominates our culture. Authentic love sees beyond external appearances and social status, and connects us with the essential humanity of the other person.

Examples of Authentic Love

  • A couple who has been married for 50 years and still hold hands and joke with each other
  • A parent who sacrifices their own comfort and convenience to provide for their child
  • A friend who listens without judgement and supports you through difficult times

Authenticity in Relationship

Authenticity is the key ingredient to a healthy and fulfilling relationship. Genuine communication, mutual respect, and emotional vulnerability are all crucial aspects of authenticity. When we allow ourselves to be true to ourselves and the other person, we create a deep and meaningful connection that transcends the physical and emotional levels.

For instance, discussing your desires and boundaries, without fear of judgement, brings a level of intimacy that is deeper than any physical interaction. It requires bravery, trust, and honesty to expose yourself in such a way and to create a safe space for the other person to do so as well.

Conclusion

  1. Authentic love is the foundation of a healthy and fulfilling relationship
  2. Sex is just a part of physical attraction and pleasure
  3. Commitment towards communication, mutual respect, and emotional vulnerability, leads to a deeper level of intimacy.
