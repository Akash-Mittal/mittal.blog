Australia's Involvement in the Vietnam War

Although Australia's involvement in the Vietnam War was controversial, the government had made the strategic decision to be a part of the conflict in order to bolster its alliance with the US and provide military support to its military forces in Asia. This involved sending troops to Vietnam, as well as providing logistical support and supplies.

Australia's involvement in the Vietnam War lasted from 1962 to 1975, with over 60,000 Australian soldiers serving in the conflict. While it was a long and difficult war that spanned two decades, it was ultimately unsuccessful, with the communist forces of North Vietnam finally capturing Saigon, the capital of South Vietnam, in April 1975.

The Draft in Australia

During the Vietnam War, the Australian government introduced a lottery system to select individuals for conscription. The draft was initially focused on those aged 20 and over, who were deemed fit to serve in the military due to their age, health, and education. The lottery was designed to ensure that conscripts were selected fairly and could not avoid the draft based on their social status or connections.

Those who were conscripted were required to serve for two years in the military, but many chose to enlist voluntarily in order to choose their preferred branch of service and receive additional training. While the draft was controversial, it did ensure that the army had enough personnel to fill the ranks and provide the support that was needed to aid the US in the conflict.
