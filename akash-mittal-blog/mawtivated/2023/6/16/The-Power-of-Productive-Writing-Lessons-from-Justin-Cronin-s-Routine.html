The Power of Productive Writing: Lessons from Justin Cronin's Routine



Whether you are a professional writer or just starting out, you know that writing can be a challenging task. Distractions, lack of inspiration, and procrastination can stand in the way of your writing goals and prevent you from achieving your full potential. However, there are successful writers out there whose habits and routines can teach you how to become more productive and efficient.

An Inspiring Story: Justin Cronin's 6-Hour Writing Routine

One such author is Justin Cronin, known for his bestselling novel "The Passage". In an interview, Cronin shared his daily writing routine, which involves starting at 9 o'clock in the morning and writing until 3 – his best hours, he says. These hours fit the other rhythms of the world.

Cronin writes for six hours pretty much without any breaks. According to him, this routine allows him to get into a deep state of concentration and stay there for a prolonged period. He blocks the world out and molds his ideas into a well-crafted story.

This routine might seem too intense for some, but it highlights the importance of having a consistent writing routine that works for you. No matter how busy you are with other responsibilities, finding a specific timeframe in your day to focus on writing can make a significant difference.

How to Make Your Writing Productive?

Here are some tips inspired by Justin Cronin and other successful authors that can help you make your writing productive:

  • Find Your Optimal Writing Time: Experiment with different times of the day to see when your creativity and productivity are at their peak.
  • Create a Writing Ritual: Develop a writing ritual that signals to your brain it's time to write, whether it's lighting a candle, brewing a pot of tea, or listening to music.
  • Set Writing Goals: Set specific and achievable goals for your writing. This can be a number of words you want to write every day or a specific deadline for completing a chapter.
  • Eliminate Distractions: Turn off your phone, disconnect from the internet, and eliminate any other distractions that might interrupt your writing flow.

Author - Justin Cronin's Story or Background

Justin Cronin is an American author born and raised in New England. He attended Harvard University and obtained his MFA from the Iowa Writers' Workshop, where he won the prestigious Michener fellowship. He has published many novels and stories, including "The Passage" trilogy, which has been adapted into a television series.
