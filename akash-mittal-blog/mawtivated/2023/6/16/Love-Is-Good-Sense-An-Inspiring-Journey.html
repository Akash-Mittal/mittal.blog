Love Is Good Sense: An Inspiring Journey



Ken Kesey, the author of One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest, once said,

People think love is an emotion. Love is good sense.

Although love is often portrayed as a feeling that comes and goes, it is, in fact, a decision that is based on good judgment and strong reasons.

The Story of Jane and Mark

Jane and Mark were high school sweethearts. They met when they were only fourteen years old and fell in love at first sight. They felt as if they were meant to be together forever and promised each other they would never part.

However, after they graduated, Jane went to college, while Mark stayed at home to work in his father's business. The distance and the pressure of their different lifestyles started to take a toll on their relationship. They grew apart, argued more often, and stopped seeing each other.

One day, Jane received a phone call from Mark's mother. She told Jane that Mark had been in an accident and was in critical condition. Jane rushed to the hospital and found out that Mark was in a coma. She stayed by his side, hoping that he would wake up and that they would have a second chance. She realized how much she loved him and how important he was to her.

After a few days, Mark woke up from his coma. He was disoriented, but he recognized Jane's voice and touched her hand. He smiled weakly, and Jane felt a surge of hope and happiness.

They talked for hours, holding hands and crying. They remembered the good times and the bad times, the laughs and the tears, the dreams and the fears. They realized that they had taken each other for granted and that they had forgotten what love was all about.

Finally, Mark asked Jane a question that changed their lives forever. He said, "Do you still love me?"

Jane looked into his eyes and said, "Yes, I do. I never stopped loving you."

Mark smiled and said, "Thank you. You have given me a reason to live."

Jane and Mark got married a few months later, in the same church where they had gone to Sunday school as children. They invited their families, their friends, and their old classmates. They danced, laughed, and cried. They knew that they had found true love.

Love Is Good Sense

The story of Jane and Mark shows us that love is not an emotion that wavers or fades away. Love is good sense, a decision that is based on solid reasons, values, and beliefs. Love is a commitment that lasts through thick and thin, through joy and sorrow, through sickness and health.

Love is good sense because it makes us think and act wisely. When we love someone, we want to make them happy, we want to share our lives with them, and we want to grow old with them. We choose to be patient, kind, respectful, and honest. We choose to forgive, to compromise, to listen, and to learn.

Love is good sense because it helps us see things as they are, not as we wish them to be. Love requires us to be realistic, pragmatic, and adaptable. We need to accept our partner's strengths and weaknesses, their goals and dreams, their fears and flaws. We need to respect their individuality and support their growth.

Love is good sense because it enriches our lives and makes us better people. When we love someone, we become more compassionate, more generous, and more understanding. We learn to appreciate the beauty of life and the power of connection. We discover our strengths and our talents, and we use them to make a difference in the world.

The Power of Love

Love is not a simple or easy thing, but it is a wonderful and powerful thing. Love can heal wounds, bridge gaps, and create miracles. Love can inspire us to greatness, to creativity, and to generosity. Love can unify us, uplift us, and transform us.

If you want to experience the power of love, here are some practical tips:

  • Be honest with yourself and your partner.
  • Communicate openly and respectfully.
  • List your values and priorities and discuss them.
  • Celebrate your achievements and support each other's goals.
  • Forgive and apologize when you make mistakes.
  • Show gratitude and appreciation for the little things.
  • Don't compare your relationship to others.
  • Seek advice and help when needed.
  • Keep learning about each other and the world.

The Conclusion

To sum up, love is good sense. It is a decision that is based on solid reasons, values, and beliefs. Love requires us to be patient, kind, respectful, and honest. It requires us to accept our partner's strengths and weaknesses, their goals and dreams, their fears and flaws. Love enriches our lives and makes us better people.

If you choose to love, you choose to live a life of joy, growth, and purpose. You choose to embrace the power of connection and to share it with the world.
