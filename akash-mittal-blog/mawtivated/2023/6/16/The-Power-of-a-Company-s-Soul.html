The Power of a Company's Soul



It was a scorching hot day in the summer of 1990 when John and his younger brother, Paul, set out to start their own lemonade stand. They had spent the entire morning mixing lemon juice, water, and sugar to perfection. When everything was set up and the sign was hung, they were ready to start selling.

After an hour of waiting, a man walked up to the counter and ordered a cup of lemonade. John took the man's money and handed him a cup. The man smiled and took a sip of the lemonade. Suddenly, a look of pure joy spread across his face. "This is the best lemonade I've ever tasted!" he exclaimed. "What's your secret?"

John thought for a moment and replied, "It's not a secret, sir. We just put a little bit of love into everything we make."

This story perfectly illustrates the power of a company's soul. Companies that focus solely on growth or money are short-sighted. To really succeed in the long run, a company must have a soul. This means that they must take pride in their work, respect others, and strive to make people happy.

Charles Handy is a renowned philosopher and author who has written extensively about the role of the soul in a company. He argues that the companies that survive the longest are the ones that understand what they uniquely can give to the world. It's not just about making money or growing the business. It's about making a positive impact on the world.

What Makes a Company's Soul?

  • Excellence: A company that has a soul strives for excellence in everything they do. They take pride in their work and are constantly looking for ways to improve.
  • Respect for Others: A company that has a soul treats everyone with respect, whether it's customers, employees, or suppliers. They understand that everyone is important and deserves to be treated with dignity.
  • Making People Happy: A company that has a soul understands that its purpose is to make people happy. This could be through a great product, exceptional customer service, or simply creating a positive workplace culture.

Examples of Companies with a Soul

There are many companies that have a soul. Here are just a few examples:

"The Body Shop was founded on the belief that business could be a force for good."

– The Body Shop

The Body Shop is a company that has been focused on sustainability and ethical business practices since its founding in 1976. They source their ingredients responsibly and are committed to reducing their environmental impact. They also strive to create a positive workplace culture and empower their employees.

"Starbucks is committed to inspiring and nurturing the human spirit—one person, one cup, and one neighborhood at a time."

– Starbucks

Starbucks is a company that has a strong sense of community and social responsibility. They are committed to sourcing their coffee ethically and have a program called "Starbucks Shared Planet" that focuses on sustainability and social issues. They also encourage employee volunteerism and have a program called "Starbucks Community Service".

"Patagonia is in business to save our home planet."

– Patagonia

Patagonia is a company that has a deep commitment to environmental sustainability. They were one of the first companies to be certified as a B Corp, meaning that they meet rigorous standards of social and environmental performance. They also encourage their customers to reduce their environmental impact through their "Worn Wear" program.

The Benefits of Having a Soul

Having a soul isn't just the right thing to do—it's also good for business. Companies that have a soul enjoy many benefits, including:

  • Increased customer loyalty: Customers are more likely to stick with a company that they feel has a strong sense of purpose and values.
  • Better employee engagement: Employees are more likely to be engaged and motivated when they feel that their work is making a positive impact on the world.
  • Improved brand reputation: A company that has a soul is more likely to be seen as trustworthy and responsible, which can lead to a stronger brand reputation.

A Call to Action

If you're a business owner or leader, it's time to start thinking about your company's soul. What can you uniquely give to the world? How can you make a positive impact on your customers, employees, and community? Here are three practical tips to get you started:

  1. Identify your company's values: What do you stand for? What are your non-negotiables? Write them down and make sure that everyone in your company knows what they are.
  2. Get involved in your community: Find ways to give back to your community, whether it's through volunteerism, donations, or partnerships with local organizations.
  3. Measure your impact: Keep track of the positive impact that your company is making on the world. Set goals and track your progress towards achieving them.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the companies that survive the longest are the ones that have a soul. Companies that focus solely on growth or money are short-sighted, while those that focus on making a positive impact on the world will thrive in the long run. By striving for excellence, treating others with respect, and making people happy, your company can become a force for good in the world.
