The Power of Instincts - Lessons from Sachin Tendulkar



Legend has it that Sachin Tendulkar started playing cricket at the tender age of 11, and it wasn't long before the world realized that he was destined for greatness. Over the years, Sachin went on to become one of the most accomplished cricketers in the world, earning accolades for his exceptional batting skills and his ability to read the game. What set Sachin apart, however, was his natural instinct which allowed him to perform under pressure like no other.

Sachin Tendulkar, in his own words, once said, "I get 0.5 seconds to react to a ball, sometimes even less than that. I can't be thinking of what XYZ has said about me. I need to surrender myself to my natural instincts. My subconscious mind knows exactly what to do. It is trained to react."

As an entrepreneur, professional or student, you too can learn from Sachin's approach to life and work. In this article, we will delve deeper into the power of instincts and how they can help you achieve your goals.

Trusting Your Instincts

Our instincts are a result of our life experiences and the knowledge we have accumulated over the years. They are our subconscious mind's way of communicating with us, and more often than not, they are right. By trusting your instincts, you can make better decisions that are guided by your inner wisdom rather than your fears and doubts.

When Sachin is on the field, he doesn't have the luxury of time to mull over his options. He has to rely on his instincts to make quick decisions that can change the outcome of the game. His natural ability to read the game and his years of practice come together to give him an edge over his opponents. Similarly, in business or life, trusting your instincts can give you an edge over your rivals.

Shutting Out the Noise

Sachin's statement that he can't be thinking of what XYZ has said about him is a reminder of the importance of shutting out the noise and focusing on what's important. When we listen to external voices, we can lose sight of our goals and start second-guessing ourselves. By shutting out the noise, we can create space for ourselves to listen to our inner voice and gain clarity on our next steps.

At home, Sachin's family doesn't discuss media coverage. This allows Sachin to focus on his game and shut out the noise so that he can perform at his best. Similarly, in life, we need to create boundaries that allow us to focus on what's important and shut out the noise that can distract us from our goals.

Developing Your Instincts

Finally, developing your instincts is a process that requires discipline and practice. Sachin's years of dedication and practice have honed his instincts to the point where he can rely on them to make split-second decisions on the field.

To develop your instincts, you need to create space for yourself to reflect on your experiences and learn from them. Mindfulness practices such as meditation can help you develop your self-awareness and connect with your inner wisdom. Additionally, seeking out new experiences and challenges can help you expand your knowledge and develop your instincts.

Conclusion

Trust your instincts, shut out the noise, and develop your instincts. These are the three key takeaways from Sachin Tendulkar's approach to life and work. By following in Sachin's footsteps, you can tap into the power of your instincts and achieve your goals with ease.

Practical Tips for Trusting Your Instincts:

  • Take time to connect with your inner wisdom daily
  • Practice mindfulness techniques such as meditation or journaling
  • Seek out new experiences and embrace learning opportunities
