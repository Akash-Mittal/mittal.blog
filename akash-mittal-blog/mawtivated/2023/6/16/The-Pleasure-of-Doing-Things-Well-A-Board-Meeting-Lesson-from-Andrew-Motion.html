The Pleasure of Doing Things Well: A Board Meeting Lesson from Andrew Motion



Have you ever experienced a moment when everything just clicks into place, when the team seems to be in perfect sync, and every decision you make seems to be the right one? That moment when you feel like you're truly accomplishing something great? For Andrew Motion, it's not a moment, it's a feeling that he chases in every board meeting he attends.

Andrew Motion is a poet and a former UK poet laureate, but he's also served as a board member for the Museums, Libraries and Archives Council (MLA). In a recent interview, he described his experiences attending MLA board meetings and how they have helped him understand the true value of doing things well.

The Power of Purposeful Meetings

During his time at MLA, Motion realized that the key to making the most of every meeting is to establish a clear purpose. When everyone knows what they're there to accomplish, it's easier to stay on track and make progress towards a shared goal.

Tip #1: Always start with a clear agenda.

Your meeting agenda should outline the purpose of the meeting, the topics to be covered, and any action items that need to be decided on. Share the agenda with your attendees beforehand, so they have a chance to prepare and come ready to contribute.

The Power of Collaboration

Another lesson Motion learned is the importance of collaboration. When everyone around the table is given a chance to contribute, you can arrive at solutions that are smarter, more creative, and more effective than anything you could have come up with on your own.

Tip #2: Encourage everyone to speak up.

Make sure you create an environment where people feel safe to share their ideas and opinions. Encourage them to ask questions and challenge each other constructively. Remember, the best ideas often come from unexpected sources.

The Power of Focus

Finally, Motion learned that the key to making progress in any meeting is to stay focused. When everyone is able to keep their attention on the task at hand, you can accomplish a surprising amount in a short amount of time.

Tip #3: Stay focused on the agenda items.

It's easy to get sidetracked by tangents or distractions, but at the end of the day, you're all there to accomplish something specific. Keep the conversation on track by gently steering it back to the agenda whenever you need to.

"I'm not precisely saying that a really good board meeting at the MLA makes me want to go and write poetry, but there is a pleasure in doing that sort of thing well." - Andrew Motion

By following these three tips, you'll be well on your way to creating board meetings that are productive, purposeful, and even (dare we say it?) enjoyable. Motion's insights can apply to any kind of meeting, from project planning sessions to team check-ins. The key is to keep the focus on doing things well and achieving your shared goals.
