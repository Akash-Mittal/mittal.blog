Why Marrying Early Might Not Be the Best Idea



Jackie was the type of girl who had it all figured out. She graduated high school with top honors, went on to her dream college, and managed to meet the perfect man her sophomore year. They were inseparable, and the entire campus swooned when he proposed to her just before graduation. Jackie was ecstatic. She had the perfect man and the perfect life planned out: she was going to marry him in a few months, start her own business, and pop out a few perfect babies before age thirty. The wedding was a fairytale, and everyone was so happy for her.

Fast forward five years and Jackie was a shell of her former self. Her business never took off, her husband was distant and aloof, and the two kids she had given birth to were adorable but constantly in need of her attention. Jackie was exhausted, and her perfect life had turned into a nightmare. It was the most difficult decision she had ever made, but Jackie knew she needed to leave her husband and start over.

Stories like Jackie's are all too common, unfortunately. Women who marry early are often overly enamored by the idea of a fairytale wedding and a perfect life with the man of their dreams. But as Anna Quindlen so aptly put it, "Women who marry early are often overly enamored of the kind of man who looks great in wedding pictures and passes the maid of honor his telephone number."

But why is this such a common occurrence? Why are young women so eager to tie themselves down to a man before they've even had a chance to live their own lives?

The Pressure to Settle Down

From a young age, girls are taught that finding a husband and settling down is the ultimate goal in life. We are bombarded with images of women in bridal gowns and baby carriages, and it's almost as if our society doesn't value women unless they are in a committed relationship. This pressure only gets stronger as we get older, and it can be difficult to go against the grain and pursue a different path.

The Illusion of Happiness

Marrying early can seem like the perfect solution to all of life's problems. You have a loving partner who will be with you through thick and thin, you have financial stability, and you can start a family whenever you're ready. But the reality is often much different. Marrying too young means that you haven't had a chance to discover who you are as a person, and you may end up sacrificing your dreams and ambitions for the sake of your partner and your family.

Alternatives to Marrying Early

  • Travel: Take some time to explore the world and find out what you're passionate about. You'll meet new people, experience different cultures, and maybe even discover a new perspective on life.
  • Education: Pursue a degree or certification in a field that interests you. Not only will you gain valuable knowledge and skills, but you'll also have the opportunity to meet like-minded individuals who are passionate about the same things as you.
  • Volunteer: Giving back to your community can be incredibly rewarding and fulfilling. Find a cause or organization that you're passionate about and devote your time and energy to making a difference.

Marrying early can seem like the perfect fairy tale ending, but the reality is often a nightmare. It's important for young women to take the time to discover who they are and pursue their passions before tying themselves down to a lifetime commitment that may not bring them happiness.

"Women who marry early are often overly enamored of the kind of man who looks great in wedding pictures and passes the maid of honor his telephone number." - Anna Quindlen

In conclusion, remember:

  1. You are valuable as an individual. Your worth does not rely on your marital status or relationship with a man.
  2. Explore your passions and interests. This is the time to discover who you are and what you want from life.
  3. Don't rush into marriage. Take the time to build a strong foundation for a healthy and happy relationship.
