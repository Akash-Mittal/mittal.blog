Growing Up With the Classics



When I was a kid, my favorite time of the week was when my mom and I would settle in for a movie night. We'd get out the popcorn and hot chocolate, snuggle up in our blankets, and watch classic movies like 'Singin' in the Rain' and 'White Christmas'. It was a tradition that we kept up even as I got older and started to pursue my own interests.

Now, as an adult, I can look back and see just how much those movie nights shaped me. Sure, they were just a fun way to spend time with my mom, but they also gave me a love of storytelling, music, and old Hollywood glamour. More than that, though, they taught me some important lessons that have stuck with me through the years.

Lessons Learned from Classic Movies

1. The Importance of Hard Work

One of the themes that comes up time and again in classic movies is the idea that success doesn't come to those who sit around waiting for it. Whether it's Gene Kelly dancing in the rain, or Bing Crosby and Danny Kaye putting together a show from scratch, the characters in these movies are always working hard to achieve their goals. They don't take the easy way out, and they don't expect anyone else to do their work for them.

This is a lesson that's served me well in my own life. As an actor and musician, I know that success doesn't just fall into your lap. You have to put in the hours practicing, networking, and honing your craft. But it's not just about building your own career - it's about being a responsible, contributing member of society. When you work hard and do your best, you're not just benefiting yourself; you're contributing to the world around you.

2. The Power of Creativity

Another thing that classic movies taught me is the importance of creativity. From the imaginative dance numbers in 'An American in Paris' to the clever song lyrics in 'Holiday Inn', these films are full of examples of people using their creativity to connect with others and express themselves.

As someone who grew up as a performer, I can attest to the fact that creativity is a powerful tool. When you're creating something - whether it's a song, a painting, or a performance - you're connecting with a deeper part of yourself. You're expressing your emotions, your experiences, and your view of the world. And when you share your creativity with others, you're creating a connection between yourself and your audience. It's an incredibly rewarding feeling.

3. The Value of Friendship

Finally, classic movies taught me the importance of friendship. Whether it's the buddy-cop dynamic of 'Lethal Weapon' or the sisterly bond between Judy and Betty in 'White Christmas', these films show that human connections are an essential part of our lives.

For me, friendship has always been a source of strength and support. When you have people in your life who understand you, believe in you, and are there for you no matter what, you have the foundation you need to tackle anything that comes your way. And when you're able to be that kind of friend to someone else, you're making the world a better place.

Conclusion: The Legacy of Classic Movies

In many ways, the classic movies that I grew up with were just a means of entertainment. But they were also much more than that. They were tools that my mom used to instill important values in me - values that have served me well throughout my life. And beyond that, they were stories that connected me to a wider world of history, culture, and creativity.

  1. The classics taught me the importance of hard work.
  2. The classics taught me the power of creativity.
  3. The classics taught me the value of friendship.
