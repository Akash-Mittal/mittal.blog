In Seed Time Learn, In Harvest Teach, In Winter Enjoy:



An Inspirational Article on the Wisdom of William Blake

Let me start this article with a heartwarming story about a young teacher from a small town in the Philippines.

She was fresh out of college, idealistic, and full of energy. She was assigned to a far-flung barrio where most of the students were indigenous and spoke a different language. She struggled to communicate with them, but she never gave up. She learned their language, their culture, and their struggles. She taught them with passion, using songs, games, and stories to make the lessons come alive. She inspired them to dream big, to learn more, and to help their community. After two years, her students topped the district and provincial exams. They were the pride of their parents and teachers. And the young teacher, despite the challenges, felt fulfilled and happy.

- Anonymous

Why do I share this story? Because I believe it captures the essence of what William Blake meant when he wrote the famous quotation, "In seed time learn, in harvest teach, in winter enjoy." To me, this means that life is a cycle of learning, teaching, and enjoying, and that we should embrace each stage with grace, wisdom, and joy.

Who is William Blake?

For those who are not familiar with William Blake, let me give you a brief background.

  • William Blake (1757-1827) was an English poet, painter, and printmaker.
  • He is considered a seminal figure in the history of the Romantic Age.
  • Some of his famous works include "Songs of Innocence and of Experience", "The Marriage of Heaven and Hell", and "The Tyger".
  • He was known for his mystical and visionary poetry that explored themes of nature, spirituality, and social injustice.

What does "In seed time learn, in harvest teach, in winter enjoy" mean?

Now, let's dive deeper into the meaning of the quotation itself. What can we learn from it?

  • In seed time learn. This phrase refers to the time when seeds are planted, or when we start something new. It's a time of preparation, of gathering knowledge and skills, of setting goals and intentions. It's also a time of curiosity, of asking questions, of exploring possibilities. Learning never stops, but it's important to lay a strong foundation at the beginning.
  • In harvest teach. This phrase refers to the time when the fruits of our labor are ready to be harvested, or when we achieve our goals. It's a time of celebration, of gratitude, of sharing. It's also a time of teaching, of passing on what we have learned to others. Teaching is not only a way of giving back, but also a way of reinforcing our own learning. When we teach, we deepen our understanding and connect with others.
  • In winter enjoy. This phrase refers to the time when nature rests, or when we take a break from our work. It's a time of reflection, of relaxation, of rejuvenation. It's also a time of enjoyment, of savoring the fruits of our labor, of appreciating the beauty around us. Enjoyment is not a luxury, but a necessity. Without it, life becomes dull and meaningless.

How can we apply this wisdom in our lives?

Now that we have understood the meaning of the quotation, let's explore some practical tips on how we can apply it in our lives.

  1. Learn with curiosity and humility. Whether we are starting a new job, a new hobby, or a new relationship, it's important to approach it with an open mind and a willingness to learn. We should not be afraid to ask for help, to make mistakes, or to try new things. Learning is a lifelong process, and we should embrace it with curiosity and humility.
  2. Teach with generosity and gratitude. When we have achieved something, whether big or small, we should not keep it to ourselves. We should share it with others, especially those who are struggling or who could benefit from it. Teaching is not only about imparting knowledge, but also about inspiring, motivating, and empowering others. When we teach, we also learn, and we should be grateful for the opportunity to give back.
  3. Enjoy with mindfulness and balance. Finally, when we have worked hard and achieved our goals, we should not forget to enjoy the fruits of our labor. We should take time to relax, to reflect, and to appreciate what we have. However, we should also be mindful of our limits, and avoid excess or addiction. Enjoyment should be balanced with self-care, responsibility, and social awareness.
