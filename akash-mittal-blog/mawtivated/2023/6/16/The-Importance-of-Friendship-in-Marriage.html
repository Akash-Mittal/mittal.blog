The Importance of Friendship in Marriage



Sam and Jessica had a love story straight out of a fairy tale. They met in college and immediately fell in love. After graduation, they got married and began their happily ever after. At least that's what they thought. A year into their marriage, they found themselves arguing and unhappy. It wasn't because they didn't love each other; it was because they had forgotten to be friends.

Their busy lives, work schedules, and different interests led them to drift apart. They stopped communicating, stopped doing things together, and stopped encouraging each other. They were together but felt alone because they had forgotten to cultivate their friendship.

This is a common problem that many couples face. A strong friendship is the foundation of a healthy marriage. Friedrich Nietzsche once said, "It is not a lack of love, but a lack of friendship that makes unhappy marriages." In this article, we'll explore the importance of friendship in marriage and how to nurture this vital aspect of your relationship.

Author - Friedrich Nietzsche

Friedrich Nietzsche was a German philosopher, cultural critic, and poet who lived in the 19th century. He is known for his writings on morality, religion, and the human condition. Nietzsche had a unique perspective on life and love, and his quote on the importance of friendship in marriage highlights the essence of a healthy relationship.

Examples of the Importance of Friendship in Marriage

Friendship is the glue that holds a marriage together. It is the foundation that helps couples weather the storms of life. Here are a few examples of how friendship can make a difference in a marriage:

  • Support: Friends support each other through difficult times, and the same is true in a marriage. A spouse who is also a friend can provide emotional, physical, and financial support when needed.
  • Communication: Friendship thrives on open and honest communication. A married couple who are also friends are more likely to communicate well and resolve any conflicts that arise in a healthy manner.
  • Fun: A strong friendship in marriage means having fun and enjoying each other's company. Couples who prioritize their friendship make time for each other and do things together that bring them joy.
  • Intimacy: A deep friendship in marriage leads to greater intimacy. Emotional intimacy is a key aspect of a healthy relationship and is built on trust, vulnerability, and shared experiences.

Practical Tips to Nurture Friendship in Marriage

Now that we understand the importance of friendship in marriage let's explore some practical tips to help you cultivate and strengthen your relationship:

  1. Make time for each other: Schedule regular date nights, take up a hobby together, or plan a holiday. Whatever you choose to do, make sure it's something you both enjoy and that gives you an opportunity to connect.
  2. Communicate effectively: Practice active listening, be respectful, and avoid criticism and defensiveness. Work together to resolve conflicts and find win-win solutions that meet both your needs.
  3. Keep learning about each other: Ask questions, show interest, and be curious about your partner's dreams, fears, and aspirations. Share your own as well, and be vulnerable and honest.
  4. Show gratitude: Express appreciation and gratitude for the small things your partner does. This reinforces positive behaviors and creates a sense of goodwill in your relationship.
  5. Laugh together: Create opportunities to laugh and have fun together. Whether it's watching a comedy, going to a gig, or playing a game, engaging in activities that make you laugh creates a positive atmosphere and strengthens your bond.

Conclusion

To create a happy, healthy, and long-lasting marriage, we need to prioritize our friendship. A strong friendship in marriage provides us with the support, communication, fun, and intimacy we need to weather the ups and downs of life. Cultivate your friendship with your spouse by making time for each other, communicating effectively, learning about each other, showing gratitude, and laughing together. By doing so, you'll create a strong foundation for a lifetime of love.
