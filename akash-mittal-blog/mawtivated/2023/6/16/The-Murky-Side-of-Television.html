The Murky Side of Television



An Inspiring Story

Janice was a happy-go-lucky teenager who loved to watch TV all day long. She was thrilled by the action, drama, and suspense. But one day, while watching a crime drama on TV, she saw something that she didn't expect - a murder. The scene was so realistic that she flinched and turned her head away. She realized that television had brought murder into the home, and she wasn't sure if it belonged there.

Author's Background

Alfred Hitchcock, the legendary filmmaker, once said, "Television has brought back murder into the home - where it belongs." Hitchcock was known for his suspenseful films about murder and mystery, and he saw television as a tool to bring that same suspense into people's homes. But his quote also touches on a deeper issue - the impact of television on our society.

and Case Studies

According to a study conducted by the University of Michigan, children who watch more television are more likely to engage in aggressive behavior. This is because they are exposed to violent imagery on a regular basis, and they begin to see it as normal. The study also found that children who watched educational programs had better academic performance than those who watched violent programs.

Another study conducted by the American Psychological Association found that exposure to violent media can lead to desensitization to real-life violence. This means that people who watch violent TV shows or movies are less likely to be affected by real acts of violence, and are therefore less likely to take action to stop it.

Examples of the Impact of Television

  • The rise of true crime shows, which often glorify murderers and their crimes, has led to a phenomenon called "murder fandom". This is when people become obsessed with murderers and their crimes, and may even write letters or send gifts to them in prison.
  • The popularity of reality TV shows has led to an increase in bullying and aggressive behavior, as contestants compete for fame and fortune.
  • The portrayal of women on TV often reinforces harmful gender stereotypes, and can have a negative impact on young girls.

The Murky Side of Television

While television can be a source of entertainment and education, it also has a darker side. Here are three key points to consider:

  1. Exposure to violence on TV can lead to aggressive behavior and desensitization to real-life violence.
  2. The portrayal of certain groups, such as women or people of color, can reinforce harmful stereotypes.
  3. Watching too much television can lead to a sedentary lifestyle, which is harmful to physical and mental health.

Practical Tips

  • Limit screen time for children and adults, and encourage other activities such as reading or outdoor play.
  • Choose TV shows and movies carefully, and avoid those that contain excessive violence or harmful stereotypes. Look for educational programming or shows that promote positive values.
  • Take breaks during long TV marathons to stretch and move around. Exercise regularly, even if it's just a short walk every day.
