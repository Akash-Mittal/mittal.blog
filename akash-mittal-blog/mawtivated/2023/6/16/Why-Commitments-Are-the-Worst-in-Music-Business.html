Why Commitments Are the Worst in Music Business



Sometimes the road to success is not always easy. You need to put in the hard work, dedication, and persistence. But what about commitments? How do they affect the music industry, and why are they considered the worst thing to have in this field? I'll tell you a story.

A few years back, I met with a friend who happened to have a music production company. She was always busy with meetings, events, recording sessions, and many more activities. One day, I accompanied her to a studio session with an upcoming triple platinum artist who happened not to show up for the recording. My friend was disappointed, annoyed and couldn't get her day's plans in order.

It was then that I learned how commitments affect the music industry. Kesha expresses it best when she says,

"The truth is, when somebody says, 'We want you,' they're really saying, 'We want the version of you that we are creating for our own self-interest.' "

The music industry can make promises that it can't keep, and this can lead to frustrations and disappointments. In this article, we shall look at why commitments are not welcome and how much harm they can cause in the music industry.

Neil Young – Commitment Suspicions

Neil Young, a legendary musician, suffered from commitment problems in the music industry. He explains how he saw himself changing every time he signed a new contract to a record label. He says, "The record company has its pledges and obligations to stockholders, production requirements, and investment returns. That means everybody must do what he or she is told. I became addicted to ideas, and they were very powerful." (NY Archives).

Neil Young was not the only artist who suffered from commitments. Other musicians have expressed their disappointments in this field, such as Prince, who changed his identity to a symbol to get away from his contract with his record label.

The Problem with Commitments in the Music Industry

  • False Promises - Record label companies can fail to keep promises made to artists, leading to disappointments and frustrations.
  • Restrictions – Commitments can limit the artist's creativity and affect the final output of their work.
  • Dominance - Record companies want to produce artists to suit their self-interest and not necessarily the artists'.

Examples of Commitments Gone Awry

Many artists have been affected by commitments in the music industry. These are some examples.

  • TLC - Left bankrupt due to their contract signed with their record label.
  • Prince - Changed his identity to get out of his contract.
  • Beyoncé - Her father, Matthew Knowles, was both her manager and label executive, which later led to their fallout as he was enforcing self-interest policies.

The Solution?

The best solution is not to have commitments, but since this is not possible, some steps can be taken to protect your interests.

  • Read and understand your contract thoroughly, or have an expert review it.
  • Be aware of all legal aspects as much as possible.
  • Ensure that your contract has all clauses, including termination clauses, and payment clauses.

Conclusion

Commitments are a significant problem in the music industry, with artists having to bear the brunt of it all. From restrictions to false promises, commitments are not always the best option. Neil Young is a good example of how commitments can change a musician's identity. Unfortunately, contracts are a necessary evil, and the best way is only to read, understand, and be vigilant about them. With proper legal representation and careful consideration, artists will be able to negotiate contracts that protect their interests and avoid commitments gone wrong.
