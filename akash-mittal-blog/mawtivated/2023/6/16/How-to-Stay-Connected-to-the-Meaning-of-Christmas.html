How to Stay Connected to the Meaning of Christmas



Christmas is a special time of the year that brings together family, friends, and loved ones. But amidst the hustle and bustle of the holidays, it can be easy to lose sight of the true meaning of Christmas. This article offers practical tips on how to stay connected to the meaning of Christmas and make it a meaningful celebration.

An Inspiring Story

Caroline Kennedy, the daughter of former US President John F. Kennedy, once shared a story about her family's Christmas tradition. Every year, her father would take his children to the National Christmas Tree lighting ceremony in Washington, DC. As they admired the twinkling lights and listened to the carols, he would remind them of the importance of giving, love, and kindness. The family would then go home and decorate their own Christmas tree, showcasing the homemade ornaments and cherished memories of Christmases past. This tradition helped Caroline and her siblings stay connected to the meaning of Christmas and carry on their family's values.

Practical Tips

  • Make time for reflection: Set aside a few minutes each day to ponder on the true meaning of Christmas. You can do this by reading a Bible passage, meditating, or simply enjoying the natural beauty around you.
  • Engage in acts of kindness: Christmas is the perfect time to spread love and goodwill. Consider volunteering at a local charity, sending a thoughtful message to a friend, or donating to a worthy cause.
  • Create meaningful traditions: Find ways to infuse your family's holiday celebrations with joy and purpose. This can include cooking a special meal, making handmade decorations, or sharing stories and memories.
  • Focus on gratitude: Take a moment to appreciate the blessings in your life, whether big or small. This can help cultivate a spirit of thankfulness and contentment, which are key aspects of the Christmas message.
  • Simplify your schedule: Avoid overscheduling yourself and focus on the activities that truly matter to you and your loved ones. This can reduce stress and help you enjoy the holiday season more fully.

Examples

One example of a meaningful Christmas tradition is the Mexican custom of posadas. These celebrations reenact Joseph and Mary's search for a place to stay in Bethlehem, and involve a procession, singing, and the sharing of treats. This tradition not only honors the religious aspect of Christmas but also fosters community and togetherness.

Another example is the Icelandic tradition of giving books on Christmas Eve. This practice, known as Jolabokaflod, encourages people to cozy up with a good book and enjoy a quiet night in with their loved ones. This simple but meaningful gesture promotes literacy and family bonding.

"Christmas is not a time nor a season, but a state of mind. To cherish peace and goodwill, to be plenteous in mercy, is to have the real spirit of Christmas." - Calvin Coolidge

Quotes like this remind us that the true spirit of Christmas is not about material things or extravagant gifts, but about embodying values such as peace, generosity, and compassion.

Conclusion

To stay connected to the meaning of Christmas, we can:

  1. Reflect on the true meaning of Christmas.
  2. Engage in acts of kindness and spread love and goodwill.
  3. Create meaningful traditions that honor your family's values.

By doing these things, we can make Christmas a time of joy, connection, and purpose.
