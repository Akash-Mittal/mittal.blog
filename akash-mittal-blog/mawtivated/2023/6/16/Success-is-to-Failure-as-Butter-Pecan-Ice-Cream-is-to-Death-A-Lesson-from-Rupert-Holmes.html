Success is to Failure as Butter Pecan Ice Cream is to Death



A Lesson from Rupert Holmes

An Inspiring Story

Years ago, Rupert Holmes, the famous playwright and novelist, was struggling financially. He had written several plays that had been produced on Broadway, but they hadn't been commercial successes. He was feeling down on his luck when he got a call from his agent. It was bad news. Another of his plays had just flopped, and he was now $50,000 in debt.

But instead of giving up, Rupert decided to use his failure as a reason to create something new. He wrote a song called "Escape," which became a worldwide hit and won him multiple awards. Today, it's still one of the most popular songs of all time, and has been covered by dozens of artists.

What inspired Rupert to keep going was the realization that failure was simply part of the journey to success. As he put it, "Having in my life been bitten by the jaws of both victory and defeat, I must rush to add that success is to failure as butter pecan ice cream is to death."

Success and Failure

We live in a world where success is celebrated and failure is often seen as something to be ashamed of. But the truth is that success and failure are two sides of the same coin. Without one, the other doesn't exist. As Rupert Holmes reminds us, success is to failure as butter pecan ice cream is to death.

But why is failure so important?

  • Failure teaches us valuable lessons. When we fail, we learn what doesn't work, which can help us improve our approach next time.
  • Failure builds resilience. When we experience setbacks and challenges, we learn to pick ourselves up and keep going.
  • Failure fosters innovation. When something doesn't work, we're forced to come up with new ideas and solutions.

These are just a few of the reasons why failure is not something to be afraid of. In fact, embracing failure can be a key component of success.

"Success is not final, failure is not fatal: it is the courage to continue that counts." - Winston Churchill

Practical Tips

If you're struggling with the fear of failure, here are a few practical tips:

  • Reframe failure as a learning opportunity. Instead of seeing failure as a negative, try to learn from your mistakes and use them as an opportunity to grow.
  • Celebrate small successes. It's important to celebrate every step of the journey, even the small ones. This can help you stay motivated and focused on the bigger picture.
  • Surround yourself with positive people. When we're surrounded by people who believe in us and encourage us, it can be easier to push past our fears and take risks.

Remember, success is not about never failing. It's about getting back up when we do.
