From Degree to Career: My Experience Studying Political Science



When I was in high school, I was always interested in current events and politics. I loved to engage in debates with my friends and family, and I knew that I wanted to pursue a career in this field. When it was time for me to choose a college major, I knew that I wanted to study political science. Little did I know that it would shape my future in ways I never could have imagined.

An Inspiring Story

One day, I met a woman named Sara Paretsky. She was a bestselling author and her books were being adapted into television shows and movies. However, what inspired me about her was not just her success as an author. It was the fact that she had studied political science, just like me.

She shared with me how her degree had helped her develop critical thinking skills and understand the ways in which power is distributed in society. This had not only informed her writing, but also informed her activism work. She used her platform as a writer to speak out about social issues and advocate for change.

Listening to her story made me realize that my degree in political science could be more than just a piece of paper. It could be a tool for creating positive change in society.

My Background

I went to college at the University of Kansas, where I got a degree in political science. During my time there, I took classes on American politics, international relations, public policy, and political theory. I learned about different political systems and ideologies, and how they shape the world we live in.

But what I found most valuable were the critical thinking skills I developed. I learned how to analyze and evaluate arguments, how to conduct research and gather data, and how to communicate my ideas in a clear and concise manner.

and Case Studies

While studying political science, I had the opportunity to participate in various internships and volunteer work. One of my most memorable experiences was working on a local election campaign. I helped with canvassing, phone banking, and organizing events. It was amazing to see how much work went into getting a candidate elected.

I also took a class on public policy, which involved working on a group project where we had to research and write a policy proposal. It was a challenging experience, but it taught me how to work effectively in a team and apply my knowledge in a real-world setting.

Practical Tips

If you're considering studying political science, here are some practical tips:

  • Take a variety of classes to get a broad understanding of the field.
  • Get involved in extracurricular activities, like internships and volunteer work, to gain practical experience.
  • Develop your critical thinking and analytical skills through class projects and research papers.
  • Network with professors and professionals in the field to learn about job opportunities and career paths.

Conclusion

In conclusion, studying political science can open up a world of opportunities. It can provide you with valuable skills and knowledge that can be applied in a variety of fields, from law to non-profit work to journalism. But most importantly, it can be a tool for creating positive change in society.

So if you're passionate about politics and want to make a difference in the world, consider studying political science. Who knows where it may take you.
