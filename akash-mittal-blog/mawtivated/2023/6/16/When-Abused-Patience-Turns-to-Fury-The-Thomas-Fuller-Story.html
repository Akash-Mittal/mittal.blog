When Abused Patience Turns to Fury - The Thomas Fuller Story



Have you ever felt like you just cannot wait anymore? Frustrated by the lack of progress or results, or just tired of the same old thing happening again and again? You are not alone. Patience is a virtue, but even virtues have limits. As the famous quote goes, "Abused patience turns to fury."

One inspiring story that illustrates this idea is that of Thomas Fuller. An English clergyman and historian, Fuller lived during the 17th century and is known for his wise sayings and poetic writing. He was also known for his patience, as he was often asked to mediate disputes and bring peace to troubled situations. However, there came a time when his patience was tested to the extreme.

During the English Civil War, Fuller's hometown of Warminster was occupied by the parliamentarian army. As a loyal royalist, Fuller did not support their cause and refused to swear allegiance to them. This led to him being imprisoned and put on trial for treason. The trial was delayed for several months, during which time Fuller was held in a small, dark cell with just enough food and water to keep him alive.

Initially, Fuller remained calm and patient, believing that the truth will prevail and he would be acquitted. However, as the months went by and he saw no progress or hope for his release, he became increasingly frustrated and angry. He started to doubt his faith and question whether his suffering was worth it. In his diary, he wrote:

"I am heartily weary of my thoughts, my meat, and my cell. The earth seems to me a steril desert, life is vanity and God forgotten. Oh, pray for me, pray for me!"

Fuller's patience had indeed been abused, and he was on the brink of giving up. However, just when he thought all hope was lost, a group of royalist soldiers managed to break into the prison and free him. Fuller was overjoyed and wrote:

"After a long night, I see the day, and thank God for my deliverance."

Fuller's story shows us that patience is not an infinite resource, and that there comes a point where it can turn into frustration, anger, and even despair. However, it also shows us that there is hope, and that sometimes, when we least expect it, things can turn around for the better.

and Case Studies

If you are still skeptical about the idea that abused patience can lead to fury, let me share with you some personal anecdotes and case studies.

Anecdote #1: The Traffic Jam

Last week, I was stuck in traffic on my way to an important meeting. I had left home early, but there was a massive accident on the highway that caused a major delay. At first, I was patient and tried to remain calm. I listened to some music, took deep breaths, and reminded myself that there was nothing I could do to change the situation. However, after an hour of inching forward at a snail's pace, I started to lose my cool. I honked my horn, shouted at other drivers, and even considered abandoning my car and running to my destination. By the time I arrived, I was sweaty, angry, and flustered. Needless to say, the meeting did not go well.

Anecdote #2: The Job Search

My friend Elizabeth has been searching for a job for the past six months. She is highly qualified and has a great resume, but she keeps getting rejected or ignored by potential employers. At first, she was patient and optimistic. She believed that her hard work would pay off, and that she would find the right job soon enough. However, as the months went by and she saw her savings dwindle, her confidence started to wane. She felt frustrated, bitter, and betrayed by the job market. She started to criticize herself and wonder if there was something wrong with her. By the time she landed an interview for a promising position, she was so anxious and negative that she bombed it.

Examples of Abused Patience

These anecdotes may seem trivial, but they are just two examples of the many ways in which patience can be abused. Here are some other situations that can test our patience to the extreme:

  • Dealing with bureaucracy and red tape
  • Waiting in long lines or for hours/days at the DMV, doctor's office, or airport
  • Working with difficult or incompetent colleagues, bosses, or clients
  • Living with chronic pain, illness, or disability
  • Trying to break a bad habit or addiction
  • Parenting a challenging child or teenager
  • Being in a toxic or abusive relationship
  • Surviving a natural disaster, war, or pandemic

These situations can take a toll on our mental and physical health, and may even lead to depression, anxiety, or other serious issues if left unchecked.

How to Avoid Abusing Your Patience

So, what can we do to avoid falling into the trap of abused patience? Here are some practical tips:

  • Set realistic expectations: If you know that a situation will take a long time to resolve or change, don't expect it to happen overnight. Be patient, but also be realistic.
  • Take breaks: If you feel like you are reaching your limit, take a break. Go for a walk, listen to some music, or do something enjoyable to recharge your batteries.
  • Ask for help: Sometimes, we cannot handle a situation on our own and need to ask for help. Whether it's from a friend, family member, or professional, don't be afraid to reach out.
  • Practice self-care: Taking care of yourself is key to having a reserve of patience. Make sure you eat well, sleep well, and exercise regularly. Don't neglect your mental health either.
  • Find meaning: Having a sense of purpose or meaning in your life can help you stay patient in difficult times. Whether it's through your work, hobbies, spirituality, or relationships, make sure you have something that gives you joy and fulfillment.

Conclusion

In conclusion, patience is a precious virtue, but it has its limits. When abused, patience can turn into fury, frustration, and despair. However, we should not give up hope, as there is always a chance for things to turn around. By following practical tips like setting realistic expectations, taking breaks, asking for help, practicing self-care, and finding meaning, we can avoid abusing our patience and maintain our mental and physical health.

Remember, as Thomas Fuller wrote:

"Patience is bitter, but its fruit is sweet."
