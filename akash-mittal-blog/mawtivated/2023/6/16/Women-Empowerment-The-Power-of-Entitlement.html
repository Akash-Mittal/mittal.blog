Women Empowerment: The Power of Entitlement



It was a cold winter evening. Linda Vester, an experienced journalist, was getting ready for her evening news bulletin. As usual, she was confident and fully prepared. However, what she did not expect was the shocking incident that was about to change her life forever.

As Linda started her bulletin, she noticed that the male anchor was getting more airtime than her. She decided to speak up during the break, but the male producers dismissed her concerns. It then hit her that she was being treated unfairly because of her gender.

However, Linda did not let this incident bring her down. Instead, she used the experience to fuel her passion for women empowerment. She realized that women need to feel entitled to positions of authority, just like men do. That realization led her to a lifetime commitment to empowering women.

According to Linda, women's entitlement is not a negative trait, but a necessary one. It is the drive that leads women to believe that they have a natural place in leadership, just as men do. This sense of entitlement gives women the mental and emotional confidence to lead and make a positive impact in the world.

Examples of Women Entitlement

  • Malala Yousafzai's unwavering belief in girls' right to education, despite the threats and violence she faced
  • Greta Thunberg's fierce advocacy for climate action and holding world leaders accountable for their actions
  • Ruth Bader Ginsburg's lifelong dedication to gender equality and fighting for women's rights in the US Supreme Court
"Women belong in all places where decisions are being made... It shouldn't be that women are the exception." - Ruth Bader Ginsburg

These women believed that they had a rightful place in leadership and were entitled to advocate for their causes. Their sense of entitlement motivated them to pursue their goals and make a positive impact.

Practical Tips to Feel Entitled

  1. Believe in Yourself: Trust in your abilities and know that you deserve to be in leadership positions
  2. Speak Up: Do not be afraid to express your opinions and advocate for yourself and others
  3. Support Other Women: Recognize the achievements of other women and lift them up. Women supporting women leads to a more empowered and inclusive community

Conclusion

  1. Women deserve to have the same opportunities and rights as men do
  2. Women's sense of entitlement is not a negative trait but a necessary one to lead and make a positive impact in the world
  3. By believing in themselves and supporting other women, women can feel entitled and empower themselves and others
