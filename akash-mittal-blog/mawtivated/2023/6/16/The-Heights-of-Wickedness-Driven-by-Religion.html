The Heights of Wickedness Driven by Religion



As humans, we are often driven by our beliefs and convictions. Religion is one such belief that has been around for centuries. It has helped shape societies and civilizations around the world. However, when religion is taken to the extremes, the consequences can be deadly. Lucretius, a Roman philosopher, reflects on this in his famous quote: "Such are the heights of wickedness to which men are driven by religion."

Inspiring Story

We have all heard stories of individuals who have used religion for personal gain or to justify heinous acts. However, there are also stories of individuals who have used religion for good. One such story is that of Abdul Sattar Edhi, a Pakistani humanitarian. Edhi was a devout Muslim who lived a simple life and dedicated his entire life to helping others.

Edhi started his humanitarian work in 1951, when he opened a free dispensary in Karachi. His work expanded over the years and he went on to establish a network of clinics, orphanages, shelters, and ambulance services across Pakistan. Edhi was a firm believer that every human being deserves respect and dignity, regardless of their religion.

He lived by this belief and dedicated himself to providing care and support to those in need. Edhi passed away in 2016 but his legacy lives on. He was not driven by religion to commit wicked acts but instead used his religious beliefs as a source of inspiration to make the world a better place.

Author - Lucretius Story or Background

Lucretius was a Roman poet and philosopher who lived in the first century BC. He is best known for his epic poem, "De Rerum Natura," which explores the philosophy of Epicureanism. Lucretius was a firm believer in the importance of reason and the pursuit of knowledge.

He was skeptical of religion and believed that it was a source of fear that kept people in a state of ignorance. Lucretius believed that religion had the potential to lead individuals to commit wicked acts because it was based on superstition and irrational beliefs.

Examples of Wickedness Driven by Religion

  • The Crusades - The Crusades were a series of religious wars fought between Christians and Muslims in the Middle Ages. These wars were fueled by religious beliefs and resulted in the deaths of millions of people.
  • The Spanish Inquisition - The Spanish Inquisition was a tribunal established in the 15th century to root out heretics and non-believers. It led to the torture and execution of thousands of people.
  • The Salem Witch Trials - The Salem Witch Trials were a series of hearings and prosecutions of people accused of witchcraft in colonial Massachusetts. These trials were driven by religious beliefs and resulted in the execution of 20 people.

Conclusion

Religion has the potential to inspire individuals to do great things, but it can also lead to wickedness. When taken to extremes, religion can be used to justify heinous acts and perpetuate superstition and ignorance. To prevent this from happening, we must approach religion with a critical and rational mind.

Practical Tips:

  1. Question religious beliefs and seek knowledge.
  2. Respect the beliefs of others but do not let them infringe on your own beliefs and values.
  3. Avoid using religion to justify acts of violence or discrimination.
