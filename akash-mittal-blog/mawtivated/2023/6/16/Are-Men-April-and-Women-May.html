Are Men April and Women May?



William Shakespeare once said, "Men are April when they woo, December when they wed. Maids are May when they are maids, but the sky changes when they are wives." This quote has been analyzed and dissected for centuries, but what does it truly mean? Let's take a closer look.

The Personality of Men and Women

Shakespeare suggests that men and women have different personalities when they are in the wooing stage and when they are married. Men are like the month of April, which is associated with new beginnings, growth, and vitality. When men are wooing, they are full of excitement and energy, eager to impress and win over their intended partners.

However, when men are married, they behave more like the month of December, which is associated with the end of the year and a time of passage. Like December, men become more reflective and contemplative. They may feel a sense of loss of their youthful vigor or freedom.

On the other hand, Shakespeare compares women to the month of May when they are maids. May is a time of beauty, youth, and promise. Women exhibit these same qualities when they are young and unmarried. However, once they become wives, "the sky changes" and they take on a different persona.

The Changes in Married Life

When women get married, their lives change. They take on new roles and responsibilities as wives and mothers. They may sacrifice their own dreams and aspirations for the good of their families. Many women face societal pressures to conform to traditional gender roles, which can be limiting and frustrating.

Shakespeare's quote suggests that men and women have different experiences in marriage. Men may feel a sense of loss of their freedom or independence, while women may feel pressured to conform to societal norms. However, these experiences are not universal and can vary depending on the individual.

Examples of Shakespeare's Quote

  • Prince Harry and Meghan Markle: The couple's courtship was full of excitement and anticipation, which is similar to the month of April. However, after their wedding, they stepped back from their royal duties, which suggests a more contemplative and reflective phase, similar to December.
  • Tom and Daisy Buchanan from "The Great Gatsby": Tom's behavior towards Daisy changes after they get married. He becomes more controlling and domineering, which is similar to the December persona. Daisy, on the other hand, compromises her own happiness for the sake of her husband and family, which is similar to the changes women experience after getting married.

Conclusion

William Shakespeare's quote, "Men are April when they woo, December when they wed. Maids are May when they are maids, but the sky changes when they are wives," suggests that men and women have different personalities when they are in the wooing stage and when they are married. However, these experiences are not universal and can vary depending on the individual.

Here are three key takeaways to keep in mind:

  1. Men and women have different experiences in marriage and may take on different personas.
  2. The societal pressures of traditional gender roles can be limiting and frustrating for women.
  3. Individual experiences may vary and are not determined by gender.
