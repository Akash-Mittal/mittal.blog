Dependence on Government: Modern Democrat Party's Definition of Success



Inspiring Story

In a small town in rural America, a young woman named Emily grew up in a family struggling to make ends meet. Her parents worked long hours, but could barely afford to provide basic necessities for their family. Emily was determined to break the cycle of poverty and attended college, where she worked multiple jobs to support herself. She eventually graduated with honors and secured a well-paying job in the private sector. However, instead of becoming self-sufficient, Emily turned to the government for assistance. She believed that she had a right to certain benefits and felt that the government owed her something for her struggles. This dependence on the government was the beginning of a slippery slope that would ultimately limit her potential and restrict her freedom.

Definition of Success for the Modern Democrat Party

The modern Democrat party has made it their objective to create as many people dependent on the government as possible. They believe that providing government assistance is the solution to many of society's problems, including poverty and inequality. However, this approach has resulted in a growing number of individuals who rely on government assistance for basic needs. This dependence can limit their potential and restrict their freedom.

Examples of Government Dependence

  • Individuals who receive welfare benefits and choose not to work or seek employment.
  • People who rely on government-funded healthcare programs and avoid making lifestyle changes that could improve their overall health.
  • Businesses that receive government subsidies and lose their competitive edge due to a lack of innovation and efficiency.
  • College students who take out large amounts of student loans without considering the potential financial burden they may face in the future.

The Harmful Effects of Dependence on Government

There are several harmful effects of dependence on the government, including:

  • Limiting potential: When individuals become dependent on the government for basic needs, they may not work as hard or pursue their full potential. This can limit their personal and professional growth.
  • Restricting freedom: When the government has control over individuals' basic needs, it can restrict their choices and limit their freedom.
  • Straining the economy: When too many individuals and businesses rely on government assistance, it can strain the economy and lead to increased taxes and debt.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the modern Democrat party's definition of success is creating as many people dependent on the government as possible. However, this approach can limit individuals' potential, restrict their freedom, and strain the economy. To avoid dependence on the government, individuals should prioritize self-sufficiency and responsibility. This can be achieved through education, hard work, and making informed decisions about finances and lifestyle choices.

  1. Prioritize self-sufficiency and responsibility.
  2. Foster a culture of hard work and education.
  3. Make informed decisions about finances and lifestyle choices.
