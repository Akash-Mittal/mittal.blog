The Shameful Side of Sexual Experiences



An article by Sue Johanson

Let me tell you a story.

Once, a young woman named Susan had her first sexual experience with her boyfriend. It was consensual, enjoyable and she felt on top of the world afterwards.

But then, she started to feel guilty. Her upbringing and society's views on sex made her feel ashamed and embarrassed. She couldn't talk to anyone about it and felt abnormal for enjoying something that was supposed to be dirty and wrong.

You see, this is the sad truth about sexual experiences. Even the most glorious and fulfilling ones can make us feel guilty, ashamed, embarrassed and abnormal. And this is not okay.

The Shame and Stigma of Sexual Pleasure

The reason for this shame and stigma surrounding sexual pleasure is deeply rooted in societal and cultural norms. For centuries, sex has been portrayed as a sin, a taboo, and something that should not be talked about in public. Women, in particular, have been shamed for enjoying sex, and their sexuality has been viewed as something to be controlled and tamed.

"Virginity is like a balloon: one prick and it's gone forever." - Sue Johanson

But this is a harmful and outdated narrative that needs to change. Sexual pleasure is a natural and healthy part of human life, and there should be no shame or stigma attached to it. Everyone should be able to explore their sexuality and enjoy sex without feeling guilty or ashamed.

Breaking the Taboo: Tips for Overcoming Sexual Shame

Breaking down centuries of sexual shame and stigma is not an easy task, but it can be done. Here are some tips to help you overcome any sexual shame or guilt you may be feeling:

  • Talk to someone you trust. Find someone you feel comfortable talking to about sex, whether it's a friend, a therapist or a sexual health educator like me. Having someone to discuss your feelings with can help you realize that you're not abnormal, and that many others feel the same way.
  • Get educated about sex. Learn more about sex, sexual health and pleasure. Education can help you understand your body and your sexual preferences, which can help reduce feelings of shame or guilt.
  • Challenge your beliefs about sex. Take a critical look at your beliefs about sex and try to understand where they come from. Are they rooted in outdated cultural norms or personal experiences? If so, try to shift your perspective and embrace a more positive and healthy view of sexuality.

The Power of Embracing Sexual Pleasure

Embracing and enjoying your sexual pleasure is a powerful act that can have a positive impact on your overall well-being. Studies have shown that sexual pleasure can reduce stress, improve mood, and even boost the immune system. So why deny yourself this natural and healthy experience?

By breaking down the taboo surrounding sexual pleasure and embracing it as a natural and healthy part of life, we can create a more sex-positive society that allows everyone to explore and enjoy their sexuality without shame or stigma.
