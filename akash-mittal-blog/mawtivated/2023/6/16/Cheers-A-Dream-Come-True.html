Cheers: A Dream Come True



George Wendt's story of love and passion for 'Cheers'

When Dreams Become Reality

Have you ever had a dream that felt so real, you woke up feeling disappointed that it wasn't? I have, but there's one dream that's come true for me - working on 'Cheers'. For those who don't know, Cheers was a popular American television show that aired for eleven seasons. It revolves around a group of friends who regularly meet up at a Boston bar called "Cheers". I played the lovable Norm Peterson who quickly turned into one of the most iconic and beloved characters of the show.

It was a dream come true for me to be able to work on such a great show. Every day felt like being with family and friends. Even during difficult times, we managed to laugh and enjoy the ride. The camaraderie between us on set was just like that of the characters on the show.

My Love for 'Cheers'

Cheers was not only a great work experience for me, but it was where I met my wife, Bernadette. She guest-starred on the show in the fourth season as a runaway bride. I was struck by her beauty and her performance, so I took it upon myself to introduce myself to her.

"I always tell people she was the most beautiful woman I ever saw in my life, and that's really a true statement."

We hit it off immediately and got married two years after meeting on set. My character on the show even got married in the seventh season in an episode that was shot on location in Italy.

'Cheers' was also instrumental in shaping my acting career. Before Cheers, I had already made several appearances on television shows, movies, and stage productions. But it was playing Norm on 'Cheers' that took my career to the next level.

The Legacy of 'Cheers'

'Cheers' started airing in 1982 and ended in 1993, but its impact and legacy live on. For instance, the bar that inspired the show's setting, Bull & Finch, became a tourist attraction, and the name was changed to Cheers Beacon Hill. The show also popularized the idea of a neighbourhood bar where everyone knows your name and fostered the idea of community building.

  • Cheers was the birthplace of some of the most famous catchphrases in popular culture, such as "Norm!" and "What's shaking, bacon?"
  • The show won several awards, including Primetime Emmy Awards for Outstanding Comedy Series, and Golden Globe Awards for Best Television Series - Musical or Comedy.
  • Melville's novel, Moby-Dick inspired 'Cheers' creators to call the bar "Moby Dick's."

The show became a cultural phenomenon that brought together people of all ages and backgrounds. It was a showcase of talented actors, writers, and producers who delivered a compelling storyline and iconic characters that we still talk about years later. That's the legacy of Cheers.

Conclusion

  1. Cheers was more than just a television show; it was a family. A group of talented and passionate individuals who brought together entertainment and art, and created a cultural phenomenon to be remembered for years to come.
  2. 'Cheers' is a reminder of what can happen when you take a chance and pursue your passion. Anything is possible if you work hard and surround yourself with like-minded people.
  3. The love and passion I have for 'Cheers' will always be a part of my story. It's a chapter written in the history of television that I am proud to have been a part of.
