From Quotation: If I have a style, I am not aware of it.



Have you ever wondered what your personal style is? The way you dress, the way you speak, the way you write - all of these things come together to create a unique expression of who you are. But what if you're not sure what that expression is? What if you feel like you don't have a style?

Enter Michael Graves, renowned architect and product designer. Graves once said, "If I have a style, I am not aware of it." This statement may seem contradictory - how could someone who has created so many iconic designs not have a style? But when you look deeper, you'll find that Graves' approach to design holds a valuable lesson for anyone struggling to define their own personal style.

The Background of Michael Graves

Before we dive into Graves' philosophy on style, let's take a brief look at his background. Graves was born in Indianapolis in 1934. He earned degrees in architecture from the University of Cincinnati and Harvard University before launching his career as an architect.

In 1982, Graves began designing products for Target, bridging the gap between high-end design and affordable accessibility. Over the years, he created dozens of iconic products for Target, including tea kettles, toasters, and kitchen scales. His designs were characterized by their playful, whimsical style, often incorporating bright colors and bold patterns.

The Importance of Unconscious Style

So, what can we learn from Graves' statement about not being aware of his own style? According to Graves, the key to creating a unique style is to not overthink it. When you try too hard to cultivate a certain look or persona, you risk losing the authenticity and spontaneity that make personal style so powerful.

Instead, Graves suggests that we should embrace our "unconscious" style - the natural expression of who we are that comes out when we're not trying to impress anyone. This doesn't mean that you should stop putting effort into your appearance or your work, but rather that you should stay true to yourself and let your style evolve organically.

Examples of Unconscious Style

So, how can you recognize your own unconscious style? Here are a few examples:

  • The clothes you put on when you're not trying to impress anyone
  • The way you speak when you're talking to friends or family, versus when you're in a formal setting
  • The creative projects you work on in your free time, without the pressure of a deadline or paycheck

By paying attention to these moments when your guard is down, you can start to get a sense of your own personal style.

Practical Tips for Discovering Your Style

If you're still struggling to define your own personal style, here are a few practical tips to get you started:

  • Take note of the things that catch your eye - whether it's a piece of clothing, a piece of art, or a piece of furniture. What do they all have in common? This can help you identify your aesthetic preferences.
  • Don't be afraid to try new things - you never know what you might discover about yourself. Experiment with different styles, colors, and patterns until something feels right.
  • Look at the people you admire - whether it's a celebrity, a friend, or a coworker. What qualities do they possess that you would like to incorporate into your own style?

Conclusion: How to Embrace Your Unconscious Style

In conclusion, discovering your own personal style is about embracing your "unconscious" style - the natural expression of who you are that comes out when you're not trying to impress anyone. This requires a certain amount of self-awareness, but also a willingness to let go of control and let your style evolve organically.

To sum up, here are three key takeaways:

  1. Pay attention to the moments when your guard is down and your true self emerges.
  2. Don't try too hard to cultivate a certain look or persona - instead, let your style evolve organically.
  3. Experiment with different styles and aesthetics until you find something that feels authentic and true to you.
