Ageing: The Importance of Enjoying the Golden Years



"Life should not only be lived, it should be celebrated." This quote from Osho reminds us that life is not just about surviving, but also about thriving. Many people have the privilege of reaching old age, but few are able to enjoy it. We've put more effort into helping folks reach old age than into helping them enjoy it. In this article, we'll explore why it's important to enjoy your golden years and some practical tips on how to make the most of them.

An Inspiring Story of Aging Gracefully

In 2017, a 90-year-old woman named Mimi raised over $50,000 for Syrian refugees by baking and selling pies. Mimi had been baking pies her entire life and decided to continue doing so for a cause she felt passionate about. She was able to turn her passion into a way to help others and felt fulfilled in the process.

There are many stories of people who have aged gracefully, enjoying every moment of their lives. These individuals have taken control of their lives, pursuing activities that bring them joy and fulfillment. Aging is inevitable, but how we age is within our control. We can choose to spend our later years merely existing or make the conscious decision to thrive.

Why Should We Enjoy Our Golden Years?

Many people view aging as a time when their health and mobility decline, leading to a less fulfilling life. However, this doesn't have to be the case. Studies have shown that people who remain active and engaged in their later years experience better physical and mental health. Enjoying our golden years also allows us to:

  • Strengthen relationships with loved ones
  • Try new things and discover new passions
  • Reflect on our lives and feel a sense of accomplishment

Practical Tips for Enjoying Your Golden Years

Here are some practical tips for making the most of your golden years:

  • Stay active: Engage in regular physical activity that you enjoy, such as walking, swimming, or dancing. Physical activity helps keep both your body and mind in good shape.
  • Continue learning: Take up new hobbies, learn a new language, or enroll in a class. Stimulating your brain can help keep it sharp and boost mental health.
  • Stay connected: Connect with loved ones, make new friends, and volunteer your time. A strong social network can provide support and a sense of purpose.
  • Travel: Whether it's a weekend getaway or an extended vacation, travel allows you to experience new places and make new memories.
  • Embrace change: Don't be afraid to try new things or make changes in your life. Change can lead to personal growth and a renewed sense of purpose.

The Story of Frank A. Clark

Frank A. Clark, the author who once said, "We've put more effort into helping folks reach old age than into helping them enjoy it," lived until the age of 82. He spent his later years writing and reflecting on life. His words remind us that we must not wait until our later years to start enjoying life. Instead, we should strive to live intentional, fulfilled lives at every stage.

Conclusion

To sum it up, aging is a natural part of life. However, it doesn't have to be a time of decline and despair. By staying active, engaged, and curious, we can make the most of our golden years. Remember to:

  1. Stay physically and mentally active
  2. Cultivate strong relationships and connect with others
  3. Embrace new experiences and make time for enjoyment
