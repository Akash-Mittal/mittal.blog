The Art of Balancing Opposing Goods



An Inspiring Story

Julie was an ambitious young woman. She was determined to make a name for herself in the corporate world and was willing to work hard to achieve her goals. However, she soon realized that her eagerness to succeed was causing her to overlook other important aspects of her life. She had neglected her health, her relationships, and her personal interests. It was then that Julie realized that she needed to find a way to balance her desire for professional success with a fulfilling personal life.

"There are goods so opposed that we cannot seize both, but, by too much prudence, may pass between them at too great a distance to reach either." - Samuel Johnson

As Samuel Johnson's famous quote suggests, there are times in life when we must choose between opposing goods. These goods can range from work and personal life to productivity and creativity. It is easy to become so focused on one aspect of our lives that we neglect the others. However, learning to balance these opposing goods is the key to achieving optimal results. In this article, we will discuss the art of balancing opposing goods and provide practical tips to help you achieve a well-rounded life.

Author Background

Samuel Johnson was an 18th-century writer, essayist, and lexicographer. He was best known for his literary works, including the Dictionary of the English Language. Throughout his life, Johnson struggled with various health issues and financial problems. However, his perseverance and passion for writing made him one of the most influential figures in English literature.

Examples of Opposing Goods

  • Work and personal life
  • Productivity and creativity
  • Perfectionism and completion
  • Health and indulgence
  • Socializing and solitude

These are just a few examples of the many opposing goods that we must balance in our lives. It is important to recognize which goods are most important to you and prioritize them accordingly. However, it is equally important to not completely neglect the opposing good.

Practical Tips

1. Set Realistic Goals

It is important to set realistic goals that take into account both the goods that you are trying to balance. For example, if you want to achieve a promotion at work, you must also make time for your personal life and health. Set goals that align with your values and prioritize them accordingly.

2. Schedule Your Time

One of the best ways to balance opposing goods is to schedule your time. Set aside time for work, personal life, and health. Make sure that you give each good the time and attention it deserves. For example, if you want to spend more time with your family, schedule a regular family night once a week.

3. Be Mindful

Being mindful is a key component of balancing opposing goods. Pay attention to how much time and energy you are devoting to each good. If you find that you are neglecting one good too much, take steps to correct the balance. For example, if you are spending too much time working and not enough time on your health, take regular breaks to exercise or meditate.
