Let Children Read Whatever They Want - An Inspiring Journey



Introduction

When I was a child, I remember sneaking into my big sister's room and scouring her bookshelves for something interesting to read. One day, I found a small, unassuming book called "Are You There God? It's Me, Margaret" by Judy Blume.

I was immediately captivated by the honest and relatable story of a young girl struggling with puberty and her religious identity. It was a book that spoke to me in a way that no other book had before. It also awakened in me a love for reading and the desire to explore more books that spoke directly to my experiences and interests.

Fast forward many years later, and I now find myself advocating for children's freedom to choose their own reading materials. As a parent, teacher, and children's book author, I have seen firsthand the power and importance of letting children read whatever they want and then discussing it with them.

Author - Judy Blume Story or Background

Judy Blume is a beloved children's book author who has written many classic and influential books such as "Tales of a Fourth Grade Nothing," "Superfudge," and "Forever." What makes Blume's writing so special is her ability to connect with and speak directly to children and young adults. She has tackled difficult and sometimes taboo topics such as bullying, divorce, and sexuality, and has helped countless young readers navigate these issues with her honest and empathetic approach.

Blume's own journey in becoming a writer began as a child when she struggled with dyslexia and reading difficulties. She has spoken publicly about the importance of letting children read whatever they want and how it can be a powerful tool in overcoming reading challenges and promoting a lifelong love of reading.

The Benefits of Letting Children Choose Their Own Reading Materials

  • Promotes a love of reading: Allowing children to choose their own reading materials can help them discover books that speak directly to their interests and experiences, and can inspire a love of reading that lasts a lifetime.
  • Fosters independence and critical thinking: Giving children the freedom to choose their own books encourages independence and can help develop critical thinking skills as they engage with different ideas and perspectives.
  • Provides a safe space for exploration and discovery: Reading allows children to explore new ideas and experiences in a safe and controlled manner, which can be especially important for sensitive or taboo topics.

The Importance of Parent-Child Discussion

Of course, simply letting children read whatever they want is not enough. It is equally important for parents to engage their children in discussions about what they are reading and to help them make sense of what they are learning.

"If parents and kids can talk together, we won't have as much censorship because we won't have as much fear."

Judy Blume's words ring true here - open and honest communication between parents and children can empower children to navigate difficult topics with confidence and can help parents better understand their children's perspectives and experiences.

Practical Tips for Encouraging a Love of Reading and Discussion

  • Make reading an everyday activity: Encourage children to read a little bit every day and make it a part of their daily routine. This can help make reading a habit and a natural part of their lives.
  • Offer a variety of reading materials: Provide a wide range of reading materials that cater to different interests and reading levels, including fiction, non-fiction, poetry, and graphic novels.
  • Make discussions a regular part of reading: Encourage children to share their thoughts and ideas about what they are reading, and have open and honest discussions about potentially sensitive topics.

Conclusion

  1. Let children read whatever they want and let them explore and discover the joys of reading on their own terms.
  2. Engage children in open and honest discussions about what they are reading to provide a safe and supportive environment for learning.
  3. Encourage a lifelong love of reading and a passion for learning by providing a variety of reading materials and making reading a part of daily life.
