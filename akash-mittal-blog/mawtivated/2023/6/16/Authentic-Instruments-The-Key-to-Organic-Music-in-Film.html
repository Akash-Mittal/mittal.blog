Authentic Instruments: The Key to Organic Music in Film



The Inspiring Story

Imagine watching a movie where the music doesn't match the content or emotions displayed on screen. It can be distracting, even cringe-worthy, and it can take away from the entire film-watching experience.

This is where authentic instruments come in. Not only do they create a more organic relationship between the music and the content, they also provide a unique and memorable experience for the viewer.

One of the most inspiring stories regarding the use of authentic instruments in film is from the movie "Once." The film tells the story of a musician (played by Glen Hansard) who meets a Czech immigrant (played by Markéta Irglová). Together, they embark on a musical journey that culminates in a beautiful and emotional performance.

The music in the film was recorded using authentic instruments, and the result was a raw and organic sound that perfectly matched the characters and their journey.

"The authenticity of the instruments resonated with audiences and helped create a deeper emotional connection to the film," said Markéta Irglová, who also won an Academy Award for Best Original Song for the film.

Author Background

Ken Burns is a renowned documentary filmmaker who has won numerous awards for his work, including several Emmy Awards and a Lifetime Achievement Award from the Academy of Television Arts and Sciences. His films often feature authentic music from various time periods and genres.

As an avid believer in the power of music in film, Burns has said that he always records his music with authentic instruments in a studio before editing. He then does many versions, shaping the music to the content as he edits.

Examples

Emma

The recent adaptation of Jane Austen's "Emma" (2020) used authentic instruments in its score, which added to the film's charming and whimsical feel. The use of instruments like the harp and strings created a sense of elegance and romance that matched the film's aesthetic perfectly.

The Hateful Eight

In Quentin Tarantino's 2015 film "The Hateful Eight," the composer, Ennio Morricone, used authentic Western instruments like the guitar and harmonica to create a haunting and atmospheric score. The result was a unique and immersive experience for the viewer.

The Lord of the Rings

Howard Shore's score for "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy (2001-2003) features authentic instruments as well as original compositions. The use of instruments like the fiddle, tin whistle, and uillean pipes added to the film's mystical and epic feel.

Practical Tips

  • Research the time period and setting of your film to determine which instruments would be most authentic.
  • Take the time to record your music with live musicians in a studio before editing.
  • Experiment with different versions of the music to find the perfect fit for your film.
  • Consider hiring a composer who specializes in the type of music you need for your film.

Conclusion

  1. Authentic instruments provide a more organic relationship between the music and the content in film.
  2. Authentic instruments create a unique and memorable experience for the viewer.
  3. Using authentic instruments requires research, experimentation, and dedication, but the result is worth it.
