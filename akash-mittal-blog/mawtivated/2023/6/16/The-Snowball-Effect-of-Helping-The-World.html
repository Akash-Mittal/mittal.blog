The Snowball Effect of Helping The World



Have you ever watched a snowball grow as it is rolled down a hill? At first, it's small and seemingly insignificant. But as it rolls down, it gains momentum and becomes bigger and bigger. This is precisely the way I feel about my power to help the world as I get older. I am like a snowball - the further I am rolled the more I gain.

A Personal Anecdote

As a young woman, I didn't understand the full extent of the impact that I could have on the world. Like many of my contemporaries, I was focused on my own life and my own ambitions. It wasn't until I began to get involved in social and political causes that I realized just how powerful one person can be.

I remember a particular incident that crystallized this idea for me. I was attending a rally for women's suffrage, and I noticed that one of the speakers had forgotten her notes. She was flustered and seemed on the verge of giving up. But I felt compelled to help her. I quickly jotted down the major points of her speech on a piece of paper and handed them to her. She looked at me with gratitude and went on to deliver a powerful message that I will never forget.

A Role Model: Susan B. Anthony

Susan B. Anthony, a pioneering women's rights activist, understood the power of persistence and determination. She fought tirelessly for the right to vote for women and, despite numerous setbacks, never gave up. When she died in 1906, women still did not have the right to vote, but she paved the way for future generations of women to fight for their rights.

"The older I get, the greater power I seem to have to help the world I am like a snowball - the further I am rolled the more I gain." - Susan B. Anthony

What are Some Examples?

  • Volunteering at a local charity
  • Donating to a worthy cause
  • Participating in a community clean-up event
  • Getting involved in politics or social activism
  • Mentoring or teaching young people

Practical Tips

Here are a few practical tips for harnessing the power of the snowball effect to help the world:

  • Start small. Don't feel like you have to change the world overnight. Start with a small act of kindness or a donation to a worthy cause.
  • Stay focused. It's easy to get overwhelmed by the problems of the world, but try to focus on one thing at a time. Choose a cause or issue that you are passionate about and learn as much as you can about it.
  • Stay persistent. Making a difference can be hard work and it's easy to get discouraged. But remember that every small step you take is moving you closer to your goal. Keep going.

Conclusion

  1. One person can make a big difference.
  2. As you get older, your power to help the world grows.
  3. The snowball effect can be harnessed to make positive change in the world.
