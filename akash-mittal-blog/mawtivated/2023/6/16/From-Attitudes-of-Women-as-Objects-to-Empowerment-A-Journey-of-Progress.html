From Attitudes of Women as Objects to Empowerment: A Journey of Progress



Author: John Schlesinger

A Story of Inspiration

Years ago, I worked on a film set where the male lead routinely made inappropriate comments to the female crew members and cast. At the time, no one batted an eye - it was just part of the culture of the industry. But over time, I began to realize that this behavior was not only unprofessional, but deeply harmful and disrespectful to the talented women I worked alongside.

Eventually, I spoke out about it, and while the reaction was mixed, I knew I was doing the right thing. And over the years, I've seen the industry and society as a whole begin to shift towards a more equitable and empowering view of women and their roles in the world.

The Journey of Progress

For decades, women have been fighting for their rights and for the recognition they deserve as equal, valuable members of society. From gaining the right to vote to breaking through glass ceilings in male-dominated fields, the progress has been hard-won and often painful. And throughout this journey, one of the biggest obstacles has been the attitude towards women as objects.

For generations, women have been seen primarily as objects to be looked at, judged, and acted upon by men. This toxic mindset has been perpetuated in media, advertising, and culture at large. And while there have been strides towards changing this, it's an ongoing battle that we all must be a part of.

  • It's time to recognize that women are not objects to be ogled, but individuals with their own thoughts, feelings, and aspirations.
  • It's time to push back against the objectification of women in media and advertising, and demand more realistic, empowering depictions.
  • And it's time to engage in conversations about what it means to truly value and support women, in all of their diversity and complexity.
That attitude toward women as objects may have worked for the late Sixties, but it doesn't do so now.

Examples of Empowerment

Thankfully, there are many inspiring examples of women who are breaking down barriers and leading the way in empowering women and girls:

  1. Malala Yousafzai, who was targeted by the Taliban for advocating for education for girls, and has since become one of the foremost voices for women's rights worldwide.
  2. Megan Rapinoe, who not only helped lead the U.S. women's soccer team to multiple championships, but has also been an outspoken advocate for gender equality in sports and beyond.
  3. Emma Watson, who has used her platform as an actress and activist to champion feminism and promote education for girls around the world.

These women serve as examples of the power and potential of women to effect change and create a more just and equitable world for all.

Conclusion

  1. We must recognize and reject the objectification of women in all spheres of life, from media to the workplace.
  2. We must support and amplify the voices of women who are leading the way in breaking down barriers and challenging the status quo.
  3. And we must continue to have open and honest conversations about what it means to truly value and empower women.

By doing so, we can create a world where women are seen not as objects, but as equal and valuable contributors to society.
