Xiaomi Seeks to Claw Back Lost India Market Share with More Local Production

In 2018, Xiaomi's reign as the top smartphone maker in India came under threat when Samsung took back the crown. Despite aggressively cutting prices and launching new devices, Xiaomi struggled to regain its footing. But now, the Chinese tech giant is hoping that a new strategy centered around locally-produced smartphones will put it back on top.

The Problem with Xiaomi's Global Production Model

Xiaomi's success has been built on its ability to produce high-quality smartphones at incredibly low prices. This was made possible through a global production model, which allowed Xiaomi to source components from all over the world and manufacture devices in China.

While this model worked well for Xiaomi in the early years, it has become less effective in recent times. In particular, the company has faced several challenges when trying to sell devices in India, the world's second-largest smartphone market.

One challenge has been India's strict import regulations, which make it difficult for Xiaomi to import smartphones from China. This has led to supply chain issues and delays in getting devices to market. Another challenge has been rising labor costs in China, which have eroded Xiaomi's profit margins.

To address these challenges, Xiaomi has decided to shift its production model towards local production in India.

Xiaomi's Commitment to Local Production

Xiaomi has been producing smartphones in India since 2015, but until recently, these devices were only assembled in India. The components were still imported from China, which meant that Xiaomi was subject to India's import regulations.

Now, Xiaomi has announced that it will start sourcing components locally and assembling smartphones entirely in India. This will allow Xiaomi to bypass India's import regulations and reduce supply chain issues. It will also help Xiaomi to reduce costs, since labor costs in India are lower than in China.

Xiaomi's commitment to local production is reflected in its investments in India. The company has set up several manufacturing plants in India, which have the capacity to produce millions of smartphones each year. Xiaomi has also partnered with several Indian component suppliers, which will help the company to source components locally.

The Benefits of Local Production for Xiaomi

  • Reduced Costs: Local production will help Xiaomi to reduce costs, since labor costs in India are lower than in China. Xiaomi will also be able to reduce shipping costs and bypass India's strict import regulations.
  • Better Supply Chain: Local production will help Xiaomi to better manage its supply chain, since it won't have to rely on imports from China. This will help Xiaomi to avoid delays and better meet demand for its devices.
  • Improved Relationships with Indian Government: By committing to local production, Xiaomi is showing its commitment to India and its economy. This may help Xiaomi to improve its relationships with the Indian government, which has been critical of Xiaomi in the past.
  • Increased Market Share: By reducing costs and improving supply chain, Xiaomi will be better positioned to compete with rivals such as Samsung. This may help Xiaomi to increase its market share in India and regain its position as the top smartphone maker.

Conclusion

Xiaomi's decision to shift towards local production in India is a smart move that reflects the challenges faced by the company's global production model. The move will help Xiaomi to reduce costs, improve supply chain, and increase its market share in India. By committing to India's economy, Xiaomi is also showing its commitment to the country's future. If Xiaomi's bet pays off, it may become the top smartphone maker in India once again.
