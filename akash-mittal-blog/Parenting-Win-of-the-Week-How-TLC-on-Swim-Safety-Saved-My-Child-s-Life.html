Parenting Win of the Week: How TLC on Swim Safety Saved My Child's Life

Summer is the time for splashing around in the pool, having fun with friends, and making unforgettable memories. It's also the time when water safety matters the most, especially for children.

I still remember the day when my son nearly drowned in a community pool. It was a bright summer afternoon, and we were enjoying our time in the water with my friends and their children. Suddenly, I heard screams and turned around to find my son struggling to stay above the surface. His face was panicked, and his limbs were flailing. I couldn't believe what was happening.

In that moment, every parent's worst nightmare became my reality. I frantically swam towards him, trying to stay calm, but my thoughts were racing. I was terrified that I would lose my child forever. However, my years of being a swim instructor kicked in, and all I could think about was getting my baby to safety.

At the pool's edge, I pulled him up and out of the water, and he was choking and coughing up water. The next few moments felt like an eternity, but thankfully, he was alive. It was only then that I realized how important it is to take swim safety very seriously and to teach it to our children.

Swimming is an excellent exercise, and it's crucial for your child's physical development. It's also essential for their safety, and that's why you should consider implementing the following tips.

Make Swim Safety a Priority

At the time, I had never thought that a near-drowning experience could happen to us. However, it can happen to anyone, at any time, and that's why it's crucial to prioritize swim safety.

The first thing you should do is to enroll your child in swim lessons as soon as possible. This allows them to learn about proper water safety techniques, which could save their lives in unfortunate circumstances.

Secondly, it's essential to keep your child within reach at all times when they're near water. Whether it's a bathtub, a pool, or a lake, never leave them unattended. This is what I was doing wrong at the pool that day. I had turned to talk to my friends, but I had taken my eyes off my child. A few seconds were enough for a potentially deadly event.

Get the Right Swimming Gear

Swimming gear may seem insignificant, but it's actually a crucial aspect of swim safety.

Make sure your child wears a life vest that fits snugly and is approved by the Coast Guard whenever they're in or around water. Children should not wear inflatable arm floaties or toys that can slip off easily, and they should also avoid wearing loose clothing.

It's essential to equip your child with the appropriate gear, as it can prevent serious injuries or drowning incidents in the water.

Teach Your Child About Safety Techniques

According to the CDC, about one in five people who die from drowning are children, age 14 and younger. This statistic is alarming, and it highlights the importance of teaching your child about safety techniques.

Here are a few safety techniques that you can teach your child to protect themselves in the water:

- Teach them how to tread water and how to float on their backs, as these can both help when they're feeling tired and need a break.

- Ensure that they know how to call for help and how to recognize when someone is in need of assistance.

- Encourage them to swim with a buddy and never alone.

Personal Anecdote:

When my son recovered from the traumatic experience, I made it a point to teach him the importance of water safety. I bought him a life vest that he was happy to wear, and we went for a swim every day for a week. During that time, I taught him the various techniques that could save his life in a tough situation. I'm happy to report that he's now a confident swimmer and loves being in the water.

Case Study:

According to the National Safety Council, drowning is the leading cause of death for children ages 1 to 4, and it's the second leading cause of death for children ages 5 to 14. It's a sobering statistic, but it highlights the importance of swim safety for parents.

One example of how swim safety can save lives is the story of 5-year-old Soroush Ghahari-Motlagh, who was saved from drowning by his 15-year-old sister. She had learned CPR at her school, and she was able to perform it on her brother, while waiting for the ambulance to arrive. Thanks to her quick thinking, Soroush was revived, and he made a full recovery.

Conclusion:

Swimming is an excellent summer activity for children, but it's essential to prioritize safety to prevent any potential accidents. In summary, make swim safety a priority, get the right swimming gear, and teach your child about safety techniques. As parents, it's our responsibility to make sure our children are as safe as possible, and investing in swim safety is one of the most important things we can do.
