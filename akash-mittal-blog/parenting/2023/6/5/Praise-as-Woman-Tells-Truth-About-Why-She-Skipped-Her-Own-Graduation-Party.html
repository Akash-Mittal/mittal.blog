The Day I Skipped My Own Graduation Party

It was the day of my graduation party and I was nowhere to be found. My family and friends had all gathered to celebrate my achievements and to congratulate me on my graduation. However, I chose not to attend the party.

When I informed my family that I would not be attending my graduation party, they were taken aback. They could not understand why I would want to skip such an important event in my life. However, I had my reasons.

The Pressure of Society

Throughout my academic journey, I had felt the pressure to perform well and to achieve great things. Society had set certain standards that I felt I had to meet in order to be considered successful. However, when I finally graduated, I realized that those standards didn't really matter.

Growing up, I had always been told that if I worked hard enough and got good grades, I would be able to get a good job and be successful. However, after graduating, I realized that this was not entirely true. Getting good grades and having a degree did not guarantee success.

I felt like society had put too much emphasis on academic achievements, and that it didn't take into account the hard work and dedication it takes to succeed in life. I realized that I didn't want to be defined by my academic achievements, and that there was more to life than just getting good grades.

The Need to Define Success on My Own Terms

As I approached graduation, I started to question what success really meant to me. Did it mean getting a high-paying job and climbing the corporate ladder? Did it mean having a certain title or level of education? After some soul searching, I realized that success meant something different to me than it did to society.

I wanted to define my own version of success, and that didn't necessarily involve following the traditional path that society had laid out for me. I wanted to pursue my passions and do something that I loved, even if it didn't fit into the traditional definition of success.

I didn't want to be defined by my degree or by my job title. I wanted to be defined by my actions and by the impact that I had on the world.

The Importance of Mental Health

Throughout my academic journey, I had also struggled with mental health issues. There were times when I had pushed myself too hard and had neglected my own well-being in order to meet society's expectations.

As I approached graduation, I realized that my mental health was more important than any degree or academic achievement. I needed to take care of myself first and foremost, in order to be able to pursue my passions and make a positive impact on the world.

I knew that attending my graduation party would have been a stressful experience for me, and that it would have taken a toll on my mental health. So, I chose to prioritize my well-being over attendance at the party.

Conclusion

In skipping my own graduation party, I made a decision that was difficult for my family and friends to understand. However, it was a decision that was necessary for me to define success on my own terms, to prioritize my mental health, and to push back against the pressure of society.

  1. It's important to define success on your own terms, rather than letting society dictate what success means to you.
  2. Taking care of your mental health should always be a priority, even if it means making difficult decisions.
  3. Push back against societal pressure and pursue your passions, even if they don't fit into the traditional definition of success.
