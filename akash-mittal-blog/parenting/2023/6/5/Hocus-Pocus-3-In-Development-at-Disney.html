Hocus Pocus 3 In Development at Disney

Are you excited to see the Sanderson sisters again?

It's been almost 30 years since the first Hocus Pocus movie hit theaters, but the beloved Halloween classic still has a dedicated fanbase. And now, after years of rumors and speculation, it's official: Hocus Pocus 3 is in development at Disney, with writer Jen D'Angelo attached to the project.

  • The original Hocus Pocus movie grossed over $39 million at the box office.
  • In a recent poll conducted by The Hollywood Reporter, Hocus Pocus was voted the favorite Halloween movie of all time by 33% of respondents.
  • The Hocus Pocus merchandise sold at Disney parks and online is consistently in high demand.

Why Hocus Pocus 3 is a Good Idea

There are many reasons why Hocus Pocus 3 is a smart move for Disney:

  • The first Hocus Pocus movie has become a cult classic over the years, with a strong and dedicated fanbase that spans generations.
  • The success of recent Disney+ reboots and sequels (such as The Mighty Ducks and The Muppet Show) proves that fans are hungry for more content featuring familiar characters and worlds.
  • The Halloween season is a goldmine for Disney, with Hocus Pocus being one of the company's most popular and profitable properties during this time of year.

What Hocus Pocus 3 Could Be About

Although no plot details have been officially announced, there are many directions that Hocus Pocus 3 could take:

  • A direct sequel to the first two films, following the Sanderson sisters as they return from the dead once again.
  • A prequel that explores the origins of the Sanderson sisters and their witchcraft powers.
  • A spinoff that focuses on new characters in the Hocus Pocus universe, while still incorporating elements from the original films.

Conclusion

In conclusion, it's clear that Hocus Pocus 3 is a highly anticipated and potentially lucrative project for Disney. By revisiting beloved characters and a well-established universe, the company is sure to draw in a large audience and generate significant revenue. And for fans of the original films, the prospect of seeing the Sanderson sisters in action once again is reason enough to get excited.
