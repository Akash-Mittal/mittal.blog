Do Yourself and Other Parents a Favour: Lower the Bar

An eye-opening perspective on stress-free parenting

As a parent, you probably have high expectations for yourself and your children. You want your kids to excel in school, sports, and extracurricular activities, while ensuring their emotional well-being and developing their character. All while maintaining a clean house, preparing healthy meals, staying on top of work, and having a social life.

However, this idealized version of parenting can be overwhelming and unsustainable, leading to burnout, guilt, and even resentment towards your children.

"We have turned child-rearing into a profession that requires a degree from the Harvard Business School and, increasingly, two incomes."

This quote by Barbara Ehrenreich, an American author and activist, sums up the absurdity of the modern parenting culture. We have created a competitive and pressurized environment for our kids, where success is defined by achievements and recognition rather than happiness and fulfillment.

The Cost of High Expectations

So, what are the consequences of having high expectations for your children and yourself?

  • Stress and anxiety: Parents who have unrealistic expectations for their kids often feel stressed and anxious about their children's performance. This can lead to a negative parenting style and a tense home environment.
  • Burnout and exhaustion: Trying to do it all and be it all can be draining and exhausting, especially when you don't have a support system or time for yourself.
  • Guilt and shame: When you fail to meet your standards or compare yourself to others, you can experience feelings of guilt, shame, and inadequacy.
  • Resentment and distance: When your children don't meet your expectations or you feel overly burdened by their demands, you can develop resentment and emotional distance towards them.

The Benefits of Lowering the Bar

On the other hand, when you lower the bar and embrace a more relaxed and flexible parenting style, you and your children can benefit in many ways:

  • Less stress and more joy: When you accept your kids' unique qualities and abilities, you can enjoy the journey of parenting instead of obsessing over the destination.
  • More time and energy: When you let go of perfectionism and unrealistic standards, you can free up time and energy for self-care, hobbies, and meaningful connections.
  • Stronger relationships: When you focus on the process of parenting and not just the outcomes, you can build stronger and more authentic relationships with your children based on mutual respect and trust.
  • Resilience and adaptability: When you teach your children that failure and imperfection are a natural part of life, you can foster their resilience and adaptability to different situations and challenges.

How to Lower the Bar

If you want to reap the benefits of stress-free parenting and lower the bar, here are some practical tips:

  • Reframe your expectations: Instead of focusing on achievement and perfection, redefine success in terms of effort, progress, and enjoyment.
  • Prioritize your values: Identify your core values and use them as a guide for your parenting decisions and actions.
  • Delegate and outsource: Don't be afraid to ask for help from your partner, family, friends, or hired professionals when you need it.
  • Practice self-compassion: Be kind and understanding towards yourself when you make mistakes or face challenges.
  • Create a supportive community: Surround yourself with like-minded parents who share your values and can offer empathy, advice, and humor.

Conclusion

To sum up, lowering the bar in parenting is not only a smart and practical approach to modern life but also a compassionate and empowering one.

By letting go of unrealistic expectations and embracing flexibility and resilience, you and your children can enjoy a more fulfilling and joyful parenting experience.
