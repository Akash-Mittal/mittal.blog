When Sarah and her partner, Maria, decided to start a family, they faced some challenges due to their LGBTQ status. They were worried about finding a fertility clinic that would treat them with respect and provide them with the necessary medical care. They also knew they would have to deal with some misconceptions and stigma once their child was born.

However, Sarah and Maria found strength and support through the LGBTQ parenting community on social media. Through various platforms, they connected with other LGBTQ parents, shared their experiences, and learned about resources and tips that helped them navigate the parenting journey.

Example 1

LGBTQ parenting influencer, Dana Rudolph, started the parenting website, Mombian, in 2005 to provide resources and support for LGBTQ parents. The website has grown into a community hub for LGBTQ parents, offering advice, news, and entertainment content. Mombian's social media presence includes over 8K followers on Twitter, over 15K followers on Facebook, and over 2K followers on Instagram.

Example 2

Drag queen and trans activist, Marti Gould Cummings, shares his journey as a gay dad on social media, along with tips and resources for LGBTQ parents. Cummings has over 68K followers on Instagram, where he posts about his family life, his drag performances, and his advocacy work. In addition, Cummings co-hosts a podcast, Dragged: A Podcast for the Drag Industry, where he discusses various topics related to drag culture and LGBTQ issues.

Conclusion

  1. The LGBTQ parenting community on social media provides a safe and supportive space for LGBTQ parents to connect and share resources and experiences.
  2. LGBTQ parenting influencers, such as Dana Rudolph and Marti Gould Cummings, offer valuable advice and advocacy work through their social media presence.
  3. By following LGBTQ parenting influencers on social media, LGBTQ parents can feel empowered and informed as they navigate the parenting journey.
