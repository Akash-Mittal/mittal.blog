When Curiosity Kills: A Parent's Guide to Childproofing the Home

It was a typical lazy Saturday afternoon in the Roloff household, with little Bode, the 3-year-old bundle of energy, running around the house. As he ran past the living room carrying his favorite toy car, he stumbled upon a trip hazard, lost his footing, and fell face forward onto the sharp edges of the coffee table. Audrey could hear the bloodcurdling screams from the kitchen and rushed to find her little one writhing in pain with a bloody gash to the forehead.

This is a scene familiar to many parents. Children have an innate curiosity and boundless energy. It is what makes them so adorable, but it also poses a significant risk to their safety. As parents, it is our responsibility to keep our children safe from harm, and that starts with childproofing our homes. According to the National Safety Council, home accidents are one of the leading causes of death and injury in children under age 5.

the Importance of Childproofing

  • In the United States, every 20 seconds, a child is treated in the emergency room for injuries sustained in a home accident.
  • Each year, nearly 2.3 million children under the age of 15 are treated in emergency rooms for nonfatal injuries related to accidents in the home, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).
  • The National Safety Council estimates that 90% of child injuries are preventable by taking simple safety precautions.

Childproofing Tips

1. Control the Clutter

To make your home safe for children, you need to get rid of the clutter. It is easy to overlook potential risks when there is too much stuff in the way. Start by decluttering your home one room at a time and donate, sell, or trash anything that is broken or outdated. Keep the floors clear of toys, clothing, and other items that could cause your child to trip and fall.

2. Secure Furniture

Children are mischievous and love to climb on furniture. They can easily tip over chairs, tables, and bookcases, which can cause serious injuries. Anchor your furniture to the wall to prevent tipping. Use furniture straps or L-brackets to secure tall furniture such as bookcases, dressers, and TV stands. Cover the sharp edges of furniture with corner protectors to prevent cuts and bruises.

3. Install Safety Gates

Safety gates are a crucial tool for childproofing your home. They prevent your child from accessing areas of the home that are not safe, such as stairs or rooms with potential hazards. There are different types of safety gates available that you can use depending on the layout of your home and your child's age. Pressure-mounted gates are easy to install and are ideal for blocking off areas temporarily. Hardware-mounted gates are more secure and are ideal for use in areas that will be blocked off for a longer period.

Conclusion

Childproofing your home is a must if you want to keep your little ones safe. It may seem overwhelming, but if you take it one step at a time, you will get there. By controlling the clutter, securing furniture, and installing safety gates, you will be well on your way to creating a safe home for your child to grow, play, and explore.
