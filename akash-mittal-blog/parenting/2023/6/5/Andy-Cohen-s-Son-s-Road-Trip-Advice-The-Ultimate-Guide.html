Andy Cohen's Son's Road Trip Advice: The Ultimate Guide

How Your Next Road Trip Can Be Awesome

Do you love road trips? I certainly do. There's something special about exploring the country by car, seeing new places, and meeting new people. But let's face it, road trips can also be stressful, boring, and even dangerous. So, how can you make sure your next road trip is awesome?

Well, I found the perfect answer in the most unexpected place. Andy Cohen, the famous television personality, recently shared on his Instagram account some amazing road trip advice he got from his son Benjamin.

According to Cohen, his son told him that during a road trip, you should always make sure you have:

  • Good snacks
  • Good music
  • Good stops

That's it! Simple, right? But let's delve a little deeper into each of these points and see how they can make a huge difference in your next road trip.

Good Snacks

Snacks are an essential part of any road trip. They can keep you energized, satisfied, and even entertained during long hours on the road. But, not all snacks are created equal. You want to make sure you pack snacks that are:

  • Healthy: You don't want to feel sluggish or guilty after eating a bag of chips or candy.
  • Easy to eat: You don't want to waste time or make a mess trying to unwrap or cut your food.
  • Variety: You don't want to get bored or sick of eating the same thing over and over again.

Some good snack options for a road trip include:

  • Fruits: Apples, bananas, grapes, oranges, and berries are easy to pack and provide vitamins and fiber.
  • Nuts: Almonds, cashews, walnuts, and peanuts are rich in protein, healthy fats, and energy.
  • Granola bars: They come in different flavors and textures and can satisfy your sweet or salty cravings.
  • Veggies and dip: Carrots, celery, peppers, and cucumbers are crunchy and refreshing, and can be paired with hummus or yogurt dip.
  • Cheese and crackers: They are filling and delicious, and you can choose different types of cheese and crackers to mix and match.

Good Music

Music is another important component of a road trip. It can set the tone for your journey, boost your mood, and create memories. But, not all music is suitable for a road trip. You want to choose music that:

  • You and your fellow travelers enjoy: You don't want to annoy or bore each other with your music choices.
  • Fits the mood and the scenery: You can pick different genres or artists depending on whether you're driving through mountains, fields, or cities.
  • Is easy to sing along to: You don't want to miss the opportunity to belt out your favorite tunes and bond with your companions.

Some good music options for a road trip include:

  • Classic rock: The Beatles, Queen, Led Zeppelin, and Fleetwood Mac have timeless hits that everyone knows.
  • Sing-along anthems: Journey's "Don't Stop Believin'," Bon Jovi's "Livin' On A Prayer," and Neil Diamond's "Sweet Caroline" are crowd pleasers.
  • American road trip songs: Tom Petty's "Free Fallin'," Bruce Springsteen's "Born To Run," and John Denver's "Take Me Home, Country Roads" capture the spirit of the open road.
  • Podcasts: If you want to learn something, laugh, or be inspired, you can download podcasts on a variety of topics and enjoy them together.

Good Stops

Stops are the third and final part of the road trip equation. They can break up the monotony of driving, allow you to stretch your legs, and introduce you to new places and experiences. But, not all stops are worth your time. You want to choose stops that:

  • Are safe and clean: You don't want to risk your health or your car's safety at a sketchy or dirty rest area.
  • Are interesting and unique: You can research ahead of time and find out about quirky roadside attractions, historical landmarks, or natural wonders that are off the beaten path.
  • Are convenient and budget-friendly: You don't want to waste time or money driving too far off course or spending too much on admission or food.

Some good stop options for a road trip include:

  • National parks: They offer breathtaking views, hiking trails, and wildlife sightings that can stay with you forever.
  • State parks: They may be less crowded and more affordable than national parks, but still offer natural beauty and recreation opportunities.
  • Historical sites: They can teach you about the culture, people, and events that shaped the country you're traveling through.
  • Local restaurants: They can introduce you to regional specialties, fresh ingredients, and friendly people.
  • Scenic drives: They can take you through stunning landscapes and offer photo opportunities.

By following these three tips for good snacks, good music, and good stops, you can ensure your next road trip is awesome. You'll feel nourished, entertained, and enriched by the experience, and you'll create lasting memories with your fellow travelers.

Conclusion

In summary, Andy Cohen's son's road trip advice can be the ultimate guide for your next adventure. Make sure you pack good snacks that are healthy, easy to eat, and varied. Choose good music that you all enjoy, fits the mood and the scenery, and is easy to sing along to. And select good stops that are safe and clean, interesting and unique, and convenient and budget-friendly. With these three elements, you can have the road trip of a lifetime.

So, what are you waiting for? Grab your keys, load up your snacks and tunes, and hit the open road!
