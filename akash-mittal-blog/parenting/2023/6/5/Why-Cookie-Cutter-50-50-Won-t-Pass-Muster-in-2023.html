Why Cookie Cutter 50/50 Won't Pass Muster in 2023

In my years of experience as a family law attorney, I've seen numerous cases where a cookie cutter 50/50 custody arrangement simply doesn't work for the families involved. One such case involved a little girl who was adamant about spending more time with her father, but the mother was pushing for a strict 50/50 split. As a result, the girl felt torn between her parents and was dealing with a lot of emotional distress.

While the idea of equal time for each parent may seem fair on the surface, there are a number of factors that can make this arrangement impractical or even harmful to a child's well-being. Here are some examples:

  • Logistics: If one parent lives far away from the child's school or activities, it can be difficult to achieve a 50/50 schedule without causing disruptions or putting undue stress on the child.
  • Parental Conflict: In cases where parents have a high level of conflict or communication issues, a 50/50 arrangement may not be healthy for the child to be around. This can be especially true if there is a history of traumas such as domestic violence.
  • Child's Preferences: Parents should pay attention to their children's desires and comfort levels when it comes to a parenting plan. In some cases, a child may prefer to spend more time with one parent over the other, or may feel uncomfortable with switching back and forth between homes frequently.

Given these factors, it's clear that a one-size-fits-all approach to custody arrangements simply isn't practical or effective. Instead, parents should work with their attorneys and family court professionals to create a personalized plan that takes into account the unique needs of their family.

One case that illustrates this point involved a mother who was struggling with addiction and unable to provide a stable home for her children. Despite this, the father was insistent on a 50/50 custody split. After much negotiation and advocacy on the part of the mother's attorney and a child welfare professional, it was determined that a supervised visitation schedule would be the safest and most appropriate option for the children.

Another case involved a father who worked night shifts and was concerned about being able to spend quality time with his children while maintaining his work schedule. With the help of his attorney and a custody evaluator, he was able to create a custom arrangement that allowed for more time with the children on his days off, without disrupting their school or extracurricular activities.

Practical Tips for Custody Arrangements

  • Consider the logistics of your child's daily routine and how a custody schedule might impact that.
  • Be mindful of any conflicts or communication issues that may make a 50/50 arrangement impractical or harmful for your child.
  • Listen to your child's preferences and concerns, and work with professionals to create a plan that is tailored to their needs and comfort level.
  • Stay flexible and willing to adjust the plan if necessary, as your child's needs and circumstances may change over time.

Conclusion

  1. A cookie cutter 50/50 custody arrangement is not always practical or effective.
  2. A personalized plan that takes into account the unique needs of each family is often a better solution.
  3. With the help of family law professionals, parents can create a custody plan that prioritizes their child's well-being and offers stability and comfort in a time of transition.
