The Duggar Family's Harsh Method of Teaching Obedience: An Eye-Opening Documentary

It was a typical Wednesday afternoon when I decided to watch a documentary on the Duggar family, famous for their 19 children and conservative values. But what I saw was nothing short of shocking.

The documentary showed the Duggar parents, Jim Bob and Michelle, teaching their babies obedience in a way that I had never seen before. They used a method called "blanket training," where a baby is placed on a blanket and is hit with a hand or a ruler every time they try to crawl off.

This method is supposed to teach children to obey their parents and avoid danger, but it comes at a steep cost. The babies were seen crying and visibly upset, while the parents remained stoic and unmoved.

The Harsh Reality of Blanket Training

Blanket training is just one example of the Duggar family's extreme methods of parenting. Their beliefs stem from their conservative Christian values, and they see their children as blessings from God to be trained and raised in a strict, disciplined manner.

The Duggar parents believe that discipline and obedience are essential to raising godly children. They have even written a book on the subject called "The Duggars: 20 and Counting!" that details their methods and beliefs.

But what does blanket training actually entail? According to the documentary, the babies are placed on a blanket and told not to move. If they do, they are hit with a hand or a ruler. This is repeated until the baby learns to stay on the blanket without moving.

The documentary showed babies as young as six months old being subjected to this method. It's easy to see how this could be extremely damaging to a child's mental and emotional well-being.

The Negative Effects of Harsh Parenting Methods

The Duggar family's methods of parenting may seem extreme, but they are not uncommon. Many parents around the world believe in strict discipline as a way to raise obedient children.

However, research shows that these methods can have negative effects on a child's development. According to a study by the American Academy of Pediatrics, harsh parenting practices such as physical punishment, verbal abuse, and neglect can lead to mental health problems such as anxiety and depression.

Children who are raised with these methods also tend to have lower levels of self-esteem, are more likely to engage in risky behavior, and have higher rates of aggression and violence.

It's important for parents to find a balance between discipline and nurture, and to understand the negative effects of harsh parenting methods.

Conclusion: The Importance of Gentle Parenting

After watching the documentary on the Duggar family, it's clear that there is a better way to raise children. Gentle parenting, also known as positive parenting, focuses on building a strong relationship with your child and using positivity and encouragement to guide their behavior.

Here are three important points to remember when it comes to gentle parenting:

  1. Listen to your child: Underlying every child's behavior is a need that they are trying to communicate. By listening to your child and understanding their needs, you can help guide them towards positive behavior.
  2. Use positive reinforcement: Instead of punishing your child for bad behavior, focus on rewarding good behavior. This can be as simple as praising them or giving them a small treat.
  3. Show empathy: When your child is upset or struggling, show empathy and understanding. This helps build a strong emotional connection between you and your child.

It's time to move away from harsh parenting methods and towards gentler, more compassionate ways of raising children. Let's create a world where every child can thrive and grow in a loving, nurturing environment.
