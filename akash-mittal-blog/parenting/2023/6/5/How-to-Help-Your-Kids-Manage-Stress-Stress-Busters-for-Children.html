How to Help Your Kids Manage Stress: Stress Busters for Children

As parents, we want to protect our children from the pressures of the world, but unfortunately, stress is an inevitable part of life. Kids today face a range of stressors from school pressure, peer pressure, relationship problems and family difficulties. Learning how to help your child manage stress is one of the most important things you can do as a parent.

Denise Marek, a stress management expert, shares some of her stress busters for kids in the following parenting playbook:

There was a young girl who was very unhappy at school. She was having difficulty with her school work, and her classmates were bullying her on a daily basis. Her mother noticed that her daughter was becoming increasingly anxious and withdrawn, and she was worried about her daughter's wellbeing.

The mother decided to take action and spoke to her daughter's teacher about the situation. The teacher suggested that the daughter attend a stress management workshop for kids, which was being held at the school.

The daughter attended the workshop and learned some valuable stress management techniques, which she began to use when she felt overwhelmed and anxious. She started to feel more confident and was able to stand up to her bullies. The mother noticed a significant improvement in her daughter's mood and behavior, and she was grateful that she had taken action.

According to recent research, stress levels among children are on the rise. The American Psychological Association reports that nearly one-third of children are experiencing unhealthy levels of stress and anxiety. Additionally, the National Institute of Mental Health states that 25 percent of children between the ages of 13 and 18 suffer from anxiety disorders.

Studies have also shown that stress can have a negative impact on a child's physical and mental health. Children who experience chronic stress are more likely to suffer from depression, anxiety, and other mental health issues. They are also more prone to developing physical health problems such as headaches, stomach aches and sleep disorders.

One way to help your child manage stress is to teach them how to identify and manage negative thoughts. According to Marek, "Negative thoughts are like weeds in a garden. If you don't pull them out, they'll take over and choke out the positive thoughts."

Marek suggests that parents encourage their children to stop negative thoughts by using a visualization technique. When a negative thought pops into their heads, they can picture a stop sign and say "Stop" out loud. Then, they can replace the negative thought with a positive affirmation, such as "I am capable" or "I am loved."

Another stress management technique is exercise. Regular physical activity can help your child cope with stress by releasing endorphins, which are feel-good hormones that can improve mood and relieve stress. Encourage your child to participate in activities they enjoy such as swimming, dancing or playing sports.

Practical Tips

  • Teach your children healthy habits such as eating well, getting enough sleep and practicing good hygiene. A healthy body can better handle stressors.
  • Ensure that your child feels heard. Listen carefully to their concerns and offer empathy and validation. Sometimes, just knowing that someone cares can help reduce stress levels.
  • Encourage relaxation. Make sure that your child has time each day to relax and unwind, whether it be by reading a book or taking a bath. Relaxation can help reduce stress levels and improve overall wellbeing.

Conclusion: Helping Your Child Thrive Despite Stress

As parents, it's important that we teach our children how to manage stress so that they can enjoy happy, healthy lives. By incorporating stress management techniques into your child's daily routine, you can help them cope with the challenges they face and thrive in the world around them.

The tips discussed in this article, such as identifying negative thoughts, regular exercise, healthy habits, feeling heard and encouraging relaxation, are all effective ways to help your child manage stress. Remember, it's not about eliminating stress from your child's life altogether, but helping them build resilience so that they can handle whatever comes their way.
