Parenting Is a Superpower

How 'Into the Spider-Verse' Shows Us the Power of Nurturing

When Miles Morales becomes the new Spider-Man in the animated hit 'Into the Spider-Verse,' we see how nurturing and guidance can unlock superhero potential. While he struggles to control his newfound powers, he learns from the older Peter Parker how important it is to have a mentor. But it's not just in the world of comic book heroes that parenting has this kind of power.

In fact, being a parent can be a superpower in and of itself. By providing love, support, and discipline to our children, we have the ability to shape them into the best possible versions of themselves. Here are three ways that parenting can be a superpower, based on quantifiable examples and personal anecdotes.

  1. Building Emotional Intelligence

    2. Studies have shown that children who have been raised with love and support are more likely to have better mental health outcomes as adults. By modeling empathy, kindness, and emotional intelligence ourselves, we can teach our children to navigate their own emotions and relationships in a healthy way. For example, when my daughter was upset about a friend betraying her trust, we talked through her emotions together and brainstormed ways to communicate her feelings in a constructive way.

  2. Fostering Creativity

    3. Perhaps one of the most rewarding aspects of parenting is watching our children grow and develop their own unique talents and interests. By encouraging and nurturing their hobbies and creative pursuits, we can help them find joy and meaning in their lives. For instance, when my son showed a natural aptitude for painting, we enrolled him in a local art class and gave him space and supplies at home to experiment with different techniques.

  3. Preparing for the Future

    4. Ultimately, our job as parents is to prepare our children for the challenges and opportunities that lie ahead. Whether it's by teaching them practical skills like cooking and cleaning, or by instilling in them a love of learning and a curiosity about the world, we have the ability to set our children up for success. When my daughter was getting ready to go off to college, we worked together to create a budgeting plan and talked about how she could prioritize her time and energy to achieve her goals.

Of course, every family is different and there is no one-size-fits-all approach to parenting. But by recognizing that we have the power to shape and influence our children's lives in a positive way, we can build stronger, healthier families and communities.
