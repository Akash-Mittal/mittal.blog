Parenting Advice: Understanding Your Child's Favorite Game

Why My Son's Favorite Game Horrifies Everyone

My 8-year-old son's favorite game is called "Zombie Apocalypse". In this game, he and his friends pretend to be survivors in a world overrun by zombies. They have to scavenge for food, water, and weapons, avoid the zombies, and try to find a safe place to hide.

When I first heard about this game, I was horrified. I thought it was too violent and scary for kids his age. I tried to persuade him to play something else, but he was adamant. He said all his friends played it and he didn't want to be left out.

So, I decided to observe him playing the game and talk to him about it. What I found surprised me.

The Benefits of Playing "Zombie Apocalypse"

  • Creative thinking: My son and his friends have to come up with creative solutions to survive in a post-apocalyptic world. They have to think outside the box and use their imagination to invent weapons, traps, and hiding places.
  • Teamwork: In order to survive, they have to work as a team. They have to communicate, cooperate, and share resources. They learn to trust each other and respect each other's ideas.
  • Problem-solving: There are many challenges in the game, such as how to find food and water, how to avoid or fight zombies, and how to find a safe place to hide. They have to analyze the situation, evaluate their options, and make decisions.

These skills are not only useful in a game, but also in real life. Creative thinking, teamwork, and problem-solving are essential for success in school, work, and relationships.

How to Monitor Your Child's Game

Of course, there are some concerns about violent themes and inappropriate language in some games. As a parent, you should be aware of the content of the game and set rules and guidelines for your child.

  • Check the rating: All games are rated by the Entertainment Software Rating Board (ESRB). You can find the rating on the box or online. The rating indicates the appropriate age and content of the game. Make sure the game is appropriate for your child's age and maturity level.
  • Play with your child: You can play the game with your child and monitor the content. You can also offer suggestions for alternative games or activities that are more age-appropriate.
  • Set time limits: You should limit the amount of time your child spends playing games and encourage other activities, such as sports, hobbies, or reading.

In Conclusion

Playing games can be a fun and educational activity for kids. As a parent, you should be aware of the content of the game and set rules and guidelines for your child. "Zombie Apocalypse" and other similar games can offer benefits such as creative thinking, teamwork, and problem-solving. By monitoring your child's game, you can ensure that they are having fun and learning valuable skills.
