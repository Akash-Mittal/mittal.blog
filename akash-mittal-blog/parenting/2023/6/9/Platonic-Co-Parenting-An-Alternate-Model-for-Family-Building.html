Platonic Co-Parenting: An Alternate Model for Family Building

by John Smith

A New Way to Build a Family

When Sarah and John first met, they knew they wanted to start a family together. However, they didn't want to get married and didn't want a romantic relationship. Instead, they decided to explore platonic co-parenting as an alternative model for family building.

Platonic co-parenting is when two or more people decide to have and raise a child together without being in a romantic relationship. It's a growing trend among millennials who prioritize co-parenting over marriage and romantic relationships.

According to a survey by Modamily, a website that connects co-parents and donors, there has been a 74% increase in platonic co-parenting profiles since 2014. This shows that more and more people are interested in this alternative model for family building.

The Benefits of Platonic Co-Parenting

  • Shared responsibility: With platonic co-parenting, the responsibilities of raising a child are shared among two or more parents. This means that there is no one person who is solely responsible for the child's upbringing.
  • Financial benefits: Co-parenting can also be financially beneficial because the costs of raising a child can be shared among the parents.
  • Diversity of parenting styles: With multiple parents, a child can benefit from different parenting styles, which can lead to a more well-rounded upbringing.
  • Flexibility: With platonic co-parenting, parents have more flexibility to pursue their own interests and careers because they can share the responsibilities of raising a child.

Practical Tips for Platonic Co-Parenting

  • Be upfront about your expectations: Before entering into a platonic co-parenting arrangement, it's important to be upfront about your expectations. This includes things like parenting styles, financial responsibilities, and living arrangements.
  • Create a parenting agreement: It's important to create a parenting agreement that outlines the responsibilities of each parent, as well as how decisions will be made and how conflicts will be resolved.
  • Communicate openly and honestly: Communication is key in any co-parenting relationship. It's important to communicate openly and honestly about any issues or concerns that arise.
  • Consider legal advice: Platonic co-parenting can be complex and it's important to consider legal advice to ensure that everyone's rights and responsibilities are protected.

A Growing Trend

Platonic co-parenting is a growing trend that offers an alternate model for family building. It's an option for people who want to have a child but don't want to get married or be in a romantic relationship. With shared responsibility, financial benefits, diversity of parenting styles, and flexibility, it's a model that has many advantages.

However, it's important to be upfront about your expectations, create a parenting agreement, communicate openly and honestly, and consider legal advice. With these factors in mind, platonic co-parenting can be a successful and fulfilling way to build a family.
