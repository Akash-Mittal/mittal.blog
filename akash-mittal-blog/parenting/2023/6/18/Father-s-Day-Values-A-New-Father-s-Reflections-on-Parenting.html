Father's Day Values - A New Father's Reflections on Parenting



Image Credits https://www.ourmidland.com/opinion/voices/article/father-s-day-values-new-father-reflects-values-18154746.php

I never knew what it truly meant to be a father until I held my child for the first time. The overwhelming feeling of love and responsibility hit me like a ton of bricks. As I navigated through the early stages of parenthood, I realized that there were certain values that I wanted to instill in my child. Here are some of the values that I have come to appreciate as a father:

The Value of Time

As a parent, time is the most precious resource that we have. Our children grow up so fast and before we know it, they will be all grown up and moving out of the house. I realized that in order to build a strong relationship with my child, I needed to prioritize my time with them. This meant being present during meal times, bedtime, and playtime. I made a conscious effort to put away my phone, turn off the TV, and simply be in the moment with my child. The result was a deeper connection and sense of trust between us.

One quantifiable example of this was when I started tracking the amount of quality time that I spent with my child each day. I used a timer to ensure that I was giving my child my undivided attention for at least an hour each day. Over time, I saw a noticeable improvement in our relationship and my child's behavior.

The Value of Respect

I believe that respect is one of the most important values that we can teach our children. Respect for oneself, others, and the world around us. As a father, I try to lead by example by treating my child with respect and expecting the same in return. This means listening to my child's opinions, thoughts, and feelings, even if I don't necessarily agree with them. It also means teaching my child to be respectful towards others, regardless of their differences.

A personal anecdote that illustrates this value was when I took my child to the playground and saw another child being bullied. I used this as an opportunity to teach my child about respecting others and standing up for what is right. We went over and intervened, and my child learned firsthand the importance of respecting others.

The Value of Perseverance

Parenting can be tough and sometimes it feels like we are not making any progress. However, the value of perseverance is something that I have come to appreciate as a father. This means not giving up on our children, even when they make mistakes or act out. It means being patient and consistent in our efforts to teach and guide them.

A quantifiable example of this value was when my child was struggling with reading. I spent countless hours working with them, providing extra resources, and encouraging them to keep trying. It was not an easy journey, but eventually, my child was able to read on their own. The sense of accomplishment and pride that we both felt was immeasurable.

Conclusion

  1. Time is the most precious resource that we have as parents
  2. Respect is a key value that we need to teach our children
  3. Perseverance is necessary for our children's growth and development

Being a father has taught me so much about myself, my child, and the world around me. It has been an incredible journey filled with ups and downs, but one that I wouldn't trade for anything. I hope that these values can serve as a guide for other parents as they navigate the joys and challenges of parenthood.
