Platonic Co-Parenting: Finding a New Way to Raise Kids Together



Image Credits https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/platonic-co-parenting-is-on-the-rise-and-i-can-see-why-m6p85zdtb

John and Emily had been best friends since high school. Despite their differences in personalities and backgrounds, they had always understood and supported each other. One day, they both realized that they also shared a common dream: to become parents. However, they did not want to go through the conventional route of marriage or adoption. They wanted to co-parent as friends, without any romantic or sexual involvement. They did their research, consulted lawyers and therapists, and decided to give it a try.

Today, John and Emily are proud parents of two children, aged five and three. They live in separate apartments, but both are actively involved in their kids' lives. They share the custody, expenses, and decision-making equally. They celebrate their kids' birthdays, attend their school events, and go on vacations together. They are happy, fulfilled, and confident that they are doing the right thing.

John and Emily are not alone. Platonic co-parenting is on the rise, as more and more people realize that it can be a viable, rewarding, and healthy way to raise kids. According to a survey by Modamily, a social network for alternative families, the number of platonic co-parenting profiles on their site has increased by 118% since 2014. Similarly, Family By Design, another co-parenting platform, reported a 24% increase in registrations in 2017.

Why is Platonic Co-Parenting Becoming Popular?

There are several reasons why platonic co-parenting is gaining momentum:

  • Changing social norms: Marriage and parenthood are no longer seen as inseparable or mandatory. People are more open to non-traditional relationships and families.
  • Evolution of technology: Social media, dating apps, and co-parenting sites have made it easier for people to connect and find compatible partners.
  • Challenges of solo parenting: Raising a child alone can be hard, both financially and emotionally. Co-parenting allows for sharing the responsibilities, burdens, and joys of parenting.
  • Desire for deeper connections: Many people crave for authentic, intimate, and long-lasting relationships, but struggle to find them in the dating scene. Platonic co-parenting can provide a deep and meaningful bond between two or more individuals, based on trust, respect, and love.

How Does Platonic Co-Parenting Work?

Platonic co-parenting is a complex and evolving concept, but it generally involves the following steps:

  1. Define your goals and values: Before looking for a co-parenting partner, you need to clarify what you want to achieve and what kind of person you are looking for. Do you want to have a child naturally or through assisted reproduction? Do you want to live together or separately? Do you share the same religious, cultural, or political beliefs? Do you agree on how to raise a child?
  2. Find a compatible partner(s): Once you have a clear idea of your expectations, you can start searching for a potential co-parent. You can use online platforms, social networks, or personal referrals. You can also attend co-parenting events or workshops to meet other like-minded individuals.
  3. Establish legal and financial agreements: Once you have found a suitable partner, you need to set up a legal and financial framework that protects your rights and responsibilities. You need to consult a lawyer, draft a co-parenting agreement, and agree on how to share the custody, expenses, and assets.
  4. Create a parenting plan: Once you have a legal and financial structure in place, you need to devise a parenting plan that outlines the details of your co-parenting arrangement. You need to agree on the schedule, routines, discipline, education, healthcare, and other aspects of raising a child.
  5. Live your co-parenting life: Once you have a parenting plan, you can start living your co-parenting life. You can take turns caring for your child, spending time together as a family, and supporting each other's needs and goals. You can also seek support from co-parenting resources, such as therapists, counselors, or mentors.

What Are the Benefits and Challenges of Platonic Co-Parenting?

Like any parenting style, platonic co-parenting has its own advantages and disadvantages. Here are some of them:

Benefits

  • Strong emotional bond between co-parents and child: Platonic co-parenting can create a deep and authentic bond between the child and the co-parents, based on trust, respect, and love. The child can benefit from having more than one source of support, guidance, and affection.
  • Shared responsibilities and resources: Platonic co-parenting can relieve the financial, practical, and emotional burdens of solo parenting, as the co-parents can share the duties equally. They can also pool their resources to provide a better life for the child.
  • Flexibility and autonomy: Platonic co-parenting can offer more flexibility and autonomy than traditional parenting or marriage, as the co-parents can design their own rules, routines, and boundaries. They can also pursue their own goals and dreams, without compromising their parenting duties.
  • Diversity and open-mindedness: Platonic co-parenting can expose the child to diverse cultures, values, and perspectives, as the co-parents may come from different backgrounds and have different views on life. This can broaden the child's horizons and foster tolerance and respect.

Challenges

  • Legal and financial complexities: Platonic co-parenting requires a complex legal and financial structure, which may involve changes in tax, insurance, and inheritance laws. It also requires a trustworthy and transparent communication between the co-parents, to avoid conflicts and misunderstandings.
  • Social stigma and prejudice: Platonic co-parenting may face social stigma and prejudice, as it may challenge the traditional notions of family, gender roles, and parenting. The co-parents may face criticism or rejection from their families, friends, or society.
  • Emotional risks and conflicts: Platonic co-parenting can create emotional risks and conflicts, especially if the co-parents have different expectations, values, or communication styles. They may also face emotional challenges when dealing with the child's needs, behaviors, or preferences.
  • Logistical and practical concerns: Platonic co-parenting requires a lot of logistical and practical coordination, such as arranging schedules, transportation, and child care. The co-parents may face challenges when dealing with emergencies, illness, or travel.

Conclusion

Platonic co-parenting is not for everyone, but it can be a viable, rewarding, and healthy way to raise kids. If you are considering platonic co-parenting, here are three key points to keep in mind:

  1. Be clear about your goals and values: Before committing to a co-parenting partnership, make sure you share the same goals and values with your partner(s), and define your expectations and boundaries clearly.
  2. Be realistic about the challenges and risks: Platonic co-parenting is not an easy or risk-free option, and it requires a lot of preparation, planning, and communication. Be prepared to face the legal, financial, emotional, and logistical challenges that come with co-parenting.
  3. Be creative and flexible: Platonic co-parenting involves creating a new family dynamic, which may require you to be creative and flexible in your approach. Be open to new ideas, perspectives, and solutions, and be willing to adapt to changing circumstances and needs.

Platonic co-parenting may not be the norm, but it is a growing trend that reflects the changing attitudes and needs of modern families. By finding a way to raise kids together without romantic or sexual involvement, co-parents can create a unique and fulfilling bond that can last a lifetime.
