The Surprising Parenting Milestone of Chris Pratt and Katherine Schwarzenegger



Image Credits https://www.eonline.com/news/1376849/why-chris-pratt-and-katherine-schwarzeneggers-wedding-anniversary-was-also-a-parenting-milestone

It's no secret that parenting is one of the toughest jobs in the world. And when you're a high-profile celebrity, the challenges are even greater. That's why it's refreshing to hear about Chris Pratt and Katherine Schwarzenegger's recent parenting milestone. They revealed that their fourth wedding anniversary vacation was also their first night away from their kids in three years.

As Pratt shared on his Instagram, "We decided to go somewhere for our anniversary... and we haven't been on a vacation in at least four years and we haven't had a night away from our daughter in eons. So let's go! It's been a long time."

This may seem like a small feat, but for any parent, taking a night away from their children can be a very daunting prospect. And for celebrities like Pratt and Schwarzenegger, where their every move is scrutinized, it takes an extra amount of courage to let go of their parenting responsibilities for even a short while.

According to a 2018 survey by the Pew Research Center, 57% of parents say parenting is "exhausting" and 46% say it's "stressful." Another survey by Care.com found that the average parent goes 3.2 years without taking a vacation without their children. This means that Pratt and Schwarzenegger's vacation is actually a rare and impressive feat.

In addition, studies have shown that taking a break from parenting can have significant benefits for both the parents and the children. A study published in the Journal of Marriage and Family found that parents who take vacations without their children have stronger marital relationships and experience less stress and burnout. Another study published in the Journal of Family Psychology found that children who spend time in quality daycare have better social and academic outcomes than those who do not.

And if you can't afford to take a vacation, even just taking a night off to go to a movie or have dinner with your partner can make a big difference in your mental and emotional well-being.
