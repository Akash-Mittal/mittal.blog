The Sneaky Parenting Hack Kate Used at Coronation to Avoid Tantrums



Image Credits https://www.express.co.uk/life-style/life/1777856/kate-middleton-prince-louis-body-language-exclusive

Kate Middleton, the Duchess of Cambridge, is known for her hands-on parenting approach. In the lead up to King Charles's coronation, a body language expert noticed that Kate used a subtle strategy to keep her children well-behaved during the ceremony.

The Strategy Kate Used

According to body language expert Judi James, Kate used subtle gestures and cues to keep her youngest son, Prince Louis, from acting out. She used this strategy to ensure that the children behaved well during the ceremony and avoided tantrums.

James observed that Kate often asked her children to hold her hand or whispered in their ears, reminding them to behave. For Prince Louis, James noticed that Kate subtly nudged him whenever he started to get too playful.

In an interview with Express.co.uk, James explained that the Duchess of Cambridge used a technique known as 'nudging' to adjust her child's behaviour. She said, "We know it's really hard to tell a toddler to stop doing something. So what she was doing was nudging him into slightly less playful activity...that's the kind of thing that parents do without even realizing it."

Why Nudging Works

Kate's subtle technique is a form of positive reinforcement, which is a powerful tool in parenting. Instead of scolding her son for misbehaving, Kate provided gentle physical cues that directed him towards more desirable behaviour.

In many ways, nudging is a more effective approach to parenting than scolding or punishment. When we scold children for their behaviour, it can make them feel ashamed and may even lead to them hiding their behaviour from us. Nudging, on the other hand, shows our children that we're on their side and that we want to help them behave in a way that's more appropriate.

Other Parenting Hacks

Kate's nudging technique is just one of many effective strategies parents can use to help guide their children's behaviour. Here are some other examples:

  • The Numerical Countdown - When it's time to leave a playdate or an event, use a countdown to help prepare your child. Start with "10 more minutes," then "5 more," and finally "we're leaving in 2 minutes." This will give your child a sense of control and make the transition less jarring.
  • The 'Yes...But' Technique - If your child is asking for something they can't have, use this technique to validate their feelings while still being firm. For example, "Yes, I know you want to stay up late, but we have school tomorrow and need our rest."
  • The Reward Chart - Make a chart that tracks your child's progress towards a goal, such as reading a certain number of books or completing their chores. When they reach their goal, reward them with a special treat or activity.

Conclusion

Kate's nudging technique is an effective, positive way to guide children's behaviour. Rather than scolding or punishing, nudging helps children understand what's expected of them and adjust their behaviour accordingly. To be an effective parent, it's important to use a variety of strategies and approaches to find what works best for you and your child.

In summary, here are the key takeaways:

  1. Positive reinforcement is a powerful tool in parenting
  2. Nudging is a subtle, effective technique that directs children towards desirable behaviour
  3. There are many other effective parenting hacks, such as countdowns, the 'yes...but' technique, and reward charts
