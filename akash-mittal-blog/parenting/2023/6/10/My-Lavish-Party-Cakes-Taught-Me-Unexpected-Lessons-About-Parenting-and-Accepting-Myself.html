My Lavish Party Cakes Taught Me Unexpected Lessons About Parenting and Accepting Myself



Image Credits https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/first-person-party-cakes-1.6861362

The Story of How My Love for Lavish Party Cakes Helped Me Accept Myself as a Parent

For as long as I can remember, I've loved to bake. I find it therapeutic to focus my energy on creating something beautiful and delicious for my loved ones to enjoy. Over the years, I've become known for my lavish party cakes - towering creations with multiple tiers, intricate details, and elaborate decorations.

However, as much as I loved the process of baking and decorating, I always felt guilty for spending so much time and energy on something that seemed frivolous. I'm a mom of two young kids, after all, and I should be spending my time doing more important things, right?

It wasn't until I was diagnosed with ADHD that things started to make sense. Suddenly, my overabundance of energy and laser focus on the cakes had an explanation. And as I learned more about the condition and how it affects my brain, I started to realize that my love for baking was actually one of my strengths as a parent.

How My Love for Lavish Party Cakes Helped Me Grow as a Parent

  • I learned to embrace my strengths and accept my limitations - as someone with ADHD, I've always struggled with time management and organization. But when it came to baking, I found that I could hyperfocus for hours on end without getting distracted. Instead of feeling guilty for spending so much time on my cakes, I started to see it as a way to channel my energy in a positive and productive way.
  • I connected with my kids in a new way - my love for baking has turned into a shared hobby with my kids. They love to help me mix the batter, decorate the cakes, and come up with new ideas for designs. It's given us a new way to bond and a way for me to show my kids that it's okay to indulge in our passions and hobbies, even if they seem trivial.
  • I gained a new perspective on parenting - before my ADHD diagnosis, I often felt like a failure as a parent because I couldn't seem to keep up with the demands of running a household and caring for my kids. But as I learned more about ADHD and how it affects my brain, I started to see that my challenges were just a different way of processing information and that my strengths could be used to my advantage. By embracing my love for baking and learning to work with my strengths and limitations, I became a more confident and happy parent.

In Conclusion: 3 Unexpected Lessons I Learned from My Love for Lavish Party Cakes

  1. Embrace your strengths and accept your limitations - we all have unique talents and weaknesses, and it's important to celebrate what makes us special.
  2. Find new ways to bond with your kids - whether it's through baking, sports, or art, finding a shared hobby with your kids can be a great way to strengthen your relationship and build lasting memories.
  3. Change your perspective and see challenges as opportunities - by reframing my ADHD as a different way of processing information, I was able to tap into my strengths and become a more confident and happy parent.

Overall, my love for lavish party cakes has taught me unexpected lessons about parenting and accepting myself. It's given me a way to channel my energy and connect with my kids, and it's helped me see myself in a new light. So the next time you find yourself indulging in a hobby or passion, don't feel guilty - it could be one of your strengths as a parent.
