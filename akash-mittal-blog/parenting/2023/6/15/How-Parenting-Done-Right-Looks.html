How Parenting Done Right Looks



Image Credits https://www.shawlocal.com/daily-chronicle/opinion/letters-to-the-editor/2023/06/14/letter-how-parenting-done-right-looks/

When my son was about six years old, we were visiting my parents at their house. While we were there, my dad wandered off into the backyard to do some gardening. My son, who was always curious and loved to follow people around to see what they were doing, followed him outside.

A little while later, I went outside to check on them and found my son sitting on the ground next to my dad, intently watching as he dug holes and planted flowers. I smiled as I watched them for a few minutes, feeling grateful that my dad was such a wonderful influence on my son.

This small moment encapsulates so much of what I believe parenting done right looks like. It's about passing on skills and values to the next generation, spending quality time together, and being a positive role model.

Examples of Parenting Done Right

Of course, every family is different, and what works for one might not work for another. But here are a few quantifiable examples of what parenting done right can look like:

  • Children who feel loved and supported are more likely to be confident and have high self-esteem.
  • Parents who model healthy habits, like exercise and healthy eating, are more likely to have children who adopt those habits as well.
  • Good communication between parents and children can help build strong relationships and prevent conflicts.
  • Providing children with opportunities to explore their interests and passions can help them develop a sense of purpose and direction.
  • Teaching children responsibility and giving them age-appropriate chores can help them develop important life skills and a sense of contributing to the family as a whole.

Practical Tips for Parenting Done Right

While there's no one-size-fits-all approach to parenting, there are some practical tips that can help create an environment of love, support, and growth:

  • Listen to your children without judgment. Let them express their thoughts and emotions without fear of punishment or shame.
  • Show affection and praise your children often. Let them know how much you love and appreciate them, even in the little things.
  • Set clear boundaries and consequences. Children need structure and consistency to feel secure and understand what's expected of them.
  • Take care of yourself. Parents who prioritize their own physical and emotional health are better able to be present and engaged with their children.
  • Be willing to learn and grow as a parent. Read books or articles, go to parenting classes, or seek advice from trusted sources.

Conclusion

Parenting done right isn't about being perfect or having all the answers. It's about showing up, being present, and doing your best to raise happy, healthy, and well-adjusted children.

Here are three key takeaways:

  1. Model the behaviors you want to see in your children. Whether it's healthy eating, exercise, or good communication, children are more likely to adopt those habits if they see their parents doing them as well.
  2. Spending quality time with your children is one of the most important things you can do as a parent. It doesn't have to be expensive or elaborate, it just needs to be focused and meaningful.
  3. Remember that parenting is a journey, not a destination. There will be ups and downs, successes and failures, but as long as you're willing to learn and grow, you can help your children thrive.
