You're Not a Real Parent Until You've Been Through Hilarious Things



Image Credits https://www.insider.com/not-a-real-parent-until-book-jason-chatfield-scott-dooley-2023-6

It's no secret that being a parent comes with its own set of challenges. From sleepless nights to tantrums in public places, there's never a dull moment. But through the chaos and craziness, we often find ourselves experiencing some of the funniest moments of our lives. In an adapted excerpt from Jason Chatfield and Scott Dooley's new book "You're Not a Real Parent Until...", two dads share their experiences of hilarious things every parent has been through.

As a parent, have you ever found yourself:

  • Walking around with a sticker on your clothing without realizing it?
  • Using baby wipes to clean your own face?
  • Getting excited about a sale on diapers or baby wipes?
  • Being covered in baby food or spit up at least once a day?
  • Laughing uncontrollably at your child's innocent mispronunciations?

If you answered yes to any of these questions, then you're not alone. These are just some of the hilarious things that every parent experiences at some point or another. But why do we find these moments so funny? As Chatfield and Dooley point out in their book, it's because they're relatable and universal. Every parent can relate to the struggles and joys of raising children, and that's what makes the humor so enjoyable.

Some Quantifiable Examples

If you need more proof that parenting can be downright hilarious, here are some quantifiable examples:

  • According to a survey by Huggies, 80% of parents have used baby wipes to clean something other than their child, such as spilled coffee or pet fur.
  • A study by BabyCenter found that 47% of parents have accidentally put a diaper on backwards.
  • Research from The Honest Company revealed that 90% of parents have caught their child eating something they shouldn't have, such as dirt or crayons.

These statistics prove that parenting can be messy, chaotic, and downright hilarious at times. But despite the challenges, we wouldn't trade it for the world.

Conclusion in Three Points

  1. Parenting comes with its ups and downs, but we often find ourselves experiencing some of the funniest moments of our lives.
  2. These moments are relatable and universal, which is why they're so enjoyable.
  3. Despite the mess and chaos, parenting is an experience that we wouldn't trade for anything.

As a parent myself, I can relate to many of the experiences shared in Chatfield and Dooley's book. One funny moment that stands out to me is when my daughter was learning to talk, she couldn't say "spaghetti" correctly and instead said "pasketti". It was so adorable and hilarious that we still refer to it that way today.

Another moment that stands out is when my son was a baby and had a blowout diaper in a public place. As I was cleaning him up with baby wipes, I accidentally got some poop on my shirt. It was embarrassing at the time, but looking back on it now, it's a funny memory that we all still laugh about.

Practical Tips

If you're a new parent or soon-to-be parent, here are some practical tips to keep in mind:

  • Invest in a good sense of humor. Parenting is full of surprises, and being able to laugh at the unexpected moments will make it more enjoyable.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. Parenting is a team effort, and it's okay to ask for help from your partner, family, or friends when you need it.
  • Celebrate the small moments. From your child's first steps to their first words, every milestone is worthy of celebration. Take time to appreciate these moments and cherish them.

References
