The Invidious Nature of Social Comparison: How to Avoid the Pitfalls of Envy



Image Credits https://www.nytimes.com/2023/06/13/learning/word-of-the-day-invidious.html

Once upon a time, in a small village, there lived two friends- John and Peter. They had grown up together and shared everything since childhood until one day, John inherited a large sum of money from his grandfather. Suddenly, everything changed. John started bragging about his wealth, showing off his new car, designer clothes, and fancy gadgets. Peter began to feel envious and resentful of his friend's success, and their once close friendship turned sour. This is an example of how social comparison can lead to invidious behavior.

Invidious is a term used to describe the negative and harmful effects of comparison. It is the tendency to feel envy, resentment, or hostility towards others, particularly when we perceive that they have more success, status, or possessions than we do. Social comparison can be detrimental to our mental health and wellbeing, leading to stress, anxiety, and depression. In this article, we will explore the invidious nature of social comparison and provide practical tips on how to avoid its pitfalls.

Quantifiable Examples:

According to research, social comparison is a common experience, with 80% of people comparing themselves to others on social media. Social comparison has been linked to a range of negative outcomes, such as decreased life satisfaction, self-esteem, and happiness. A study conducted by Lennarz and Lichtenthaler (2020) found that social comparison on Instagram was associated with increased body dissatisfaction, negative mood, and lower social comparison orientation. Furthermore, social comparison can also lead to narcissism, as people strive to compete and be better than others rather than focusing on their personal growth and development.

Tips to Avoid the Invidious Nature of Social Comparison:

1. Cultivate Gratitude: It is crucial to practice gratitude and appreciate what we have rather than focusing on what we lack. Gratitude increases our sense of well-being and satisfaction, leading to mental and emotional stability. We can keep a gratitude journal or take a moment each day to reflect on the things we are grateful for.

2. Limit Your Exposure to Social Media: Social media is a breeding ground for social comparison. We are bombarded with images and stories of people living seemingly perfect lives, which can make us feel inadequate or inferior. To avoid falling into the comparison trap, we should limit our exposure to social media, unfollow people who make us feel envious or unhappy, and use social media mindfully.

3. Practice Self-Compassion: Self-compassion involves treating ourselves with kindness, understanding, and acceptance. It is essential to avoid blaming ourselves or being too harsh when things do not go our way. We should acknowledge our feelings of envy and self-doubt and practice self-empathy instead of self-criticism.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, social comparison can be an invidious behavior that can lead to negative outcomes, such as envy, resentment, and low self-esteem. We must practice gratitude, limit our exposure to social media, and practice self-compassion to avoid falling into the comparison trap. By focusing on our personal growth and development, we can resist the urge to compare ourselves to others and live a happier and more fulfilling life.
