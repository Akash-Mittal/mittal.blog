While it may seem like a harmless and fun activity for children, jumping off a yacht can actually be quite dangerous. According to the United States Coast Guard, falls overboard are the leading cause of boating fatalities on open waters. In fact, just last year there were 613 reported incidents of deaths or injuries due to falls overboard.

That being said, it's important to note that the Brady family's yacht was most likely anchored in relatively calm and shallow waters, and that Brady himself is a seasoned boater. Nevertheless, it's still a good idea for parents to be mindful of the potential risks involved when letting their children play in and around water. It's always better to err on the side of caution and take extra safety precautions.

Practical Tips

If you're planning on taking your kids out on a boat, here are some practical safety tips to keep in mind:

  1. Always make sure that everyone onboard is wearing a properly-fitted life jacket. This goes for kids and adults alike.
  2. Teach your kids how to swim and make sure they are comfortable in the water before letting them jump off the boat.
  3. Take the time to familiarize yourself with the boat's equipment and safety features. This includes knowing where the life jackets, emergency flares, and fire extinguisher are located.
  4. Check weather conditions before heading out on the water and avoid going out in stormy or rough conditions.
  5. Designate a responsible adult to be in charge of the boat at all times and to keep an eye on children who are playing in or near the water.

Conclusion: Tom Brady's "Bad Parenting" in 3 Points
