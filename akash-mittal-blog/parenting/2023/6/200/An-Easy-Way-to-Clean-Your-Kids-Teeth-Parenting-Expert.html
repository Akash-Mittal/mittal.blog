The Toothbrush & Paste Battle

I used to dread the evening ritual of brushing my kids' teeth. It always turned into a battle of wills. They didn't like the taste of toothpaste, the texture of the brush, or the feel of the bristles on their gums. They would wiggle, squirm, cry, and sometimes even bite. I would end up frustrated, angry, and feeling like a failure.

But then I discovered a simple and effective way to clean their teeth without the drama. I call it the "2 M's Method": Music & Massage.

The 2 M's Method: Music & Massage

Here are the steps for the 2 M's Method:

  • Choose a favorite song or playlist of your child (or yours) that is at least 2 minutes long.
  • Play the music while you massage your child's cheeks, lips, and gums with a clean and damp washcloth or gauze pad. You can use a tiny amount of toothpaste if you want, but it's not necessary.
  • Make it a fun and relaxing experience by singing, humming, or making silly faces. Gently rub the cloth or pad in circular motions, following the contours of your child's teeth and gums. Be gentle and firm, but not rough or painful.
  • Encourage your child to spit out the excess saliva and rinse his or her mouth with water. You can use a small cup or a spray bottle for this. Avoid using mouthwash or fluoride rinse unless recommended by your dentist.

The Benefits of the 2 M's Method

Here are some benefits of the 2 M's Method compared to the traditional toothbrush & paste method:

  • No resistance: Most kids enjoy listening to music and getting a massage, so they are more likely to cooperate and even look forward to the toothache ritual.
  • No mess: There is no need to wet or rinse a toothbrush, no need to spit, and no need to clean a sink or counter. The washcloth or pad can be easily washed or disposed of.
  • No risk of swallowing: Some kids have a tendency to swallow toothpaste or foam, which can be harmful and unpleasant. With the 2 M's Method, there is no such risk, as there is no foam or lather.
  • No damage to enamel: Some toothpaste can be abrasive and erode the enamel of teeth or gums. With the 2 M's Method, there is no such risk, as there is no direct friction between the teeth and the cloth or pad.

The Evidence & Feedback

The 2 M's Method is not only based on my personal experience as a parent, but also on scientific evidence and positive feedback from other parents and professionals. Here are some examples:

  • A study published in the Journal of Dentistry found that massaging the gums with a washcloth or silicone finger brush was equally effective as brushing with toothpaste in reducing oral bacteria and improving gum health in infants and toddlers (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/28043805).
  • A survey conducted by the American Dental Association found that more than half of parents struggled with getting their children to brush their teeth twice a day, and that many of them resorted to bribes, threats, and force. However, when asked about alternatives, such as singing a song or telling a story, many parents said they were willing to try them (https://www.ada.org/en/publications/ada-news/2017-archive/october/ada-survey-finds-many-parents-struggle-to-get-kids-to-brush-teeth).
  • A review of various toothbrushing methods for young children by the British Dental Association concluded that there was no one-size-fits-all approach, and that parents should find what works best for their child's age, ability, and preference. The review suggested that massage, music, and reward systems could be useful alternatives to toothpaste and toothbrush (https://bda.org/advice/parents/oral-health-for-babies-and-children/toothbrushing-for-kids).

The Conclusion: 3 Points to Remember

Here are the key takeaways from the 2 M's Method:

  1. The 2 M's Method is an easy and fun way to clean your kids' teeth without the fuss of toothbrush and paste.
  2. The 2 M's Method uses music and massage to engage your child and make the experience enjoyable and relaxing.
  3. The 2 M's Method has benefits for both you and your child, such as reducing stress, saving time and money, and improving oral health.

If you want to try the 2 M's Method with your child, remember to choose a favorite song, use a clean and damp washcloth, and make it a positive and consistent routine. You can also modify the method as you see fit, such as using a toothbrush or finger brush instead of a cloth, or adding a reward or affirmation after the massage.
