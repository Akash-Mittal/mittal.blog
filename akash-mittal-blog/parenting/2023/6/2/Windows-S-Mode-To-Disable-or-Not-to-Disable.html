Windows S Mode - To Disable or Not to Disable?

It was a cold winter day and I was on a mission to purchase a new laptop. As soon as I walked into the tech store, I was overwhelmed with options. There were laptops of different sizes, with various specifications and operating systems. After a long discussion with the sales representative, I finally made a decision and purchased a laptop with Windows 10 S Mode. Little did I know that this decision would lead me to the question - should I disable Windows S Mode?

Windows S Mode is a streamlined version of Windows 10 that was introduced in 2017 to improve security and performance. It only allows installation and use of Microsoft Store apps, ensuring that all software is verified and safe. It is specifically designed for low-end laptops and tablets and aims to provide a lightweight and fast experience.

At first sight, Windows S Mode may seem like a great option, especially for those who only use a limited set of apps and want enhanced security. However, there are some downsides to consider:

1. Limited app selection - While the Microsoft Store does offer a decent selection of apps, it may not have all the apps that you need. If you need to install software from an outside source, you'll need to disable S Mode.

2. Restrictive nature - S Mode is designed as a "locked down" operating system. While this improves security, it also restricts user control, and some may find this frustrating.

3. The need for flexibility - As your computing needs grow, you may find that S Mode no longer meets your requirements, and disabling it may be the only way to unlock more control over your operating system.

So, should you disable S Mode?

If you're happy with the selection of apps in the Microsoft Store, and you don't require any additional software, staying with S Mode is a great option. However, if you need more flexibility, here are some steps to consider:

1. Disable S Mode - This is the most straightforward method to gain more control over your system. Go to the Microsoft Store, search for "Switch out of S Mode" and follow the instructions.

2. Choose a different operating system - If you're looking for a more lightweight and simpler system, consider a Chromebook or a Linux system.

3. Consider the resources of your device - If you're using a low-end laptop or tablet, disabling S Mode could cause a significant impact on your device's performance. Ensure that you understand the risks before disabling S Mode.

In conclusion, Windows S Mode is an excellent option for those who are happy with the limitations of the Microsoft Store's app selection. However, if you require more flexibility, disabling S Mode is the best option. Consider your needs and the resources of your device before making a final decision.
