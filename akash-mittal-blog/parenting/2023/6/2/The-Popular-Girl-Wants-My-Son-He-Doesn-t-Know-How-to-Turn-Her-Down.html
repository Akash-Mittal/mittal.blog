Quantifiable examples of how peer pressure or manipulation can affect teens are all around us. According to the National Institutes of Health, drug use, alcohol consumption, and risky sexual behavior can all be influenced by peer pressure. And while those examples might seem extreme, even the pressure to fit in socially can be a major stressor for teenagers.

So how can parents help their kids stay true to themselves when faced with peer pressure? Here are three tips:

  1. Talk to your child about their values. Before your child faces any type of peer pressure, it's important for them to have a strong sense of their own values. This can help guide them when faced with difficult decisions.
  2. Practice saying "no." In some cases, just knowing how to say "no" can be enough to avoid peer pressure. Encourage your child to practice saying it in a non-confrontational way, so they won't feel uncomfortable or awkward when the time comes.
  3. Encourage them to find like-minded friends. It's important for kids to have a support system of friends who share their values and interests. This can help reduce the pressure to conform to the behaviors of popular kids or others who might try to influence them.

As a parent, it can be difficult to watch our children navigate the ups and downs of peer pressure. But by helping them develop a strong sense of self and teaching them to stand up for their own beliefs, we can help them make smart decisions and build a positive sense of self-worth.
