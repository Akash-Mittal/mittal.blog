Parenting Tips: 6 Mistakes You Must Avoid While Raising Mentally Strong Kids

As a parent, raising mentally strong kids is one of your top priorities. You want your children to be resilient, confident, and able to handle whatever life throws their way. However, there are some common mistakes that parents make that can actually hinder their children's mental strength. In this article, we'll discuss six mistakes you must avoid while raising mentally strong kids.

Many parents believe that shielding their children from negative experiences will help protect their mental health. However, psychologist Dr. Madeline Levine argues that this can actually be harmful. In her book, "The Price of Privilege," she tells the story of a girl whose parents did everything they could to make sure their daughter never experienced disappointment or failure. When the girl grew up and faced a setback, she was unable to cope and fell into a deep depression.

The following are six mistakes that parents commonly make while raising mentally strong kids:

  1. Overprotecting: Protecting your child from harm is important, but overprotecting can actually hinder your child's mental strength. Letting your child take calculated risks and face reasonable consequences can help them learn and grow.
  2. Ignoring Emotions: Teaching your child how to manage their emotions is essential for their mental strength. Ignoring your child's emotions or telling them to "suck it up" can cause them to become emotionally stunted.
  3. Overpraising: Praising your child for their accomplishments is important, but overpraising can cause them to become dependent on external validation. It's important to praise effort and progress rather than just achievements.
  4. Undermining Failure: Failure is a natural part of life, and learning to handle it is essential for mental strength. If you rescue your child from every failure or make excuses for them, it can hinder their ability to cope with setbacks.
  5. Comparing: Comparing your child to others can be damaging to their self-esteem and can create a constant need for external validation. It's important to focus on your child's unique strengths and encourage them to develop their potential.
  6. Discouraging Risk-taking: Encouraging your child to take calculated risks can help them develop mental strength. If you constantly discourage them from trying new things or taking risks, they may become afraid of failure and miss out on valuable experiences.

and Case Studies

One parent, John, shared with us how he was guilty of overpraising his daughter, Amy:

"Amy was always a great student and athlete. I praised her constantly for her achievements, and she became addicted to the praise. When she faced a setback in high school, she didn't know how to handle it and became deeply depressed. It was a wake-up call for me to start praising her effort and progress instead of just her achievements."

In another case, parent Emily realized she was overprotecting her son, Jake:

"I was always afraid that Jake would get hurt or fail, so I constantly intervened to protect him. It wasn't until he went to college that I realized I had done him a disservice. He didn't know how to manage risk or face the consequences of his actions. Now, I encourage him to take calculated risks and learn from his mistakes."

Practical Tips

Here are some practical tips for avoiding these six mistakes while raising mentally strong kids:

  • Encourage your child to take calculated risks and face reasonable consequences.
  • Teach your child how to manage their emotions and encourage them to express their feelings.
  • Praise your child's effort and progress rather than just their achievements.
  • Allow your child to fail and teach them how to learn from their mistakes.
  • Focus on your child's unique strengths and encourage them to develop their potential.
  • Encourage your child to try new things and take risks in a safe and controlled environment.

Conclusion

In conclusion, raising mentally strong kids is a challenging task that requires careful consideration and attention. It's important to avoid these six mistakes that can hinder your child's mental strength and instead focus on promoting resilience, confidence, and healthy risk-taking.

  1. Encourage your child to take calculated risks and face reasonable consequences.
  2. Teach your child how to manage their emotions and encourage them to express their feelings.
  3. Focus on your child's unique strengths and encourage them to develop their potential.
