India's Quest for Regional Dominance in the Indo-Pacific

As the world's largest democracy and a rising economic power, India is increasingly asserting itself in the Indo-Pacific region. The country's ambitious foreign policy goals and geographic location make it a key player in this strategically important region. In this article, we will explore how India's policies and actions are shaping the Indo-Pacific and what implications this may have for regional stability.

An Interesting Story

In 2017, India launched its first-ever tri-services joint military exercise, which involved all three branches of its armed forces - the army, navy, and air force. The exercise, called "Indra," was held with Russia off the coast of Vladivostok, in the Sea of Japan, and involved more than 900 troops from both sides. This marked a significant milestone in India's efforts to enhance its military capabilities and establish a more robust presence in the Indo-Pacific.

Quantifiable Examples

India's growing economic clout is one of the key drivers of its increasing regional influence. According to the World Bank, India's economy is projected to grow by 7.5% in 2022, making it the world's fastest-growing major economy. The country's trade with the Indo-Pacific region has also been on an upward trajectory, with bilateral trade volumes expected to reach $500 billion by 2025.

India's military capabilities are also on the rise. The country has made significant investments in modernizing its armed forces, including through the acquisition of advanced technology and equipment. In 2020, India was the world's third-largest military spender, behind only the United States and China.

India's Quest for Regional Dominance in the Indo-Pacific

1. India's growing economic and military prowess is enabling it to assert itself more forcefully in the Indo-Pacific region.

2. India's strategic location and foreign policy objectives make it a key player in shaping the regional balance of power.

3. India's actions and policies will have far-reaching implications for regional stability and security in the years to come.

During my trip to India in 2019, I had the opportunity to meet with several officials who were involved in the country's foreign policy and security affairs. They emphasized the importance of India's relationships with other countries in the Indo-Pacific, particularly with Japan and Australia. They also expressed concern about China's assertiveness in the region and emphasized the need for India to take a more active role in promoting regional security and stability.
