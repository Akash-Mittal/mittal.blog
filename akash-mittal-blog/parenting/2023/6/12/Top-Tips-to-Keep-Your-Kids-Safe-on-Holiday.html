Top Tips to Keep Your Kids Safe on Holiday

Travelling abroad with your children can be stressful for any parent, but a parenting expert shares top tips to keep your kids safe on holiday.

The Importance of Child Safety on Holiday

As a parenting expert, I've seen countless families take precautions and invest time, energy and money on keeping their children safe. However, it's still a common occurrence to see kids get injured on holiday. Research by MoneySupermarket revealed that:

  • 91% of parents blame themselves if their child falls ill or gets injured on holiday
  • 60% of parents don't buy travel insurance, leaving their family at risk of financial loss and medical expenses
  • 34% of parents don't realize that not all insurance policies offer cover for children under 18

It is vital that parents take steps to ensure their children's safety while travelling abroad. Here are my top tips for keeping your kids safe on holiday:

Top Tips for Keeping Your Kids Safe on Holiday

  1. Teach Your Kids the Key Phrase: Teach your child to say "help me, I'm lost" or "mum/dad, where are you?" in the local language. Practice it together so they feel confident using it if needed. This simple phrase could be a lifesaver if your child gets lost or separated from you.
  2. Buy Travel Insurance: Don't overlook this important step. Accidents and illnesses can happen anywhere, and you don't want to be caught without insurance. Read the fine print and make sure you understand what's covered, especially with regards to your children. Factors to consider include the duration of the trip, your family's health history, the activities you plan to do, and the policy limits and excesses.
  3. Use Child-Friendly Accommodation: before booking your accommodation, check if they have child-friendly amenities such as a pool, a playground, or a kids' club. Consider staying in a family-friendly resort or renting a holiday home with kid-friendly features such as a crib, a high chair, and safety gates.
  4. Take Precautions in Transit: Whether you're travelling by plane, train, or car, make sure you have enough supplies to keep your child entertained and comfortable. Pack snacks, water, toys, books, and a first aid kit. Use a child car seat if necessary and make sure you understand the emergency procedures on the mode of transport you are using.
  5. Keep an Eye on Your Child: Although it's great to give your child some independence, you still need to keep a close eye on them, especially in crowded places such as airports, train stations, and tourist attractions. Use a backpack or leash to keep your child close to you, and teach them to recognize uniformed officials or security guards if they need help.
  6. Teach Your Kids Sun Safety: Sun safety is especially important when travelling to sunny destinations. Make sure your child wears a hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen with a high SPF. Avoid being in the sun during peak hours and stay in the shade when possible.

Conclusion

As a parent, it's important to prioritize your child's safety while travelling. Don't leave it to chance or assume that everything will be okay. Take proactive steps to ensure that you and your children have a fun, memorable, and safe holiday.

  • Teach your child a key phrase in the local language
  • Buy travel insurance and read the fine print
  • Choose child-friendly accommodation

By following these tips, you'll increase your chances of having a stress-free and enjoyable holiday with your kids.
