Breweries Are Better Than Playgrounds for Families to Hang Out



Image Credits https://www.insider.com/mom-says-breweries-better-than-playgrounds-for-families-2023-6

As a mom of three, I've been taking my kids to breweries since they were newborns. And I have to say, breweries are better than playgrounds.

Why Breweries Are Better for Families than Playgrounds

  • More space: Breweries typically have more room to run around than your average playground. Kids can explore the outdoor space, play games, and meet other kids.
  • Relaxed atmosphere: Unlike the high-energy chaos of a playground, breweries offer a more relaxed atmosphere where parents can actually have conversations while their kids play.
  • Better food and drinks: Let's face it, the food and drinks at a brewery are typically better than what you'd find at a playground. Plus, parents can enjoy a cold beer or two while the kids play.
  • Opportunity to learn: Finally, breweries offer a chance for kids to learn about the brewing process, and maybe even get a taste of how beer is made (in the form of root beer, of course).

Here are some quantifiable examples of why breweries are better for families:

  • A survey by Brewers Association found that breweries are now the 2nd most popular tourist attraction in the US, behind only National Parks. So it's clear that a lot of families are already enjoying their time at breweries.
  • A study by the Journal of Epidemiology and Community Health found that moderate alcohol consumption by parents can actually have a positive impact on family relationships and childhood development.
  • According to the Pew Research Center, craft beer has become increasingly popular with Millennials and Gen Xers, who are also entering their prime parenting years. So it's likely that more parents will be seeking out breweries as family-friendly hangouts.

Practical Tips for Taking Your Kids to Breweries

If you're interested in taking your kids to breweries, here are a few practical tips to keep in mind:

  • Research the brewery beforehand to make sure they're okay with kids. Some breweries may have specific rules about minors.
  • Bring snacks and toys to keep your kids occupied while you enjoy your beer.
  • Try to visit during off-peak hours to avoid crowds and give your kids more space to play.
  • Consider bringing another adult along to help watch the kids.

Conclusion: Why Breweries Are Better for Families

In summary, here are three key reasons why breweries are better than playgrounds for families:

  1. Breweries offer more space, a relaxed atmosphere, and better food and drinks than playgrounds.
  2. Quantifiable examples show that breweries are already a popular family-friendly hangout.
  3. Practical tips can help parents make the most of their time at breweries with their kids.
