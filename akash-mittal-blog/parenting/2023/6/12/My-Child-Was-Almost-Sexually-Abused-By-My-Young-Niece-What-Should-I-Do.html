My Child Was Almost Sexually Abused By My Young Niece—What Should I Do?



Image Credits https://www.newsweek.com/wsid-family-sexual-assault-parenting-child-1805340

As I was getting ready for work one morning, my 5-year-old daughter walked into the bathroom and told me that her 8-year-old cousin had tried to touch her private parts during a sleepover at her aunt's house. I was shocked, angry and confused all at the same time.

As someone who had been sexually abused as a child, the news hit me very hard. I didn't want my daughter to go through the same trauma I had gone through. But at the same time, I didn't want to overreact and cause unnecessary drama within the family.

After taking a step back and seeking advice from professionals, I realized that this was a serious situation that needed to be addressed. Here are some lessons that I learned along the way:

  • 1 in 4 girls and 1 in 6 boys will be sexually abused before they turn 18
  • 90% of child sexual abuse victims know their abuser
  • 60% of child sexual abuse victims never tell anyone
  • Child sexual abuse can have long-lasting effects, including depression, anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder, and drug and alcohol abuse

Lessons Learned

  1. Believe your child: It is important to take your child seriously when they disclose abuse, and to let them know that it is not their fault.
  2. Seek professional help: Whether it is a therapist, a medical professional, or law enforcement, it is important to reach out to people who have the expertise to handle these situations properly.
  3. Take action: It is easy to get overwhelmed and unsure of what to do, but it is important to take action and protect your child from further abuse.

and Case Studies

One of my friends was sexually abused by her stepfather when she was a child. She didn't tell anyone until she was in her 30s, and the abuse had a profound impact on her life. She struggled with depression, anxiety and addiction for many years.

Another friend of mine was abused by a neighbor when she was a child. She told her parents, but they didn't believe her because the neighbor was a respected member of the community. She felt alone and betrayed, which led to trust issues later in life.

These stories illustrate the devastating effects that sexual abuse can have on a person's life, and the importance of taking action to prevent further abuse.

Practical Tips

  • Teach your child about body safety: Talk to your child about appropriate and inappropriate touching, and let them know they can come to you if something happens.
  • Know the signs: Keep an eye out for behavioral changes in your child, such as withdrawal, anger, or anxiety, as they can be indicators of abuse.
  • Report suspected abuse: If you suspect that a child is being abused, it is your duty to report it to law enforcement or child protective services.
