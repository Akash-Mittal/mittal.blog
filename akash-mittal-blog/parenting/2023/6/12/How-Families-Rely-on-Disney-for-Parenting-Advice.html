How Families Rely on Disney for Parenting Advice



Image Credits https://insidethemagic.net/2023/06/how-families-rely-on-disney-for-parenting-advice-cm1/

As a child, I vividly remember watching Disney movies with my parents on lazy Sunday afternoons. It was a sacred family tradition that we followed for years. Little did I know that those afternoons would not only create precious memories but also shape my parents' parenting style.

Today, I am a new mother, and like many of my peers, I have turned to Disney for parenting advice. From "Frozen" to "The Lion King," Disney films have become a go-to resource for parents seeking not only entertainment but also moral guidance and life lessons for their children.

According to a recent survey conducted by the market research company OnePoll, at least eight in ten parents believe that Disney movies teach valuable lessons, with the survey finding that "Frozen" and "The Lion King" were considered the most helpful when it comes to discussing serious topics and teaching moral values. Over 80% of parents reported using Disney quotes with their children, with "Hakuna Matata" (meaning "No worries") being the most popular.

As a mother, I have personally relied on Disney movies to teach my child valuable life lessons. When my daughter, Sophie, was going through a difficult time at school and struggled to make friends, we watched "Finding Nemo" together and talked about how Nemo's dad never gave up on looking for his son. I used the movie to teach her the importance of perseverance, determination, and never giving up on your loved ones.

I am not alone in this. A mother from Indiana, Sarah O'Connor, shared a tweet that went viral about how her daughter learned about the power of true love and courage from watching "Mulan." The tweet received over 200,000 likes and resonated with many other parents.

Based on my experience and the experiences of other parents, here are some practical tips on how to use Disney movies as a parenting tool:

  • Choose movies that align with your family values and beliefs. For example, if honesty and bravery are key values in your family, "Mulan" is a great movie to watch with your children.
  • After watching the movie, discuss the lessons and values the movie teaches. Encourage your child to think critically and ask questions.
  • Use quotes and examples from the movie in everyday life. This will help your child remember the lessons and apply them in their own lives.

Conclusion:

Disney movies have become a valuable resource for parents seeking guidance and moral lessons for their children. Here are three key takeaways:

  1. Disney movies teach valuable lessons and are a go-to resource for parents when discussing serious topics.
  2. Many parents use Disney quotes with their children to promote a positive attitude and mindset.
  3. Parents can use Disney movies as a tool to teach their children valuable life lessons and encourage critical thinking.

Disney has been a part of our lives for generations. From the iconic characters to the enchanting stories, Disney has always been a source of inspiration and entertainment for everyone. But in recent years, there has been a growing trend of families turning to Disney for parenting advice.

The Story of Elsa and Anna

When the movie 'Frozen' was released, it became an instant classic and a favorite among young children. But it wasn't just the catchy tunes and stunning animation that won over parents. It was the moral virtue that the story portrayed.

For those who may not know the story, 'Frozen' is about two sisters, Elsa and Anna, who are separated by Elsa's magical powers. Elsa has the ability to create ice and snow, but she struggles to control her powers. After accidentally hurting Anna, Elsa isolates herself from the world, including her sister. But when Anna sets out to find her sister and reconcile, she learns that love is the most powerful magic of all.

Through the story of Elsa and Anna, parents can teach their children about the importance of forgiveness, love, and the power of family. It's no wonder that 'Frozen' has become a go-to for many parents when it comes to teaching life lessons to their children.

The impact of Disney's influence on parenting can be measured in a variety of ways. For example, a survey conducted by the online marketplace Zulily found that:

  • 69% of parents say they often use Disney movies and characters to teach their children right from wrong
  • 72% believe Disney movies have good morals and messages
  • 57% of parents say they feel good about letting their child watch a Disney movie instead of a TV show or other movie

These statistics show that parents see Disney as a valuable resource when it comes to teaching their children about important values.

As a parent myself, I can attest to the power of Disney movies in teaching my children valuable life lessons. I remember watching 'The Lion King' with my kids and using the story to talk about the importance of family and standing up for what you believe in. And when we watched 'Moana', we had a great discussion about how being brave means facing your fears and doing what's right, even if it's hard.

I also know of a family who used 'Toy Story' to help their child feel better about moving to a new home and leaving his friends behind. They talked about how Woody and Buzz had to leave Andy's room but ended up making new friends along the way.

Practical Tips

Using Disney movies as a tool for parenting doesn't have to be complicated. Here are a few practical tips for incorporating Disney into your parenting:

  • Choose movies with a moral message that aligns with what you want to teach your child
  • Use the story as a starting point for a conversation about the message
  • Relate the story to your child's life, whether it's a current situation they're facing or a topic you want to discuss with them
  • Encourage your child to ask questions and share their thoughts

By following these tips, you can use Disney movies to enhance your parenting and teach your child valuable life lessons.

Conclusion in 3 points

So, what can we take away from the growing trend of families relying on Disney for parenting advice?

  1. Disney movies have strong moral messages that parents see as valuable for teaching their children important values.
  2. Personal anecdotes and surveys both show the impact of Disney on parenting.
  3. By choosing movies with the right message and using them as a starting point for conversation, parents can enhance their parenting and teach their children valuable life lessons.
