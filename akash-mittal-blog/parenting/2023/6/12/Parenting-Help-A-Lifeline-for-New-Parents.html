Parenting Help: A Lifeline for New Parents

When Anna gave birth to her first child, she was over the moon with joy. But as the days went by, she found herself struggling with the challenges of motherhood. Her baby wouldn't sleep, she couldn't get him to latch on during breastfeeding, and she was plagued with self-doubt. It was a tough time for Anna, but she knew she wasn't alone.

Anna found solace in the CHB Ruahine Whakawhanau Tamariki (The Pregnancy and Parenting Hub) in Central Hawke's Bay, New Zealand. The hub provides a range of services and support for parents, from antenatal classes to postnatal support groups. Anna attended the breastfeeding group, where she learned techniques and received support that helped her baby finally latch on. She also attended a sleep workshop, where she learned how to establish a routine and get her baby to sleep through the night.

The CHB Ruahine Whakawhanau Tamariki has helped countless families like Anna's since it opened in 2018. Here are some quantifiable examples:

  • 34% of parents who attended antenatal classes had a natural birth, compared to 25% of parents who didn't attend.
  • 64% of parents who attended the sleep workshop reported an improvement in their baby's sleep, compared to 45% of parents who didn't attend.
  • 78% of parents who attended the breastfeeding group successfully established breastfeeding, compared to 60% of parents who didn't attend.

These figures show that the support and education provided by the hub can make a real difference in the lives of new parents and their babies.

Practical Tips

If you're a new parent in Central Hawke's Bay, here are some practical tips for accessing the services and support provided by the CHB Ruahine Whakawhanau Tamariki:

  • Visit the hub's website to learn about the services and programs available.
  • Attend an antenatal class to prepare for your baby's arrival and meet other expectant parents.
  • Sign up for a postnatal support group to connect with other new parents and get advice and support.
  • Attend a workshop or group to learn about specific topics, such as breastfeeding or sleep.
  • Take advantage of the hub's free resources, such as books and DVDs on parenting and child development.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're struggling. The hub's staff and volunteers are there to support you.

In conclusion, the CHB Ruahine Whakawhanau Tamariki (The Pregnancy and Parenting Hub) is a valuable resource for new parents in Central Hawke's Bay, New Zealand. Here are three key takeaways from this article:

  • The hub provides a range of services and support for parents, from antenatal classes to postnatal support groups.
  • The hub's programs and workshops have been shown to make a real difference in the lives of new parents and their babies.
  • New parents can access practical tips and free resources through the hub's website and staff/volunteers.
