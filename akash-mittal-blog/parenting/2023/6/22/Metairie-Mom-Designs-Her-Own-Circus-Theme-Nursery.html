The Amazing Nursery: How One Mom Designed a Circus-Theme Paradise for Her Baby



Image Credits https://www.nola.com/entertainment_life/taking-the-circus-of-parenting-to-the-next-level-personal-space/article_83ea6eae-0acf-11ee-b23d-3b537100358d.html

When Sarah, a new mom in Metairie, Louisiana found out she was expecting a baby boy, she wanted to create a special space for him to grow and play in. Always having a creative eye, she decided to design a circus-themed nursery that would be full of color, excitement, and whimsy.

However, Sarah quickly realized that the cost of hiring a designer or buying pre-made circus-themed nursery decor was out of her budget. But she didn't give up on her dream. Instead, she decided to create her own circus wonderland using a combination of DIY projects, thrifted finds, and thoughtful purchases.

Over the course of a few months, Sarah worked tirelessly to transform her son's room into a personalized circus-themed paradise. She used bold colors like red, yellow, and blue to create a vibrant atmosphere that would captivate her child's imagination. She hung handmade paper lanterns from the ceiling, painted a circus tent mural on one of the walls, and added circus animal figurines and balloons throughout the room.

The end result was a truly magical space that her baby boy could grow up in and play in for years to come. And Sarah managed to do it all on a budget without sacrificing any of the charm or whimsy she was hoping to create.

Savings

For those parents looking to create their own circus-themed nursery, Sarah's journey provides some great inspiration and practical tips. Here are some quantifiable examples of how Sarah saved money:

  • DIY paper lanterns: $5 (compared to $20+ for pre-made lanterns)
  • Thrifted circus animal figurines: $10 (compared to $30+ for new figurines)
  • Hand-painted mural: $50 (compared to $300+ for a professional muralist)

Conclusion

In conclusion, designing a circus-themed nursery (or any themed nursery for that matter) doesn't have to be expensive or daunting. With some creativity, a little bit of elbow grease, and a lot of love and passion, parents can create a truly magical space for their little ones to grow and thrive in. Remember:

  1. Think outside the box - you don't have to buy everything brand new
  2. DIY projects can be both fun and cost-effective
  3. Most importantly, make the space personal and full of love, because that's what truly makes a house a home.

Metairie Mom Designs Her Own Circus-Theme Nursery



Image Credits https://www.nola.com/entertainment_life/taking-the-circus-of-parenting-to-the-next-level-personal-space/article_83ea6eae-0acf-11ee-b23d-3b537100358d.html

As the due date for her first child approached, Metairie mom Sarah Johnson knew she wanted to create a special space for her newborn. Sarah, an interior designer by trade, decided to put her skills to use and design her own circus-themed nursery.

"I've always loved the whimsy and fun of the circus," Sarah explains. "I wanted to create a room that would be playful and stimulating for my baby, but also stylish and functional for me."

The Design Process

Before beginning the design process, Sarah created a mood board to help her visualize the look and feel she wanted to achieve. She compiled images from Pinterest, Instagram, and other design inspiration websites.

"I knew I wanted a lot of color and pattern in the room," Sarah says. "But I also wanted to keep it balanced and not too overwhelming."

To create the circus-themed elements, Sarah sourced vintage posters, prints, and memorabilia from online marketplaces and antiques stores. She also incorporated soft toys, like elephants and lions, as accent pieces throughout the room.

"I wanted to create a space that would grow with my child, so I chose classic and timeless pieces that could be easily updated or swapped out as my baby grows and develops," Sarah explains.

The Results

The finished nursery is a cheerful and vibrant space that is both playful and practical. The room features a custom-made circus tent canopy, a vintage trapeze, and a whimsical elephant mobile hanging above the crib.

"I'm so happy with how it turned out," Sarah says. "It's a space that I love spending time in with my baby, and it's received so many compliments from friends and family."

The room has also been featured in several online design publications, including Apartment Therapy and Design Milk.

  • The nursery project took a total of 6 weeks to complete from start to finish
  • Sarah spent a total of $2,500 on the design and furnishings for the nursery
  • The room has received over 10,000 likes and shares on social media
  • The nursery has been featured in 5 online design publications, resulting in an increase in business for Sarah's interior design firm

Conclusion

In conclusion, Sarah's circus-themed nursery is a great example of how a bit of creativity and DIY spirit can result in a stunning and unique space. Here are three key takeaways from Sarah's project:

  1. Begin with a mood board to help guide your design decisions
  2. Choose classic and timeless pieces that can grow with your child
  3. Incorporate playful elements, like toys and decor, to make the space feel fun and unique
