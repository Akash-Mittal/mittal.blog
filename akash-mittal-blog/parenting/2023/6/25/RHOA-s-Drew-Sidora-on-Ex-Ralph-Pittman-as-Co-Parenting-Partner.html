RHOA's Drew Sidora on Ex Ralph Pittman as 'Co-Parenting Partner'

An Unlikely Partnership

When Drew Sidora and Ralph Pittman ended their marriage, they never anticipated that they would become one of the most effective co-parenting partnerships in Hollywood.

As recently revealed in an exclusive interview with E! News, Sidora and Pittman are now more cooperative co-parents than they ever were as a married couple.

"We both have a common goal which is we love our children and we want them to see that no matter what, Mommy and Daddy can work together," said Sidora.

This partnership has been crucial for both Sidora and Pittman, as their careers have taken off in recent years. Despite their busy schedules, they always find a way to put their children first.

Setting the Example

According to a study published in the Journal of Marriage and Family, children of amicably divorced parents experience less stress and emotional turmoil than those whose parents make an adversarial split.

Sidora and Pittman's co-parenting success is having a positive impact on their children's upbringing, as well as setting an example for others in similar situations.

"We have to love our children more than we hate each other," said Sidora. "We have to prioritize them and their needs before our own."

Practical Tips for Co-Parenting Success

Whether you are going through a divorce or simply sharing custody with a co-parent, Sidora and Pittman's success story provides some valuable insights into how to make it work.

  • Communicate Often: It's important to keep the lines of communication open, whether it's about school or extracurricular activities. Make sure you are on the same page and that your child's needs are being met.
  • Show Respect: Even if you don't always agree with your co-parent, it's important to remain respectful. Avoid speaking negatively about your ex in front of your child.
  • Put Your Children First: As Sidora and Pittman have demonstrated, your children should always be your top priority. Make sure your decisions are based on their needs, not your own.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Drew Sidora and Ralph Pittman's co-parenting partnership is a testament to the power of communication, respect, and prioritizing your children. By following their practical tips, you can also create a successful co-parenting relationship that benefits everyone involved.

  1. Effective communication is key.
  2. Show respect to your co-parent.
  3. Put your children's needs first.
