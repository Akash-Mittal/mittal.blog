Kelly Clarkson Talks Parenting On Glennon Doyle's Podcast



Image Credits https://www.today.com/parents/moms/kelly-clarkson-parenting-divorce-glennon-doyle-rcna89112

Motherhood can be overwhelming, especially during a pandemic. Recently, Kelly Clarkson shared her experiences and advice on parenting with Glennon Doyle on her podcast, "We Can Do Hard Things."

Clarkson, who is a mom of two, talked about how she deals with mommy guilt and the importance of taking care of oneself as a parent.

As a successful artist, Kelly Clarkson has been able to balance her career and parenting, which is admirable. Here are some quantifiable examples of how she approaches parenting.

Encouraging Individuality

Clarkson believes in letting kids be themselves, even if it means diverging from what is considered "normal" by society. In her interview, she shares how she allows her daughter to express herself through fashion, even if it means wearing a superhero costume.

This approach allows kids to build confidence and self-esteem, which are crucial to their growth.

Emphasizing Communication

Communication is key to any relationship, but it is especially important in parenting. Clarkson encourages parents to talk to their kids openly and honestly, even about difficult topics like mental health and addiction.

It is essential to create a safe space where kids can express themselves without fear of judgement. This approach helps build trust between parents and children which can lead to stronger relationships.

Encouraging Creativity

At a young age, kids have a lot of natural creativity. Kelly Clarkson believes in fostering this innate creativity, and encourages parents to do the same.

Whether it's through singing, drawing, writing or other forms of artistic expression, creativity allows children to express themselves uniquely and develop critical thinking skills.

Conclusion

  1. Encourage Individuality
  2. Emphasize Communication
  3. Encourage Creativity

Kelly Clarkson's approach to parenting is refreshing and practical. She believes in building a strong relationship between parents and children by fostering open communication, encouraging creativity, and embracing individuality.

At the same time, she emphasizes the importance of self-care as a parent, which is essential to one's well-being.
