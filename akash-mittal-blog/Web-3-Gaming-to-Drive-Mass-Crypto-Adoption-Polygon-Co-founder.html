In 2017, a small game called CryptoKitties was launched on the Ethereum blockchain. The game allowed users to collect, breed, and trade virtual cats using cryptocurrency. Within a few weeks, CryptoKitties became so popular that it clogged the entire Ethereum network and caused transaction fees to skyrocket. The game's creators were stunned as they never expected such a huge response. It was a clear indication that people were eager to use cryptocurrency beyond just investing and trading.

CryptoKitties was just the beginning. Today, there are many Web 3 games that use blockchain technology in innovative ways. For example, Gods Unchained is a collectible card game that allows players to buy, earn, and trade digital assets (cards) using Ethereum. The game was created by Immutable, a blockchain gaming company, and has raised over $15 million in funding.

Another example is Axie Infinity, a game that allows players to raise and battle creatures called Axies. It uses a native cryptocurrency called AXS, which has soared in value in recent months. Axie Infinity has now become so popular that players in third-world countries are quitting their jobs and earning a living just by playing the game.
