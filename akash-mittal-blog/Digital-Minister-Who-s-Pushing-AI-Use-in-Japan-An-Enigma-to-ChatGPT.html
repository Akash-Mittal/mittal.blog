The Digital Minister's Call for a Comprehensive AI Strategy

In March 2019, Takuya Hirai, the Digital Minister, made an announcement of Japan's AI strategy at the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Office. According to a report by BCN, Takuya Hirai called for "a comprehensive AI strategy" that would bring government, industry, and academia together to push Japan's technological advancement.

His call came at a time when Japan had fallen behind in terms of AI adoption, ranking fourth out of five in the "2018 Regional Artificial Intelligence Adoption Scorecard for Asia Pacific" by the Economist Intelligence Unit. In 2020, Takuya Hirai launched the "AI Kaigi," a monthly gathering aimed at bringing together government officials and private companies to discuss AI innovation. 2. The Creation of AI Ethics Guidelines

Takuya Hirai has been vocal about the need for AI ethics guidelines to govern the development and deployment of AI in Japan. 3. Collaboration with International Governments and Organizations

Takuya Hirai has worked closely with international governments and organizations, such as the US and Singapore, to develop AI policies and strategies and collaborate on AI research and development.
