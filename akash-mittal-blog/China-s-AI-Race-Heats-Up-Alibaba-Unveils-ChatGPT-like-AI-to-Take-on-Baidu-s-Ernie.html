China's AI Race Heats Up: Alibaba Unveils ChatGPT-like AI to Take on Baidu's Ernie

Once upon a time, there was a man named Jack Ma who founded a company called Alibaba. Today, Alibaba is not just an e-commerce giant, but a leader in artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning as well. This week, Alibaba unveiled its latest AI model, called ChatGPT-like Tongyi Qianwen AI, to compete with Baidu's Ernie.

Alibaba's ChatGPT-like model is designed to understand human language and respond to questions in a natural way, making conversational AI more human-like. It can be used in chatbots, virtual assistants, customer service, and other applications.

This is not the first time that Alibaba has made headlines in the AI race. In 2017, Alibaba's AI beat humans in a reading comprehension test. And last year, Alibaba's AI-powered healthcare platform, called ET Medical Brain, helped doctors diagnose brain tumors with 90% accuracy.
