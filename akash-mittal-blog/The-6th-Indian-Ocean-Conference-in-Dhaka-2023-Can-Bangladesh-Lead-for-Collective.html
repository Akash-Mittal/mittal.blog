The 6th Indian Ocean Conference in Dhaka 2023: Can Bangladesh Lead for Collective?

The Indian Ocean region is home to some of the fastest growing economies in the world and plays a crucial role in shaping the global economy and political landscape. The Indian Ocean Conference is a platform that brings together leaders, thinkers, and academics from across the region to discuss and collaborate on issues that impact these economies and the world.

The 6th Indian Ocean Conference is scheduled to be held in Dhaka, Bangladesh in 2023. As the host of the conference, Bangladesh has a unique opportunity to lead the region towards collective development. In this article, we explore how Bangladesh can make the most of this opportunity.

In the early 2000s, Bangladesh was considered one of the poorest countries in the world, with high levels of poverty, illiteracy, and malnutrition. However, decades of sustained economic growth and development have led to significant improvements in the country's social and economic indicators.

In the last ten years alone, Bangladesh has achieved impressive growth rates, with an average annual GDP growth rate of 6.5%. The country has also made significant progress in reducing poverty and improving access to education and healthcare.

The success of Bangladesh's development can largely be attributed to its focus on inclusive and sustainable development. The government has implemented policies aimed at improving the living standards of its citizens, while simultaneously protecting the environment and promoting social harmony.

Over the years, Bangladesh has achieved several quantifiable achievements that demonstrate its progress towards development and inclusivity. Some of these achievements include:

  • Reduction in poverty rate from 47.5% in 2000 to 20.5% in 2020
  • Increased access to education, with net primary enrollment of 97.2% in 2018
  • Improved healthcare access, with life expectancy increasing from 66.9 years in 2000 to 72.6 years in 2019
  • Increased participation of women in the workforce, with female labor force participation rate increasing from 25.1% in 2010 to 36.5% in 2020
  • Reduction in greenhouse gas emissions, with a commitment to achieving carbon neutrality by 2050

These achievements demonstrate Bangladesh's commitment to inclusivity, sustainability, and collective development - values that are at the heart of the Indian Ocean Conference.
