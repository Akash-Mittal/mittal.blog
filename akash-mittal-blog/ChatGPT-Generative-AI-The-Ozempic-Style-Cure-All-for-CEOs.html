The Unfortunate Story of Johnson and Johnson

In the 1980s, Johnson and Johnson (J&J) were solely known for their popular pain relievers. One of their products, Tylenol, was the leader in the market. However, that changed quickly when a tampering issue occurred. Someone swapped the Tylenol with cyanide capsules, resulting in the death of seven people. Tylenol stock value plummeted, and J&J found themselves in deep trouble.

After the tragedy, the CEO of J&J, James Burke, took action and launched an immediate recall. Burke called in hundreds of distributors and quickly took accountability for the incident. He also introduced new packaging standards that are in use today. As a result of his swift action and care for the public feeling, J&J was able to regain their reputation and come out of the crisis stronger than ever before.

Fast forward 40 years, and we now have ChatGPT Generative AI, capable of recognizing similar situations quickly and giving CEOs recommended actions on how to go about it. It can also provide insights into how public opinion has shifted and predict how to manage brand reputation efficiently.
