The Rise and Fall of Cryptocurrency in China: A Story of Web3 White Paper and Hong Kong Crypto

It was a humid day in Beijing when the Chinese government released its long-awaited white paper on Web3, the next generation of the internet. As the CEO of Binance, the largest cryptocurrency exchange in the world, I couldn't help but feel both excited and anxious. Excited because Web3 promised to revolutionize the way we interact online, and anxious because the white paper came amid a crackdown on crypto trading and mining in China.

China has a complicated relationship with cryptocurrency. On one hand, the government has long been fascinated with blockchain technology, and even launched its own digital currency, the digital yuan, in 2020. On the other hand, it has been equally wary of the decentralized and borderless nature of cryptocurrencies, which it sees as a threat to its centralized control.

The crackdown on crypto trading and mining began in earnest in May 2021, when the government ordered banks and payment platforms to stop facilitating crypto transactions. In June, several provinces including Sichuan, Inner Mongolia, and Xinjiang, which collectively account for over half of China's bitcoin mining, announced plans to shut down all mining operations.

The Web3 white paper, then, was a surprise. It positioned China as a leader in the development of the next generation of the internet, one that would be decentralized and open. It promised to create a more secure and privacy-respecting internet, and emphasized the role of blockchain in achieving this goal.

However, the white paper also contained a not-so-subtle warning: any project that violated Chinese laws would not be tolerated. This, coupled with the ongoing crackdown on crypto, left many in the industry wondering: was Web3 just a PR stunt, or did the government actually believe in its potential?

Quantifiable Examples

The impact of the white paper was felt almost immediately. On the day of its release, the price of bitcoin, the leading cryptocurrency, surged by over 5%. Several blockchain-related stocks also saw gains, with one company, called NFT Inc, seeing its stock price rise by over 30%.

However, the gains were short-lived. Within a few weeks, the government announced new measures to combat crypto trading and mining, including a ban on all crypto-related social media accounts and events. Several exchange operators, including Binance, were also forced to suspend services in China.

The effect on the industry was immediate and devastating. Within a month, the total market capitalization of cryptocurrency had fallen by over $1.3 trillion, from over $2.5 trillion to just over $1 trillion. Several companies that had raised funds through initial coin offerings, or ICOs, were forced to return investors' money.

Conclusion in Three Points

1. China's relationship with cryptocurrency is complex and constantly evolving. While the government has shown interest in blockchain technology, it is wary of the decentralized nature of cryptocurrencies.

2. The release of the Web3 white paper in Beijing promised to revolutionize the internet, but it came amid a crackdown on crypto trading and mining in China.

3. The subsequent fall in the cryptocurrency market capitalization following the release of the Web3 white paper showed that China's stance on cryptocurrency remains unpredictable, with the government seemingly willing to both embrace and reject the technology.

As the CEO of Binance, I have witnessed firsthand the impact of China's shifting stance on cryptocurrency. When the government first announced its crackdown, we were forced to shut down our operations in China almost overnight. We have since focused on expanding our presence in other jurisdictions, but it has been a difficult and uncertain time.

At the same time, I remain hopeful about the potential of cryptocurrencies and blockchain technology. I believe that they have the power to create a more equitable and decentralized financial system, one that is not beholden to centralized authorities.

Practical Tips

For those working in the cryptocurrency industry, it is important to stay up-to-date on the regulatory environment in China and other jurisdictions. While there are undoubtedly risks associated with investing in cryptocurrencies, there are also potentially significant rewards. Investors should do their due diligence and research projects thoroughly before investing.
