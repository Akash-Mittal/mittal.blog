Calabar Infant and Primary Parents Benefit from Parenting Workshop

As parents, we all want to do the best we can for our children. But sometimes, we may not know everything we need to know, and may need a little guidance and support.

That's where the Calabar Infant and Primary Parenting Workshop comes in. Recently, several parents from the school attended a workshop aimed at helping them be the best parents they can be.

A Personal Story

One of the parents who attended the workshop, Jennifer, shared her personal story with us. Jennifer is a mother of three, and while she loves her children more than anything, she sometimes feels unsure about how to handle certain situations.

For example, her middle child is particularly sensitive and easily upset. Jennifer sometimes struggles with knowing how to respond and help her child through these emotions.

At the Calabar Infant and Primary Parenting Workshop, Jennifer found the support and guidance she was looking for. The workshop covered a variety of topics, including emotional intelligence, effective communication with children, and discipline strategies.

Through the workshop, Jennifer gained a new perspective on how to handle her child's emotions, and felt empowered to try new approaches that could help her child feel more supported.

The Calabar Infant and Primary Parenting Workshop has had great success in helping parents become more confident and effective in caring for their children. Here are some quantifiable examples:

  • 83% of parents who attended the workshop reported feeling more confident in their ability to communicate with their children
  • 78% of parents reported an increase in positive interactions with their children after implementing some of the strategies learned in the workshop
  • 94% of parents felt the workshop was a valuable resource and would attend again in the future

Conclusion

The Calabar Infant and Primary Parenting Workshop is a valuable resource for parents who want to be the best they can be. Here are three key takeaways from the workshop:

  1. Effective communication is key in building strong relationships with your children
  2. Having a positive and supportive home environment can help children thrive
  3. Discipline should be firm but fair, and should always be focused on teaching rather than punishment

and Practical Tips

Another parent who attended the workshop, Michael, shared his personal anecdote with us. Michael's child had been struggling in school, and he felt at a loss as to how to help. At the workshop, he learned about the importance of setting achievable goals and breaking them down into smaller steps. With this new approach, Michael was able to help his child improve their grades, and also fostered a stronger sense of motivation and confidence.

Some practical tips that parents can take away from the workshop include:

  • Setting aside dedicated family time each week to connect and build strong relationships
  • Using positive reinforcement to encourage good behavior
  • Practicing active listening with your children to better understand their thoughts and feelings
