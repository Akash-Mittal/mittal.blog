The Magical Trip That Could Have Gone Very Wrong: Parenting Advice on Taking Your Kids to Disneyland

My family and I had been planning our trip to Disneyland for months. My kids were ecstatic at the thought of seeing Mickey, riding in the teacups, and immersing themselves in the magical world of Disneyland. But as we were enjoying our day in the park, I couldn't help but notice other parents struggling with their kids. Some were yelling, others seemed overwhelmed, and a few looked like they were on the verge of tears.

As we packed up our tired and happy kids at the end of the day, I realized that while our trip had gone smoothly, there were certainly things we could have done differently to make it even better. And so, I present to you my top dos and don'ts for taking your kids to Disneyland.

The Dos

Plan Ahead

Make a plan for which rides and attractions you want to visit, and be prepared to adjust that plan depending on crowds, wait times, and your kids' moods. Download the Disneyland app to keep up-to-date on wait times and other information about the park.

Also, consider packing some snacks and water bottles to save money and make sure your kids are well-fed and hydrated throughout the day.

Establish Rules at the Outset

Before you even enter the park, have a conversation with your kids about what behavior is acceptable and what is not. Be clear about your expectations, and make consequences for misbehavior known. Remember to praise your kids for good behavior as well.

Take Breaks

There's a lot to see and do in Disneyland, but it's important to take breaks throughout the day. Whether it's sitting down for a meal or finding a quiet spot to rest for a few minutes, taking breaks can help prevent overstimulation and meltdown.

The Don'ts

Don't Over-Schedule Yourself

It's easy to get caught up in the excitement of Disneyland and want to do everything. But remember that you're on vacation, and it's okay to take it slow and enjoy the park at a leisurely pace. Don't try to cram too much into one day.

Don't Ignore Your Kids' Needs

It's easy to get absorbed in the magic of Disneyland and forget that your kids have needs beyond riding every ride. Remember to check in with them throughout the day, ask if they need a break or a snack, and be prepared to adjust your plans accordingly. Ignoring your kids' needs can lead to tantrums and meltdowns that can quickly ruin a day at the park.

Don't Underestimate the Power of Souvenir Temptation

Disneyland is full of shiny, sparkly, tempting souvenirs that can quickly add up. Set a budget for souvenirs before you go, and stick to it. You can also encourage your kids to save their own money ahead of time to buy something special at the park.

Conclusion

Taking your kids to Disneyland can be a magical and unforgettable experience, but it can also be overwhelming and stressful. By taking the time to plan ahead, establish rules and expectations, and be mindful of your kids' needs, you can help ensure a fun and stress-free day at the park.

My top three takeaways:

  1. Plan ahead, but remain flexible.
  2. Check in with your kids throughout the day.
  3. Set a budget for souvenirs.

Remember, the goal of a trip to Disneyland is to have fun and create happy memories with your family. By following these dos and don'ts, you can help ensure that your trip is a success.
