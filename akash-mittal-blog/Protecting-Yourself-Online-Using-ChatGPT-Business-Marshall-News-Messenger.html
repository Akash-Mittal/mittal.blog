The Risks of Online Security Breaches

According to a recent report by the Better Business Bureau (BBB), cybercrime is on the rise. In 2019, the BBB received more than 250,000 reports of online scams, resulting in more than $1.3 billion in losses. The most common types of scams reported include phishing, identity theft, and tech support scams.

Phishing scams involve criminals posing as legitimate companies or organizations to trick people into giving up their personal information. Identity theft occurs when someone steals your personal information, such as your Social Security number or bank account information, and uses it for fraudulent purposes. Tech support scams involve scammers pretending to be tech support representatives who convince people to give them access to their computers, allowing the scammers to install malware or steal personal information.

These types of scams can have devastating consequences, including financial losses, damage to your credit score, and even legal issues if your personal information is used to commit a crime.
