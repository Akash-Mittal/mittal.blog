Kim Kardashian Admits She CRIES Over Parenting Challenges

Kim Kardashian is one of the most famous women on the planet, but even she struggles with the challenges of parenting. In an interview with E! News, the reality TV star admitted that she often cries over the difficulties of being a mother.

Kardashian has four children: North, Saint, Chicago, and Psalm. She confessed that there are times when she feels overwhelmed and even guilty for not being able to give them more of her time and attention.

"I think parenting is hard, and anyone who says it's easy is lying," Kardashian said. "It's a constant battle of trying to do the right thing, but also trying to take care of yourself. And sometimes you just feel like you're failing at both."

Parenting Challenges

Parenting is one of the most difficult jobs in the world, and it is not without its challenges. Here are some quantifiable examples of the difficulties that parents face:

  • According to a study by the American Psychological Association, parents of young children experience higher levels of stress and fatigue compared to adults without kids.
  • Another study by the Center for Economic and Policy Research found that mothers who work outside of the home are more likely to experience depression and anxiety than those who stay at home.
  • Research from the University of California, Los Angeles, found that parents of children with autism experience higher levels of stress and depression than parents of typically developing children.
  • And finally, a survey by the Pew Research Center found that 46% of parents say that parenting is extremely or very stressful.

Practical Tips for Dealing with Parenting Challenges

If you're a parent and you're feeling overwhelmed, know that you're not alone. Here are some practical tips for dealing with parenting challenges:

  • Take breaks when you can. It's important to give yourself some time to recharge and refocus.
  • Find a support network. Whether it's other parents or family members, having people you can lean on can make all the difference.
  • Be kind to yourself. Parenting is hard, and it's okay to make mistakes. Give yourself some grace and know that you're doing the best you can.
  • Practice self-care. This can mean different things to different people, but it's important to take care of your own mental, emotional, and physical health.
  • And finally, know when to ask for help. Whether it's from a partner, friend, or professional, there is no shame in asking for assistance when you need it.
