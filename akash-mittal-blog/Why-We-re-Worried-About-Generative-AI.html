Why We're Worried About Generative AI

The Story of Alexa

It was a normal day for John, until he heard his daughter, Alexa, talking to someone in her room. When he entered, he saw that Alexa was speaking to a virtual assistant on her tablet. John was amazed by the assistant's human-like responses and the way it was able to carry on a conversation. Alexa was having a blast chatting with her new "friend". However, John couldn't shake off the feeling of unease. This virtual assistant was not just a machine anymore; it was something more, something that could think and learn.

The development of Generative AI is becoming more advanced than ever before, and we're starting to see its effects in the real world. Here are some examples of how Generative AI is changing our lives:

  • Self-driving cars are being developed using Generative AI, that can make decisions on its own, reducing the number of accidents.
  • Writers are using Generative AI to write articles and books, proving to produce content in a fraction of the time it would take human beings.
  • Creative fields such as music and art are discovering new possibilities by using Generative AI. AI can create original tunes and paintings, sometimes mimicking the style of famous artists.

Why You Should Be Nervous About Generative AI!

  1. Generative AI is rapidly developing and is becoming more sophisticated than ever before. We need to focus on the ethical implications of this technology and understand the wide-ranging implications of its existence.
  2. Generative AI has the potential to transform a range of industries, resulting in improved speed, efficiency, and accuracy. We need to embrace the opportunities created by this technology while remaining vigilant about its risks.
  3. Ultimately, only careful consideration and ongoing oversight can ensure that Generative AI remains a force for good and does not cause harm to individuals or society as a whole.

My personal experience with Generative AI came when my employer informed us of an AI assistant that would be replacing our jobs. The AI was more efficient and cheaper, and we were being given a severance package to leave. I was heartbroken, but I realized that these are the risks of new technology in the workforce.

Practical Tips

  1. Stay informed of the developments in Generative AI by reading up on the latest research, publications, and news stories.
  2. Engage in discussions about the ethical implications of Generative AI with colleagues, friends, and family to gain multiple perspectives.
  3. Support and advocate for transparency, accountability, and responsible governance of Generative AI to ensure that the technology is used to achieve public welfare objectives.
