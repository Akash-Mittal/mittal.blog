What is Web3?

Before we dive into how Dentsu's tool works, let's first talk about what web3 is.

Web3, also known as the decentralized web, is the next phase of the internet's evolution. Currently, most of the internet is controlled by a handful of powerful companies (think Google, Facebook, Amazon, etc.). Web3 seeks to decentralize this power by leveraging blockchain technology to create a more open, transparent, and equitable internet.

Some of the key features of web3 include:

  • Decentralization: Instead of relying on centralized servers, web3 leverages a network of interconnected nodes to ensure data is stored securely and transparently.
  • Interoperability: Web3 allows different decentralized technologies to communicate and interact with each other seamlessly, opening up new possibilities for innovation.
  • Trustlessness: By leveraging blockchain-based consensus mechanisms, web3 eliminates the need for trust between parties, making transactions more secure and reliable.

How Dentsu's Tool Works

Now that we have a basic understanding of web3, let's talk about how Dentsu's tool fits into the equation.

According to Dentsu, the tool is designed to help digital marketers assess their readiness for web3 and identify opportunities to leverage decentralized technologies in their campaigns. The tool works by analyzing a company's existing digital infrastructure and providing a customized roadmap for integrating web3 technologies.

Some of the ways Dentsu's tool can complement AI include:

  1. Improving Data Privacy and Security: Web3's decentralized architecture makes it more difficult for bad actors to compromise data, ensuring customer privacy and security. This is especially important in light of recent data breaches and privacy scandals.
  2. Enabling Micropayments: Web3 allows for seamless micropayments between parties, opening up new revenue models for digital marketers. Instead of relying solely on advertising revenue, marketers can now monetize their content directly through web3-based payment systems.
  3. Creating New Forms of Engagement: Web3 technologies like NFTs (non-fungible tokens) and decentralized autonomous organizations (DAOs) allow for new, more engaging forms of customer interaction. For example, a brand could create an NFT that represents a limited edition product, incentivizing customers to engage more deeply with the brand in order to acquire the NFT.
