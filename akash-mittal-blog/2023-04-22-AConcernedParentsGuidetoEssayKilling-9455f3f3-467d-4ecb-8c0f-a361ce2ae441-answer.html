A Concerned Parent's Guide to Essay-Killing

As a parent, you always want your child to excel in academics. You believe that academic success will lead to a better future for your child. However, sometimes you may feel helpless when your child receives a lower grade on an essay. You may think that your child has put in hours of work and effort and still hasn't achieved the desired outcome.

So, what can we do to help our children in this situation? We need to identify the common mistakes that students make while writing essays and help them avoid these pitfalls. Here are some concrete examples:

  • Lack of structure: Many students dive straight into writing without having a proper plan in place. This can result in a poorly organized essay that lacks coherence and clarity.
  • Grammar and spelling errors: Even if the content of an essay is strong, a number of grammar and spelling errors can make it difficult for the reader to comprehend the main arguments.
  • Plagiarism: Copying and pasting chunks of text from online sources is a common mistake that students make. Not only does this reflect poorly on the student's integrity, but it also shows a lack of original thought and critical thinking.

As parents, we can help our children avoid these mistakes by encouraging them to plan their essay, proofread their work thoroughly, and use their own words to express their thoughts and opinions.

It's important to remember that writing an essay is not just about getting good grades. It's about practicing important life skills such as critical thinking, organization, and communication. By helping our children improve their essay writing skills, we are preparing them for success in all areas of life.

In conclusion, as parents, we can play an important role in helping our children avoid the common mistakes that can kill an essay. By providing them with guidance and support, we can help them develop important skills that will benefit them throughout their lives. Remember:

  1. Plan your essay before you begin writing.
  2. Proofread your work thoroughly for errors and clarity.
  3. Use your own words to express your thoughts and ideas.

With these tips, your child will be on the path to essay success!
