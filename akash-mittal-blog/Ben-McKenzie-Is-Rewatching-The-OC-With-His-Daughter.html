Ben McKenzie Is Rewatching 'The OC' With His Daughter

When "The OC" premiered in 2003, it quickly became a cultural phenomenon. The show followed a group of wealthy teenagers living in Orange County, California, and their families. It was famous for its dramatic storylines, catchy music, and, of course, Ben McKenzie's brooding portrayal of Ryan Atwood.

Almost two decades later, McKenzie is now a father himself, and he's sharing the show with his daughter. In a recent interview, he spoke about the experience of rewatching "The OC" with her and how it's been a bonding activity for them.

The Benefits of Rewatching Your Favorite Shows With Loved Ones

McKenzie's experience highlights a few key benefits of revisiting your favorite TV shows with family or friends. These include:

  • Nostalgia: Revisiting a show you loved as a kid or teenager can bring back memories and feelings from that time. Sharing those memories with others can create a sense of connection and nostalgia.
  • Bonding: Watching a show together gives you an opportunity to bond over common interests and inside jokes. It can also provide a shared experience that you can reference later.
  • Discovering New Things: When you watch a show with someone else, you might notice things you didn't before. They might have a different perspective or pick up on details or themes that you missed.

Of course, rewatching a show with loved ones isn't just about the benefits. It can also be a lot of fun!

Personal Anecdote: How We Bonded Over "Friends"

When I was in college, I started rewatching "Friends" with my mom. I had seen the show before, but it was her first time. We would watch an episode or two whenever I came home for a visit.

Not only did it give us something to do together and bond over, but it also sparked some interesting conversations. My mom would often comment on how different the show was from the ones she watched in her youth. We talked about how sitcoms have evolved over time and how certain themes and topics have become more mainstream.

Watching "Friends" with my mom was a small thing, but it gave us a way to connect and share something we both enjoyed. It was a fun activity that we looked forward to every time I came home.

If You're Thinking of Rewatching a Show With Someone Else, Here Are Some Tips:

  • Be Open to Discussion: Watching a show with someone else can lead to interesting conversations and insights. Be open to discussing what you're watching and hearing their thoughts.
  • Let Them Set the Pace: If you're watching with someone who hasn't seen the show before, don't spoil what's coming. Let them have their own reactions and opinions.
  • Embrace Different Perspectives: Just because someone else sees something differently than you do doesn't mean they're wrong. Be open to hearing different perspectives and opinions.
