How Ai Assisted Design Helped Don Allen III Create Converses and Sharpie Instagram Posts

Don Allen III, a creative director at Converse, wanted to create a unique Instagram campaign for the brand's new line of sneakers. He spent weeks brainstorming ideas, sketching designs, and collaborating with his team. But nothing seemed to stick.

Then he heard about Ai assisted design, a technology that uses artificial intelligence to help designers with their creative processes. He decided to give it a try and was amazed at the results.

With the help of Ai, Don was able to generate hundreds of designs in a matter of minutes. He was able to experiment with colors, textures, and typography in ways he never thought possible. And when he found a design he liked, Ai gave him suggestions for variations and improvements.

The result? A stunning Instagram campaign that showcased the new sneaker line in a way that no one had ever seen before.

Real Life Examples of Ai Assisted Design

Don Allen III is just one of many creatives who have benefited from Ai assisted design. Here are a few other examples:

  • Adobe Sensei - Adobe's Ai tool that helps designers with everything from image selection to layout.
  • Autodesk's AI-Powered Sketching - An Ai tool that can create 3D designs from 2D sketches.
  • Qlik's AI Augmented Analytics - A tool that can help users identify patterns and insights in their data.

Conclusion

As Ai technology continues to evolve, we can expect to see more and more Ai assisted design solutions being developed. While some designers are skeptical about the impact of Ai on their industry, the reality is that Ai is here to stay and is already helping creatives work faster and smarter.

Here are three critical takeaways:

  1. Ai assisted design can help designers generate ideas and create designs faster than ever before, allowing them to focus on the creative process instead of the mechanical aspects.
  2. Ai can also help designers explore new design possibilities by generating variations and suggestions that they may not have considered otherwise.
  3. Despite the benefits, Ai assisted design is not a replacement for human creativity and intuition. It's important for designers to learn how to collaborate with Ai rather than relying on it completely.
