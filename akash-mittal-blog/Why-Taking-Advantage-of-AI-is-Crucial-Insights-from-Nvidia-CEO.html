Why Taking Advantage of AI is Crucial: Insights from Nvidia CEO

Stories, Examples, and Practical Tips for Graduates

The Wake-up Call from Nvidia CEO

At a recent graduation ceremony at the University of California, Berkeley, Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang delivered a wake-up call to the graduates. He warned them that they would be left behind if they did not take advantage of AI - which he called "the single most powerful force of our time." Huang told the graduates that they have the opportunity to shape the future with AI, or they will become "consumers of it, instead of architects of it."

Huang's message is not just for the graduates, but for everyone who wants to keep up in the fast-changing world of technology. AI is changing the way we work, learn, and live, and those who ignore or resist it will be at a disadvantage.

the Power of AI

AI is not just a buzzword or a futuristic concept - it is already transforming industries and societies around the world. Here are some quantifiable examples of the power of AI:

  • AI-powered virtual assistants like Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant have become a ubiquitous part of our lives, with over 49 million smart speakers sold worldwide in Q1 2019 alone, according to Canalys. These devices are not just convenient and entertaining, but also useful for tasks like setting reminders, ordering food, or controlling smart home devices.
  • AI is also making a big impact in healthcare, where it is used for tasks like diagnosing diseases, discovering new drugs, and predicting medical outcomes. For example, Google's DeepMind has developed an AI system that can detect over 50 eye diseases from retinal scans with 94% accuracy, which is better than most human doctors.
  • AI is also changing the way we travel, with self-driving cars, trucks, and drones becoming more common. These vehicles not only offer more safety and convenience, but also have the potential to reduce traffic congestion, energy consumption, and carbon emissions.

The Three-point Conclusion on AI for Graduates

So what can graduates, and everyone else, do to take advantage of AI and avoid being left behind? Here are three points to consider:

  1. Learn the basics of AI. You don't have to be a computer science major or a math genius to understand AI. There are plenty of online courses, tutorials, and books that can help you grasp the concepts and applications of AI. Some recommended resources include Udacity, Coursera, edX, and the book "Machine Learning Yearning" by Andrew Ng.
  2. Apply AI to your field of interest. Whether you are a writer, a designer, a scientist, or a musician, there is a way to use AI to enhance your skills and productivity. For example, you can use AI tools like Grammarly, Canva, TensorFlow, or DeepDream to create better content, visualize data, or generate new ideas. By incorporating AI into your work, you can stay ahead of the curve and stand out from the competition.
  3. Collaborate with others who know AI. AI is not a solo game - it requires teamwork, diversity, and creativity. By partnering with people from different backgrounds, cultures, and perspectives, you can gain new insights and skills that will help you tackle complex problems and achieve great results. Whether it is joining an AI club, attending a hackathon, or contributing to an open-source project, you can build your network and make a difference.

and Case Studies on AI
