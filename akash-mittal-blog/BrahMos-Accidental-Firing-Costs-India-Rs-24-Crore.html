BrahMos Accidental Firing Costs India Rs 24 Crore

A recent incident involving the BrahMos missile has caused quite a stir in India. The missile, capable of supersonic speeds, accidentally fired and landed in Pakistan. The cost of the incident to India? A whopping Rs 24 crore!

The above incident is just one example of the high costs associated with military hardware. Here are a few more:

  • The Indian Air Force's Rafale deal with France is estimated to cost Rs 59,000 crore
  • The INS Vikrant, India's first indigenous aircraft carrier, cost Rs 23,000 crore to build
  • The Indian Army is set to acquire 464 T-90 tanks from Russia for Rs 13,448 crore

  1. Military hardware is expensive - incidents like the BrahMos missile firing only add to the costs
  2. The Indian government should ensure that proper safety protocols are in place to prevent such incidents from occurring in the future
  3. The high cost of military hardware highlights the need for India to invest in indigenous research and development capabilities
