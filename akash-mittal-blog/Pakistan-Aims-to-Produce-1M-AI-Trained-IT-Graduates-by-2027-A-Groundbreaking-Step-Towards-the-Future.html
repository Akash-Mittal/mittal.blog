Pakistan Aims to Produce 1M AI Trained IT Graduates by 2027: A Groundbreaking Step Towards the Future

As the world is rapidly moving towards an AI-driven economy, Pakistan is stepping up its game to play its part. The government is taking a groundbreaking step to produce 1 million AI trained IT graduates by 2027. This initiative is aimed at boosting the country's IT and AI industries.

Examples of AI Applications in Pakistan

Pakistan's IT and AI industries have already made significant progress, both at the national and global level. Here are some quantifiable examples of AI applications in Pakistan:

  • Healthcare: AI is being used to diagnose and treat various diseases, including cancer. The Institute of Radiotherapy and Nuclear Medicine (IRNUM) in Islamabad uses AI to detect early-stage cancer through MRI scans.
  • Education: AI is being used in Pakistan to improve the quality of education. The Punjab Information Technology Board (PITB) has launched an AI-powered learning management system (LMS) that helps students learn at their own pace.
  • Finance: AI is being used in the banking sector to detect fraud and create personalized financial plans. HBL, Pakistan's largest bank, has integrated AI into its operations to provide better financial services to its customers.
