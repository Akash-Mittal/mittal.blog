Microsoft Misusing its Data: The Foreboding AI Battle

By NBC News

Imagine a world where your every move is being tracked and analyzed by technology without your knowledge or consent. A world where your personal data is sold to the highest bidder, without any consideration for your privacy or security. This is the world we live in today, and it's only getting worse.

Recently, Twitter users have accused Microsoft of misusing its data, bringing to light the increasing need for transparency and ethics in the use of technology. The accusations foreshadow a fight over the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in our daily lives.

Microsoft's data misuse accusations are just the tip of the iceberg. It is estimated that 2.5 quintillion bytes of data are created every day, and a significant portion of that data is being used without our knowledge or consent. Consider the following examples:

  • Facebook's Cambridge Analytica scandal in which user data was obtained to influence the 2016 US presidential election.
  • Google's personalized search results which show you content based on your browsing history, potentially limiting your exposure to diverse perspectives.
  • Amazon's recommendation engine which suggests products based on your purchase history, potentially reinforcing your existing biases and limiting exploration of new ideas.

All of these examples highlight the importance of transparency and ethics in the use of technology.
