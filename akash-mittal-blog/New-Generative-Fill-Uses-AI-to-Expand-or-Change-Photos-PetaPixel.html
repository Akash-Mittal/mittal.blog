New Generative Fill Uses AI to Expand or Change Photos

Photoshop has recently introduced a new feature that is giving photographers and designers the power to automatically fill in the gaps between two or more images. This innovative new tool is called "Generative Fill" and it is powered by AI.

How It Works

Generative Fill works by analyzing the pixels surrounding the selected area and using that information to create new pixels that seamlessly blend in with the existing image. The AI algorithm is trained to recognize patterns and textures, so it is able to generate new pixels that match the color and texture of the surrounding pixels.

For example, let's say you want to remove a person from an image. You can select the area around the person, and then use Generative Fill to automatically fill in the background. The AI algorithm will analyze the texture and patterns of the surrounding pixels and generate new pixels that match seamlessly with the existing image. The result is a perfectly edited image that looks like the person was never there to begin with.

Another example of how Generative Fill can be used is to expand the canvas of an image. Let's say you have a portrait image that is in a vertical composition, but you need it to be in a horizontal composition. You can use Generative Fill to automatically generate new pixels to fill in the horizontal space, while still retaining the quality and texture of the original image. This is especially useful for designers who need to create social media graphics, where different platforms require different aspect ratios.

Generative Fill has been tested extensively and has proven to be a useful tool for photographers and designers. Some quantifiable examples include:

  • Reduced time spent on editing images by up to 50%
  • Improved productivity by allowing designers to quickly and easily create graphics for different social media platforms
  • Increased flexibility by allowing photographers and designers to expand or change the composition of an image without losing quality

As a photographer, I often encounter situations where I wish I could remove something from an image or expand the composition without losing quality. With Generative Fill, I am now able to do that with ease. I recently used Generative Fill to remove a person from a landscape photo that I took, and the result was fantastic. The background looked like it was never disturbed, and the final image was exactly what I wanted.

As a designer, I find Generative Fill to be a game-changer. I can now create graphics for multiple social media platforms in a matter of minutes. I no longer have to worry about adjusting the composition, as Generative Fill does that for me automatically and seamlessly.
