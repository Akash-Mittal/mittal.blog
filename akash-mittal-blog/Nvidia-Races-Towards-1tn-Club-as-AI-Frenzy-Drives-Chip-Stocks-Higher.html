Nvidia Races Towards 1tn Club as AI Frenzy Drives Chip Stocks Higher

When it comes to artificial intelligence (AI), Nvidia is a name that is consistently at the top of the list. The company's graphics processing units (GPUs) are not only the go-to choice for gamers, but also a critical component for companies looking to harness the power of AI. With AI becoming more integral to industries like healthcare, finance, and transportation, Nvidia's stock has seen a remarkable surge in value.

But this success hasn't come overnight. Nvidia actually started out as a maker of graphics chips for gaming in the 1990s. It wasn't until the mid-2000s that the company began to pivot towards the market for high-performance computing, which includes supercomputers, data centers, and, of course, AI. Nvidia's early investments in this area have paid off tremendously, with the company's market cap currently sitting at over $800 billion.

  • In Q2 2021, Nvidia's revenue was up 68% year-over-year, driven in large part by its data center and gaming businesses.
  • Nvidia's market cap has more than tripled since the beginning of 2020, when it was valued at around $250 billion.
  • The AI chip market is projected to reach $83 billion by 2027, with Nvidia holding a significant share of that market.

One company that has benefited from Nvidia's AI technology is Blue River Technology, a California-based startup that uses computer vision to identify and spray herbicide on weeds in crops. By using Nvidia GPUs for its AI algorithms, Blue River has been able to significantly reduce the amount of herbicide used, resulting in healthier crops and a more sustainable farming operation.

Another case study comes from the healthcare industry, where Nvidia's technology is being used to improve patient outcomes. One example is Paige.AI, a startup that has developed an AI tool for detecting cancer in pathology images. By using Nvidia GPUs to power its machine learning algorithms, Paige.AI is able to process images much faster and more accurately than traditional methods, potentially saving lives in the process.
