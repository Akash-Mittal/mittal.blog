An Ethical Debacle: The Demise of SBF Game and the Problematic Sign-up Process of Web3 Games

A Lesson on Ethical Gaming and Technological Advancements

Imagine playing an online game and being rewarded with virtual currency, which in turn could be sold for real cash. This was the premise of SBF Game, a decentralized finance game that allowed players to earn cryptocurrency through in-game achievements. Sounds promising, right? But then came the ethical dilemma.

The Collapse of SBF Game

SBF Game was developed by a group of anonymous programmers who called themselves the Scammers Behind Fire. Despite its name, SBF Game had built a loyal following and became one of the most popular Web3 games. However, on June 28, 2021, SBF Game suddenly shut down, leaving players with no warning and losing their virtual currency investments. The reason behind the shutdown was a lack of ethical consideration in the game's economic model, as players were incentivized to recruit new players who would later be scammed. The game's developers also profited from this scheme.

The Problematic Sign-up Process of Web3 Games

Web3 games, like SBF Game, operate using blockchain technology, which provides transparency and security to its users. However, the sign-up process for these games may leave much to be desired. Most of them require players to have a digital wallet and own cryptocurrency, which can be a complicated process for those unfamiliar with technology. The lack of proper information may lead to players being scammed by fake websites or wallets.

Real-Life Examples of Ethical and Unethical Web3 Games

While SBF Game may have been an ethical disaster, there are Web3 games out there that strive for ethical excellence. For example, Axie Infinity, a game that allows players to breed, battle, and trade fantasy creatures, has a well-defined economic model and rewards players who have long-term investments in their game. Likewise, Tomb Chaser, a puzzle-escape game with cryptocurrency rewards, has a fair and transparent distribution model that does not incentivize players to recruit others.

On the other hand, other Web3 games may have questionable ethical and moral practices. For instance, Web3 Gamer, a game that allows players to earn cryptocurrency through in-game achievements, has a referral system that can be manipulated and benefits only those who recruit the most players. As such, it is important for players to be critical of the games they play and to strive for ethical alternatives.

Conclusion

The collapse of SBF Game should serve as a warning for the Web3 gaming industry. As technological advancements continue to shape the online gaming world, it is crucial to prioritize ethics and avoid schemes that are built for the profit of the few at the expense of the many. Furthermore, Web3 games must consider their sign-up processes and make sure that they are accessible and secure for all players.
