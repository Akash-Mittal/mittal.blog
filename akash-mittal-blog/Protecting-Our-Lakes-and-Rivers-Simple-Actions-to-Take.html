Protecting Our Lakes and Rivers: Simple Actions to Take

August 25, 2021

Have you ever gone swimming in a lake or rafting down a river and been amazed at the crystal-clear waters and stunning natural surroundings? Maybe you've caught fish or gone camping on the shore, enjoying the peacefulness of a pristine environment. Unfortunately, many of our lakes and rivers are facing serious threats from pollution, climate change, and invasive species. But there is hope. By taking a few simple actions, we can help protect these precious resources and make sure they are available for future generations to enjoy.

Here are some quantifiable examples of how our lakes and rivers are being impacted:

  • Over 40% of lakes in the US are considered polluted, according to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).
  • Algal blooms caused by excess nutrients from agriculture and wastewater treatment plants have led to toxic conditions in many bodies of water, including Lake Erie, which experienced a major crisis in 2014.
  • Invasive species like zebra mussels and Asian carp can devastate native ecosystems and cost billions of dollars in damage and control efforts.
  • Climate change is altering water temperatures, precipitation patterns, and flow rates, affecting the health of fish populations and water quality.

Simple Actions to Take

Here are some simple actions you can take to help protect our lakes and rivers:

  1. Reduce your use of fertilizers and pesticides. These chemicals can run off into nearby waterways and cause harmful algal blooms and other water quality issues.
  2. Dispose of trash properly. Litter can end up in rivers and lakes, harming wildlife and spoiling the natural beauty of these areas. Always pack out what you pack in when camping or hiking, and never dump waste or chemicals into the water.
  3. Support conservation efforts. Join a local environmental group or donate to a charity that works to protect lakes and rivers. You can also participate in clean-up events and spread awareness about the importance of conserving these natural resources.
  4. Be mindful of your water usage. Conserving water not only helps the environment, but also saves you money on your water bill. Repair leaks, turn off the faucet when brushing your teeth, and use a broom instead of a hose to clean your driveway or sidewalk.
  5. Prevent the spread of invasive species. Clean your equipment and gear before and after entering a body of water to avoid spreading unwanted plants and animals. Also, avoid releasing pets or aquarium plants into natural waterways.

By taking these actions, you can make a significant difference in the health and beauty of our lakes and rivers.

Conclusion

In conclusion, protecting our lakes and rivers is crucial for the health of our environment and the enjoyment of future generations. By reducing our use of harmful chemicals, properly disposing of trash, supporting conservation efforts, conserving water, and preventing the spread of invasive species, we can make a positive impact. Let's work together to ensure that our lakes and rivers remain clean, healthy, and beautiful for years to come.

  1. Reduce use of fertilizers and pesticides
  2. Dispose of trash properly
  3. Support conservation efforts
