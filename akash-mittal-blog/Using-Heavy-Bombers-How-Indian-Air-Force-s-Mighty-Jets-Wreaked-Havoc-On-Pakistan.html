Using Heavy Bombers: How Indian Air Force's Mighty Jets Wreaked Havoc On Pakistan

On February 26, 2019, Indian Air Force's (IAF) mighty jets carried out a series of airstrikes on targets in Pakistan. The surprise attack, conducted in response to the Pulwama terror attack that claimed the lives of 40 Indian CRPF personnel on February 14, caused significant damage to the Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM) terrorist training camps located in Balakot, Chakothi, and Muzaffarabad. The use of heavy bombers, such as the Mirage 2000, played a crucial role in the success of the mission.

The Role of Heavy Bombers

During the mission, the IAF used Mirage 2000 jets, which are known for their ability to carry a wide variety of weapons. The Mirage 2000 is equipped with a laser targeting pod and can carry laser-guided bombs, making it a powerful weapon in precision strikes. The jets also have a range of up to 4,000 km, making them highly suitable for long-range missions. The IAF used the Mirage 2000 to drop SPICE-2000 bombs, which are capable of penetrating reinforced concrete structures, to destroy the terror camps in Pakistan.

The use of heavy bombers was critical to the success of the mission, as it allowed the IAF to deliver a high volume of firepower on targets in a short period of time. The precision strikes helped to minimize collateral damage and civilian deaths. The use of heavy bombers was also an effective psychological tactic, as it demonstrated India's determination to take decisive action against Pakistan-based terrorists.

The use of heavy bombers by the IAF during the 2019 airstrikes in Pakistan resulted in the destruction of multiple terror training camps. The bombing was so precise that it only took 12 bombs to destroy the targets. According to a statement by India's Foreign Secretary, the airstrikes resulted in the elimination of a "large number" of terrorists and trainers of JeM. The attack also sent a strong message to Pakistan and the international community about India's commitment to combat terrorism.

Furthermore, the use of heavy bombers by the IAF demonstrated the superior capabilities of Indian weapons and technology. The Mirage 2000, in particular, has proven to be a highly effective weapon in precision strikes, and its success during the Balakot airstrikes has garnered international attention and praise.
