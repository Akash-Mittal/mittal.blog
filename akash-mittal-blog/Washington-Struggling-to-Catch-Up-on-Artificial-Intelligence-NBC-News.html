Washington Struggling to Catch Up on Artificial Intelligence

By NBC News

Published on September 3, 2021

The rise of artificial intelligence (AI) has transformed countless industries, from healthcare and robotics to transportation and finance. With AI becoming more prevalent in everyday life, it's no surprise that policymakers in Washington are looking for ways to navigate the rapidly=evolving technological landscape.

Washington's Slow Adoption of AI

Despite the importance of AI, Washington has been slow to adopt the technology. The federal government has been criticized for falling behind other countries in terms of AI development, and there is a growing concern that the United States risks losing its competitive edge in this field.

Part of the problem is that government agencies have been slow to adapt to new technological developments. This is not a new problem; the government has traditionally been behind the private sector when it comes to technology adoption. However, with AI creating new opportunities and threats, it's becoming more important for Washington to catch up with the private sector.

The Cost of Falling Behind

The cost of falling behind in AI can be significant. AI has the potential to drive innovation and economic growth, but only if it is developed in a way that is safe and ethical. Without proper oversight and investment, AI could lead to unintended consequences, such as job displacement, algorithmic bias, and security threats.

Moreover, AI could have major implications for national security. Countries with advanced AI capabilities could gain a military advantage over those without, and there is a risk that AI could be used as a tool for cyberattacks or other malicious activities. Therefore, it's crucial for Washington to prioritize AI development and take steps to ensure a safe and ethical implementation of the technology.

Steps to Catch Up on AI

So, what can Washington do to catch up on AI? Here are a few steps that could help:

  • Invest in AI research and development: The federal government should allocate more resources to AI research and development. This could involve funding for academic and private sector research, as well as investments in AI infrastructure and workforce development.
  • Establish a national AI strategy: The government should develop a national strategy for AI, which would include guidelines for the development and deployment of the technology. This would help ensure that AI is developed in a way that is both safe and ethical.
  • Collaborate with the private sector: The federal government should work closely with the private sector to identify areas where AI can be applied to solve problems or create new opportunities. This could involve partnerships between government agencies and tech companies, as well as initiatives to encourage the adoption of AI across different sectors of the economy.

Conclusion

Washington's slow adoption of AI is a cause for concern, given the potential benefits and risks associated with the technology. To catch up, the federal government will need to invest in AI research and development, establish a national AI strategy, and collaborate with the private sector to identify new opportunities and ensure a safe and ethical implementation of the technology.

By doing so, Washington can help ensure that the United States remains at the forefront of AI development and can reap the benefits of this transformative technology.
