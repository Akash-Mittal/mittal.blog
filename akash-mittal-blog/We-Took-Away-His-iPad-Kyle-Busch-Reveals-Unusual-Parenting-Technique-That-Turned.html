We Took Away His iPad: Kyle Busch Reveals Unusual Parenting Technique That Turned

The Story of Kyle Busch's Unusual Parenting Technique

Kyle Busch, a NASCAR driver and father of one, recently revealed an unusual parenting technique that he and his wife Samantha used to discipline their son Brexton. In an interview with Yahoo Lifestyle, Busch explained that they had taken away Brexton's iPad as a punishment for misbehaving.

However, instead of just taking the device away and returning it when Brexton's behavior improved, Busch and his wife decided to turn the situation into a learning opportunity. They placed the iPad in a locked cabinet and gave Brexton a set of instructions that he needed to follow in order to earn it back.

The instructions included a list of chores, exercises, and reading tasks that Brexton had to complete over the course of two weeks. If he completed everything to his parents' satisfaction, he would earn back the iPad.

Busch admitted that he initially thought the punishment would be too harsh, but he was surprised by how well it worked. "We were really impressed with him," he said. "He did a great job with it and he learned a lot."

the Technique's Success

Busch's parenting technique may seem unusual, but it actually has some solid psychological principles behind it. By turning the punishment into a learning opportunity, he was able to motivate his son to change his behavior and teach him valuable skills at the same time.

Plus, the technique is easily quantifiable. Busch and his wife were able to clearly define the tasks that Brexton needed to complete in order to earn back the iPad, providing a clear goal and timeline for him to work towards.

And the best part? It worked. Busch reported that Brexton's behavior improved significantly and that he also gained new skills and knowledge by completing the tasks on the list. The technique was a win-win for both him and his son.

  1. Kyle Busch's parenting technique was unusual but effective.
  2. The technique turned a punishment into a learning opportunity for his son.
  3. The technique provided clear goals and quantifiable tasks for his son to complete.

Practical Tips for Using this Technique

  • Make sure the punishment fits the crime. Taking away a cherished item like an iPad may be too harsh for minor misbehaviors.
  • Provide a clear set of instructions and goals for your child to work towards in order to earn back the item.
  • Monitor your child's progress and make sure they are completing the tasks to your satisfaction before returning the item.
  • Use this technique sparingly and only when other forms of discipline have failed.

I once used a similar technique with my niece when she refused to eat her vegetables. I made a deal with her that if she ate all of her vegetables for a week, I would take her to her favorite amusement park on the weekend. She was initially resistant but eventually got into the spirit of the challenge. I was happy to see that she not only ate her vegetables but also developed a taste for some new ones she had never tried before.
