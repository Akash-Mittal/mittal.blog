Polestar Smart: Opening the Door to EV Partnerships

As the electric vehicle market continues to grow at an unprecedented rate, automakers are starting to realize the value in collaboration. One company leading the charge in this new era of partnership is Polestar Smart.

Polestar Smart is a joint venture between Volvo Cars and Geely, created in 2017 with the goal of developing electric performance cars. Since then, the company has released a handful of successful models, including the Polestar 2, which is widely regarded as one of the best EVs on the market.

But Polestar Smart isn't content to simply rest on its laurels. In recent months, the company has been actively seeking out new partnerships with other companies in the industry. Why Partnership Matters

There are a few key reasons that automakers are beginning to explore more collaborative approaches to developing electric vehicles. One is that the technology is still relatively new, and there's a lot to learn. By working with other companies in the industry, automakers can share knowledge and resources, which can lead to faster innovation and better products.

Another reason is that the EV market is highly competitive. There are dozens of automakers vying for market share, and the companies that come out on top will be those that can create the best products. Collaboration can help companies achieve this by bringing together different strengths and areas of expertise.

Finally, partnerships can help to spread the cost of developing electric vehicles. These vehicles often require significant investment, and partnerships can help companies to pool resources and minimize risk.

Quantifiable Examples

Polestar Smart has already begun to see the benefits of collaboration. In 2020, the company partnered with Waymo, Google's self-driving car branch, to develop a fully autonomous electric car. The resulting vehicle, which is based on the Polestar 2 platform, is set to debut later this year.

This is just one example of a partnership that has helped both companies to move forward with their goals. Waymo brings its expertise in autonomous driving technology, while Polestar Smart offers a sophisticated EV platform. Together, the two companies can create a vehicle that is greater than the sum of its parts.

Another example of Polestar Smart's collaborative approach is its use of modular platforms. The company has designed its vehicles to be highly adaptable, with a modular architecture that can accommodate a wide range of components. This approach makes it easier for the company to partner with other companies on specific projects, as it can integrate new components quickly and efficiently.
