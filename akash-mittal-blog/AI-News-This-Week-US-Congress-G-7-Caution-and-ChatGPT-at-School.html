A friend of mine recently shared an interesting story about how AI is being used in education. At their children's school, they have started using an AI-powered chatbot called ChatGPT to provide personalized tutoring to students.

ChatGPT is a language model that was trained on a large corpus of text data. It can understand natural language and generate responses to questions and statements. The chatbot is able to understand a student's strengths and weaknesses and provide personalized tutoring based on their individual needs.

Since the school started using ChatGPT, my friend's children have noticed a significant improvement in their grades. They are able to ask the chatbot questions whenever they don't understand something and get immediate feedback. The chatbot is always available, so they can study at their own pace, whenever they want.

The benefits of AI are clear, but there are also some concerns that need to be addressed. This week, both the US Congress and the G7 expressed caution about the use of AI.

The US Congress held a hearing on Tuesday to discuss the impact of AI on the workforce. Some lawmakers expressed concern that AI could lead to job losses, particularly in industries such as trucking and manufacturing. Others argued that AI could create new job opportunities.

The G7 also expressed caution about the use of AI. In a statement released on Monday, the group of seven industrialized nations called for the development of ethical guidelines for the use of AI. The statement also emphasized the need to ensure that AI is used for the benefit of all people.
