Why Kate Middleton's Go-To Parenting Move is Worth Emulating

During a recent royal engagement, the Duchess of Cambridge, Kate Middleton, relied on her go-to parenting move as she interacted with children and parents. As she engaged with them, it was evident that her parenting style was a combination of warmth, grace, and attentiveness.

As a parent, it's not always easy to know what to do in certain situations. However, Kate shows us how effective and practical parenting strategies can be.

Examples of Kate's Go-To Parenting Move

Kate Middleton's go-to parenting move is quite simple but impactful; get down to the child's level. No matter what the situation, Kate always takes her time to kneel down, squat, or sit down on the floor to be at the same eye level with the children.

During the Royal Foundation Forum, Kate sat on the floor to get close to the children and their parents as they chatted. She also knelt down as she heard the children's sweet little secrets during her visits to hospitals, schools, and care homes.

Getting down to the child's level has its benefits. Firstly, it makes children feel seen and heard. Secondly, it enhances the bond between the parent and child. Lastly, it builds trust and positivity towards the parent, which can translate into positive behavior and actions from the child.

Why is Kate's Parenting Move Worth Emulating?

Kate's parenting move of getting down to the child's level is worth emulating for the following reasons:

1. It Enhances Communication with the Child

Children often feel belittled or ignored when they have to talk to adults who stand over them. By going down to the child's level, Kate creates a comfortable environment for children to express themselves fully. She engages with them in a way that promotes healthy and positive communication, which is vital for children's development.

2. It Fosters Trust and Respect from the Child

Going down to a child's level also fosters trust and respect from the child. By doing this, Kate shows the child that she values them as individuals, and she's listening to what they have to say. The child then trusts and respects her more, leading to positive emotions towards Kate. This positivity reflects on their actions, behavior, and attitudes hence producing positive outcomes.

3. It Builds a Strong Connection with the Child

Connection between a parent and a child is crucial for healthy development. By getting down to the child's level, Kate fosters a personal connection that strengthens the bond between her and the child. This connection promotes healthy communication between them, and the child finds Kate approachable and supportive, leading to a positive outlook on life since a child feels comfortable expressing themselves.

How Can You Apply This Move in Your Parenting?

Kate Middleton's go-to parenting move is not complicated to apply in your parenting. Here are some ways in which you can apply this parenting move:

1. Kneel down or squat when listening to your child

When your child speaks to you, try to kneel down or squat to be at the same level as your child. You can also sit down alongside them while you listen to them talk. This position allows you to connect personally with them, which enhances communication, trust building, and connection.

2. Encourage other trusted adults to do the same

Encourage other people who engage with your children frequently to use this move. Grandparents, teachers, and babysitters, for instance, can use this strategy to enhance their relationship with your children.

3. Be open-minded and attentive

When you get down to your child's level, make sure that you're open-minded and attentive to what they're saying. Engage with them, listening to them carefully, and be present with them (put away the phone or any other distractions when talking with them). This attentiveness helps you connect with them and foster a positive relationship.

Conclusion

Kate Middleton's go-to parenting move of getting down to the child's level is quite effective in various areas of parenting, such as communication (fostering healthy communication), trust building (fostering trust and respect from the child), and connection (building a strong connection between the parent and the child).

Applying this parenting move is worth emulating as it can have positive outcomes in your child's development. By kneeling down or squatting when communicating with your child and being open-minded and attentive, you can build a strong, positive relationship that leads to healthy emotion and behavior in your child.
