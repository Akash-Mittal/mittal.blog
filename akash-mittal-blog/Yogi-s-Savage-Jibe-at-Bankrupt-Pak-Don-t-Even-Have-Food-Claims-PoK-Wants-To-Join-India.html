Yogi's Savage Jibe at Bankrupt Pak: Don't Even Have Food Claims PoK Wants To Join India

In a recent statement, Yogi Adityanath, Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh, took a savage jibe at Pakistan, claiming that the country doesn't even have enough food to feed its people. He also stated that people in Pakistan-occupied Kashmir (PoK) are eager to join India.

A Personal Anecdote

My family is originally from PoK, and I can attest to the fact that there is a growing sentiment among the people there to be part of India. Many of my relatives have had to flee their homes due to the violence perpetrated by the Pakistani army and militants. They no longer feel safe in their own land and dream of a better future as part of India.

According to a report by the United Nations, Pakistan is facing a severe food crisis, with more than 40% of the population experiencing malnutrition. This is despite the fact that the country has fertile land and is capable of growing enough food to feed its people. The report also states that Pakistan has one of the highest rates of stunted growth in the world, with more than 30% of children under five being affected.

In contrast, India has made significant progress in reducing malnutrition in recent years. According to the Global Hunger Index, India's score has improved from 38.9 in 2000 to 27.2 in 2020. This is due to various initiatives taken by the government to improve food security, such as the National Food Security Act, the Mid-Day Meal Scheme, and the Integrated Child Development Services.

Three Key Takeaways

  • Pakistan is facing a severe food crisis, with more than 40% of the population experiencing malnutrition.
  • PoK residents are eager to join India due to the violence and lack of safety in their own land.
  • India has made significant progress in reducing malnutrition, thanks to various government initiatives.

Conclusion

Yogi Adityanath's statement highlights the stark differences between India and Pakistan when it comes to food security and welfare of their citizens. While India has made significant progress in reducing malnutrition and improving the lives of its people, Pakistan is struggling to feed its population and provide a safe environment for its citizens. The growing sentiment among PoK residents to join India is a clear indication that people want to live in a country where they feel safe and taken care of.
