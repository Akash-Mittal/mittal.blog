Tom Hanks Talks Possibility of AI Version of Him Acting After Death

By USA Today

Tom Hanks is known for his incredible acting talents and has brought countless characters to life on the big screen. However, in a recent interview with The Guardian, he discussed a rather interesting possibility - the idea of creating an AI version of himself to act in films after his death.

According to Hanks, "We're actually building enough data from my many movies that they will feed the AI version of me and he will be able to play any role that has been in my repertoire. You know, digitally they can just recreate every aspect of who you are and reproduce it onscreen. Forever."

While the idea of an AI version of an actor may seem far-fetched, it raises important questions about the future of movie-making and the role artificial intelligence will play in the entertainment industry.

AI and machine learning are already being used in various parts of the movie-making process. For example:

  • AI algorithms are used to analyze script data and predict which movies will be successful at the box office.
  • AI-powered editing tools can suggest changes to improve pacing, lighting, and more.
  • AI-generated visual effects can create hyper-realistic scenes that would be impossible to film in real life.

These examples demonstrate the potential for AI to change the way movies are made, and the idea of an AI actor raises even more possibilities.
