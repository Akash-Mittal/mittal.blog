An Interesting Story: From Hobbyist to Innovator

In 2011, a group of MIT students and researchers founded Formlabs, with the goal of making 3D printing accessible to everyone. They started by creating the Form 1, a small, affordable desktop printer that quickly gained a following among hobbyists and tinkerers. However, they soon realized that there was a much larger market for professional-grade 3D printers that could produce complex geometries, accurate details, and durable parts.

After years of research, development, and collaboration with top industrial designers and engineers, Formlabs released the Form 3 in 2019, their latest and greatest 3D printer. It boasts a wide range of advanced features and improvements over its predecessors, including:

  • Improved resin tanks and cartridges that reduce waste, mess, and maintenance;
  • A larger build volume that allows for more complex and sizable prints;
  • Automatic resin level sensing and real-time print monitoring that eliminate guesswork and downtime;
  • Adaptive layer height technology that balances speed and detail for optimal performance;
  • Cloud-based software and a touchscreen interface that streamline workflow and increase collaboration;
  • And more.

So far, the Form 3 has received rave reviews from users across various industries, such as architecture, engineering, medicine, jewelry, and education. Let's take a closer look at what makes it such a game-changer in the world of 3D printing.

Some Quantifiable Examples: Precision, Speed, and Reliability

To truly understand the value and potential of the Form 3, one must see and touch the prints it produces. Here are some examples of what it can do:

  • Print intricate dental models with 20-micron layer thickness that rival the accuracy of milling;
  • Create lifelike prosthetics and orthotics with smooth surfaces, fine textures, and subtle gradients;
  • Produce detailed architectural prototypes and models with precise dimensions, curves, and angles;
  • Fabricate jewelry pieces with intricate designs, delicate features, and subtle variations;
  • Print educational models and tools that demonstrate complex concepts and structures in vivid detail;
  • And more.

Moreover, the Form 3 can accomplish these feats in a fraction of the time and cost of traditional manufacturing methods, such as injection molding, CNC machining, or casting. It can also handle a variety of materials, including standard, flexible, durable, and biocompatible resins, as well as experimental ones that push the boundaries of what's possible.

Perhaps even more impressively, the Form 3 can do all this with minimal intervention or expertise from the user. Its intuitive software and hardware combine to create a seamless and stress-free printing experience, from setup to support.
