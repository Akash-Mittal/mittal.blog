Live Action Little Mermaid: AI Renders Cast As Animated

How AI technology is making waves in Hollywood

Once upon a time, Hollywood actors gave life to timeless animation classics that captured the imaginations of both children and adults alike. However, with advancements in AI technology, animating human actors has become a lot easier and more accurate.

The Little Mermaid Cast

The most recent example of this technology in action is the upcoming live-action adaptation of "The Little Mermaid," where AI was used to render the cast of human actors as animated underwater creatures. The cast includes Halle Bailey as Ariel, Harry Styles as Prince Eric, and Melissa McCarthy as Ursula the Sea Witch.

The AI rendering technology allowed the animators to create accurate and lifelike characters that stay true to the original animation style while also retaining the unique traits of each actor. This is a massive breakthrough for the film industry, and could lead to a new generation of animated features that combine real-life performances with animated characters.

  • The Little Mermaid cast looks almost identical to their animated counterparts, with subtle differences that make them unique.
  • The AI rendering technology saved significant time and expense in post-production, as the animators didn't need to manually animate each character frame-by-frame.
  • The technology allows for a greater range of creative expression and artistic vision, as the animators can adjust and tweak the characters based on the actors' performances.
