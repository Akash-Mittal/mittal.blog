Can ChatGPT Replace UX Researchers? - An Empirical Analysis of Comment Classifications

Imagine you are a UX researcher trying to analyze feedback from customers on a new feature in your app. You have hundreds of comments to go through, and it will take hours to categorize each one. But what if you could use a Chatbot like ChatGPT to automatically classify those comments? Sounds interesting, right?

A recent study conducted by a team of researchers from the University of Washington found that ChatGPT was able to classify a large number of comments with a high degree of accuracy. They used a dataset of 10,000 customer comments on various apps and asked ChatGPT to classify them into different categories such as usability, functionality, and design. The accuracy rate was around 85%, which is impressive considering the fact that it was an unsupervised learning process.

Practical Tips

  • When using ChatGPT, it's important to prepare the dataset properly and choose the right categories to classify the comments into.
  • It's also important to check the accuracy of the results manually and make corrections if necessary.
  • ChatGPT should be used as a supplementary tool to the research process, and not as a replacement for human insights and expertise.
