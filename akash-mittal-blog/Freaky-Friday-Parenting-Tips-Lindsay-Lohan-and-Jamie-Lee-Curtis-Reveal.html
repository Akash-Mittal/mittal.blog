The Parenting Lessons I Learned from Lindsay Lohan and Jamie Lee Curtis in Freaky Friday

Have you ever watched a movie and thought, "Wow, I could really apply those lessons to my own life?" That's exactly how I felt when I saw the Disney classic Freaky Friday, starring Lindsay Lohan and Jamie Lee Curtis. In case you haven't seen it (though I highly recommend you do), the movie is about a mother and daughter who switch bodies for a day. Through their hilarious misadventures and eventual heartfelt realizations, they learn some valuable lessons about parenting.

Lesson 1: Communication is Key

Throughout the movie, we see the mother and daughter frustrated with each other, often feeling like the other doesn't understand them. Once they switch bodies, they're forced to communicate in ways they hadn't before. They start really listening to each other, and as a result, they gain a greater understanding of where the other is coming from.

This is a lesson that can be applied to any relationship, but especially with our children. It can be easy to fall into a pattern of just talking at them, rather than with them. But if we take the time to really listen and communicate effectively, we can build stronger bonds and better understand our kids.

For example, I used to get frustrated with my son when he would have a tantrum. But once I started asking him questions about what was really bothering him, I discovered that often it had nothing to do with the thing that had set him off. By communicating with him and getting to the root of his emotions, we were able to come up with solutions together.

Lesson 2: Kids Need Independence

One of the things the daughter in Freaky Friday struggles with is feeling like she doesn't have any say in her own life. Her mother is constantly telling her what to do and how to act. Once they switch bodies, the daughter relishes in her newfound independence, while the mother realizes how much she was stifling her daughter.

While it's important to set boundaries and guidelines for our kids, it's equally important to allow them to have some independence and make decisions for themselves. This can help them build confidence and independence, and it allows them to learn from their mistakes.

For example, when my daughter started middle school, I was worried about her getting lost in the crowd and making the wrong friends. But I realized I couldn't control every aspect of her life. Instead, I focused on having open communication with her and allowing her to make some decisions for herself. She's now thriving in school and has made some amazing new friends.

Lesson 3: It's Never Too Late to Change

In the movie, the mother realizes that she's been so focused on her career that she's neglected her relationship with her daughter. Similarly, the daughter realizes how much her mother has sacrificed for her and gains a newfound appreciation for her. Through their body switch, they're able to see things from a different perspective and make positive changes in their lives.

It's never too late to change our parenting style or to improve our relationships with our kids. Even if we've made mistakes in the past, we can always make a conscious effort to be better in the future. It's important to acknowledge our shortcomings and work on improving them.

For example, I used to be a yeller when I got really frustrated with my kids. But I realized that wasn't a positive or effective way to communicate. So I started practicing mindfulness and breathing techniques to help me better manage my emotions and communicate more calmly with my kids. It's been a tough journey, but I've seen improvements in my relationships with them.

In Conclusion

  1. Communication is key
  2. Kids need independence
  3. It's never too late to change

Watching Freaky Friday might seem like just a fun movie, but it actually offers some valuable parenting lessons. By focusing on communication, independence, and the ability to change, we can improve our relationships with our kids and be better parents.
