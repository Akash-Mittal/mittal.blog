The Art of Parenting Through Pride Month

June is commonly celebrated as Pride month in honor of the LGBTQ+ community. While many of us look forward to colorful parades and events during Pride month, it's also an essential time to instill important values in our children regarding love, diversity, and acceptance. As a parent, you have the power to influence your child's perceptions and attitudes. So, it's critical to take advantage of this time to actively teach your kids about the LGBTQ+ community and create a safe space for all kids.

When my son, Tom, was six years old, we went to the park and saw a family with two moms. Tom curiously asked me, "Mommy, why does that family look a little different than ours?". I seized the moment and explained to him how families could have different structures. I explained to him that some families have two moms or dads, and it's essential to respect everyone, no matter how they look or who they love. Since then, our family's conversations around Pride month and diversity have become more rigorous and educated.

  • Teach your children about the existence of the LGBTQ+ community. Studies show that conversations with children about gender and sexual orientation have a positive impact on their attitudes and improve their decision-making skills.
  • Show and share with your children books, documentaries, and media representation of LGBTQ+ people. The representation of diversity, especially in children's media, can influence how they perceive others and themselves.
  • Participate in Pride month events in your area. Watching the parade and other activities could help clear up children's confusion and misconceptions about LGBTQ+ people and foster a sense of social responsibility.
  • Encourage your children to ask any questions they may have and provide them with accurate and age-appropriate responses to help them understand the world around them better.
  • Teach your children to use inclusive language, such as the use of "they" as a gender-neutral pronoun, instead of "he" or "she."

How to raise an accepting child in the month of Pride: The Essential Guide

or Case Studies

Recently, a family friend shared a story with me about how she had used Pride month to explain to her five-year-old child how gender worked. Her child had always been curious about how people decided whether they were boys or girls, and her friend decided it was high time she explained it to her. Together, they discussed different labels and how they applied. The child was surprised to learn that sex organs don't identify gender, and that she could be and do anything she wanted, regardless of whether it's usually considered masculine or feminine.

Conclusion in Three Points

  1. Pride month is an essential time to encourage children to be accepting of all people, regardless of their background, beliefs, or gender identity.
  2. As parents, it's important to have frequent conversations around diversity, inclusion, and acceptance. Children learn through active engagement and repetition.
  3. It's critical to provide age-appropriate and accurate information about the LGBTQ+ community to help dispel stereotypes and promote positive attitudes.
