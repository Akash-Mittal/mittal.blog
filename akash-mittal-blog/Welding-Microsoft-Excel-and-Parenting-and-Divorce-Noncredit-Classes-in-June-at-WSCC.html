From Welding to Microsoft Excel and Personal Growth: Noncredit Classes at WSCC

As the summer heat sets in, so does the anticipation of learning something new. Whether you're interested in building a new skill or navigating personal challenges, noncredit classes at Westchester Community College (WSCC) offer a diverse range of options. From welding and Microsoft Excel to parenting and divorce, here's a glimpse at some of the classes happening in June at WSCC.

Welding 101: From Melted Metal to Masterful Art

Curious about welding? Want to learn how to create metal sculptures or fix broken objects around the house? WSCC's welding noncredit class offers an introduction to the basics of welding, including safety, equipment use, and techniques for different types of welding.

In just four classes, students will gain hands-on experience in welding and can create projects ranging from a simple metal cube to a metal sculpture. Joining the class requires a few basic tools, such as a welding helmet and gloves, but WSCC also offers access to a well-equipped welding shop for class projects.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, welding jobs are expected to grow by 3% by 2029, with an average pay of $44,190 per year. With WSCC's noncredit class, you could have the foundation to pursue a career in welding or just master a new skill for personal projects.

Excel your Microsoft Excel Skills with Noncredit Training

Whether you're a beginner or advanced user, WSCC's Microsoft Excel noncredit class can take your skills to the next level. The class offers lessons in Excel's most useful features, such as organizing data, using formulas and functions, creating charts and graphs, and managing large spreadsheets.

Excel is one of the most in-demand skills in the United States, with 82% of middle-skill jobs requiring some level of digital skills, such as Excel. By completing the Excel noncredit class, students can show their proficiency in Excel to employers and increase their chances of getting hired in digital-based jobs.

The class also provides access to Office 365, which offers online spreadsheet collaboration and file sharing. With just $300 for the four-class program, you'll gain new skills to excel in your work and personal projects.

Parenting and Divorce: Navigating the Challenges for Personal Growth

Parenting and divorce can be challenging, emotionally and legally. WSCC's Parenting and Divorce noncredit class provides a safe and supportive space for students to share their experiences and learn how to handle situations and conflicts related to parenting and divorce.

The class covers topics such as the legal process of divorce, parenting and visitation issues, child support, and communication techniques. A licensed attorney facilitates the classes and provides useful insights and resources for students.

If you're a parent going through a divorce or you have friends or family members in that situation, the Parenting and Divorce class can offer ways to cope and heal. By participating in the class, you'll discover new perspectives and approaches to parenting and divorce and connect with others experiencing similar challenges.

Conclusion: Join a Noncredit Class in June to Learn, Grow, and Connect with Others

WSCC's noncredit class program offers something for every interest and need, from welding and Excel to parenting and divorce. By participating in a noncredit class, you can gain new skills, show proficiency to employers, and cope with personal challenges. Additionally, you'll have the opportunity to meet and connect with like-minded individuals and enjoy a unique learning environment.

So, what are you waiting for? Join a noncredit class today and see how you can excel in your life!
