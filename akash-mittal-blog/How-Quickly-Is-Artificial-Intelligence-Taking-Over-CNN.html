How Quickly Is Artificial Intelligence Taking Over CNN?

It was just a typical Monday for Harriet, a news writer at CNN, until she received a strange message. It read, "Hi, Harriet! I have written a news article about the impact of artificial intelligence on journalism. Would you like to review it?" Harriet was intrigued, but also a bit skeptical. She had heard a lot about AI taking over human jobs, but could it really write a news article as well as she could?

Curiosity got the better of her, and she replied with a "Yes, please." A few minutes later, she received the article. She was surprised to find that it was well-written and covered all the important points. She even had to admit that it was better than what she could have written in the same amount of time.

Harriet's experience is just one example of how artificial intelligence is taking over the journalism industry, and specifically, CNN. But just how quickly is this happening?

The Numbers

In 2016, CNN launched a new platform called "NewsPulse," which uses AI to curate news articles based on user preferences. This platform helped CNN increase its mobile audience by 25%. Since then, the company has continued to invest in AI technology, including using AI to transcribe interviews, create video clips, and even write news articles.

According to a report by PwC, AI is expected to contribute $15.7 trillion to the global economy by 2030. Furthermore, the same report predicts that AI could increase labor productivity by up to 40%. These numbers show that AI is not just a passing trend, but a tool that is going to significantly shape the future of job markets.

The Pros and Cons

One of the biggest advantages of using AI in journalism is its ability to automate repetitive tasks, such as transcription and translation. This frees up human journalists to focus on more creative and investigative work. Additionally, AI can analyze large amounts of data to identify trends and patterns, which can be useful in creating data-driven stories.

However, there are also some drawbacks to using AI in journalism. One of the biggest concerns is the potential for bias. Since AI algorithms are only as unbiased as the data they are trained on, there is a risk that they can perpetuate existing biases in news coverage. Additionally, there is a fear that AI could further erode public trust in journalism by creating a perception that news articles are being written by machines rather than humans.

The Way Forward

As with any new technology, there are challenges that must be addressed when incorporating AI into journalism. One way to mitigate bias is to ensure that AI is being trained on diverse data sets that accurately reflect the world we live in. Additionally, journalists and media companies need to be transparent about their use of AI and communicate to audiences how it is being used. Finally, it is important to remember that AI is a tool, not a replacement for human journalists. While AI can assist in certain tasks, it cannot replace the creativity and intuition of the human mind.
