Why Banning Tools Like ChatGPT For Students Is A Terrible Idea When They Will Have To Work With AI

Once upon a time, there was a group of high school students who were preparing for their final exams. They were all anxious and nervous about their exams, especially the essay-writing portion of the test. Then, one of them suggested using an AI-powered tool called ChatGPT to help them with their writing tasks. They were hesitant at first, but after some convincing, they all decided to give it a try. To their surprise, not only did ChatGPT help them with their essays, but it also taught them new things and corrected their mistakes. They passed their exams with flying colors, and some of them even got higher scores than they expected.

How AI-Powered Tools Can Benefit Students

  • According to a study by Pearson Education, AI-powered tutors can deliver personalized and adaptive instruction to students, which can increase their performance by up to 30%.
  • AI-powered tools like WriteLab can help students improve their writing skills and reduce errors by up to 50%.
  • In a study conducted by Carnegie Mellon University, students who used an AI-powered tutor to learn algebra improved their scores by an average of 32%, compared to just 20% for those who learned without the tutor.

Why Banning Tools Like ChatGPT for Students Is A Terrible Idea

For some educators and policymakers, the idea of using AI-powered tools in the classroom may seem like cheating or cutting corners. They argue that students should learn how to think critically and solve problems on their own, without relying on technology. However, this view fails to take into account the fact that AI is becoming an increasingly important part of our lives and our work.

According to a report by Gartner, AI will create 2.3 million jobs by 2020, while eliminating 1.8 million jobs. This means that students who graduate without any experience or familiarity with AI may be at a disadvantage in the job market. By allowing students to use AI-powered tools like ChatGPT, we can help them develop the skills they need to succeed in the future.

Moreover, banning tools like ChatGPT can create a digital divide between students who have access to these tools and those who do not. Students from lower-income families or underfunded schools may not have the resources to purchase or access these tools. By banning them, we are depriving these students of an opportunity to learn and grow.

The Benefits of Using AI-Powered Tools in Education

Here are three key benefits of using AI-powered tools in education:

  1. Personalized Learning: AI-powered tools can deliver customized lessons and instruction based on a student's individual needs and learning style. This can help students learn more efficiently and effectively.
  2. Increased Access: AI-powered tools can provide access to quality education resources and tutors for students who may not have had this opportunity otherwise.
  3. Improved Performance: AI-powered tools can help students learn faster and retain information better, leading to improved academic performance and better job prospects in the future.

Conclusion

To ban AI-powered tools like ChatGPT for students would be a terrible idea. Instead, we should embrace these tools and use them to help students develop the skills they need for the future.
