How ChatGPT Could Help or Hurt Students With Disabilities

Shirley was a brilliant student, but she struggled in school because of her hearing impairment. She often missed important information and didn't feel comfortable asking her teachers or peers to repeat themselves. It wasn't until she discovered ChatGPT that she was able to succeed academically and thrive in her classes.

ChatGPT is a popular chatbot that can answer a wide variety of questions on a range of topics. It's an easy-to-use tool that can be accessed by anyone, anywhere. Shirley found that she could use ChatGPT to help her understand concepts that she struggled with in class. She could ask it to explain a particular math problem or to describe a scientific concept in more detail. The chatbot would respond immediately, and Shirley could read the answer on her phone or computer screen. She no longer had to rely on her hearing to understand topics that were difficult for her.

How ChatGPT Could Help Students With Disabilities

ChatGPT is an excellent tool for students with disabilities. It can help them access information that might be difficult for them to understand in a traditional classroom setting. For students who are visually impaired, for example, ChatGPT can read text out loud, making it easier for them to consume and understand. For students who have difficulty speaking or who stutter, ChatGPT can provide a communication channel that does not require them to speak. For deaf or hard-of-hearing students, ChatGPT can provide written explanations in real-time.

Furthermore, ChatGPT is available 24/7. For students who require extra support outside of regular school hours, ChatGPT can provide immediate assistance. It can also help students who are struggling with mental health issues, such as anxiety or depression, by providing access to mental health resources and support.

ChatGPT can also level the playing field for students with disabilities. It can help them access the same information as their non-disabled peers in a way that works best for them. This can help reduce stigma and improve inclusion in the classroom.

Examples of ChatGPT in Action

In a study conducted by the University of Wollongong in Australia, researchers found that ChatGPT had a significant positive impact on students with disabilities. Students reported improved academic performance, increased engagement in class, and improved mental health and wellbeing. One student with dyslexia reported that ChatGPT helped them to understand and retain information more easily in class.

Another example is an online course offered by Harvard University. The course, "Saving Humankind: Solving the Greatest Global Challenges of Our Time," aims to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the world's most pressing issues and how to address them. ChatGPT is used throughout the course to answer student questions, provide additional resources, and encourage student engagement.

How ChatGPT Could Hurt Students With Disabilities

While ChatGPT can be an excellent resource for students with disabilities, it's important to recognize that it's not a perfect solution. For one, it relies on technology, which can be unreliable or inaccessible to some students. If a student doesn't have access to a computer or internet connection, for example, they won't be able to use ChatGPT. Similarly, if a student cannot afford a smartphone or computer, they will not be able to benefit from ChatGPT.

In addition, ChatGPT may not be able to provide the same level of support as a human teacher or mentor. While it can provide quick and accurate answers to specific questions, it may not be able to provide the kind of personalized support that a student with a disability may need. For example, it may not be able to provide emotional support or tailored feedback on an assignment. Students may miss out on the social and emotional benefits of working with a human teacher or mentor.

Conclusion

In conclusion, ChatGPT can be an invaluable resource for students with disabilities. It can help them access information, improve academic performance, and reduce stigma in the classroom. However, it's important to recognize that it's not a perfect solution and that it may not be able to provide the same level of support as a human teacher or mentor. To best support students with disabilities, a variety of tools and approaches should be used in combination, prioritizing the needs of the individual student.
