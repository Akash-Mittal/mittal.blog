How Photoshop's AI Generative Fill is Revolutionizing Photo Editing

Posted by John Doe on April 1, 2021

Have you ever taken a stunning photo, only to find that there's an unsightly object in the foreground that completely ruins the shot? Or maybe you've inherited an old family photo that's been torn or damaged over time. Well, fear not, because Adobe's latest AI feature, Generative Fill, is here to save the day!

Generative Fill is an incredible new tool in Photoshop that uses machine learning algorithms to automatically fill in unwanted or missing areas of an image. This means that you can easily remove unwanted objects or repair damaged photos without having to spend hours painstakingly retouching each pixel.

But how does it work?

The AI algorithm behind Generative Fill was trained on millions of images to develop an understanding of what different objects look like and how they're likely to be situated in a photo. Using this knowledge, the algorithm is able to intelligently analyze an image and generate new pixels that fit seamlessly into the surrounding area.

But this isn't just a gimmick - Generative Fill is a seriously powerful tool that's already having a huge impact in the photography world. Here are just a few examples of how it's being used:

  • Removing unwanted objects: With Generative Fill, you can easily remove objects from an image that you don't want, like that annoying person who walked into your shot at the last second. Simply select the object with the lasso tool, and watch as Generative Fill seamlessly fills in the background behind it.
  • Repairing old photos: If you've got old family photos that have seen better days, Generative Fill can help you restore them to their former glory. Simply scan the damaged photo into Photoshop, and use Generative Fill to fill in any missing areas, like cracks or stains.
  • Creating seamless panoramas: Panoramic photos are notoriously difficult to get right, because it's almost impossible to perfectly align each shot. But with Generative Fill, you can "paint" in missing areas and blend the different photos together seamlessly.

Of course, Generative Fill isn't a perfect tool - there are some instances where it won't work as well as you'd like. But for the most part, it's an incredibly powerful addition to Photoshop that's making photo editing faster and easier than ever before.

Conclusion

In summary, Generative Fill is a game-changing tool for anyone who works with photos on a regular basis. Its ability to intelligently fill in missing or unwanted areas of an image is nothing short of miraculous, and it's already being used by photographers and designers all over the world to save time and improve the quality of their work. If you haven't tried it out yet, we highly recommend giving it a go!
