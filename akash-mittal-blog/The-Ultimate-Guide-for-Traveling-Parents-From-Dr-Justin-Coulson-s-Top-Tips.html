The Ultimate Guide for Traveling Parents: From Dr Justin Coulson's Top Tips

As a parent, we all understand the challenges of traveling with children. The moment you step on a plane, it's as if your entire life is suddenly transported to another world. It's not just the physical act of getting from point A to point B that can be stressful, but also the emotional toll of being away from loved ones and the comfort of home. Dr. Justin Coulson, parenting expert and author of the book 21 Days to a Happier Family, is sharing his top tips for traveling parents to make the journey easier and more enjoyable for the entire family.

An Interesting Story:

When we asked Dr. Coulson about his personal travel experience, he shared a poignant story. "I remember the first time I took my young family on a long-haul flight to the US. We landed in LA, and the first thing my daughter said was, "Daddy, can we go home?" She was only four years old, but I realised how overwhelming it must have been for her. For the rest of the trip, we made sure to take things at a slower pace, and we also took the time to fold in familiar routines, like bedtime stories and morning walks. After that, she felt more comfortable, and we all enjoyed the trip a lot more."

Conclusion in 3 points:

1. Preparation: Plan ahead and use checklists to stay organised and on track. Consider things like packing essentials, travel insurance, and passports. Also, involve your children in the process to create a sense of excitement and ownership.

2. Communication: Set expectations and communicate openly with your kids about the trip. Talk about things like potential language barriers, different customs and cultures, jet lag, and how to handle unexpected situations.

3. Flexibility: Be prepared for the unexpected, whether that means a last-minute flight delay or a sudden illness. Be ready to change plans or take a break if necessary. Finally, try to find the balance between a structured travel itinerary and spontaneous moments of joy and wonder.

:

In his book, Dr. Coulson shares several real-life examples of families who have thrived while traveling together. One story that stands out is about a family that took a six-month trip around the world. Despite the challenges they faced, such as missing a plane and having to sleep in a park, the family reported that the experience was transformational. They learned to rely on each other, and they discovered new cultures and perspectives that they will carry with them for a lifetime.

Practical Tips:

Here are some practical tips from Dr. Coulson to help make your next family trip a success:

- Involve your children in the planning process, from choosing destinations to packing their own bags.

- Bring familiar routines and comforts with you, like favorite books or toys.

- Keep a sense of humor and stay positive, even when things don't go as planned.

- Take breaks when needed, and don't forget to give each other space and alone time.

- Use travel apps and online resources to help you navigate unfamiliar places.

- Set limits on screen time and encourage your kids to engage in the world around them.
