Predicting Diabetes Burden on India by 2050

A Research Article by Akash Mittal

Diabetes is a growing concern in India, with the number of people affected by this chronic disease increasing steadily year after year. It is estimated that currently, over 75 million people in India suffer from diabetes, and it is predicted that this number will rise to 123 million by 2040, and 134 million by 2050.

The impact of this growing burden of diabetes is already being felt across the country, with millions of people struggling to manage the disease and its complications. This trend is expected to continue unless drastic measures are taken to address the root causes of the problem.

Several major companies in India have already taken steps to promote diabetes awareness, prevention, and management. One such company is Apollo Hospitals, which has launched several programs aimed at educating the public about diabetes and providing support to patients.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the growing burden of diabetes in India is a major concern that requires urgent attention from all stakeholders.
