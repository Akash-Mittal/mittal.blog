Web3: The Thinking Web Takes Centre Stage - IO Innovation Origins

The future of the internet is already here, and it's called Web3. Unlike its predecessors, Web1 and Web2, which were primarily about connecting people to information and each other, Web3 takes things to a whole new level. It's not just about connecting people, but machines too. And it's not just about sharing information, but value too.

But to truly understand what Web3 is all about, let's start with an example. Imagine you want to buy a house. Traditionally, you would go through a real estate agent and other intermediaries to make the purchase. With Web3, however, you can buy the house directly from the seller using a digital contract called a smart contract. This smart contract would automatically execute once certain predetermined conditions are met. The process would be faster, cheaper, and more transparent.

Real Life Examples

Here are some other real life examples of how Web3 is being used:

  • Musician Imogen Heap is using Web3 to build a platform called Mycelia, which aims to create a fairer music industry by giving artists more control over their work and revenue streams.
  • Blockchain-based gaming platform Decentraland allows users to buy, sell, and trade virtual real estate using cryptocurrency.
  • Startup Civic is using Web3 to create a digital identity platform, where users can securely store and share their personal information.

Main Companies in the Article

Some of the main companies driving Web3 forward include:

  • Ethereum, which is the second largest cryptocurrency by market capitalization and the most widely used blockchain for building decentralized applications.
  • Filecoin, which is a decentralized storage network that allows users to earn cryptocurrency by renting out their unused storage space.
  • Algorand, which is a blockchain platform that claims to solve some of the biggest scalability, security, and decentralization challenges facing Web3 today.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Web3 represents a fundamental shift in how we think about the internet. It's not just about connecting people and information, but also machines and value. The examples and companies listed above are just a small taste of what's to come. However, there are still many challenges and obstacles to overcome before Web3 can become mainstream. Some of these include scalability, interoperability, and regulation. Nevertheless, Web3 is here to stay, and it's up to us to shape its future.
