People Would Rather Cuddle Their Pet Than Partner Study Finds

Jack and Jill have been together for many years. They have two children, a dog and a cat. They live in a cozy home with a fireplace and every night they cuddle under a warm blanket on their sofa. However, something changed after they read a recent study.

The study found that 61% of pet owners prefer to cuddle with their pets than their partners. The research, conducted by OnePoll on behalf of pet food company Itchpet.com, surveyed 2,000 pet owners and found that pets provide emotional benefits that are hard to replicate in human relationships. The study also found that 69% of owners said their pets are always there for them, while 56% said their pets never judge them.

After reading about this study, Jack and Jill became mindful of their behavior. They noticed that they spent more time cuddling with their pets than with each other. They also realized that they were taking their relationship for granted. They decided to make a change and started to prioritize their intimate moments together.

The study revealed some interesting statistics:

  • 61% of pet owners prefer to cuddle with their pets than their partners.
  • 69% of owners said their pets are always there for them.
  • 56% of owners said their pets never judge them.
  • 49% of owners said their pets were better listeners than their partners.
  • 29% of owners said they spoke more openly to their pets than their partners.
  • 25% of owners said they confided in their pets more than in their partners.

These numbers indicate that pets play an important role in the emotional lives of their owners. They provide a sense of comfort, non-judgment, and support that is often missing in human relationships.

Practical Tips

If you find yourself spending more time cuddling with your pet than your partner, here are some practical tips to help you reconnect:

  • Schedule romantic nights with your partner where you have no distractions.
  • Practice active listening by giving your partner your full attention when they talk to you.
  • Express appreciation and affection for your partner by saying "I love you" or giving them a hug or a kiss.
  • Make a conscious effort to be present in the moment when you're cuddling with your partner, rather than checking your phone or watching TV.
  • Learn how to communicate better by seeking counseling or reading relationship books.
